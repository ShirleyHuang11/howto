## Goal

A typical nosebleed is slowed and stopped by leaning forward, pinching the soft part of the nose, and recognizing when medical care is needed.

## Preconditions

- Tissues or clean cloth, timer, sink or bowl, and a place to sit.
- Gloves if helping another person.
- Emergency services available if bleeding is severe or linked to trauma.
- Knowledge of blood thinners or bleeding disorders if present.
- This recipe is for common nosebleeds, not major facial injury.

## Steps

1. **Sit upright.** Keep the head above the heart and stay calm. → *Expect:* blood pressure in the nose is not increased by lying down.
2. **Lean forward, not back.** Let blood drain out of the nose or mouth instead of swallowing it. → *Expect:* blood does not run down the throat.
3. **Blow gently once if needed.** Clear large clots only at the start, then stop disturbing the nose. → *Expect:* nostrils are clear enough to pinch.
4. **Pinch the soft part.** Use thumb and finger below the bony bridge, pressing both nostrils closed. → *Expect:* pressure is on the fleshy lower nose, not the hard bridge.
5. **Hold for 10-15 minutes continuously.** Use a timer and do not peek. → *Expect:* steady pressure gives a clot time to form.
6. **Breathe through the mouth.** Spit blood into a tissue if it reaches the mouth. → *Expect:* less blood is swallowed and nausea risk drops.
7. **Check once when time ends.** Release pressure gently after the full interval. → *Expect:* bleeding has stopped or clearly slowed.
8. **Repeat one more cycle if needed.** Pinch again for another 10-15 minutes. → *Expect:* most simple nosebleeds stop by the second cycle.
9. **Avoid restarting the bleed.** Do not blow, pick, strain, or bend low for several hours. → *Expect:* clot remains undisturbed.
10. **Seek care when warning signs appear.** Call emergency services for heavy bleeding, trouble breathing, fainting, major injury, or bleeding that will not stop. → *Expect:* dangerous cases are escalated promptly.

## Decision points

- Bleeding follows a hard blow, fall, or possible broken nose → seek medical care.
- Bleeding lasts more than about 20-30 minutes despite firm pressure → get urgent medical advice.
- Person takes blood thinners or has a bleeding disorder → contact a clinician sooner.
- Blood is mostly going down the throat → lean farther forward and confirm soft-part pressure.
- Nosebleeds recur often → schedule non-emergency medical evaluation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pinching the bridge:** pressure misses the bleeding vessels; move fingers lower to the soft nostrils.
- **F2 Peeking too soon:** clot breaks before forming; restart a full timed pressure cycle.
- **F3 Swallowed blood nausea:** leaning back caused drainage; lean forward and spit blood out.
- **F4 Bleeding restarts after blowing:** clot was dislodged; pinch again and avoid nose blowing.
- **F5 Severe or prolonged bleeding:** home care is failing; call emergency services or seek urgent care.

## Verification

Bleeding has stopped after continuous soft-nose pressure, the person is upright and breathing comfortably, and no warning sign requiring urgent care is present.

## Variations

- Dry air prevention: use humidification or saline spray after bleeding has stopped and tissue is healed.
- Helping a child: have them sit forward and pinch for them while using a timer.
- Nose spray advice: use only if recommended by a clinician or dispatcher.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because heavy bleeding or trauma can require urgent care. Lean forward, avoid swallowing blood, use gloves when helping, and call emergency services for severe symptoms.
