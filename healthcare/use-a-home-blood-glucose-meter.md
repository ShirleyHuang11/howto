## Goal

A home blood glucose reading is taken with clean technique, logged with context, and followed up according to the care plan or urgent thresholds.

## Preconditions

- Glucose meter, compatible test strips, lancing device, new lancet, tissues, and sharps container.
- Hands can be washed with soap and water.
- Test strips are not expired and match the meter.
- You know when your clinician asked you to test, such as fasting, before meals, or after meals.
- You have instructions for what readings require action.

## Steps

1. **Wash and dry hands.** Use soap and warm water, then dry fully. → *Expect:* sugar residue and moisture are removed.
2. **Prepare the meter and strip.** Insert a compatible test strip and wait for the blood-drop prompt. → *Expect:* the meter is ready to accept blood.
3. **Load a new lancet.** Place a fresh lancet in the lancing device and set depth as needed. → *Expect:* the device is ready and the needle was not reused.
4. **Lance the side of a fingertip.** Press the device on the side, not the pad center, and trigger it. → *Expect:* a small blood drop forms with less tenderness.
5. **Apply blood to the strip.** Touch the drop to the strip edge or target area as directed by the meter. → *Expect:* the meter begins counting or processing.
6. **Read and log the result.** Record the number, unit, date, time, meal timing, medication, exercise, and symptoms. → *Expect:* the reading has enough context to interpret later.
7. **Dispose safely.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* put the used lancet directly into a sharps container and do not recap by hand if unsafe. → *Expect:* the sharp cannot stick anyone.

## Decision points

- Reading seems surprising → wash hands again, use a new strip, and retest.
- Meter shows an error → check strip orientation, expiration, sample size, and battery.
- Reading is very low or high compared with your clinician's action plan → follow that plan promptly.
- You have confusion, fainting, seizure, severe weakness, vomiting, trouble breathing, or cannot keep fluids down → seek emergency care.
- You do not have personal thresholds → ask your clinician what numbers should trigger food, medication, retesting, calling, or urgent care.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wet or sugary finger:** result is unexpectedly high or diluted → wash, dry, and retest with a new strip.
- **F2 Too little blood:** meter errors or does not count down → warm hands, lower the hand briefly, and lance a new site with a new strip.
- **F3 Expired strips:** repeated errors or odd readings → replace strips and control-test if your kit includes solution.
- **F4 Lancet reuse:** finger soreness or infection risk increases → use a fresh lancet each time.
- **F5 Unsafe sharp:** lancet left loose in trash → place it in an approved sharps container or puncture-resistant container per local rules.

## Verification

The meter displays a result, the value is logged with timing context, and the used lancet is secured in a sharps container.

## Variations

- Alternate-site testing: use only if your meter allows it and not when glucose may be changing quickly.
- Continuous glucose monitor backup: use a fingerstick when symptoms do not match sensor readings or the device asks for confirmation.
- Caregiver testing: wear gloves if contact with blood is likely and document whose reading it is.
- Travel: carry extra strips, lancets, batteries, and a compact sharps solution.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because readings can affect medication, food, and urgent care decisions. Follow your clinician's thresholds, and ask for written instructions if you do not have them. Treat all lancets as sharps and all readings as private health information. Seek urgent help for severe symptoms or readings your care plan labels dangerous.
