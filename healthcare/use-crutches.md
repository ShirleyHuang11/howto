## Goal

Crutches are fitted and used safely for walking, sitting, and stairs without putting pressure on the armpits.

## Preconditions

- A clinician has told you how much weight, if any, the injured leg may bear.
- Call emergency services for severe injury, loss of feeling, blue or cold foot, or inability to move toes after trauma.
- Seek medical care if pain worsens, swelling increases, or crutch use causes falls, numb hands, or armpit pain.
- Use shoes with backs and nonslip soles.
- Clear loose rugs, cords, wet floors, and clutter from the walking path.

## Steps

1. **Fit the height.** Stand tall with crutch tips 6 inches ahead and to the side of each foot. → *Expect:* the top pads sit about 1 to 2 inches below the armpits.
2. **Set the handgrips.** Adjust grips so elbows bend slightly when hands hold the grips. → *Expect:* weight can go through the hands, not the armpits.
3. **Start in tripod position.** Place both crutches ahead with the injured leg between or slightly behind them. → *Expect:* your body feels balanced over three points.
4. **Walk with the prescribed weight.** [BRANCH: non-weight-bearing | partial weight-bearing] move crutches first, then the injured leg as allowed, then step through with the good leg. → *Expect:* the injured leg does not take more weight than allowed.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not ignore a non-weight-bearing order because it can disrupt healing or a repair.
5. **Keep pressure off armpits.** Push down through the handgrips and keep shoulders relaxed. → *Expect:* armpits are not carrying body weight.
6. **Sit down safely.** Back up until the chair touches your good leg, hold both crutches on the injured side, and lower with the other hand on the chair. → *Expect:* you sit without falling backward.
7. **Stand up safely.** Hold both crutches on the injured side, push from the chair, then place one crutch under each arm. → *Expect:* you regain balance before walking.
8. **Use stairs correctly.** Go up with the good leg first, then crutches and injured leg; go down with crutches and injured leg first, then the good leg. → *Expect:* good-leg-up and bad-leg-down stays consistent.
9. **Stop when unsafe.** Pause for dizziness, wet floors, fatigue, numb hands, or pain. → *Expect:* falls are prevented by stopping early.

## Decision points

- No rail on stairs and balance is poor → sit and scoot, or get help.
- Hands become numb or armpits hurt → refit crutches and reduce armpit pressure.
- Foot becomes cold, blue, numb, or much more swollen → seek urgent medical care.
- Weight-bearing status is unclear → call the clinician before walking.
- Fall occurs or pain sharply increases → stop using the crutches and get medical advice.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Armpit pressure:** detect soreness, tingling, or numb hands, recover by lowering shoulders and using handgrips.
- **F2 Crutches too tall:** detect pads jammed into armpits, recover by shortening one notch and rechecking grip height.
- **F3 Stair sequence reversed:** detect injured leg leading upward, recover by repeating good-leg-up and bad-leg-down.
- **F4 Slipping tips:** detect worn rubber or wet surfaces, recover by replacing tips and drying the path.

## Verification

Crutches fit below the armpits, weight goes through the hands, and the person can walk several steps without breaking the clinician's weight-bearing limit.

## Variations

- Partial weight-bearing: place only the allowed amount of weight on the injured leg as it moves with the crutches.
- One step or curb: treat it like stairs, up with the good leg and down with crutches plus the injured leg.
- Tall user or child: use correctly sized crutches rather than overextending the adjustment holes.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because falls and excess weight-bearing can worsen injury. Get medical help for circulation changes, new numbness, worsening pain, or repeated instability.
