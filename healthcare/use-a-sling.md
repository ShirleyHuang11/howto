## Goal

An injured arm is supported in a sling while circulation and warning signs are monitored.

## Preconditions

- A clinician has recommended a sling, or it is temporary support while arranging care.
- Call emergency services for major deformity, open fracture, severe bleeding, blue or cold fingers, loss of feeling, or injury with chest pain or trouble breathing.
- Seek same-day medical care for suspected fracture, dislocation, severe pain, numbness, weakness, or inability to use the arm.
- Supplies: commercial sling or triangular cloth, plus a small towel if padding is needed.
- Remove rings and tight wrist items early if swelling is possible.

## Steps

1. **Support the arm.** Keep the elbow bent about 90 degrees and the hand slightly higher than the elbow. → *Expect:* the forearm rests comfortably across the chest.
2. **Place the sling under the arm.** [BRANCH: commercial sling | cloth sling] slide the pouch or cloth under the forearm from elbow to hand. → *Expect:* the whole forearm is supported, not just the wrist.
3. **Tie or fasten at the neck.** Bring the ends behind the neck and secure them so the wrist stays supported. → *Expect:* the arm does not sag and the neck is not pinched.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not force a deformed shoulder, elbow, or wrist back into place.
4. **Secure the elbow.** Tuck or pin the sling so the elbow cannot slip backward. → *Expect:* the elbow stays inside the support.
5. **Pad pressure points.** Add a small towel under the neck strap if it rubs or presses. → *Expect:* the neck and shoulder feel supported without sharp pressure.
6. **Check circulation.** Look at finger color and warmth, then ask about numbness or tingling. → *Expect:* fingers are warm, pink, and normally sensitive.
7. **Adjust if needed.** Loosen or retie the sling if fingers swell, turn blue, become cold, or tingle. → *Expect:* circulation symptoms improve after adjustment.
8. **Elevate when resting.** Prop the hand higher than the elbow on pillows when sitting or lying down. → *Expect:* swelling and throbbing are reduced.
9. **Limit use.** Avoid lifting, pushing, pulling, or driving until cleared. → *Expect:* the injured arm stays protected.

## Decision points

- Suspected fracture, dislocation, major swelling, severe pain, numbness, or weakness → see a doctor today.
- Finger color, warmth, or sensation changes and does not improve after loosening → seek urgent care.
- Shoulder injury with chest pain, shortness of breath, or sweating → call emergency services.
- Sling causes neck pain → repad, shorten the strap, or ask for a different support.
- Pain is mild and improving after clinician advice → continue sling use as instructed.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrist hangs low:** detect hand below elbow, recover by shortening the sling.
- **F2 Neck strap painful:** detect rubbing or pressure, recover by adding padding or using a wider strap.
- **F3 Circulation reduced:** detect cold, blue, numb, or swollen fingers, recover by loosening and seeking care if unresolved.
- **F4 Injury masked:** detect worsening pain despite immobilization, recover by getting medical assessment.

## Verification

The forearm is supported across the chest with the hand slightly elevated, and fingers remain warm, pink, and normally sensitive.

## Variations

- Child: use a smaller sling and recheck finger circulation often.
- Wrist injury: support the hand fully so the wrist does not bend downward.
- Temporary cloth: fold a square cloth into a triangle and tie the two long ends behind the neck.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because slings can hide serious fractures, dislocations, or circulation problems. Seek medical help for deformity, numbness, severe pain, or any circulation change that persists.
