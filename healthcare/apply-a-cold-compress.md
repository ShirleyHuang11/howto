## Goal

Cold is applied safely through a barrier to reduce early pain and swelling without causing skin injury.

## Preconditions

- A recent minor sprain, bruise, bump, strain, insect bite, or mild swelling where cold is appropriate.
- Ice pack, frozen gel pack, or bag of ice, plus a towel or cloth barrier.
- No open deep wound, numb skin, poor circulation, frostbite, or major injury with deformity or inability to use the limb.

## Steps

1. **Check the injury first.** Look for deformity, severe swelling, open wound, numbness, or inability to bear weight or move normally. → *Expect:* the injury appears minor enough for home care.
2. **Wrap the cold source.** Put ice or a cold pack inside a towel; never place ice directly on skin. → *Expect:* cold passes through cloth without burning the skin.
3. **Position comfortably.** Rest the injured area and elevate it if swelling is present. → *Expect:* the area is supported and not under strain.
4. **Apply for 20 minutes.** Keep the compress on for up to 20 minutes, checking skin every few minutes. → *Expect:* pain or swelling feels reduced and skin stays normal colored.
5. **Take 20 minutes off.** Remove the compress and let the skin return fully to normal temperature. → *Expect:* skin feels comfortable before another round.
6. **Repeat early if useful.** Use 20 minutes on and 20 minutes off during the first day or two after minor injury. → *Expect:* swelling is managed without numbness or skin damage.
7. **Switch strategy when stiffness dominates.** After the early swelling phase, gentle movement or heat may feel better for tight muscles. → *Expect:* treatment matches the symptom.
8. **Protect the skin.** Stop cold immediately if skin turns white, blue, waxy, numb, or painful. → *Expect:* skin returns to normal after warming.

## Decision points

- Use cold for recent swelling, bruising, sprain, strain, bump, or acute pain after activity.
- Use heat instead for chronic muscle tightness without swelling, but avoid heat over fresh swelling or infection.
- See a doctor for deformity, inability to bear weight, worsening pain, numbness, large swelling, or symptoms not improving after 2 to 3 days.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Skin becomes numb or blotchy:** remove the compress, warm gently, and shorten future sessions.
- **F2 Pain worsens under cold:** stop and reassess for a more serious injury.
- **F3 Ice leaks onto skin:** replace the barrier and dry the area before continuing.
- **F4 Swelling keeps increasing:** elevate, stop activity, and seek medical advice.

## Verification

Cold was applied through a barrier for no more than 20 minutes at a time, with equal warming breaks and no abnormal skin changes.

## Variations

- Face injury: use lighter pressure and shorter checks because skin is sensitive.
- Insect bite: cold can reduce itch and swelling, but spreading redness or breathing symptoms need care.
- After vaccination: a cool damp cloth can help soreness without heavy pressure.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when timed and wrapped. Seek urgent care for severe injury, deformity, numbness, blue or pale limb, allergic breathing symptoms, or signs of infection such as fever, warmth, pus, or spreading redness.
