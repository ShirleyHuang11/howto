## Goal

A walking cane is fitted and used on the correct side to improve balance and reduce strain on a weak or painful leg.

## Preconditions

- Use a cane for mild balance support or clinician-approved leg weakness, not for major non-weight-bearing injuries.
- Call emergency services for sudden weakness, stroke signs, chest pain, fainting, or a new inability to walk.
- Seek medical care for new leg weakness, severe pain, repeated falls, or a cold, blue, numb, or swollen foot.
- Use a cane with an intact rubber tip.
- Wear supportive nonslip shoes.

## Steps

1. **Set the cane height.** Stand tall with the cane tip near the outside of the foot. → *Expect:* the handle lines up with the wrist crease.
2. **Check elbow bend.** Hold the handle with the arm relaxed. → *Expect:* the elbow bends slightly, about 15 to 30 degrees.
3. **Hold it on the strong side.** [BRANCH: weak right leg | weak left leg] hold the cane in the hand opposite the weak or painful leg. → *Expect:* the cane supports the weak side during stepping.
4. **Move cane and weak leg together.** Step the cane forward with the weak leg, then step through with the strong leg. → *Expect:* weight shifts smoothly through cane and strong leg.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not use a cane to avoid evaluation for sudden weakness or stroke symptoms.
5. **Keep steps short.** Place the cane only a comfortable step ahead, not far out in front. → *Expect:* posture stays upright and balanced.
6. **Turn slowly.** Take small steps instead of pivoting on the weak leg. → *Expect:* the cane stays planted during turns.
7. **Use stairs with a rail.** Go up with the strong leg first, then cane and weak leg; go down with cane and weak leg first, then strong leg. → *Expect:* strong-leg-up and weak-leg-down remains consistent.
8. **Inspect the tip.** Check the rubber tip for cracks, wear, or looseness. → *Expect:* the cane grips the floor.
9. **Pause for unsafe symptoms.** Stop for dizziness, chest pain, shortness of breath, or sudden weakness. → *Expect:* emergency symptoms are not treated as a balance problem.

## Decision points

- Sudden one-sided weakness, face droop, speech trouble, or confusion → call emergency services.
- Cane does not provide enough support → ask about a walker or crutches.
- Pain increases with cane use → stop and contact a clinician.
- Stairs feel unsafe → use an elevator or get assistance.
- Rubber tip is worn → replace it before continued use.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cane on wrong side:** detect cane held on weak side, recover by switching to the strong side.
- **F2 Cane too low or high:** detect stooping or shoulder hiking, recover by adjusting handle to wrist height.
- **F3 Overreaching:** detect cane far ahead and wobbling, recover with shorter steps.
- **F4 Stair misstep:** detect weak leg leading upward, recover by pausing and restarting with the strong leg.

## Verification

The cane handle reaches the wrist crease, it is held on the strong side, and cane plus weak leg move forward together.

## Variations

- Quad cane: place all four tips flat before stepping.
- Mild balance issue without a weak side: use the cane on the preferred side and keep steps short.
- Outdoor walking: use extra caution on gravel, ice, grass, and uneven pavement.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because falls and sudden neurologic symptoms can be serious. Call emergency services for stroke signs, chest pain, fainting, or sudden inability to walk.
