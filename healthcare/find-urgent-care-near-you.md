## Goal

You identify an appropriate nearby urgent care option, confirm it is open and suitable for your problem, and know when emergency care or primary care is the better choice.

## Preconditions

- Phone or computer with internet access, or a phone number for your insurer or clinic.
- Your location, transportation options, insurance card, ID, and payment method.
- A clear description of symptoms, injury, timing, and severity.
- Ability to call emergency services if symptoms are severe.
- Awareness that urgent care is not a substitute for emergency care.

## Steps

1. **Screen for emergency symptoms.** Check for chest pain, trouble breathing, stroke signs, severe allergic reaction, severe bleeding, major trauma, or suicidal intent. → *Expect:* emergency symptoms are ruled out or you go to the ER.
2. **Use finder tools.** Search your insurer directory, health system website, map app, or urgent care chain finder near your location. → *Expect:* you have a short list of nearby clinics.
3. **Check hours and status.** Confirm today's hours, holiday changes, last check-in time, and whether walk-ins are accepted. → *Expect:* the clinic is actually open when you can arrive.
4. **Confirm scope.** Look for services such as X-ray, stitches, labs, pediatric care, occupational health, or respiratory testing. → *Expect:* the clinic can handle your likely need.
5. **Check insurance and cost.** Verify in-network status if possible and note copay, self-pay price, and required ID. → *Expect:* you know what to bring and likely pay.
6. **Compare wait and travel time.** Use online wait estimates, call the clinic, or reserve a slot if available. → *Expect:* you choose the practical fastest appropriate option.
7. **Bring essentials.** Take ID, insurance card, medication list, allergies, and relevant records or photos. → *Expect:* check-in can proceed smoothly.

## Decision points

- Symptoms are life-threatening or could worsen quickly → go to the ER or call emergency services.
- Problem is minor, stable, and can wait days → primary care may be cheaper and more continuous.
- Need stitches, X-ray, fluids, or same-day evaluation but not emergency care → urgent care may fit.
- Clinic lacks needed service, such as pediatric care or X-ray → choose another site.
- Wait time is long and symptoms worsen → escalate to the ER.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Closed clinic:** hours online were wrong → call before leaving when possible and choose the next verified option.
- **F2 Out of network:** cost is unexpectedly high → ask about self-pay price or call your insurer for alternatives.
- **F3 Wrong level of care:** urgent care redirects you to ER → go promptly and bring their paperwork.
- **F4 Missing documents:** check-in is delayed → provide photo ID, insurance details, and medication list from your phone if possible.
- **F5 Wait estimate inaccurate:** delay becomes unsafe → leave for ER if symptoms become severe.

## Verification

You have selected an open clinic that accepts your situation, know its location and costs as well as possible, and have ID, insurance, and medication information ready.

## Variations

- Rural area: call ahead because hours and services may be limited.
- Child patient: confirm the site treats the child's age.
- Work injury: ask whether the clinic handles occupational health paperwork.
- No insurance: ask for self-pay price and nearby community clinic options.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because choosing the wrong care level can delay treatment. Use emergency services for chest pain, breathing trouble, stroke signs, severe allergic reaction, severe bleeding, major trauma, poisoning, severe dehydration, suicidal intent, or sudden severe symptoms. Share only needed health and insurance information with verified clinics.
