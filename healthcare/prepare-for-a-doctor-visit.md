## Goal

Arrive at a doctor visit with the information, records, and questions needed to use the appointment well.

## Preconditions

- Appointment date, time, location or video link, clinician name, and visit reason.
- Insurance card or payment information if relevant.
- Current medications, supplements, allergies, and recent symptoms or records.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the appointment details.** Check date, time, location, parking, telehealth link, and arrival instructions. → *Expect:* you know when and where to check in.
2. **Write the main concern.** Summarize the reason for the visit in one or two sentences. → *Expect:* you can explain the visit quickly at the start.
3. **Build a symptom timeline.** Note when symptoms started, what changed, triggers, treatments tried, and what makes it better or worse. → *Expect:* timeline is specific enough to answer common questions.
4. **Make a medication list.** Include prescriptions, over-the-counter medicines, vitamins, supplements, dose, and frequency. → *Expect:* list matches what you actually take.
5. **List allergies and reactions.** Write the medicine or substance and what happened. → *Expect:* allergy information distinguishes rash, nausea, swelling, or other reaction.
6. **Prepare questions.** Put the top 3 questions first so they are covered if time is short. → *Expect:* questions are visible during the appointment.
7. **Gather records.** Bring recent test results, imaging reports, discharge papers, vaccine records, or prior visit notes if the office may not have them. → *Expect:* relevant records are saved, printed, or uploaded.
8. **Bring ID and insurance.** Pack photo ID, insurance card, referral, copay method, and forms. → *Expect:* check-in documents are ready before leaving.
9. **Plan the next step before leaving.** Ask about diagnosis, treatment, warning signs, follow-up, referrals, tests, and how results will arrive. → *Expect:* you leave with a clear action plan.

## Decision points

- Symptoms are severe, sudden, or dangerous → seek urgent or emergency care rather than waiting for the scheduled visit.
- Visit is virtual → test camera, microphone, portal login, and a private space beforehand.
- You need forms completed → bring the exact form and know the deadline.
- Language or accessibility needs → request interpreter or accommodations before the appointment.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Forgot medications:** detect uncertainty during intake, recover by showing pharmacy list, portal list, or photos of bottles.
- **F2 Ran out of time:** detect unanswered concerns, recover by stating the top priority early and scheduling follow-up.
- **F3 Records unavailable:** detect office cannot see outside results, recover by uploading, bringing copies, or signing a release.
- **F4 Unclear plan:** detect you cannot explain next steps, recover by asking the clinician to repeat the plan in plain language.

## Verification

Before the visit, you have appointment details, medication list, symptom timeline, top questions, relevant records, ID, insurance, and payment method ready.

## Variations

- New patient visit: add medical history, surgeries, family history, and prior clinician contact information.
- Specialist visit: bring referral details and prior tests related to that body system.
- Caregiver visit: bring legal authorization if needed and notes from the patient.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because health information is sensitive and care decisions may follow. Use private channels for records and seek urgent care for severe symptoms.
