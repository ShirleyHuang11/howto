## Goal

A minor burn is cooled, protected, and escalated for medical care when the burn may be serious.

## Preconditions

- This is first aid guidance, not a substitute for professional burn care or hands-on first aid training.
- Call emergency services for large, deep, electrical, chemical, inhalation, face, airway, genital, hand, foot, major joint, or circumferential burns.
- Move away from heat, electricity, chemicals, smoke, or fire only if it is safe to do so.
- Use clean cool running water if available, and avoid ice, butter, oils, toothpaste, or adhesive dressings on the burn.
- Seek medical advice for any burn in a baby, young child, older adult, or medically fragile person.

## Steps

1. **Stop the burning.** Move the person away from the heat source, turn off power if electrical, or remove them from smoke if safe. → *Expect:* no ongoing heat or exposure is injuring the skin.
2. **Call for serious burns.** [BRANCH: serious burn signs | small superficial burn] call emergency services now for serious signs; continue first aid while waiting. → *Expect:* urgent care is activated when needed.
3. **Cool the burn.** Hold the burned area under cool running water for 20 minutes, starting as soon as possible. → *Expect:* pain and heat begin to reduce.
4. **Protect from chilling.** Cool the burn area, not the whole body, and keep the rest of the person warm. → *Expect:* the person is not shivering or becoming cold.
5. **Remove tight items.** Remove rings, watches, belts, or tight clothing near the burn unless stuck to skin. → *Expect:* swelling will not trap jewelry or fabric.
6. **Leave stuck material alone.** Do not pull off clothing or objects attached to burned skin. → *Expect:* deeper tissue is not torn.
7. **Cover loosely.** Use a sterile nonstick dressing, clean cloth, or clean plastic wrap laid loosely over the burn. → *Expect:* the burn is protected without pressure or sticking.
8. **Avoid home substances.** Do not apply ice, butter, oils, toothpaste, or creams unless a clinician instructs you. → *Expect:* heat is not trapped and skin is not damaged further.
9. **Reassess severity.** Look for blistering, white or charred skin, numbness, spreading redness, severe pain, or burns crossing joints. → *Expect:* the need for medical care is reconsidered.
10. **Arrange follow-up.** Seek urgent care if pain is severe, the burn is worsening, tetanus status is uncertain, or infection signs appear. → *Expect:* the burn is not left unmanaged.

## Decision points

- Burn is deep, large, on a high-risk area, or caused by electricity or chemicals → call emergency services.
- Chemical burn → brush off dry powder with protection if trained, then flush with water and call poison control or emergency services.
- Burn forms blisters or involves a child → seek medical advice.
- Person has trouble breathing after smoke or heat exposure → call emergency services immediately.
- Pain improves and skin is only mildly red in a small area → continue simple protection and monitor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ice applied:** detect ice or freezing pack on skin, recover by removing it and using cool running water.
- **F2 Jewelry left on:** detect rings or tight items near swelling, recover by removing them early if not stuck.
- **F3 Stuck clothing pulled:** detect fabric attached to skin, recover by cutting around it and leaving attached parts for clinicians.
- **F4 Burn underestimated:** detect large area, numbness, white skin, or location on face or hands, recover by seeking urgent care.

## Verification

The burn has been cooled for 20 minutes, covered loosely with a clean nonstick barrier, and serious burn criteria have been checked for escalation.

## Variations

- No running water: use repeated cool clean compresses until running water or professional help is available.
- Hand burn: remove rings early and seek care for blistering, stiffness, or reduced movement.
- Sunburn: cool skin, hydrate, and seek care for blistering over a large area, fever, confusion, or dehydration.

## Safety & privacy

Medium to high risk because burns can worsen after the heat source is gone. Call emergency services when criteria are met, get first aid training, and use this recipe only as temporary care until proper medical advice is available.
