## Goal

A person who drank too much is kept safe, monitored continuously, and emergency help is called if alcohol poisoning is possible.

## Preconditions

- You can stay with the person or assign a sober adult to stay with them.
- A phone is available for emergency services, poison control, or local urgent help.
- Seek professional or emergency care when the person is unconscious, cannot be awakened, breathes slowly or irregularly, vomits repeatedly, has pale or blue skin, has a seizure, or is very confused.
- This recipe does not make alcohol intoxication safe and is not a substitute for emergency care.

## Steps

1. **Move away from hazards.** Guide the person away from roads, water, balconies, kitchens, or crowds. → *Expect:* the immediate environment is safer.
2. **Check responsiveness.** Speak clearly and gently tap a shoulder if they seem asleep. → *Expect:* you know whether they can wake and answer.
3. **Call emergency help for danger signs.** Treat unconsciousness, slow breathing, repeated vomiting, seizure, or blue skin as an emergency. → *Expect:* emergency care is started when alcohol poisoning is possible.
4. **Place in recovery position.** If drowsy or vomiting but breathing, roll them on their side with the top knee bent and head tilted slightly down. → *Expect:* vomit can drain away from the airway.
5. **Monitor breathing.** Count breaths and watch chest movement every few minutes. → *Expect:* breathing remains regular and not dangerously slow.
6. **Keep them warm.** Use a blanket or dry clothing without overheating. → *Expect:* shivering or cold skin is reduced.
7. **Offer small sips only if alert.** Give water only if they are awake, sitting up, and able to swallow normally. → *Expect:* they can drink without coughing or choking.
8. **Do not give more substances.** Avoid alcohol, sedatives, sleep medicine, or recreational drugs. → *Expect:* intoxication is not worsened.
9. **Do not make them vomit.** Keep their airway protected instead. → *Expect:* choking risk is not increased.
10. **Stay until sober care takes over.** Never leave them alone to sleep it off. → *Expect:* a sober person is continuously watching them.

## Decision points

- Breathing is fewer than about 8 breaths per minute, irregular, or has long pauses → call emergency services.
- Person cannot sit up, speak clearly, or stay awake → call emergency services.
- [BRANCH: awake and improving | hard to wake or worsening] improving still needs monitoring, while worsening is an emergency.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not let them drive, swim, or leave alone because intoxication can lead to fatal injury.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Left alone:** detect no sober watcher, recover by assigning one immediately or calling for help.
- **F2 Choking risk:** detect lying flat, vomiting, or gurgling, recover by using recovery position and calling emergency services if breathing is affected.
- **F3 Symptoms minimized:** detect statements like they just need sleep despite danger signs, recover by following emergency criteria.
- **F4 Mixed substances:** detect pills, drugs, or unknown drinks, recover by calling poison control or emergency services.

## Verification

A sober adult is continuously monitoring the person, breathing is regular, and emergency services have been called for any alcohol-poisoning sign.

## Variations

- Campus or event setting: call the venue medical team or resident staff in addition to emergency services.
- Unknown substances: treat as higher risk and seek urgent advice.
- Minor involved: involve a responsible adult and emergency care when safety is uncertain.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because alcohol poisoning can be fatal. Seek professional or emergency care when consciousness, breathing, temperature, skin color, seizures, repeated vomiting, or mixed substances raise concern.
