## Goal

You inspect a mole using the ABCDE rule, document changes, and know what warrants a dermatologist instead of self-treatment.

## Preconditions

- Good light, a mirror or helper, and a camera if you want to track changes.
- The mole or spot is not actively bleeding from injury.
- Do not attempt cutting, freezing, burning, or scraping a mole at home.

## Steps

1. **Find the spot clearly.** Use bright light and expose the whole area of skin. → *Expect:* the entire mole border is visible.
2. **Check A for asymmetry.** Imagine a line through the middle and compare both halves. → *Expect:* you know whether the halves roughly match.
3. **Check B for border.** Look for ragged, blurred, notched, or uneven edges. → *Expect:* border is described as even or irregular.
4. **Check C for color.** Note whether color is uniform or mixed tan, brown, black, red, white, or blue. → *Expect:* color pattern is recorded.
5. **Check D for diameter.** Measure or compare with a pencil eraser, about 6 mm, while remembering smaller spots can still matter. → *Expect:* approximate size is known.
6. **Check E for evolving.** Compare with memory, photos, or nearby moles for change in size, shape, color, elevation, bleeding, itching, or pain. → *Expect:* change is identified or ruled out.
7. **Photograph and track.** Take a clear photo with a ruler or coin beside the mole and record the date. → *Expect:* future comparison is possible.
8. **Do not self-remove.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* home removal can scar, infect, and destroy tissue needed for diagnosis. → *Expect:* the mole remains intact for medical assessment if needed.

## Decision points

- Any ABCDE warning sign, new bleeding, nonhealing sore, or a mole that looks unlike your others → book a dermatology visit.
- Rapid change over weeks, dark streak under a nail, or a new changing spot after age 40 → seek prompt medical evaluation.
- Personal or family history of melanoma, many atypical moles, or immune suppression → use a lower threshold for dermatologist checks.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Photo is blurry:** retake in daylight, hold the camera steady, and include a size reference.
- **F2 You cannot see the area:** use a mirror, ask someone trusted, or schedule a skin check.
- **F3 Mole was picked or scratched:** let it heal, photograph afterward, and see a clinician if it bleeds again or will not heal.
- **F4 You are unsure:** treat uncertainty as a reason to ask a dermatologist, not as a reason to cut it off.

## Verification

The mole has an ABCDE assessment, a dated size-reference photo when possible, and any suspicious or changing feature is scheduled for dermatologist review.

## Variations

- Darker skin: also check palms, soles, nails, and mucosal areas where acral melanoma may appear.
- Many moles: use body-map photos or periodic dermatologist exams.
- Scalp or back: a helper or clinician may be needed for adequate inspection.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because delayed melanoma diagnosis is serious and self-removal can hide evidence. See a dermatologist for ABCDE changes, bleeding without injury, nonhealing spots, rapid growth, or an ugly-duckling mole.
