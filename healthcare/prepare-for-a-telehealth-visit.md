## Goal

You enter a telehealth visit on time with working technology, useful health information, and a private setting that supports a focused medical conversation.

## Preconditions

- Appointment time, clinician name, and portal or video link.
- Smartphone, tablet, or computer with camera, microphone, speaker, and charger.
- Current medication list, allergies, pharmacy name, and insurance details if needed.
- Notes about symptoms, timing, home measurements, and questions.
- A quiet private place with adequate light.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the appointment details.** Check date, time zone, link, clinician, and whether forms are due. → *Expect:* you know exactly when and where to join.
2. **Test the technology.** Open the portal or video app, allow camera and microphone access, and check audio. → *Expect:* you can see and hear a test screen or preview.
3. **Prepare the space.** Sit somewhere quiet, private, well lit, and close to power or strong battery. → *Expect:* your face is visible and background interruptions are reduced.
4. **Gather health information.** Put medications, allergies, pharmacy, symptoms, photos, home readings, and questions within reach. → *Expect:* you can answer common intake questions quickly.
5. **Measure if relevant.** Take temperature, blood pressure, pulse, weight, glucose, or oxygen level only if you have the device and it fits the visit. → *Expect:* readings are written with time and units.
6. **Join early.** Enter the virtual waiting room 5 to 10 minutes before the appointment. → *Expect:* you are checked in before the clinician arrives.
7. **Ask for next steps.** Before ending, confirm diagnosis impression, treatment, warning signs, prescriptions, work notes, and follow-up. → *Expect:* you know what to do after the call.

## Decision points

- The video link fails → call the clinic number or use the portal message option.
- Symptoms require hands-on exam, imaging, stitches, or urgent treatment → ask whether in-person or urgent care is needed.
- You are discussing sensitive topics → use headphones and make sure no one can overhear.
- You need an interpreter or caregiver present → arrange it before the visit if possible.
- New severe symptoms occur before the visit → seek urgent or emergency care instead of waiting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Camera or microphone blocked:** the clinician cannot see or hear you → check browser permissions, switch device, or call the clinic.
- **F2 No privacy:** someone can overhear → move location, use headphones, or reschedule if needed.
- **F3 Missing medication list:** names or doses are unclear → show bottles on camera or read labels from the containers.
- **F4 Late connection:** waiting room entry fails at appointment time → call immediately so the visit is not marked missed.
- **F5 Needs exam:** clinician cannot evaluate safely by video → accept referral to in-person care.

## Verification

You joined the visit with working audio and video, had your medication list and questions ready, and left with documented next steps.

## Variations

- Phone-only visit: keep the same notes ready and confirm callback number.
- Dermatology or wound visit: take clear photos in good light before the appointment if the clinic allows upload.
- Child visit: have the child nearby, plus weight, temperature, and medication doses.
- Shared household: choose a room, car, or private corner where health details are not overheard.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for preparation, but health information is private. Use the official clinic link or portal, avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive visits when possible, and do not delay emergency care for chest pain, trouble breathing, stroke signs, severe allergic reaction, suicidal intent, or severe injury.
