## Goal

Your medical records are requested from one healthcare organization and sent to the correct recipient, with a copy kept for your own files when possible.

## Preconditions

- Names of the sending clinic, hospital, lab, or imaging center.
- Recipient name, clinic, address, fax number, portal instructions, or secure email if accepted.
- Your legal name, date of birth, phone number, and patient ID if known.
- Government ID if the release process requires identity verification.
- A clear date range and record types needed.

## Steps

1. **Identify what to transfer.** List visit notes, labs, imaging reports, actual images, vaccine records, medication lists, or billing records. → *Expect:* the request is specific enough to process.
2. **Find the release form.** Use the provider's medical records, health information management, or patient portal release process. → *Expect:* you have the correct authorization form or portal workflow.
3. **Fill in identity details.** Enter your name, date of birth, contact information, and any patient identifier exactly. → *Expect:* the records office can match you.
4. **Specify the recipient.** Add the clinician, organization, fax, mailing address, portal address, or pickup instructions. → *Expect:* the destination is unambiguous.
5. **Choose record scope and dates.** Request only the records needed, and include imaging files separately if relevant. → *Expect:* the release covers the correct material.
6. **Submit with required ID.** Send the signed form through the portal, fax, mail, or in person as instructed. → *Expect:* you receive confirmation or a reference point for follow-up.
7. **Track and keep a copy.** Save the submitted form, confirmation, and any records you receive. → *Expect:* you can prove what was requested and when.

## Decision points

- The receiving clinician needs records before an appointment → request early and call to confirm receipt.
- Imaging is needed → ask for both the radiology report and the actual image files or cloud link.
- You only need your own copy → choose patient copy if the form separates patient and provider delivery.
- The provider charges a fee → ask whether records sent for continuing care are free or lower cost.
- The request involves mental health, substance use, HIV, genetic, or minor records → extra consent rules may apply.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong destination:** fax, address, or portal recipient is incorrect → submit a corrected request and notify both offices.
- **F2 Missing signature or ID:** the request is rejected → sign, date, attach ID if required, and resubmit.
- **F3 Records incomplete:** labs, images, or notes are missing → file a supplemental request naming the missing items.
- **F4 Deadline missed:** records have not arrived before the visit → bring your own copy and ask the office to call records.
- **F5 Privacy concern:** records sent to the wrong place → contact the sending privacy office immediately.

## Verification

The receiving office confirms the correct records arrived, and you have saved a copy or confirmation of the transfer request.

## Variations

- Portal-to-portal sharing: some systems can exchange records electronically after you authorize it.
- Paper pickup: bring ID and check whether CDs, USB drives, or printed copies are offered.
- Legal or insurance request: ask exactly which records are required before releasing broad history.
- Changing doctors: request a summary plus key labs, imaging, procedures, and medication history.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because medical records contain sensitive identity and health details. Verify fax numbers, addresses, and recipient names before submitting. Keep copies in a secure place. Ask about timelines, which may range from a few days to several weeks depending on location and record type.
