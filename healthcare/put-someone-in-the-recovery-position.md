## Goal

An unresponsive person who is breathing normally is placed on their side with an open airway while emergency services are called when appropriate.

## Preconditions

- This is first aid guidance, not a substitute for professional care, dispatcher instructions, or hands-on first aid training.
- Call emergency services if the person is unresponsive, hard to wake, injured, intoxicated, had a seizure, or you are unsure what happened.
- Do not use the recovery position for someone who is not breathing normally; call emergency services and start CPR if trained.
- Do not move someone with suspected spinal injury unless needed for airway, CPR, bleeding control, vomiting risk, or immediate danger.
- Keep monitoring because breathing can change.

## Steps

1. **Check safety and response.** Make sure the scene is safe, then tap shoulders and speak loudly. → *Expect:* you know the person is unresponsive or not fully responsive.
2. **Call for help.** Ask a bystander to call emergency services and get an AED, or call on speaker if alone. → *Expect:* help is on the way or being contacted.
3. **Check breathing.** Look, listen, and feel for normal breathing for no more than 10 seconds. → *Expect:* normal breathing is confirmed before rolling.
4. **Decide whether to move.** [BRANCH: no spinal concern | spinal concern] roll if airway protection is needed and no spinal concern; avoid movement if spinal injury is suspected and breathing is clear. → *Expect:* movement risk is considered.
5. **Position the near arm.** Place the arm nearest you at a right angle to the body with palm up. → *Expect:* the arm will help stop over-rolling.
6. **Place the far hand.** Bring the far hand across the chest and hold the back of that hand against the cheek nearest you. → *Expect:* the hand supports the head during the roll.
7. **Bend the far knee.** Lift the far knee so the foot stays flat on the ground. → *Expect:* the leg becomes a lever for rolling.
8. **Roll toward you.** Pull the bent knee gently so the person rolls onto their side, keeping the hand against the cheek. → *Expect:* the person rests stably on their side.
9. **Open the airway.** Tilt the head slightly back and lift the chin, checking that the mouth points downward for drainage. → *Expect:* the airway is open and fluids can drain.
10. **Monitor continuously.** Recheck breathing and responsiveness until help arrives, and roll onto the other side after about 30 minutes if waiting long and no injury prevents it. → *Expect:* breathing changes are detected early.

## Decision points

- Not breathing normally or only gasping → do not use recovery position; start CPR if trained and use an AED.
- Suspected neck, back, or major trauma → avoid movement unless airway, vomiting, safety, CPR, or bleeding control requires it.
- Vomiting or fluid in mouth → recovery position may be needed to protect the airway.
- Pregnant person → place on the left side if practical.
- Breathing becomes abnormal after rolling → roll onto back and start CPR if trained.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 CPR delayed:** detect absent or abnormal breathing, recover by calling emergency services and starting CPR instead of rolling.
- **F2 Neck twisted:** detect chin forced sharply or head unsupported, recover by gently aligning and opening the airway.
- **F3 Spinal risk ignored:** detect fall, crash, back pain, weakness, or numbness, recover by minimizing movement and calling emergency services.
- **F4 Monitoring stopped:** detect rescuer walking away, recover by assigning someone to watch breathing continuously.

## Verification

The person is on their side, breathing normally, airway open with the mouth draining downward, emergency services are called if indicated, and breathing is monitored.

## Variations

- After seizure: roll onto the side once jerking stops and breathing is present.
- Vomiting risk: prioritize drainage while keeping movement as gentle as possible.
- Alone: call emergency services on speaker before or immediately after positioning.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because unresponsiveness can become cardiac arrest or hide serious injury. Call emergency services when appropriate, get first aid and CPR training, preserve dignity, and do not use this recipe as a substitute for professional care.
