## Goal

A wound dressing is removed, the wound is checked, and a clean dressing is applied without contaminating the area.

## Preconditions

- Follow clinician instructions if they differ from this general recipe.
- Supplies: soap, clean water, clean towel, gloves if available, sterile gauze or nonstick pad, tape or wrap, and trash bag.
- Call a doctor for spreading redness, warmth, swelling, pus, fever, worsening pain, red streaks, bad odor, or wound edges opening.
- Call emergency services for uncontrolled bleeding, shock symptoms, or a wound with exposed bone or organ tissue.
- Do not remove packing, drains, staples, stitches, or special dressings unless instructed.

## Steps

1. **Prepare a clean area.** Put supplies on a clean surface and place a trash bag nearby. → *Expect:* everything needed is reachable before the dressing is opened.
2. **Wash your hands.** Scrub with soap and water, dry with a clean towel, and put on gloves if available. → *Expect:* hands are clean before touching the old dressing.
3. **Loosen the old dressing.** [BRANCH: releases easily | stuck] peel tape toward the wound; if stuck, moisten with clean water or saline and wait. → *Expect:* the dressing lifts without tearing new tissue.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not pull out packing, drains, or anything embedded in the wound unless a clinician told you to.
4. **Discard safely.** Fold the soiled side inward and place the old dressing in the trash bag. → *Expect:* drainage does not touch the clean supplies.
5. **Assess the wound.** Look for bleeding, opening edges, swelling, spreading redness, warmth, pus, odor, or increased drainage. → *Expect:* infection or reopening signs are noticed before redressing.
6. **Clean only as directed.** Rinse gently with clean water or saline if instructed, and wipe surrounding skin away from the wound. → *Expect:* the wound and nearby skin are clean without scrubbing the wound bed.
7. **Dry surrounding skin.** Pat the skin around the wound dry with clean gauze. → *Expect:* tape will stick to dry skin and the wound is not rubbed.
8. **Apply the new dressing.** Place a sterile nonstick pad or gauze over the wound, then secure it without tight pressure. → *Expect:* the wound is covered and circulation is not restricted.
9. **Set the change cadence.** Change daily, when wet or dirty, or on the schedule from the clinician. → *Expect:* the next dressing change time is known.
10. **Wash hands again.** Remove gloves if used, throw them away, and wash hands. → *Expect:* contamination does not spread after care.

## Decision points

- Bleeding soaks through or does not slow after 10 minutes of firm pressure → seek urgent medical care or emergency help.
- Drainage increases, becomes thick, green, yellow, bloody, or foul-smelling → call a doctor.
- Dressing repeatedly sticks → use a nonstick pad and ask a clinician whether ointment is appropriate.
- Skin under tape is irritated → switch tape type, use gauze wrap, or ask for a skin-safe dressing.
- Wound was surgical and instructions are unclear → call the surgical team before changing the plan.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dressing stuck:** detect pain or resistance during removal, recover by soaking and peeling slowly.
- **F2 Infection suspected:** detect spreading redness, warmth, pus, fever, or worsening pain, recover by calling a doctor promptly.
- **F3 Dressing too tight:** detect numbness, tingling, swelling, blue color, or cold skin, recover by loosening and rechecking circulation.
- **F4 Supplies contaminated:** detect a dropped pad or touched wound side, recover by discarding it and opening a clean one.

## Verification

The wound is covered with a clean dry dressing, circulation is normal beyond the dressing, and infection signs have been checked.

## Variations

- Heavy drainage: use more absorbent sterile gauze and change sooner, then ask a clinician if drainage is increasing.
- Fragile skin: use paper tape, silicone tape, or wrap instead of strong adhesive.
- Home health instructions: follow the provided dressing type, cleaning solution, and schedule exactly.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because infection and circulation problems can worsen quickly. Keep wound photos private, wash hands before and after, and escalate promptly for fever, spreading redness, uncontrolled bleeding, or severe pain.
