## Goal

Mild hypothermia is recognized and treated with gentle warming while severe symptoms trigger emergency services.

## Preconditions

- This is first aid guidance, not a substitute for medical care, wilderness training, or emergency responder instructions.
- Call emergency services for confusion, extreme drowsiness, loss of consciousness, weak or absent shivering, slow breathing, very cold skin, or suspected severe hypothermia.
- Move the person only as gently as safety allows because rough handling can worsen severe hypothermia.
- Do not rub the skin, put the person in a hot bath, or use direct high heat.
- Give warm sweet drinks only if the person is fully alert, able to swallow, and not vomiting.

## Steps

1. **Get out of cold exposure.** Move the person to shelter, a warm room, a vehicle, or a wind-protected area. → *Expect:* wind, wet, and cold exposure are reduced.
2. **Call for severe signs.** [BRANCH: alert and shivering | confused or very drowsy] call emergency services if mental status is abnormal, shivering stops, or breathing is concerning. → *Expect:* severe hypothermia is escalated immediately.
3. **Handle gently.** Keep movements slow and avoid unnecessary walking or jostling. → *Expect:* the person stays calm and stable.
4. **Remove wet clothing.** Cut away clothing if needed to avoid rough movement, and dry the skin gently. → *Expect:* evaporative cooling stops.
5. **Add dry insulation.** Wrap in blankets, coats, sleeping bags, or dry layers, including the head and neck. → *Expect:* body heat is retained.
6. **Warm the core first.** Place warm packs wrapped in cloth on the chest, armpits, or groin if available. → *Expect:* warming targets major blood vessels without burning skin.
7. **Share body heat if needed.** Use skin-to-skin core warming under dry blankets only when appropriate and privacy can be protected. → *Expect:* gentle warmth increases without direct heat injury.
8. **Offer warm drinks if safe.** Give small sips of warm sweet nonalcoholic fluid only if fully alert. → *Expect:* the person swallows safely and receives warmth and energy.
9. **Monitor breathing and alertness.** Recheck frequently and be ready for CPR if they stop breathing normally. → *Expect:* deterioration is noticed.
10. **Continue gradual rewarming.** Keep the person dry, insulated, and resting until fully recovered or care arrives. → *Expect:* shivering and alertness improve without overheating.

## Decision points

- Confusion, collapse, weak breathing, or no shivering despite cold → call emergency services.
- Person is wet and cold but alert and shivering → remove wet clothing, dry, insulate, and warm gradually.
- Person cannot swallow safely → do not give drinks.
- Skin looks frostbitten → protect from refreezing and seek medical care.
- Warmth causes pain, burns, or worsening symptoms → remove heat source and seek care.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rubbing skin:** detect vigorous massage or friction, recover by stopping and using passive insulation.
- **F2 Direct heat burn:** detect heating pad, fire, or hot water against skin, recover by wrapping heat sources and checking skin.
- **F3 Alcohol given:** detect alcohol offered for warmth, recover by stopping because it can worsen heat loss and judgment.
- **F4 Severe signs missed:** detect confusion or stopped shivering, recover by calling emergency services.

## Verification

The person is dry, insulated, handled gently, warmed gradually, given fluids only if alert, and emergency services are called for severe signs.

## Variations

- Wilderness setting: insulate from the ground and wind while arranging evacuation.
- Older adult: seek medical care earlier because symptoms may be subtle.
- Cold-water exposure: remove wet clothing quickly and prioritize dry insulation and emergency evaluation.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because hypothermia can impair judgment, breathing, and heart rhythm. Call emergency services for severe signs, get first aid or wilderness training, and use this recipe only as temporary care until professionals advise.
