## Goal

The folding chair is opened without fingers in pinch points, locked or fully spread, tested for stability, and occupied with weight centered on the seat.

## Preconditions

- Chair is intact, dry, and rated for the intended occupant.
- Floor or ground is level enough that all feet can contact it.
- Space around the chair is clear of people and obstacles.

## Steps

1. **Identify pinch zones.** Look at hinges, crossing legs, and the gap between seat and frame before moving anything. → *Expect:* fingers can avoid all closing joints.
2. **Grip safe frame points.** Hold the top of the backrest with one hand and the front seat edge or side frame with the other, away from hinges. → *Expect:* hands are on broad fixed surfaces.
3. **Open the chair.** Pull the backrest upward and the seat forward or downward in a controlled arc. → *Expect:* legs swing apart and the seat approaches horizontal.
4. **Lock or fully spread.** Continue opening until the brace clicks, the seat rests on stops, or the legs reach their designed spread. → *Expect:* frame no longer collapses inward under light pressure.
5. **Check finger clearance.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a closing hinge can pinch skin; keep fingers outside hinge gaps before pressing the seat. → *Expect:* no body part is inside a moving joint.
6. **Test stability.** Press down on the center of the seat with one hand while the other steadies the backrest. → *Expect:* chair does not fold, slide, or rock sharply.
7. **Position for sitting.** Stand in front of the seat, feel the chair behind the legs, and keep one hand on the back or seat if needed. → *Expect:* body is centered over the seat path.
8. **Sit centered.** Bend knees and lower weight onto the middle of the seat, then release hands after balance is stable. → *Expect:* chair supports weight evenly with all feet on the ground.

## Decision points

- Chair has a locking latch → engage it before testing the seat.
- Ground is soft or uneven → move to firmer level ground or use a different chair.
- Another person is close behind the chair → ask them to step back before unfolding.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Chair starts to fold:** seat rises or legs move inward under test pressure → stand clear, reopen fully, and verify the brace or latch.
- **F2 Rocking chair feet:** one foot does not touch ground → reposition on a flatter surface before sitting.
- **F3 Pinch contact:** skin or clothing catches at hinge → stop motion, reverse the hinge slightly, and free the material.
- **F4 Slide on floor:** chair moves during test → place it on a nonslip surface or choose a chair with rubber feet.

## Verification

The chair remains fully open under a firm seat press and supports a centered seated occupant without folding, sliding, or rocking.

## Variations

- Metal event chair: listen and feel for the rear brace seating against its stops.
- Fabric camp chair: unfold until the fabric seat is taut and all leg hubs are spread.
- Chair with armrests: use armrests only after the frame is locked, not as opening levers.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk. ⚠️ Pinch points at hinges and crossing legs can injure fingers, and an incompletely opened chair can collapse, so test stability before sitting.
