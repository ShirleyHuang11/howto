## Goal

Carry a hot drink from one surface to another without spilling, burning anyone, or dropping the cup.

## Preconditions

- The drink is in a cup designed for hot liquid.
- The path is visible and mostly clear.
- A stable destination surface is available before lifting.

## Steps

1. **Check fill level.** Leave headroom below the rim, especially for walking through people or over thresholds. → *Expect:* liquid sits below the rim and can slosh without immediately spilling.
2. **Secure the lid if used.** Press around the lid edge and align the sip opening away from the carry hand. → *Expect:* the lid does not lift when lightly nudged.
3. **Dry the outside.** Wipe drips from the cup, lid, and bottom. → *Expect:* the grip surface is dry enough not to slip.
4. **Choose the grip.** Hold the handle if present, or wrap fingers around the insulated sleeve or cup body below the rim. → *Expect:* the cup feels balanced without squeezing hard.
5. **Use a saucer or second hand if needed.** For a fragile mug or brimful drink, support from below with a saucer or second hand. → *Expect:* the cup is steadier before it leaves the table.
6. **Lift vertically first.** Raise the cup a few centimeters and pause. → *Expect:* no drip, tilt, or lid shift appears.
7. **Scan the path.** Look for rugs, cords, door thresholds, pets, children, and people turning unexpectedly. → *Expect:* you know where to slow or reroute.
8. **Walk smoothly.** Keep the cup near torso height, elbow slightly bent, and eyes alternating between path and drink surface. → *Expect:* the liquid surface moves gently rather than sloshing up the sides.
9. **Slow near people.** Give space, announce "hot drink" if someone may bump you, and pause for sudden crossings. → *Expect:* nearby humans have time to adjust.
10. **Set down before release.** Place the cup fully on the destination surface, confirm it is stable, then let go. → *Expect:* the base is flat on the surface before fingers open.

## Decision points

- Cup is filled to the brim → sip or pour off some liquid before carrying.
- Lid feels loose → replace it, carry uncovered only a short distance, or use a tray.
- Path is crowded → wait, reroute, or ask someone to clear space.
- Destination is cluttered → clear a landing spot before lifting the drink.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Slosh rising:** detect liquid climbing toward the rim, recover by stopping and letting the surface settle.
- **F2 Grip too hot:** detect pain or reflex loosening, recover by setting down immediately and adding a sleeve, napkin, or saucer.
- **F3 Lid leak:** detect drops at the seam or sip hole, recover by stopping, wiping, and reseating or removing the lid.
- **F4 Bump risk:** detect a person entering the path, recover by slowing, pausing, and turning the cup away from them.

## Verification

The hot drink reaches the destination surface upright, with no spill, no burn, and fingers released only after stable set-down.

## Variations

- Tray carry: use two hands, keep the tray level, and place the cup away from tray edges.
- Mug without lid: walk slower and keep extra headroom.
- Stairs: use a lid or avoid carrying hot liquid on stairs when possible.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from scalds and broken cups. Hot surfaces are present; slow near people, keep the path clear, and treat children or crowding as a reason to pause.
