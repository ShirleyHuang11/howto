## Goal

The child is seated in the car seat with harness straps over the shoulders, buckle and chest clip fastened, chest clip at armpit level, and harness snug enough to pass the pinch test.

## Preconditions

- Car seat is installed according to its manual and appropriate for the child's size and direction of travel.
- Child is calm enough to position safely, and an adult remains within arm's reach.
- Bulky coats, thick blankets, and compressible padding are removed from under the harness.

## Steps

1. **Prepare the harness.** Loosen straps, open the buckle and chest clip, and move straps to the sides without twisting. → *Expect:* seating area is clear and both shoulder straps are visible.
2. **Seat the child fully back.** Place the child with hips and back against the car-seat shell, centered between side bolsters. → *Expect:* child's bottom is deep in the seat and spine follows the seat back.
3. **Route straps over shoulders.** Bring each harness strap over the matching shoulder and down the torso without crossing. → *Expect:* straps lie flat from shoulder slot to buckle tongue.
4. **Check shoulder slot height.** [BRANCH: rear-facing | forward-facing] for rear-facing, straps come from at or below shoulders; for forward-facing, at or above shoulders. → *Expect:* harness exits at the correct height for seat direction.
5. **Buckle the crotch buckle.** Insert each buckle tongue into the buckle until each side clicks, then tug both tongues upward. → *Expect:* both tongues stay locked.
6. **Fasten the chest clip.** Bring clip halves together at the child's chest and press until they click. → *Expect:* clip is closed and centered on the sternum.
7. **Tighten the harness.** Pull the adjuster strap steadily while feeding slack from hip and belly areas upward toward the shoulders. → *Expect:* harness lies flat and slack disappears from the torso.
8. **Set clip height and pinch test.** Slide the chest clip to armpit level, then pinch the webbing vertically at the collarbone. → *Expect:* no webbing fold can be pinched between fingers.

## Decision points

- Child is wearing a bulky coat → remove it before buckling and place a blanket over the secured harness if warmth is needed.
- Harness is twisted → unbuckle or loosen enough to flatten the strap before tightening.
- Strap height is wrong for direction of travel → adjust the harness slots or headrest according to the car-seat manual before driving.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Loose harness:** webbing can be pinched at the collarbone → pull slack from hips upward and tighten again with the adjuster strap.
- **F2 Chest clip too low:** clip rests on belly or ribs → slide it up to armpit level after tightening.
- **F3 Buckle not latched:** one tongue releases during tug test → reinsert until it clicks and test again.
- **F4 Bulky clothing compression:** harness seems snug over a coat → remove the coat, rebuckle, and tighten against normal clothing.

## Verification

The child is centered, buckle and chest clip are latched, chest clip is at armpit level, harness straps are flat, and the collarbone pinch test cannot lift a fold of webbing.

## Variations

- Infant rear-facing seat: keep the child's head and airway positioned according to the manual and use only approved inserts.
- Convertible seat: confirm the harness slot rule changes when switching from rear-facing to forward-facing.
- Cold weather: buckle over thin layers, then place a coat or blanket over the harness.

## Safety & privacy

High risk. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* an incorrectly tightened or misrouted harness can fail to protect the child in a crash; pause around the child, remove bulky coats, use the car-seat manual, and confirm every latch before the vehicle moves.
