## Goal

Help a person move from sitting to standing only if they can participate and bear weight, then confirm they are steady before walking.

## Preconditions

- The person is awake, responsive, and agrees to stand.
- They can move legs or arms enough to help.
- Shoes or non-slip socks are on.
- Walker, cane, gait belt, or chair arms are available if normally used.

## Steps

1. **Ask how they feel.** Check pain, dizziness, weakness, and whether they can put weight on their legs. → *Expect:* the person answers clearly or signals stop.
2. **Decide if help is safe.** Do not lift dead weight or pull someone who cannot bear weight. → *Expect:* either they can assist, or the task pauses for more help.
3. **Set the environment.** Lock wheelchair brakes, move rugs, and place walker or cane in front if used. → *Expect:* feet and assistive device have clear space.
4. **Position their feet.** Cue feet flat, hip-width, and slightly behind the knees. → *Expect:* shins angle forward enough for standing.
5. **Move them to the seat edge.** Ask them to scoot forward using chair arms or the seat. → *Expect:* hips are near the front edge, not deep in the chair.
6. **Apply a gait belt if trained.** Place it snugly over clothing at the waist, avoiding tubes or tender areas. → *Expect:* two fingers fit under the belt and it does not ride up.
7. **Set your stance.** Stand close, feet staggered, knees bent, back neutral. → *Expect:* your body can guide without twisting.
8. **Cue the motion.** Count "one, two, three, stand" and ask them to push from the chair, not pull your neck. → *Expect:* they lean forward and begin pushing with legs and arms.
9. **Brace and guide.** Hold the gait belt or support at the trunk while they stand. → *Expect:* you guide balance but their legs supply the lift.
10. **Pause upright.** Keep support until they report no dizziness and knees are not buckling. → *Expect:* posture is steady for several breaths.
11. **Check gait readiness.** Ask them to shift weight, march once, or place hands on walker. → *Expect:* they can bear weight before taking a step.
12. **Stop if unsafe.** [BRANCH: steady | not weight-bearing] continue only if steady; if not, sit them back down and call for help. → *Expect:* no forced walking occurs.

## Decision points

- If they cannot bear weight → do not lift; call another caregiver, medical staff, or emergency help depending on context.
- If they fell before sitting there → assess injury first and call for help rather than standing them immediately.
- If they feel dizzy on standing → sit them back down and wait, then reassess.
- If they use a prescribed transfer method → follow that plan instead of improvising.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dead weight:** detect no leg push or collapsing knees → lower them back to the chair or floor safely and call for help.
- **F2 Pulling on helper:** detect hands grabbing neck or shoulders → stop, reset hands to chair arms or walker, and cue again.
- **F3 Dizziness:** detect pale face, swaying, or report of lightheadedness → keep close support and sit them down.
- **F4 Device misplaced:** detect walker too far away or brakes unlocked → sit or pause, reposition equipment, and restart.

## Verification

The person stands with their own weight through both legs, remains steady for several breaths, and either walks with the proper aid or sits back down safely.

## Variations

- From bed: raise the bed if possible and have the person sit at the edge before standing.
- With walker: the person pushes from the chair first, then moves hands to the walker after standing.
- Two-person assist: one trained helper cues and controls the trunk while the second guards the weaker side.

## Safety & privacy

High risk. Do not lift dead weight, yank arms, or continue if the person cannot bear weight. Protect dignity by explaining each contact before touching, and call for help when ability is uncertain.
