## Goal

A clean sheet of plastic wrap is torn from the roll, stretched across the target without tangling, and the box edge is left ready for the next pull.

## Preconditions

- Box and cutter edge are intact and resting on a stable counter.
- Target dish or item is close enough that the wrap does not need to travel far.
- Hands are dry; wet fingers make the film fold onto itself.

## Steps

1. **Find the film edge.** Rotate the roll until the loose edge or matte line is visible, then pinch both corners lightly. → *Expect:* a full-width leading edge lifts from the roll.
2. **Pin the box and roll.** Place one forearm or hand on the box lid while fingertips restrain the roll ends. → *Expect:* the roll resists spinning freely.
3. **Pull a measured length.** Draw the film straight out at counter height with even tension across both corners. → *Expect:* sheet advances without diagonal wrinkles.
4. **Set the length against the cutter.** Lower the film onto the serrated edge with the desired tear line just beyond the teeth. → *Expect:* film is taut across the cutter from left to right.
5. **Tear across the edge.** Pull down and slightly sideways from one corner, letting the teeth start the cut, then continue across the width. → *Expect:* sheet separates in a mostly straight line.
6. **Prevent cling tangle.** Keep the free sheet stretched between both hands and avoid letting the middle touch itself. → *Expect:* sheet stays open and transparent, not roped or folded.
7. **Cover the target.** Lay the near edge on the rim first, then smooth outward with flat fingertips and light tension. → *Expect:* wrap seals to the rim or surface without large trapped folds.
8. **Leave a starter tab.** Press the remaining roll edge lightly to the box lip or fold a small corner back on itself. → *Expect:* next-use edge is visible and reachable.

## Decision points

- Cutter is inside the lid rather than on the front edge → close the lid to trap the film before tearing.
- Wrap clings to itself before reaching the dish → discard the tangled sheet if it cannot be unfolded without holes.
- Target is hot → wait until steam and heat drop, because plastic wrap can deform or melt.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Film tears diagonally:** one corner carries more tension → stop pulling, reset the full width on the cutter, and start from the shorter side.
- **F2 Edge lost on roll:** film lies flush and invisible → rub thumb around the roll circumference until the edge lifts, then mark it with a folded tab.
- **F3 Sheet balls up:** center touches itself → hold two outside corners, gently shake apart, or cut a fresh sheet.
- **F4 Cutter hazard:** fingertip contacts serrations → release tension, move fingers above the film, and resume with the box stabilized.

## Verification

The target is covered by one intact sheet and the remaining roll has a visible starter edge at the box lip.

## Variations

- Slide-cutter box: pull the length, close the lid, then move the slider fully across before lifting the sheet.
- Commercial wide roll: use two hands spaced far apart and tear against the mounted bar.
- Freezer wrap: press longer at the rim because cold surfaces reduce cling.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The serrated cutter can nick skin, so keep fingertips above the teeth, and do not stretch plastic wrap over hot cookware or open flame.
