## Goal

A spoonful of food or liquid is gathered, transported level, and released at the intended target.

## Preconditions

- A spoon of appropriate size is clean and available.
- The source container is stable and open.
- The destination is within easy reach or already positioned nearby.

## Steps

1. **Grip the spoon.** Hold the handle near its middle with the bowl of the spoon facing up. → *Expect:* the spoon stays level when lifted empty.
2. **Stabilize the source.** Hold the source container or confirm it will not slide. → *Expect:* the container resists a light spoon push.
3. **Set angle of attack.** Lower the spoon bowl into the food at a shallow forward angle, about 20 to 45 degrees. → *Expect:* the front edge enters without pushing the food away.
4. **Draw through the food.** Pull or push the spoon through the material until the bowl fills. → *Expect:* food collects inside the spoon bowl.
5. **Level the fill.** Lift slightly and pause over the source so excess falls back. → *Expect:* the spoon holds a rounded but stable portion.
6. **Transport level.** Move the spoon slowly to the destination, keeping the bowl horizontal and below chest height. → *Expect:* contents remain in the spoon.
7. **Release.** Tip the spoon over the destination or touch it to the target surface and roll the wrist. → *Expect:* the scoop leaves the spoon and lands in the intended place.
8. **Reset.** Return the spoon to level or set it on a clean rest. → *Expect:* no trailing drip crosses the work surface.

## Decision points

- Liquid contents → fill below the rim and move slower.
- Sticky contents → scrape the spoon against the destination edge to release.
- Granular contents → use a deeper entry angle and avoid shaking during transport.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Underfilled scoop:** spoon exits mostly empty → enter deeper or increase the draw distance.
- **F2 Overflowing scoop:** food mounds beyond stable height → pause above the source and let excess fall back.
- **F3 Spill during carry:** contents drop before destination → shorten the path, reduce fill, and level the wrist.
- **F4 Source slides:** container moves under spoon force → stabilize it with the free hand or a towel.

## Verification

One controlled spoonful has been transferred to the target, with no significant food left on the path between source and destination.

## Variations

- `soup`: skim below the surface and keep the spoon half full for transport.
- `rice`: compress lightly against the side if a shaped scoop is needed.
- `ice-cream`: use a stronger spoon or scoop tool and expect higher entry force.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. For hot food, keep portions smaller and transport below face level so a spill falls into the container or counter area rather than onto skin.
