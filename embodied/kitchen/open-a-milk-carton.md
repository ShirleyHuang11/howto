## Goal

The gable-top carton is opened into a clean pouring spout, a small pour test runs without side leaks, and the carton remains upright and resealable.

## Preconditions

- Carton is sealed, upright, and cold enough to handle without slipping.
- A stable counter and cup or sink are available for the pour test.
- Hands are dry so the waxed paperboard can be pinched accurately.

## Steps

1. **Orient the carton.** Place the carton upright with the printed opening side facing you and the top ridge centered under both thumbs. → *Expect:* the marked spout panel is visible and the base does not rock.
2. **Brace the body.** Wrap one hand around the upper carton body using light inward pressure that does not dent the side walls. → *Expect:* carton stays vertical while the top can move.
3. **Push the folded wings back.** Press both triangular side wings away from the spout panel until the gable opens into a shallow V. → *Expect:* the top ridge separates slightly and both wings stay folded back.
4. **Pull the spout panel outward.** Pinch the front lip between thumb and index finger, then pull outward and slightly upward with steady force. → *Expect:* the sealed paper seam begins to peel without tearing down the carton wall.
5. **Pinch the spout open.** Place thumbs behind the two spout cheeks and index fingers outside them, then squeeze the cheeks apart into a diamond opening. → *Expect:* an oval or diamond pour mouth stays open by itself.
6. **Clear stuck inner layers.** [BRANCH: inner seam open → continue | inner seam stuck → insert a clean fingertip at the lip and tease the layers apart] → *Expect:* light is visible through the spout and no paper flap blocks the path.
7. **Perform a pour test.** Tilt the carton a few degrees over a cup or sink, then return upright before flow becomes heavy. → *Expect:* a narrow stream exits only from the spout.
8. **Reseat for storage.** Press the spout cheeks together and fold the wings back toward the ridge if storing. → *Expect:* top is mostly closed and carton can stand in the refrigerator without dripping.

## Decision points

- Carton has a plastic screw cap → unscrew the cap instead and skip the gable-top opening sequence.
- Spout tears below the fold → stop using that edge for pouring and transfer milk to a clean lidded container.
- Pouring for a child or crowded table → perform the pour test at the sink before moving the carton.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wings will not stay back:** folded side panels spring forward → crease each wing backward with thumb pressure along the printed fold, then retry the pull.
- **F2 Spout opens asymmetrically:** one cheek remains glued → pinch close, pull the stuck cheek outward alone, then reopen both cheeks evenly.
- **F3 Side leak during test:** liquid beads from a torn seam → keep carton upright, wipe it, and pour from the opposite corner or decant.
- **F4 Carton crushed:** grip pressure caves the body → relax the brace, reshape the side walls, and support from the base for the next pour.

## Verification

The carton has an open, stable spout and a brief tilt produces a controlled stream from the spout with no liquid escaping from other seams.

## Variations

- Small school carton: use fingertip pinches instead of whole-hand pressure and pour immediately after opening.
- Large half-gallon carton: brace near the base with the second hand during the pour test.
- Waxed paperboard that is very cold: warm the top briefly between hands to reduce cracking before pulling the spout.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the carton upright until the spout is confirmed open, wipe milk spills promptly because they make counters slippery, and discard any carton with torn paper fibers falling into the liquid.
