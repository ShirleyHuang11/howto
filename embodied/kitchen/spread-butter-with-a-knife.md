## Goal

Butter is spread in an even layer across bread or toast, including edges, without tearing the surface.

## Preconditions

- Bread or toast is on a stable plate or cutting board.
- Butter is soft enough to dent under light knife pressure.
- A butter knife or dull table knife is clean and available.

## Steps

1. **Stabilize the bread.** Place the bread flat and hold one corner lightly if it slides. → *Expect:* the bread does not move under light knife contact.
2. **Load the knife.** Scrape a small curl of soft butter onto one side of the knife blade. → *Expect:* butter adheres near the middle third of the blade.
3. **Deposit butter.** Touch the loaded blade to the bread center and press gently. → *Expect:* butter transfers from knife to bread without tearing.
4. **Spread outward.** Use short, flat strokes from center toward the edges with low pressure. → *Expect:* a thin layer extends across the surface.
5. **Reload as needed.** Add small amounts rather than one large lump. → *Expect:* butter coverage increases without thick ridges.
6. **Cover the edges.** Angle the knife slightly and sweep along each edge while staying on the bread surface. → *Expect:* corners and edges have visible butter.
7. **Smooth high spots.** Drag the knife lightly across ridges to even the layer. → *Expect:* thickness appears consistent across the bread.
8. **Set knife down safely.** Place the knife on the plate edge or a clean rest with the blade not protruding. → *Expect:* knife is stable and buttered side faces up or inward.

## Decision points

- Butter is too hard → wait briefly, shave thinner pieces, or soften according to the parent recipe.
- Bread tears → reduce pressure, use smaller butter pieces, and spread with shorter strokes.
- Toast is hot → butter will melt faster, so use lighter finishing strokes.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Butter clumps:** thick pieces remain unmoved → hold the knife flatter and spread from the clump outward.
- **F2 Bread slides:** slice moves across the plate → stabilize an edge with fingertips away from the blade path.
- **F3 Surface tears:** crumb pulls up under the knife → stop pressing and switch to dabbing motions.
- **F4 Uneven edge coverage:** dry border remains → reload with a small amount and sweep along the perimeter.

## Verification

The bread has a continuous butter layer from center to edges, with no major torn area and the knife resting safely.

## Variations

- `toast`: spread while warm for melting, then smooth once.
- `bagel`: work each cut half separately and follow the curved rim.
- `compound-butter`: distribute inclusions evenly with shorter strokes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk with a dull spreading knife. Keep fingers out of the stroke path, use a butter knife when possible, and avoid force that can skid off the bread.
