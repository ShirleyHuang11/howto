## Goal

The childproof cap is opened using its intended simultaneous motion, contents are accessed without spilling, and the cap is resealed so children cannot open it easily.

## Preconditions

- Bottle label and cap instructions are readable.
- Bottle is on a stable surface away from children, food prep, and open drinks.
- Hands are dry enough to apply downward or side pressure while turning.

## Steps

1. **Read the cap cue.** Find text, arrows, squeeze pads, or alignment marks on the cap and neck. → *Expect:* the required action is identified before force is applied.
2. **Brace the bottle.** Hold the bottle body upright in the non-turning hand, fingers around the label area and base on the counter. → *Expect:* bottle does not tip or flex.
3. **Choose the mechanism.** [BRANCH: push-down-and-turn | squeeze-and-turn | align-arrows-then-turn] follow the visible cue on the cap. → *Expect:* grip points match the cap markings.
4. **Apply simultaneous pressure.** For push-down caps, press the palm straight down while rotating counterclockwise; for squeeze caps, pinch both side pads inward while rotating. → *Expect:* cap clicks past the safety catches and begins to loosen.
5. **Reduce force after release.** Once threads move freely, continue turning with lighter pressure so the bottle stays upright. → *Expect:* cap rises on the threads without jerking.
6. **Lift the cap cleanly.** Remove the cap straight up and place it inner side up on a clean surface. → *Expect:* no tablets, liquid, or powder spill onto the counter.
7. **Use or inspect the contents.** Keep the opening above the counter and close the bottle immediately after removing the needed item. → *Expect:* contents remain inside except the intended dose or item.
8. **Reseal and test.** Set the cap on square, turn clockwise until tight, then try a light counterclockwise twist without pressing or squeezing. → *Expect:* cap resists casual turning and stays seated.

## Decision points

- Cap arrows must align before opening → rotate until the arrows meet, then push or squeeze as directed.
- Cap will not move with moderate force → stop and ask an adult with stronger grip or use a rubber grip pad.
- Children are nearby → pause the task, move the bottle out of reach and line of sight, then continue.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cap spins without loosening:** safety lugs are not depressed → increase straight downward pressure or squeeze both pads evenly while turning.
- **F2 Bottle slips:** grip is oily or wet → dry hands and bottle, place it on a nonslip mat, and retry.
- **F3 Cross-thread on reseal:** cap tilts or clicks unevenly → back it off, set it level, and turn clockwise again.
- **F4 Missing safety lock:** cap opens without the required action → move contents to a properly locking container and keep away from children.

## Verification

After closing, the cap cannot be removed by a plain counterclockwise twist without the required push, squeeze, or alignment action.

## Variations

- Reversible pharmacy cap: choose the child-resistant side, not the easy-open side, when children may access the area.
- Liquid medicine: keep the bottle vertical and wipe threads before resealing to prevent sticky false closure.
- Large vitamin bottle: use the heel of the palm for downward pressure while fingers stabilize the shoulder.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a child ingesting medicine can cause serious harm; keep the bottle closed, out of reach, and out of sight whenever it is not actively in an adult hand.
