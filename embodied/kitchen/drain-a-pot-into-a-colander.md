## Goal

Hot water is drained from a pot through a colander while food is retained and steam exposure is controlled.

## Preconditions

- A colander is clean and fits fully inside the sink without rocking.
- The pot handles can be gripped with potholders or dry towels.
- The sink drain is clear enough for hot water to flow away.

## Steps

1. **Seat the colander.** Place the colander in the sink with feet down and handles clear of the pouring path. → *Expect:* the colander is stable and centered over the drain.
2. **Clear the approach.** Remove dishes, soap bottles, and loose tools between stove and sink. → *Expect:* the carry path is open and dry.
3. **Grip the hot pot.** Use dry potholders on both handles and keep wrists straight. → *Expect:* hands are insulated and the pot lifts level.
4. **Move to the sink.** Carry the pot close to the body, then position its far rim over the colander. → *Expect:* the pot is above the colander with the pour direction away from the face.
5. **Tilt away from face.** Rotate the pot slowly so water exits the far side first. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* steam burns can occur instantly, so keep face and forearms out of the plume. → *Expect:* hot water flows into the colander and steam rises away.
6. **Control the last pour.** Lower the tilt angle as food reaches the rim, using the pot lip to hold solids back if needed. → *Expect:* water drains while food lands in or stays above the colander.
7. **Shake to drain.** Set the pot down if empty, then lift the colander handles and shake gently up and down over the sink. → *Expect:* water droplets fall through and food remains contained.
8. **Set safely.** Place the drained food or colander on a heat-safe surface. → *Expect:* the sink holds the drained water and all hot items are stable.

## Decision points

- Pot is too heavy to control → ladle water out first or ask for a second grip before pouring.
- Colander shifts → stop tilting, set the pot down, reseat the colander, and restart.
- Steam plume moves toward face → pause and change body position before continuing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Food escapes:** solids spill beside the colander → stop pouring, recover safe pieces with tongs, and clean the sink area.
- **F2 Burn sensation:** heat reaches hand or arm → set the pot down immediately and replace wet insulation.
- **F3 Drain backs up:** water pools in the sink → stop pouring until water drains, then continue in smaller pours.
- **F4 Colander tip:** colander rocks under water flow → reduce pour rate and stabilize the handle outside the hot stream.

## Verification

Most water has passed through the colander, food remains contained, and the hot pot and colander are stable with no active steam exposure to the face.

## Variations

- `pasta`: reserve cooking water before draining if the parent recipe requires it.
- `vegetables`: use a gentler shake to avoid breaking soft pieces.
- `small-pot`: pour one-handed only if the pot remains level under wrist control.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from boiling water, steam, and hot metal. Pour away from the face, keep potholders dry, and slow or pause when anyone enters the sink-side workspace.
