## Goal

A wet cloth is wrung over the sink until damp rather than dripping, using opposing twists and a stable grip.

## Preconditions

- Cloth, towel, or rag is wet and safe to squeeze by hand.
- Sink or basin is clear enough to catch water.
- Hands can grip both ends without pain.

## Steps

1. **Move over the sink.** Hold the cloth fully above the basin before squeezing. → *Expect:* dripping water falls into the sink, not onto the floor.
2. **Gather the cloth lengthwise.** Pull opposite corners or ends apart so the cloth forms a loose rope. → *Expect:* water collects along the twisted length.
3. **Set hand positions.** Grip one end in each hand with thumbs wrapped around the cloth. → *Expect:* the cloth does not slip when lifted.
4. **Twist opposite directions.** Rotate the right hand forward and the left hand backward, or the reverse. → *Expect:* the cloth tightens and water streams out.
5. **Squeeze while twisting.** Maintain pressure for a few seconds at peak twist. → *Expect:* the water flow slows from stream to drops.
6. **Relax and shift grip.** Release tension slightly, move hands closer to the wetter section, and twist again. → *Expect:* remaining water is driven from the middle.
7. **Check drip level.** Hold the cloth above the sink without squeezing. → *Expect:* it drips slowly or not at all.
8. **Repeat if needed.** For wiping, leave it damp; for storage, wring until only occasional drops fall. → *Expect:* the cloth matches the intended wetness.
9. **Smooth or hang.** Shake the cloth open and place it where it can dry or be used. → *Expect:* it is not balled up and dripping.

## Decision points

- Cloth is hot → let it cool or rinse with cool water before wringing.
- Cloth contains cleaner → wear gloves if the cleaner irritates skin.
- Fabric is delicate → squeeze flat instead of hard twisting to avoid stretching.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Slipping grip:** detect cloth sliding through fingers; gather a thicker rope and grip closer to the ends.
- **F2 Water on floor:** detect drips outside the basin; move hands deeper over the sink before continuing.
- **F3 Over-twisted fabric:** detect stretched seams or distorted weave; stop twisting and press water out flat.
- **F4 Wrist strain:** detect pain during rotation; use two-handed pressing against the sink wall instead.

## Verification

The cloth is damp to the intended level, does not leave a puddle when held still, and is placed for use or drying.

## Variations

- Large towel: fold lengthwise first and wring in sections.
- Sponge cloth: press between palms instead of twisting.
- Mop cloth: use the wringer bucket if available to reduce wrist load.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid wringing hot cloths, chemical-soaked cloths, or contaminated cleanup rags with bare hands.
