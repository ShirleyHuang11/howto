## Goal

Cooked food is portioned evenly from its pot or pan onto individual plates, with clean rims, correct portions per eater, and every plate delivered to the table while still hot.

## Preconditions

- Cooking finished; burners off or at keep-warm low.
- A count of eaters and any special-portion notes (small eater, seconds expected, someone arriving late).
- Plates staged in a row next to the stove, a serving utensil per dish (spoon, ladle, or tongs matched to the food), and a trivet on the counter or table.

## Steps

1. **Warm the plates if the food is saucy or quick-cooling.** Hot tap water rinse and a towel-dry, or 30 seconds in the microwave in a stack. Cold plates kill hot food in two minutes. → *Expect:* plates warm to the touch, dry.
2. **Do the portion math before the first scoop.** Eyeball the pot as N equal shares, and hold back roughly 10 percent as a reserve. The classic error is generous early plates and a scraped-thin last one. [BRANCH: seconds culture (family style) → plate slightly under, leave the rest in the pot | plated-and-done → divide fully, still reserve-last] → *Expect:* a stated plan ("about two ladles each") before serving starts.
3. **Bring plates to the pan, not the pan to the plates.** Serve at the stove with the pan on its burner or on a trivet beside it. Carrying a hot full pan across the kitchen to a table of plates is the highest-risk move in home cooking. → *Expect:* the hot vessel moves zero or one short step, ever.
4. **Handle the hot vessel with a dry barrier only.** Dry pot holder or folded dry towel on the handle; a damp towel conducts steam through instantly. Angle handles inward, and steady the pan by the handle while scooping, never with a bare hand on the rim. → *Expect:* firm grip, no bare skin near metal, pan stable while the utensil works.
5. **Plate in rounds, not one plate at a time.** First component to every plate, then second component to every plate. Rounds average out portion drift and every plate finishes at the same temperature. → *Expect:* all plates visually level with each other after each round.
6. **Place food, don't drop it.** Bring the spoon low over the plate before releasing; sauce ladled from a height splashes the rim and the counter. Wet foods first in the plate's center, drier sides beside, sauce over the item it belongs to. → *Expect:* food inside the plate's inner well; rim clean or nearly so.
7. **Wipe the rims.** Fold a clean damp towel over one finger and run it around each rim. Thirty seconds total, and it is the difference between "dinner" and "mess". → *Expect:* every rim bare and clean; towel back on its hook, not on the counter in a puddle.
8. **Deliver plates two at a time maximum, announced.** One plate per hand, thumbs on the rim edge only, no forearm-stacking with hot plates. Say "hot plate" as each lands, especially to children. → *Expect:* each plate set down flat in front of an eater without a tilt; recipients warned.
9. **Park the pan safely.** Vessel back on the off burner or trivet, serving utensil resting in the pan (not flat on the counter), reserve portion covered. → *Expect:* no hot vessel at a counter edge; utensil contained; leftovers ready for seconds or storage.

## Decision points

- Food is loose and wet (soup, stew) → ladle, filled below its brim, and plates become bowls; count "one ladle each, then top-up rounds".
- A latecomer is expected → plate their full portion first, cover it, park it away from the counter edge; scraping together a portion later never matches.
- Pan too heavy to hold one-handed while serving → set it on a trivet at counter center and use two hands: one steadies, one scoops.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ran out before the last plate:** early plates over-portioned → rebalance one spoonful from each generous plate before anyone sits; even-but-smaller beats one empty plate.
- **F2 Sauce splash across a rim or counter:** wipe the rim now (step 7 towel), counter after delivery; dried sauce doubles the cleanup.
- **F3 Grip failing on a hot handle mid-serve:** set the vessel down on the nearest burner or trivet immediately, re-fold a thicker dry barrier; never push through a slipping hot grip.
- **F4 Plate hotter than expected at delivery:** you flinch on pickup → carry with a folded towel under the thumb-side and warn the eater explicitly; do not slide a too-hot plate across the table at speed.

## Verification

Every eater has a plate with a visually equal portion, all rims are clean, the hot vessel rests on a burner or trivet away from edges with its utensil inside, the reserve or leftovers are covered, and no one (server included) touched bare hot metal.

## Variations

- Family style: skip rounds and rim-wiping; instead deliver the vessel itself on a trivet with a serving utensil, same dry-barrier and short-carry rules.
- Kids' plates: half portions, cooled a minute before delivery, set down away from the table edge.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot vessels and hot plates. The load-bearing rules: plates travel to the pan (never a full hot pan across the room), only dry barriers on handles, and announced deliveries. Portioning errors are social, not dangerous; fix them before people sit and no one remembers.
