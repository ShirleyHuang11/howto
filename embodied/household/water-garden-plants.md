## Goal

Every plant that actually needs water has been watered deeply at the root zone, pots drain freely, and the can and faucet are shut down and stowed.

## Preconditions

- A watering can (ideally with a removable rose head) and a working outdoor faucet or rain barrel.
- Morning hours if at all possible; before roughly 10:00 the water soaks in instead of evaporating, and leaves that get splashed dry off during the day instead of sitting wet overnight, which invites fungus.
- A rough route plan: which beds and pots are on today's round.

## Steps

1. **Walk the route before filling anything.** Check each bed and pot for wilt, and note which containers dried out. Not everything needs water every day. → *Expect:* a short mental list of targets; obviously moist beds struck off.
2. **Do the soil check on each candidate.** Push a finger 3 to 5 cm into the soil beside the stem (not against it). [BRANCH: dry at depth → water it | still moist at depth → skip it, surface dryness lies] → *Expect:* a clear wet/dry verdict per plant; skipped plants noted for tomorrow.
3. **Fill the can at the faucet.** Fill to a weight you can carry with a straight back; two lighter trips beat one staggering one. Close the faucet fully after each fill. → *Expect:* can filled, faucet not dripping, path back to the beds unobstructed.
4. **Water the root zone, not the leaves.** Remove or bypass the rose, lower the spout close to the soil at the base of each plant, and pour slowly around the stem's drip circle. Wet foliage is the main avoidable cause of leaf fungus. → *Expect:* water pooling briefly then soaking in around the base; leaves staying dry.
5. **Water deeply, not often.** Give each plant a slow, generous dose so moisture reaches full root depth; a daily light sprinkle trains roots to stay shallow. If water runs off, pause, let it soak, and continue in a second pass. → *Expect:* soil dark and moist at finger depth on a re-probe, not just a wet surface film.
6. **Handle pots to the drain point.** Water potted plants until water just emerges from the drainage holes, then stop. → *Expect:* first trickle at the holes; saucer catching it. Water races straight through instantly → F2.
7. **Use the rose for seedlings and delicate plants.** Attach the rose head and water with a gentle sweeping pass; a hard pour from the spout flattens seedlings and blasts soil off roots. → *Expect:* seedlings upright after watering, soil surface intact.
8. **Refill and repeat along the route.** Track roughly how many cans each zone took; that number becomes your calibration for the season. → *Expect:* all targets from step 1 watered; can count noted.
9. **Shut down.** Empty the last part-can onto any plant rather than storing it (stored water breeds mosquitoes), close the faucet firmly, and store the can inverted. → *Expect:* faucet off and dry, can empty and upside down, path clear.

## Decision points

- Heat wave or vacation coming → water deeply the evening before, mulch exposed soil to slow evaporation, and group pots into shade.
- Only evening slots available → still water, but strictly at the roots; wet leaves at nightfall are the worst case for fungus.
- Rain fell recently → probe anyway; canopy and mulch can leave beds under them bone dry after a light rain.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Plant wilts even though soil is moist:** overwatering or root damage, not thirst → stop watering it, check drainage, and let the soil dry to depth before the next dose; more water makes this worse.
- **F2 Water runs straight through a pot without wetting the soil:** peat-based potting mix has dried and shrunk from the pot walls (hydrophobic) → set the pot in a basin of water for 20 to 30 minutes to rewet from below, then resume normal watering.
- **F3 Runoff and crusting on a bed:** pouring faster than the soil absorbs → break the dose into passes, rough up the crust gently with fingers or a hand fork, and add mulch.
- **F4 Faucet left dripping after the round:** heard or seen on the walk back → close it fully; a slow drip over a day exceeds what the whole garden drank.

## Verification

Re-probe two or three watered plants: soil is moist at 3 to 5 cm depth, pots have drained into their saucers without standing water above the soil, foliage is dry, the faucet is off with no drip, and the can is empty and stowed inverted.

## Variations

- Hose with a wand: same root-zone rules; the wand's shower setting replaces the rose, and the shut-off is at both the wand and the faucet.
- Drip irrigation present: the can round covers only pots and gaps the drip lines miss; probe near emitters occasionally to confirm the system actually ran.
- Hot climates: morning watering becomes mandatory rather than preferred, and unmulched beds may need probing twice daily.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch the two mundane hazards: a full can is heavy (lift with the legs, keep the spout side away from your shins) and wet paving is slippery on the return trip. Empty standing water after the round; it is a mosquito breeding site within days.
