## Goal

Thread passes through the needle eye with a usable tail, and the far end is knotted for sewing.

## Preconditions

- Needle eye is visible under good light.
- Thread end is cleanly cut, not frayed into many fibers.
- Hands can rest on a stable surface to reduce shake.

## Steps

1. **Trim the thread end.** Cut a fresh angled end if the tip is fuzzy. → *Expect:* thread narrows to one compact point.
2. **Wet and flatten the tip.** Lightly moisten the last centimeter, then pinch it flat between thumb and index finger. → *Expect:* fibers cling into a stiff blade.
3. **Steady both hands.** Rest wrists or little fingers on the table with needle in one hand and thread in the other. → *Expect:* the eye and tip move slowly relative to each other.
4. **Orient the eye.** Rotate the needle until the eye opening faces the thread tip squarely. → *Expect:* a bright slot or hole is visible.
5. **Push through the eye.** Advance the flattened tip through the center of the eye with minimal bend. → *Expect:* a small loop or thread point appears on the far side.
6. **Pull the tail.** Pinch the appearing thread and draw 8 to 12 cm through the eye. → *Expect:* needle hangs on the thread without slipping off.
7. **Equalize or leave a tail.** [BRANCH: single thread | doubled thread] set the working length required. → *Expect:* chosen tail length remains stable when lifted.
8. **Tie the knot.** Wrap the long end around a fingertip, roll the loop, and pull it tight at the end. → *Expect:* a knot stops at the thread end.

## Decision points

- Thread will not enter → recut, wet, and flatten again before retrying.
- Eye is too small → choose a larger needle or use a threader.
- Hands shake → brace wrists, bring objects closer to the table, and move the thread into the needle rather than both.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Frayed tip:** fibers spread at the eye → trim above the fray and reform the flattened tip.
- **F2 Missed eye:** tip bends beside the opening → reset alignment and push with less exposed length.
- **F3 Tail pulls out:** not enough thread through the eye → pull a longer tail before moving the needle.
- **F4 Knot slips:** loop tightens away from the end → pinch the end while drawing the knot down.

## Verification

The needle carries thread through its eye, a usable tail remains, and the far end has a knot that resists a light pull.

## Variations

- Needle threader: pass the wire loop through the eye, place thread in the loop, then pull the loop back.
- Waxed thread: skip wetting if wax already binds the fibers.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from the sharp needle. Keep the point aimed away from fingers and slow down when another person is within arm reach.
