## Goal

The litter box is free of clumps and solids, litter sits at the correct depth, and waste is bagged and binned. On the weekly cycle, the box itself is washed and refilled with fresh litter.

## Preconditions

- A litter box with clumping litter (non-clumping changes the routine, see Variations).
- A slotted litter scoop and a bag that can be tied shut (grocery bags with holes fail here).
- A trash bin that closes; cats will revisit an open bag of their own waste.

## Steps

1. **Stage the bag before touching anything.** Open the waste bag and set it beside the box, or cuff it over one hand like a glove. → *Expect:* bag open and reachable one-handed; the other hand stays clean for the scoop.
2. **Scoop solids first.** Lift each stool with the scoop, tilt to let clean litter sift back through the slots, drop the solid into the bag. → *Expect:* only waste in the bag; litter rain falls back into the box, not the floor.
3. **Probe for urine clumps.** Drag the scoop along the box floor and into the corners; clumps stick to the base and corners where cats aim. Lift gently: a rushed lift shatters the clump into crumbs you cannot recover. → *Expect:* intact clumps in the bag; the scoop passes corner to corner without resistance.
4. **Check litter depth.** Level the remaining litter and eyeball the depth against roughly 5 to 7 cm (2 to 3 inches). Too shallow and urine hits the box floor and welds to it; too deep and some cats refuse the box. → *Expect:* even bed at target depth after any top-up from the litter supply.
5. **Tie off and bin the bag.** Knot the bag and put it in a closed bin. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never flush clumping litter, even one clump; it expands and sets in pipes like mortar. → *Expect:* bag knotted, bin lid closed, no odor escaping.
6. **Wipe the surround.** Sweep or wipe scatter from the floor and the box rim. → *Expect:* no granules within a step of the box; the rim is dry.
7. **Weekly cycle: full change.** [BRANCH: daily visit → done at step 6 | weekly visit → continue] Dump all litter into a bag, wash the box with mild dish soap and warm water, dry it fully, refill to target depth. Skip ammonia and strong citrus cleaners: the smell drives cats away from the box. → *Expect:* box visibly clean, fully dry before refill, fresh litter at 5 to 7 cm.
8. **Wash hands.** Soap, 20 seconds, even if a scoop and bag did all the touching. → *Expect:* hands washed before handling food or face.

## Decision points

- More than one cat in the home → run one box per cat plus one spare, and scoop each daily; multi-cat boxes foul faster than the schedule assumes.
- Cat starts eliminating just outside the box → treat as a signal, not spite: check depth, cleanliness, and whether a recent litter-brand switch happened.
- Litter tracking through the house → add a textured mat under the box exit side before blaming the cat.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Clump welded to the box floor:** detect by scoop resistance at the base → let a splash of warm water sit on it for a minute, then scrape with the scoop edge; prevent by keeping depth at target.
- **F2 Overwhelming ammonia smell on opening the room:** the scoop schedule slipped or the weekly change is overdue → full change now, not a scoop; ammonia at that level means saturated litter throughout.
- **F3 Crumbled clump contaminating the bed:** caused by rushed lifting → sift the area with the scoop repeatedly and accept some loss; note to lift slower on the next pass.
- **F4 Cat interferes mid-clean:** curious cats climb into a half-cleaned or freshly refilled box immediately → not a problem, continue; but never scoop with the cat actively using the box.

## Verification

Scoop passes the full box floor with no resistance and brings up nothing; litter is level at 5 to 7 cm; waste bag is knotted inside a closed bin; floor around the box is granule-free; hands washed. On a weekly visit, additionally: the box was washed and fully dried before refill.

## Variations

- Non-clumping litter: solids are scooped daily but urine soaks the bed, so the full change runs twice weekly instead of weekly.
- Automated self-cleaning boxes: the daily scoop is replaced by emptying the waste drawer, but the weekly wash and depth check still apply.
- Silica crystal litter: no clumps to probe; stir daily, change when crystals are mostly yellow.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall. The one medical note practitioners actually respect: cat feces can carry Toxoplasma, so pregnant people and immunocompromised people should delegate this task or use gloves plus a mask, and everyone washes hands after. Never flush clumping litter. Keep the waste bin closed so dogs and cats cannot raid it.
