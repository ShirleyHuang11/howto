## Goal

The yoga mat is rolled tight and even according to its care preference, strapped if needed, and stored upright or flat without curled edges becoming damaged.

## Preconditions

- Mat is dry and free of loose grit.
- Care preference is known if the mat has a marked top side or textured coating.
- Strap, band, or storage space is available.

## Steps

1. **Identify the top side.** Look for texture, logo placement, or care instructions that indicate which surface should face in or out. → *Expect:* rolling orientation is chosen before folding.
2. **Clear and align the mat.** Lay the mat flat, brush away grit, and square the long edges with both hands. → *Expect:* corners are visible and edges are parallel.
3. **Set the first edge.** Fold or curl the starting short edge into a small straight roll using both thumbs. → *Expect:* first turn is even across the width.
4. **Roll with even pressure.** Push the roll forward from the center while outer fingers guide both ends. → *Expect:* roll advances as a cylinder, not a cone.
5. **Correct drifting edges.** If one end protrudes, pause and nudge that side inward before the next turn. → *Expect:* edges return to alignment.
6. **Tighten without crushing.** Apply light downward pressure with palms as each turn closes, keeping the mat surface smooth. → *Expect:* roll feels firm but not creased.
7. **Strap the roll.** Wrap the strap around the center or both ends and fasten with moderate tension. → *Expect:* roll stays closed when released.
8. **Store upright or flat.** Place the roll upright in a rack or flat on a shelf away from direct heat. → *Expect:* mat does not unroll or lean into a sharp bend.

## Decision points

- Care says top side out → roll with practice surface outside to protect texture from transfer.
- Mat curls at the ends during practice → roll in the opposite direction for storage if the material permits.
- Mat is damp → air dry before rolling to prevent odor and mildew.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cone-shaped roll:** one edge advances faster → unroll two turns, realign edges, and roll with equal hand pressure.
- **F2 Trapped grit:** bumps appear under the surface → unroll to the bump, brush it away, and continue.
- **F3 Strap dents mat:** band is too tight or narrow → loosen it or use a wider strap.
- **F4 Roll opens in storage:** strap misses the center of mass → reposition strap near the middle or add a second strap.

## Verification

The mat forms an even cylinder with aligned edges, remains closed under its strap, and is stored without dampness or sharp bends.

## Variations

- Natural rubber mat: keep away from direct sun and avoid tight compression for long storage.
- Travel mat: fold once lengthwise only if the manufacturer permits folding.
- Shared studio mat: roll with the clean top surface protected according to studio protocol.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not roll a wet mat, and keep the rolled mat out of walking paths where it can become a trip hazard.
