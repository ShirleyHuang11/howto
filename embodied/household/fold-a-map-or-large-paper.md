## Goal

The map or large paper is folded along existing creases, accordioned into panels, folded in half if designed that way, and stored without new forced creases.

## Preconditions

- Paper is dry and clean.
- Existing creases or panel grid are visible.
- A table, clean floor, or wide flat surface is available.

## Steps

1. **Lay the paper flat.** Spread it on the surface and smooth gently from center to edges with open palms. → *Expect:* existing crease pattern is visible.
2. **Find the original folds.** Look for alternating mountain and valley creases, worn edges, or printed panel boundaries. → *Expect:* a fold sequence can be followed without guessing.
3. **Align the first crease.** Lift one edge and let the paper bend where it already wants to bend. → *Expect:* the old crease rises cleanly without a new diagonal line.
4. **Accordion the panels.** Fold one panel forward, the next panel backward, continuing the existing zigzag pattern. → *Expect:* panel edges stack evenly.
5. **Square each fold.** Before pressing, align corners and printed borders, then run fingertips along the crease with light pressure. → *Expect:* folded stack stays rectangular.
6. **Fold the stack in half if designed.** [BRANCH: center cross-crease present → fold in half | no cross-crease → stop at accordion stack] follow only the existing cross-crease. → *Expect:* final packet matches the old worn outline.
7. **Avoid forcing resistance.** If paper buckles away from a fold, open it and search for the missed earlier crease. → *Expect:* resistance disappears when the correct sequence is restored.
8. **Store flat or sleeved.** Slide the folded packet into its sleeve, drawer, or book without bending corners. → *Expect:* packet remains flat and corners are protected.

## Decision points

- Paper has no existing creases → roll loosely around a tube or fold once only if a new crease is acceptable.
- Map is laminated → use wider bends and avoid sharp crease pressure.
- Tear starts at a crease → stop pressing and support the tear with archival tape only if repair is intended.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong fold sequence:** panels refuse to stack or corners drift → unfold to the last aligned crease and resume the alternating pattern.
- **F2 New diagonal crease:** paper buckles under pressure → open immediately, flatten with palms, and refold along the nearest original crease.
- **F3 Corner dog-ear:** corner catches during storage → unfold that corner gently and slide the packet into storage with the corner supported.
- **F4 Tear at crease:** fibers separate along an old fold → stop folding that line and store the map flat until repaired.

## Verification

The paper is folded into its original panel stack with aligned edges and no new forced crease crossing the printed surface.

## Variations

- Road map: accordion lengthwise first, then fold the long stack to the cover panel.
- Poster or art print: roll instead of folding unless creases already exist.
- Sewing pattern tissue: fold loosely along original lines and avoid fingertip pressure that can tear thin paper.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Paper cuts are possible, and old maps or documents may be fragile or contain private location notes, so handle edges lightly and store them out of casual view if needed.
