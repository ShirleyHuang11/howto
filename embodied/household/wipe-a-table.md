## Goal

The table surface is clear of items, free of crumbs and residue, wiped clean, and left dry, ready for the next meal or task.

## Preconditions

- A clean cloth or sponge, a source of water (sink or spray bottle), and a dry cloth or towel for the final pass.
- Somewhere to put the items currently on the table (sink, counter, tray).
- Knowledge of the table finish if it is wood (sealed wood tolerates damp; raw or waxed wood wants a barely-damp cloth only).

## Steps

1. **Clear the table completely.** Move dishes to the sink, papers and objects to a counter or tray. Wiping around objects leaves a ring of grime at every base. → *Expect:* fully bare surface; fragile items (glassware) carried one or two at a time, not stacked.
2. **Do a dry crumb sweep first.** Cup one hand at the table edge and sweep crumbs into it with the other hand or a dry cloth, working toward the edge, or sweep into a plate held under the edge lip. Wetting crumbs first turns them into smeared paste. → *Expect:* loose debris off the surface and in the hand, plate, or bin, not on the floor or chairs.
3. **Prepare the cloth: damp, not wet.** Rinse and wring the cloth until it does not drip when held flat. For greasy residue, add a drop of dish soap or an all-purpose spray. Smell the cloth first: a sour cloth spreads the smell onto the table. → *Expect:* clean-smelling cloth, damp to the touch, no drips.
4. **Wipe in overlapping S-strokes from the far edge toward you.** Cover the whole surface in one directional pass so no strip is skipped; fold the cloth to a fresh face when a face loads up. → *Expect:* visible sheen of moisture behind the stroke; residue lifting onto the cloth faces.
5. **Spot-treat stuck spills.** Lay the damp cloth over dried spots for 30 to 60 seconds to soften, then rub in small circles. Scrape stubborn spots with a fingernail or plastic scraper through the cloth, never a knife blade. → *Expect:* fingertip glides over the spot with no ridge.
6. **Wipe the edges and the edge underside lip.** The rim where hands and plates rest is the grimiest zone and the most skipped. → *Expect:* cloth shows pickup from the rim; edge feels smooth, not tacky.
7. **Do the dry pass.** Go over the whole surface with the dry cloth in the same S-pattern. On wood this is not optional: standing moisture clouds the finish; on glass and stone it prevents streaks. → *Expect:* surface fully dry within a few seconds, uniform matte or gloss with no swirl marks.
8. **Finish and reset.** Rinse and hang the wet cloth to dry, return any items that live on the table. → *Expect:* cloth hung open (not balled up damp), table in its resting state.

## Decision points

- Raw, oiled, or waxed wood → skip sprays and soap; barely-damp cloth, immediate dry pass, and accept that deep stains need refinishing, not scrubbing.
- Sticky sugar spills (juice, honey) → warm water on the cloth outperforms any cleaner; cold water sets sugar harder.
- Table doubles as a food-prep surface → finish with a food-safe cleaner or plain water pass rather than a scented all-purpose spray.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Streaks visible in raking light:** cloth was overloaded or dirty, or the dry pass was skipped → rinse the cloth, re-wipe with clean water only, and dry-pass again.
- **F2 Smear paste of crumbs and liquid:** wet wipe started before the dry sweep → scrape the paste to the edge into a plate with the cloth folded stiff, rinse the cloth, restart from step 3.
- **F3 Sour smell on the table after wiping:** the cloth itself was soured → re-wipe with a fresh cloth and diluted dish soap; retire the old cloth to the wash.
- **F4 White cloudy patch on wood after wiping:** moisture sat too long on the finish → buff dry immediately; a persistent haze may lift with a brief pass from a hairdryer on low, held moving.

## Verification

Sight across the surface at a low angle against a light source: no crumbs, streaks, or damp patches anywhere including the rim. A palm swept across the middle and edges comes away clean and dry with no tacky spots.

## Variations

- Restaurant-style bus wipe: crumb scraper into a bus tub, sanitizer-bucket cloth, air dry; speed over finish care.
- Outdoor tables: hose-rinse or brush off debris first; expect a grime layer that needs soap every time.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The two habits that matter: carry glassware and knives off the table one at a time before any wiping starts, and never sweep crumbs onto the floor where they become the next task. No personal data involved.
