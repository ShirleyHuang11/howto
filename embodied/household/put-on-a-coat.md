## Goal

The coat is worn with both arms through sleeves, shoulders seated, hem hanging evenly, and fasteners closed if desired.

## Preconditions

- Coat is oriented with inside facing the wearer and collar at the top.
- Sleeves are not inside out or blocked by gloves.
- The wearer has enough clearance to move elbows backward.

## Steps

1. **Find the collar and sleeves.** Hold the coat by the collar or upper shoulders. → *Expect:* left and right sleeve openings are visible.
2. **Insert the first arm.** Slide one hand into its sleeve while keeping the sleeve path open. → *Expect:* hand emerges at or near the cuff.
3. **Lift behind the back.** Bring the coat around the shoulders without twisting the first sleeve. → *Expect:* empty sleeve hangs near the other hand.
4. **Insert the second arm.** Guide the other hand into the second sleeve opening and push through. → *Expect:* both hands reach their cuffs.
5. **Shrug over shoulders.** Raise and roll shoulders backward to seat the coat yoke. → *Expect:* collar rests at the back of the neck.
6. **Settle the hem.** Pull front panels down and smooth sleeves from shoulder to cuff. → *Expect:* hem hangs evenly without bunching.
7. **Fasten the front.** [BRANCH: zipper | buttons | open] close the chosen fastener without forcing. → *Expect:* front panels align.
8. **Check mobility.** Move arms forward and down once. → *Expect:* coat stays seated and does not bind painfully.

## Decision points

- Sleeve is twisted → pull the arm back partway and rotate the sleeve before continuing.
- Backpack or carried load is present → set it down or put the coat on before lifting it.
- Fastener resists → align hem and start from the bottom before pulling upward.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Arm in lining gap:** hand does not reach cuff → withdraw and find the true sleeve tube.
- **F2 Coat backward:** collar sits at front or panels face wrong way → remove and reorient before retrying.
- **F3 Shoulder bunch:** fabric piles at upper arm → tug cuff downward and shrug again.
- **F4 Tight fastener:** front strains across body → leave open or use a larger coat.

## Verification

Both arms pass through sleeves, shoulders and collar are seated, hem is even, and any chosen front fastener is closed without strain.

## Variations

- Assisted dressing: helper holds the second sleeve opening steady while the wearer inserts the arm.
- Long coat: clear the hem behind the knees before walking.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Maintain balance while threading arms and do not cover the face or restrict the throat with fasteners.
