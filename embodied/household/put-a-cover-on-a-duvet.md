## Goal

The duvet fills the cover evenly, all far corners are seated, the opening is fastened, and the finished duvet lies smooth on the bed.

## Preconditions

- Duvet cover is clean, unbuttoned or unzipped, and large enough for the duvet.
- Bed or floor area is clear enough to spread the duvet fully.
- Corner ties, loops, or snaps are located if present.

## Steps

1. **Turn the cover inside out.** Put both hands through the opening, grab the far inside corners, and pull them out so seams face outward. → *Expect:* cover is inside out with the opening near you.
2. **Reach through to the far corners.** Insert both hands through the cover opening and hold the two far corners from inside. → *Expect:* each hand controls one far cover corner.
3. **Grab the duvet corners.** With cover corners still in hand, pinch the matching duvet far corners through the fabric or tie them if loops exist. → *Expect:* duvet corners are locked to cover corners.
4. **Flip the cover over the duvet.** Lift the paired far corners and shake downward so the cover starts sliding over the duvet body. → *Expect:* cover turns right side out around the upper half.
5. **Shake down in pulses.** Hold both far corners high, snap the fabric downward in short controlled shakes, and let gravity pull the cover over the fill. → *Expect:* duvet moves deeper into the cover without bunching at the top.
6. **Seat the near corners.** Lay the bundle down, reach into the opening, and pull each near duvet corner into its matching cover corner. → *Expect:* all four corners feel filled and square.
7. **Fasten the opening.** Button, snap, or zip from one end to the other while keeping the duvet edge tucked inside. → *Expect:* closure lies flat and no fill protrudes.
8. **Smooth the panels.** Spread the duvet on the bed and sweep palms from center to edges to distribute loft. → *Expect:* top surface is even with no empty corner pockets.

## Decision points

- Cover has corner ties → tie far corners before shaking and near corners before closing.
- Duvet is heavy or king size → perform shakes in smaller pulses or use a second person at the other corner.
- Corners end up swapped → remove only the nearest half and realign before fastening.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Empty corner:** cover corner collapses when pinched → open that side, reach in, pull the duvet corner fully into place, and retie if available.
- **F2 Duvet bunched in middle:** thick lump forms after shaking → lay flat, push the lump outward with forearms, then shake again.
- **F3 Closure catches fabric:** zipper or buttons grab duvet shell → back up, flatten the edge, and fasten with less fabric near the track.
- **F4 Cover twisted:** seams spiral across the bed → reopen, find all four corners, and rotate the duvet inside until seams align.

## Verification

All four cover corners contain the matching duvet corners, the opening is fastened, and the duvet lies flat without a central bunch.

## Variations

- Burrito method: roll the duvet and inside-out cover together from the closed end, invert the opening over the roll, then unroll.
- Snap closure: press snaps from one edge across while holding the fabric flat between snaps.
- Button closure: start with the middle button to keep the placket aligned, then finish outward.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep feet clear of fabric piles to avoid tripping, and avoid forceful overhead shaking near lamps, shelves, or another person.
