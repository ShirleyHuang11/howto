## Goal

The shirt is supported by the hanger at both shoulders, the top button or placket stabilizes the collar, and the hanger is hooked securely on the rail.

## Preconditions

- Shirt is dry and ready to hang.
- Hanger is the right width for the shirt shoulders and has no sharp burrs.
- Closet rail or hook has space for the hanger.

## Steps

1. **Hold the shirt by the collar.** Lift the shirt so the front faces you and the shoulder seams hang naturally. → *Expect:* collar opening and both shoulders are visible.
2. **Button the top.** Fasten the top button or the highest practical button to keep the shirt from sliding off. → *Expect:* collar forms a closed loop or narrow opening.
3. **Orient the hanger.** Hold the hanger hook upward with the left and right arms matching the shirt shoulders. → *Expect:* hanger curve faces the same direction as the shirt chest.
4. **Insert through the collar or hem.** [BRANCH: collar opening wide enough → slide one hanger arm through collar | delicate collar → insert through bottom hem] guide one arm at a time. → *Expect:* hanger arms are inside the shirt body without stretching fabric.
5. **Seat the first shoulder.** Push one hanger tip into the matching shoulder seam using gentle outward pressure. → *Expect:* fabric drapes over the hanger tip without a pointy bulge.
6. **Seat the second shoulder.** Lift the opposite shoulder fabric and guide the second hanger tip into place. → *Expect:* both shoulder seams rest near the hanger ends.
7. **Square the shirt.** Tug lightly at both side seams and smooth the front placket downward. → *Expect:* shirt hangs symmetrical with no twisted sleeve.
8. **Hook the rail.** Lift by the hanger hook, place it over the closet rail, and release only after the hook is fully seated. → *Expect:* hanger remains on the rail and shirt hangs clear of the floor.

## Decision points

- Shirt is knit or stretchy → use a broad or padded hanger to avoid shoulder peaks.
- Collar is small or stiff → insert the hanger from the bottom hem to avoid stretching the neck.
- Rail is crowded → make a gap before lifting the shirt so the fabric does not snag.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shoulder peak:** hanger tip presses beyond the seam → use a smaller hanger or slide the shoulder inward until the seam aligns.
- **F2 Shirt falls off hanger:** top is open or hanger is too slick → button the top and use a textured or notched hanger.
- **F3 Sleeve twisted:** sleeve seam wraps around the hanger arm → lift that sleeve, rotate it free, and smooth downward.
- **F4 Hanger slips from rail:** hook is not fully seated → rehook from above and confirm the curved hook encloses the rail.

## Verification

The shirt hangs from the rail with both shoulder seams supported evenly, the top secured, and no sleeve or placket twist visible.

## Variations

- Button-down shirt: button the top and one middle button for better placket alignment.
- Wet shirt: use a rustproof hanger and leave space for airflow.
- Suit hanger: align the shirt shoulders over the wider hanger ends before adding a jacket.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid stretching collars, pulling on loose buttons, or crowding hangers so tightly that fabric catches and tears.
