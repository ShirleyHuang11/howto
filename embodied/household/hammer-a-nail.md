## Goal

The nail is driven straight into the target surface until the head sits flush or at the intended proud height.

## Preconditions

- Target material is approved for a nail and clear of hidden wires or pipes.
- Hammer face is clean, handle is secure, and nail length matches the job.
- Workpiece is stable and people are outside the swing path.

## Steps

1. **Mark the target.** Place a small mark where the nail point should enter. → *Expect:* mark is visible and reachable by the hammer face.
2. **Grip the nail safely.** Hold the nail near its head with fingertips pinched lightly at the sides. → *Expect:* point touches the mark and fingers stay above the target.
3. **Start with light taps.** Tap from the wrist until the point bites into the material. → *Expect:* nail stands without full hand support.
4. **Release fingers.** Move the holding hand away from the strike zone. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* missed blows can injure fingers or mar the surface, so confirm the nail stands alone first. → *Expect:* only the hammer hand remains near the nail.
5. **Swing from the elbow.** Hold the hammer near the handle end and strike the nail head squarely. → *Expect:* nail advances with each hit and does not bend.
6. **Correct early lean.** [BRANCH: nail straight | nail leaning] tap the high side gently before it is deep. → *Expect:* shaft returns near perpendicular.
7. **Sink to final height.** Use controlled lighter blows as the head nears the surface. → *Expect:* nail head reaches flush without denting around it.
8. **Stop and inspect.** Set the hammer down away from edges and check the fastened item. → *Expect:* nail holds firm under a light tug.

## Decision points

- Surface is masonry, tile, or metal → use the correct anchor or drill method instead.
- Nail bends before halfway → pull it and restart with a new nail.
- Decorative surface matters → use a nail set for the final millimeters.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bent nail:** shaft curves or head angles → grip with claw, rock out along the entry line, and replace it.
- **F2 Glancing strike:** hammer face slips sideways → slow down, square the face, and shorten the swing.
- **F3 Surface dent:** hammer hits around the head → stop heavy blows and finish with a nail set or smaller taps.
- **F4 Hidden obstruction:** nail stops abruptly → withdraw it and choose a different location after checking utilities.

## Verification

The nail is straight, seated at the intended depth, and the workpiece or hanging point resists a light load without shifting.

## Variations

- Small brad: hold it with pliers or cardboard instead of fingertips.
- Framing nail: larger swing is acceptable only after the nail is started and the area is clear.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from sharp nail points, hammer impact, and hidden utilities. Pause work when anyone enters the swing zone.
