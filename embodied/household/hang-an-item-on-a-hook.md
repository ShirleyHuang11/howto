## Goal

An item with a loop, collar, handle, or hanging point is placed on a hook and remains supported after release.

## Preconditions

- The hook is visible, fixed, and rated for the item weight.
- The item has a usable loop, strap, collar, hole, or handle.
- The actor can reach the hook without overextension.
- The hook area is clear of items that could be knocked down.

## Steps

1. **Approach the hook.** Move until the hook is centered in the work area and below or near comfortable reach height. → *Expect:* hook tip and load direction are visible.
2. **Identify the hanging point.** Find the item's loop, collar, strap, or hole that should bear weight. → *Expect:* hanging point is graspable and not tangled.
3. **Grip below the hanging point.** Hold the item so the loop or collar can move freely over the hook. → *Expect:* item is controlled and the hanging point is exposed.
4. **Lift to hook height.** Raise the hanging point slightly above the hook tip. → *Expect:* loop or collar clears the hook tip.
5. **Align with the hook opening.** Move the hanging point toward the hook from the open side. → *Expect:* hook tip enters the loop, collar, or handle.
6. **Lower onto the hook.** Let the item's weight transfer down onto the deepest part of the hook. → *Expect:* item settles against the hook curve or backstop.
7. **Release gradually.** Open the grip while keeping a guard hand nearby. → *Expect:* item remains hanging without sliding off.
8. **Confirm the hold.** Watch for sway, tilt, or hook flex for two seconds. → *Expect:* item is still and supported.
9. **Clear the area.** Withdraw the hand downward and away from the hook tip. → *Expect:* item and nearby objects remain undisturbed.

## Decision points

- Hook is too high → use a stable step aid or choose a lower hook.
- Hanging point is twisted → untwist before lifting to avoid partial engagement.
- Hook tip points forward → push the loop fully behind the tip before lowering.
- Item is heavy or long → use two hands and keep the lower end away from knees.
- Hook flexes → remove the item and choose a stronger support.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Loop misses hook:** detect loop outside the hook after lowering → re-grip, raise above the tip, and realign.
- **F2 Item slides off:** detect downward slip or fall start → catch if safe, then seat the loop deeper or use another hook.
- **F3 Hook loosens:** detect wobble, cracking, or wall movement → remove load immediately and stop using the hook.
- **F4 Item swings into wall:** detect pendulum motion → steady the lower part until motion stops.
- **F5 Strap tangles:** detect uneven hanging or twist → lift off, untangle, and rehang.

## Verification

The item hangs from the intended point, the hook supports the load without movement, and the item remains in place after release.

## Variations

- `coat`: use the collar loop or hanger rather than stretching the fabric.
- `bag`: hang by a closed strap and confirm the bag body does not pull the hook sideways.
- `key-ring`: align the ring plane with the hook tip before lowering.
- `tool-hook`: orient the handle so the tool cannot rotate free.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for light items. Do not hang heavy objects over people, breakables, or walkways. Avoid looking through or exposing private bag contents while handling the item.
