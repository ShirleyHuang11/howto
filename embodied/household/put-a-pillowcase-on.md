## Goal

The pillow is fully seated inside the pillowcase, corners are squared, and the open end is tucked or aligned neatly.

## Preconditions

- Pillowcase is clean, right side out unless using the flip method, and sized for the pillow.
- Pillow is dry and free of loose items or tags that can snag.
- Bed or table surface is clear enough to set the pillow flat.

## Steps

1. **Orient the case.** Hold the pillowcase with the opening facing your body and seams aligned left to right. → *Expect:* the far closed corners are visible or easy to feel.
2. **Choose the method.** [BRANCH: tuck-corners method | flip method] use tuck-corners for standard cases and flip for tight or decorative cases. → *Expect:* opening is controlled and not twisted.
3. **Tuck-corners method: compress the pillow.** Pinch both pillow corners at one short end and squeeze them slightly toward the center. → *Expect:* pillow end narrows enough to enter the case.
4. **Insert and seat the far end.** Slide the compressed pillow end into the case until both pillow corners reach the closed corners. → *Expect:* far corners feel filled when pinched from outside.
5. **Pull the case over the pillow.** Hold the seated corners with one hand and draw the case opening over the pillow body with the other. → *Expect:* fabric advances evenly without rolling into a band.
6. **Flip method: grab through the case.** Turn the case inside out, grab the far case corners and pillow corners together, then flip the case over the pillow. → *Expect:* case rolls right side out around the pillow.
7. **Square the corners.** Pinch each corner from outside and tug the pillow fill into it using small shakes, not hard pulls. → *Expect:* four corners are filled and angular.
8. **Finish the open end.** Smooth the pillow flat, tuck any internal flap around the pillow, and align the opening seam. → *Expect:* pillow lies flat with no exposed inner pillow except at an intentional opening.

## Decision points

- Pillowcase has an envelope flap → insert the pillow under the flap before final smoothing.
- Pillow is overfilled → use the flip method and compress the pillow lengthwise before seating corners.
- Decorative trim or zipper is present → avoid pulling on trim and keep zipper teeth away from pillow fabric.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Corner not seated:** one case corner is flat or empty → reach in, grab the pillow corner, and push it into the case corner.
- **F2 Case twisted:** seams spiral around the pillow → pull the case halfway off, rotate it, and reseat both far corners.
- **F3 Pillow bunches:** fill folds in the middle → hold two corners and shake lightly until the fill spreads.
- **F4 Opening gapes:** pillow edge shows past the flap → push the pillow deeper and fold the flap over the end.

## Verification

The pillow fills the pillowcase from closed end to open end, all corners are seated, and the surface is smooth when laid flat.

## Variations

- King pillowcase: support the middle of the pillow with a forearm so it does not sag while inserting.
- Memory foam pillow: avoid sharp folding; compress slowly and release after the case is in place.
- Sham: close buttons or ties after the pillow is squared, then smooth the decorative face.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the work surface clear so the pillow does not pick up dirt, and avoid whipping the pillow near another person's face or fragile bedside items.
