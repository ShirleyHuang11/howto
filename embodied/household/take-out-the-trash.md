## Goal

The kitchen bin's full bag is sealed, deposited in the correct outdoor/building collection bin, and the kitchen bin is relined and back in place.

## Preconditions

- The kitchen bin is full or scheduled for emptying.
- Replacement liner bags are in reach.
- The route to the outdoor bin / disposal room is passable, and you know which collection bin is correct (general waste vs. recycling vs. compost).

## Steps

1. **Open the bin lid and check the contents' top layer.** No liquids pooling, no protruding sharp objects. If glass shards or skewers protrude, fold cardboard around them before proceeding. → *Expect:* the bag's contents are containable; nothing pierces the bag wall.
2. **Lift the bag's edges off the bin rim and tie a secure knot.** Gather the liner's loose edges, twist, and double-knot; leave no open mouth. → *Expect:* bag sealed; nothing can fall out when inverted slightly.
3. **Lift the bag straight up and out of the bin.** Lift from the knot with one hand, support the base with the other if heavy. If resistance (vacuum or snag), tilt the bin and break the seal — don't yank. → *Expect:* bag comes free intact; no tearing sound; bin stays upright.
4. **Check the bag for leaks before crossing the home.** Hold over the bin for 3 s and watch for drips. [BRANCH: drips → double-bag before moving | dry → proceed] → *Expect:* no drips on the floor route.
5. **Carry the bag to the outdoor/collection bin.** Hold away from your body/chassis and clear of walls; open doors fully before passing through; slow near people. → *Expect:* no contact between bag and walls, door frames, or people en route.
6. **Deposit into the correct collection bin.** Open the outdoor bin lid, place (don't throw) the bag in, close the lid fully. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* retrieval from collection bins is not assumed possible — confirm this is the trash bag, not a similar bag of belongings, before depositing. → *Expect:* bag inside the correct bin; lid closed flat (animal-proof).
7. **Return and reline the kitchen bin.** Pull a new liner over the rim, push it to conform, leaving overhang. → *Expect:* liner snug on the rim, no air pocket at the bottom.
8. **Replace the bin in its position and wash/wipe hands or grippers.** → *Expect:* bin lid closes; workspace as found, minus the trash.

## Decision points

- Bag is overfull (won't tie) → transfer the top items into a second bag; never compress with hands (hidden sharps).
- Collection bins are full → use the designated overflow area if one exists; otherwise return the bag to a holding spot and retry after collection day — never leave bags beside bins where prohibited.
- Recyclables mixed in and local rules require separation → set obvious clean recyclables aside into the recycling stream before tying (step 2).

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bag tears mid-route:** set it down immediately, fetch a second bag, bag-the-bag from the top; then clean the drip trail.
- **F2 Liquid pooled at the bin bottom after bag removal:** wipe the bin interior with paper towels and a disinfectant pass before relining.
- **F3 Wrong bin (recycling contaminated):** if the lid was just closed and retrieval is safe and permitted, retrieve and re-deposit; otherwise flag to a human — contamination fines exist in some municipalities.

## Verification

The sealed bag sits in the correct collection bin with its lid fully closed, the kitchen bin is relined and repositioned, the carry route has no drips or debris, and hands/grippers have been cleaned.

## Variations

- Apartment chute buildings: step 6 becomes chute-door open → bag in → door closed; oversized bags are prohibited in chutes — carry them to the disposal room instead.
- Municipal sorted collection (`jp`, `de`): bags must be the municipality's designated color/type per waste class, and each class has fixed collection days — verify today's class before step 6.

## Safety & privacy

Sharp objects may pierce bags — lift and carry away from the body; never compress a bag. Slow movement through shared corridors and doorways when people are present. Trash contents can reveal personal information — deposit only in the designated bin, not public street bins.
