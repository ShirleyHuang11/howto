## Goal

Broken glass is collected from a floor or counter, including tiny shards, and disposed of in a puncture-resistant container.

## Preconditions

- People and pets are kept away from the area.
- Shoes, gloves, broom, dustpan, tape or bread, damp paper towel, and rigid container are available.
- Lighting can be adjusted to reveal small reflective pieces.

## Steps

1. **Freeze traffic.** Tell nearby people to stop and step away from the break area. → *Expect:* no bare feet, hands, or paws enter the shard zone.
2. **Put on protection.** Wear shoes and gloves before touching anything. → *Expect:* skin is covered where accidental contact is likely.
3. **Mark the area.** Mentally include the obvious break zone plus at least 1 meter around it. → *Expect:* cleanup covers the scatter field, not just the center.
4. **Pick up big pieces first.** Grasp large shards by dull edges if visible and place them in a rigid container. → *Expect:* large pieces are removed without squeezing sharp edges.
5. **Sweep medium pieces.** Sweep slowly toward the dustpan using short strokes. → *Expect:* visible fragments collect in the pan without skittering away.
6. **Use low-angle light.** Shine a flashlight or phone light across the surface from the side. → *Expect:* tiny shards sparkle against the floor or counter.
7. **Press bread or tape for shards.** Dab soft bread or tape sticky-side down over the area. → *Expect:* fine glass specks stick to the bread or tape.
8. **Wipe last residue.** Use a damp paper towel and fold it inward after each pass. → *Expect:* dust and remaining grit are captured inside the towel.
9. **Package disposal.** Place glass, tape, bread, and towels into a rigid container or wrapped bundle. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* loose glass in a thin trash bag can cut handlers, so contain it before discarding. → *Expect:* sharp waste cannot poke through normal handling.
10. **Final light check.** Re-scan the area from multiple angles before allowing traffic. → *Expect:* no reflections or gritty sounds remain.

## Decision points

- Glass broke near food → discard exposed food and wipe the surface before food contact resumes.
- Glass entered carpet → use tape repeatedly and vacuum only after large pieces are removed.
- Human is too close → pause manipulation until the person moves outside the scatter zone.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hidden shards:** detect sparkle under side light or crunch under shoe; repeat tape or bread dabbing and rescan.
- **F2 Cut glove or skin:** detect snagging, blood, or pain; stop, clean the wound, bandage, and replace gloves.
- **F3 Spread by sweeping:** detect fragments moving outward; switch to damp towel or tape for fine pieces.
- **F4 Unsafe disposal:** detect loose shards in a soft bag; repackage into cardboard, jar, or rigid container.

## Verification

The area shows no visible reflections under low-angle light, no gritty sound under a protected shoe, and all glass is contained for disposal.

## Variations

- Countertop: use damp towel and tape more than a broom.
- Dark floor: white paper towel under the dustpan helps reveal fragments.
- Outdoor break: sweep wider because shards travel farther on hard surfaces.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from cuts and secondary injury. Keep humans and animals away until verification is complete, and label wrapped glass if someone else may handle the trash.
