## Goal

The hose or cable is coiled in even over-under loops, secured without kinks, and stored on a hook or shelf without stressing connectors.

## Preconditions

- Hose or cable is disconnected, safe to handle, and free of active water or power.
- Storage hook, strap, or shelf is ready.
- Floor path is clear so the length can be straightened.

## Steps

1. **Straighten the length.** Walk the hose or cable out enough to remove crossings and sharp bends. → *Expect:* the line lies mostly straight with no trapped loops.
2. **Hold the starting end.** Grip one end in the non-dominant hand with the connector hanging below the hand, not bearing weight. → *Expect:* first connector is controlled and protected.
3. **Make the first over loop.** Draw a loop toward your hand in the natural curl direction and place it into the holding hand. → *Expect:* loop is round and about the planned coil diameter.
4. **Make the under loop.** For the next loop, rotate the incoming line half a turn opposite the natural curl before placing it in the hand. → *Expect:* the loop lies flat without twisting the previous loop.
5. **Alternate over and under.** Continue one over loop, one under loop, keeping loop diameters consistent. → *Expect:* coil hangs straight from the hand and does not spiral.
6. **Remove small kinks early.** If a twist appears, lower the coil and let the free length rotate until tension releases. → *Expect:* line relaxes and the next loop forms without a hard bend.
7. **Secure the coil.** Wrap a strap, hook-and-loop tie, or soft cord around the middle with enough tension to hold shape but not flatten the line. → *Expect:* loops stay together when lifted.
8. **Hang or store.** Lift from the coil body, not the connector, and place it on a wide hook or shelf. → *Expect:* coil rests evenly with connectors visible and unstressed.

## Decision points

- Hose is stiff or pressurized → drain it and coil in larger loops.
- Cable has a memory from a reel → follow the existing curl and still alternate over-under to prevent twist buildup.
- Connector is heavy or fragile → secure it to the coil so it does not swing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Coil twists into a figure eight:** under loops are missing or inconsistent → uncoil several loops and restart with clear over-under alternation.
- **F2 Hard kink appears:** line bends sharply at one point → straighten that segment by hand and enlarge the coil diameter.
- **F3 Strap too tight:** hose flattens or cable jacket dents → loosen the strap and support the coil with a wider band.
- **F4 Connector strain:** plug or nozzle carries the coil weight → rehang using the coil body and tie the connector to the side.

## Verification

The stored coil has even loops, no sharp kinks, and connectors or hose ends are visible without bearing the coil's weight.

## Variations

- Garden hose: drain fully and use larger loops to reduce memory and cracking.
- Audio or extension cable: over-under every loop so the cable deploys without spinning.
- Short cord: use a loose figure-eight wrap around the hand only if over-under loops are too small.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Confirm electrical cables are unplugged before handling, avoid tripping over the free length, and do not hang a heavy coil from a small connector.
