## Goal

Every book is shelved upright under a single stated sort scheme, rows are stable without leaning, and any given book can be found and removed with one hand.

## Preconditions

- A bookshelf that stands solid (anchored to the wall if tall or in a home with children).
- Floor or table space nearby to stage every book during the sort.
- Bookends for any shelf that will be left partly filled.
- A rough sense of the collection size versus shelf capacity (if books clearly exceed shelves, plan a cull box before starting).

## Steps

1. **Empty the shelf completely, one shelf at a time.** Stack books on the floor in the order removed. Working around books left in place produces a half-sorted mess. → *Expect:* bare shelves; floor stacks no taller than 5 or 6 books so they cannot topple.
2. **Wipe the empty shelves.** Dust builds a gray felt at the back edge that transfers to page tops. → *Expect:* clean, dry shelf surfaces before anything goes back.
3. **Commit to one sort scheme.** [BRANCH: by subject then author (reference-heavy collections) | by author surname (fiction-heavy) | by size or color (display shelves, harder to search)] Mixed schemes are the classic failure: pick one and note the rule. → *Expect:* a rule you can state in one sentence that assigns every book a unique region.
4. **Pre-sort into labeled floor piles by the scheme.** Sort on the floor first; sorting directly onto shelves forces constant reshuffling. → *Expect:* piles matching the scheme, plus a "cull" pile for donations and a "misfit" pile for oversized items.
5. **Assign heights to shelves before shelving.** Tall books (atlases, art books) to the bottom shelf, standard heights mid, small paperbacks up top. Heavy rows low also keeps the unit's center of gravity down. → *Expect:* each pile mapped to a shelf whose clearance fits its tallest spine.
6. **Shelve upright, spines flush to the front edge.** Front-flush spines read better and leave the rear gap for airflow. Within a shelf, keep a size-graded row: tallest at the ends or one end, so no tall book stands isolated between short ones and warps. → *Expect:* an even front line of spines; every book fully vertical.
7. **Do not overpack.** Leave a hand's width of slack per shelf: a book should slide out with a one-finger tilt at the headcap area top without dragging its neighbors. Overpacked rows tear headcaps and split spines. → *Expect:* any test book removable with one hand; row does not spring back shut.
8. **Set bookends on partial rows.** A leaning row warps every book in it within weeks. Use real bookends or lay 2 or 3 heavy books horizontally as an end block. → *Expect:* all rows vertical with no lean anywhere on the unit.
9. **Place misfits and finish.** Oversized books lie flat in short stacks (3 or 4 max) on the bottom shelf; box the cull pile and remove it from the room today, or it re-shelves itself. → *Expect:* no books left on the floor; cull box by the door.

## Decision points

- Books exceed capacity even after the cull → do not double-row by default; double rows hide the back row and it stops existing. If forced, put only known-duplicates or archives in back.
- Valuable or fragile books (old glue, loose hinges) → shelve them snug but not tight, out of direct sunlight, and pull them by gripping mid-spine, never by the headcap.
- Adjustable shelf pins available → move shelves to match the height plan from step 5 instead of forcing books to fit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Row leans after you walk away:** end support missing or slack too generous → add a bookend or redistribute a few books from a tight shelf into the gap.
- **F2 Shelf sags in the middle:** particleboard span overloaded with heavy books → move the heavy run to the bottom shelf or split it across two shelves; a sagged shelf flipped upside down straightens slowly under load.
- **F3 Cannot find a book after organizing:** the scheme has an ambiguity (anthologies, multi-subject books) → pick a tiebreak rule (first author, primary subject) and apply it everywhere, now.
- **F4 Sort stalls because every book invites reading:** the classic time sink → set a rule of 10 seconds per keep-or-cull decision and a separate "decide later" pile capped at 10 books.

## Verification

State the sort rule aloud and test it: pick 3 random titles from memory and locate each within 15 seconds. Every row stands vertical with no lean, each test book slides out one-handed, and the floor and staging area are empty.

## Variations

- Color-sorted display shelves: photogenic but search cost is high; keep a phone note mapping titles to shelf regions.
- Kids' shelves: front-facing covers in low bins beat spines-out; durability outranks order.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, with two real cautions: carry books in small stacks (a full shelf run is heavier than it looks, and dropped stacks break toes), and never let a tall unloaded bookcase stand unanchored while children are around, since emptying the bottom shelves first makes it top-heavy.
