## Goal

Carry a bike on a car rack securely while keeping the vehicle, lights, plate, bike, and road users safe.

## Preconditions

- You have a rack rated for your vehicle, hitch, trunk, roof bars, or spare tire.
- Rack weight limit covers the bike, including e-bike weight if applicable.
- Straps, arms, keys, and safety clips are present and undamaged.

## Steps

1. **Pick the correct rack type.** Use a hitch, trunk, roof, or spare-tire rack only as designed for the vehicle and bike weight. → *Expect:* the rack manual permits this vehicle and bike combination.
2. **Install the empty rack first.** Tighten hitch bolts, trunk straps, roof clamps, or spare-tire mounts according to the rack instructions. → *Expect:* the empty rack does not wobble beyond normal flex.
3. **Prepare the bike.** Remove bottles, bags, computers, loose lights, child seats, and batteries if the e-bike manual allows removal. → *Expect:* nothing loose can fly off.
4. **Load the bike in the intended position.** Lift with help if needed and place tires, frame, or fork exactly where the rack supports them. → *Expect:* the bike rests on the load-bearing parts, not on cables or fenders.
5. **Secure frame and wheels.** [BRANCH: platform rack | hanging rack | roof rack] clamp the frame or tire, strap both wheels, and add anti-sway straps where provided. → *Expect:* the bike cannot roll, swing, or bounce free.
6. **Protect contact points.** Pad frame rub areas and keep pedals, bars, and wheels from touching the vehicle or another bike. → *Expect:* normal vibration will not grind paint or bend parts.
7. **Check plate and light visibility.** Confirm brake lights, turn signals, and license plate remain visible, or install an auxiliary light or plate board where required. → *Expect:* following drivers and enforcement can see required signals.
8. **Do a shake and clearance test.** Push the bike and rack, then check garage height, hatch clearance, exhaust heat, and rear-camera obstruction. → *Expect:* movement is controlled and known clearances are safe.
9. **Recheck after driving briefly.** Stop after 5-10 minutes, then retighten straps and clamps. → *Expect:* settling is corrected before highway speed or long distance.

## Decision points

- E-bike is heavy → use a rated hitch platform rack and remove the battery if allowed.
- Roof rack is used → measure total vehicle height and avoid garages, drive-throughs, and low branches.
- Trunk rack blocks lights or plate → add auxiliary lighting or choose a different rack.
- Multiple bikes touch → reorder or pad them so movement cannot damage frames.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rack loosens:** straps or hitch move after the shake test → stop, unload if needed, and reinstall before driving.
- **F2 Wheel spins:** wheel strap is missing or loose → strap each wheel or add a soft tie before moving.
- **F3 Plate or lights blocked:** rear visibility is illegal or unsafe → relocate plate/light board or use another transport method.
- **F4 Bike hits vehicle:** pedal, bar, or tire contacts paint → reposition and pad before continuing.
- **F5 Clearance strike risk:** roof bike approaches a low structure → stop before entering and remove or reroute.

## Verification

The rack is tight, each bike's frame and wheels are secured, lights and plate are visible or legally supplemented, and straps remain tight after a short recheck stop.

## Variations

- Carbon frames: avoid clamps on frame tubes unless the rack and bike maker approve it.
- Step-through frames: use a top-tube adapter for hanging racks if required.
- Long highway trips: inspect at fuel stops and after rough roads.
- Rain or grime: clean contact points after transport to prevent grit damage.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because a loose bike can injure others. Follow rack load limits, drive slower with added rear or roof load, lock the bike only as a theft deterrent, and never rely on a lock as the primary tie-down.
