## Goal

Lock a bike so the frame and at least one wheel are secured to a fixed rack with minimal leverage for theft.

## Preconditions

- You have a quality U-lock or hardened chain, plus a cable or second lock if possible.
- The bike has no loose valuables left on it.
- A legal, fixed bike rack or solid post is available.

## Steps

1. **Choose a fixed rack.** Pull and shake the rack or post to confirm it is bolted down and not a removable signpost. → *Expect:* the anchor cannot be lifted, unscrewed, or carried away.
2. **Avoid weak lock spots.** Skip trees, thin signs, scaffolding, fences, and anything the bike can be lifted over. → *Expect:* the anchor is stronger than the lock.
3. **Position the bike close.** Put the frame triangle and rear wheel near the rack with little open space between them. → *Expect:* the lock can capture frame, wheel, and rack tightly.
4. **Lock frame and rear wheel with the U-lock.** Pass the U-lock through the rear triangle, rear wheel, and fixed rack. → *Expect:* the most valuable removable parts are captured by the strongest lock.
5. **Fill the gap.** Use the smallest lock space possible so a jack or pry bar cannot easily fit inside. → *Expect:* the lock sits snugly instead of floating in open air.
6. **Secure the front wheel.** [BRANCH: cable | second U-lock | remove wheel] cable it to the U-lock, use a second lock, or remove it and lock it with the frame. → *Expect:* both wheels are either locked or intentionally protected.
7. **Remove accessories.** Take lights, computer, bags, pump, phone mount, and quick-release seat if theft is likely. → *Expect:* easy-grab items are not left on the bike.
8. **Keep the lock off the ground.** Rotate the keyway downward or sideways and keep the lock body suspended. → *Expect:* smashing or picking access is harder.
9. **Do a final lift test.** Try to move the bike away from the rack without damaging it. → *Expect:* frame, wheel, and rack stay connected.

## Decision points

- Only a cable lock is available → use it for a very short, visible stop only, then upgrade to a U-lock.
- Quick-release wheels → prioritize locking both wheels or replacing skewers with security skewers.
- High-theft area → use two different lock types and park near other bikes in a visible location.
- E-bike → remove battery or display if designed to detach.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Locked only the wheel:** frame can be carried away → unlock and capture the frame before leaving.
- **F2 Bad rack:** anchor wobbles or can be lifted over → move to a fixed rack even if it is farther away.
- **F3 Lock has too much space:** tools fit inside → reposition the bike and lock tighter.
- **F4 Accessory theft:** lights or bags are gone → replace them and make removal part of the lock routine.
- **F5 Lost key or code:** bike is secure but inaccessible → use the spare key or contact a locksmith with proof of ownership.

## Verification

The bike frame and at least one wheel are locked to a fixed object with a tight U-lock or chain, and removable accessories are taken with you.

## Variations

- Frame-mounted rear wheel locks: useful for quick stops, but pair them with a fixed-object lock.
- Indoor bike rooms: still lock to a rack because shared rooms can be accessed by many people.
- Cargo bikes: lock the frame to the rack and secure removable boxes, batteries, and child seats.
- Campus racks: check local rules because bikes locked to railings may be removed.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because theft can strand you and cause financial loss. Do not block sidewalks, ramps, fire exits, or accessible paths, and avoid posting a predictable daily parking location.
