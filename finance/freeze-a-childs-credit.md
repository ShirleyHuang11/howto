## Goal

Freeze a child's credit file so identity thieves cannot easily open credit using the child's personal information.

## Preconditions

- You are the child's parent, legal guardian, or authorized representative.
- You have the child's legal name, date of birth, address, and national ID number if applicable.
- You have documents proving the child's identity and your authority, such as birth certificate, guardianship order, your ID, and proof of address.
- You can submit requests to all major credit bureaus in your locale.

## Steps

1. **Gather required documents.** Collect the child's identity proof, your government ID, proof of address, and proof of guardianship. → *Expect:* each document is current, readable, and shows matching names or addresses.
2. **Make secure copies.** Scan or photocopy only what each bureau requires and store copies in a protected folder. → *Expect:* you have legible files or pages ready for submission.
3. **Find official bureau instructions.** Navigate to each credit bureau's child security freeze page. → *Expect:* requirements and mailing or upload options are listed by the bureau.
4. **Complete the first bureau request.** Fill out the minor freeze form with child and guardian details. → *Expect:* the form matches the documents you will send.
5. **Submit documents securely.** Use the bureau's secure upload or mailed address from the official site. → *Expect:* you receive upload confirmation, tracking number, or mailing receipt.
6. **Repeat for every major bureau.** Submit separate requests to all three major bureaus or all relevant bureaus in your country. → *Expect:* no bureau is skipped.
7. **Save confirmation letters.** Keep bureau responses, freeze PINs, passwords, or reference numbers in a secure location. → *Expect:* you can later thaw or remove the freeze.
8. **Check whether a file already existed.** Read bureau responses for signs that a credit file was found before the freeze. → *Expect:* either a new protected file was created or an existing file was frozen.
9. **Investigate existing files.** If a bureau reports activity, request the child's credit report and review accounts. → *Expect:* any fraudulent accounts are identified for dispute.
10. **Dispute fraud if found.** Send disputes with the child's documents and identity-theft report if needed. → *Expect:* each fraudulent entry receives an investigation case number.
11. **Record thaw instructions.** Note how to temporarily lift the freeze when the child later needs credit, housing, student loans, or employment screening. → *Expect:* future thaw steps are available without hunting for old mail.
12. **Review after major events.** Recheck if documents are stolen, mail arrives about credit, or the child approaches adulthood. → *Expect:* the freeze remains intentional and documented.

## Decision points

- The child has no credit file → the bureau may create a protected file solely to freeze it.
- A bureau finds existing credit → treat it as possible child identity theft and request the report.
- You lack guardianship proof → obtain the legal document before submitting.
- The child needs credit later → thaw only the needed bureau and time window, then refreeze.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missing document:** detect bureau rejection for insufficient proof, recover by resubmitting the exact requested document.
- **F2 Name mismatch:** detect rejection due to different names or addresses, recover with birth certificate, adoption order, or court order.
- **F3 Skipped bureau:** detect only one or two confirmations, recover by submitting to the missing bureau.
- **F4 Lost PIN:** detect inability to thaw later, recover through the bureau's identity verification process.
- **F5 Fraud already present:** detect an existing file with accounts, recover by filing identity-theft reports and disputes.

## Verification

Every major credit bureau confirms that the child's credit file is frozen or that a protected minor file was created and frozen, and all thaw credentials are stored securely.

## Variations

- United States: submit minor freeze requests separately to Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.
- Foster or guardianship case: court paperwork may be required instead of a birth certificate.
- Older teen: explain the freeze and where thaw credentials are stored before adulthood.
- Mail-only bureau: use tracked mail and keep copies of everything sent.

## Safety & privacy

Child identity theft can go unnoticed for years because children rarely use credit. Send documents only to official bureaus, store freeze PINs securely, and thaw narrowly when credit access is truly needed.
