## Goal

Your credit files are frozen at the three major credit bureaus so new creditors cannot access them without a thaw.

## Preconditions

- You know the credit bureaus that operate in your country; in the United States, use Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion.
- You have identity details ready, such as legal name, address history, date of birth, and government ID number if required.
- You can store PINs, passwords, or recovery codes in a secure place.
- You are not about to submit a loan, credit card, rental, or phone financing application today unless you can thaw first.

## Steps

1. **Open the first bureau's official freeze page.** Type the address directly or use a trusted government or bureau link. → *Expect:* the page describes a security freeze, not paid monitoring.
2. **Create or access the bureau account.** Enter identity details and complete any verification questions. → *Expect:* the bureau recognizes your file or gives a manual verification path.
3. **Place the freeze.** Choose freeze or security freeze and confirm the request. → *Expect:* the bureau shows the file is frozen.
4. **Save recovery details.** Store the PIN, confirmation number, username, and password in a password manager or locked file. → *Expect:* you can find the details without exposing them.
5. **Repeat for all bureaus.** Complete the same process for each major bureau, not just one. [BRANCH: online succeeds | phone or mail required] → *Expect:* every bureau has a freeze confirmation.
6. **Record thaw instructions.** Note how to temporarily lift each freeze and how long it takes. → *Expect:* you know the steps before applying for credit.
7. **Test account access.** Log out and confirm you can sign back in or have recovery methods saved. → *Expect:* you are not locked out of managing the freeze.
8. **Tell household applicants.** If a joint application is coming, remind the other applicant to freeze or thaw their own credit too. → *Expect:* everyone involved understands each person has separate bureau files.

## Decision points

- If applying for credit soon, ask the lender which bureau they pull and schedule a temporary thaw.
- If identity questions fail, use the bureau's phone or mail verification process instead of guessing repeatedly.
- If freezing for a minor or protected person, expect extra identity and authority documents.
- If a site offers a paid lock or monitoring plan, look for the free security freeze option.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Only one bureau frozen:** detect confirmations from fewer than all major bureaus, recover by completing the missing bureaus.
- **F2 PIN lost:** detect inability to thaw, recover through the bureau's account recovery or identity verification process.
- **F3 Paid product confusion:** detect checkout or subscription language, recover by backing out to the free security freeze page.
- **F4 Application denied for frozen file:** detect lender cannot pull credit, recover by temporarily thawing the requested bureau.
- **F5 Fraud already present:** detect unknown accounts during setup, recover by filing disputes and an identity theft report.

## Verification

Each major bureau account or confirmation page states that your credit file is frozen, and your recovery details are stored securely.

## Variations

- United States: federal law makes placing, lifting, and removing credit freezes free.
- Other countries: bureau names, legal rights, and response times differ.
- Fraud alert: a fraud alert is separate from a freeze and may be useful after suspected identity theft.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because sensitive identity data is entered. Use official bureau sites, avoid paid upsells unless wanted, store PINs securely, and thaw only for a specific application window.
