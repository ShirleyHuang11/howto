## Goal

Read a rental lease closely enough to understand obligations, costs, exit options, and negotiation points before signing.

## Preconditions

- You have the full lease, addenda, house rules, and fee schedule.
- You know the property address, landlord or management company, and move-in date.
- You can ask questions before signing or paying non-refundable money.
- You can check local tenant rules if the lease language seems unusual.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the parties and premises.** Check names, address, unit, parking, storage, pets, and included areas. → *Expect:* the lease describes the exact place and people involved.
2. **Read the term.** Note start date, end date, renewal type, notice period, and month-to-month conversion. → *Expect:* you know when the lease begins and how it ends.
3. **Map the rent terms.** Find rent amount, due date, grace period, payment method, late fees, and rent increase language. → *Expect:* total monthly cost and increase rules are visible.
4. **List deposits and move-in money.** Separate security deposit, last month's rent, application fee, pet fee, key fee, and prepaid rent. → *Expect:* refundable and non-refundable amounts are labeled.
5. **Check utilities and services.** Identify who pays electricity, gas, water, trash, internet, parking, snow removal, lawn care, and pest control. → *Expect:* no recurring housing cost is hidden.
6. **Study break clauses.** Look for early termination fees, subletting rules, job relocation clauses, military clauses, and notice requirements. → *Expect:* you know the cost and process of leaving early.
7. **Review conduct and maintenance duties.** Note repair reporting, entry notice, quiet hours, guests, pets, smoking, alterations, and insurance requirements. → *Expect:* daily rules and repair responsibilities are clear.
8. **Negotiate before signing.** [BRANCH: unclear term | unacceptable term] ask for written clarification or edits before signing; do not rely on spoken promises. → *Expect:* agreed changes appear in the lease or addendum.
9. **Save the signed copy and evidence.** Keep the lease, move-in checklist, photos, payment receipts, and messages. → *Expect:* you can prove the agreed terms and property condition later.

## Decision points

- A clause conflicts with local tenant law → ask a tenant clinic, attorney, or housing authority before signing.
- The landlord promises repairs before move-in → require a written deadline in the lease or addendum.
- The lease renews automatically → calendar the notice deadline.
- Fees are vague or unlimited → ask for caps or examples before signing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Verbal promise missing:** detect by lease not mentioning the promise, recover by requesting written language before signing.
- **F2 Surprise rent increase:** detect by renewal or escalation clause, recover by negotiating a cap or planning for notice.
- **F3 Deposit confusion:** detect by unclear refundable status, recover by asking for a written deposit schedule.
- **F4 Early exit penalty:** detect by break clause requiring months of rent, recover by negotiating before signing or planning a sublet if allowed.

## Verification

You can state the lease term, rent due date, increase rules, deposit refund rules, early exit cost, and top negotiation requests from the written lease.

## Variations

- Room rental: confirm shared-space rules, guest policy, utilities, and who is on the master lease.
- Furnished rental: attach an inventory and condition photos.
- Rent-controlled area: compare lease terms with local required disclosures and limits.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because lease terms affect housing, money, credit, and legal obligations. Do not sign under pressure; unsigned questions are cheaper than signed disputes.
