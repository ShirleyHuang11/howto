## Goal

Your gym membership is canceled under the contract terms, with proof retained and future charges monitored.

## Preconditions

- You have the membership agreement, account number, billing method, and login if there is a portal.
- You know the contract term, notice period, cancellation fee, and required cancellation method.
- You can send written notice if the contract requires email, certified mail, form upload, or in-person signature.
- You have a calendar reminder to watch the next billing cycle.

## Steps

1. **Read the cancellation clause.** Find notice days, minimum term, accepted delivery method, and final billing rule. → *Expect:* you know exactly what the gym says it requires.
2. **Collect account details.** Write your name, membership ID, phone, email, home club, and last four digits of payment card if needed. → *Expect:* staff can locate the membership.
3. **Choose the required channel.** [BRANCH: online form | email | certified mail | in-person desk | phone plus written follow-up] → *Expect:* your method matches the contract.
4. **Send a clear cancellation request.** State that you are canceling, give the effective date allowed by the notice period, and request written confirmation. → *Expect:* the gym receives an unambiguous cancellation.
5. **Return access items if required.** Hand in key fob, locker key, or borrowed equipment and ask for a receipt. → *Expect:* no equipment charge should remain.
6. **Get proof.** Save email replies, form screenshots, postal tracking, staff name, date, and confirmation number. → *Expect:* you can prove when cancellation was submitted.
7. **Check final bill.** Confirm whether one more charge is due because of the notice period. → *Expect:* the final expected charge and date are known.
8. **Monitor continued charges.** Watch the payment method for at least two billing cycles and dispute unauthorized charges promptly. → *Expect:* billing stops after the confirmed final date.

## Decision points

- If still inside a fixed contract, ask about medical, relocation, military, or fee-based cancellation options.
- If the gym says cancellation must be in person, read local law because some places require easier cancellation for online signups.
- If staff offer a freeze instead, accept only if you want a pause rather than cancellation.
- If the gym refuses written confirmation, create your own record by email summarizing the conversation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Notice period missed:** detect another billing date inside the notice window, recover by confirming the true final charge date.
- **F2 Wrong cancellation channel:** detect no confirmation because phone notice was insufficient, recover by sending the required written notice immediately.
- **F3 Continued billing:** detect charges after final date, recover by contacting the gym with proof and disputing through the card issuer if needed.
- **F4 Sales pressure:** detect offers that avoid cancellation, recover by repeating "cancel my membership" and asking for confirmation.
- **F5 Lost proof:** detect no saved evidence, recover by requesting account notes and sending a dated follow-up email.

## Verification

You have written confirmation or delivery proof showing cancellation, the effective final date, and no gym charges after the final allowed billing cycle.

## Variations

- Month-to-month membership: notice period matters more than contract term.
- Annual prepaid membership: ask whether any unused portion is refundable before canceling.
- Corporate or insurance gym plans: cancel through the sponsoring portal as well as the gym if needed.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because recurring billing can continue. Keep proof, avoid giving new payment details during cancellation, and watch statements after the supposed final date.
