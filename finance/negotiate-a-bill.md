## Goal

You ask a provider to lower, credit, or restructure a bill and leave with a documented result.

## Preconditions

- You have the bill, account number, current rate, contract end date, and payment history.
- You have researched competitor rates or promotional prices for comparable service.
- You know the outcome you want, such as a monthly discount, fee waiver, payment plan, or cancellation quote.
- You can spend enough time for hold, transfer, and confirmation.

## Steps

1. **Read the bill line by line.** Identify base rate, fees, taxes, equipment charges, and recent increases. → *Expect:* you know which charge you are challenging.
2. **Research the market rate.** Check competitor offers, new-customer prices, and your provider's current posted plans. → *Expect:* you have a realistic target number.
3. **Prepare a short script.** Write: "I want to keep service, but this bill is too high compared with X. What can you do?" → *Expect:* your ask is specific and calm.
4. **Call the provider.** Use the number on the bill or official website and pass account verification. → *Expect:* the representative can view your account.
5. **Ask for retention or loyalty.** If the first person cannot adjust price, ask for the cancellation, retention, or loyalty team. → *Expect:* you reach someone with discount authority.
6. **Make the ask and wait.** State the target rate, mention competitor pricing, then stop talking while they check options. [BRANCH: discount offered | no discount offered] → *Expect:* you receive a concrete offer or reason.
7. **Confirm total cost and terms.** Ask about taxes, fees, equipment, contract length, expiration date, and autopay requirements. → *Expect:* the monthly all-in amount and conditions are clear.
8. **Get it in writing.** Request an email, chat transcript, confirmation number, or updated bill. → *Expect:* you have proof of the negotiated change.

## Decision points

- If the offer requires a long contract, compare savings against early termination fees.
- If cancellation is credible, ask for the final service date and return rules before agreeing.
- If you cannot pay now, ask for hardship, due-date change, or payment plan instead of only a discount.
- If the representative is unhelpful, end politely and try again later with a different agent.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Discount excludes fees:** detect low advertised rate but same high bill, recover by asking for all-in monthly cost.
- **F2 Verbal promise vanishes:** detect no written confirmation, recover by calling back with date, time, and representative name.
- **F3 Worse plan accepted:** detect lost speed, coverage, channels, or features, recover by comparing plan details before confirming.
- **F4 Contract trap:** detect early termination language, recover by declining or calculating the real break-even.
- **F5 Emotional escalation:** detect arguing or threats, recover by pausing, returning to the script, or ending the call.

## Verification

You have written confirmation showing the new bill amount, effective date, duration, and any contract or autopay conditions.

## Variations

- Medical bills: ask for itemized billing, coding review, financial assistance, or payment plan.
- Utilities: regulated rates may not be negotiable, but fees, plans, and hardship programs may be.
- Subscription services: chat cancellation flows may offer retention discounts without a phone call.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because account data and money are involved. Use official contact numbers, avoid sharing full payment details unnecessarily, and do not agree to terms you have not heard back clearly.
