## Goal

Report identity theft, block new misuse, and create records needed to dispute fraudulent accounts or charges.

## Preconditions

- You have noticed a fraudulent account, charge, debt notice, tax notice, benefits claim, or credit-report entry.
- You can access your email and phone for confirmations.
- You have a secure place to store reports, letters, screenshots, and case numbers.
- You are prepared to contact credit bureaus, financial institutions, and official government reporting sites.

## Steps

1. **Secure core accounts first.** Change passwords for email, banking, and phone-carrier accounts, then sign out unknown sessions. → *Expect:* only your devices remain active.
2. **Freeze your credit.** Place security freezes with the three major credit bureaus or the relevant bureaus in your locale. → *Expect:* each bureau gives a confirmation, PIN, or online account status showing frozen.
3. **File the official identity-theft report.** Use the official government reporting portal for your country, such as IdentityTheft.gov in the United States. → *Expect:* you receive a report, recovery plan, or reference number.
4. **Create an evidence folder.** Save the official report, bureau confirmations, collection letters, account statements, screenshots, and call notes. → *Expect:* every document has a date and source.
5. **Contact affected financial institutions.** Call the number on the back of the card, statement, or official website, not a number from a suspicious message. → *Expect:* each institution opens a fraud case or closes the fraudulent account.
6. **Dispute fraudulent credit entries.** Send disputes to credit bureaus with the identity-theft report and specific account names. → *Expect:* each bureau confirms receipt and investigation timing.
7. **Dispute debts with collectors.** Request debt validation in writing and state that the debt is from identity theft. → *Expect:* the collector must pause or document the claim according to local rules.
8. **Replace compromised cards and account numbers.** Ask issuers to close stolen credentials and issue new numbers. → *Expect:* old cards or account numbers no longer work.
9. **Check tax and benefits exposure.** Review official tax, unemployment, health, or benefits accounts if your national ID was exposed. → *Expect:* no unfamiliar filings or claims appear, or a fraud case is opened.
10. **Set a recovery schedule.** Calendar follow-ups for bureau investigations, bank provisional credits, police reports if needed, and statement reviews. → *Expect:* every open case has a next action date.
11. **Send written confirmations.** Follow phone calls with secure messages or letters summarizing what was agreed. → *Expect:* you have written proof beyond call notes.
12. **Keep monitoring after cleanup.** Review credit reports, statements, and mail for several months. → *Expect:* new suspicious activity is caught quickly.

## Decision points

- New accounts are still being opened → keep freezes in place and add fraud alerts as a visible warning.
- A creditor asks for a police report → file one with the official identity-theft report and bring evidence.
- Your phone number was hijacked → secure the carrier account before relying on SMS codes.
- Government ID was stolen → check official guidance for replacing or flagging that document.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fake reporting site:** detect ads, fees, or unofficial domains, recover by navigating from a government website or known bureau address.
- **F2 Bureau account locked:** detect failed online freeze setup, recover by using mail or phone options with identity documents.
- **F3 Creditor refuses dispute:** detect demands for payment without investigation, recover by sending a written dispute with the official report.
- **F4 Missing records:** detect vague case status with no number, recover by calling back and requesting written confirmation.
- **F5 Repeat fraud:** detect new alerts after freezes, recover by checking whether a smaller bureau, utility, or telecom file also needs a freeze.

## Verification

Credit freezes are active, the official identity-theft report is saved, each fraudulent account or charge has a dispute case number, and follow-up dates are recorded.

## Variations

- United States: IdentityTheft.gov creates an FTC report and recovery plan.
- Minor victim: follow child credit-freeze procedures and request deletion of fraudulent files.
- Tax identity theft: use the official tax agency form or PIN program for your locale.
- Medical identity theft: request records from providers and dispute false treatment or insurance claims.

## Safety & privacy

Identity-theft recovery involves national IDs, account numbers, and police or government records. Use official portals, keep documents encrypted or locked, and do not pay collectors for debts you are disputing as fraud.
