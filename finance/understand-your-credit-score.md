## Goal

You know what your credit score is, which behaviors move it, where to check it for free without harming it, and the real steps that repair it over time (not the myths).

## Preconditions

- Identity details to pass verification with a credit bureau or a free score service (name, address history, sometimes account details).
- Understanding that a "score" is a lender-facing summary of your credit report; the report is the underlying data and matters more.
- Patience: credit is a slow-moving signal, and anything promising an instant fix is a scam.

## Steps

1. **Get your score and report for free.** Use a bureau's statutory free report or a reputable free score service. Checking your own is a "soft" inquiry and never lowers your score. → *Expect:* a current score plus the full report behind it.
2. **Read the report for errors first.** Wrong balances, accounts you never opened, a debt already paid still shown as owing, or an address that is not yours. Errors are common and drag the score. → *Expect:* every account and inquiry recognized, or an error list to dispute (F1).
3. **Learn the levers that actually move it.** In rough order of weight: payment history (pay on time, always), amounts owed (keep balances low relative to limits), length of history, credit mix, and new applications. → *Expect:* you can name your weakest lever from your own report.
4. **Fix utilization, the fastest legitimate lever.** Keep balances well under your limits (a common guideline is under 30%, lower is better). Paying a card down before its statement date lowers the reported balance. → *Expect:* reported balances that are a small fraction of limits.
5. **Automate on-time payments.** One missed payment can dent a score for years; set autopay for at least the minimum on every account so a busy month never becomes a late mark. → *Expect:* autopay active on every credit account.
6. **Space out new applications.** Each formal application is a "hard" inquiry that dips the score slightly; several in a short window reads as distress. Apply only when needed. → *Expect:* no unnecessary hard inquiries planned.
7. **Discard the myths.** Checking your own score does not hurt it; carrying a balance to "build credit" is false and just costs interest; closing an old card can hurt by shortening history and cutting available credit. → *Expect:* you can refute each myth. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not close your oldest account to tidy up; the lost history and limit can drop the score for years.
8. **Re-check periodically and after big steps.** Look again in a few months, and before any major application (mortgage, loan). → *Expect:* a trend line over time, not a single snapshot.

## Decision points

- Score is dragged by one specific error → dispute it with the bureau (F1); a corrected error can lift the score faster than any behavior change.
- Thin or no credit history → you need to build, not repair: a small, fully-paid, on-time account over time establishes history; consider a starter or secured product where available.
- A collection or default is on the report → it is legitimate and cannot simply be erased; pay or settle it, keep proof, and let time reduce its weight (see Safety on "repair" scams).

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Report contains an error:** dispute it directly with the credit bureau (free), attaching evidence; they must investigate and correct or explain within the statutory window. Fixing the report is the highest-leverage repair.
- **F2 Score dropped unexpectedly:** check for a new hard inquiry, a raised balance near a limit, a closed account cutting your total credit, or a missed payment; the report names the cause.
- **F3 Identity theft on the report:** an account you never opened means fraud; freeze your credit with the bureaus, dispute the account, and file the relevant fraud report for your jurisdiction.
- **F4 Tempted by a paid "credit repair" service:** they can do nothing you cannot do free, and cannot remove accurate negative items; anyone promising to erase real debts or boost a score instantly is a scam.

## Verification

You have retrieved your score and full report for free, every account and inquiry on it is recognized (or under dispute), you can name the levers moving your score and refute the common myths, and on-time autopay plus low utilization are in place.

## Variations

- `us`: FICO and VantageScore are the common models; you are entitled to free reports from the three major bureaus, and a security freeze is free.
- `uk`: Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion each score differently; there is no single universal number, so read the report data rather than fixating on one figure.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: credit checks touch your full identity, and the space is thick with scams. Only use official bureau channels or well-known free services, never pay for your own statutory report, and treat any "instant repair" or "pay to remove accurate debts" pitch as fraud. Freezing your credit is a free, reversible defense against new-account identity theft.
