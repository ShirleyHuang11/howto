## Goal

Record and review spending clearly enough to see where money goes and choose specific changes.

## Preconditions

- You have recent bank, card, cash, and payment app records.
- You can choose a tracking method you will actually maintain.
- You have a private place to store financial notes or files.
- You can set aside 15 to 30 minutes each week for review.

## Steps

1. **Pick one method.** [BRANCH: app | spreadsheet | envelope] choose an app for automation, a spreadsheet for control, or envelopes for cash limits. → *Expect:* one system is selected before you start categorizing.
2. **List all spending sources.** Include checking, credit cards, cash, payment apps, subscriptions, and shared bills. → *Expect:* no regular payment path is missing.
3. **Create practical categories.** Use categories such as housing, groceries, transport, eating out, subscriptions, health, debt, gifts, and fun. → *Expect:* every transaction has an obvious place.
4. **Enter the last 30 days.** Import or type transactions, then split mixed purchases only when the split changes decisions. → *Expect:* one month of spending is visible in the chosen system.
5. **Mark fixed and flexible costs.** Separate rent, insurance, and debt from groceries, dining, rides, shopping, and subscriptions. → *Expect:* you can see which costs can change soon.
6. **Find the leaks.** Sort categories from largest to smallest and look for repeated small charges, unused subscriptions, fees, and impulse purchases. → *Expect:* at least one specific spending leak is named.
7. **Set a weekly review.** Put a recurring 15 minute calendar block to update transactions and compare against your target. → *Expect:* the next review has a date and time.
8. **Choose one adjustment.** Pick a measurable change, such as canceling one subscription or setting a dining-out cap. → *Expect:* the tracking leads to an action, not just a report.

## Decision points

- Manual tracking feels too heavy → reduce categories and track weekly totals.
- Automation feels invasive → use a spreadsheet and import CSV files instead of linking accounts.
- Cash spending disappears → keep receipts in one pocket or use a cash envelope log.
- Shared expenses blur the picture → track what you actually pay and reimbursements separately.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missing account:** detect by budget surplus that never appears in the bank, recover by adding the missing card, cash, or app.
- **F2 Over-detailed categories:** detect by avoiding updates because categorizing is tedious, recover by merging categories.
- **F3 Subscription leak hidden:** detect by small monthly charges scattered across cards, recover by searching statements for recurring merchants.
- **F4 Shame spiral:** detect by stopping after seeing bad numbers, recover by tracking without judgment and choosing one next action.

## Verification

At least 30 days of spending is categorized, a weekly review is scheduled, and one named spending leak has a concrete action assigned.

## Variations

- Variable income: track a minimum baseline budget and separate irregular income from regular bills.
- Couples or roommates: agree on categories before reviewing so the data does not become a blame session.
- Debt payoff: add interest and minimum payments as separate categories.

## Safety & privacy

Low direct risk, but financial data is sensitive. Use strong passwords, avoid shared spreadsheets with public links, and do not connect accounts to apps you do not trust.
