## Goal

Collect and organize tax documents so a filer or tax preparer can complete a return with fewer missing forms, duplicated entries, or unsupported deductions.

## Preconditions

- You know the tax year you are organizing.
- You have access to mail, email, payroll portals, bank portals, brokerage portals, and prior-year tax files.
- You have a secure physical folder or encrypted digital storage location.
- You know whether you will self-file or hand documents to a preparer.

## Steps

1. **Create the tax-year workspace.** Make one physical folder or digital folder named with the tax year and taxpayer name. → *Expect:* all documents have one obvious place to go.
2. **Build the income checklist.** List wages, self-employment, interest, dividends, retirement income, unemployment, benefits, rental income, crypto or investment sales, and other taxable income. → *Expect:* each expected income source has a checkbox.
3. **Build the deduction and credit checklist.** List mortgage interest, property tax, charitable gifts, education costs, medical costs, childcare, retirement contributions, business expenses, energy credits, and local deductions. → *Expect:* each possible deduction or credit has a place for proof.
4. **Sort documents by type.** [BRANCH: income | deductions] place income forms in one group and deduction or credit proof in another group, then separate by payer or category. → *Expect:* a preparer can find each document without searching the whole folder.
5. **Scan paper records.** Photograph or scan forms, receipts, letters, and year-end statements at readable quality. → *Expect:* every paper item has a legible backup file.
6. **Name digital files consistently.** Use tax year, category, payer, and form or receipt name in the filename. → *Expect:* files sort predictably and duplicates stand out.
7. **Track missing items.** Add a missing list for expected forms, corrected forms, passwords, charitable receipts, mileage logs, or identity documents. → *Expect:* unresolved items are visible before filing.
8. **Record retention dates.** Note the default retention period you plan to use, commonly at least several years after filing, and longer for property, business, or unresolved tax matters. → *Expect:* you know what can be archived and what must be kept.
9. **Prepare the hand-off packet.** For a preparer, add prior-year return, identification instructions, organizer questionnaire, contact information, direct deposit choice, and signed engagement documents if requested. → *Expect:* the preparer receives one complete packet instead of scattered files.
10. **Review before delivery.** Remove irrelevant documents, mark originals, and list questions that need tax advice. → *Expect:* the folder is complete enough to file or to start the preparer's review.

## Decision points

- A form is expected but not received → check the payer portal, then contact the payer before assuming it is unavailable.
- A corrected form arrives after filing → ask the software provider or preparer whether an amended return is needed.
- A receipt supports a deduction → keep proof of amount, date, payee, and business or tax purpose.
- You are unsure whether something is deductible → include it in a question list instead of guessing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missing income form:** detect by comparing current employers, banks, brokers, and platforms to documents received, recover by downloading or requesting the missing form.
- **F2 Duplicate entry:** detect two copies of the same payer, account, form, and amount, recover by marking one as duplicate.
- **F3 Unreadable scan:** detect blurry numbers or cropped pages, recover by rescanning before discarding or mailing the original.
- **F4 Weak deduction proof:** detect a deduction without receipt, statement, log, or acknowledgment, recover by gathering support or excluding it until confirmed.
- **F5 Late preparer hand-off:** detect a deadline near with documents still missing, recover by asking about an extension and any required payment.

## Verification

The tax-year folder contains income documents, deduction and credit proof, scanned backups, a missing-item list, retention notes, and a preparer hand-off packet when applicable.

## Variations

- Jurisdiction: tax forms, deduction categories, deadlines, extension rules, and retention periods vary by country and local tax agency.
- Self-employed filer: add bookkeeping reports, invoices, mileage logs, asset purchases, and estimated tax payments.
- Household filing jointly: separate documents by taxpayer, dependent, and shared items.
- Paper filing: keep copies of signed returns, schedules, attachments, and mailing proof.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because tax records contain identity numbers, income, account data, and dependent information. Use secure storage, share files only through the preparer's approved channel, and confirm before discarding originals.
