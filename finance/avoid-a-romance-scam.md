## Goal

Recognize and exit a romance scam before sending money, identity documents, account access, or intimate material.

## Preconditions

- You are communicating with someone online who may be asking for trust, secrecy, money, investments, travel help, or emergency support.
- You can save messages and profile images for review.
- You have a trusted friend, family member, or counselor you can ask for outside perspective.
- No payment has to be made immediately.

## Steps

1. **Pause any money request.** Do not send cash, gift cards, crypto, wire transfers, payment-app funds, devices, or account logins. → *Expect:* no value has left your control.
2. **List the red flags.** Note if they never meet, avoid video, claim sudden crises, ask for secrecy, or move fast to love and commitment. → *Expect:* the pattern is written down outside the chat.
3. **Save the profile evidence.** Keep username, phone number, email, photos, payment handles, and message screenshots. → *Expect:* you have records if the person deletes the profile.
4. **Reverse-image search photos.** Search profile pictures and any sent images with a reputable image search tool. → *Expect:* reused photos, model images, or unrelated identities are easier to spot.
5. **Ask for a live verification call.** Request a real-time video call with ordinary conversation and no excuses. → *Expect:* a real person can appear live, while scammers often delay or fake technical issues.
6. **Verify stories out-of-band.** For claims about hospitals, customs, military, business deals, or travel, check through official sources or independent contacts. → *Expect:* the story has evidence beyond the person's messages.
7. **Reject investment links.** Do not open trading, crypto, or banking links they recommend, even if fake profits appear. → *Expect:* you do not create accounts or deposit funds through their referral.
8. **Protect identity and photos.** Do not send ID, bank details, account codes, passwords, or intimate images. → *Expect:* the scammer cannot use new material for fraud or blackmail.
9. **Talk to someone offline.** Show the messages and money request to a trusted person who is not emotionally invested. → *Expect:* you get a reality check before acting.
10. **End contact firmly.** If red flags remain, say you will not send money and stop responding. → *Expect:* the person either respects the boundary or escalates pressure, which confirms risk.
11. **Block and report.** Report the profile to the app, payment service, bank, or fraud agency if money or threats were involved. → *Expect:* the platform records the report and the person cannot message you there.
12. **Act fast if you paid.** Call the bank, card issuer, wire provider, or crypto exchange immediately. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* wire, gift-card, crypto, and payment-app transfers are often unrecoverable, so recovery depends on speed. → *Expect:* a fraud case, recall request, or dispute is opened.

## Decision points

- They ask for money before meeting in person → treat it as a scam until independently proven otherwise.
- They claim a sudden crisis → verify through official or known channels, not numbers or documents they provide.
- They threaten to leak photos → stop paying, save evidence, report blackmail, and ask local authorities or a hotline for help.
- You feel ashamed or isolated → involve a trusted person because secrecy protects the scammer.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fake video call:** detect short, glitchy, muted, or scripted calls, recover by asking for a live action and independent verification.
- **F2 Crypto investment trap:** detect pressure to use a special platform, recover by refusing deposits and checking the domain independently.
- **F3 Gift-card request:** detect requests for card numbers or photos, recover by not buying cards and reporting the account.
- **F4 Blackmail escalation:** detect threats after refusal, recover by preserving evidence, blocking, and reporting instead of paying.
- **F5 Already sent money:** detect completed transfer, recover by contacting the payment provider immediately and filing a fraud report.

## Verification

No money, gift cards, crypto, account access, identity documents, or intimate material have been sent, and suspicious accounts are blocked or reported with evidence saved.

## Variations

- Dating app: report in-app before blocking so moderators can see the conversation.
- Social media: check whether photos and biography are copied from another account.
- Military or offshore worker story: verify through official public facts and never pay for leave, equipment, customs, or travel fees.
- Older adult target: involve a trusted contact or financial institution early.

## Safety & privacy

Romance scams combine emotional pressure and irreversible payments. Do not let secrecy isolate you, do not send money to someone you have not independently verified, and treat threats as evidence to report rather than a reason to keep paying.
