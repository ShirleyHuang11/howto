## Goal

Choose a bank account that fits your habits, minimizes avoidable fees, and provides the access and protections you need.

## Preconditions

- You know whether you need checking, current account, savings, joint account, business account, or a basic account.
- You can estimate monthly deposits, withdrawals, card use, cash needs, and international use.
- You have identity documents required to open an account in your jurisdiction.
- You can compare official fee schedules, not only marketing pages.

## Steps

1. **Define the account job.** Decide what the account must do, such as receive pay, pay bills, hold savings, use cash, or separate spending. → *Expect:* the required account type is clear.
2. **List your habits.** Count expected monthly card purchases, ATM withdrawals, transfers, checks, cash deposits, and overseas transactions. → *Expect:* your likely usage pattern is written down.
3. **Compare monthly fees.** Check maintenance fees, waiver rules, minimum balance, direct deposit requirements, and inactivity fees. → *Expect:* you know the realistic monthly cost.
4. **Check access.** Compare branches, ATMs, cash deposit options, mobile check deposit, phone support, and app reliability. → *Expect:* the account can handle your normal access needs.
5. **Review transaction fees.** Check ATM surcharges, foreign transaction fees, overdraft fees, wire fees, check fees, and transfer limits. → *Expect:* expensive mismatches are identified.
6. **Assess interest and rewards.** Compare rate, balance caps, rewards conditions, and whether requirements fit your habits. → *Expect:* promotional benefits are separated from durable value.
7. **Confirm deposit protection.** Verify the bank or credit union is covered by the relevant deposit protection scheme and note the limit. → *Expect:* you know what amount is protected.
8. **Plan the switch.** List deposits, bill payments, subscriptions, linked wallets, and old account closure steps. → *Expect:* the move will not interrupt essential payments.
9. **Open only after reviewing terms.** Submit the application through the official channel after checking fees, privacy terms, and eligibility. → *Expect:* you receive an approval, denial, or request for documents.

## Decision points

- You often use cash → prioritize ATM and branch access over a slightly higher online rate.
- You keep a low balance → prioritize no monthly fee and low overdraft risk.
- You travel or buy internationally → compare foreign transaction and ATM fees carefully.
- You are denied an account → ask about a basic account, second-chance account, or credit union option.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fee waiver missed:** detect by a monthly fee posting, recover by meeting the waiver rule or switching accounts.
- **F2 ATM mismatch:** detect by repeated out-of-network fees, recover by changing withdrawal habits or account provider.
- **F3 Switching payment failure:** detect by missed bill notice, recover by paying manually and updating the merchant.
- **F4 Promo disappointment:** detect by a rate or reward ending, recover by comparing standard terms and switching if worthwhile.

## Verification

The chosen account's fees, access, interest, protection limit, eligibility, and switching checklist are documented before opening or switching.

## Variations

- `us`: compare FDIC or NCUA coverage, overdraft practices, ATM networks, and account screening reports.
- `uk`: current account switching services, arranged overdrafts, and FSCS protection shape the choice.
- `eu`: IBAN access, SEPA transfers, basic payment account rights, and national deposit guarantee limits vary.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because account choice affects access to wages, bill payment, fees, and personal data. Do not treat a higher interest rate as investment advice, and read official disclosures before moving essential payments.
