## Goal

A jury summons is answered correctly and on time: you have either confirmed your service date, secured a lawful deferral or excusal, and you know exactly where and when to appear.

## Preconditions

- A physical or electronic jury summons naming you, with a juror number, a report date, and a response deadline.
- Photo ID matching the name on the summons.
- Your calendar for the next few months and any documentation for a hardship (medical, caregiving, travel, work).

## Steps

1. **Read the summons top to bottom before doing anything.** Find four things: the response deadline, the report date, the court address, and the juror ID / badge number. → *Expect:* those four items located and written down; the deadline is usually days, not weeks, away.
2. **Verify it is genuine.** Real courts summon by mail or official portal and never demand payment, gift cards, or a Social Security number by phone. → *Expect:* the summons matches the court named on the court's own official website; a phone caller "collecting a fine" is a scam, not the court.
3. **Respond by the stated deadline, in the stated way.** [BRANCH: online portal with the juror ID | phone system | mail-back card] Complete the eligibility questionnaire honestly (residency, citizenship, age, language, prior felony where it disqualifies). → *Expect:* a confirmation number, stamped card, or portal receipt. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* ignoring the summons is a missed legal obligation and can lead to a fine or contempt order; never let the deadline pass silently.
4. **Request a deferral or excusal if you cannot serve on the date.** Deferral moves you to a later date (usually one you propose within a set window); excusal removes you for this cycle. Use the same portal/form and attach any required proof. → *Expect:* a new date issued, or an excusal confirmation; unanswered requests default to the original date, so keep the receipt.
5. **Set up the day-one logistics once your date is fixed.** Confirm the report time (often earlier than court opening), parking or transit, security screening rules, and the standby vs. in-person question. Many systems require a call-in or portal check the evening before. → *Expect:* you know whether you actually report, and by when, the night before.
6. **Arrange work and care coverage.** Notify your employer in writing and keep the summons copy; most jurisdictions legally protect jury leave. Line up childcare or eldercare for a potentially full day. → *Expect:* employer acknowledgment in hand; no dependent left uncovered.
7. **Report on the day with the right kit.** Bring the summons, photo ID, badge number, a book, chargers, and small cash. Leave prohibited items (large bags, some electronics, anything weapon-like) at home; security will turn you away with them. → *Expect:* you clear screening, check in with your juror number, and receive seating instructions.

## Decision points

- Report date lands on a genuine hardship (surgery, exams, non-refundable travel) → request deferral early with documentation; late requests are often refused.
- You moved out of the court's jurisdiction → that is usually a disqualification; respond and state the new address rather than ignoring it.
- Summons says "on call / standby" → do not travel to court until the evening check-in tells you to; you may never be called in.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missed the response deadline:** call the court's jury office immediately and explain; courts routinely reset first-time misses but not silent no-shows.
- **F2 Deferral request went unanswered:** assume the original date still stands and appear, or call to confirm; do not treat silence as approval.
- **F3 Scam "jury fine" call demanding payment:** hang up; no court collects fines by phone or gift card. Report it and respond only through the official portal.
- **F4 Turned away at security for a prohibited item:** step out, stow the item (car, locker, home) and re-enter; keep enough time buffer that this does not make you late.

## Verification

You hold a dated confirmation (number, stamped card, or portal receipt) of your response, you know your exact report date and time (or standby status), and your employer and dependents are covered for that day.

## Variations

- `us`: rules vary by state and federal vs. county court; the summons names which. One-day-or-one-trial systems are common. Jurors are paid a small daily stipend plus mileage.
- `uk`: Crown Court summons via the Jury Central Summoning Bureau; you reply within 7 days and can defer once within 12 months.
- Non-citizens or non-residents summoned in error still must respond and declare ineligibility rather than discard the notice.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: it is a legal obligation with penalties for non-response, and the questionnaire asks for identity details. Give those only through the court's official channel. The summons itself proves your obligation and your leave rights, so keep a copy until service is complete.
