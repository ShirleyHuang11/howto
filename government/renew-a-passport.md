## Goal

Your passport is renewed: application submitted with compliant photo and payment, old passport handled per the rules, and the new one in your hands before you need it.

## Preconditions

- Your current (or expired) passport, and knowledge of its expiry date.
- No trip inside the processing window — check *today* whether upcoming travel collides with standard processing times (weeks to months, varying by country and season). Colliding → the expedited path decision below.
- Your country's official passport authority website found — the **.gov**-style official domain, not a lookalike "passport help" service that charges for free forms.

## Steps

1. **Confirm you're a renewal, not a first-time case.** Renewal usually requires: previous passport available, issued as an adult, within a validity window, name unchanged (or with proof of change). Fail any → the first-time/replacement process, different recipe. → *Expect:* your case matches the renewal criteria list on the official site.
2. **Read the current requirements from the official source.** Form number/online portal, photo spec, fee, and whether your country renews by mail, online, or in-person appointment. Rules change; last year's knowledge is stale. → *Expect:* a checklist written from today's official page.
3. **Get a compliant photo.** The spec is strict (size, background color, neutral expression, no glasses in many countries, recency ≤6 months). A photo service that knows passport specs beats DIY; if DIY, print at exact size and check against the template. → *Expect:* photos matching every line of the spec — photo rejection is the #1 processing delay.
4. **Complete the application.** Online portal or the exact current form, fields matching your documents precisely (name as in the old passport, or with the name-change document attached). → *Expect:* application validated/printed with no blank required fields.
5. **Pay the fee via the official channel.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* fees are typically non-refundable even for rejected applications — recheck the photo and fields once more before paying. → *Expect:* payment receipt/reference number saved.
6. **Submit per your country's channel.** [BRANCH: mail → tracked envelope containing exactly the checklist items (old passport if required, photos, form, receipt) | in-person appointment → book the slot, bring originals | online → upload and confirm] → *Expect:* submission confirmation with a tracking/application number.
7. **Track and receive.** Monitor the application status page; the new passport arrives by secure mail or pickup. **Sign it** and verify every printed detail (name spelling, birth date, sex, dates) on day one. → *Expect:* correct new passport signed and stored; old passport returned canceled (corner-cut) where that's the practice.
8. **Update dependents of the passport number.** Travel bookings made with the old number, trusted-traveler programs, visa applications in flight. → *Expect:* no active booking still pointing at the dead number.

## Decision points

- Travel sooner than standard processing → the expedited/urgent service (extra fee, sometimes in-person only, proof of travel required) — decide now, not at the anxiety point later.
- Passport also serves as your ID while it's away → some countries let you keep the old one until pickup; otherwise plan around the documentless window (national ID, driving licence coverage).
- Visas still valid in the old passport → many countries let you travel with old+new passports together; verify for the specific visa before assuming — and never cut or discard an old passport holding a live visa.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Application rejected (photo, field error):** the notice states the defect — fix exactly that and resubmit; fee handling varies (sometimes preserved for a window, hence speed).
- **F2 Status stuck past the stated processing time:** use the authority's inquiry channel with your application number; for travel-imminent cases, most countries have an escalation path with proof of travel.
- **F3 New passport has an error:** report immediately via the correction process — typically free when it's their error, and far cheaper before its first use than at a border.
- **F4 Old passport lost mid-process (or before starting):** stop — that's the lost/stolen replacement process (a police/declaration step enters), not renewal; report the loss first, it protects you from misuse.

## Verification

The new passport is in hand, signed, every biographic detail verified correct, validity ~10 (or your country's) years out, the old one canceled-and-returned or accounted for, live visas preserved, and no upcoming booking references the old number.

## Variations

- `us`: DS-82 mail renewal or online portal for eligible adults; `uk`: online-first with a digital photo code; `cn`: appointment at entry-exit administration halls; expats everywhere: your embassy/consulate runs the same process on slower clocks — start earlier.
- Child passports rarely "renew" — they're fresh applications with both-parents consent rules; different recipe.

## Safety & privacy

High risk is the identity document itself: apply only through official domains, ship only via tracked mail, and treat the application number like a credential. The passport is the master key of identity theft — its whereabouts should be boring and known at every step of this process.
