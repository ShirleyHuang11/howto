## Goal

Apply for a marriage license through the correct local authority so the ceremony can be legally recorded.

## Preconditions

- Both parties know the jurisdiction where the license must be obtained or used.
- Both parties can provide required identification and prior-marriage documents if applicable.
- You know the planned ceremony date and officiant or ceremony location.
- You can pay the application fee by an accepted method.

## Steps

1. **Find the issuing office.** Use the official city, county, state, province, or national civil registry site for the ceremony location. → *Expect:* the correct licensing authority and application method are identified.
2. **Check eligibility rules.** Review age, consent, relationship, residency, prior marriage, and witness requirements. → *Expect:* both parties know whether they can apply.
3. **Gather identity documents.** Collect government ID, birth certificate, proof of address, divorce decree, death certificate, or translation if required. → *Expect:* required documents are ready.
4. **Schedule or start the application.** [BRANCH: online pre-application | in-person application] complete the official form or book an office appointment. → *Expect:* the office confirms submission or appointment details.
5. **Attend together if required.** Both parties appear, sign, swear or affirm information, and present documents as the authority requires. → *Expect:* the clerk accepts the application or lists missing items.
6. **Pay the fee.** Pay only through accepted official methods and keep the receipt. → *Expect:* the payment is recorded by the issuing office.
7. **Check waiting period and validity.** Note when the license becomes usable and when it expires. → *Expect:* the ceremony date falls inside the valid window.
8. **Give instructions to the officiant.** Provide the license, return deadline, witness rules, and filing address to the officiant. → *Expect:* the officiant knows how to complete and return it.
9. **Order certificates after filing.** Request certified marriage certificates once the completed license is recorded. → *Expect:* you know how and when to obtain proof of marriage.

## Decision points

- Ceremony is in another jurisdiction → follow the rules where the marriage will be solemnized or registered.
- Prior marriage ended → bring the exact divorce, annulment, or death proof required.
- Name change is planned → check whether the marriage certificate is enough or a separate process is needed.
- Deadline is tight → verify waiting period, appointment availability, and license validity before booking the ceremony.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong office:** detect by clerk refusal or website mismatch, recover by contacting the authority for the ceremony location.
- **F2 Missing document:** detect by application rejection, recover by obtaining certified copies or approved translations.
- **F3 License expired:** detect by ceremony date outside validity, recover by applying again and paying any new fee.
- **F4 Officiant filing failure:** detect by no recorded certificate, recover by contacting the officiant and issuing office promptly.

## Verification

The license is issued or appointment is confirmed, the waiting period and expiration date are known, and the officiant has filing instructions.

## Variations

- `us`: marriage licenses are usually county or local, with state-specific waiting periods, expiry windows, and officiant rules.
- `uk`: notice of marriage or civil partnership has distinct residency, venue, and timing rules.
- `eu`: civil registration, church ceremony recognition, residency, and document translation rules vary by country.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because legal status, identity records, and fees are involved. Use official government sources and avoid sharing identity documents with unofficial wedding vendors.
