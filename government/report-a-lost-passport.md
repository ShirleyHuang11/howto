## Goal

Report a lost or stolen passport promptly, reduce fraud risk, and start the correct replacement process.

## Preconditions

- You know the issuing country of the passport.
- You have any available passport number, copy, photo, or identity document.
- You know whether the passport is lost, stolen, damaged, or possibly held by a third party.
- You know whether you have upcoming international travel.

## Steps

1. **Act immediately.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* reporting usually cancels the passport, so confirm it is truly lost or stolen before filing. → *Expect:* you are ready for the old passport to become invalid.
2. **Use the official reporting channel.** Find the issuing government's passport agency, embassy, consulate, or online lost-passport form. → *Expect:* you are on an official government site or phone line.
3. **Complete the loss report.** Provide name, date of birth, passport details if known, where and when it was lost, and theft details if applicable. → *Expect:* the agency accepts the report or gives next steps.
4. **Record the confirmation.** Save the report number, date, and agency contact details. → *Expect:* you can prove the passport was reported.
5. **File a police report if needed.** For theft, local legal requirement, insurance, or embassy instructions, report to local police. → *Expect:* you have a police report number if required.
6. **Assess travel impact.** Check airline, border, visa, and entry rules for any upcoming trip. → *Expect:* you know whether travel must be postponed or emergency documents are needed.
7. **Apply for replacement.** [BRANCH: standard replacement | urgent travel document] submit the normal replacement application or contact an embassy for urgent travel. → *Expect:* the replacement route and timeline are clear.
8. **Replace linked visas if necessary.** Contact relevant visa or residence permit authorities if the lost passport contained valid visas or permits. → *Expect:* affected documents are listed with replacement steps.
9. **Monitor for misuse.** Watch identity accounts, credit files where relevant, and immigration notices after loss. → *Expect:* suspicious activity can be detected early.

## Decision points

- You find the passport after reporting → do not use it unless the issuing authority explicitly says it is valid.
- You are abroad → contact your embassy or consulate and ask about emergency travel documents.
- A visa was inside → check the issuing country's replacement or transfer rules before travel.
- Travel is within days → use urgent passport or emergency document procedures, not normal processing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Unofficial report site:** detect by extra fees or non-government branding, recover by using the issuing government's site.
- **F2 Passport later found:** detect by finding the booklet after cancellation, recover by following agency instructions and applying for replacement.
- **F3 Visa disruption:** detect by missing visa tied to old passport, recover by contacting the visa issuer before departure.
- **F4 Identity misuse:** detect by alerts or unfamiliar accounts, recover by filing fraud reports and freezing credit where available.

## Verification

The lost or stolen passport report is accepted by the issuing authority, confirmation is saved, and the correct replacement or emergency travel process is started.

## Variations

- `us`: lost or stolen passports are reported to the Department of State, and urgent travel may require an agency appointment.
- `uk`: HM Passport Office handles cancellation and replacement, with separate emergency travel document help abroad.
- `eu`: reporting, police involvement, and emergency travel documents are handled by each member state's passport authority.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because a passport can be used for identity fraud and travel. Report through official channels, protect copies, and do not travel on a passport after it has been reported lost.
