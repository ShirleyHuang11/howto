## Goal

You hold a confirmed appointment at the correct government office for your specific service, at a workable date and time, with a checklist of the exact documents that office requires so the visit succeeds in one trip.

## Preconditions

- A clear service goal (renew a license, submit a visa application, register a birth, tax matter), since offices are organized by service, not by building.
- The official booking channel identified (the agency's government domain or its listed phone line), not a third-party site that charges to book free appointments.
- Any reference or case number already assigned to you, if this continues an existing matter.

## Steps

1. **Identify the exact office and service.** Confirm which agency and which branch handles your specific need and your catchment area; the right service at the wrong office is a wasted trip. → *Expect:* a named office and service that matches your goal and location.
2. **Find the official booking channel.** Use the agency's own site or listed number; ignore paid intermediaries that resell free slots. → *Expect:* an official booking page or phone line for that service.
3. **Read the document checklist before booking.** Most agencies publish exactly what to bring; note originals versus copies, photos, fees, and proof-of-address rules. → *Expect:* a written list of every required document for your service.
4. **Choose the booking interface.** [BRANCH: online self-service portal | phone line (for services not bookable online, or if you need accommodations) | in-person queue ticket where no appointment system exists] → *Expect:* a booking method appropriate to the service.
5. **Hunt for a slot, and widen the search if empty.** [BRANCH: slots available → book the best time | fully booked → check nearby branches, look for a released-slot refresh, or set an alert] → *Expect:* an offered date and time, or a concrete plan to catch a released slot.
6. **Book a realistic time with travel and buffer.** Avoid the first slot if you cannot reliably arrive early; leave margin for security and check-in. → *Expect:* a held slot at a time you can actually make.
7. **Confirm and capture the booking.** Save the confirmation number, QR code, or email, and add it to your calendar with the address and document checklist attached. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* some agencies penalize no-shows with a cooldown before you can rebook, so cancel through the official channel if plans change rather than skipping. → *Expect:* a booking reference stored with all visit details.
8. **Assemble documents the day before.** Work the checklist item by item, originals plus any required copies, in one folder. → *Expect:* every checklist item physically present and organized.
9. **Attend with margin.** Arrive early, bring the confirmation and ID, and keep the reference number accessible. → *Expect:* you are checked in against your appointment and seen.

## Decision points

- No slots anywhere within your deadline → check whether a walk-in or emergency service exists, or whether the deadline itself can be extended through the agency.
- Service turns out to be handled online end-to-end → skip the appointment entirely; do not travel for something the portal completes.
- Booking site demands payment to "reserve" a free government service → it is a third-party reseller; back out and find the official channel.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 All slots full:** check released-slot patterns (agencies often drop new slots at a fixed time or after cancellations), try adjacent offices, and use any waitlist or alert feature.
- **F2 Turned away for a missing document:** the checklist was incomplete or misread; get the exact list from the officer, rebook, and bring everything next time.
- **F3 Booked the wrong service or office:** cancel through the official channel to free the slot, then rebook the correct one; do not just no-show.
- **F4 System error or lost confirmation:** re-check the portal with your reference or the email; call the official line before assuming the booking failed.

## Verification

You have a confirmation reference for the correct service at the correct office and a workable time, plus a complete document checklist for that service assembled before the visit.

## Variations

- `us`: DMV, Social Security, USCIS, and passport agencies each run separate booking systems; some release next-day slots at a set hour, which is the practical way to get a scarce appointment.
- High-demand consular or visa appointments: slots can vanish in seconds; log in early, have details pre-filled, and refresh at the known release time.
- Areas with no online system: an in-person queue-ticket or call-center booking is the norm; arrive at opening for same-day tickets.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Guard the reference number and any personal details entered during booking; use only the official domain or phone line, since fake government-appointment sites exist to harvest identity data and booking fees.
