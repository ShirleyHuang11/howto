## Goal

Submit a complete application for the right government benefit program and know how to track, correct, or appeal the result.

## Preconditions

- A description of the need, such as food, housing, unemployment, disability, health coverage, childcare, or energy help.
- Identity documents for each applicant.
- Proof of address, income, household size, and relevant expenses.
- Contact information that you can receive mail, email, or phone calls at.
- Time to respond to follow-up requests.

## Steps

1. **Identify the right program.** Use an official benefit finder, agency website, or community benefits navigator. → *Expect:* you have the exact program name and administering agency.
2. **Read eligibility rules.** Check income limits, residency, age, disability, work history, household size, and immigration or citizenship requirements. → *Expect:* you know which rules you appear to meet and which are uncertain.
3. **Build a document pack.** Gather ID, proof of residence, recent pay stubs or benefit letters, bank statements if required, lease or utility bills, and medical or job records if relevant. → *Expect:* every required document is named and copied.
4. **Choose the application path.** [BRANCH: online | phone | paper | in-person] Pick the fastest official path that you can complete accurately. → *Expect:* you have the form, portal account, appointment, or phone number.
5. **Complete the application.** Answer every required question, using "0" or "none" only where true. → *Expect:* the form has no blank required fields or unexplained mismatches.
6. **Upload or attach documents.** Label files with applicant name, document type, and date before upload. → *Expect:* the portal or worker confirms each document is received.
7. **Submit and save proof.** Review the summary, then submit. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* false statements can trigger repayment, denial, or penalties, so correct estimates before submitting. → *Expect:* you receive a confirmation number, receipt, or stamped copy.
8. **Track follow-up requests.** Check mail, email, texts, portal messages, and voicemail during the processing window. → *Expect:* every request has a due date and response plan.
9. **Respond to the decision.** [BRANCH: approved | denied | more information needed] Follow the notice instructions for enrollment, correction, or appeal. → *Expect:* you know the next required action and deadline.

## Decision points

- You may qualify for several programs → apply for the broad screening or combined application first.
- Income changes month to month → use the agency's rule for averaging or reporting recent income.
- A deadline is close → submit the core application first, then provide documents within the allowed follow-up window.
- You cannot access the portal → ask for phone, paper, or in-person assistance.
- The denial seems wrong → request the case file and file an appeal before the deadline.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong program:** detect when the agency says it does not cover your need; recover by asking for a referral to the correct program.
- **F2 Missing document:** detect a pending status or deficiency notice; recover by uploading the named document and saving proof.
- **F3 Portal lockout:** detect repeated login or identity-proofing failure; recover by calling the agency and requesting account reset or alternate submission.
- **F4 Missed interview:** detect a closure notice or missed call; recover by requesting rescheduling as soon as possible.
- **F5 Denial deadline near:** detect appeal rights with a short deadline; recover by filing the appeal first and adding documents later if allowed.

## Verification

The agency has issued a confirmation, case number, or stamped receipt for the benefit application, and you have the next deadline recorded.

## Variations

- `disability`: medical records and provider statements usually drive eligibility.
- `unemployment`: weekly certifications may be required after the initial claim.
- `food-assistance`: household purchase and meal-sharing rules can change eligibility.
- `health-coverage`: choosing a plan may be a separate step after approval.
- `emergency-benefit`: proof of crisis, shutoff, eviction, or disaster impact may be required.

## Safety & privacy

Benefit applications contain sensitive identity, income, medical, household, and immigration information. Use official portals, keep copies in a private folder, and never pay an unofficial helper who promises guaranteed approval.
