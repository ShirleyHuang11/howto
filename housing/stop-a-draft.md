## Goal

Obvious air leaks around doors, windows, and outlets are found and reduced with low-cost fixes first.

## Preconditions

- You have a calm-weather day or can compare drafts while heating or cooling is running.
- You have painter's tape, weatherstripping, door sweep, outlet gaskets, caulk, draft stopper, or towels as available.
- You can safely use a candle only away from curtains, paper, and gas odors.
- You know whether you rent and what changes require permission.

## Steps

1. **Make a draft map.** Walk exterior walls, doors, windows, baseboards, outlets, and attic hatches with your hand. → *Expect:* cold or moving-air spots are marked with tape.
2. **Confirm carefully.** Use a hand, tissue, incense, or candle flame held away from combustibles. → *Expect:* the indicator moves near real leaks.
3. **Start with door bottoms.** Install or adjust a door sweep, or place a draft stopper at the threshold. → *Expect:* light and air under the door are reduced.
4. **Seal door and window edges.** Apply adhesive weatherstripping where the sash or door closes against the frame. → *Expect:* the door or window closes snugly without forcing.
5. **Lock windows fully.** Close and latch each window because locks often pull seals tight. → *Expect:* the gap at the meeting rail shrinks.
6. **Treat outlet drafts.** Turn off power to the circuit if removing covers, then install foam gaskets behind exterior-wall outlet plates. → *Expect:* air movement at the plate decreases.
7. **Use temporary cheap wins.** [BRANCH: renter-safe tape or film | permanent caulk or trim repair] cover seasonal leaks without blocking exits. → *Expect:* the room feels less drafty without damage.
8. **Recheck the map.** Repeat the hand or tissue test and remove tape from fixed spots. → *Expect:* remaining drafts are fewer and easier to prioritize.

## Decision points

- If a candle flame leans strongly near a gas appliance, stop and check for combustion or gas safety concerns.
- If the door will not latch after weatherstripping, use thinner material rather than forcing the strike.
- If water damage or rot surrounds a draft, repair the building problem before sealing it closed.
- If renting, prefer removable film, draft stoppers, and written permission for caulk or sweeps.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Weatherstrip too thick:** detect door bounce or failed latch, recover by replacing with thinner strip or adjusting placement.
- **F2 Caulk blocks drainage:** detect window weep holes covered, recover by removing caulk from drainage paths.
- **F3 Draft persists at outlet:** detect air after gasket install, recover by sealing larger wall gaps with approved fire-safe materials.
- **F4 Temporary film falls:** detect loose plastic, recover by cleaning the frame and applying fresh tape with heat-shrink if suitable.
- **F5 Hidden moisture:** detect condensation or mildew after sealing, recover by improving ventilation and investigating leaks.

## Verification

Previously marked draft points show little or no tissue movement during the same conditions, and all doors and windows still open, close, and latch.

## Variations

- Cold climates: window film and door sweeps give large seasonal gains for low cost.
- Hot climates: the same sealing keeps cooled air in and humid outside air out.
- Older houses: expect many small leaks, so prioritize the strongest drafts first.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep flames away from fabric and gas odors, do not seal required ventilation, and keep exits operable after adding sweeps or stoppers.
