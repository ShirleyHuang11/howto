## Goal

Shut off gas at the meter only during a suspected gas emergency or when directed by the gas utility or emergency services.

## Preconditions

- You smell gas, hear gas escaping, suspect damage, or have been told to shut gas off.
- You can reach the outdoor meter without entering a dangerous area.
- You have a wrench if the meter valve does not have a hand lever.

## Steps

1. **Decide whether shutoff is appropriate.** Use the meter valve only for suspected gas release, damaged piping, earthquake response, or utility instruction. → *Expect:* you have a clear emergency reason.
2. **Do not create ignition sources.** Avoid flames, smoking, phones near the odor, light switches, doorbells, plugs, and garage door openers. → *Expect:* nothing electrical or flame-producing is operated near the gas.
3. **Evacuate people first if gas odor is strong.** Move everyone outside and upwind if possible before working near the meter. → *Expect:* people are away from the building.
4. **Find the meter shutoff valve.** Look for the valve on the gas pipe feeding into the meter. → *Expect:* you see a valve tab or lever aligned with the pipe when open.
5. **Turn the valve one quarter turn.** Use a wrench if needed and turn until the tab or lever is perpendicular to the pipe. → *Expect:* the valve is crosswise to the pipe, indicating closed.
6. **Leave the area.** Do not re-enter to check appliances or open windows if the gas smell was inside. → *Expect:* you are outside at a safe distance.
7. **Call the gas emergency line or fire service from outside.** Use a mobile phone only after leaving the suspected gas area, or call from a neighbor's phone. → *Expect:* trained responders are notified.
8. **Do not turn gas back on yourself.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* restoring gas without leak checks and appliance relighting can cause explosion or carbon monoxide danger; only the utility or qualified professional should restore service. → *Expect:* the meter remains off until cleared.

## Decision points

- Gas odor is faint but persistent → leave and call the gas emergency line from outside.
- Valve is inaccessible or you must enter the building to reach it → evacuate and wait for responders.
- You hear hissing gas or see damaged piping → evacuate farther and call emergency services.
- Utility tells you not to touch the meter → follow utility instructions.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Valve will not turn:** detect no movement with moderate wrench pressure → stop, evacuate, and tell the gas utility.
- **F2 Wrong valve closed:** detect appliance branch valve moved instead of meter valve → leave it closed and report details to the utility.
- **F3 Gas smell remains outside:** detect odor near the meter after shutoff → move farther away and wait for emergency responders.
- **F4 Someone re-enters:** detect a person going back inside → call them out without entering yourself.

## Verification

The meter valve is perpendicular to the pipe, everyone is outside, the gas emergency line or fire service has been called, and gas service is not restored by the occupant.

## Variations

- Propane tank: close the tank service valve clockwise and call the propane supplier or fire service.
- Apartment: evacuate and call building management and the gas emergency line; do not search shared mechanical rooms during an odor event.
- Earthquake area: follow local emergency guidance for when meter shutoff is recommended.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from explosion, fire, and carbon monoxide. Never use switches or phones inside a suspected gas atmosphere, and never restore gas service without a qualified leak check.
