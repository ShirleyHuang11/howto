## Goal

You relocate your household to a new home on schedule, with belongings packed and labelled, transport arranged, utilities and address switched, and a first-night box that means you can eat and sleep the moment you arrive.

## Preconditions

- A confirmed move date and access to both properties on that date.
- A rough inventory of what you own and whether you will hire movers or use friends.
- Packing supplies: boxes, tape, markers, and wrapping for fragile items.

## Steps

1. **Build a backward timeline from move day.** Six weeks out: book transport. Three weeks: notify utilities, mail redirect, and change of address. One week: pack non-essentials. Move day: essentials only. → *Expect:* a dated checklist, not a vague intention.
2. **Decide movers versus friends honestly.** [BRANCH: professional movers (heavy/large home, tight schedule, insurance matters) | friends and a rented van (small home, budget, flexible timing)] → *Expect:* a booked mover with written quote, or a confirmed van and a headcount of helpers.
3. **Declutter before you pack, not after.** Sell/donate/bin anything you would not pay to transport. → *Expect:* fewer boxes than you first estimated.
4. **Pack room by room and label every box on the top and one side.** Write the destination room and contents, and mark fragile boxes clearly. Number boxes and keep a master list. → *Expect:* no unlabelled box; the count matches your list.
5. **Pack a first-night box and keep it with you, not on the truck.** Phone chargers, medications, toiletries, a change of clothes, bedding, basic tools, snacks, kettle/mug, toilet paper, and important documents. → *Expect:* one clearly marked box that never leaves your side.
6. **Protect fragiles and heavy items properly.** Plates on edge not flat, glasses wrapped individually, heavy items in small boxes so they stay liftable. → *Expect:* boxes you can carry without the bottom failing.
7. **Take final meter readings and photos at the old home; do the same at the new one.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* leaving without readings can leave you billed for the next occupant's usage. → *Expect:* dated meter photos for both properties.
8. **Do the load-out and a final sweep.** Load heavy first and low, check every cupboard and the loft, then hand back keys. → *Expect:* an empty old home and a full, secured vehicle.
9. **At the new home, direct boxes to their labelled rooms and set up beds first.** → *Expect:* boxes in the right rooms and somewhere to sleep before nightfall.

## Decision points

- Move date clashes with lease overlap or completion timing → keep a few days' overlap if you can afford it; a same-day chain is fragile.
- Large or awkward items (piano, wardrobes, appliances) → these tip the decision toward professional movers regardless of home size.
- Long-distance or international move → add customs, insurance, and transit time; the timeline stretches by weeks.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Movers cancel or run late:** confirm 48 hours before and have a backup van contact; keep the first-night box so a delay is survivable.
- **F2 Boxes unlabelled or mislabelled:** you cannot find essentials → the first-night box covers the gap while you locate the master list.
- **F3 Damage in transit:** breakage on arrival → photograph before unpacking further and claim on the mover's insurance within their deadline.
- **F4 Utilities not switched:** arrive to no power/water → readings and prior notice let the supplier restore quickly; the first-night box and a torch bridge the gap.

## Verification

Your belongings are in the new home in their labelled rooms, the vehicle and helpers are released, meter readings for both properties are recorded, utilities and address are switched, and the first-night box gives you food, bedding, and documents without unpacking anything else.

## Variations

- Renting: coordinate move-out inspection at the old place and move-in inspection at the new one (see do-a-move-in-inspection).
- With children or pets: arrange care for move day; chaos and open doors are the main hazard.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: physical strain and the money tied up in deposits and mover fees. Lift with your legs and keep boxes light enough to carry, get mover quotes and insurance in writing, and guard the box holding your documents (ID, contracts) throughout the move.
