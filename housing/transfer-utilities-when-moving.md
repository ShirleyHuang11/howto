## Goal

Close or transfer utilities at the old home and open utilities at the new home with correct dates, readings, and billing.

## Preconditions

- You know the old move-out date, new move-in date, and both full addresses.
- You have current supplier names, account numbers, login details, and payment method.
- You know which services apply: electricity, gas, water, trash, internet, or district heating.
- You can access meters at both properties on the relevant dates.

## Steps

1. **Make a utility list for both homes.** Record supplier, account number, meter type, billing cycle, and whether the service transfers or closes. → *Expect:* no utility is handled from memory.
2. **Schedule old-place closure.** Tell each supplier the move-out date, forwarding address, and final billing instructions. → *Expect:* each old account has a planned final date.
3. **Open new-place accounts.** Contact current or chosen suppliers with the move-in date and address. [BRANCH: single required supplier | competitive supplier choice] register directly or compare tariffs first. → *Expect:* each new account has a start date and reference number.
4. **Plan a small overlap.** Keep essential service active until the final day at the old place and starting the first day at the new place. → *Expect:* cleaning, handover, and move-in are not blocked by a shutoff.
5. **Take final old readings.** Photograph gas, electric, and water meters on the day you leave, with meter serials if visible. → *Expect:* final usage is separated from the next occupant's use.
6. **Take opening new readings.** Photograph the new meters before heavy use starts. → *Expect:* opening readings match the date you became responsible.
7. **Submit every reading.** Enter readings online or by phone and save confirmations. → *Expect:* accounts show final or opening readings rather than estimates.
8. **Confirm billing and payment.** Check autopay, tariff, paperless billing, deposits, and final bills. → *Expect:* no old account continues billing and no new account is on an unknown default.
9. **Keep a utility folder.** Store confirmations, photos, account numbers, and final bills together. → *Expect:* any future dispute can be answered quickly.

## Decision points

- Utility is landlord-managed → get written confirmation that it is included or billed through rent.
- Meter is inaccessible → request access from the landlord, building manager, or supplier before handover.
- Smart meter reports automatically → still photograph the display or account reading at move-in and move-out.
- Final bill seems estimated → challenge it using the dated final meter photo.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Old account keeps billing:** detect charges after move-out → send closure confirmation and final meter photo.
- **F2 New account starts late:** detect no account or supplier rejection → contact the incumbent supplier and ask for move-in registration.
- **F3 Reading entered wrong:** detect mismatch with photo or bill → submit a correction with the dated image.
- **F4 Supplier cannot find address:** detect rejected postcode or unit → use meter serial number and previous occupant or landlord supplier details.
- **F5 No service on move-in:** detect outage at arrival → call the utility emergency or distributor line, not only the retailer.

## Verification

Each old utility has a closure confirmation or final bill, each new utility has an active account from the correct date, and all final and opening readings are saved with photos.

## Variations

- `rental`: water, trash, or heat may be landlord-billed rather than supplier-billed.
- `sale-purchase`: coordinate completion date with both real estate teams and utility providers.
- `prepaid-meter`: transfer top-up responsibility and recover any credit by supplier process.
- `roommate-change`: separate utility transfer from lease transfer and agree cost splits in writing.

## Safety & privacy

Utility accounts contain identity and payment data. Use supplier websites or known phone numbers. Medium risk comes from billing disputes, missed payments, service shutoff, and gas or electrical faults that require emergency handling.
