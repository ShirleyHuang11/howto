## Goal

Reduce the chance that water pipes freeze and burst during freezing weather or while you are away.

## Preconditions

- Freezing temperatures are forecast or the home has vulnerable pipes.
- You can access exposed pipes, cabinets, thermostats, and outdoor hose connections.
- You have pipe insulation, covers, or basic supplies if needed.

## Steps

1. **Identify vulnerable pipes.** Check exterior walls, crawlspaces, attics, garages, basements, and under-sink cabinets. → *Expect:* exposed or poorly heated pipe runs are listed.
2. **Disconnect outdoor hoses.** Remove hoses and drain outdoor spigots if they have shutoffs. → *Expect:* trapped water is not held against the faucet.
3. **Insulate exposed pipes.** Fit foam sleeves or approved heat tape according to manufacturer instructions. → *Expect:* vulnerable pipe sections are covered without gaps.
4. **Seal cold air leaks.** Close vents, repair gaps, and block drafts near pipes without blocking required appliance combustion air. → *Expect:* cold air no longer blows directly on plumbing.
5. **Keep heat on.** Set the thermostat to a safe minimum, commonly at least 55 °F or 13 °C unless local guidance differs. → *Expect:* interior areas with plumbing remain above freezing.
6. **Open cabinet doors.** Open doors under sinks on exterior walls so room air reaches pipes. → *Expect:* warmer air circulates around supply lines.
7. **Drip taps during severe freezes.** Let a vulnerable faucet drip cold water slowly, especially at the farthest fixture. → *Expect:* water movement and pressure relief reduce freezing risk.
8. **Prepare before travel.** Leave heat on, open vulnerable cabinets, shut off and drain water if appropriate, and ask someone to check the home. → *Expect:* the home has active freeze protection while unoccupied.
9. **Know the water main location.** Tag the shutoff so anyone checking the home can close it quickly. → *Expect:* emergency shutoff can be found in seconds.

## Decision points

- Heat will be off or unreliable → shut off the water main and drain pipes, or arrange professional winterization.
- Pipes are in unheated crawlspaces or attics → use insulation and approved heat cable, or call a plumber.
- You have a fire sprinkler system → ask the installer or fire protection professional before shutting water off.
- Local utility issues a freeze alert → follow local drip and conservation guidance.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Heat tape misused:** detect overlapping, damaged, or wrong-type cable → unplug it and replace with listed heat tape installed as directed.
- **F2 Faucet drip stops:** detect no flow during freeze → suspect frozen pipe and follow a thawing procedure.
- **F3 Thermostat set too low:** detect cold rooms near plumbing → raise heat and open interior doors.
- **F4 Travel check missed:** detect no one can inspect the home → consider shutting water off and draining before departure.

## Verification

Vulnerable pipes are insulated or warmed, outdoor hoses are disconnected, heat remains on, selected taps drip during severe freezes, and the water main is identified.

## Variations

- Vacant home: professional winterization may be safer than relying on heat.
- Manufactured home: protect skirting and heat tape under the home.
- Very cold climates: local code may require deeper insulation or specific heat cable products.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from burst pipes and improper heat tape. Use only listed electrical heating products, keep combustibles away from heaters, and do not reveal travel plans broadly when arranging home checks.
