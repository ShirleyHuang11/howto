## Goal

Temporarily secure a broken window against weather, injury, and entry until proper glass repair is arranged.

## Preconditions

- The area is safe to approach and no break-in is actively in progress.
- You have gloves, eye protection, sturdy shoes, tape, measuring tape, plywood or board, screws, drill, and trash container.
- You can work from a stable position without unsafe ladder use.
- You have permission to board the window or are acting to prevent urgent damage.

## Steps

1. **Make the scene safe.** Keep people and pets away, wear gloves and eye protection, and check for sharp hanging glass. → *Expect:* no one is moving through the glass hazard.
2. **Photograph before cleanup.** Capture the broken window, frame, room, and outside view for landlord, police, or insurance. → *Expect:* evidence exists before anything is moved.
3. **Remove loose glass carefully.** Lift large shards into a rigid container and vacuum small pieces only with suitable equipment. → *Expect:* loose glass is cleared without bare-hand contact.
4. **Tape remaining cracked glass.** Use wide tape across cracked panes if glass is still held in place. → *Expect:* remaining glass is less likely to shed fragments while boarding.
5. **Measure the opening.** Measure width and height beyond the damaged area, including frame surface for fastening. → *Expect:* the board will cover the whole vulnerable opening.
6. **Cut the board.** Cut plywood or a rigid panel to overlap the frame enough for screws or braces. → *Expect:* the board covers the opening with usable fastening edges.
7. **Fasten or brace it.** [BRANCH: screw to frame | brace from inside] screw into solid frame if allowed or brace with cross pieces from inside. → *Expect:* the board cannot be pushed in by wind or light force.
8. **Seal as a stopgap.** Tape edges or add plastic sheeting to reduce drafts and rain entry. → *Expect:* weather exposure is reduced until repair.
9. **Arrange permanent repair.** Contact a glazier, landlord, insurer, or emergency maintenance with measurements and photos. → *Expect:* a real repair appointment or claim is started.

## Decision points

- Window damage is from a break-in → follow police instructions before disturbing evidence.
- Broken glass is above head height → use professional help instead of unsafe ladder work.
- Frame is rotted or split → brace from inside and arrange urgent repair.
- Rental property → notify landlord immediately and keep receipts.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cut from glass:** detect bleeding or embedded shard → stop work, clean wound, and seek medical help for deep cuts.
- **F2 Board pulls loose:** detect movement in wind or push test → add screws into solid frame or interior braces.
- **F3 Rain leaks in:** detect wet sill or floor → add plastic sheeting, tape edges, and raise valuables.
- **F4 Evidence lost:** detect cleanup after crime before reporting → document what was moved and tell police or insurer.
- **F5 Unsafe access:** detect unstable ladder, roof, or exterior height → stop and hire emergency boarding service.

## Verification

The broken glass hazard is contained, the opening is fully covered by a secure board or brace, weather entry is reduced, and permanent repair has been requested.

## Variations

- `sliding-window`: brace the track to prevent opening as well as covering broken glass.
- `large-picture-window`: use multiple boards or professional emergency boarding.
- `storm-prone-area`: add more fasteners and inspect after wind.
- `rental`: photograph and report before and after boarding.

## Safety & privacy

High risk comes from sharp glass, height, unstable frames, and possible crime scenes. Wear gloves, eye protection, and sturdy shoes. Do not touch blood, tools, or forced-entry marks if police evidence may matter. Boarding is temporary and not equivalent to a repaired window.
