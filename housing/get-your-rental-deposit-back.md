## Goal

Recover the rental deposit by proving the handover condition, fixing fair minor issues, and disputing unsupported deductions.

## Preconditions

- You have the lease, deposit amount, move-in inventory, and move-in photos if available.
- You know the move-out date, handover method, and inspection process.
- You can access the property before key return.
- You have receipts for rent, utilities, cleaning, and approved repairs.

## Steps

1. **Read the deposit rules.** Check the lease, inventory, local deposit scheme if any, and deadlines for claims. → *Expect:* you know what can be deducted and when disputes must be raised.
2. **Compare against the move-in inventory.** Walk room by room and mark damage, wear, missing items, cleanliness, keys, meters, and furnishings. → *Expect:* you have a punch list tied to original condition.
3. **Fix minor damage you caused.** Tighten loose screws, replace bulbs, patch tiny nail holes if allowed, remove adhesive, and clean marks with surface-safe products. → *Expect:* avoidable deductions are resolved without creating new damage.
4. **Clean to the written standard.** Focus on appliances, bathrooms, floors, windowsills, cabinets, trash areas, and odor sources. → *Expect:* the home looks inspection-ready.
5. **Photograph and video the final state.** Capture wide room shots, close-ups, appliances, inside drawers, meters, keys, and any pre-existing or disputed condition. → *Expect:* evidence is dated and organized by room.
6. **Attend or request a walkthrough.** Ask the landlord or agent to identify concerns before keys are returned. [BRANCH: they attend | they refuse or are unavailable] document either result in writing. → *Expect:* inspection feedback is captured before you lose access.
7. **Return keys with proof.** Use a signed receipt, email confirmation, lockbox photo, or tracked delivery. → *Expect:* the tenancy end and key return time are documented.
8. **Request the deposit in writing.** Provide forwarding details and ask for an itemized list if any deduction is proposed. → *Expect:* the landlord must either return the deposit or explain deductions.
9. **Dispute unsupported deductions.** Respond with photos, inventory excerpts, receipts, fair wear arguments, and a concise requested refund amount. → *Expect:* the dispute is narrowed to evidence, not opinion.

## Decision points

- Damage is beyond minor repair → get a quote before agreeing to a landlord's estimate.
- Landlord proposes cleaning charges after refusing a walkthrough → use your final photos and cleaning receipts.
- Deposit is held by a formal scheme → use that scheme's dispute process and deadlines.
- You owe rent or utilities → separate true unpaid charges from unsupported damage deductions.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No move-in photos:** detect missing baseline evidence → rely on inventory, emails, age of item, and fair wear.
- **F2 Deduction is vague:** detect wording like "cleaning" with no details → request itemization, invoices, photos, and dates.
- **F3 Landlord delays refund:** detect missed statutory or lease deadline → send a written deadline and escalate to the deposit scheme or local process.
- **F4 Repair made it worse:** detect mismatched paint, scratches, or cracked fittings → stop, photograph, and get a professional quote if the amount is material.
- **F5 Missing key claim:** detect a charge for unreturned keys → provide key receipt, handover photo, or tracked delivery record.

## Verification

The deposit is returned in full or every remaining deduction has an itemized written reason, evidence response, and active dispute path.

## Variations

- `uk`: protected deposits usually have free alternative dispute resolution with strict evidence windows.
- `us`: rules vary by state; many require itemized deductions within a fixed number of days.
- `furnished`: inventory evidence matters more, including mattresses, remotes, manuals, and small appliances.
- `room-rental`: photograph shared areas if the landlord may allocate shared damage to you.

## Safety & privacy

Do not sign a deduction agreement you do not understand. Keep communications written and factual. Photos may contain personal items, mail, or account numbers, so remove private material before sharing. Medium risk comes from money loss and avoidable liability.
