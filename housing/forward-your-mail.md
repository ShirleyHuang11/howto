## Goal

Set up temporary mail forwarding and directly update important senders so letters stop going to the old address.

## Preconditions

- You know the old address, new address, move date, and every person whose mail must be redirected.
- You can verify identity with the postal service if required.
- You have a payment method for paid forwarding services.
- You have access to online accounts or phone numbers for important senders.

## Steps

1. **List everyone covered.** Include each adult, common name variants, and dependents if the postal service permits. → *Expect:* the forwarding order will catch the names that actually receive mail.
2. **Choose the postal redirect service.** Use the official postal service for the old address, not an ad or reseller. → *Expect:* you are on the legitimate forwarding or change-of-address page.
3. **Set the start date.** Pick the move date or the first day mail should leave the old address. [BRANCH: already moved | moving in future] start immediately or schedule ahead. → *Expect:* forwarding starts when the old mailbox stops being safe to use.
4. **Choose the duration.** Select the shortest duration that covers the transition, commonly months rather than weeks. → *Expect:* you have time to update senders directly before forwarding expires.
5. **Submit and save confirmation.** Enter addresses exactly, pay if required, and save the confirmation number. → *Expect:* the redirect is active or scheduled.
6. **Notify high-risk senders directly.** Update banks, tax agencies, employer, insurers, medical providers, schools, licenses, subscriptions, and legal contacts. → *Expect:* critical mail is addressed to the new home without relying on forwarding.
7. **Notify ordinary senders next.** Update shopping accounts, memberships, charities, magazines, loyalty cards, and friends. → *Expect:* low-risk mail gradually shifts to the new address.
8. **Monitor the first forwarded letters.** Check labels and dates to see which senders still need direct updates. → *Expect:* each forwarded item becomes an action item.
9. **Close out the old mailbox.** Ask the landlord, buyer, or building manager not to discard stray mail during the first weeks. → *Expect:* accidental deliveries have a recovery path.

## Decision points

- Household members move to different addresses → file separate redirects for each person.
- Business mail involved → use the business redirect process and update registrations directly.
- Sensitive mail expected → update the sender directly and ask whether mail can be paused or made electronic.
- International move → postal forwarding may be limited, so direct notifications become the main control.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Forwarding misses a name:** detect mail at the old address → add name variants or file a separate order.
- **F2 Wrong start date:** detect mail forwarded too early or too late → amend the order if allowed and notify critical senders directly.
- **F3 Fraudulent site used:** detect unexpected fees or no confirmation from the official carrier → contact the card issuer and file through the official service.
- **F4 Sender ignores forwarding:** detect important mail still addressed to the old place → update the account address and request duplicate documents.
- **F5 Redirect expires too soon:** detect forwarded mail near the end date → renew or extend before expiry.

## Verification

The postal forwarding confirmation is saved, critical senders show the new address in their own systems, and no important mail is expected at the old address without a recovery plan.

## Variations

- `us`: USPS change-of-address and premium forwarding are separate services.
- `uk`: Royal Mail redirection is person-based and usually identity-checked.
- `multi-unit-building`: include apartment, unit, floor, or mailbox number exactly.
- `privacy-risk`: use electronic statements while the address transition settles.

## Safety & privacy

Mail exposes identity, banking, medical, tax, and legal information. Use official postal channels, keep confirmation numbers private, and prioritize direct updates for sensitive senders. Medium risk comes from identity theft and missed legal or financial notices.
