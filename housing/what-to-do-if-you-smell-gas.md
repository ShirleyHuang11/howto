## Goal

Get people away from a possible gas leak and contact the proper emergency responders without creating an ignition source.

## Preconditions

- You notice a rotten-egg odor, hissing, dead vegetation near a gas line, or a gas alarm.
- You can leave the area without operating electrical devices.
- You have access to a phone after reaching a safe outside location.

## Steps

1. **Stop what you are doing.** Do not light matches, smoke, use candles, or operate appliances. → *Expect:* no flame or spark source is introduced.
2. **Avoid switches and electronics.** Do not flip lights, unplug cords, use a phone inside, ring doorbells, or use a garage door opener. → *Expect:* electrical contacts are not operated in the suspected gas area.
3. **Leave immediately.** Walk out, bringing other people with you, and leave doors as they are unless closing one is effortless on the way out. → *Expect:* everyone is outside the building.
4. **Move away from the odor.** Go to the sidewalk, neighbor, or upwind area away from the meter and vents. → *Expect:* you are no longer smelling strong gas.
5. **Call from outside.** Contact the gas emergency line or fire service, and give the address and what you noticed. → *Expect:* emergency responders or the utility are dispatched.
6. **Keep others away.** Warn arriving people not to enter, use switches, or start vehicles nearby. → *Expect:* the area remains clear.
7. **Shut off the gas only if trained or instructed.** If the meter is outside and safely reachable, close the meter valve; otherwise wait for responders. → *Expect:* you do not re-enter or linger in the gas area.
8. **Wait for clearance.** Do not return until the fire service, gas utility, or qualified professional says it is safe. → *Expect:* re-entry happens only after leak testing or official clearance.

## Decision points

- Gas smell is strong, sudden, or accompanied by hissing → evacuate immediately and call emergency services from outside.
- Smell is near an appliance but not elsewhere → still avoid ignition sources, leave, and call the gas emergency line.
- Someone feels dizzy, nauseated, or has a headache → get fresh air and request medical help.
- You cannot evacuate a person safely → call emergency services from outside and describe their location.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Phone used indoors:** detect a call or text started inside → stop using it, leave immediately, and call again from outside.
- **F2 Doorbell or switch used:** detect someone operated electrical hardware → evacuate farther and tell responders.
- **F3 Odor disappears:** detect smell fading before help arrives → stay outside anyway until the utility clears it.
- **F4 Re-entry pressure:** detect someone wants belongings or pets → do not re-enter; tell responders what is inside.

## Verification

All people are outside, the gas emergency line or fire service has been called from a safe location, and no one returns until responders or the utility clear the building.

## Variations

- Propane: because propane can collect low to the ground, move uphill or upwind if possible and call the propane supplier or fire service.
- Natural gas: because it is lighter than air, still evacuate rather than opening windows or searching for the leak.
- Workplace or school: activate the site's emergency plan from outside and meet at the assembly point.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from explosion, fire, and carbon monoxide. Do not investigate the source yourself during an odor event, and share only necessary address and contact information with emergency responders.
