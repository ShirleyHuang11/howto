## Goal

Find and close the main water shutoff so uncontrolled water stops entering the home.

## Preconditions

- You can safely enter the area where the shutoff may be located.
- You have a flashlight and, for some curb valves, the proper water key or utility assistance.
- You know that closing the valve may stop water to the entire home.

## Steps

1. **Look near where water enters the building.** Check basement front wall, crawlspace, garage, utility closet, water heater area, or meter box. → *Expect:* you find a main valve on the pipe before it branches through the home.
2. **Identify the valve type.** [BRANCH: round gate handle | lever ball valve | curb or meter valve] choose the matching closing motion. → *Expect:* you know which part moves before applying force.
3. **Close a round handle clockwise.** Turn gently until it stops without using excessive force. → *Expect:* the handle reaches a firm closed position.
4. **Close a lever valve with a quarter turn.** Move the lever until it is perpendicular to the pipe. → *Expect:* the lever crosses the pipe at a right angle.
5. **Use the curb valve only if allowed and equipped.** If the shutoff is at the street or meter pit, follow local utility rules or call the utility. → *Expect:* you do not damage a utility-owned valve.
6. **Open a cold faucet at the lowest safe fixture.** Use a tub, laundry sink, or outdoor spigot away from electrical hazards. → *Expect:* pressure drops and flow slows to a trickle.
7. **Tag the valve.** Add a durable label showing "water main shutoff" and the closing direction. → *Expect:* another person can identify it quickly later.
8. **Test the valve yearly.** Close it briefly, confirm flow stops, then reopen slowly if there is no emergency. → *Expect:* the valve moves and still controls water.
9. **After a leak, reopen carefully only when repairs are ready.** Open slowly while someone watches the repaired area. → *Expect:* pressure returns without renewed leakage.

## Decision points

- Valve will not move by hand → do not force it until it breaks; call a plumber or utility.
- Water does not slow after closing → you may have the wrong valve or a failed valve; call a plumber.
- Water is touching electrical equipment → avoid the area and shut power only from a dry safe location.
- Multi-unit building → confirm whether the valve affects neighbors before closing except in an emergency.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Valve handle breaks:** detect spinning or detached handle → leave it closed if possible and call a plumber urgently.
- **F2 Gate valve does not seal:** detect continued strong flow → use another upstream shutoff or call the utility.
- **F3 Reopened too fast:** detect banging pipes or leaks → close partly, reopen slowly, and inspect joints.
- **F4 Wrong valve closed:** detect no change at faucets → reopen if safe and continue tracing the incoming line.

## Verification

With faucets open and no fixtures running, water pressure drops to a trickle or stops, and the shutoff is labeled for future emergencies.

## Variations

- Cold climates: the main may be inside to prevent freezing, with the meter outside or remote-read.
- Warm climates: the main may be in an exterior box near the property line.
- Condos: individual unit shutoffs may be under sinks, behind access panels, or in a shared mechanical room.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from water damage and valve failure. Do not enter flooded electrical areas, and call a plumber or utility when a valve is seized, leaking, or utility-owned.
