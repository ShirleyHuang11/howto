## Goal

Install and aim a security light so it illuminates useful areas, avoids nuisance glare, and works reliably at night.

## Preconditions

- You are allowed to install exterior lighting and have landlord or owner approval if needed.
- You have a light suited to the location: wired, solar, or battery.
- You have ladder access, mounting hardware, drill, screwdriver, and weather-safe fittings.
- For wired work, you know which circuit feeds the location or will use a qualified electrician.

## Steps

1. **Choose the target area.** Pick entries, side paths, bins, garages, or dark corners, not neighbor windows or roads. → *Expect:* the light covers a security-relevant zone.
2. **Select the power type.** [BRANCH: wired | solar | battery] choose permanent power, sun exposure, or easy installation based on the site. → *Expect:* the model can run reliably where mounted.
3. **Set placement and angle.** Mount high enough to resist tampering, angled downward to reduce glare and false triggers. → *Expect:* light falls on ground and door areas, not faces or windows.
4. **Make wiring safe.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* switch power off at the breaker before touching wiring, verify dead if you can, and stop for an electrician if wiring is unclear. → *Expect:* no live conductors are handled.
5. **Mount the bracket.** Drill pilot holes, use exterior-rated screws or anchors, and seal penetrations if exposed to weather. → *Expect:* the bracket is solid and weather-resistant.
6. **Connect or attach the fixture.** Wire per instructions or clip the solar or battery unit to its bracket. → *Expect:* the light is secured and the housing closes properly.
7. **Set sensor controls.** Adjust range, sensitivity, dusk threshold, and timer. → *Expect:* movement in the intended area triggers the light for a useful duration.
8. **Test at night.** Walk through approach paths after dark and observe from inside and outside. → *Expect:* the light triggers when needed without flooding neighbors.
9. **Tune after false triggers.** Reduce sensitivity, angle away from roads, trim moving branches, or change timer. → *Expect:* nuisance activations drop while coverage remains.

## Decision points

- No safe wiring knowledge → use solar or battery, or hire an electrician.
- Public walkway nearby → angle down and reduce range to avoid dazzling people.
- Wildlife or traffic triggers often → narrow the sensor zone or relocate the fixture.
- Camera integrated light → configure recording, notification, and privacy zones.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Light never turns on:** detect no activation at night → check power, batteries, solar charge, dusk setting, and switch state.
- **F2 Constant false alarms:** detect frequent activations with no visitor → reduce range, aim away from motion sources, and shorten timer.
- **F3 Water gets inside:** detect condensation or flicker → power off, dry, reseal, or replace with a proper outdoor-rated fixture.
- **F4 Glare complaint:** detect neighbor or household complaint → re-aim downward and lower brightness if supported.
- **F5 Unsafe wiring:** detect sparks, heat, tripped breaker, or uncertainty → leave power off and call an electrician.

## Verification

At night, the security light triggers from the intended approach paths, stays on for the set time, and does not shine directly into neighboring windows or traffic.

## Variations

- `solar`: mount where the panel receives enough direct sun in winter, not only summer.
- `battery`: set a replacement reminder and use weather-rated batteries.
- `wired`: local electrical codes may require an exterior junction box and qualified installer.
- `camera-light`: configure privacy zones and account security before relying on alerts.

## Safety & privacy

High risk if wired incorrectly or installed from an unsafe ladder position. Turn power off for wiring, keep three points of contact on ladders, and respect neighbors' privacy. Cameras and motion logs reveal household routines and visitor patterns.
