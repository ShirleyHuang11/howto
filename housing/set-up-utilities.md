## Goal

A new home has working electricity, gas (if applicable), water, and internet — each in your name, on sane tariffs, with first meter readings recorded and billing running to you.

## Preconditions

- A move-in date and the new address in its exact official form.
- Identity and payment details; in some systems, a local bank account or deposit capability.
- From the landlord/agent/seller: which utilities serve the property, current suppliers, and meter locations.

## Steps

1. **Inventory the property's connections before moving day.** Electricity always; gas maybe; water (often municipal and landlord-mediated in rentals); internet options at *this address* (checker tools on providers' sites — availability is address-specific). Ask the outgoing party which suppliers currently serve it. → *Expect:* a per-utility list: supplier-now, options, and who's responsible (you vs. landlord) per your lease.
2. **Sequence by lead time, internet first.** Internet installation appointments run days-to-weeks out — book it first against your move date. Electricity/gas/water are usually switchable in days. → *Expect:* an internet install date near move-in; the rest scheduled around it.
3. **Open each account (or transfer service) effective move-in date.** Supplier's website/phone: address, your details, start date, tariff choice. [BRANCH: regulated single-supplier region → registration is the whole task | competitive market → compare tariffs on a comparison site first: unit rate, standing charge, contract length, exit fees] → *Expect:* per utility: an account number, a start date, and a chosen tariff you can name the shape of.
4. **On move-in day: read every meter and photograph it.** Electricity, gas, water meters — numbers + photo with the move-in date. This is the boundary between the previous occupant's consumption and yours. → *Expect:* dated photos of all meters; readings submitted to each supplier as opening readings.
5. **Verify the physical services actually work.** Lights and sockets; gas appliances light (smell gas → stop, ventilate, no switches/flames, call the gas emergency line — this is the recipe's hard-stop); water runs clear after a few minutes; heating/hot water fire up. → *Expect:* every service functioning, or a fault reported to the right party (supplier vs. landlord per the lease).
6. **Set billing to autopay + e-billing, and file the details.** ⚠️ *Irreversible-ish:* missed utility bills damage credit records and can end in disconnection — autopay converts the whole class of failure into a solved problem. → *Expect:* each account showing an active payment method and your contact details; account numbers stored where you store such things.
7. **Confirm the internet install and test it.** Be present for the appointment; test speed against the contracted tier before the technician leaves. → *Expect:* live connection at roughly the promised speed; router details recorded.
8. **Close the loop on the old place (if you moved from one).** Final readings + photos there, accounts closed or transferred, forwarding address given for final bills. → *Expect:* no zombie account billing you for a stranger's showers.

## Decision points

- Rental where utilities stay in the landlord's name (bills-included lets) → your task collapses to verifying inclusion terms in writing and the fair-usage caps.
- Deposit demanded (no local credit history) → normal for newcomers; ask about deposit-return conditions and timelines, and get it in writing.
- Green/fixed/variable tariff choice → fixed = price certainty for the term; variable = market exposure both ways; decide by how long you'll stay and your tolerance, not the salesperson's weather.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Arrived to no electricity/gas (previous account closed dead):** suppliers run emergency/priority reconnection paths — call the *distributor* (the wires/pipes company, findable by address) if no supplier is attached; days-long gaps are rare but this is the door.
- **F2 Billed for the previous occupant's usage:** your dated move-in meter photos settle it — send them; this is exactly what step 4 buys.
- **F3 Internet install missed/failed ("no line to the property"):** escalate within the provider (they own the subcontractor), and get the next date in writing; meanwhile a mobile hotspot bridges — check your phone plan's tethering before buying anything.
- **F4 Discovered months later on a punitive default tariff:** suppliers park unengaged customers on their worst rates — compare and switch (switching is typically free and fast in competitive markets); set an annual calendar nudge to re-check.

## Verification

Every utility serving the property has an account in your name with the right start date, opening meter readings are submitted and photo-archived, all services physically work, autopay + e-billing are live on each, internet performs to contract — and the old address (if any) is fully closed out.

## Variations

- `us`: utilities often demand SSN-linked credit checks or deposits; municipal water frequently stays landlord-side in rentals.
- `uk`: you inherit a "deemed contract" with the sitting supplier on move-in — you're free to switch immediately; `de`: Anmeldung (address registration) frequently gates account opening — sequence it first.
- Prepaid-meter properties: top-up cards/apps replace billing — learn the top-up channel *before* the meter hits zero at midnight.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: financial (autopay + the meter-photo evidence rule cover it) and one physical hard-stop — the gas-smell protocol in step 5, which outranks every other instruction in this file. Utility accounts are identity-rich; close old ones properly rather than abandoning them.
