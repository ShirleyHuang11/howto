## Goal

Stop water from a burst pipe, reduce electrical and structural hazards, and start repair and documentation.

## Preconditions

- You can reach the affected area without standing in water near electrical equipment.
- You know or can find the water main shutoff.
- You have a phone, towels or buckets, and a camera for documentation.

## Steps

1. **Keep people out of the water.** Block access to the room if outlets, cords, or appliances may be wet. → *Expect:* no one is standing in potentially energized water.
2. **Shut off the water main.** Close the main valve fully, or call building maintenance for an emergency shutoff. → *Expect:* spraying or flowing water slows.
3. **Turn off power if water is near electrical systems.** Use the breaker only from a dry location; if the panel is wet or unsafe, call emergency services or an electrician. → *Expect:* affected circuits are de-energized without touching wet equipment.
4. **Open low cold taps.** Open a tub, sink, or outdoor spigot below the burst to drain pressure. → *Expect:* remaining water drains away from the break.
5. **Contain active water.** Use buckets, towels, plastic sheeting, or a temporary pipe wrap only after water and electrical hazards are controlled. → *Expect:* water is directed away from valuables and openings.
6. **Call a plumber or emergency maintenance.** Describe the pipe location, material if known, and whether the main is off. → *Expect:* a repair visit is requested.
7. **Document damage before moving items.** Photograph the pipe, standing water, walls, floors, and damaged belongings. → *Expect:* insurance evidence is captured.
8. **Start drying what is safe to dry.** Remove wet rugs, run fans or dehumidifiers only on dry safe power, and ventilate if outdoor conditions allow. → *Expect:* moisture begins reducing.
9. **Notify insurance or the landlord.** Report the event promptly with photos and repair details. → *Expect:* a claim or maintenance record is started.

## Decision points

- Water is near the breaker panel or live wiring → stay out and call emergency services or the utility.
- Ceiling is bulging or sagging → leave the room and call a professional.
- Water came from sewage or a contaminated source → avoid contact and call remediation.
- You cannot find the water main → call the utility, landlord, or emergency plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Water keeps flowing:** detect strong flow after main shutoff → close an upstream utility valve if authorized or call the utility.
- **F2 Electrical shock risk:** detect tingling, sparking, or wet electrical gear → leave immediately and call emergency services.
- **F3 Temporary wrap fails:** detect renewed spray → keep water off and wait for plumbing repair.
- **F4 Mold risk increases:** detect wet materials after 24 to 48 hours → call water mitigation or mold remediation.

## Verification

The water main is off, active flow has stopped or is contained, electrical hazards are avoided, a plumber or maintenance service is contacted, and damage photos are saved.

## Variations

- Apartment: call building emergency maintenance because shutoffs may affect multiple units.
- Frozen pipe burst: keep the main off until the damaged section is repaired.
- Well system: turn off the pump breaker from a dry safe panel after shutting valves.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from electrical contact, contaminated water, and hidden structural damage. Do not enter wet electrical areas, and keep insurance photos limited to relevant damage when possible.
