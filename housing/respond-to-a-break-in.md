## Goal

Respond to a suspected break-in with personal safety first, preserve evidence, report the crime, document losses, and secure the home afterward.

## Preconditions

- You suspect unauthorized entry, forced entry, missing property, or signs of disturbance.
- You have a phone or nearby safe place to call from.
- You can identify whether anyone may still be inside.
- You have insurance, landlord, or emergency repair contacts if applicable.

## Steps

1. **Stay out if danger may be present.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not enter if the break-in may be in progress, if you hear movement, see an open door, or feel unsafe; leave and call emergency services. → *Expect:* you are in a safe place before taking any other action.
2. **Call the police.** Report location, whether anyone may be inside, visible entry points, injuries, and immediate risks. → *Expect:* you receive instructions and an incident or report number when available.
3. **Do not touch likely evidence.** Avoid handles, broken windows, disturbed drawers, footprints, tools, and dropped items unless needed for safety. → *Expect:* the scene remains useful for police or insurance review.
4. **Check people and pets only when safe.** [BRANCH: police cleared entry | no sign of active threat] confirm occupants are safe or wait for responders. → *Expect:* life safety is handled before property inventory.
5. **Document the scene.** Take photos and video of entry damage, rooms, missing-item locations, serial numbers, and security alerts. → *Expect:* evidence exists before cleanup or repairs.
6. **Make an initial loss list.** List stolen or damaged items with make, model, serial, purchase date, value, and receipts if available. → *Expect:* police and insurer have a starting inventory.
7. **Notify insurer and landlord.** Provide the police report number, photos, loss list, and urgent repair needs. → *Expect:* claim and repair responsibilities are opened.
8. **Secure the home after evidence handling.** Change compromised locks, board broken windows, close damaged doors, and arrange emergency repairs. → *Expect:* the home is not left exposed.
9. **Protect accounts and identity.** If devices, cards, documents, or keys were taken, freeze cards, change passwords, revoke sessions, and consider credit monitoring. → *Expect:* theft does not become wider identity or account compromise.

## Decision points

- You arrive while entry may be ongoing → leave immediately and call emergency services from a safe location.
- Medication, passports, keys, or financial documents are stolen → treat as urgent identity and access risk.
- Rental property → coordinate repairs with landlord, but still report and document independently.
- Security footage exists → preserve clips before automatic deletion.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Entered too soon:** detect signs someone may still be inside → leave immediately and tell police you entered briefly.
- **F2 Evidence disturbed:** detect cleaned surfaces or moved items → tell police and insurer what changed and when.
- **F3 Missing items discovered later:** detect new loss after first report → update police and insurance claim with details.
- **F4 Home cannot be secured:** detect broken lock, door, or window after hours → use emergency boarding, locksmith, or temporary safe lodging.
- **F5 Stolen keys or documents:** detect missing keys, ID, passport, checkbook, or cards → rekey locks and start fraud-protection steps.

## Verification

You are safe, police have been contacted, evidence is documented before cleanup, insurance or landlord has been notified, and the home is physically secured or you have moved to safe temporary lodging.

## Variations

- `occupied-home`: prioritize accounting for all people and pets before inventory.
- `apartment`: notify building management about common-entry footage and access control.
- `vehicle-key-stolen`: treat the vehicle as at risk and contact insurer or dealer.
- `traveling`: ask a trusted local person, police, or landlord to secure and document the property.

## Safety & privacy

Personal safety overrides property recovery. Do not confront suspects or search the home alone if risk remains. High risk comes from possible violence, broken glass, exposed wiring, compromised keys, and identity documents. Share photos and reports only with police, insurer, landlord, and necessary repair services.
