## Goal

Confirm carbon monoxide alarms can sound and know what to do if a real alarm occurs.

## Preconditions

- Carbon monoxide alarms are installed or available for installation.
- You can reach each test button safely, using a stable step stool if needed.
- Household members know the alarm sound may be loud during testing.

## Steps

1. **Confirm placement.** Check that alarms are outside sleeping areas, on each level where recommended, and near fuel-burning appliances as allowed by the manual. → *Expect:* alarm locations match manufacturer and local guidance.
2. **Check the date.** Look for manufacture, replace-by, or end-of-life markings. → *Expect:* the alarm is within its service life.
3. **Press and hold the test button.** Follow the alarm label until the test sound starts. → *Expect:* the alarm emits a loud test pattern.
4. **Confirm everyone can hear it.** Ask people in sleeping areas whether they heard the alarm. → *Expect:* the sound is audible where people sleep.
5. **Replace batteries if needed.** Install the specified battery type when the alarm chirps, fails a test, or the schedule calls for it. → *Expect:* chirping stops and the test passes.
6. **Clean the alarm lightly.** Dust vents with a vacuum brush or dry cloth without spraying cleaners. → *Expect:* vents are clear.
7. **Record the monthly test.** Note the date on a calendar or maintenance log. → *Expect:* the next test date is easy to see.
8. **Practice the real-alarm response.** If the alarm sounds outside a test, get everyone outside to fresh air and call emergency services or the fire service from outside. → *Expect:* no one stays inside to investigate.

## Decision points

- Alarm does not sound during test → replace battery once, then replace the alarm if it still fails.
- Alarm shows end-of-life or keeps chirping after battery replacement → replace the alarm.
- Real alarm sounds or anyone has headache, dizziness, nausea, confusion, or weakness → evacuate and call emergency services.
- Fuel-burning appliance is suspected → do not use it again until inspected by a qualified technician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Test button silent:** detect no sound → replace battery or unit, and do not rely on it.
- **F2 Alarm too far from bedrooms:** detect sleeping area cannot hear test → add an alarm closer to sleeping rooms.
- **F3 Nuisance alarms ignored:** detect repeated alarms without investigation → treat each alarm as real and call professionals.
- **F4 Wrong detector type:** detect smoke-only alarm where CO protection is needed → install a listed carbon monoxide alarm.

## Verification

Each carbon monoxide alarm is within service life, sounds when tested, is audible near sleeping areas, and the household response to a real alarm is to get outside and call emergency services.

## Variations

- Combination smoke and CO alarm: verify both test patterns if the manual supports separate tests.
- Hardwired alarm: check backup battery and replace the unit at end of life.
- Rental housing: report failed or missing alarms to the landlord in writing.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because carbon monoxide is poisonous and cannot be seen or smelled. Never ignore a real alarm, and do not re-enter until responders clear the building.
