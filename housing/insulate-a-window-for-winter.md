## Goal

A drafty window is sealed for winter with removable film, weatherstrip, or both so cold air leaks are reduced without damaging the frame.

## Preconditions

- You have a window insulation film kit or removable weatherstrip, scissors, clean cloth, mild cleaner, tape measure, and hair dryer if using film.
- The window closes fully and the frame surface is dry and paint is sound enough for removable tape.
- For egress windows, keep at least one safe exit operable according to local safety needs.

## Steps

1. **Find the drafts.** On a windy or cold day, feel around sash edges, locks, trim, and meeting rails. → *Expect:* the leakiest spots are identified.
2. **Choose the method.** [BRANCH: film kit | weatherstrip | both] use film for whole-window air leakage, weatherstrip for gaps where moving parts meet. → *Expect:* the product matches the draft path.
3. **Clean the frame.** Wipe the tape or strip contact area and let it dry fully. → *Expect:* adhesive can bond to a clean surface.
4. **Measure and cut.** Cut film larger than the window opening or weatherstrip to the exact gap length. → *Expect:* pieces cover the intended area with slight allowance.
5. **Apply tape or strip.** Press double-sided tape around the frame, or press weatherstrip into the gap without blocking locks. → *Expect:* adhesive is continuous and secure.
6. **Mount the film.** Press film onto the tape from top to bottom, keeping it lightly tensioned. → *Expect:* the film covers the opening with wrinkles but no large loose gaps.
7. **Heat-shrink the film.** Use a hair dryer moving constantly, starting near the center and working outward. → *Expect:* wrinkles tighten and the film becomes clear.
8. **Trim excess.** Cut extra film carefully without scoring the frame or glass. → *Expect:* edges look neat and tape remains sealed.
9. **Check window operation.** Confirm locks are engaged and required exits remain usable. → *Expect:* the window is secure and safety access is preserved.
10. **Plan spring removal.** Note the product removal instructions and expected residue cleanup. → *Expect:* the temporary insulation can be removed without surprise damage.

## Decision points

- Paint flakes when tape is tested → use removable rope caulk, interior storm panel, or another low-adhesion method.
- Window must open during winter → use weatherstrip instead of full film.
- Condensation appears between film and glass → reduce indoor humidity and check for hidden moisture.
- Draft comes from trim gaps, not sash → use removable caulk or ask a pro about air sealing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tape lifts:** film peels from the frame, clean and dry the surface again and apply fresh tape.
- **F2 Film tears:** hole appears during shrinking, patch with clear tape or replace the film.
- **F3 Window will not lock:** weatherstrip is too thick, remove and use a thinner profile.
- **F4 Moisture collects:** condensation pools on sill, ventilate, lower humidity, and dry the area to prevent mold.

## Verification

After installation, the window area has no felt draft at the treated gaps, the film stays tight, locks still work, and required egress is not blocked.

## Variations

- Sliding windows: weatherstrip meeting rails and use film only if the sash can remain closed.
- Old wood frames: test adhesive in a hidden spot before applying full tape.
- Renters: choose removable products and document any pre-existing paint damage.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: avoid blocking emergency exits, overheating film with the hair dryer, trapping moisture, or pulling weak paint during removal. Remove temporary film in spring or when ventilation and egress are needed.
