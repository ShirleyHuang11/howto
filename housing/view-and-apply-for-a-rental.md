## Goal

You inspect a rental property properly, spot dealbreakers before signing anything, and submit a complete application with your deposit protected under local rules.

## Preconditions

- A budget ceiling including rent plus utilities, and proof-of-income documents ready.
- References (previous landlord, employer) and ID that you can share.
- The listing details: address, monthly rent, deposit amount, and who you are meeting.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the viewing and the person.** Verify the agent/landlord and that the address matches the listing before you travel; view in daylight if possible. → *Expect:* a confirmed time and a name that matches the listing contact.
2. **Test everything that moves or flows during the viewing.** Run taps and flush, check water pressure and hot water, open every window, try light switches, look under sinks for leaks, and check phone signal. → *Expect:* working fixtures noted; any fault written down on the spot.
3. **Inspect for the expensive hidden problems.** Look for damp/mold at wall bases and ceiling corners, sniff for musty air, check window seals and any black spotting, and note heating type and age. → *Expect:* a clear read on damp, heating, and insulation, not just cosmetics.
4. **Ask the money and management questions.** Total monthly cost, what deposit scheme protects it, notice period, who handles repairs, and why the last tenant left. → *Expect:* straight answers; evasiveness is itself a data point.
5. **Score against red flags before applying.** [BRANCH: red flags present (see Decision points) → walk away, keep looking | acceptable → proceed to apply] → *Expect:* a go/no-go decision you can justify.
6. **Assemble the application pack.** ID, proof of income (payslips/bank statements), references, and a completed application form. → *Expect:* one folder/PDF with every document the landlord asked for.
7. **Pay only a holding deposit, and get it in writing.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never pay a full deposit or rent before viewing in person or before a written agreement; wire transfers to strangers are unrecoverable. Confirm what the holding fee reserves and whether it is refundable. → *Expect:* a dated receipt stating the amount, purpose, and refund conditions.
8. **Confirm deposit protection before move-in.** Ask which government/registered scheme will hold the deposit and get the scheme reference. → *Expect:* written confirmation of where your deposit money sits.

## Decision points

- Landlord asks for cash-only, off-record rent, or refuses a written contract → walk away.
- Deposit demanded before any viewing, or pressure to pay "to hold it today" sight-unseen → classic scam pattern, stop.
- Damp/mold, no working heating, or insecure doors/windows → either negotiate a written fix before signing or decline.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rental scam:** listing far below market, absentee "owner", up-front wire request → reverse-image-search the photos, verify ownership, never pay before viewing.
- **F2 Missing documents stall the application:** landlord requests something you lack → ask the full list up front and prepare a guarantor if income is thin.
- **F3 Deposit not protected:** landlord vague about the scheme → withhold move-in until you have the scheme name and reference in writing.
- **F4 Verbal promises evaporate:** "we'll fix the boiler" said, never written → get every promised repair listed in the tenancy agreement with a date.

## Verification

You have viewed the property in person, recorded its condition and faults, submitted a complete application, paid only a documented holding fee (if any), and hold written confirmation of the deposit-protection scheme before any money beyond the holding fee changes hands.

## Variations

- `us`: expect a credit check and application fee; deposits often 1 to 2 months, capped by state law.
- `uk`: deposits are capped (commonly 5 weeks) and must be in a government-backed protection scheme within 30 days.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: money and identity documents are exposed. The two rules that prevent almost all rental fraud are never pay before an in-person viewing and never pay by irreversible transfer to an unverified person. Share ID copies only after you are confident the landlord is genuine.
