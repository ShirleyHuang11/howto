## Goal

A gas appliance pilot light is relit only when the appliance instructions allow it and no gas hazard is present.

## Preconditions

- You can read the appliance lighting instructions and reach the gas control knob, pilot window, and igniter.
- You have a flashlight and long lighter only if the appliance instructions permit manual lighting.
- ⚠️ Gas safety first: if you smell gas, hear hissing, feel unwell, or lack clear instructions, stop, leave the area, avoid switches and flames, and call emergency gas services or a licensed technician.

## Steps

1. **Check for gas smell.** Smell near the appliance and the floor without kneeling into an enclosed space. → *Expect:* no gas odor is present.
2. **Read the appliance label.** Find the exact pilot-light instructions on the appliance or manual. → *Expect:* the procedure matches the controls in front of you.
3. **Turn control to off.** Rotate the gas control knob to OFF. → *Expect:* the appliance is not feeding pilot gas.
4. **Wait.** Leave it off for at least five minutes, or longer if the label requires. → *Expect:* any small gas accumulation has time to dissipate.
5. **Set to pilot.** Turn the knob to PILOT and press or hold it as directed. → *Expect:* pilot gas is available only while the control is held.
6. **Ignite the pilot.** [BRANCH: built-in igniter | approved manual lighter] press the igniter button, or use a long lighter only if the label permits. → *Expect:* a small steady pilot flame appears.
7. **Hold the valve.** Keep holding the pilot control for the label's time, often 30 to 60 seconds. → *Expect:* the flame stays lit while the safety sensor warms.
8. **Release slowly.** Let go of the control knob gradually. → *Expect:* the pilot remains lit.
9. **Turn to on.** Rotate the control from PILOT to ON and restore the thermostat or appliance call. → *Expect:* the main burner lights normally when called.
10. **Observe briefly.** Watch for stable flame and normal operation without smell, rollout, or sputtering. → *Expect:* flame remains steady and contained.

## Decision points

- You smell gas at any time → stop, leave, avoid electrical switches, and call emergency gas services.
- Pilot will not light after two tries → stop and call a licensed technician.
- Pilot lights but will not stay lit → likely thermocouple or safety device issue, call a technician.
- Flame is yellow, lifting, roaring, or rolling out → shut off and call a technician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gas odor appears:** odor is detected before or after lighting, stop immediately and follow emergency gas procedures.
- **F2 Igniter fails:** clicking produces no spark, stop after the allowed tries and call service unless manual lighting is explicitly allowed.
- **F3 Pilot goes out:** flame dies when control is released, call a technician rather than bypassing the safety.
- **F4 Main burner behaves oddly:** burner booms, rolls out, or smells, turn control off and get professional service.

## Verification

The pilot remains lit after the control is released, the appliance runs normally on demand, and no gas smell or abnormal flame appears during several minutes of observation.

## Variations

- Sealed-combustion appliances: do not open sealed panels unless the user manual explicitly permits user access.
- Modern appliances: many use electronic ignition and have no pilot to relight.
- Rental housing: contact the landlord or property manager if the lease requires authorized service.

## Safety & privacy

High risk: gas leaks, carbon monoxide, fire, and explosion are possible. If you smell gas, stop and leave; never use flames or switches near suspected gas, never bypass safety controls, and call a licensed technician when the pilot does not relight promptly.
