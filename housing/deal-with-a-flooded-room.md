## Goal

Make a flooded room safe, stop the water source, remove water, and dry materials quickly enough to reduce damage and mold risk.

## Preconditions

- You can assess the room from dry ground without stepping into unsafe water.
- You have access to shutoff valves, towels, buckets, wet vacuum, fans, or professional help.
- The flood is not from a major natural disaster requiring evacuation.

## Steps

1. **Assess electrical danger first.** Look for submerged outlets, cords, appliances, or water near the breaker panel. → *Expect:* you know whether the room is safe to enter.
2. **Stay out if power may be live in water.** Shut power off only from a dry safe panel, or call emergency services or an electrician. → *Expect:* no one enters energized water.
3. **Stop the water source.** Close the fixture valve, appliance valve, water main, or roof opening if safe. → *Expect:* new water stops entering or slows.
4. **Photograph the room.** Capture water depth, source, damaged floors, walls, and belongings before moving them. → *Expect:* insurance evidence is recorded.
5. **Remove standing water.** Use buckets, mop, squeegee, wet vacuum, or a pump rated for the conditions. → *Expect:* visible standing water decreases.
6. **Move wet items out.** Remove rugs, cardboard, cushions, and low furniture to a dry ventilated area. → *Expect:* absorbent items stop feeding moisture into the room.
7. **Dry the room fast.** Run dehumidifiers and fans only on dry safe power, and ventilate if outdoor humidity is lower. → *Expect:* surfaces feel progressively drier and humidity drops.
8. **Remove wet porous materials when needed.** Pull baseboards, padding, or drywall only if you can do so safely, or hire mitigation. → *Expect:* trapped moisture is exposed for drying.
9. **Call insurance, landlord, or mitigation for significant flooding.** Report water category, source, photos, and time discovered. → *Expect:* professional drying or claim guidance is arranged.

## Decision points

- Floodwater may contain sewage, chemicals, or outdoor runoff → avoid contact and call professional remediation.
- Water depth is rising or structural damage appears → evacuate and call emergency services.
- Wet area is larger than you can dry within 24 to 48 hours → call a water mitigation company.
- You rent → notify the landlord or property manager promptly.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Power cannot be shut off safely:** detect wet panel or submerged path → stay out and call emergency services or the utility.
- **F2 Source unknown:** detect continued water with fixtures off → shut the water main and call a plumber.
- **F3 Drying stalls:** detect damp smell or high humidity after a day → add dehumidification or hire mitigation.
- **F4 Mold appears:** detect visible growth or musty odor → stop disturbance and call a mold professional for large areas.

## Verification

The room is electrically safe or isolated, the water source is stopped, standing water is removed, drying equipment or professional mitigation is active, and damage photos are saved.

## Variations

- Clean supply-water leak: cleanup may be possible if addressed quickly and thoroughly.
- Sewage backup: treat as contaminated and use professional remediation.
- Basement flood: check sump pump operation and do not use extension cords in wet areas.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from electrical shock, contaminated water, and mold. Keep children and pets away, wear protective gear, and avoid sharing photos that show unrelated personal information.
