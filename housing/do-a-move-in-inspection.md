## Goal

On move-in day you document the property's exact condition with dated photos, agree a signed inventory, record meter readings, and report every defect in writing so nothing is later deducted from your deposit.

## Preconditions

- Keys and access, ideally before furniture arrives so rooms are empty and visible.
- A phone/camera with a working date stamp, plus the landlord's inventory or a blank template.
- The tenancy agreement to hand, and the deposit-scheme details.

## Steps

1. **Do a first walkthrough before you unload anything.** Empty rooms reveal scuffs, stains, and damage that furniture will hide for months. → *Expect:* a mental map of every room and problem area.
2. **Photograph everything, systematically.** One wide shot per room, then close-ups of every mark, chip, appliance, and existing damage. Enable the timestamp or shoot a phone with the date visible. → *Expect:* a dated photo set covering all rooms, floors, walls, and appliances.
3. **Test appliances and fixtures and film them working (or not).** Oven, hob, extractor, boiler, taps, toilet flush, window locks, smoke/CO alarms. → *Expect:* a short clip or note per item confirming function or fault.
4. **Read every meter and photograph the dial.** Electricity, gas, and water, with the serial number visible in the shot. → *Expect:* meter type, serial, and reading captured for each utility.
5. **Complete the inventory line by line.** Go through the landlord's list; mark each item's true condition and add anything missing or damaged that they omitted. → *Expect:* an inventory where your notes match your photos.
6. **Report defects in writing the same day.** Email the landlord/agent the full list plus photos, keep a copy, and ask them to acknowledge. → *Expect:* a sent, timestamped message with the defect list attached.
7. **Sign the inventory only when it is accurate.** [BRANCH: inventory matches reality → sign and keep your copy | discrepancies remain → annotate "signed subject to the attached corrections" and send both] → *Expect:* a signed document you and the landlord both hold. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* an unqualified signature on an inaccurate inventory becomes the agreed baseline against your deposit; correct it before signing.
8. **Store the whole record safely.** Back up photos, meter shots, the signed inventory, and the sent email to cloud storage. → *Expect:* one dated, backed-up folder you can retrieve at move-out.

## Decision points

- Landlord pressures you to sign the inventory immediately, unread → slow down; you have the right to inspect first.
- A defect could be a safety issue (gas smell, exposed wiring, non-working CO alarm) → report urgently and do not use the appliance.
- No inventory provided at all → create your own with photos and email it as the record.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Photos have no date:** camera timestamp off → email the set to yourself the same day; the email header dates them.
- **F2 Damage found after signing:** discovered a week later → photograph and report immediately, noting it was missed on inspection; earlier is more credible.
- **F3 Meter dispute at move-out:** no opening reading → without it you may be billed for a predecessor's usage; always capture readings on day one.
- **F4 Landlord ignores the defect email:** no acknowledgement → resend, then log it with the deposit scheme so a record exists independent of the landlord.

## Verification

You hold a dated photo/video set of every room and defect, meter readings with serials, and a signed inventory that matches those photos, and you have sent (and kept) a same-day defect report to the landlord with proof of delivery.

## Variations

- `uk`: an independent inventory clerk often prepares the check-in report; still take your own photos and add comments before signing.
- Furnished lets: inventory each item of furniture and its condition individually, not just the fabric of the building.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: the money at stake is your deposit, and a weak record is how it gets withheld. Flag genuine safety defects (gas, electrical, alarms) as urgent rather than routine, and keep your evidence backed up off-device until the tenancy ends.
