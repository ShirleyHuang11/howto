## Goal

Leave a rental empty, clean, photographed, and ready for inspection so ordinary dirt is not mistaken for damage.

## Preconditions

- You have removed personal belongings or boxed them away from cleaning areas.
- You have the lease, move-in inventory, and any cleaning standard from the landlord or agent.
- You have cleaning supplies, trash bags, gloves, a camera, and access to water and electricity.
- Repairs that require tools, paint, or professional help are identified before the cleaning day.

## Steps

1. **Empty and sort each room.** Remove belongings, trash, food, and loose packaging before cleaning surfaces. → *Expect:* every room is accessible wall to wall.
2. **Clean from high to low.** Dust ceiling corners, vents, light fittings, shelves, blinds, baseboards, then floors. → *Expect:* falling dust no longer lands on already-clean areas.
3. **Deep-clean the kitchen appliances.** Degrease the hob, oven, racks, hood filters, fridge seals, freezer drawers, dishwasher filter, microwave, and sink. → *Expect:* appliances are odor-free, crumb-free, and visibly free of grease.
4. **Reset bathrooms carefully.** Remove limescale, soap residue, hair, mildew, and stains from toilets, tubs, showers, tiles, mirrors, drains, and extractor fans. → *Expect:* fixtures look sanitary and drains are clear.
5. **Hit the deposit-critical spots.** Clean inside cabinets, behind appliances, window tracks, skirting boards, door handles, switches, radiators, closets, and entry thresholds. → *Expect:* a slow inspection finds no obvious missed dirt.
6. **Treat floors last.** Vacuum carpets, mop hard floors, spot-clean marks, and let floors dry before walking through. [BRANCH: carpet cleaning required by lease | ordinary vacuuming acceptable] follow the written standard. → *Expect:* floors are dry, clean, and free of loose debris.
7. **Remove minor wall marks without damage.** Use a suitable eraser or mild cleaner, testing a hidden spot first. → *Expect:* marks are reduced without lifting paint.
8. **Photograph the finished condition.** Take dated wide shots and close-ups of each room, appliances, meters, keys, and any pre-existing defects. → *Expect:* you have a complete photo record before handing over keys.
9. **Dispose and hand over.** Remove final bags, return bins, close windows, turn off appliances, and return all keys as instructed. → *Expect:* the property is locked, empty, and documented.

## Decision points

- Lease requires professional cleaning → decide whether to book a cleaner and keep the invoice.
- Furnished rental → clean under cushions, inside drawers, mattress protectors, and behind movable furniture.
- Damage found during cleaning → photograph before and after, then decide whether a minor repair is safer than leaving it.
- Shared property → agree which person owns each room and keep one shared photo folder.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cleaner damages a surface:** detect fresh scratches, bleaching, or lifted paint → stop using that product, photograph it, and disclose if required.
- **F2 Appliance still smells:** detect odor after cleaning → clean filters, seals, trays, and leave doors open to dry.
- **F3 Inspection says cleaning was incomplete:** detect a deduction or complaint → send dated photos and offer targeted re-clean if keys have not been surrendered.
- **F4 You run out of time:** detect rooms still untouched near handover → prioritize kitchen, bathrooms, floors, and deposit-critical spots first.
- **F5 Trash pickup missed:** detect bags left at the property → arrange removal immediately and keep proof of collection.

## Verification

The property is empty, keys are ready to return, every room and appliance has dated after-clean photos, and no visible trash, loose dirt, grease, or bathroom residue remains.

## Variations

- `professional-clean-required`: keep the cleaner's invoice with the checkout photos.
- `student-housing`: shared kitchens and bathrooms need dated photos showing your assigned areas.
- `pet-occupied-home`: vacuum edges and upholstery repeatedly, then check for odor after one hour.
- `winter-move`: leave heating and ventilation in the lease-required state to avoid damp claims.

## Safety & privacy

Wear gloves and ventilate when using cleaning chemicals. Never mix bleach with ammonia or acidic descalers. Keep photos focused on property condition, not private documents, neighbors, or children. Medium risk comes from chemical exposure, ladder use, and deposit money at stake.
