## Goal

Thaw a frozen water pipe using gentle heat while reducing burst, fire, and electrical risks.

## Preconditions

- A faucet has little or no water flow during freezing conditions.
- You can access the suspected frozen pipe safely.
- You have towels and a safe heat source such as a hair dryer, heat lamp, warm towels, or space heater used as directed.

## Steps

1. **Check for a burst first.** Look for split pipe, spraying water, bulging walls, or ceiling leaks. → *Expect:* you know whether water must be shut off immediately.
2. **Shut the water main if a pipe has burst.** Close the main and call a plumber for repair. → *Expect:* active water flow stops or slows.
3. **Open the affected tap.** Open both hot and cold handles for that fixture, or the single mixer to a warm position. → *Expect:* steam and meltwater have a pressure-relief path.
4. **Locate the frozen section.** Feel for very cold sections and check pipes near exterior walls, crawlspaces, cabinets, and unheated areas. → *Expect:* the likely frozen segment is identified.
5. **Warm from the open faucet back toward the blockage.** Apply gentle heat starting near the faucet and moving along the pipe. → *Expect:* water begins to drip, then flow more steadily.
6. **Use only safe heat.** Use a hair dryer, warm towels, or a listed space heater kept away from water and combustibles. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never use an open flame, torch, charcoal heater, or fuel-burning heater indoors to thaw a pipe, because it can start a fire or produce carbon monoxide. → *Expect:* pipe warms without scorching or flame.
7. **Keep heating until full flow returns.** Continue after the first trickle so ice farther back can melt. → *Expect:* flow becomes normal at the open tap.
8. **Inspect for leaks while pressure returns.** Watch the pipe, walls, and ceiling as water flow resumes. → *Expect:* no spraying, dripping, or new stains appear.
9. **Prevent refreezing.** Insulate the area, open cabinets, keep heat on, and let the faucet drip during continued severe cold. → *Expect:* the pipe remains flowing.

## Decision points

- Pipe is inaccessible inside a wall or ceiling → call a plumber rather than opening finished surfaces blindly.
- Electrical outlets or cords are wet → stop and call a professional.
- No flow returns after careful heating → call a plumber before increasing heat.
- Multiple fixtures lose water → suspect a frozen main or larger section and call the utility or plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pipe bursts during thaw:** detect spray, rushing water, or spreading stain → shut the main, avoid electricity, and call a plumber.
- **F2 Unsafe heater setup:** detect extension cord in wet area or heater near combustibles → unplug from a dry location and reposition or stop.
- **F3 Faucet closed too soon:** detect pressure noise or no meltwater path → reopen the tap before continuing heat.
- **F4 Refreeze overnight:** detect reduced flow again → keep a drip running and improve insulation or heat.

## Verification

The affected faucet has normal flow, the pipe and nearby surfaces show no leaks, and freeze protection is in place before temperatures drop again.

## Variations

- Plastic pipe: use lower heat and keep hot devices moving to avoid softening.
- Metal pipe: it conducts heat well but can hide ice farther along the run.
- Apartment: call maintenance because frozen pipes may affect shared plumbing.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from burst water, electric shock, fire, and carbon monoxide. Use only flameless heat and call a plumber for inaccessible, leaking, or repeatedly frozen pipes.
