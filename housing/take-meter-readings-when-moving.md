## Goal

Record accurate gas, electric, and water meter readings at move-in or move-out so billing responsibility starts and ends at the right point.

## Preconditions

- You have legal access to the property and meter areas.
- You know whether the home has gas, electricity, water, heat, or prepaid meters.
- You have a phone or camera and enough light to photograph displays.
- You can contact the supplier, landlord, agent, or building manager if a meter is locked away.

## Steps

1. **Find every meter.** Check cupboards, basements, exterior boxes, utility rooms, garages, hallways, or building meter banks. → *Expect:* each gas, electric, and water meter is located or marked inaccessible.
2. **Match the meter to the home.** Compare unit labels, meter serial numbers, and supplier records if available. → *Expect:* you are reading your meter, not a neighbor's.
3. **Wake or cycle displays.** Press the display button on smart or digital meters until the total import reading appears. → *Expect:* a stable number and unit are visible.
4. **Photograph the reading.** Include the number, meter serial, and enough surroundings to identify the meter. → *Expect:* the image proves both reading and meter identity.
5. **Add the date context.** Capture a phone timestamp, newspaper, key handover email, or written note in one photo if helpful. → *Expect:* the reading is tied to the move date.
6. **Write the numbers down.** Record each reading exactly, including decimal treatment if the supplier instructs it. → *Expect:* you can submit values without re-opening the meter box.
7. **Submit readings promptly.** [BRANCH: closing old account | opening new account] send final readings or opening readings to the relevant supplier. → *Expect:* the account shows an actual reading, not an estimate.
8. **Save confirmations.** Store photos, submitted numbers, account references, and confirmation emails together. → *Expect:* future billing disputes have evidence.

## Decision points

- Meter is unsafe or behind locked equipment → do not force access; ask the landlord, building manager, or supplier.
- Multiple meters are unlabeled → use serial numbers and ask the supplier to confirm which one serves the address.
- Smart meter has many screens → photograph all likely total import screens and ask the supplier which value they need.
- Prepaid meter has credit remaining → record credit and ask supplier or landlord how transfer or refund works.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Read the wrong meter:** detect impossible usage or neighbor label → submit corrected serial and photo evidence.
- **F2 Photo is blurry:** detect unreadable digits → retake before leaving, or ask agent to witness a new reading.
- **F3 Supplier uses an estimate:** detect estimated final or first bill → challenge with dated actual readings.
- **F4 Decimal entered incorrectly:** detect bill much higher or lower than expected → compare with the photo and request correction.
- **F5 Meter inaccessible on handover:** detect locked room or missing key → document the attempt and request a witnessed reading immediately.

## Verification

All meters serving the property have dated photos, written readings, and submitted opening or closing confirmations from the correct account.

## Variations

- `smart-meter`: account portals may show readings, but a move-date photo is still useful.
- `apartment-meter-bank`: serial number matching is essential where many meters sit together.
- `water-included-rental`: photograph any private water meter anyway if visible.
- `prepaid-meter`: record credit, debt screens, and top-up method as well as usage.

## Safety & privacy

Do not open sealed electrical panels, climb unstable objects, or enter restricted plant rooms. Meter photos may reveal address or account labels, so share only with suppliers, landlords, agents, or dispute bodies. Low risk becomes higher around damaged gas or electrical equipment.
