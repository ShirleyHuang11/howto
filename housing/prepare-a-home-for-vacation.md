## Goal

The home is left safe and low-cost for a multi-day absence: water and power hazards removed, food managed, mail handled, a spare key with someone trusted, and enough signs of life to not advertise an empty house.

## Preconditions

- You know the departure date/time and the return date (drives fridge, timer, and mail-hold windows).
- Access to the main water shutoff, the breaker/fuse panel, and the thermostat.
- At least one trusted local contact (neighbor, friend, family) who can hold a key.
- Any pets/plants have a confirmed sitter or plan (out of scope here, but must be settled first).

## Steps

1. **Decide the water strategy by trip length and climate.** Short trip (2-4 days), mild weather: leave water on. Longer trip, or any freeze risk while away: shut the main valve. → *Expect:* a clear decision written down so nothing is half-done.
2. **Shut off the main water valve if you chose to.** Turn the main clockwise until it stops, then open a low faucet to drain pressure. In freeze risk, also shut off and drain outdoor spigots. → *Expect:* faucets run to a trickle then stop; toilet tank refills once and goes quiet.
3. **Turn off the water heater or set it to "vacation"/pilot.** Electric: flip its dedicated breaker or the vacation setting. Gas: turn the dial to "vac". → *Expect:* no reason for the heater to cycle while no one draws hot water.
4. **Set the thermostat to a holding range, not off.** Summer: ~28-30 C (82-85 F). Winter: no lower than ~13 C (55 F) to protect pipes. Never fully off in freezing climates. → *Expect:* thermostat shows the hold setpoint, HVAC idle but armed.
5. **Manage the fridge and perishables.** Eat, freeze, or bin anything that spoils before your return; check the produce drawer and any open dairy. Keep the fridge running (do not unplug for a normal trip). → *Expect:* nothing left that will rot; no science experiments waiting for you.
6. **Take out all trash and run the disposal.** Empty every bin, especially kitchen and bathroom; a week-old bin is odor and pests. → *Expect:* bins empty, liners fresh, sink drain clear.
7. **Unplug standby electronics and reduce phantom load.** TV, computers, chargers, coffee maker, toaster. Leave the fridge, and anything on a lived-in-look timer. → *Expect:* fewer glowing standby lights; only intended loads powered.
8. **Set the lived-in look on timers.** Put one or two lamps on a mechanical or smart timer with slightly randomized evening hours; optionally a radio. Do NOT leave a porch light on 24/7 (that reads as away). → *Expect:* a lamp cycles on at dusk during a test.
9. **Handle mail and deliveries.** Place a mail hold (post office site/app or in person), pause package deliveries and subscription boxes, and cancel or redirect the newspaper. A stuffed mailbox is the classic empty-house tell. → *Expect:* hold confirmation number saved; no scheduled deliveries in the window.
10. **Give a spare key and info to your trusted contact.** Hand over a physical key (not hidden outside), your return date, a phone number, and which lamp is on a timer so they are not alarmed. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never hide a key under the mat or a fake rock. Burglars check there first. → *Expect:* contact has the key in hand and your dates.
11. **Lock and arm on the way out.** All windows and doors locked, garage down, any alarm armed, sliding doors pinned. → *Expect:* every entry point checked and secured; a final walk-through done.

## Decision points

- Freeze possible while away (any winter trip in a cold climate) → shut and drain water OR keep heat at 13 C minimum; do not do neither.
- Trip under ~4 days in mild weather → leaving water on and heater on vacation mode is reasonable; full shutdown is optional.
- No trusted local contact → prioritize a mail hold, timers, and a smart camera/doorbell over a hidden key; do not compensate by hiding a key outside.
- Have a security system → notify the monitoring company of dates and give the contact the disarm code.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Frozen/burst pipe:** detect on return by no water or a wet ceiling → had you shut and drained, or held heat at 13 C, this is prevented; if it happens, shut the main immediately and call a plumber before touching power near water.
- **F2 Water heater ran dry or was left drawing power oddly:** relight/reset per the unit's label after restoring water; never power an electric tank heater with the tank drained (element burns out).
- **F3 Fridge fails while away:** a smart plug or fridge alarm catches it; on return, if food is warm and refrozen-looking, discard rather than risk it.
- **F4 Overflowing mailbox / piled packages:** the hold failed or a sender ignored it → ask the contact to collect visible mail mid-trip.
- **F5 Locked out on return (contact has the only key):** confirm before leaving that you also carry your own key; keep the contact's number reachable from the road.

## Verification

Water is in its chosen state (off+drained, or on with heater on vacation), thermostat holds a safe setpoint, the fridge runs with no perishables left to spoil, all trash is out, a mail hold is confirmed, at least one lamp is on a working timer, a trusted person physically holds a spare key with your dates, and every door and window is locked. No key is hidden outdoors.

## Variations

- `apartment`: no main-valve or water-heater access usually; skip water shutdown, focus on fridge, trash, mail hold, timers, and telling a neighbor.
- Long trips (weeks+): shut water, drain lines, consider turning the fridge off and propping it open after emptying, and forward mail rather than holding it (holds have a max duration).
- Smart-home: use app-scheduled lights, a video doorbell, and a leak/temperature sensor near the water heater for remote alerts.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: the money hazards are a burst pipe and spoiled food; the security hazard is signaling an empty home. Do not post travel dates publicly before or during the trip. Share your key and schedule only with people you trust, and never substitute a hidden outdoor key for a real one.
