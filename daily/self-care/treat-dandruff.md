## Goal

Scalp flaking and itch are reduced using appropriate shampoo contact time, rotation, and scalp care while recognizing when it may be a medical condition.

## Preconditions

- Access to a shower or sink and a dandruff shampoo.
- Shampoo options may include zinc pyrithione, selenium sulfide, ketoconazole, salicylic acid, coal tar, or tea tree oil.
- No open scalp wounds, severe swelling, or sudden hair loss.
- You can follow label directions and avoid getting medicated shampoo in your eyes.
- A clean towel and gentle regular shampoo are available.

## Steps

1. **Choose one active ingredient.** Start with a dandruff shampoo that matches your scalp tolerance and label instructions. → *Expect:* you know which active ingredient you are testing.
2. **Wet hair and scalp fully.** Use warm water and part the hair enough to reach the scalp. → *Expect:* the scalp is wet, not just the hair surface.
3. **Massage into the scalp.** Apply shampoo to the scalp with fingertips, not nails. → *Expect:* the lather touches the flaky or itchy areas.
4. **Leave it on.** Keep the medicated shampoo on for the label time, often 3 to 5 minutes. → *Expect:* the shampoo has contact time before rinsing.
5. **Rinse thoroughly.** Rinse until the water runs clear and the scalp no longer feels slippery. → *Expect:* no medicated residue remains in the hair.
6. **Repeat on schedule.** Use 2 to 3 times weekly at first, then reduce as flaking improves. → *Expect:* flakes and itch trend down over several weeks.
7. **Rotate if needed.** [BRANCH: improving | not improving] keep the same shampoo if improving, or alternate active ingredients after a few weeks if not. → *Expect:* the routine is adjusted without over-washing.

## Decision points

- Flakes are greasy, yellow, or extend to eyebrows, beard, chest, or ears → seborrheic dermatitis may need clinician guidance.
- Thick silvery plaques or bleeding cracks appear → consider psoriasis or eczema and see a doctor.
- Hair loss, scalp pain, pus, ring-shaped rash, or swollen lymph nodes occur → see a doctor promptly.
- Shampoo irritates or burns → stop that product and rinse well.
- No improvement after 4 weeks of correct use → ask a clinician or dermatologist.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rinsed too soon:** flakes persist because contact time was skipped → set a timer for the label dwell time.
- **F2 Scalp irritated:** burning, redness, or tightness after washing → stop the product, rinse, and switch to a gentler option after the skin calms.
- **F3 Hair feels dry:** strands feel rough or brittle → condition the lengths and ends while keeping conditioner off the scalp if it worsens oiliness.
- **F4 Product stops working:** flakes return after initial success → rotate to a different active ingredient once or twice weekly.
- **F5 Medical rash:** pain, swelling, pus, crusting, or patchy hair loss → see a doctor.

## Verification

After 2 to 4 weeks, scalp itch and visible flakes are clearly less frequent without redness, pain, or worsening hair loss.

## Variations

- Curly or textured hair: focus shampoo on the scalp and condition lengths to prevent dryness.
- Color-treated hair: check labels for color safety and test frequency conservatively.
- Beard dandruff: use a small amount on beard skin, avoiding eyes and lips.
- Sensitive skin: start with the least irritating option and increase only if tolerated.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but medicated shampoos can irritate eyes and skin. Follow the label, keep products away from children, and seek medical care for infection signs, severe inflammation, sudden hair loss, painful plaques, or symptoms that do not improve with correct use.
