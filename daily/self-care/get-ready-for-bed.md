## Goal

You are in bed at your intended time with tomorrow prepared, the home secured, and conditions set for falling asleep.

## Preconditions

- An intended bedtime (from the required wake time minus ~7–9 hours).
- The home's closing-up points known: doors, windows, appliances.

## Steps

1. **Start winding down 30–60 minutes before the target.** Finish active tasks; dim the lights. → *Expect:* nothing "quick" is still being started inside the wind-down window.
2. **Prepare tomorrow's friction points.** Lay out clothes, pack the bag, note the top task for tomorrow; set the alarm and *verify AM/PM and volume*. → *Expect:* alarm confirmed set for the right time; morning needs staged.
3. **Secure the home.** Lock doors, check the stove is off, blow out candles, water/let out pets per routine. → *Expect:* a consistent short checklist mentally ticked — same order every night makes omissions feel wrong.
4. **Do the bathroom routine.** Brush teeth (`daily/brush-your-teeth`), wash face, medications if prescribed. → *Expect:* routine complete; water off.
5. **Set the bedroom conditions.** Cool (≈17–19 °C), dark (curtains/eye mask), quiet (or steady white noise); phone charging *away from the pillow*, on do-not-disturb. → *Expect:* room dark, cool, quiet; phone out of arm's reach.
6. **Get into bed at the target time and do something calm and screen-free.** Read a few pages, breathe slowly; lights fully out when drowsy. → *Expect:* lights out within ~15–20 minutes of getting in.

## Decision points

- Not sleepy at lights-out after ~20 minutes → get up, do something boring in dim light, return when drowsy — lying awake trains the bed as a frustration place.
- Racing thoughts → park them on paper (the step-2 note) and tell yourself they're stored for tomorrow.
- Late caffeine/heavy meal/intense exercise → note it as tonight's likely culprit and shift its cutoff earlier tomorrow (caffeine ~8 h, heavy food ~3 h, hard exercise ~2 h before bed).

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Doomscrolling ate the wind-down:** the phone's location rule (step 5) is the fix — charging across the room removes the in-bed loop at the source.
- **F2 Alarm set wrong (AM/PM, silent mode):** the verify clause in step 2 exists because this failure is discovered at the worst possible time.
- **F3 Woke at 3 a.m. certain the door is unlocked:** the fixed-order check in step 3 is what lets you trust the memory and stay in bed.

## Verification

Alarm verified, home secured, tomorrow staged, room dark/cool/quiet, phone out of reach, and lights out within the intended window.

## Variations

- Shift workers: the same sequence anchored to *your* sleep window, plus blackout curtains doing the sun's job.
- Shared households: quiet-hours coordination replaces some autonomy in step 5 — earplugs and masks are the portable version.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The stove-and-candles check in step 3 is the one genuinely safety-critical item; everything else protects tomorrow-you.
