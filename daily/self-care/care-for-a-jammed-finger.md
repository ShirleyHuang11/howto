## Goal

A jammed finger is cooled, protected, and kept gently mobile while signs of fracture, dislocation, tendon injury, or circulation trouble are identified.

## Preconditions

- Ice or cold pack, towel, optional finger splint or tape, and a neighboring uninjured finger.
- The finger is not severely deformed, numb, blue, or unable to move at all.
- Rings are removable before swelling increases.
- You can compare the injured finger with the same finger on the other hand.
- You can seek care if pain, shape, or motion suggests a serious injury.

## Steps

1. **Remove rings.** Take off rings from the injured hand right away. → *Expect:* swelling will not trap jewelry.
2. **Check shape and circulation.** Compare alignment, color, temperature, feeling, and nail-bed refill with the other hand. → *Expect:* you know whether urgent care is needed.
3. **Ice through a towel.** Apply cold for 15 to 20 minutes, then remove it. → *Expect:* swelling and throbbing begin to settle without skin numbness from ice.
4. **Rest from impact.** Stop the sport or task that caused the jam. → *Expect:* the finger is not being repeatedly hit or bent.
5. **Buddy-tape lightly.** Tape the injured finger to a neighboring finger with padding between them, if it sits straight. → *Expect:* the finger is supported but not numb, blue, or squeezed.
6. **Move gently.** Bend and straighten only within a comfortable range a few times daily. → *Expect:* stiffness is limited without sharp pain.
7. **Recheck after swelling drops.** Look again for deformity, major bruising, inability to fully straighten, or worsening pain. → *Expect:* serious signs are either absent or prompt medical care.

## Decision points

- Finger is crooked, shortened, rotated, or visibly deformed → see a doctor or urgent care today.
- You cannot straighten or bend the finger, or the fingertip droops → seek medical evaluation.
- Numbness, blue color, cold finger, or severe swelling develops → seek urgent care.
- Pain is intense, bruising is heavy, or tenderness is directly over bone → get an X-ray.
- Symptoms do not improve after 2 to 3 days → see a clinician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tape too tight:** fingertip tingles, turns pale, blue, or cold → remove tape immediately and retape loosely after color returns.
- **F2 Missed fracture:** pain is focal over bone or deformity appears after swelling drops → seek urgent care or X-ray.
- **F3 Stiffness:** finger becomes hard to bend after total rest → use gentle range-of-motion if serious injury is ruled out.
- **F4 Skin irritation:** tape causes redness or blisters → add padding, use hypoallergenic tape, or switch to a splint.
- **F5 Worsening swelling:** swelling increases despite rest and ice → elevate and seek care if severe or persistent.

## Verification

The finger remains straight with normal color and feeling, swelling is controlled, and gentle motion is possible without severe pain.

## Variations

- Sports sideline: remove rings and assess deformity before deciding whether to continue.
- Child injury: seek care sooner because growth plates can be injured.
- Nail-bed injury: blood under the nail with severe pressure pain needs clinician advice.
- Work needs: use a protective splint only if it does not hide worsening circulation signs.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because fractures, dislocations, and tendon injuries can look like a simple jam. See a doctor for deformity, severe pain, inability to move or straighten, numbness, blue or cold finger, heavy bruising, open wound, or symptoms that do not improve in a few days.
