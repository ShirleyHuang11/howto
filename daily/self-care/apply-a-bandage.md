## Goal

A minor wound is cleaned and covered with a bandage that protects it without sticking to the wound or cutting off circulation.

## Preconditions

- Clean water, soap, gauze or clean cloth, adhesive bandage or sterile pad and tape.
- Hands washed or gloves available.
- The wound is minor: bleeding is controllable, edges are close, and no deep object remains.

## Steps

1. **Wash hands.** Clean hands with soap and water before touching supplies. → *Expect:* hands are clean enough to handle the wound.
2. **Stop bleeding.** Press clean gauze or cloth on the wound for several minutes. → *Expect:* bleeding slows or stops under steady pressure.
3. **Rinse the wound.** Run clean water over the wound and remove visible dirt with clean tweezers if easy. → *Expect:* the wound bed looks free of grit.
4. **Clean around it.** Wash surrounding skin with soap, keeping soap out of the wound when possible. → *Expect:* nearby skin is clean and less oily.
5. **Choose the right size.** Pick a bandage with a pad larger than the wound on all sides. → *Expect:* adhesive will land on intact skin, not in the wound.
6. **Place the no-stick pad.** Center the pad directly over the wound surface. → *Expect:* the wound is covered by pad material only.
7. **Secure without tension.** Press adhesive edges down or tape gauze in place without stretching tightly. → *Expect:* bandage stays put and skin color remains normal.
8. **Check movement.** Bend the joint or move the area normally. → *Expect:* the bandage does not gap, peel, or pinch.
9. **Set change cadence.** Change daily, when wet or dirty, or sooner if drainage soaks through. → *Expect:* the covering stays clean and dry.
10. **Recheck healing.** Look for spreading redness, warmth, swelling, pus, or increasing pain at each change. → *Expect:* the wound looks calmer over time.

## Decision points

- Bleeding does not stop after steady pressure → seek medical care.
- Cut is deep, gaping, from a bite, or contains embedded debris → do not close with a simple bandage.
- Adhesive allergy → use non-adhesive pad with wrap or paper tape on tolerant skin.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bandage sticks:** detect pain and scab lifting during removal; soak with clean water and use a no-stick pad next time.
- **F2 Too tight:** detect numbness, tingling, swelling, or pale skin; remove and reapply looser.
- **F3 Wet dressing:** detect soaked pad or trapped moisture; replace promptly after cleaning.
- **F4 Infection signs:** detect spreading redness, warmth, pus, fever, or worsening pain; get medical advice.

## Verification

The wound is clean, bleeding controlled, fully covered by a nonstick pad, and the bandage is secure without restricting circulation.

## Variations

- Fingertip: use a fingertip bandage or crossed strips so adhesive does not bunch.
- Joint: use flexible fabric bandage and check motion before finishing.
- Scrape: a thin layer of plain petroleum jelly can reduce sticking if appropriate.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for minor wounds. Update tetanus guidance for dirty wounds and seek urgent care for severe bleeding, deep punctures, bites, or loss of function.
