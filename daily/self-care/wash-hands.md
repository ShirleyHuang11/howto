## Goal

Your hands are hygienically clean and dry, appropriate for eating, food handling, or after contamination.

## Preconditions

- A sink with running water and soap (bar or dispenser).
- Something to dry with: clean towel, paper towel, or air dryer.
- Sleeves pushed up; rings/watch ideally slid off or moved.

## Steps

1. **Turn on the water and wet both hands fully.** Comfortable temperature — hot water is not required for hygiene. → *Expect:* both hands wet to the wrists.
2. **Apply soap.** One pump of liquid soap or rub the bar between palms. → *Expect:* enough soap to cover both hands.
3. **Lather and scrub for at least 20 seconds.** Cover all surfaces in sequence: palms together, backs of hands, between fingers interlaced, backs of fingers, both thumbs (grip and rotate), fingertips scrubbed against palms. → *Expect:* full lather on every surface; ~20 s elapsed (about the time of singing "Happy Birthday" twice).
4. **Rinse thoroughly under running water.** Fingers pointing down. → *Expect:* no visible soap remains; water runs clear off the fingertips.
5. **Turn off the tap.** In public restrooms, use the paper towel or your elbow on lever taps to avoid re-contaminating. → *Expect:* tap closed without bare-palm contact where avoidable.
6. **Dry completely.** Pat with a clean/paper towel or use the air dryer until fully dry — damp hands transfer microbes far more readily. → *Expect:* hands feel dry, including between fingers.

## Decision points

- No soap available → rub vigorously under running water (partial benefit), then use ≥60 % alcohol hand sanitizer if available; sanitizer alone is the fallback when there's no sink.
- Hands visibly greasy or soiled with dirt/food → sanitizer won't work; washing is required.
- After handling raw meat, using the toilet, sneezing, or caring for someone sick → this recipe is mandatory, not optional.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rushed scrub (<10 s):** the common failure — repeat step 3 fully; timing is the active ingredient.
- **F2 Soap dispenser empty:** check adjacent sinks; fall back to the no-soap path above.
- **F3 Nothing to dry with:** shake off excess and air-dry fully before touching food or your face.

## Verification

All hand surfaces were lathered for ~20 seconds, rinsed clear, and hands are completely dry, without re-touching the dirty tap.

## Variations

- Food-service and clinical settings mandate longer protocols (nail brushes, elbow taps, specific durations) — follow the posted standard, which supersedes this recipe.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Very hot water causes scalds and dry skin without hygiene benefit; moisturize if washing frequently.
