## Goal

Throat pain is reduced enough to swallow, rest, and speak lightly while you watch for signs that need medical care.

## Preconditions

- You can swallow saliva and breathe normally.
- You have clean water, salt, and a cup or mug.
- Lozenges are only for people old enough to use them without choking.
- Check medicine labels if you plan to use pain relievers.

## Steps

1. **Check for urgent symptoms first.** Look for trouble breathing, drooling, inability to swallow, severe one-sided swelling, stiff neck, rash, or confusion. → *Expect:* you either proceed with home care or seek urgent care immediately.
2. **Make a salt-water gargle.** Stir about 1/2 teaspoon salt into a cup of warm water until dissolved. → *Expect:* the water tastes salty but not painfully strong.
3. **Gargle without swallowing.** Tilt the head back, gargle 10 to 15 seconds, spit, and repeat until the cup is gone. → *Expect:* mucus loosens and the raw feeling dulls for a while.
4. **Sip warm fluids.** Drink warm water, tea, broth, or honey-lemon water if honey is safe for you. → *Expect:* swallowing feels smoother and the throat stays moist.
5. **Use a lozenge or hard candy.** Let it dissolve slowly rather than chewing it. → *Expect:* saliva production increases and the scratchy feeling eases.
6. **Add humidity.** [BRANCH: humidifier | steam] run a clean humidifier, or sit near a warm shower steam source without burning yourself. → *Expect:* dry air irritation decreases within 10 to 20 minutes.
7. **Rest the voice.** Speak quietly only when needed and avoid whispering, which can strain the throat. → *Expect:* talking causes less scraping pain.
8. **Track red flags.** See a doctor for fever above 101.5 F or 38.6 C, symptoms longer than 3 to 5 days, pus on tonsils, dehydration, repeated strep exposure, or pain that keeps worsening. → *Expect:* home care stays limited to mild, improving symptoms.

## Decision points

- Pain is mild with runny nose or cough → supportive care is usually reasonable.
- Fever, swollen tender neck glands, no cough, or white patches appear → arrange medical testing for strep or other infection.
- You cannot swallow fluids, are drooling, or have high fever with severe throat pain → seek urgent medical care.
- Symptoms follow smoke, chemicals, or a new food or medication → remove the exposure and consider medical advice.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gargle burns or gags:** detect stinging, nausea, or coughing → dilute with more water or skip gargling and use warm sips.
- **F2 Lozenges are unsafe:** detect choking risk, sleepiness, or young child use → avoid lozenges and use fluids or chilled soft foods.
- **F3 Dry room keeps irritating throat:** detect worse pain on waking or in heated air → increase humidity and drink water before bed.
- **F4 Red flags develop:** detect inability to swallow, high fever, rash, breathing trouble, or severe one-sided swelling → see a doctor urgently.

## Verification

Within 30 minutes, you can swallow small sips of fluid with less pain and no urgent red-flag symptom is present.

## Variations

- `no-salt`: use warm fluids, honey if appropriate, and humidity.
- `nighttime`: keep water by the bed and run a clean humidifier away from bedding.
- `voice-use-needed`: use a microphone, text, or short spoken phrases instead of forcing volume.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when swallowing and breathing are normal. Avoid giving honey to children under 1 year old. Follow painkiller label limits, avoid mixing duplicate ingredients, and seek medical care at the thresholds listed in the steps and decision points.
