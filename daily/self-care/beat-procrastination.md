## Goal

Start a task you have been avoiding by making the next action small, visible, and time-limited.

## Preconditions

- A task you are avoiding but still intend to do.
- A timer or clock.
- Enough information to identify the first physical or digital action.

## Steps

1. **Name the avoided task plainly.** Write one sentence: "I am avoiding X." → *Expect:* the task is visible outside your head.
2. **Find the real blocker.** [BRANCH: unclear | unpleasant | too large | fear of judgment] Pick the main reason without making a courtroom case. → *Expect:* you know what kind of friction you are facing.
3. **Shrink to the first visible action.** Convert "do taxes" into "open the tax folder" or "find last year's return." → *Expect:* the step can be done in under two minutes.
4. **Remove one friction point.** Clear the desk, open the document, plug in the laptop, silence one app, or gather the tool. → *Expect:* starting requires fewer moves than before.
5. **Set a 5-minute start.** Commit to working until the timer ends, with permission to stop after. → *Expect:* the task has a short doorway, not an afternoon-shaped threat.
6. **Work badly on purpose if needed.** Make the ugly first draft, messy list, or rough outline. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not submit, send, delete, or pay anything during the rough-start phase without a separate review. → *Expect:* some imperfect output exists.
7. **Timebox the next round.** If momentum appears, set 15-25 minutes and define what done for that block means. → *Expect:* progress has a container.
8. **Close with self-compassion and a next cue.** Note what helped, skip insults, and write the next action before leaving. → *Expect:* returning later is easier.

## Decision points

- The task is unclear → spend the first block defining the next question, not forcing execution.
- The task is emotionally loaded → use a shorter timer and consider doing it near a supportive person.
- The task depends on someone else → send the specific request, then switch to a task you control.
- The deadline is today → use timeboxing and reduce scope before chasing perfection.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Planning disguise:** detect elaborate systems with no task contact, recover by doing one visible action now.
- **F2 Shame spiral:** detect self-attacks, recover by naming the next action neutrally.
- **F3 Infinite first step:** detect a step that still takes an hour, recover by shrinking it again.
- **F4 Distraction leak:** detect tabs or messages pulling you away, recover by blocking or moving them for one timer.

## Verification

You completed a 5-minute start, produced or changed something concrete, and recorded the next action without relying on guilt.

## Variations

- Household chore: make the first action physical, such as picking up one item.
- Writing task: create a terrible outline before editing.
- Admin task: gather documents first, then schedule a separate submission review.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The main privacy issue is leaving sensitive papers or screens exposed while removing friction. Protect irreversible actions with a deliberate review step.
