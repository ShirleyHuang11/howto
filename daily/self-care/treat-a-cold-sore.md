## Goal

A cold sore is treated early, kept clean, and managed so it heals while reducing spread to other people and other body sites.

## Preconditions

- Tingling, burning, blistering, or crusting on or near the lip that matches previous cold sores.
- Access to handwashing, petroleum jelly or lip balm, and optional antiviral cream or prescribed antiviral tablets.
- No eye symptoms, widespread rash, severe immune suppression, or first severe outbreak with fever. Those need medical care.

## Steps

1. **Start at the tingle.** Apply over-the-counter antiviral cream as directed, or start prescribed antiviral tablets if you already have them for outbreaks. → *Expect:* treatment begins before or soon after blisters appear.
2. **Wash before and after touching it.** Use soap and water any time you apply medicine or balm. → *Expect:* lower risk of spreading virus to eyes, fingers, or other people.
3. **Keep it moist.** Apply petroleum jelly or bland balm to reduce cracking. → *Expect:* the sore feels less tight and is less likely to split.
4. **Do not pick.** Let blisters and crusts stay in place. Picking delays healing and spreads virus. → *Expect:* crust remains intact until it loosens naturally.
5. **Avoid sharing mouth-contact items.** Do not share cups, utensils, lip balm, towels, razors, or kissing while the sore is active. → *Expect:* less chance of passing it on.
6. **Avoid oral contact.** Skip oral sex during tingling, blisters, or crusting because HSV can spread to genitals. → *Expect:* no direct contact with active lesions.
7. **Use sun and wind protection.** Apply SPF lip balm when outdoors and protect cracked skin from wind. → *Expect:* less irritation and fewer trigger exposures.
8. **Track healing.** Most cold sores crust and heal in about 7 to 10 days, sometimes up to 2 weeks. → *Expect:* pain and crusting steadily decrease.

## Decision points

- You get frequent outbreaks, severe outbreaks, or outbreaks before important events → ask a clinician about prescription antivirals.
- The sore is near the eye, eye redness occurs, or vision changes → seek urgent care.
- It is your first outbreak, lasts more than 2 weeks, or occurs with high fever or immune suppression → see a doctor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sore cracks and bleeds:** rinse gently, apply petroleum jelly, and avoid stretching the lip.
- **F2 Cream irritates skin:** stop that product and ask a pharmacist or clinician for alternatives.
- **F3 New sores appear elsewhere:** avoid touching them and seek medical advice, especially near eyes or fingers.
- **F4 Outbreaks keep recurring:** document timing and triggers, then discuss suppressive treatment with a clinician.

## Verification

The sore is treated early if possible, kept moist and unpicked, contact-spread precautions are in place, and healing improves within 7 to 14 days.

## Variations

- Prescription tablets: work best when taken at the first tingle according to your clinician's plan.
- Makeup: avoid applying over an open blister, and discard products that touched the sore.
- Children: prevent sharing cups and towels, and watch for dehydration if mouth pain limits drinking.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because HSV spreads by contact and eye involvement can be serious. See a doctor for eye symptoms, frequent outbreaks, symptoms lasting more than 2 weeks, severe pain, dehydration, pregnancy concerns, or immune suppression.
