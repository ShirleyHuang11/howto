## Goal

Nasal passages are rinsed with sterile saline using a neti pot or squeeze bottle without introducing unsafe water or forcing fluid.

## Preconditions

- Distilled water, sterile water, or tap water boiled for 1 minute and cooled before use.
- Premixed saline packet or non-iodized salt measured according to the device instructions.
- Clean neti pot or nasal rinse bottle.
- Sink, tissues, and time to drain afterward.

## Steps

1. **Use safe water only.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* unsafe water can cause rare but severe infection, so use distilled, sterile, or previously boiled and cooled water → *Expect:* rinse water source is confirmed safe before mixing.
2. **Mix saline.** Add the saline packet or measured salt to lukewarm safe water and dissolve fully → *Expect:* the solution is clear and feels close to body temperature.
3. **Wash hands and device.** Clean hands and use a clean pot or bottle → *Expect:* no old residue or visible debris remains.
4. **Lean over the sink.** Tilt your head forward and slightly to one side, keeping the forehead and chin roughly level → *Expect:* the upper nostril is positioned above the lower nostril.
5. **Pour gently.** Place the spout at the upper nostril and let saline flow without squeezing hard [BRANCH: neti pot | squeeze bottle] → *Expect:* saline exits the lower nostril or mouth without sharp pain.
6. **Breathe through your mouth.** Keep the mouth open and do not inhale through the nose during the rinse → *Expect:* breathing remains easy while fluid flows.
7. **Switch sides.** Use the remaining solution on the other nostril with the opposite head tilt → *Expect:* both sides feel rinsed.
8. **Drain without force.** Lean forward, gently blow through one nostril at a time, and avoid hard pressure → *Expect:* excess saline drains out over several minutes.
9. **Clean and dry the device.** Wash the pot or bottle and air-dry completely → *Expect:* the device is clean, open, and dry for next use.

## Decision points

- Saline burns → check salt concentration and water temperature.
- Fluid will not flow → adjust head angle, clear the nostril gently, or stop and try later.
- Ear pressure occurs → stop, drain gently, and avoid forceful blowing.
- You have frequent infections, nosebleeds, or recent sinus surgery → ask a clinician before rinsing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Unsafe water used:** detect tap water was not boiled or sterile, recover by discarding the mix and starting over with safe water.
- **F2 Burning rinse:** detect stinging, recover by remaking isotonic saline with correct salt and lukewarm water.
- **F3 Water in throat:** detect salty taste or coughing, recover by leaning farther forward and breathing through the mouth.
- **F4 Ear fullness:** detect pressure or popping, recover by stopping and draining gently without force.
- **F5 Contaminated device:** detect slime, odor, or residue, recover by washing thoroughly and replacing damaged parts.

## Verification

Both nostrils have been rinsed with sterile saline, excess fluid has drained, and there is no sharp pain, ear pressure, or bleeding.

## Variations

- Squeeze bottle: use light pressure only, since force can push fluid where it should not go.
- Allergy season: rinse after exposure, then clean the device each time.
- Post-nasal drip: drain for several minutes before lying down.
- Children: use only with clinician guidance and an age-appropriate device.

## Safety & privacy

Medium health risk because water safety matters. Seek medical care for severe headache, fever, stiff neck, vision changes, persistent nosebleeds, severe pain, or symptoms that worsen after rinsing.
