## Goal

Hair is detangled with minimal breakage by adding slip, working in sections, and starting at the ends instead of pulling from the roots.

## Preconditions

- Wide-tooth comb, fingers, or detangling brush suited to your hair type.
- Conditioner, leave-in conditioner, or detangling spray for slip.
- Clips or ties for sections if hair is thick, long, curly, or coily.
- Enough time to work slowly without ripping knots.
- No scalp wound, severe pain, or sudden unexplained hair shedding.

## Steps

1. **Choose wet or dry detangling.** [BRANCH: curly or coily | straight or wavy] use conditioner on wet hair for curlier textures, or detangle dry or damp hair if wet hair stretches and snaps. → *Expect:* the hair state matches its breakage risk.
2. **Add slip.** Apply conditioner, leave-in, oil, or detangling spray to the tangled areas. → *Expect:* strands slide more easily between fingers.
3. **Divide into sections.** Clip hair into manageable areas, starting with the most accessible section. → *Expect:* only one small area is being worked at a time.
4. **Start at the ends.** Hold the section above the knot and comb or finger-detangle the last few inches first. → *Expect:* the ends loosen without pulling the scalp.
5. **Move upward gradually.** Work a few inches higher only after the lower part is clear. → *Expect:* the comb travels farther with each pass.
6. **Separate stubborn knots by hand.** Pull strands apart sideways with fingers before combing again. → *Expect:* the knot loosens instead of tightening.
7. **Finish and protect.** Comb through from root to end once clear, then braid, wrap, or style to prevent new tangles. → *Expect:* the full section allows smooth passage without snagging.

## Decision points

- Hair stretches, snaps, or feels mushy when wet → stop wet detangling and try damp or dry with product.
- Hair is curly, coily, or tightly textured → use generous conditioner and sections, often while wet.
- Knot is matted close to the scalp → do not yank; use fingers, more slip, and small sections.
- Scalp pain, sores, or sudden heavy shedding occurs → see a clinician or dermatologist.
- A knot cannot be loosened after patient work → consider a stylist before cutting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Started at roots:** the comb jams and tightens knots → return to the ends and work upward.
- **F2 Too little slip:** hair squeaks or resists movement → add more conditioner or detangler and wait a minute.
- **F3 Scalp pulling:** pain at the roots → hold the section firmly above the knot to shield the scalp.
- **F4 Breakage:** short broken hairs collect on the comb → use wider teeth, smaller sections, and less force.
- **F5 Matting remains:** a dense knot will not open → pause and ask a stylist for help before cutting near skin.

## Verification

Each section can be combed or finger-raked from ends to roots without painful pulling, tight knots, or visible breakage beyond normal shedding.

## Variations

- Short hair: use fingers first, then a wide comb if needed.
- Extensions or braids: hold the attachment point and detangle below it to avoid loosening.
- Children: use smaller sections, extra slip, and breaks before frustration builds.
- Very dry hair: deep condition before detangling if the routine allows.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid force that hurts the scalp or snaps hair. See a doctor or dermatologist for sudden patchy hair loss, scalp sores, severe tenderness, scaling with inflammation, or shedding that is much heavier than usual.
