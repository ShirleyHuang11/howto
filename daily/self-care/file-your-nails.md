## Goal

Your nails are shortened, shaped, and smoothed without splitting, sawing damage, or thinning the nail plate.

## Preconditions

- A clean nail file or emery board, plus clippers if the nails are much too long.
- Nails are dry. Wet nails bend and tear more easily while filing.
- No painful swelling, drainage, black nail after injury, or nail lifting. Those signs need medical or podiatry advice before cosmetic filing.

## Steps

1. **Clean and dry your hands.** Wash, dry fully, and remove lotion from the nail edge so the file can grip. → *Expect:* dry nails with no slippery residue.
2. **Clip only if length is excessive.** Trim long nails close to the target length, leaving a little extra for filing. → *Expect:* shorter nails with squared rough ends.
3. **Choose the shape.** [BRANCH: rounded | squoval | square] pick rounded for short nails, squoval for most everyday nails, or square only if corners do not catch. → *Expect:* a clear target shape before filing.
4. **File in one direction.** Start at one side and stroke toward the center, then repeat from the other side toward the center. Do not saw back and forth. → *Expect:* length comes down gradually without white peeling layers.
5. **Keep the file under the free edge.** Angle it slightly under the nail tip, not flat across the nail surface. → *Expect:* the edge shortens while the top of the nail stays intact.
6. **Match the nails by sight.** Compare both hands with fingers relaxed and adjust only the longer or sharper edges. → *Expect:* nails look even enough without chasing perfect symmetry.
7. **Smooth the edge.** Use the fine side of the file or a buffer block on the free edge only. → *Expect:* no scratchy points when you lightly brush the nail over fabric.
8. **Stop before thinning.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* heavy filing across the nail plate removes protective layers until they grow out. Avoid filing the top surface unless smoothing one tiny snag. → *Expect:* the nail plate still feels firm, not bendy or hot.
9. **Moisturize the nail folds.** Apply hand cream or cuticle oil around the nail and under the free edge. → *Expect:* skin around the nail feels comfortable, not tight.

## Decision points

- Splitting keeps traveling into the pink nail bed → stop filing and cover the nail edge with a bandage until it grows out.
- Thick, yellow, painful, crumbling, or lifting nails → see a clinician or podiatrist, especially with diabetes or poor circulation.
- A dark streak or new pigment band that does not grow out with the nail → book a dermatology visit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Edge still catches:** use a finer grit file in one direction until the catch is gone, then stop.
- **F2 Nail feels hot or sore:** you filed too aggressively; stop, moisturize, and leave it alone for several days.
- **F3 Corner becomes too sharp:** round only the sharp corner with two or three light strokes.
- **F4 Layer peels after filing:** trim the peeling part if it is loose, then avoid back-and-forth filing next time.

## Verification

Each nail has a chosen shape, a smooth free edge that does not snag fabric, and no visible thinning or soreness from filing.

## Variations

- Glass file: gentle and washable; still use one-direction strokes.
- Very short nails: shape only the corners and avoid digging under the nail.
- Toenails: file straight across with lightly softened corners to reduce ingrown-nail risk.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but avoid sharing files if anyone has a nail infection. See a doctor for spreading redness, pus, severe pain, nail trauma with trapped blood, or nail changes linked with diabetes or poor circulation.
