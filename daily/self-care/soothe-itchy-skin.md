## Goal

Itchy skin is cooled, moisturized, protected from scratching, and escalated if it suggests allergy or infection.

## Preconditions

- Cool water, clean cloth, moisturizer, mild soap, and optional antihistamine or anti-itch product are available.
- The itch is mild and not linked to severe allergy, chemical burn, or widespread illness.
- Seek professional or emergency care when itching comes with trouble breathing, swelling of lips or tongue, widespread hives, fever, pus, severe pain, or rapidly spreading rash.
- This does not diagnose the cause of a rash.

## Steps

1. **Stop scratching.** Press or tap around the area instead of digging nails into skin. → *Expect:* the skin is not broken further.
2. **Cool the area.** Apply a cool damp cloth for 10 to 15 minutes. → *Expect:* itching feels less intense.
3. **Wash gently.** Rinse away sweat, plants, soap residue, or irritants with mild soap and water. → *Expect:* obvious irritants are removed.
4. **Pat dry.** Use a towel gently without rubbing. → *Expect:* the skin is dry but not abraded.
5. **Moisturize.** Apply fragrance-free moisturizer while skin is slightly damp. → *Expect:* dryness and tightness ease.
6. **Use anti-itch medicine carefully.** Consider an oral antihistamine or topical product only as labeled. → *Expect:* medicine is used safely if chosen.
7. **Cover if needed.** Use loose clothing or a clean bandage to reduce scratching. → *Expect:* nails cannot easily damage the skin.
8. **Avoid triggers.** Pause fragranced products, hot showers, wool, and harsh detergents. → *Expect:* irritation is not reinforced.
9. **Watch the pattern.** Note whether the itch spreads, forms hives, blisters, pus, or red streaks. → *Expect:* concerning changes are recognized.
10. **Seek care if worsening.** Contact a clinician for spreading, infected, or persistent rash. → *Expect:* serious causes are evaluated.

## Decision points

- Trouble breathing, swelling lips or tongue, or widespread hives → seek emergency care.
- Redness becomes hot, painful, swollen, or draining pus → seek medical care.
- [BRANCH: dry itchy patch | spreading rash] dry patches can be moisturized, while spreading rash needs closer assessment.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not scratch until bleeding because broken skin can scar or become infected.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hot shower:** detect itch worsens after heat, recover with cool compress and lukewarm washing.
- **F2 Product irritation:** detect burning after lotion or topical medicine, recover by washing it off and stopping use.
- **F3 Skin broken:** detect bleeding or open scratches, recover by cleaning gently and covering.
- **F4 Allergy signs:** detect hives, swelling, or breathing symptoms, recover by seeking emergency care.

## Verification

Itching is reduced, skin remains unbroken, and there are no severe allergy, infection, or spreading-rash signs.

## Variations

- Dry winter skin: moisturize after every wash.
- Bug bite: cool compress and avoiding scratching are the priority.
- Medication-related rash: contact a clinician promptly.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for mild itch, but seek professional or emergency care when allergic symptoms, infection signs, severe pain, fever, or rapid spreading occurs.
