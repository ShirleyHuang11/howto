## Goal

All ten fingernails are trimmed to a comfortable, even length with smooth edges, and the clippings are contained and discarded.

## Preconditions

- Nail clippers (or nail scissors) and ideally a file/emery board.
- Good light and a surface where clippings can be caught (over a bin, a spread tissue, or a table to wipe).

## Steps

1. **Soften if needed.** Trim after a shower/handwash when nails are slightly soft — dry brittle nails crack and shatter clippings. → *Expect:* nails flex slightly under thumb pressure.
2. **Set up over a catch surface.** Clip over a bin or a spread tissue. → *Expect:* clippings will land somewhere collectable, not across the sofa.
3. **Clip each nail in 2–3 small cuts following the fingertip's curve.** Not one big cut across. Leave a thin white margin (~1 mm) — cutting to the quick hurts and invites infection. → *Expect:* nail edge follows a gentle curve, white margin visible, no pink exposed.
4. **Work through all ten in a fixed order.** Dominant hand clips the other first (the awkward hand then does its work while you're warmed up). → *Expect:* all ten trimmed to visually consistent length.
5. **File the edges smooth.** One-direction strokes (not sawing) along each edge until nothing snags when brushed against fabric. → *Expect:* no catch on a fabric drag test.
6. **Collect and discard clippings; wash hands.** Fold the tissue, wipe the surface. → *Expect:* no clippings left on floor or table; hands washed.

## Decision points

- Toenails → cut *straight across*, not curved, to prevent ingrowth; thicker nails may need the larger clipper.
- Hangnail present → clip it at its base with the clipper's corner — never pull or bite it (tears live skin).
- Nails split/discolored/painful → that's a condition, not a trimming problem; leave it and see a pharmacist/doctor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cut too short, tender or bleeding:** rinse, press briefly, dab antiseptic; keep it dry and it settles in a day — and recalibrate the white-margin rule.
- **F2 Nail cracked/split during a big cut:** file the split smooth before it snags and tears deeper; small cuts (step 3) prevent this.
- **F3 Flying clippings escaped:** the catch surface (step 2) is the fix; sweep what escaped now — stray clippings are unpleasant to discover later.

## Verification

All ten nails show an even curve with a thin white margin, nothing snags on fabric, and the workspace and floor hold zero clippings.

## Variations

- Nail scissors: same margins and small-cuts rule; scissors follow curves more naturally but need the non-dominant hand practice.
- Children/others you assist: press the finger pad down and away from the blade line; clip mid-day in good light, never rushed.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The two rules that matter: never cut into the pink (step 3), never rip a hangnail (decision point). Clippers are personal-hygiene items — don't share them casually.
