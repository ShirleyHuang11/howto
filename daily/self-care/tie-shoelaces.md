## Goal

Both shoes are tied with a snug, flat bow that stays tied through a day of walking.

## Preconditions

- Laced shoes on feet, laces intact and roughly even in length on both sides.

## Steps

1. **Snug the lacing from the toe upward.** Pull each crossing pair gently up the shoe so the shoe hugs the foot, finishing with both lace ends equal. → *Expect:* shoe feels evenly snug; two equal free ends.
2. **Make the starting knot.** Cross the ends, wrap one under the crossing point, pull both tight against the shoe. → *Expect:* a flat base knot lying against the tongue.
3. **Form a loop ("bunny ear") with one end.** Pinch it at its base. → *Expect:* one stable loop, a few centimeters tall.
4. **Wrap the other end around the loop's base, and push a fold of it through the gap behind the wrap.** → *Expect:* a second loop emerging through the gap.
5. **Grab both loops and pull apart firmly.** → *Expect:* a symmetric bow with two loops and two free ends, seated tight against the base knot.
6. **Check the bow's orientation.** A correctly tied (square) bow sits *horizontal* across the shoe. A vertical, twisting bow is a granny knot — it will untie itself; see F1. → *Expect:* horizontal bow.
7. **Repeat for the other shoe.** → *Expect:* both shoes tied, bows horizontal, ends clear of the ground.

## Decision points

- Loops or ends drag near the ground → double-knot: tie the two loops themselves in a simple overhand.
- Laces constantly loosening even when square → use the surgeon's variant: wrap the second end around the loop *twice* in step 4.
- Small children / dexterity constraints → two-loop method: make both ends into loops first, then tie the loops in a simple knot.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bow sits vertical and unties all day (granny knot):** reverse the direction of *either* the starting knot's cross (step 2) or the wrap (step 4) — not both; retie and confirm the bow now lies horizontal.
- **F2 Knot collapses when a loop's free end got pulled through:** you pulled an end instead of a fold — redo step 4 pushing a *bight* (fold), not the tip.
- **F3 Lace snapped:** relace skipping the top eyelet pair as a stopgap; replace laces at the next chance.

## Verification

Both bows lie horizontal and tight, nothing touches the ground, and the shoes stay tied through normal walking without re-tying.

## Variations

- "Ian knot" (simultaneous two-loop pull-through): fastest known method, same resulting square bow.
- Locked runner's lacing: feed each lace through the top-eyelet loop on its own side before tying — stops heel slip for running.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk; the one real hazard is tripping on undone or dragging laces — which the horizontal-bow check (step 6) prevents at the source.
