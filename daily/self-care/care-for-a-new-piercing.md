## Goal

A new piercing is kept clean, undisturbed, and monitored so it can heal with less irritation and lower infection risk.

## Preconditions

- Fresh piercing with starter jewelry installed by a piercer.
- Sterile saline wound wash, clean gauze or paper towel, and hand soap.
- Healing-time guidance from the piercer for the specific body site.
- No urgent signs of allergic reaction, severe swelling, or spreading infection.

## Steps

1. **Wash hands first.** Clean hands with soap and water before touching near the piercing → *Expect:* hands are clean and dry before care begins.
2. **Leave jewelry in place.** Do not remove starter jewelry during healing unless a professional tells you to → *Expect:* the channel stays open and supported.
3. **Use sterile saline only.** Spray or soak with sterile saline, avoiding alcohol, peroxide, ointment, and harsh soap → *Expect:* crust softens without chemical burning.
4. **Do not twist.** Leave the jewelry still instead of rotating it; ⚠️ *Irreversible:* tearing new tissue can delay healing and cause scarring → *Expect:* jewelry moves only naturally, not from cleaning.
5. **Handle crust gently.** After saline softens crust, wipe loosened material with clean gauze and leave stuck crust alone → *Expect:* dried material comes away without bleeding.
6. **Dry carefully.** Pat with clean gauze or disposable paper towel after rinsing → *Expect:* the area is not left wet under jewelry.
7. **Avoid pressure and contamination.** Keep hair, makeup, headphones, helmets, pools, and dirty hands away as much as possible [BRANCH: ear | facial | body piercing] → *Expect:* the piercing is not bumped, compressed, or coated.
8. **Track healing.** Expect swelling and tenderness early, then gradual settling over weeks to months depending on location → *Expect:* symptoms trend down rather than up.
9. **Check for infection signs.** Look for spreading redness, heat, thick pus, fever, severe pain, or jewelry embedding → *Expect:* you know whether to contact a professional.

## Decision points

- Jewelry feels too tight or skin is swallowing it → contact the piercer promptly.
- Thick yellow or green drainage, fever, or spreading redness appears → seek medical care.
- Piercing bump appears without severe symptoms → reduce pressure and irritation, then ask the piercer.
- You want to change jewelry early → wait for piercer confirmation that the piercing is ready.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overcleaning irritation:** detect dryness, cracking, or burning, recover by reducing cleaning to saline and water only.
- **F2 Twisting trauma:** detect bleeding or soreness after moving jewelry, recover by leaving it still and cleaning gently.
- **F3 Embedded jewelry:** detect jewelry sinking into swollen skin, recover by seeing a piercer or clinician quickly.
- **F4 Infection signs:** detect heat, spreading redness, pus, fever, or worsening pain, recover by seeking medical care.
- **F5 Contact contamination:** detect makeup, hair product, or sweat buildup, recover by rinsing with clean water or saline and drying well.

## Verification

The piercing is clean, dry, jewelry is in place, irritation is not worsening, and no spreading redness, heat, thick pus, fever, or embedding is present.

## Variations

- Ear cartilage: expect longer healing and protect it from sleeping pressure.
- Oral piercing: follow piercer guidance for oral rinses and swelling care.
- Navel piercing: avoid tight waistbands and friction during healing.
- Nostril piercing: be gentle with tissues, masks, and nasal products.

## Safety & privacy

Medium health risk. Use saline only unless told otherwise, do not remove jewelry from a suspected infected piercing without medical guidance, and see a professional for infection signs or embedding.
