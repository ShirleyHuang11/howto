## Goal

A torn strip of skin beside the nail is softened, clipped cleanly, protected, and watched for infection.

## Preconditions

- A small loose hangnail, not a deep cut, crushed finger, or nail-bed injury.
- Clean nail clippers or cuticle nippers, soap, water, moisturizer, and optionally petroleum jelly and a bandage.
- No spreading redness, pus, fever, severe throbbing, or swelling around the nail. Those are see-a-doctor signs.

## Steps

1. **Wash your hands and tool.** Clean your hands, then wipe the clipper or nipper with alcohol if available. → *Expect:* clean skin and a clean cutting edge.
2. **Soften the skin.** Soak the finger in warm water for 5 minutes, then pat it damp, not dry. → *Expect:* the hangnail lies flatter and feels less stiff.
3. **Identify only the loose flap.** Find where the torn skin lifts away from living skin. Do not cut normal cuticle. → *Expect:* a visible loose strip with a clear base.
4. **Clip at the base.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* clip only the dead loose flap at its base; never tear or pull it. → *Expect:* the snag is gone without a new split.
5. **Rinse if it bled.** If a pinpoint bleed appears, rinse with water and hold clean pressure for a minute. → *Expect:* bleeding stops quickly.
6. **Seal in moisture.** Apply thick hand cream, cuticle oil, or petroleum jelly around the nail fold. → *Expect:* the skin feels coated and less tight.
7. **Cover if it rubs.** Use a small bandage if clothing, gloves, or work will catch the area. → *Expect:* the spot is protected from snagging.
8. **Watch for infection.** Check daily for increasing redness, warmth, swelling, pus, red streaks, or worsening pain. → *Expect:* tenderness fades over 24 to 48 hours.

## Decision points

- The hangnail is too short to clip safely → moisturize and cover it until enough loose skin can be trimmed.
- You have diabetes, poor circulation, immune suppression, or recurrent nail-fold infections → use a lower threshold for clinician advice.
- Pain or swelling increases after clipping → stop home care and arrange medical evaluation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 You tore it accidentally:** rinse, apply petroleum jelly, cover it, and monitor closely for infection.
- **F2 It keeps snagging:** trim only the remaining loose edge, then use a bandage for a day.
- **F3 Skin becomes red and puffy:** warm water soaks may help early irritation, but pus, spreading redness, or throbbing pain means see a doctor.
- **F4 Hangnails keep recurring:** moisturize after handwashing, wear gloves for wet work, and avoid cutting cuticles.

## Verification

The loose hangnail is clipped flush without tearing, the area is moisturized or covered, and there are no infection signs on daily checks.

## Variations

- Workplace gloves: cover the finger first so gloves do not pull the edge open.
- Children: clip only when they can hold still; otherwise moisturize and cover until safe.
- Dry winter skin: apply ointment at bedtime and after every wash.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk comes from turning a small tear into an infection. See a doctor urgently for spreading redness, pus, fever, red streaks, severe swelling, or symptoms in someone with diabetes or immune suppression.
