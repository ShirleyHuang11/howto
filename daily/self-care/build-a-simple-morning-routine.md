## Goal

Create a short morning routine that starts reliably and supports the day without becoming a fragile performance.

## Preconditions

- A usual wake time or target wake time.
- Three to twenty minutes you can protect most mornings.
- Basic supplies for the habits you choose, such as water, clothes, medication, or breakfast.

## Steps

1. **Choose one anchor.** Use an event that already happens, such as alarm off, bathroom visit, kettle on, or feeding a pet. → *Expect:* the routine has a reliable starting trigger.
2. **Pick no more than three habits.** Choose small actions that matter: water, medication, light, stretch, wash face, pack lunch, or review the day. → *Expect:* the list fits on one sticky note.
3. **Make each habit tiny.** Define the smallest acceptable version, such as drink water, open curtains, or stretch for one minute. → *Expect:* the routine can survive a tired morning.
4. **Order the habits by location.** [BRANCH: bathroom anchor | kitchen anchor] Arrange actions so you do not cross the home repeatedly. → *Expect:* the path feels natural.
5. **Prep the night before.** Put clothes, bottle, bag, medication, or breakfast items in place. → *Expect:* morning choices are reduced.
6. **Protect the first 10 minutes from your phone.** Keep messages and feeds out until the routine is done. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not ignore urgent safety, caregiving, or medical alerts for the sake of routine purity. → *Expect:* attention stays with the first actions.
7. **Run the routine once as a test.** Walk through it at normal speed and remove any annoying step. → *Expect:* the routine takes less time than the available window.
8. **Track completion lightly for one week.** Check off the routine, not each habit forever. → *Expect:* patterns show without turning the routine into homework.

## Decision points

- The routine takes too long → cut it to one anchor plus one habit for a week.
- You miss a day → restart the next morning without making up the missed routine.
- A habit requires motivation → shrink it until it requires only showing up.
- Your schedule varies → keep the same anchor and change the length.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Aspirational overload:** detect a routine with exercise, journaling, cleaning, and study, recover by keeping the two that matter most.
- **F2 Phone hijack:** detect checking messages before the routine, recover by charging the phone outside the bedroom or kitchen path.
- **F3 Night-before gap:** detect searching for supplies, recover by adding a 2-minute evening setup.
- **F4 All-or-nothing miss:** detect quitting after one skipped day, recover by doing the tiny version tomorrow.

## Verification

For at least four mornings in a week, the anchor triggered a short routine that was completed before phone drift or avoidable decisions took over.

## Variations

- Family routine: anchor to the first shared event and keep personal habits private and short.
- Early commute: move packing and clothing choices to the night before.
- Medication routine: pair the habit with the same location and confirm dosage rules separately.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep medications secure and do not let routine rules override urgent messages or body needs. A routine is a support, not a moral score.
