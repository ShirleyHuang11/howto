## Goal

Teeth cleaned on all surfaces for a full two minutes, twice a day, leaving the mouth fresh and plaque disrupted.

## Preconditions

- A toothbrush (soft/medium bristles, replaced within the last ~3 months) and fluoride toothpaste.
- A sink with running water.

## Steps

1. **Wet the brush and apply a pea-sized amount of toothpaste.** → *Expect:* paste seated in the bristles, not falling off.
2. **Brush the outer surfaces, quadrant by quadrant.** Hold the brush at ~45° to the gumline; small circular strokes, gentle pressure. ~30 s per quadrant across the whole session. → *Expect:* bristles reach the gumline without splaying (splaying = pressing too hard).
3. **Brush the inner (tongue-side) surfaces.** Same angle and motion; tilt the brush vertical for the front teeth's backs. → *Expect:* every tooth's inner face contacted.
4. **Brush the chewing surfaces.** Flat scrubbing on the molars' tops. → *Expect:* full two minutes elapsed by the end of this step (use a timer or an electric brush's pacer).
5. **Brush the tongue lightly, back to front.** → *Expect:* tongue surface swept; less morning-breath film.
6. **Spit out the foam — but don't rinse with water.** Leaving fluoride residue on the teeth is the point; rinsing washes it away. → *Expect:* foam out, thin paste film left on teeth.
7. **Rinse the brush and store it upright to air-dry.** → *Expect:* clean brush, bristles up, not sealed in a closed container.

## Decision points

- Electric brush → hold the head still on each tooth, let it oscillate; follow its built-in 30-s quadrant pacer.
- Just ate acidic food/drink (citrus, soda) → wait ~30 minutes before brushing; acid-softened enamel abrades.
- Bleeding gums → keep brushing gently (don't avoid the area) and add flossing; persistent bleeding beyond ~2 weeks → dentist.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Routinely finishing in 40 seconds:** the universal failure — use a 2-minute timer; each quadrant gets a verse of a song.
- **F2 Scrubbing hard with a stiff brush:** causes gum recession — switch to soft bristles, let technique (angle + small circles) replace force.
- **F3 Frayed/flattened bristles:** clean poorly — replace the brush/head now and roughly quarterly.

## Verification

Two full minutes covered all four surfaces types (outer, inner, chewing, tongue), foam was spat without a water rinse, and the brush is stored upright to dry.

## Variations

- Braces/aligners: add interdental brushes and brush after every meal; aligner trays get their own cleaning.
- Kids: same technique, rice-grain paste under 3 years, supervised until ~8.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Swallowing large amounts of fluoride paste is the only hazard — spit, and keep tubes from small children.
