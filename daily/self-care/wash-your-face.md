## Goal

Your face is cleansed morning and evening without being stripped or scrubbed raw, which is the difference between skincare and skin damage.

## Preconditions

- A facial cleanser matched roughly to your skin (gel for oily, cream for dry, gentle anything for sensitive). Bar hand-soap is too harsh for faces.
- A clean towel that is not the shared hand towel, and hair pulled or banded back.

## Steps

1. **Wash your hands first.** Cleansing with dirty hands moves grime around. → *Expect:* clean hands (`daily/self-care/wash-hands`).
2. **Wet the face with lukewarm water.** Hot water strips and reddens; cold does not loosen oil. → *Expect:* comfortably warm water, face fully wet.
3. **Work a small amount of cleanser in gentle circles for 30 to 60 seconds.** Fingertips, light pressure, covering forehead, nose, cheeks, chin, jawline, and down onto the neck. The time matters more than the force. → *Expect:* light lather or slip everywhere; no scrubbing sensation.
4. **Mind the edges.** Hairline, eyebrows, the creases beside the nose, and under the jaw collect cleanser and grime alike. → *Expect:* the zones people miss get their two seconds each.
5. **Rinse thoroughly with the same lukewarm water.** Residue left along the hairline and jaw is a common breakout cause. → *Expect:* no slick or filmy patches when you run a finger across.
6. **Pat dry with the clean towel.** Pat, not drag. → *Expect:* dry face, no towel-burn redness.
7. **Follow with moisturizer while the skin is still slightly damp.** Damp skin locks the moisture in; morning versions ideally carry SPF (`daily/self-care/apply-sunscreen` for real sun days). → *Expect:* skin comfortable, not tight, two minutes later.

## Decision points

- Makeup or sunscreen wearers: evening needs a proper remove-then-cleanse (an oil or micellar first pass, then the normal wash). One pass leaves half of it on the pillowcase.
- Post-workout: a prompt rinse or wash beats letting sweat dry into the skin, even if it adds a third light wash that day.
- Active skincare users (retinoids, acids): the gentle-cleanser rule hardens; the actives provide the strength, the cleanser should provide none.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tight, squeaky, or flaky skin after washing:** the cleanser is too strong or the water too hot. Switch gentler and cooler; "squeaky clean" is a damage signal on faces, not a goal.
- **F2 Breakouts along the hairline or jaw:** almost always rinse residue or a dirty towel/pillowcase. Fix the rinse (step 5) and rotate towels more often.
- **F3 Stinging eyes:** keep cleanser below the brow bone and rinse eyes with plain water if it wanders.
- **F4 Overwashing (three or more full cleanses a day):** the skin compensates with more oil. Twice daily plus a post-sweat rinse is the ceiling for almost everyone.

## Verification

The face was cleansed for the full 30 to 60 seconds at lukewarm temperature, rinsed with no residue at the edges, patted dry with a clean towel, and moisturized while damp, and the skin feels comfortable rather than tight an hour later.

## Variations

- Double cleansing (oil then water-based) as the standing evening routine for daily-makeup or heavy-sunscreen wearers.
- Minimalist version: lukewarm water alone in the morning is legitimate for dry or sensitive skin; the evening cleanse still earns its keep.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The recipe's whole tension is restraint: gentle product, lukewarm water, light hands, and letting twice a day be enough.
