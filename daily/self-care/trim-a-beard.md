## Goal

A beard is trimmed dry to an even length with a clean neckline, controlled cheek line, and tidy details around the mouth.

## Preconditions

- Beard is clean, dry, and free of heavy product.
- Adjustable trimmer with guards, comb, scissors, mirror, and good lighting.
- Optional detail trimmer or razor for edges.
- Enough time to trim gradually instead of taking off too much at once.

## Steps

1. **Comb with the grain.** Comb the beard downward and outward so hairs sit naturally → *Expect:* long stray hairs and dense areas are easy to see.
2. **Start with a longer guard.** Choose a guard longer than your target length for the first pass → *Expect:* the trimmer removes only the longest uneven tips.
3. **Trim dry in sections.** Move with the grain over cheeks, jaw, and chin, using steady overlapping passes → *Expect:* bulk reduces evenly without sudden bare patches.
4. **Step down slowly.** Shorten the guard only if the beard still looks too full [BRANCH: short beard | medium beard | long beard] → *Expect:* length changes happen gradually.
5. **Set the neckline.** Place the line about one to two finger widths above the Adam's apple, curving toward each ear → *Expect:* the beard ends under the jaw rather than high on the face.
6. **Clean below the neckline.** Trim or shave below the line with light pressure → *Expect:* the neck looks clean and the beard still covers the underside of the jaw.
7. **Refine cheek lines.** Remove only obvious outliers unless you want a sharp carved line → *Expect:* cheeks look intentional without cutting into the beard body.
8. **Detail the mustache.** Comb down and snip hairs crossing the lip, then trim corners carefully → *Expect:* mouth edges are clear and the mustache remains even.
9. **Check symmetry.** Look straight, turn both sides, and touch up with comb-and-scissor for single long hairs → *Expect:* both sides balance without looking over-trimmed.

## Decision points

- Beard is curly → trim less than expected, since curls spring up after cutting.
- You want a fuller beard → use scissors for outliers instead of lowering the guard.
- Patchy cheek growth → keep cheek lines natural and avoid cutting into dense areas.
- Important event soon → stop early rather than chasing perfect symmetry.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Neckline too high:** detect beard ending on the jaw face, recover by letting it grow back and trimming lower next time.
- **F2 Uneven sides:** detect one side shorter, recover by lightly blending the longer side rather than shortening both aggressively.
- **F3 Guard mistake:** detect a short patch, recover by blending nearby hair and allowing regrowth.
- **F4 Irritated skin:** detect redness or burning, recover by stopping, rinsing, and using a gentle moisturizer.
- **F5 Mustache too short:** detect a gap above the lip, recover by trimming only stray hairs until it grows back.

## Verification

In bright light, the beard has an even overall length, a clean neckline under the jaw, and no hairs crossing the lip.

## Variations

- Stubble: use a short guard and skip detailed scissor work.
- Long beard: shape mostly with scissors and use clippers only for sides.
- Sharp line style: use a detail trimmer or razor after setting the line with a guard.
- Sensitive skin: trim instead of shaving close on the neck.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use clean tools, avoid trimming over broken skin, and see a dermatologist or clinician for persistent rash, infection, or painful ingrown hairs.
