## Goal

Take a break that actually restores attention by leaving the work surface, moving the body, hydrating, and mentally detaching.

## Preconditions

- A task you can pause without creating danger or abandoning a live obligation.
- A timer or natural stopping point.
- Access to water and a place to stand or walk.

## Steps

1. **Stop at a real boundary.** Finish the current sentence, line, dish, or message, then pause. → *Expect:* the work is left in a stable state.
2. **Write the re-entry cue.** Note the next action you will take when you return. → *Expect:* you do not need to hold the task in memory.
3. **Set the break length.** [BRANCH: short reset | longer recovery] Use 5 minutes after a short sprint, or 15-30 minutes after a long block. → *Expect:* the break has an end, not a vague escape.
4. **Leave the screen.** Stand up and move away from the desk or device. → *Expect:* your eyes and hands are no longer locked to the same work posture.
5. **Move lightly.** Walk, stretch, shake out hands, or look out a window at distance. → *Expect:* breathing deepens and physical stiffness drops.
6. **Hydrate or snack deliberately.** Drink water; eat if hunger is the issue. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not use the break for purchases, replies, or decisions you would not make while rested. → *Expect:* the body need is addressed without opening a new rabbit hole.
7. **Detach for the remaining time.** No work talk, work tabs, or problem-solving rehearsal. → *Expect:* attention is not secretly still at the desk.
8. **Return on the timer and use the cue.** Sit down, read the re-entry note, and start with the next action. → *Expect:* the transition back is clean.

## Decision points

- You are taking breaks but still fried → lengthen one break and reduce work blocks to 25-50 minutes.
- You cannot leave a duty station → do a microbreak with posture change, distance vision, and slow breaths.
- Hunger, thirst, or bathroom need is driving restlessness → handle that need before calling it distraction.
- You dread returning → shrink the re-entry action until it is easy to start.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scroll break:** detect returning more tired, recover by making the next break screen-free.
- **F2 No re-entry cue:** detect forgetting where you were, recover by writing one sentence before every break.
- **F3 Break creep:** detect 5 minutes becoming 45, recover with a timer placed where you must stand to stop it.
- **F4 Fake detachment:** detect rehearsing the problem while walking, recover by naming five things you can see.

## Verification

You left the work surface, moved, hydrated or handled a body need, avoided work content, and returned at the planned time with a written next action.

## Variations

- Pomodoro-ish rhythm: 25 minutes work, 5 minutes break, then a longer break after four rounds.
- Physical work: sit, cool down, hydrate, and check strain instead of adding more movement.
- Caregiving or shift work: use shorter but more frequent breaks tied to natural handoff points.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not abandon active cooking, machinery, driving, childcare, or safety monitoring for a timed break. Lock screens before walking away from private work.
