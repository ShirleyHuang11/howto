## Goal

A mild to moderate sunburn is cooled, moisturized, protected, and monitored for signs that require medical care.

## Preconditions

- You are out of the sun and can stay covered.
- Cool water, clean towel, gentle moisturizer or aloe gel, drinking water, and loose clothing.
- No signs of heat stroke, severe dehydration, or extensive blistering.
- Access to a clinician or urgent care if symptoms are serious.

## Steps

1. **Get out of UV exposure.** Move indoors or into full shade and cover burned skin → *Expect:* the burn is no longer exposed to direct sun.
2. **Cool with water, not ice.** Use a cool shower, bath, or damp cloth for 10 to 15 minutes → *Expect:* skin feels less hot without numbness or sharp cold pain.
3. **Pat dry gently.** Leave the skin slightly damp instead of rubbing → *Expect:* the towel does not scrape or sting the burn.
4. **Moisturize while damp.** Apply fragrance-free moisturizer or aloe gel in a thin layer → *Expect:* skin feels less tight and no greasy heat-trapping layer forms.
5. **Hydrate.** Drink water or an electrolyte drink, especially after sweating → *Expect:* urine becomes pale and thirst eases over time.
6. **Leave blisters intact.** Do not pop blisters; ⚠️ *Irreversible:* opened blisters increase infection risk and slow healing → *Expect:* blister roofs remain clean and unbroken.
7. **Reduce friction.** Wear loose, soft clothing and avoid exfoliants, retinoids, and harsh soaps → *Expect:* clothing does not scrape the burned area.
8. **Monitor severity.** Watch pain, swelling, fever, chills, dizziness, confusion, or widespread blistering [BRANCH: mild | serious] → *Expect:* you know whether home care is enough.

## Decision points

- Burn covers a large area, blisters widely, or involves face, hands, genitals, or joints → seek medical advice.
- Fever, chills, confusion, fainting, severe headache, or vomiting occurs → seek urgent care.
- Skin shows spreading redness, pus, worsening swelling, or red streaks → seek medical care for possible infection.
- Pain relief medicine is being considered → follow the label and avoid it if unsafe for your health conditions.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ice injury:** detect numb or white patches after ice contact, recover by removing ice and using cool water only.
- **F2 Greasy coating:** detect trapped heat under heavy ointment, recover by gently washing and applying a lighter moisturizer.
- **F3 Popped blister:** detect open raw skin, recover by cleaning gently, covering with a sterile nonstick dressing, and watching infection signs.
- **F4 Dehydration:** detect dizziness, dry mouth, or dark urine, recover with fluids and medical help if symptoms persist.
- **F5 Worsening burn:** detect increasing pain after 24 hours or systemic symptoms, recover by contacting a clinician.

## Verification

The burned skin is cool, moisturized, protected from sun and friction, and no serious symptoms or infection signs are present.

## Variations

- Scalp burn: use a cool compress and a loose hat for later sun protection.
- Lip burn: use plain petroleum jelly and avoid picking peeling skin.
- Child sunburn: contact a pediatric clinician for blistering, fever, or significant pain.
- Future prevention: use shade, protective clothing, and broad-spectrum sunscreen.

## Safety & privacy

Medium health risk. Do not use ice, do not pop blisters, and seek professional care for severe symptoms, infection signs, very young children, or burns that are extensive.
