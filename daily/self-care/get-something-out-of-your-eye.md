## Goal

A loose speck is flushed from the eye without rubbing, scraping, or worsening an injury.

## Preconditions

- Clean water, saline, or eyewash is available, and hands can be washed.
- The object feels loose, not embedded, sharp, chemical, or caused by high-speed impact.
- Seek professional or emergency care when something is embedded, vision changes, pain is severe, a chemical entered the eye, or symptoms persist after flushing.
- Contact lens wearers should remove lenses if easy and safe before flushing.

## Steps

1. **Stop rubbing.** Keep fingers away from the eye even if it itches or waters. → *Expect:* the surface is not scratched further.
2. **Wash your hands.** Use soap and water before touching eyelids or contacts. → *Expect:* hands are clean.
3. **Blink several times.** Let tears move the speck toward the corner of the eye. → *Expect:* watering may loosen the particle.
4. **Remove contacts if present.** Take out lenses only if they come out easily. → *Expect:* flushing can reach the eye surface.
5. **Flush the eye.** Pour clean water or saline from the inner corner outward, or use an eyewash stream. → *Expect:* fluid runs across the eye and away from the other eye.
6. **Check the lower lid.** Gently pull the lower lid down and look for a loose speck. → *Expect:* any visible loose particle can be rinsed away.
7. **Try upper-over-lower lid.** Pull the upper lid gently over the lower lid and blink. → *Expect:* lashes may sweep a loose speck out.
8. **Flush again.** Repeat gentle rinsing for several minutes if irritation continues. → *Expect:* discomfort improves.
9. **Stop if embedded.** Do not touch an object stuck in the eye or under the lid. → *Expect:* embedded material is left for medical care.
10. **Rest the eye.** Avoid contacts and eye makeup until irritation is gone. → *Expect:* the eye is less irritated.

## Decision points

- Chemical exposure → flush continuously with water and seek emergency help.
- Object is embedded, sharp, or from metal, glass, or high-speed work → cover the eye loosely and seek emergency care.
- [BRANCH: loose speck improves | pain or vision problem remains] improvement can be monitored, while persistent pain or vision change needs care.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not try to dig out an embedded object because scraping can permanently damage the eye.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Eye rubbed:** detect more pain or scratchy feeling after rubbing, recover by stopping and flushing.
- **F2 Dirty water used:** detect questionable water source, recover with clean water or sterile saline.
- **F3 Contact trapped:** detect lens not moving or painful to remove, recover by leaving it and seeking care.
- **F4 Vision change:** detect blur, loss of vision, light sensitivity, or severe pain, recover by getting urgent eye care.

## Verification

The eye feels clear after flushing, vision is normal, and no embedded object, chemical exposure, or severe pain remains.

## Variations

- Eyelash in eye: blinking and saline usually work.
- Child: have another adult help keep hands away from the eye.
- Workplace debris: use the eyewash station and report the exposure.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because eye injuries can threaten vision. Seek professional or emergency care when pain, embedded material, chemical exposure, vision change, or persistent irritation occurs.
