## Goal

Chapped lips are protected, rehydrated, and allowed to heal without licking, harsh exfoliation, or irritating products.

## Preconditions

- Dry, cracked, peeling, or tight lips without severe swelling, pus, spreading rash, or trouble breathing.
- Water, a bland lip balm or petroleum jelly, and SPF lip protection for outdoor use.
- No known allergy to the product you plan to apply.

## Steps

1. **Drink to thirst.** Sip water if your mouth is dry or you have been sweating, drinking alcohol, or breathing through your mouth. → *Expect:* mouth dryness improves.
2. **Stop licking.** Notice the urge, then apply balm instead of saliva. Saliva evaporates and worsens cracking. → *Expect:* lips stay coated longer.
3. **Use a bland balm.** Choose petroleum jelly, mineral oil, dimethicone, shea butter, or ceramide products without fragrance or flavor. → *Expect:* lips feel protected without stinging.
4. **Avoid irritants.** Skip menthol, camphor, cinnamon, peppermint, strong fragrance, plumping products, and harsh acids while healing. → *Expect:* less burning after application.
5. **Exfoliate only gently.** If flakes are loose, soften with balm and wipe lightly with a damp cloth. Do not scrub raw skin. → *Expect:* loose flakes lift without bleeding.
6. **Protect outdoors.** Use SPF 30 lip balm in sun and a thicker ointment in wind or cold. → *Expect:* lips stay coated during exposure.
7. **Apply before sleep.** Put on a thick layer of petroleum jelly or bland ointment at bedtime. → *Expect:* lips wake less cracked.
8. **Check progress.** Continue for several days and watch for corner cracks, sores, or rash. → *Expect:* pain and peeling decrease within a week.

## Decision points

- Cracks are mainly at mouth corners → possible angular cheilitis; see a clinician if it persists, bleeds, or recurs.
- Blisters or clustered sores appear → use `daily/self-care/treat-a-cold-sore` if it matches past outbreaks, or seek diagnosis for a first episode.
- Swelling, hives, or breathing trouble after a product → seek urgent care for possible allergic reaction.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Balm stings:** wipe it off and switch to plain petroleum jelly.
- **F2 Lips keep peeling:** stop exfoliation and increase bland ointment frequency.
- **F3 Bleeding crack opens:** rinse gently, apply ointment, and avoid stretching the lip until sealed.
- **F4 No improvement after 2 weeks:** see a doctor or dentist to check for dermatitis, infection, medication effects, or sun damage.

## Verification

Lips are coated with a nonirritating balm, licking has stopped, outdoor protection is used, and cracking improves without swelling or infection signs.

## Variations

- Mouth breathing at night: consider humidification and address nasal congestion if present.
- Braces or dental appliances: apply balm before insertion and clean areas where saliva pools.
- High altitude or snow: reapply SPF lip balm often because reflected UV is stronger.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. See a doctor for severe swelling, pus, spreading rash, fever, nonhealing sores, white patches in the mouth, or lip changes that persist more than 2 weeks.
