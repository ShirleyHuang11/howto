## Goal

A clean, comfortable shave with no cuts, minimal irritation, and the sink left as you found it.

## Preconditions

- A razor with a fresh or recently used blade. A dull blade causes more cuts and irritation than a sharp one.
- Shaving cream, gel, or soap; warm water; a mirror.
- Aftershave balm or a plain moisturizer for the finish.

## Steps

1. **Soften the stubble first.** Shave after a shower, or hold a warm wet cloth against the face for a minute. Softened hair cuts with far less force. → *Expect:* skin warm and damp, stubble noticeably softer to the touch.
2. **Apply lather to every area you will shave.** Work cream or gel into the stubble against the grain so it stands up. → *Expect:* even coverage with no dry patches.
3. **Shave with the grain first, in short light strokes.** Let the razor's weight do the work; pressing harder removes skin, not more hair. Rinse the blade every few strokes. → *Expect:* smooth passes, cream cleared in strips, no pulling sensation. Pulling means a dull blade; stop and swap it.
4. **Stretch the skin at tricky spots.** Jawline, chin, and upper lip: pull the skin flat with the free hand and slow down. → *Expect:* flat surfaces under the blade; these are the zones where cuts happen.
5. **Re-lather and go across or against the grain only if you want a closer finish.** Against-grain is where irritation and ingrown hairs start; skip it if your skin protests. → *Expect:* closer result at slightly higher irritation cost, chosen deliberately.
6. **Rinse with cool water and pat dry.** Cool water calms the skin; rubbing with a towel undoes that. → *Expect:* clean face, no remaining lather, minor redness at most.
7. **Apply aftershave balm and clean the sink.** Rinse stubble off the basin, rinse and shake the razor dry, store it blade-up to dry. → *Expect:* moisturized skin, clean sink, a razor that will not rust between uses.

## Decision points

- Electric shaver: dry skin rather than wet for most models, gentle circular motions, no pressure. The soften-and-lather steps are replaced by clean, dry skin.
- Sensitive skin or recurring razor burn: shave less often, with the grain only, and change blades more frequently. Fewer passes beat more product.
- Beard lines and edges: define the neckline about two finger-widths above the Adam's apple; shave below it, leave above it.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Nick or cut:** rinse, press with a clean tissue for a minute, then a styptic pencil or a dab of lip balm. Small cuts stop quickly under steady pressure.
- **F2 Razor burn (red, stinging patches):** cool water, fragrance-free moisturizer, and no shaving that area for a day or two. Review blade age and pressure; those two cause most burn.
- **F3 Ingrown hairs:** do not pick. Warm compresses and gentle exfoliation over several days; shave with the grain until the area settles.
- **F4 Blade skips or clogs:** too much stubble for the blade, common after growing a beard out. Trim with clippers first, then shave.

## Verification

The shaved areas are smooth in the direction you shaved, there are no active cuts, irritation is minor and fading, the razor is rinsed and stored dry, and the sink holds no stubble ring.

## Variations

- Safety razors: cheaper blades and a very good shave, but the angle matters (about 30 degrees) and pressure matters more. Slow first weeks.
- Other body areas follow the same soften, lather, light-strokes logic with the grain, with extra care and a fresh blade.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from the blade itself. The three rules that carry it: sharp blade, light pressure, stretched skin at the contours. Razors are personal items; do not share them, since nicks make blades a blood-contact object.
