## Goal

A small superficial splinter is removed cleanly in the direction it entered, with the skin cleaned and monitored afterward.

## Preconditions

- Clean water, soap, tweezers, rubbing alcohol, good light, and a bandage.
- Sterile needle or pin only if the splinter end is just under the skin.
- Medical help available for deep, dirty, infected, eye, or high-risk splinters.

## Steps

1. **Wash hands and skin.** Clean your hands and the splinter area with soap and water. → *Expect:* dirt and surface oils are removed.
2. **Inspect under bright light.** Identify the entry angle and whether an end is exposed. → *Expect:* you know which direction the splinter entered.
3. **Clean the tool.** Wipe tweezers, and needle if used, with alcohol. → *Expect:* tool tips are clean and dry.
4. **Grip an exposed end.** If the end is visible, grasp it close to the skin with tweezers. → *Expect:* the splinter does not crush or break.
5. **Pull with the grain.** Draw the splinter out along the same angle it entered. → *Expect:* it slides out with minimal new tearing.
6. **Expose a buried tip if needed.** If the tip is just under skin, lift only the top skin layer with a sterile needle. → *Expect:* enough tip is visible for tweezers.
7. **Stop if it is deep.** Do not dig for a splinter that is invisible, under a nail, or near an eye. → *Expect:* the area is left intact for medical removal.
8. **Wash again.** Rinse the site with soap and water after removal. → *Expect:* no visible debris remains.
9. **Cover if irritated.** Apply a small bandage if the spot bleeds or will rub on clothing. → *Expect:* the wound is protected but not tight.
10. **Monitor.** Check over the next day for redness, swelling, pus, fever, or increasing pain. → *Expect:* symptoms improve rather than spread.

## Decision points

- Wood or plant thorn → remove promptly because fragments can inflame skin.
- Glass or metal sliver → do not squeeze hard because it can cut deeper or break.
- Tetanus vaccination uncertain after a dirty puncture → contact a clinician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Splinter breaks:** detect a shorter visible piece or dark dot remaining; soak briefly, retry once, then stop digging.
- **F2 Skin tearing:** detect widening wound or bleeding; stop, clean, cover, and let a clinician remove remaining material.
- **F3 Infection:** detect spreading redness, warmth, pus, or worsening pain; seek medical care.
- **F4 Wrong angle:** detect resistance and pain while pulling; recheck the entry direction and pull along it.

## Verification

The splinter is fully removed or safely referred for care, the skin is clean, and there are no spreading infection signs.

## Variations

- Very shallow hairlike splinter: adhesive tape may lift it without tweezers.
- Child: stabilize the hand or foot and use a second adult if movement makes injury likely.
- Under fingernail: clip nail only if it exposes the tip easily; otherwise get help.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from infection, retained fragments, and unnecessary digging. Seek medical help for deep punctures, animal or plant toxins, diabetes, immune compromise, or eye involvement.
