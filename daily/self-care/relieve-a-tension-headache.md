## Goal

A likely tension headache is reduced by hydration, eye rest, muscle relaxation, and careful medicine choices, while dangerous headache patterns are recognized.

## Preconditions

- The headache is mild to moderate and feels like pressure or tightness.
- You can move normally, think clearly, and speak normally.
- You know whether caffeine or over-the-counter pain relievers are safe for you.
- You can stop work or screen use for a short break.

## Steps

1. **Screen for red flags.** Check for sudden worst headache, head injury, fever with stiff neck, weakness, confusion, vision loss, pregnancy with severe headache, or new headache after age 50. → *Expect:* no urgent warning sign before self-care continues.
2. **Drink water slowly.** Have a full glass of water, especially after heat, exercise, alcohol, or skipped meals. → *Expect:* thirst decreases and your mouth feels less dry.
3. **Rest your eyes.** Look away from screens for 10 minutes, dim harsh light, and relax the jaw. → *Expect:* forehead and eye pressure stop building.
4. **Release the neck and shoulders.** Drop shoulders, tuck chin gently, turn head left and right, then hold a light side stretch for 15 seconds per side. → *Expect:* neck motion feels smoother, with no sharp pain.
5. **Use heat or pressure.** Place a warm cloth on the neck or massage the temples and jaw with light circular pressure. → *Expect:* tight muscles soften and the pain becomes less pointed.
6. **Time caffeine carefully.** [BRANCH: regular caffeine user | caffeine-sensitive or late day] take a small normal dose early, or skip it to avoid jitters and poor sleep. → *Expect:* alertness improves without a rebound or sleep penalty.
7. **Consider a pain reliever only within limits.** Use acetaminophen, ibuprofen, or another familiar medicine only as labeled, avoiding duplicates and medical conflicts. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overdosing pain medicine can cause serious harm, so confirm dose, timing, and ingredients before taking it. → *Expect:* medicine use is recorded and stays within the package limit.
8. **Recheck after 30 to 60 minutes.** If the headache worsens, becomes unusual, or repeats frequently, contact a clinician. → *Expect:* pain trends down, or you switch from home care to medical advice.

## Decision points

- Headache is sudden, severe, neurologic, after injury, or with fever and stiff neck → seek urgent medical care.
- Pain follows long screen use or clenched jaw → prioritize eye rest, posture, and jaw relaxation.
- You need pain medicine more than a few days per week → see a doctor to avoid medication-overuse headache.
- Caffeine usually triggers anxiety, palpitations, reflux, or insomnia → skip caffeine as a treatment.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stretch causes sharp pain:** detect shooting pain, numbness, or dizziness → stop stretching and use neutral posture with gentle heat.
- **F2 Painkiller conflict:** detect blood thinners, ulcers, kidney disease, liver disease, pregnancy, allergy, or duplicate cold medicine → ask a pharmacist or clinician before taking it.
- **F3 Rebound cycle:** detect headaches returning after frequent painkiller or caffeine use → reduce unsupervised repeat dosing and seek medical guidance.
- **F4 Red flags appear:** detect neurologic symptoms, worst headache, fever with stiff neck, or confusion → get urgent medical help.

## Verification

Within 1 hour, headache intensity is clearly lower, no red flags are present, and any medicine taken is within labeled dose and timing limits.

## Variations

- `office`: step away from the screen, lower monitor brightness, and stretch at the desk without forcing range.
- `travel`: hydrate first, eat a small snack, and avoid alcohol until pain improves.
- `night`: skip caffeine and choose dim light, heat, and quiet rest.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for ordinary tension headaches, but headache red flags matter. Do not ignore sudden severe pain, neurologic symptoms, fever with stiff neck, head injury, or a pattern that is new, worsening, or frequent.
