## Goal

Dry skin is cleaned gently, moisturized while damp, protected from irritants, and screened for signs that it is a medical skin condition.

## Preconditions

- Dry, tight, rough, or flaky skin without severe pain, spreading redness, pus, fever, or open infected cracks.
- Mild cleanser, towel, and a moisturizer such as ointment, cream, or fragrance-free lotion.
- Willingness to repeat care daily for at least a week.

## Steps

1. **Use lukewarm water.** Shower or wash with lukewarm water, not hot water, and keep bathing brief. → *Expect:* skin feels clean without a tight, squeaky feeling.
2. **Use mild cleanser sparingly.** Clean sweaty or dirty areas, but avoid scrubbing dry patches with harsh soap. → *Expect:* skin is clean with less stinging.
3. **Pat dry.** Leave the skin slightly damp instead of rubbing it completely dry. → *Expect:* no dripping water, but the surface still feels moist.
4. **Moisturize damp skin.** Apply moisturizer within 3 minutes of washing. Choose ointment for very dry skin, cream for daily use, or lotion for mild dryness. → *Expect:* skin feels coated and comfortable.
5. **Choose helpful ingredients.** Look for petrolatum, dimethicone, glycerin, ceramides, hyaluronic acid, urea, or lactic acid if tolerated. → *Expect:* a product that reduces tightness without burning.
6. **Avoid common irritants.** Skip fragrance, strong exfoliants, alcohol-heavy products, and scratchy fabrics on the area. → *Expect:* fewer stinging or itching flares.
7. **Protect hands and exposed skin.** Wear gloves for cleaning or wet work, and use moisturizer after handwashing. → *Expect:* fewer cracks from repeated water exposure.
8. **Recheck after a week.** Track itching, cracks, redness, and whether the routine helps. → *Expect:* visible flaking and tightness improve over several days.

## Decision points

- Itchy red patches keep returning in the same places → consider eczema or psoriasis and book routine medical advice.
- Ring-shaped rash, scaling between toes, or one-sided spreading rash → possible fungal infection; ask a clinician or pharmacist before steroid cream.
- Cracks bleed, ooze, swell, or become warm and painful → see a doctor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Moisturizer burns:** switch to plain petroleum jelly or a fragrance-free cream and avoid acids until skin calms.
- **F2 Dryness returns quickly:** apply a thicker product at bedtime and reduce hot water exposure.
- **F3 Skin cracks open:** rinse gently, apply petroleum jelly, cover if rubbing, and watch for infection.
- **F4 Rash spreads despite care:** stop new products and arrange medical evaluation.

## Verification

After several days, the skin is less tight and flaky, moisturizer is applied after washing, and no infection or persistent rash warning signs are present.

## Variations

- Face: use noncomedogenic fragrance-free products and avoid heavy ointment near acne-prone areas.
- Hands: nighttime ointment under cotton gloves can help severe dryness.
- Older adults: thinner skin tears easily, so avoid aggressive exfoliation.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for ordinary dryness. See a doctor for spreading redness, pus, fever, severe pain, nonhealing cracks, widespread rash, or dry skin with unexplained weight loss, fatigue, or intense whole-body itching.
