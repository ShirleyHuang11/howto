## Goal

Hangover symptoms are managed with hydration, food, rest, and monitoring while avoiding ineffective or risky fixes.

## Preconditions

- You are awake, breathing normally, and not in immediate danger from alcohol poisoning.
- Water, electrolyte drink or oral rehydration solution, simple food, and a safe place to rest.
- No plan to drive, operate machinery, swim, or make important decisions while impaired.
- Access to urgent help if severe symptoms appear.

## Steps

1. **Check for emergency signs.** Look for confusion, slow breathing, repeated vomiting, blue or pale skin, seizure, or inability to wake → *Expect:* you know whether this is a hangover or an emergency.
2. **Stop drinking alcohol.** Do not use more alcohol as treatment → *Expect:* no additional alcohol is being consumed.
3. **Sip fluids.** Drink water or electrolytes slowly, especially if nauseated → *Expect:* fluids stay down and thirst eases gradually.
4. **Eat something gentle.** Try toast, rice, bananas, soup, eggs, or another tolerable food [BRANCH: hungry | nauseated] → *Expect:* blood sugar feels steadier without worsening nausea.
5. **Rest in a safe position.** Sleep or lie down where someone can check on you if symptoms are significant → *Expect:* you are not driving, cooking, or doing risky tasks.
6. **Use pain relief carefully.** Follow medicine labels and avoid acetaminophen after heavy drinking unless a clinician says it is safe → *Expect:* any medicine choice respects liver, stomach, and kidney risks.
7. **Avoid false cures.** Skip cold showers, excessive caffeine, sauna sweating, and intense exercise as fixes → *Expect:* symptoms are managed without added dehydration or injury risk.
8. **Prevent the next one.** Plan food, pacing, water, and a ride before future drinking → *Expect:* a practical prevention step is identified.

## Decision points

- Repeated vomiting prevents keeping fluids down → seek medical advice.
- Symptoms include chest pain, severe abdominal pain, head injury, or blackouts → get urgent help.
- You take medications or have liver, kidney, stomach, pregnancy, or mental health risks → ask a clinician about safe treatment.
- You often need hangover management → consider talking with a health professional about alcohol use.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Alcohol poisoning missed:** detect inability to wake, slow breathing, or seizures, recover by calling emergency services.
- **F2 Dehydration persists:** detect dizziness, dark urine, or dry mouth, recover with slow electrolytes and medical help if severe.
- **F3 Medication risk:** detect plan to take acetaminophen or excess pain relievers, recover by reading labels and avoiding unsafe combinations.
- **F4 Caffeine crash:** detect jitters or worse nausea after coffee, recover by switching to water and food.
- **F5 Risky activity:** detect intent to drive or work impaired, recover by arranging a ride, delaying plans, or asking someone for help.

## Verification

You can keep fluids down, symptoms are improving, and no emergency signs such as confusion, slow breathing, repeated vomiting, or chest pain are present.

## Variations

- Mild nausea: small sips and bland food may work better than a full meal.
- Heavy sweating or diarrhea: prioritize oral rehydration solution.
- Morning obligation: reschedule safety-sensitive tasks rather than trying to push through.
- Prevention: alternate alcoholic drinks with water and eat before drinking.

## Safety & privacy

Medium health and safety risk. Seek urgent care for emergency signs, avoid driving while impaired, and consider professional support if alcohol is repeatedly causing harm.
