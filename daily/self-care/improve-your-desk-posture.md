## Goal

Your desk setup supports a neutral posture, reduces avoidable strain, and builds in movement every 30 minutes.

## Preconditions

- A chair, desk or table, screen, keyboard, and pointing device.
- Permission to adjust the workstation or add simple supports such as a box, cushion, or book stack.
- No new weakness, numbness, chest pain, severe headache, or pain after trauma. Those need medical care.

## Steps

1. **Set foot support.** Put both feet flat on the floor, or use a stable footrest if they do not reach. → *Expect:* thighs feel supported and feet are not dangling.
2. **Set chair height.** Adjust so elbows rest near 90 degrees when hands reach the keyboard. → *Expect:* shoulders can drop instead of shrugging.
3. **Support the lower back.** Sit back in the chair and add a small cushion or rolled towel if the low back collapses. → *Expect:* the spine feels supported without forcing a stiff arch.
4. **Raise the monitor.** Place the top of the screen around eye level and about an arm's length away. → *Expect:* eyes look forward with the neck mostly neutral.
5. **Center the screen and keyboard.** Put the main screen and keyboard directly in front of your body. → *Expect:* torso does not twist for normal typing.
6. **Keep wrists neutral.** Float wrists level with forearms, keep the mouse close, and avoid bending wrists up, down, or sideways. → *Expect:* hands reach controls without strain.
7. **Relax shoulders and jaw.** Drop shoulders, unclench the jaw, and let elbows stay near the body. → *Expect:* upper body feels less braced.
8. **Move every 30 minutes.** Stand, walk, stretch calves, open the chest, or change position for 1 to 2 minutes. → *Expect:* posture resets before stiffness builds.
9. **Recheck during real work.** After 20 minutes of typing, notice which body part tightened first and adjust that part. → *Expect:* the setup matches your actual habits, not just a staged pose.

## Decision points

- Laptop only → raise the laptop screen and use a separate keyboard and mouse when possible.
- Pain, numbness, tingling, or weakness persists despite setup changes → see a clinician or occupational health professional.
- You cannot change furniture → prioritize monitor height, foot support, and movement breaks.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Feet do not reach the floor:** add a stable footrest, box, or stack of books.
- **F2 Monitor height causes neck strain:** lower or raise it until the head balances over the shoulders.
- **F3 Wrists rest on a hard edge:** move the keyboard back or pad the desk edge without forcing wrist extension.
- **F4 Break reminders get ignored:** use a timer or calendar alert and make the break short enough to actually do.

## Verification

Feet are supported, screen is near eye level, wrists stay neutral, shoulders are relaxed, and a movement break happens at least every 30 minutes.

## Variations

- Standing desk: keep the same eye, elbow, wrist, and foot principles while shifting weight.
- Multiple monitors: center the one used most and angle the secondary display close.
- Bifocals: lower the monitor slightly so you do not tip your head back to read.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Seek medical care for new weakness, numbness, pain radiating with neurological symptoms, chest pain, severe headache, or pain after a fall or accident.
