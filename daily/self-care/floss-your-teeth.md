## Goal

The spaces between all teeth — where the brush doesn't reach — are cleaned once daily, without sawing your gums.

## Preconditions

- Dental floss (string), floss picks, or interdental brushes — any of the three; the one you'll actually use daily is the correct one.
- A mirror helps the first weeks; hands washed.

## Steps

1. **Dispense a working length.** String: ~45 cm, wound around each middle finger leaving ~5 cm taut between thumbs/indexes. Pick/interdental brush: inspect it's clean and intact. → *Expect:* a controllable taut segment or a sound tool.
2. **Seesaw gently through each contact point.** Slide the floss between two teeth with a light back-and-forth *glide* — never snap it down (snapping cuts the gum). → *Expect:* floss passes the tight contact and reaches the gumline without a sting.
3. **Hug each tooth in a C-shape and wipe.** Curve the floss against one tooth's side, slide gently *under* the gumline's edge, wipe up-and-down 2–3 times; then re-curve against the neighboring tooth and repeat before moving on. → *Expect:* each gap gets both of its walls wiped, not just a straight-down poke.
4. **Advance to fresh floss as you go.** Unwind a clean section every few teeth (picks: rinse between areas). → *Expect:* you're not relocating one gap's plaque into the next.
5. **Cover every gap systematically, including behind the last molars.** A fixed route (upper right → upper left → lower left → lower right) prevents skipped zones. → *Expect:* all gaps done in one pass, backmost surfaces included.
6. **Rinse mouth, discard the floss, wash hands.** → *Expect:* debris out; floss in the bin (not the toilet — it clogs and it's plastic).

## Decision points

- Bleeding when you start flossing → in a *new* flosser this usually means inflamed gums that need flossing *more*, gently and daily; it typically resolves in 1–2 weeks. Persistent or painful bleeding beyond that → dentist.
- Tight contacts that shred string floss → switch to PTFE/glide-type floss; repeated shredding at the *same* spot can flag a broken filling — mention it at the next checkup.
- Braces, bridges, wide gaps → interdental brushes and floss threaders are the right tools, not heroics with string.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 "I keep skipping it":** the compliance failure dwarfs technique failures — anchor it to brushing (floss-then-brush, same trigger), keep picks visible where you sit at night; an imperfect daily pick beats perfect string twice a month.
- **F2 Floss stuck between teeth:** release one end and pull it *sideways* out through the gap, not upward by force.
- **F3 Sore gums from sawing:** the snap/saw entered the gum — retrain the seesaw-glide and C-shape; soreness fades in days once the technique does.

## Verification

Every interdental gap was wiped on both walls in one systematic pass, no gum was snapped or sawed, and — the real metric — it happened again tomorrow.

## Variations

- Water flossers: good adjunct and better than nothing-at-all, aim along the gumline gap by gap; string/brushes still remove sticky plaque best.
- Flossing before vs. after brushing: evidence mildly favors floss-first (frees debris for the brush's fluoride); either order beats neither.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The two lines: never snap down onto the gum, and used floss goes to the bin. Everything else compounds quietly into old age with your own teeth.
