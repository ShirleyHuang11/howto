## Goal

A paper cut is cleaned, bleeding is stopped, stinging is reduced, and the cut is protected from reopening.

## Preconditions

- This is for a shallow paper cut with light bleeding.
- Seek medical care for deep, gaping, dirty, infected, or bite-related wounds, or if bleeding does not stop after 10 minutes of firm pressure.
- Call emergency services for severe bleeding that spurts, soaks through dressings, or comes with faintness or shock symptoms.
- Supplies: soap, clean water, clean cloth or gauze, small bandage, and petroleum jelly or antibiotic ointment if tolerated.
- Check tetanus status for dirty wounds, though ordinary clean paper cuts rarely need urgent tetanus care.

## Steps

1. **Wash your hands.** Clean your hands with soap and water before touching the cut. → *Expect:* dirt and germs from your hands are reduced.
2. **Rinse the cut.** Hold the cut under clean running water for a short rinse. → *Expect:* paper dust or surface debris is gone.
3. **Stop the bleeding.** [BRANCH: light bleeding | persistent bleeding] press clean gauze or cloth firmly for several minutes; seek care if bleeding continues after 10 minutes. → *Expect:* bleeding stops or becomes a tiny ooze.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not seal a dirty or deep cut closed with glue or tape before proper cleaning and assessment.
4. **Wash around it.** Use mild soap on the surrounding skin, not deep in the cut. → *Expect:* the area is clean without extra stinging from harsh chemicals.
5. **Reduce sting.** Hold a cool damp cloth on the cut for a minute if it burns. → *Expect:* stinging settles.
6. **Apply a thin barrier.** Use a tiny amount of petroleum jelly or antibiotic ointment if you can use it safely. → *Expect:* the cut stays moist and less likely to crack open.
7. **Cover with a thin bandage.** Place a small bandage so the pad covers the cut and the adhesive sits on dry skin. → *Expect:* the cut is protected without bulky pressure.
8. **Protect the movement point.** If on a fingertip or knuckle, place the bandage so bending does not pull the cut apart. → *Expect:* normal motion does not reopen it.
9. **Change when wet or dirty.** Replace the bandage and inspect for redness, swelling, pus, warmth, or worsening pain. → *Expect:* infection signs are noticed early.

## Decision points

- Bleeding continues after 10 minutes of firm pressure → seek medical care.
- Redness spreads, pus appears, swelling increases, fever develops, or pain worsens → call a clinician.
- Cut is from dirty cardboard, metal edge, or unknown contamination → consider tetanus advice.
- Bandage keeps reopening the cut → use a fingertip or knuckle bandage shape.
- Adhesive irritates skin → use paper tape, gauze wrap, or a different bandage.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cut reopens:** detect fresh bleeding after bending, recover by rinsing, pressing, and rebandaging across the motion line.
- **F2 Bandage too tight:** detect numbness, throbbing, or color change, recover by replacing it loosely.
- **F3 Infection starts:** detect spreading redness, warmth, pus, swelling, or fever, recover by contacting a clinician.
- **F4 Harsh cleaner stings:** detect burning after alcohol or peroxide, recover by rinsing with water and avoiding further harsh products.

## Verification

Bleeding has stopped, the cut is clean and covered, and there are no signs that require medical care.

## Variations

- Fingertip cut: use a butterfly-shaped fingertip bandage or wrap around the fingertip without tightening.
- Knuckle cut: use a flexible bandage and keep the finger slightly bent while applying.
- Office setting: soap, water, pressure, and a clean bandage are enough for most paper cuts.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for a clean shallow paper cut, but infection and uncontrolled bleeding need care. Keep shared workplace supplies clean and discard bloody materials promptly.
