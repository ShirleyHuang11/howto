## Goal

A nap of 20 minutes or less is taken in the early afternoon and ends on time, restoring alertness for the rest of the day without grogginess and without damaging that night's sleep.

## Preconditions

- A place to recline or lie down where interruption is unlikely for 30 minutes (couch, car seat reclined in a safe parked location, quiet room; a desk chair with a headrest works at a pinch).
- A reliable alarm (phone or watch) with a gentle but audible tone.
- Time check: it is before roughly 3 p.m., or at least 8 hours before intended bedtime.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the window.** Nap in the early afternoon dip, roughly 1-3 p.m. for a standard schedule. Later naps drain the sleep pressure the night needs. [BRANCH: after 3 p.m. and dragging → skip the nap; use daylight, a walk, and an earlier bedtime instead | within the window → proceed] → *Expect:* current time is inside the window before any lying down happens.
2. **Optionally take the coffee-nap.** Drink a cup of coffee quickly, right before lying down. Caffeine takes about 20 minutes to hit the bloodstream, so it kicks in exactly at wake-up and stacks with the nap's benefit. Skip this if bedtime is under 8 hours away or caffeine sits badly with you. → *Expect:* coffee finished within a couple of minutes, immediately followed by lying down.
3. **Set the alarm for about 25 minutes.** That budgets around 5 minutes to fall asleep and caps actual sleep near 20. The cap is the whole trick: past 25-30 minutes of sleep you descend into deep slow-wave sleep, and waking from that produces sleep inertia (half an hour or more of heavy grogginess, worse than no nap). → *Expect:* alarm set, volume confirmed, phone face-down and silenced otherwise.
4. **Kill light and noise cheaply.** Dim the room or use an eye mask or a hood; earplugs or steady background noise if the environment is loud. Loosen tight clothing, shoes off if possible. → *Expect:* eyes closed in near-dark, no notification pings possible.
5. **Lie back and let it happen without performance pressure.** Slow breathing, let thoughts wander. Even drowsy rest without full sleep restores measurable alertness, so "I never actually fell asleep" is still a win. → *Expect:* muscles unclench within a few minutes; drifting, or at worst calm rest.
6. **Get up at the alarm, immediately and fully.** Sit up, stand, light on or curtains open, a few shoulder rolls and a glass of water. Do not negotiate for five more minutes; the second doze is the one that overshoots into deep sleep. → *Expect:* upright and in bright light within a minute of the alarm.
7. **Re-engage with something easy for the first minutes.** Light tasks first; full sharpness arrives within 5-15 minutes (near-instantly if the coffee-nap was used). → *Expect:* alertness noticeably better than pre-nap by the 15-minute mark.

## Decision points

- Nap needed because last night was very short → one longer 90-minute nap (a full sleep cycle, waking at its end) avoids inertia, but only if the schedule allows and it still ends before mid-afternoon.
- Napping daily out of necessity rather than choice → treat it as a symptom; audit the night schedule (see `daily/self-care/fix-your-sleep-schedule`) rather than institutionalizing the patch.
- Insomnia at night → skip naps entirely while rebuilding the night; nappers with insomnia are spending their sleep pressure at 2 p.m.
- Drowsy while driving → this recipe is the correct emergency response: pull over somewhere safe, doors locked, 20-minute alarm. It outperforms loud music and open windows, which do almost nothing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Woke up groggy and worse than before:** slept past the cap into deep sleep (alarm too late, or snoozed) → today, bright light, cold water on the face, and movement for 10 minutes; next time, verify the 25-minute alarm and stand up at the first ring.
- **F2 Could not fall asleep at all:** usually pressure to perform or caffeine too early in the day → reframe the session as 20 minutes of eyes-closed rest, which still pays; it also gets easier after a few days of napping at the same hour.
- **F3 Nap turned into two hours:** alarm silenced or set wrong, typically on a phone in do-not-disturb → use an alarm that overrides silent mode, place it out of arm's reach, and expect tonight's bedtime to slide; hold tomorrow's wake time anyway.
- **F4 Night sleep suffered after an evening nap:** the window was ignored → hard rule going forward: no naps after 3 p.m., no exceptions for "just resting my eyes" on the couch at 7 p.m.

## Verification

Asleep or resting within roughly 10 minutes, up at the first alarm with total lie-down time under 30 minutes, subjective alertness clearly improved within 15 minutes of rising, and that night's sleep onset was normal.

## Variations

- Coffee-nap variant detailed in step 2: espresso or cooled coffee works best since it can be drunk fast.
- Siesta cultures: a post-lunch nap is socially normal and the same caps apply; heavy midday meals deepen the dip and make the alarm more important.
- Shift workers: nap before a night shift (even 20 minutes cuts on-shift microsleeps); the before-3-p.m. rule maps to "well before the shift's main sleep period" instead.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The safety-relevant case is drowsy driving, where a parked-car power nap is genuinely protective; choose a safe, legal parking spot and lock the doors. Persistent daily crushing sleepiness despite adequate nights is a medical symptom (apnea, medication effects) and deserves a clinician rather than better napping technique.
