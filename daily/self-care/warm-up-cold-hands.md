## Goal

Warm cold hands safely by restoring body warmth, improving circulation, and avoiding burns on numb skin.

## Preconditions

- The person is indoors or moving toward shelter from cold, wind, or wet conditions.
- Dry gloves, warm layers, and lukewarm water or a warm room are available.
- Severe frostbite signs, such as waxy skin or loss of feeling that does not improve, are not being ignored.

## Steps

1. **Leave the cold source.** Move indoors, into a vehicle, or behind a windbreak. → *Expect:* hands are no longer exposed to wind or wet cold.
2. **Warm the core first.** Put on a hat, jacket, dry socks, or blanket and drink a warm nonalcoholic beverage if available. → *Expect:* the body feels less chilled overall.
3. **Remove wet items.** Take off wet gloves, rings, or tight cuffs that restrict circulation. → *Expect:* fingers have room and are not compressed.
4. **Use gentle movement.** Open and close fists, swing arms lightly, or place hands under armpits. → *Expect:* fingers begin to tingle or regain normal color.
5. **Use lukewarm water if needed.** [BRANCH: mild cold | numb hands] For numb hands, use lukewarm water only, not hot water or direct heat. → *Expect:* warming feels gradual rather than painful.
6. **Add layers.** Put on dry gloves or mittens after hands are warming, and add a windproof outer layer outdoors. → *Expect:* warmth is retained for several minutes.
7. **Avoid direct heat burns.** Do not press numb hands against heaters, fires, heating pads, or hot water bottles. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* numb skin can burn before pain is felt; test warmth with an elbow or another person first. → *Expect:* no red, blistered, or overheated skin appears.
8. **Watch for Raynaud patterns.** Notice fingers turning white or blue, then red, especially after cold or stress. → *Expect:* repeated color-change episodes are recognized.
9. **Seek medical advice when needed.** Contact a clinician for frequent Raynaud-like attacks, severe pain, blisters, or numbness that does not improve. → *Expect:* concerning symptoms are not treated as routine cold hands.

## Decision points

- Hands are cold but fully sensitive → movement, core warming, and dry mittens may be enough.
- Hands are numb → use lukewarm warming and avoid direct heat.
- Fingers become white, blue, painful, or repeatedly color-change → consider Raynaud and ask a clinician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hands stay numb:** detect no improvement after gentle warming → seek medical help, especially after freezing exposure.
- **F2 Burning pain:** detect sharp pain, redness, or blistering after heat → stop heat and cool the burn with cool running water.
- **F3 Rings tighten:** detect swelling around jewelry → remove rings early or seek help before swelling traps them.
- **F4 Cold returns quickly:** detect fingers cooling again outdoors → add mittens, wind protection, and core layers.

## Verification

Hands feel warm or improving, finger color is normalizing, and no direct-heat burn or worsening numbness is present.

## Variations

- Outdoors: use mittens over gloves and keep spare dry liners.
- Office: warm the core with a sweater before relying on hand warmers.
- Exercise: warm up gradually before gripping cold metal equipment.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from burns, frostbite, and circulation problems. Repeated Raynaud-like color changes or persistent numbness should be discussed with a clinician.
