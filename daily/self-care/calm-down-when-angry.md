## Goal

Anger is paused long enough to avoid harm, choose a response, and repair any impact afterward.

## Preconditions

- You can step away or pause the conversation without creating immediate danger.
- A phone, trusted person, counselor, or emergency number is available if anger may lead to violence or self-harm.
- Seek professional or emergency care when you might hurt yourself or someone else, feel out of control, or are in an unsafe relationship or environment.
- This is a self-regulation practice, not a substitute for mental health care or safety planning.

## Steps

1. **Pause the action.** Stop typing, talking, driving faster, or moving closer. → *Expect:* the anger has a brief gap before action.
2. **Step away if safe.** Say you need a few minutes and move to another room, outside, or a quieter spot. → *Expect:* the conflict has less immediate fuel.
3. **Breathe slowly.** Take several slow breaths with a longer exhale than inhale. → *Expect:* the body begins to come down from the surge.
4. **Name the feeling.** Say the specific word, such as angry, hurt, scared, embarrassed, or disrespected. → *Expect:* the feeling is more precise.
5. **Relax the body.** Lower shoulders, unclench fists, and loosen the jaw. → *Expect:* the body is less ready to fight.
6. **Delay the response.** Wait before sending a message, making a threat, quitting, or making a big decision. → *Expect:* the next action is not impulsive.
7. **Check the need.** Ask what boundary, request, apology, rest, or information is needed. → *Expect:* anger points toward a practical next step.
8. **Choose a safe outlet.** Walk, write an unsent note, splash cool water, or talk to a calm person. → *Expect:* energy is released without harm.
9. **Return with one request.** If the conversation continues, state one concrete need or boundary. → *Expect:* the discussion is more focused.
10. **Repair afterward.** Apologize for any harm, clarify what you meant, and make a plan for next time. → *Expect:* the relationship or situation is less damaged.

## Decision points

- You may hurt yourself, someone else, or property → leave the situation if safe and contact emergency or crisis support.
- The other person is threatening or controlling you → prioritize safety and seek trusted help.
- [BRANCH: anger settling | anger escalating] settling means continue calmly, while escalating means extend the pause and get support.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not send threats, share private information, or damage property because those actions can have lasting consequences.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pause skipped:** detect speaking or sending while still heated, recover by stopping midstream and asking for time.
- **F2 Rumination loop:** detect replaying the insult repeatedly, recover by naming the need and choosing one next action.
- **F3 Unsafe confrontation:** detect moving closer, blocking exits, or raised voices, recover by creating distance.
- **F4 No repair:** detect lingering harm after the incident, recover by apologizing and discussing a prevention plan.

## Verification

No one is harmed, no impulsive message or action is taken, and a safer response or repair step has been chosen.

## Variations

- Work setting: ask for a break and return with a written agenda.
- Text argument: draft the reply elsewhere and wait before sending.
- Family conflict: use a pre-agreed timeout phrase.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for everyday anger, but seek professional or emergency care when there is risk of violence, self-harm, coercion, or feeling unable to stay in control.
