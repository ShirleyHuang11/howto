## Goal

Reduce compulsive scrolling by noticing the trigger, adding friction, replacing the behavior, and protecting bedtime.

## Preconditions

- A phone, tablet, or computer where scrolling happens.
- Permission to change app placement, notifications, or log-in state.
- One replacement activity that is easy to start.

## Steps

1. **Name the trigger.** Notice whether scrolling starts from boredom, anxiety, avoidance, loneliness, or bedtime autopilot. → *Expect:* one common trigger is written down.
2. **Choose a target window.** Pick the riskiest time, such as waking, lunch, after work, or bed. → *Expect:* the plan focuses on one repeatable moment.
3. **Add friction to the feed.** [BRANCH: app | browser] Remove the app, log out, move it off the home screen, or block the site during the target window. → *Expect:* opening the feed now requires an extra deliberate step.
4. **Turn off nonessential notifications.** Keep calls, direct messages, and safety alerts if needed; mute algorithmic pulls. → *Expect:* fewer prompts invite you back.
5. **Set a hard time limit.** Use a built-in app limit, timer, or rule like "10 minutes after dinner." → *Expect:* scrolling has a visible stop point.
6. **Install a replacement.** Put a book, walk shoes, puzzle, music, journal, or message-a-friend option where the phone used to be. → *Expect:* the trigger has somewhere else to go.
7. **Protect bedtime with a no-feed rule.** Keep the phone out of bed or use grayscale and charging outside reach. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not use scrolling while driving, walking in traffic, or supervising a child near hazards. → *Expect:* the bed is no longer paired with feeds.
8. **Review after three days.** Keep the friction that worked and tighten only the window that failed. → *Expect:* the system improves without a dramatic reset.

## Decision points

- You need news for work or safety → use a scheduled source check, not an endless feed.
- The trigger is loneliness → replace with a direct text, call, or planned social contact.
- The trigger is avoidance → pair this with `daily/self-care/beat-procrastination`.
- App limits are ignored → remove the app for a week or require browser log-in each time.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Replacement missing:** detect reaching for the phone with nothing else ready, recover by placing the replacement in the same spot.
- **F2 Limit bypass:** detect tapping "ignore limit," recover by adding a passcode held by a trusted person or deleting the app.
- **F3 News justification:** detect checking the same story repeatedly, recover with two scheduled news checks.
- **F4 Bedtime relapse:** detect scrolling in bed, recover by charging the phone across the room tonight.

## Verification

During the target window, friction interrupted automatic opening, a replacement was used at least once, and bedtime scrolling did not happen in bed.

## Variations

- Desktop scrolling: use browser blockers and log out of accounts after planned checks.
- Shared household: create a charging station outside bedrooms.
- Crisis news cycle: choose two reliable update times and avoid live feeds between them.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, with attention risk in traffic, driving, cooking, and caregiving. App blockers may expose usage patterns if shared with another person, so choose tools you trust.
