## Goal

Fall asleep with less struggle by lowering arousal, making the room sleep-friendly, and leaving bed briefly when wakefulness takes over.

## Preconditions

- A bed or sleep surface you can use for the night.
- A way to dim lights, cool the room, and reduce noise or interruptions.
- No urgent medical symptoms, safety duties, or active driving responsibilities.

## Steps

1. **Set a wind-down start time.** Pick a 30-60 minute runway before bed and stop work, arguments, intense exercise, and bright scrolling. → *Expect:* the evening has shifted from output mode to quiet mode.
2. **Make the room cool, dark, and boring.** Lower the temperature if possible, block obvious light, silence notifications, and keep the bed for sleep. → *Expect:* the room feels slightly cool and visually dull.
3. **Move the clock out of view.** Turn the alarm face away or put the phone across the room. → *Expect:* you cannot check the time without getting up.
4. **Do one low-stimulation cue.** [BRANCH: body tense | mind busy] Take a warm shower, read paper pages, stretch lightly, or write tomorrow's first task. → *Expect:* one calm cue is complete, not a whole new project.
5. **Get into bed only when sleepy.** Lie down when your eyes feel heavy, not just because the clock says so. → *Expect:* sleepiness is present before lights-out.
6. **Use slow breathing.** Breathe in gently for 4 counts, out for 6-8 counts, and let the exhale be the main event. → *Expect:* jaw, shoulders, and belly loosen after several cycles.
7. **Apply the 20-minute get-up rule.** If you feel awake and frustrated after roughly 20 minutes, leave bed for a dim, boring activity until sleepy again. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not take extra sedatives, alcohol, or someone else's sleep medication to force sleep. → *Expect:* bed stops becoming the place where you practice being awake.
8. **Return without checking the clock.** Go back when drowsy, repeat breathing, and treat a second round as normal. → *Expect:* the night feels procedural rather than like a test.

## Decision points

- Pain, reflux, coughing, or medication effects are keeping you awake → address the body problem first or contact a clinician.
- You are not sleepy at bedtime for many nights → adjust wake time and light exposure with `daily/self-care/fix-your-sleep-schedule`.
- Anxiety is racing → write the worry and the next action on paper, then return to the boring cue.
- You wake often to check the time → remove visible clocks completely.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Clock-watching spiral:** detect repeated time checks, recover by turning the clock away and judging by sleepiness only.
- **F2 Bed frustration:** detect irritation rising in bed, recover with the 20-minute get-up rule in dim light.
- **F3 Phone trap:** detect one "quick" check becoming feeds, recover by charging the phone outside arm's reach.
- **F4 Too warm or bright:** detect sweating, squinting, or covers kicked off, recover by cooling the room and blocking light before trying again.

## Verification

You spent most wakeful waiting outside the bed, avoided clock-watching and late scrolling, and either fell asleep or kept the bed associated with sleep rather than frustration.

## Variations

- Shared room: use an eye mask, earplugs, or white noise instead of controlling the whole room.
- Shift work: align the dark, cool room and wind-down to your main sleep period, even if it is daytime.
- Travel: prioritize darkness, temperature, and no clock view before perfect routine.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid mixing alcohol, sedatives, or unprescribed sleep aids. Persistent insomnia, gasping, severe daytime sleepiness, or weeks of failed sleep onset deserve medical review.
