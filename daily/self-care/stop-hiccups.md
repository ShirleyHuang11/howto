## Goal

Hiccups stop or become mild enough to ignore, using safe breathing and swallowing tricks that interrupt the diaphragm spasm reflex.

## Preconditions

- You can breathe comfortably and swallow safely.
- You have water available if you want to try a water method.
- You are not choking, vomiting repeatedly, or having chest pain.
- Stop any trick that makes you dizzy, panicky, or short of breath.

## Steps

1. **Sit upright and relax your belly.** Put both feet on the floor and let the shoulders drop. → *Expect:* breathing feels less rushed and the hiccup rhythm is easy to notice.
2. **Try one controlled breath hold.** Inhale normally, hold for 10 to 20 seconds, then exhale slowly through the nose or pursed lips. → *Expect:* the next few breaths come slower, and the hiccup pattern may pause.
3. **Repeat once, not endlessly.** If the first hold helped but did not stop it, do one more gentle hold after three normal breaths. → *Expect:* either the hiccups stop or you know this trick is not enough.
4. **Use the small-sip water trick.** Take 8 to 10 tiny sips in a row without talking between them. → *Expect:* repeated swallowing gives the throat and vagus nerve a steady competing signal.
5. **Switch posture if needed.** [BRANCH: can bend safely | cannot bend safely] bend forward slightly and sip from the far side of the glass, or stay upright and sip through a straw. → *Expect:* swallowing feels deliberate, and the diaphragm has a chance to reset.
6. **Add a diaphragm reset.** Breathe in so the belly expands, hold 3 seconds, then exhale twice as long as the inhale. → *Expect:* the abdomen softens and the chest stops jumping as sharply.
7. **Wait two quiet minutes.** Do not talk, laugh hard, smoke, drink alcohol, or gulp carbonated drinks during the pause. → *Expect:* any remaining hiccups are spaced farther apart.
8. **Escalate if hiccups persist.** If hiccups last more than 48 hours, keep returning, or come with severe pain, weakness, confusion, vomiting, or trouble breathing, contact a doctor. → *Expect:* persistent or concerning hiccups are treated as a medical symptom, not a willpower problem.

## Decision points

- Hiccups started after eating fast or drinking carbonation → sit upright, avoid more carbonation, and use small sips.
- Breath holding causes dizziness → skip it and use the water or slow-breathing method.
- Hiccups occur with chest pain, trouble breathing, fainting, weakness, or confusion → seek urgent medical care.
- Hiccups last more than 48 hours or disturb sleep for a full night → see a doctor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Trick makes you lightheaded:** detect dizziness, tingling, or panic → stop, breathe normally, sit down, and do not repeat breath holds.
- **F2 Water goes down wrong:** detect coughing or choking → stop drinking, cough fully, and restart only when swallowing is normal.
- **F3 Hiccups return immediately:** detect a short pause followed by the same rhythm → avoid triggers and try one different method once.
- **F4 Persistent hiccups:** detect symptoms lasting beyond 48 hours or recurring for days → book medical advice, especially if sleep or eating is affected.

## Verification

No hiccup occurs for 5 continuous minutes while you breathe normally, talk softly, and swallow without discomfort.

## Variations

- `no-water`: use slow diaphragm breathing and a single gentle breath hold.
- `public-place`: use small sips or slow nasal breathing instead of loud or awkward tricks.
- `after-heavy-meal`: stay upright and loosen tight waistbands before trying breath control.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for healthy adults. Do not hyperventilate, use forceful pressure on the neck, ask someone to scare you, or keep doing breath holds after dizziness. Persistent hiccups can rarely signal nerve, stomach, medication, or neurologic problems, so use the doctor thresholds above.
