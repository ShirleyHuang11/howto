## Goal

Mild lower back soreness is eased with gentle movement, heat, and low-risk positioning while red flags are recognized early.

## Preconditions

- Pain is mild to moderate and not from a major fall, crash, or direct blow.
- You can stand, walk, and control bladder and bowel function.
- Heat pack, towel, comfortable floor or bed, and supportive shoes are available.
- No new leg weakness, groin numbness, fever, unexplained weight loss, or cancer history with new back pain.
- You can move slowly and stop if symptoms worsen.

## Steps

1. **Check for red flags.** Notice leg weakness, numbness spreading down one leg, groin numbness, fever, trauma, or bladder or bowel changes. → *Expect:* you know whether home care is appropriate.
2. **Keep moving gently.** Walk slowly for 5 to 10 minutes instead of staying in bed. → *Expect:* stiffness eases or stays stable without sharp worsening.
3. **Use heat.** Apply warm heat through a towel for 15 to 20 minutes. → *Expect:* muscles feel less tight and skin is warm, not burned.
4. **Try a supported position.** Lie on your back with knees bent or on your side with a pillow between knees. → *Expect:* the back feels supported and symptoms do not travel farther down the leg.
5. **Do gentle stretches.** Try pelvic tilts, knee-to-chest, or child pose within a comfortable range. → *Expect:* stretching feels mild and does not cause shooting pain.
6. **Adjust posture triggers.** Stand up from sitting every 30 to 60 minutes and keep loads close to the body. → *Expect:* daily movements put less strain on the lower back.
7. **Resume gradually.** Continue light activity and avoid heavy lifting until pain is clearly improving. → *Expect:* function improves over 24 to 72 hours.

## Decision points

- New numbness, weakness, pain below the knee, or worsening leg symptoms → see a doctor.
- Loss of bladder or bowel control, groin numbness, or both legs weak → seek emergency care.
- Fever, unexplained weight loss, cancer history, IV drug use, or immune suppression with back pain → see a doctor promptly.
- Pain follows major trauma or is severe at rest → seek medical care.
- Pain persists beyond a few weeks or keeps recurring → book a clinician or physical therapist.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bed rest worsens stiffness:** pain increases after long lying down → return to short walks and frequent position changes.
- **F2 Heat too hot:** skin turns red or burns → remove heat, cool the area, and use a towel barrier next time.
- **F3 Stretch triggers leg pain:** symptoms shoot down the leg → stop that stretch and seek care if it persists or worsens.
- **F4 Lifting relapse:** pain spikes during bending or carrying → stop, unload, use hip hinge mechanics, and reduce activity.
- **F5 Red flag appears:** numbness, weakness, bladder or bowel change, fever, or trauma signs → seek medical care promptly.

## Verification

Pain is stable or improving, walking is possible, and no red flag symptom such as numbness, leg weakness, or bladder or bowel change is present.

## Variations

- Desk work: alternate sitting, standing, and short walks.
- Morning stiffness: use heat and gentle movement before stretching deeply.
- Pregnancy: ask a clinician about safe positions and avoid lying flat if uncomfortable.
- Known disc or spine condition: follow your clinician's activity limits.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because some back pain signals nerve compression or serious illness. Do not push through numbness, leg weakness, pain spreading below the knee, groin numbness, bladder or bowel changes, fever, major trauma, or severe unrelenting pain. Those signs need medical evaluation.
