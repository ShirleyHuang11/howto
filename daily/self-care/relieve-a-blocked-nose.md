## Goal

A blocked nose feels easier to breathe through using safe home measures and appropriate escalation when symptoms suggest more than congestion.

## Preconditions

- Water, saline spray or rinse, tissues, and a safe way to humidify air or inhale steam are available.
- Any decongestant medicine can be used only according to its label and personal medical restrictions.
- Seek professional or emergency care when there is trouble breathing, severe facial pain, swelling around the eye, high or persistent fever, symptoms after head injury, or symptoms lasting more than expected.
- This does not replace medical care for asthma, severe allergy, or sinus infection concerns.

## Steps

1. **Drink fluids.** Sip water or warm drinks through the day. → *Expect:* mucus feels less thick over time.
2. **Use saline.** Spray saline or use a clean saline rinse device as directed. → *Expect:* the nose feels moister and easier to clear.
3. **Blow gently.** Clear one nostril at a time without force. → *Expect:* pressure does not spike in the ears or sinuses.
4. **Try steam or humidity.** Use a warm shower or humidifier, avoiding scalding steam. → *Expect:* nasal passages feel temporarily looser.
5. **Sleep propped up.** Raise the head with pillows or a wedge. → *Expect:* drainage feels less blocked when lying down.
6. **Avoid smoke and strong scents.** Move away from irritants. → *Expect:* burning or sneezing decreases.
7. **Consider medicine limits.** Use nasal decongestant sprays only for the label's short limit, often no more than 3 days. → *Expect:* rebound congestion is avoided.
8. **Check oral decongestant warnings.** Avoid or ask a clinician first with high blood pressure, heart disease, pregnancy, or certain medicines. → *Expect:* unsafe interactions are avoided.
9. **Watch for allergy pattern.** Notice itchy eyes, sneezing, or clear drainage after triggers. → *Expect:* allergy care can be considered.
10. **Reassess daily.** Track fever, facial pain, duration, and breathing. → *Expect:* worsening symptoms are not missed.

## Decision points

- Severe breathing difficulty, swelling, or blue lips → seek emergency care.
- Congestion with severe one-sided facial pain, swelling around the eye, or persistent high fever → seek medical care.
- [BRANCH: cold symptoms | allergy symptoms] cold care emphasizes fluids and rest, while allergy symptoms may need allergen avoidance or approved antihistamines.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not exceed decongestant dosing because heart, blood pressure, and rebound effects can be serious.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rebound congestion:** detect worse congestion after several days of spray use, recover by stopping and asking a clinician if needed.
- **F2 Forceful blowing:** detect ear pain or nosebleed, recover by blowing gently and using saline.
- **F3 Unsafe steam:** detect skin too hot or burn risk, recover by switching to warm shower humidity.
- **F4 Serious infection signs:** detect eye swelling, severe pain, or persistent fever, recover by seeking care.

## Verification

Breathing through the nose is easier, symptoms are not worsening, and no urgent warning sign is present.

## Variations

- Dry indoor air: use a clean humidifier and change water daily.
- Neti pot: use distilled, sterile, or previously boiled and cooled water.
- Night congestion: focus on saline, hydration, and head elevation.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for simple congestion, but seek professional or emergency care when breathing trouble, severe pain, eye swelling, high fever, injury, or prolonged symptoms occur.
