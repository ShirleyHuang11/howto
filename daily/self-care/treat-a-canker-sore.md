## Goal

A canker sore is kept clean, less irritated, and monitored until it heals or needs dental or medical care.

## Preconditions

- The sore is inside the mouth, not a cold sore on the lip, and there is no major trauma.
- Salt, clean water, soft toothbrush, and optional over-the-counter oral pain gel or protective paste are available.
- Seek professional or emergency care when sores last more than 2 weeks, are unusually large, keep recurring, cause fever, prevent drinking, or occur with severe illness.
- This does not replace dental evaluation for persistent mouth ulcers.

## Steps

1. **Identify the sore.** Look for a small round or oval painful ulcer inside the mouth. → *Expect:* it matches a typical canker sore location.
2. **Rinse with salt water.** Mix salt in warm water and swish gently, then spit. → *Expect:* the area feels cleaner, even if it stings briefly.
3. **Brush gently.** Use a soft toothbrush and avoid scraping the sore. → *Expect:* teeth are clean without reopening the ulcer.
4. **Avoid irritants.** Skip spicy, acidic, crunchy, or very hot foods while it hurts. → *Expect:* meals cause less burning.
5. **Use topical relief.** Apply an oral pain gel or protective paste as labeled. → *Expect:* pain is reduced or the sore is shielded.
6. **Keep hydrated.** Sip cool water if eating is uncomfortable. → *Expect:* dry mouth does not worsen irritation.
7. **Do not pick it.** Keep fingers and sharp foods away from the ulcer. → *Expect:* the sore is not enlarged.
8. **Track healing time.** Note the first day you noticed it. → *Expect:* you can tell if it persists beyond the usual 1 to 2 weeks.
9. **Check for triggers.** Notice recent mouth injury, stress, dental appliance rubbing, or new toothpaste. → *Expect:* avoidable irritation is identified.
10. **Escalate if persistent.** Contact a dentist or clinician if it does not improve as expected. → *Expect:* lingering ulcers are assessed.

## Decision points

- Sore is on the outside lip with blisters → consider cold sore guidance instead.
- Sore prevents drinking or is paired with fever or widespread illness → seek medical care.
- [BRANCH: single small sore | large or recurring sores] a single small sore can be monitored, while large or recurring sores need evaluation.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not apply caustic home remedies because chemical burns can damage mouth tissue.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Irritant food:** detect burning after acidic or spicy food, recover by rinsing and choosing bland foods.
- **F2 Topical overuse:** detect numbness or irritation from too much gel, recover by following the label and stopping if worse.
- **F3 Appliance rubbing:** detect sore at a brace, denture, or retainer contact point, recover by asking a dentist for adjustment.
- **F4 Persistent ulcer:** detect no healing after 2 weeks, recover by scheduling dental or medical evaluation.

## Verification

The sore is protected from irritants, pain is manageable, and it is improving within the expected healing window.

## Variations

- Braces: use orthodontic wax on rubbing hardware.
- Sensitive toothpaste: try avoiding sodium lauryl sulfate if sores recur and a dentist agrees.
- Multiple sores: track frequency and associated foods or stress.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for typical canker sores, but seek professional or emergency care when drinking is difficult, fever occurs, sores are large or recurring, or healing takes more than 2 weeks.
