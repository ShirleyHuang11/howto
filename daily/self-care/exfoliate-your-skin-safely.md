## Goal

Dead surface skin is removed gently using a physical or chemical exfoliant without causing irritation, barrier damage, or sun-sensitive injury.

## Preconditions

- A mild cleanser, moisturizer, sunscreen for daytime, and one exfoliant.
- Skin has no open cuts, active sunburn, fresh wax, recent peel, or severe rash.
- You know whether the product is physical scrub, brush, AHA, BHA, PHA, enzyme, or retinoid-adjacent.
- You can patch test before applying to a larger area.
- You will use only one exfoliating product in the same session.

## Steps

1. **Patch test first.** Apply a small amount to a low-visibility area and wait 24 hours if the product is new. → *Expect:* no burning, swelling, hives, or spreading rash.
2. **Cleanse gently.** Wash with a mild cleanser and lukewarm water, then pat skin damp or dry as the label directs. → *Expect:* skin is clean without tightness.
3. **Choose the method.** [BRANCH: physical | chemical] use light circular contact for a scrub, or apply a thin even layer for an acid or enzyme. → *Expect:* the product is distributed without rubbing hard.
4. **Limit contact and force.** Follow label timing, avoid the eye area, and do not scrub until skin turns red. → *Expect:* skin may feel smooth but not hot or raw.
5. **Rinse if required.** Remove rinse-off products fully, or leave leave-on products as directed. → *Expect:* no gritty residue or unintended product remains.
6. **Moisturize.** Apply a simple moisturizer to support the skin barrier. → *Expect:* skin feels comfortable, not tight.
7. **Use sunscreen after daytime exfoliation.** Apply broad-spectrum sunscreen to exposed skin. → *Expect:* exfoliated skin is protected before sun exposure.

## Decision points

- Sensitive, dry, or eczema-prone skin → start once weekly or less.
- Oily or acne-prone skin → BHA may be useful, but increase frequency slowly.
- Using retinoids, acne prescriptions, or recent procedures → ask a clinician before adding exfoliation.
- Burning, swelling, hives, blistering, or severe redness occurs → stop and seek care if it does not settle.
- Skin feels smooth but slightly dry → reduce frequency and moisturize more.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Over-exfoliation:** stinging, shiny tight skin, peeling, or redness → stop exfoliants, use gentle cleanser and moisturizer until normal.
- **F2 Product conflict:** irritation after combining acids, retinoids, or scrubs → use only one active and separate strong products by days.
- **F3 Scrub abrasion:** small scratches or raw patches → rinse, moisturize, avoid makeup actives, and protect from sun.
- **F4 Breakout flare:** new bumps after a product → stop it and return to the last tolerated routine.
- **F5 Sun sensitivity:** redness after outdoor exposure → cool the skin, moisturize, and use sunscreen consistently.

## Verification

Within 24 hours, skin feels smoother without burning, rawness, swelling, open areas, or worsening redness.

## Variations

- Body exfoliation: use slightly more durable skin areas, but avoid fresh shaving cuts and irritated folds.
- Face exfoliation: use lower force and lower frequency than on the body.
- Darker skin tones: avoid irritation that can trigger dark marks; prioritize gentle frequency.
- Shaving routine: exfoliate on a separate day if irritation occurs.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when mild and infrequent. Do not exfoliate broken, infected, sunburned, recently waxed, or medically treated skin unless a clinician says it is safe. See a doctor for blistering, swelling, severe pain, infection signs, or a rash that spreads.
