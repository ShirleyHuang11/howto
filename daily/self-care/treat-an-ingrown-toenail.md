## Goal

A mildly ingrown toenail is cleaned, pressure is reduced, and the nail edge is encouraged to grow out without digging into the skin.

## Preconditions

- Warm water, soap, a clean towel, clean cotton or dental floss, and roomy footwear.
- Mild tenderness only, with no spreading redness, pus, fever, or severe swelling.
- You can see the nail edge without cutting or digging into live skin.
- You do not have diabetes, poor circulation, neuropathy, or immune suppression.
- You can check the toe daily for worsening signs.

## Steps

1. **Wash your hands and foot.** Clean the toe with soap and water before touching the nail fold. → *Expect:* the skin is clean and debris is gone.
2. **Soak the toe.** Soak in warm water for 10 to 15 minutes, then pat dry. → *Expect:* the skin and nail edge feel softer.
3. **Reduce pressure.** Put on open-toe sandals or wide shoes, and avoid tight socks. → *Expect:* the sore side is not pressed by footwear.
4. **Lift only the visible edge.** If the corner is visible, slide a tiny piece of clean cotton or waxed dental floss under the edge. → *Expect:* the nail edge sits just above the skin without sharp pain.
5. **Avoid digging or cutting the side.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not cut a wedge, dig with scissors, or tear skin because this can worsen infection and shape. → *Expect:* the nail corner remains intact.
6. **Repeat gently.** Soak once or twice daily and replace the cotton with a clean tiny piece after each soak. → *Expect:* pressure gradually decreases over several days.
7. **Protect the toe.** Keep it clean and dry between soaks, and avoid running or impact until pain improves. → *Expect:* the toe is less sore during normal walking.

## Decision points

- Redness is spreading, pus appears, the toe feels hot, or you develop fever → see a doctor.
- Pain is severe or you cannot walk normally → seek urgent medical care.
- You have diabetes, poor circulation, neuropathy, or immune suppression → see a clinician or podiatrist instead of home treatment.
- The nail edge is not visible → do not dig; book care.
- Symptoms are not improving after 2 to 3 days of home care → see a doctor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cotton hurts:** sharp pain or throbbing after placement → remove it, soak again, and use a smaller piece or stop.
- **F2 Skin breaks:** bleeding or raw tissue appears → rinse, cover lightly, stop manipulation, and seek care if redness or drainage follows.
- **F3 Infection signs:** pus, warmth, swelling, spreading redness, fever, or red streaks → see a doctor promptly.
- **F4 Recurrence:** the same corner keeps growing in → trim future nails straight across and ask a podiatrist about permanent options.
- **F5 Footwear keeps pressing:** pain returns in shoes → switch to wider shoes or sandals until the edge grows out.

## Verification

The toe is clean, the nail corner is not being cut into, footwear does not press on it, and pain is improving rather than worsening over 48 hours.

## Variations

- Children: have a caregiver inspect daily and choose shoes with a roomy toe box.
- Athletes: pause tight cleats or repetitive toe impact until walking is comfortable.
- Recurrent ingrown nails: a clinician may remove part of the nail edge under local anesthesia.
- Pregnancy or swelling: choose adjustable footwear to reduce side pressure.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because toe infections can spread and nail digging can cause injury. Home care is only for mild cases. Seek medical care for pus, spreading redness, fever, severe pain, red streaking, inability to walk, or any toe problem when you have diabetes, poor circulation, neuropathy, or immune suppression.
