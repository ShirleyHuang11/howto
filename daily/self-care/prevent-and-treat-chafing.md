## Goal

Skin rubbing is prevented during movement, and mild raw chafed skin is cleaned, soothed, and protected while it heals.

## Preconditions

- Clean water, mild soap, towel, barrier balm, petroleum jelly, zinc oxide, or anti-chafe stick.
- Moisture-wicking clothing or a way to change damp clothes.
- No deep open wound, spreading redness, pus, fever, or severe pain.
- You can identify the rubbing area, such as thighs, underarms, nipples, waistband, groin, or feet.
- A clean breathable covering is available if clothing keeps rubbing the spot.

## Steps

1. **Identify friction and moisture.** Check where skin rubs skin, fabric, straps, or seams. → *Expect:* the trigger area is clear.
2. **Choose smoother clothing.** Wear moisture-wicking, well-fitting fabric and avoid rough seams over the spot. → *Expect:* the skin moves with less rubbing.
3. **Apply a barrier before activity.** Coat the high-friction area with balm, petroleum jelly, zinc oxide, or an anti-chafe product. → *Expect:* the skin feels slick or protected.
4. **Keep sweat under control.** Change wet clothing when possible and dry folds gently. → *Expect:* the area is less damp.
5. **Clean raw skin.** For existing chafing, wash with mild soap and water, then pat dry. → *Expect:* sweat and grit are removed without scrubbing.
6. **Soothe and cover.** Apply a bland barrier and use soft breathable fabric or a nonstick dressing if needed. → *Expect:* the area stings less when clothing touches it.
7. **Reduce rubbing until healed.** Modify the activity, route, pace, or clothing for a day or two. → *Expect:* the skin is not repeatedly reopened.

## Decision points

- Chafing happens during running or long walks → apply barrier before starting and carry a small refill.
- Rash is in a warm skin fold with odor or satellite spots → yeast or fungal irritation may need pharmacy or clinician advice.
- Skin is bleeding, blistered, draining, or very painful → treat like a wound and consider medical care.
- Redness spreads, pus appears, fever develops, or pain worsens → see a doctor.
- Chafing keeps recurring in the same place → change fit, fabric, straps, or footwear.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Barrier wears off:** rubbing returns mid-activity → dry the area if possible and reapply.
- **F2 Damp clothing:** sting worsens after sweating → change clothes and use moisture-wicking layers next time.
- **F3 Product irritation:** burning or rash after balm → wash it off and switch to a bland petroleum or zinc product.
- **F4 Skin breaks open:** raw wet surface or bleeding → clean, cover, reduce friction, and watch for infection.
- **F5 Infection signs:** warmth, swelling, pus, fever, red streaks, or worsening pain → see a doctor promptly.

## Verification

The rubbing source is reduced, the skin is clean and protected, and pain decreases during normal movement instead of increasing.

## Variations

- Thigh chafing: compression shorts or anti-chafe bands can reduce skin-on-skin friction.
- Nipple chafing: use small adhesive bandages or purpose-made covers before long runs.
- Foot chafing: check sock seams, shoe fit, and moisture before blisters form.
- Groin or fold chafing: keep the area dry and avoid fragranced products.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for mild chafing. Seek medical care for spreading redness, pus, fever, red streaks, severe pain, deep wounds, or chafing that does not improve after friction is removed. Avoid sharing photos of private-area rashes unless using a secure clinician portal.
