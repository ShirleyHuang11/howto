## Goal

Exposed skin is protected with enough sunscreen, applied early enough and re-applied often enough to actually prevent sunburn, which is the part most people get wrong.

## Preconditions

- Broad-spectrum sunscreen, SPF 30 or higher, that is not past its expiry date (expired sunscreen loses protection).
- A day involving real sun exposure: outdoors for more than a few minutes, strong-UV season, water, or altitude. Clouds do not cancel UV.

## Steps

1. **Apply 15 to 20 minutes before going out.** Chemical sunscreens need time to bind; applying at the beach door means twenty unprotected minutes. → *Expect:* application happens indoors, before dressing where practical.
2. **Use far more than feels natural.** The tested dose is about 2 mg per square centimeter, which works out to roughly a shot glass for a body in swimwear, or two finger-lengths of product for the face and neck. Half the dose gives far less than half the protection. → *Expect:* a visibly generous layer that takes a minute to rub in.
3. **Cover the forgotten zones deliberately.** Ears, back of the neck, hairline and any parting, tops of feet, backs of hands and knees, and lips with an SPF balm. → *Expect:* every exposed area touched, checked against a mental head-to-toe list.
4. **Get help or use tools for your back.** A partner, a lotion applicator, or spray applied heavily and rubbed in. Sprayed-and-not-rubbed is the classic streaky burn. → *Expect:* the back covered as thoroughly as the front.
5. **Reapply every two hours, and immediately after swimming, sweating, or toweling.** "Water resistant" means 40 to 80 minutes, not the afternoon. Set a phone reminder on beach days. → *Expect:* reapplication actually happens on schedule, not when the skin already feels hot.
6. **Layer with the other defenses.** Shade in the peak hours (roughly 10:00 to 16:00), a hat, sunglasses, and clothing cover more reliably than any cream. Sunscreen covers what they do not. → *Expect:* sunscreen treated as one layer of the system, not the whole system.

## Decision points

- Face products under makeup: apply sunscreen as the last skincare step, let it settle a few minutes, then makeup. SPF in foundation is a bonus, not a dose.
- Children: high SPF, frequent reapplication, and clothing/shade doing most of the work. Babies under six months stay out of direct sun rather than being creamed.
- Mineral vs chemical: mineral (zinc/titanium) works immediately on application and suits sensitive skin; the visible white cast is the tradeoff. Either works if dosed and reapplied.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned anyway:** cool showers, moisturizer or aloe, water, and pain relief if needed; no more sun on the burn until fully healed. Blistering or fever-scale burns deserve medical advice. Audit which step failed, usually dose or reapplication.
- **F2 Sunscreen in the eyes:** rinse with clean water; choose a stick or mineral formula for the eye area next time and keep applications above the brow line when sweating is likely.
- **F3 Missed streaks revealed by the evening mirror:** map them mentally; they are your personal blind spots (commonly the back, sides of the torso, and behind the ears) and get explicit attention next application.
- **F4 Ran out mid-day:** shade and cover become the whole system. A shirt beats nothing by a wide margin.

## Verification

Application happened before exposure at the generous dose, the forgotten-zones list was covered, at least one on-schedule reapplication happened per two hours of exposure, and the evening skin check shows no new redness.

## Variations

- High-UV environments (snow, water, altitude, tropics): reflection and thinner atmosphere raise the dose of everything: higher SPF, stricter reapplication, more clothing.
- Daily-city use: a facial SPF as the last morning skincare step covers commuting exposure without the beach protocol.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, high payoff. Sunburn is short-term pain; the accumulated dose is the long-term skin-cancer driver, which is why the boring parts (dose, timing, reapplication) are the whole recipe.
