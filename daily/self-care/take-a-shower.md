## Goal

You are clean, dried, and dressed after a shower, without scalds, slips, or a flooded bathroom.

## Preconditions

- A working shower with hot water; soap/shower gel, shampoo.
- A towel within arm's reach of the shower exit, and clean clothes staged.
- A bath mat or dry-standing spot outside the shower.

## Steps

1. **Stage everything before undressing.** Towel on the hook by the shower, clothes in the dry zone, toiletries inside the stall. → *Expect:* nothing will require a dripping walk across the bathroom.
2. **Start the water and set the temperature *before* getting in.** Run the mixer toward warm, test with a hand/wrist in the spray, adjust to comfortably warm — not reddening-hot. → *Expect:* stable comfortable temperature at the hand test; curtain/door positioned to keep spray inside.
3. **Enter carefully and wet down fully.** One hand on a wall/grab point while stepping in. → *Expect:* hair and body fully wet.
4. **Shampoo the hair.** A coin-sized amount worked into the scalp, then rinsed completely. → *Expect:* no slippery residue at the scalp when rinsed.
5. **Wash the body top-down with soap.** Face, neck, torso, arms, groin, legs, feet — top-down so rinse water carries soap past already-clean skin. Mind the extra-slip while feet are soapy. → *Expect:* all skin lathered and rinsed; no soap film left.
6. **Shut off the water and squeegee excess off your body with your hands.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* none — but this is the slip-critical moment: wring hair inside the stall, then reach for the towel *before* stepping out. → *Expect:* towel in hand while still standing on the wet-safe surface.
7. **Step out onto the mat and dry fully, feet last and thoroughly.** → *Expect:* dry body, dry feet before walking on tile; floor around the mat essentially dry.
8. **Leave the wet zone tidy.** Hang the towel spread out, curtain spread to dry, ventilate (fan or window). → *Expect:* moisture venting; no puddles.

## Decision points

- Water pressure/temperature fluctuates when others use taps → brief pause rather than mid-shower re-adjustment chasing spikes.
- Conditioner user → apply after shampoo, leave in while doing step 5, rinse last.
- Elderly or balance-limited → non-slip mat inside the stall, grab bars, and a shower stool convert the two highest-risk transitions (in/out) to seated moves.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sudden scald (someone flushed/ran cold water):** step *back* out of the spray line first, then adjust — don't fight the mixer while in the stream.
- **F2 Soap in eyes:** face out of the spray, rinse eyes with cupped lukewarm water, blink; keep eyes shut during shampoo rinse next time.
- **F3 No hot water:** check other taps — if all cold, the heater is off/tripped (breaker or pilot); shower can wait, or a kettle-and-basin wash substitutes.
- **F4 Water pooling past the curtain:** stop, re-lap the curtain *inside* the tub lip / close the door fully, towel up the escape water immediately — bathroom floors and ceilings below don't forgive puddles.

## Verification

You are clean, fully dry (feet included), dressed, the bathroom floor is dry, the towel and curtain are spread to dry, and ventilation is running.

## Variations

- Bathing cultures (`jp` sento/onsen etiquette): wash and rinse *fully* seated at the wash station before entering shared tubs — the tub is for soaking, not washing.
- Cold-water finish: optional; the temperature-change step is the balance moment — hold a support.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk concentrated in slips (wet tile + soap) and scalds. The rules that carry the weight: test before entering (2), towel before stepping out (6), feet dried before tile (7).
