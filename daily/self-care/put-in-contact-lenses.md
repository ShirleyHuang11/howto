## Goal

Contact lenses are inserted correctly, worn within their schedule, and removed and stored (or discarded) hygienically, keeping the habit that protects your eyes intact.

## Preconditions

- Lenses fitted and prescribed by an eye-care professional. First-time wearers learn insertion at the fitting; this recipe maintains the practice, it does not replace the fitting.
- Clean hands (`daily/self-care/wash-hands`), dried with a lint-free towel; fresh solution and a clean case for reusable lenses; a mirror and decent light.
- **Never water:** tap water, bottled water, and saliva never touch lenses or the case. Water carries organisms that cause serious eye infections.

## Steps

1. **Start with the same eye every time.** A fixed order prevents mixing left and right lenses. → *Expect:* a habit-locked sequence, right eye first for most people.
2. **Take the lens out and inspect it.** On a dry fingertip, check it is clean, undamaged, and right-side out: correct orientation looks like a bowl with straight walls; inside-out flares like a saucer at the rim. → *Expect:* an intact bowl-shaped lens. Torn or nicked lenses are discarded, never worn.
3. **Hold the lids and place the lens.** Pull the lower lid down with the middle finger of the lens hand, hold the upper lashes with the other hand, look slightly up or straight at the mirror, and place the lens gently on the eye. → *Expect:* the lens settles with a blink or two; vision clears in that eye.
4. **Repeat for the other eye, then assess.** → *Expect:* clear, comfortable vision in both eyes. Persistent grit, burning, or blur means take it out and restart at step 2; a lens that hurts is telling you something (debris, inside-out, or a tear).
5. **Respect the wearing schedule during the day.** Dailies come out and go in the trash; monthlies/two-weeklies track their opening date. Do not sleep, shower, or swim in lenses unless your prescriber explicitly approved a specific product for it. → *Expect:* removal happens before sleep, every night.
6. **Remove with clean hands.** Look up, slide the lens down onto the white with a fingertip, pinch gently with pad-of-finger and thumb. → *Expect:* lens out without fingernail contact with the eye.
7. **Clean and store reusables; discard dailies.** Rub each lens in fresh solution for a few seconds, rinse with solution, and store in fresh solution. Empty and air-dry the case daily, upside down on a tissue; replace the case monthly to quarterly. → *Expect:* lenses in fresh solution (never topped-up old solution), case drying open.

## Decision points

- Eyes red, painful, light-sensitive, or vision hazy: lenses stay out and the eye gets seen promptly. Wearing through a red eye is how minor problems become corneal ulcers.
- Makeup: lenses in first, makeup second; removal in reverse order.
- Dry environments and long screen days: preservative-free lubricating drops made for contacts, and planned lens-free hours in glasses. The glasses backup always travels with you.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lens folded or lost in the eye:** it cannot go behind the eye. Look in the opposite direction of where it feels, massage gently through the lid toward the corner, and retrieve it at the white. Add drops if it is stuck dry.
- **F2 Dropped the lens:** dailies are dead on arrival; reusables get a full rub-and-rinse with solution before another attempt. A lens off a bathroom floor is a judgment call that should usually lose.
- **F3 Fell asleep in them:** do not rip them out dry. Drops or a few blinks with artificial tears first, then remove, then a lens-free day in glasses.
- **F4 Ran out of solution while traveling:** dailies or glasses are the answer; the answer is never water (preconditions). Pharmacies at destinations sell solution; improvisation does not.

## Verification

Lenses went in clean-handed in the fixed order, vision is clear and comfortable, they came out before sleep, reusables sit in fresh solution in a drying-cycle case, and any red-or-painful eye ended the wearing immediately rather than being pushed through.

## Variations

- Rigid gas-permeable lenses have their own insertion and cleaning specifics from the fitter; the hygiene laws (hands, no water, schedule) are identical.
- Colored or costume lenses: prescription and fitting still apply legally and medically; novelty-shop lenses without a fitting are an eye-injury genre.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk concentrated in three absolutes: clean dry hands, no water ever, and out before sleep. The habit is easy; the infections that follow broken habits are not.
