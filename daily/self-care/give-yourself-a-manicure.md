## Goal

Shape, clean, and polish fingernails at home with a smooth finish and minimal irritation.

## Preconditions

- You have nail clippers, a file, a bowl of warm water, cuticle pusher, base coat, color polish, top coat, and remover.
- Nails and hands are free of open cuts, swelling, or signs of infection.
- You have a stable surface with good light and enough time for polish to dry.
- Old polish has been removed.

## Steps

1. **Wash and dry hands.** Clean under nails gently and dry completely. → *Expect:* nails are clean and not slippery.
2. **Trim if needed.** Clip only obvious extra length before filing. → *Expect:* nails are close to the desired length.
3. **Shape with a file.** File from the outer edge toward the center in one direction rather than sawing hard. → *Expect:* each nail has an even shape with no sharp corners.
4. **Soak briefly.** Soak fingertips in warm water for 3 to 5 minutes. → *Expect:* cuticles feel softer but skin is not wrinkled or sore.
5. **Push cuticles gently.** Use a cuticle pusher to nudge softened cuticle back; do not cut living skin. → *Expect:* nail plates look tidy without bleeding or pain.
6. **Clean the nail plate.** Wipe nails with remover or alcohol to remove oil, then let them dry. → *Expect:* the nail surface is dry and ready for polish.
7. **Apply base coat.** Paint a thin layer and cap the free edge lightly. → *Expect:* each nail has an even clear base.
8. **Apply color in thin coats.** [BRANCH: sheer | opaque] use two light coats for most polish, adding a third only if needed. → *Expect:* color looks even without thick ridges.
9. **Apply top coat.** Seal the color and free edge with a thin top coat. → *Expect:* nails look glossy or evenly matte, depending on the product.
10. **Let polish dry.** Keep hands still for at least 10 to 20 minutes and avoid hot water for about an hour. → *Expect:* light touch does not dent the surface.

## Decision points

- A nail edge keeps snagging → file the snag smooth before polish.
- Cuticles feel painful or look red → stop pushing and skip polish near that area.
- Polish floods the skin → clean the edge with a small brush dipped in remover before it sets.
- You need hands soon → use quick-dry drops or skip color and apply only clear coat.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cuticle cut or bleeding:** detect by pain or blood → recover by washing, applying pressure, and stopping the manicure on that finger.
- **F2 Thick polish bubbles:** detect by raised bubbles after a few minutes → recover by removing that nail's polish and repainting thinner coats.
- **F3 Smudged finish:** detect by a dent or smear → recover by smoothing with a little remover or repainting the affected nail.
- **F4 Peeling within a day:** detect by lifting at the tip → recover next time by cleaning oils off the nail and capping the free edge.

## Verification

All nails are shaped smoothly, cuticles are uncut and comfortable, and polish is dry enough that a light touch leaves no mark.

## Variations

- No-polish manicure: stop after shaping, cuticle care, and hand cream.
- Gel polish: follow the lamp and removal instructions exactly to avoid nail damage.
- Brittle nails: keep the shape shorter and use a ridge-filling base coat.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when skin is intact. Do not cut cuticles, do not share tools without cleaning them, ventilate strong polish fumes, and stop if there is swelling, heat, pus, or worsening pain.
