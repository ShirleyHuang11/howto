## Goal

Sleep and wake times land within 30 minutes of a chosen target on most nights, achieved by anchoring a fixed wake time and shifting gradually, so mornings stop requiring heroics.

## Preconditions

- A realistic target: a wake time that fits your obligations and a bedtime that allows 7-9 hours before it.
- An alarm placed across the room, out of arm's reach from the bed.
- A window with morning daylight, or a bright light source of roughly 10,000 lux, reachable within an hour of waking.
- Two weeks where the schedule can be protected (not a stretch with red-eye flights or on-call nights).

## Steps

1. **Fix the wake time first and hold it seven days a week.** The wake anchor drives the whole system; bedtime follows it. Weekend sleep-ins of 2+ hours are self-inflicted jet lag and undo the week's progress. → *Expect:* one wake time written down, same on Saturday as on Tuesday.
2. **Shift toward the target gradually.** [BRANCH: current schedule is within an hour of target → jump straight to the target wake time | it is off by more → move the alarm 15-30 minutes earlier every 1-2 days until it lands] Do not attempt a 3-hour jump in one night; you will lie awake, conclude it failed, and quit. → *Expect:* each morning's alarm is only slightly earlier than yesterday's; each is achievable.
3. **Get bright light within an hour of waking.** Ten to twenty minutes of outdoor daylight (even overcast beats indoor bulbs by an order of magnitude), or the bright lamp during breakfast. Morning light is the strongest lever that pulls the body clock earlier. → *Expect:* light exposure logged before the first hour ends; grogginess lifts faster than it did without it.
4. **Put the alarm across the room and stand up at the first ring.** Getting vertical is the commitment point; snoozing trades fragmented, useless sleep for a groggier start. → *Expect:* feet on floor within a minute of the alarm; snooze not touched.
5. **Protect the day: caffeine cutoff and no rescue naps after mid-afternoon.** Last caffeine 8+ hours before target bedtime; if a nap is unavoidable, keep it to 20 minutes and before 3 p.m. (see `daily/self-care/take-a-power-nap`). → *Expect:* normal sleep pressure building by evening instead of being drained by a 6 p.m. couch nap.
6. **Dim the last two hours and set a screens-down time.** Lower the lights, warm-shift or shelve bright screens, and pick a wind-down routine (shower, reading, stretching) that starts at the same time nightly. Evening bright light pushes the clock later and cancels the morning's work. → *Expect:* the home is dim and the routine underway two hours before target bedtime.
7. **Go to bed sleepy, not merely on schedule.** Get in bed at the target only if drowsy; if 20 minutes pass without sleep, get up, sit in dim light with something boring on paper, and return when heavy-eyed. Lying awake trains the bed as a place of frustration. → *Expect:* time in bed awake stays short; the bed stays associated with sleep.
8. **Hold the anchor for two weeks before judging.** The wake time stays fixed through the rough first days; daytime sleepiness in days 2-4 is the adjustment working, not failing. → *Expect:* by days 7-10, waking shortly before the alarm and natural evening sleepiness near the target bedtime.

## Decision points

- Schedule is inverted by many hours (night-shift recovery, severe delay) → the 15-30 minute method still works but takes weeks; shifting earlier is slower than shifting later, so be patient or consult a sleep clinician.
- One bad night happens → change nothing; keep the same wake time the next morning and let the next night's sleep pressure repair it. Compensatory sleeping-in is how one bad night becomes a bad month.
- Loud snoring with gasping, or crushing sleepiness despite 8 hours in bed for weeks → screen for sleep apnea with a clinician; no schedule discipline fixes a breathing disorder.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 The all-nighter reset:** staying up all night to "get tired enough" produces one crash night and a rebound to the old pattern → abandon the shortcut, return to gradual shifting from wherever the schedule currently sits.
- **F2 Weekend drift:** Friday and Saturday nights slide late, Monday feels like jet lag because it is → cap weekend variance at 60 minutes on both ends and keep the morning light habit on weekends especially.
- **F3 Lying awake at the new earlier bedtime:** bedtime was moved faster than the wake time earned → stop advancing bedtime; hold the wake anchor and let sleepiness catch up over 3-4 nights.
- **F4 Alarm slept through entirely:** sleep debt is deep in week one → move the alarm farther away, add a second offset alarm, and brighten the room at wake time (curtains open or a light on a timer).

## Verification

Over the trailing week: wake time within 30 minutes of target on at least 6 of 7 days including the weekend, falling asleep within about 20 minutes of lights-out on most nights, and no naps after 3 p.m. Daytime alertness without heroic caffeine confirms the schedule has actually taken.

## Variations

- Eastward travel prep: run the same gradual advance for 3-4 days before departure, shifting toward destination time; morning light on arrival, local schedule immediately.
- Teenagers: biology runs late; hold the fixed wake time but expect the shift to take longer, and be strict about evening screens rather than about willpower.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not compensate for the transition's sleepy days by driving drowsy; days 2-4 of a shift are when microsleeps at the wheel happen. Persistent insomnia beyond a month, or apnea signs, warrant a clinician. No personal data is involved unless a sleep tracker is added; the alarm-and-light method needs none.
