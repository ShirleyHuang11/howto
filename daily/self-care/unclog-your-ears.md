## Goal

Ear pressure or mild blockage is relieved safely, or warning signs are recognized and medical care is chosen instead.

## Preconditions

- Symptoms are mild pressure, fullness, or muffled hearing.
- No severe pain, fluid drainage, recent ear surgery, or known eardrum injury.
- Warm compress or warm shower access is available.

## Steps

1. **Screen for warning signs.** Check for pain, discharge, fever, dizziness, sudden hearing loss, or injury. → *Expect:* either no red flags or a clear reason to stop.
2. **Swallow and yawn.** Sip water, chew gum, or yawn several times. → *Expect:* small clicks or pressure shifts may occur.
3. **Try a gentle Valsalva.** Pinch nose, close mouth, and blow softly for 1 to 2 seconds. → *Expect:* ears may pop without pain.
4. **Use warmth.** Hold a warm compress against the ear or take a warm shower. → *Expect:* muscles relax and fullness may ease.
5. **Stay upright.** Sit or stand, especially if congestion is involved. → *Expect:* drainage feels easier than lying flat.
6. **Repeat gently.** [BRANCH: pressure after flight | pressure from cold congestion] repeat swallowing or steam, not forceful blowing. → *Expect:* gradual improvement without sharp pain.
7. **Avoid inserting objects.** Do not use cotton swabs, hairpins, or candles in the ear canal. → *Expect:* the ear canal is not irritated or scratched.
8. **Escalate if needed.** Contact a clinician if symptoms persist, worsen, or include warning signs. → *Expect:* unsafe cases are handled medically.

## Decision points

- Pain or discharge is present → do not pop the ear; call a clinician.
- Recent surgery, tube, or eardrum rupture → avoid pressure maneuvers.
- Fullness lasts more than a few days → schedule medical evaluation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Forced pressure pain:** detect sharp pain during Valsalva, recover by stopping immediately and seeking care if it continues.
- **F2 Wax pushed deeper:** detect worse muffling after swab use, recover by avoiding swabs and asking about wax removal.
- **F3 Infection signs:** detect fever, drainage, or worsening pain, recover by contacting a clinician.
- **F4 Dizziness:** detect spinning or imbalance, recover by sitting down and getting medical advice.

## Verification

Pressure or muffled hearing improves without pain, discharge, dizziness, or any object inserted into the ear canal.

## Variations

- After flying: swallowing during descent works better than waiting until landing.
- With nasal congestion: steam and hydration may help, but avoid forceful nose blowing.
- Suspected wax: use clinician-approved drops only if no eardrum concerns exist.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because ear pain, discharge, dizziness, or sudden hearing loss can signal a medical problem. Do not put tools into the ear, and get medical care for red flags.
