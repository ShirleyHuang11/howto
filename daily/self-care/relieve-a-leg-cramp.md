## Goal

A sudden leg cramp relaxes, the muscle is gently reloaded, and you know when leg pain is not just a cramp.

## Preconditions

- Sudden tight cramping in the calf, foot, or thigh without major injury.
- Ability to stand or sit safely, plus water and optional heat.
- No chest pain, shortness of breath, one-sided swelling, redness, warmth, or calf pain after long travel. Those are urgent medical signs.

## Steps

1. **Stop the trigger.** Pause exercise or sit up in bed so you can control the leg. → *Expect:* the muscle is still tight but you are balanced.
2. **Stretch the cramped muscle.** For a calf cramp, straighten the knee and pull toes toward the shin; for a hamstring, straighten the knee; for a quad, bend the knee gently. → *Expect:* the cramp begins to ease within 30 to 60 seconds.
3. **Load it lightly.** Put weight through the leg if safe, or press the foot against the floor or your hand. → *Expect:* the muscle receives a steady signal to release.
4. **Walk slowly.** Take small steps once the worst spasm lets go. → *Expect:* the muscle feels sore but no longer locked.
5. **Massage gently.** Rub along the tight band without digging hard into a tender spot. → *Expect:* remaining tightness softens.
6. **Hydrate.** Drink water, and consider an electrolyte drink after heavy sweating, vomiting, diarrhea, or long exercise. → *Expect:* thirst eases and you are not lightheaded.
7. **Apply heat after the spasm.** Use a warm towel or heating pad for 10 to 15 minutes if soreness remains. → *Expect:* the muscle feels looser and less guarded.
8. **Prevent nighttime repeats.** Stretch calves before bed, keep blankets loose around feet, and review hard workouts, dehydration, alcohol, and new medicines as triggers. → *Expect:* fewer cramps over the next nights.

## Decision points

- Pain came with a pop, bruising, or inability to bear weight → treat as an injury and seek medical advice.
- Cramps are frequent, severe, or new after starting a medication → call a clinician or pharmacist.
- One leg is swollen, red, warm, or tender in the calf → seek urgent care for possible clot evaluation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stretch makes pain sharper:** back off, support the leg, and switch to gentle walking or warmth.
- **F2 Cramp returns immediately:** repeat stretch and hydration, then reduce exercise intensity for the day.
- **F3 Soreness lasts more than a day or two:** rest, gentle range of motion, and consider medical advice if it limits walking.
- **F4 Weakness or numbness appears:** stop the recipe and seek medical care.

## Verification

The leg can move through normal range, you can walk without the muscle locking, and no clot, injury, or nerve warning signs are present.

## Variations

- Foot cramp: stand with the foot flat, then gently pull toes upward.
- Exercise cramps: reduce intensity, cool down, and replace fluids before restarting.
- Pregnancy: cramps are common, but swelling, headache, or shortness of breath needs prompt medical advice.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when it is a simple cramp. Get urgent help for chest pain, shortness of breath, one-sided swelling, red warm calf, fainting, new weakness, or pain after significant trauma.
