## Goal

A small shaving nick stops bleeding, stays clean, and is protected from irritation.

## Preconditions

- Clean tissue, gauze, cold water, and optional styptic pencil or balm are available.
- The cut is shallow, small, and not gaping.
- Seek professional or emergency care when bleeding will not stop, the cut is deep, edges gape, or the injury is near the eye.
- This recipe is for minor shaving cuts only.

## Steps

1. **Rinse the cut.** Use cool clean water to remove shaving cream and loose blood. → *Expect:* the cut surface is visible.
2. **Apply direct pressure.** Press clean tissue or gauze on the cut without peeking for several minutes. → *Expect:* bleeding slows.
3. **Use cold water.** Splash or hold cool water on the area after pressure. → *Expect:* oozing decreases.
4. **Apply styptic if available.** Wet the styptic pencil and touch only the nick briefly. → *Expect:* stinging is brief and bleeding reduces.
5. **Blot gently.** Pat around the area without wiping across the cut. → *Expect:* the clot stays in place.
6. **Add a tiny balm layer.** Dab a small amount of fragrance-free balm or petroleum jelly over the nick. → *Expect:* the area is protected without smearing.
7. **Avoid aftershave on it.** Keep alcohol-based products off the open cut. → *Expect:* irritation and burning are avoided.
8. **Pause shaving nearby.** Do not run the razor over the cut again. → *Expect:* the nick does not reopen.
9. **Check after 10 minutes.** Look for continued bleeding, swelling, or a flap of skin. → *Expect:* the cut is sealed or needs more attention.
10. **Clean the razor.** Rinse and dry the razor before storing it. → *Expect:* future irritation risk is reduced.

## Decision points

- Bleeding continues after 10 to 15 minutes of firm pressure → seek medical advice.
- Cut is deep, gaping, or caused by a dirty blade → consider urgent care and tetanus advice.
- [BRANCH: tiny nick | deeper slice] a tiny nick can be managed at home, while a deeper slice needs assessment.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not glue or seal a dirty or deep cut closed without medical advice.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Clot wiped away:** detect bleeding restarts after rubbing, recover by applying steady pressure again.
- **F2 Too much balm:** detect slippery smearing, recover by blotting excess around the cut.
- **F3 Styptic irritation:** detect strong burning or rash, recover by rinsing and avoiding repeat use.
- **F4 Deeper cut:** detect gaping edges or persistent bleeding, recover by seeking care.

## Verification

The shaving nick is no longer actively bleeding and is lightly protected without signs of a deeper wound.

## Variations

- No styptic pencil: pressure and cold water are usually enough.
- Sensitive skin: use plain petroleum jelly instead of scented balm.
- Neck cut: avoid tight collars until the area is dry.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for tiny nicks, but seek professional or emergency care when bleeding persists, the cut is deep, or infection signs appear later.
