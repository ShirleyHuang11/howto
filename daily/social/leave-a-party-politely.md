## Goal

Exit a party without derailing the room or making the host manage your departure.

## Preconditions

- You have your coat, bag, keys, phone, and ride plan.
- You know who hosted or organized the gathering.
- You can leave safely without needing an impaired driver.

## Steps

1. **Pick the timing.** Leave during a natural lull, after a song ends, between activities, or when people are moving rooms. → *Expect:* your exit does not interrupt a toast, meal, game, or serious conversation.
2. **Collect belongings quietly.** Get coat, bag, charger, and leftovers before announcing the exit. → *Expect:* you are not searching after saying goodbye.
3. **Thank the host directly.** Say a specific thanks, such as "Thanks for dinner, the food was great." → *Expect:* the host hears appreciation before logistics.
4. **Use the quick-exit line.** Say you are heading out and do not add a long excuse. → *Expect:* the host understands you are leaving now.
5. **Choose the goodbye style.** [BRANCH: quick exit | long goodbye] quick: wave to the group and leave. long: say goodbye only to close friends nearby. → *Expect:* you avoid restarting multiple conversations.
6. **Decline new offers cleanly.** If offered another drink, ride, or dessert, say "No thanks, I need to head out." → *Expect:* the offer is acknowledged without renegotiation.
7. **Leave when you say you are leaving.** Walk to the door after the goodbye rather than drifting back in. → *Expect:* the exit takes under a few minutes.
8. **Check transport before stepping away.** Confirm ride, transit, walking route, or driver sobriety. → *Expect:* you leave the location safely.
9. **Follow up the next day.** Send a short thank-you message if the event was hosted at home or required real effort. → *Expect:* the host gets appreciation without needing to reply immediately.

## Decision points

- Host is busy cooking, performing, or handling a problem → wave if appropriate and send the thanks by text later.
- You need to leave early from a seated dinner → tell the host before the meal or at the first private moment.
- Someone pressures you to stay → repeat the same exit line and move toward the door.
- You are not safe to travel → ask for help arranging a ride or wait in a safe place.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Goodbye spiral:** each goodbye starts another chat, recover by saying you have to go now and physically moving.
- **F2 Missing host:** you cannot find the host, recover by thanking a cohost and texting the host later.
- **F3 Overshared excuse:** your explanation gets awkward, recover by returning to "I need to head out, but thank you."
- **F4 Forgotten item:** you notice after leaving, recover by texting once with a specific pickup plan.

## Verification

You have left with your belongings, thanked the host directly or by follow-up, and your departure did not stop the event.

## Variations

- Large party: a group wave plus later text is often better than a receiving-line exit.
- Small dinner: tell the host directly because your seat will be noticed.
- Work event: thank the organizer and one senior contact if relevant, then leave cleanly.

## Safety & privacy

Do not invent private excuses that invite follow-up. Do not ride with an impaired driver; a polite exit is secondary to getting home safely.
