## Goal

Reopen contact after conflict with accountability, low pressure, and a small next step rather than a full courtroom replay.

## Preconditions

- Contact would be safe and respectful for both people.
- You can own your part without demanding equal confession.
- You are prepared for no reply, a slow reply, or a no.

## Steps

1. **Check your motive.** Make sure you want repair or peace, not to win the old argument. → *Expect:* the goal is reconnection, not vindication.
2. **Own your part briefly.** Name the behavior or absence you regret. → *Expect:* your accountability appears before any ask.
3. **Choose a low-stakes channel.** [BRANCH: small conflict | serious hurt] Text can open the door; a call or in-person talk can follow if welcomed. → *Expect:* they can decline without a scene.
4. **Write a simple opener.** "I have thought about how I handled things, and I am sorry for my part. I hope you are well." → *Expect:* the message does not demand immediate depth.
5. **Avoid relitigating.** Do not attach evidence, screenshots, or a full timeline. → *Expect:* the old fight is not restarted in the opener.
6. **Offer a small next step.** Suggest a short call, coffee, or permission to send a fuller apology. → *Expect:* reconnection has a manageable shape.
7. **Send once and wait.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not bypass blocks, contact through other people, or use public posts to force a response. → *Expect:* their choice remains theirs.
8. **Respond to the door they open.** If they engage, listen first and keep accountability alive; if they decline, thank them and stop. → *Expect:* respect matters more than access.

## Decision points

- You caused serious harm → lead with a complete apology and expect repair to take time.
- They caused harm and you need safety → choose distance, mediation, or no contact instead.
- They reply with anger → acknowledge it without defending, then ask whether a later conversation is welcome.
- They want to revisit details → agree on a time and purpose rather than fighting by text.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hidden debate:** detect paragraphs proving your side, recover by cutting them.
- **F2 Accountability swap:** detect "I am sorry, but you..." recover by owning your part alone.
- **F3 Pressure follow-up:** detect checking or nudging for a reply, recover by waiting or letting go.
- **F4 Unsafe contact:** detect fear, coercion, or prior no-contact boundaries, recover by not reaching out.

## Verification

You sent one respectful opener that owns your part, avoids relitigating, proposes a small next step, and leaves the other person free to answer or not.

## Variations

- Family conflict: keep the opener shorter than the history feels.
- Friendship drift after a fight: combine accountability with a simple invitation.
- Workplace falling-out: keep it behavior-focused and professional.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk when old conflict includes harm, coercion, or unwanted contact. Do not evade blocks or involve bystanders without consent. Safety and boundaries outrank reconciliation.
