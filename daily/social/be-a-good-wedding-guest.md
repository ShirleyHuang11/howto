## Goal

You attend a wedding in a way that respects the couple, the schedule, the dress code, and the tone of the event.

## Preconditions

- You received an invitation or confirmed plus-one status.
- You know the date, location, dress code, and RSVP deadline.
- Travel, lodging, and gift budget are realistic.

## Steps

1. **RSVP on time.** Reply by the requested method before the deadline. → *Expect:* the couple or planner has your attendance count.
2. **Follow the invite details.** Bring only named guests and respect adult-only or no-plus-one wording. → *Expect:* your plan matches the invitation.
3. **Choose dress code first.** Read the stated dress code and venue clues before shopping or packing. → *Expect:* your outfit fits the level of formality.
4. **Use the registry.** Buy from the registry or give money if that is customary for the couple. → *Expect:* the gift is useful and not a duplicate burden.
5. **Arrive early.** Plan to be seated 10 to 15 minutes before the ceremony. → *Expect:* you are not entering during the processional.
6. **Put the phone away.** Silence it and avoid aisle photos unless invited to take them. → *Expect:* the photographer and guests have clear views.
7. **Respect reception timing.** Wait for meal, toast, dance, and exit cues from hosts or staff. → *Expect:* you do not interrupt planned moments.
8. **Leave graciously.** Thank the couple or close family if practical, then depart without creating cleanup work. → *Expect:* your exit is calm and noticed only appropriately.

## Decision points

- You cannot attend after RSVPing yes → tell the couple or planner as soon as possible.
- You are invited to toast → keep it brief, kind, and free of private stories.
- You are unsure about attire → ask a wedding party member early, not on the day.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Late RSVP:** detect a missed deadline, recover by apologizing and responding immediately.
- **F2 Dress mismatch:** detect outfit too casual or attention-grabbing, recover by simplifying accessories or adding a jacket.
- **F3 Phone disruption:** detect ringing or blocked photos, recover by silencing and putting the phone away.
- **F4 Overlong toast:** detect restless room or staff waiting, recover by ending with a clear congratulations.

## Verification

You RSVP as requested, arrive before the ceremony starts, follow the dress code, and avoid disrupting ceremony, photos, meal, or toasts.

## Variations

- Destination wedding: book travel early and do not assume extra events are hosted.
- Cash-gift culture: use a card or envelope system customary to the couple.
- Unplugged ceremony: keep the phone fully away until cocktail hour.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not post photos before the couple does if they seem private, drink within your limits, and arrange safe transportation.
