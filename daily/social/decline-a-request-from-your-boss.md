## Goal

Decline or renegotiate a request from your boss while being honest about capacity and keeping alignment on priorities.

## Preconditions

- You understand the request, deadline, and expected result well enough to respond.
- Your current workload, deadlines, and blockers are known.
- A suitable communication channel is available: in person, message, email, or meeting.

## Steps

1. **Clarify the ask.** Restate the deliverable, deadline, and quality bar in your own words. → *Expect:* your boss can confirm what is being requested.
2. **Check real capacity.** Compare the request against your current deadlines, meetings, energy, and commitments. → *Expect:* you know what would be delayed or reduced.
3. **Acknowledge the importance.** Start with the business reason or urgency instead of a flat refusal. → *Expect:* your response shows you heard the request.
4. **State the constraint honestly.** Say what prevents you from taking it on as requested, using specific workload or timing facts. → *Expect:* the capacity issue is concrete rather than vague.
5. **Offer alternatives.** [BRANCH: reprioritize | reduce scope | delegate | extend deadline] Suggest one or two workable paths. → *Expect:* your boss has options to choose from.
6. **Ask for priority alignment.** Ask which task should move if this new request is more important. → *Expect:* tradeoffs are explicit.
7. **Confirm the decision in writing.** Send a brief note with the chosen priority, deadline, and any work you will not do. → *Expect:* both sides have the same record.
8. **Follow through on the agreed path.** Start the selected option or hand off the declined work cleanly. → *Expect:* the work plan changes without ambiguity.
9. **Avoid hidden refusal.** Do not silently accept and then miss the deadline. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* lost trust is hard to repair; raise the conflict before the deadline passes. → *Expect:* the issue is visible while there is still time to adjust.

## Decision points

- Request is urgent and truly business critical → ask what existing work stops now.
- Request is outside your role or authority → offer the right owner or ask for formal direction.
- Boss reacts negatively → stay factual, repeat the tradeoff, and ask for the priority decision.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vague no:** detect a response like "I can't" without facts → add the specific deadline or workload conflict.
- **F2 Too many alternatives:** detect confusion about options → narrow to the two most realistic choices.
- **F3 No written alignment:** detect only a hallway conversation → send a short recap.
- **F4 Emotional escalation:** detect defensiveness or blame → pause, return to priorities, and suggest a follow-up meeting.

## Verification

Your boss has confirmed which request, deadline, scope, or existing priority changes, and that decision is captured in writing.

## Variations

- Email: use a concise structure of acknowledgment, constraint, options, and requested decision.
- Live conversation: bring a visible list of current priorities.
- Contractor or hourly role: include budget or approved hours as a capacity constraint.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to work relationships and performance expectations. Do not disclose private health, family, or coworker details beyond what is necessary to explain capacity.
