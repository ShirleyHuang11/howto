## Goal

You stay in someone else's home while reducing work for the host and leaving the relationship better than you found it.

## Preconditions

- The host has clearly agreed to the visit dates and guest count.
- You know arrival time, departure time, address, and sleeping arrangement.
- You can cover your own transport, toiletries, and special food needs.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the plan.** Recheck dates, arrival time, parking, pets, and house rules before traveling. → *Expect:* the host knows exactly when to expect you.
2. **Bring something useful.** Bring a small gift, groceries, coffee, flowers, or a treat suited to the household. → *Expect:* the item adds pleasure without creating work.
3. **Arrive prepared.** Pack toiletries, chargers, medication, sleepwear, and any special dietary items. → *Expect:* you do not need emergency errands from the host.
4. **Respect the schedule.** Ask about wake times, meals, work hours, and bathroom timing. → *Expect:* your routine fits around theirs.
5. **Keep your area tidy.** Make the bed, contain luggage, and keep bathroom items together. → *Expect:* shared rooms remain usable.
6. **Help without taking over.** [BRANCH: host welcomes help | host declines help] wash dishes or set the table, or step aside gracefully. → *Expect:* help reduces effort, not control.
7. **Clean before leaving.** Strip sheets if asked, gather towels, remove trash, and check for belongings. → *Expect:* the room is easy to reset.
8. **Send thanks.** Text the same day and follow with a note or small gesture for a longer stay. → *Expect:* the host feels appreciated after you leave.

## Decision points

- Host has work or children on a schedule → be self-sufficient during busy hours.
- You break or spill something → tell the host immediately and offer to fix or replace it.
- You want to invite someone over → ask first and accept no without pressure.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overstaying:** detect host fatigue or schedule strain, recover by confirming departure and leaving on time.
- **F2 Mess creep:** detect belongings spreading into shared areas, recover by resetting your space each morning.
- **F3 Food mismatch:** detect special diet burdening the host, recover by buying your own staples.
- **F4 Hidden damage:** detect a stain, break, or lost key, recover by disclosing it and paying replacement cost.

## Verification

At departure, your sleeping area and shared spaces are tidy, the host knows you are grateful, and no unresolved mess or damage is hidden.

## Variations

- One-night stay: bring breakfast or coffee and keep luggage packed.
- Long stay: contribute groceries, laundry supplies, or a planned meal.
- Close family: still confirm schedules and cleanup expectations.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Respect private rooms, documents, medicine cabinets, Wi-Fi passwords, and household routines; do not share address or photos without permission.
