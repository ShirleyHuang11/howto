## Goal

Ask someone on a date clearly and respectfully, with low pressure and full room for any answer.

## Preconditions

- You have a reasonable context for personal interest.
- There is no power imbalance that would make refusal costly for them.
- You are ready to accept yes, no, or no reply gracefully.

## Steps

1. **Choose timing that leaves an exit.** Ask when they are not trapped at work, in a car, at your home, or under public pressure. → *Expect:* they can answer freely.
2. **Be warm and brief.** Start with a simple personal note, not a confession monologue. → *Expect:* the ask feels human, not heavy.
3. **Use the word date if clarity helps.** Say, "Would you like to get coffee with me as a date?" → *Expect:* the invitation is not hidden as vague hanging out.
4. **Suggest something specific.** [BRANCH: in person | text] Offer a simple plan, such as coffee Saturday afternoon or a walk after work. → *Expect:* they know what yes would mean.
5. **Keep pressure low.** Add "No pressure if not" only once, then stop. → *Expect:* the invitation gives space without begging.
6. **Wait for the answer.** Do not fill silence with persuasion. → *Expect:* they have time to respond honestly.
7. **Accept the answer cleanly.** If yes, make the plan; if no, say thanks for being direct and move on. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not ask in a way that could damage someone's job, housing, safety, or public dignity. → *Expect:* their answer is treated as final.
8. **Behave normally afterward.** Do not punish, tease, or recruit friends to interpret the answer. → *Expect:* respect continues after the ask.

## Decision points

- They are a coworker, employee, student, client, or service worker → consider not asking, or use a policy-aware path with extra care.
- They say maybe → offer one concrete option, then let them initiate if they want more.
- They do not reply → treat silence as no and do not chase.
- They say yes but seem uneasy → give an easy out and slow down.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vague ask:** detect "we should hang out sometime," recover by naming a date-like plan.
- **F2 Pressure spiral:** detect multiple follow-ups, recover by stopping and respecting the answer.
- **F3 Public cornering:** detect an audience forming, recover by dropping the ask and choosing privacy later.
- **F4 Rejection defensiveness:** detect arguing or joking at their expense, recover by apologizing and backing off.

## Verification

You made one clear, specific, low-pressure invitation and accepted the response without persuasion, punishment, or repeated follow-up.

## Variations

- Dating app: clarity can be shorter because the context is already romantic.
- Existing friend: acknowledge the friendship and make refusal easy.
- Long-distance: suggest a video call or planned visit only if both are realistic.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk when consent and context are respected. Avoid asking people who depend on your approval or service, and never retaliate against a no.
