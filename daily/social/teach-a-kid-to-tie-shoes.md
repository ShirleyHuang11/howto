## Goal

Teach a child the bunny-ears shoe-tying method through slow practice, visible steps, and enough repetition for muscle memory to start forming.

## Preconditions

- A lace-up shoe with medium-length laces is available, preferably off the child's foot.
- The child has enough hand control to pinch, cross, and pull laces.
- You have a calm practice window when no one is rushing out the door.
- Optional two-color laces or a practice board are available if the child mixes up sides.

## Steps

1. **Practice off-foot.** Put the shoe on a table or the child's lap with the toe pointing away from the child. → *Expect:* the child can see and reach both laces.
2. **Name the two laces.** If helpful, call them left and right or use two colors so each lace has an identity. → *Expect:* the child can point to the lace you name.
3. **Make the starting knot.** Cross one lace over the other, tuck it through the hole, and pull both ends snug. → *Expect:* a simple base knot sits flat on the shoe.
4. **Make two bunny ears.** Fold each lace into a loop, keeping the loose ends short enough that the loops do not collapse. → *Expect:* the child is pinching one loop in each hand.
5. **Cross the ears.** Cross one loop over the other, the same way the first knot crossed. → *Expect:* the loops make an X above the shoe.
6. **Tuck one ear through.** Push one loop through the opening under the X and grab it from the other side. → *Expect:* both loops are visible again, one from each side.
7. **Pull the ears, not the ends.** Pull both loops outward until the knot tightens, then adjust loops and tails to similar lengths. → *Expect:* the shoe has a bow that stays tied when gently tugged.
8. **Repeat slowly.** [BRANCH: child leads | adult demonstrates] let the child narrate each step, and only demonstrate again when they cannot find the next motion. → *Expect:* the child completes more of the sequence each round.
9. **Move to on-foot practice.** Put the shoe on the child's foot after off-foot success and repeat with the toe pointing away. → *Expect:* the child can tie in the position they will use day to day.

## Decision points

- If the child cannot pinch loops yet → practice with larger laces, pipe cleaners, or a tying board before expecting a real shoe.
- If frustration rises → stop after one successful substep and return later.
- If laces are too short or slippery → switch shoes or laces so the skill, not the equipment, is the challenge.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bow falls apart:** the child pulled the lace ends instead of loops → reset and practice pulling only bunny ears.
- **F2 Knot is loose:** the base knot was not snug → tighten the first knot before making loops.
- **F3 Loops vanish:** the child releases one loop during the tuck → slow down and have them pinch closer to the knot.
- **F4 Child memorizes backward:** shoe orientation changes each time → keep the toe pointing away until muscle memory is stable.

## Verification

The child can tie one shoe into a snug bow using bunny ears with only verbal prompts or no help.

## Variations

- `two-color-laces`: use one color per side to make crossing and tucking easier to describe.
- `one-loop-method`: teach later if the child wants a faster method after mastering the bow concept.
- `adaptive`: elastic laces or hook-and-loop shoes are reasonable if tying is painful or not developmentally ready.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep practice pressure low, avoid shaming, and check that finished laces are not so long that they drag or trip the child.
