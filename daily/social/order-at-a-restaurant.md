## Goal

You are seated at a sit-down restaurant, have ordered and received a meal, paid correctly including any customary tip, and left — following the social script smoothly at each stage.

## Preconditions

- A restaurant chosen, open, and within budget (menus with prices are usually posted online or at the door).
- A payment method the restaurant accepts (check for cash-only signs).
- For popular places/peak hours: a reservation, or willingness to wait.

## Steps

1. **Enter and wait to be seated where that's the custom.** Look for a "please wait to be seated" sign or a host stand; state party size: "Table for two, please." [BRANCH: host seats you | "seat yourself" sign → choose any open, cleaned table] → *Expect:* you are at a table with menus, not having walked past a host stand unseated.
2. **Read the menu and settle choices before engaging the server.** Note dish, drink, and any allergy/modification needs. → *Expect:* everyone at the table can state their order; questions for the server are formulated ("What's in the X?").
3. **Order drinks when first asked, food when ready.** Servers typically do a drinks pass first; if you need more time for food: "Could we have a few more minutes?" is the standard, welcome phrase. → *Expect:* drinks ordered; server returns; food ordered — with allergies stated *at ordering time*, not at delivery.
4. **Wait, and handle the food's arrival.** Confirm each plate matches what was ordered as it lands. Wrong dish → say so immediately and politely — it's fixable now, awkward after you've eaten half. → *Expect:* correct dishes in front of the right people; server's "anything else?" answered.
5. **Eat; flag needs by catching the server's eye or a small raised hand.** Shouting across the room or snapping fingers violates the script everywhere. → *Expect:* refills/extras arrive via the eye-contact-and-nod protocol.
6. **Ask for the bill.** "Could we have the check/bill, please?" — in much of the world the bill never comes until requested; waiting silently is not the script, asking is. → *Expect:* an itemized bill; scan it for accuracy (items you didn't order, doubled lines).
7. **Pay with the customary tip.** [BRANCH: card terminal at the table (tip prompt on screen) | cash left with the bill | counter payment] Tip norms vary hard by country — see Variations; when unsure of local norms, ask a local or observe. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* card tips can't be adjusted after confirming — check the total before tapping. → *Expect:* payment accepted; change/receipt returned.
8. **Leave the table in reasonable order and depart with a thanks.** → *Expect:* a "thank you" exchanged; nothing of yours left at the table.

## Decision points

- Long wait quoted at entry → decide: leave a name for the waitlist, or go elsewhere; drifting to hover near tables is off-script.
- Dietary restriction where the menu is unclear → ask the server directly before ordering; kitchens handle direct questions far better than post-delivery surprises.
- The dish is genuinely bad (undercooked, off) vs. not-to-taste → the first is a legitimate polite send-back; the second is your call to make without expecting a replacement.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bill error:** point it out to the server matter-of-factly; itemized bills get corrected without drama.
- **F2 Order forgotten (30+ min, others served):** flag the server: "I think my dish may have been missed?" — kitchens drop tickets; polite escalation gets it prioritized or comped.
- **F3 Card declined:** offer the fallback method; if truly nothing works, offer contact details and return same-day — restaurants deal with this more often than you'd think.
- **F4 Language barrier:** point at menu items, use translation apps, and numbers-with-fingers; menus with photos exist for exactly this.

## Verification

You've left the restaurant having eaten what you ordered, paid a bill you checked, tipped per local custom, with your belongings, and no unresolved dispute behind you.

## Variations

- `us`: tipping 18–20 % is effectively obligatory table-service wage; tip on the pre-tax total.
- `jp`: no tipping — leaving money can cause confusion; pay at the front counter, often taking the bill slip up yourself.
- Much of Europe: service often included ("service compris"); rounding up or ~5–10 % for good service is customary, not obligatory.
- `cn`: no tipping; scan-the-QR ordering and payment at the table is common — the server steps compress into app interactions.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. State allergies explicitly every time (step 3) — it's a safety disclosure, not a preference. Card handling: in high-skimming regions prefer the terminal brought to you over the card leaving your sight.
