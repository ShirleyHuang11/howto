## Goal

Handle a coworker problem by documenting facts, addressing behavior directly when appropriate, and escalating through the right work channel if it continues.

## Preconditions

- The issue affects work, safety, respect, or communication.
- You can separate observed behavior from guesses about motive.
- You know, or can find, your manager or HR escalation path.

## Steps

1. **Write down facts promptly.** Record date, time, location, people present, behavior, and work impact. → *Expect:* you have a factual note, not a vent.
2. **Check the pattern.** [BRANCH: one-off irritation | repeated or serious behavior] Decide whether direct repair is enough or documentation should continue. → *Expect:* the response matches the severity.
3. **Clarify expectations.** Identify what behavior needs to change, such as deadlines, tone, interruptions, or handoffs. → *Expect:* you can state the ask in one sentence.
4. **Address it directly first when safe.** Say, "When X happens, Y is blocked. I need Z going forward." → *Expect:* the focus stays on behavior and impact.
5. **Confirm agreements in writing.** Send a brief follow-up with decisions, owners, and dates. → *Expect:* there is a shared record.
6. **Keep documenting repeats.** Note new incidents and any attempts to resolve them. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not secretly record audio or share private HR details unless company policy and local law allow it. → *Expect:* escalation evidence is organized.
7. **Escalate to your manager.** Bring facts, impact, what you tried, and what help you need. → *Expect:* the manager receives an actionable workplace issue.
8. **Use HR or formal channels for harassment, discrimination, threats, or retaliation.** Do not manage serious misconduct alone. → *Expect:* the issue reaches the channel responsible for risk.

## Decision points

- The behavior is unsafe, discriminatory, threatening, or retaliatory → skip informal coaching and escalate.
- Your manager is the problem or ignores it → use HR, skip-level, ethics, or documented company process.
- The coworker improves → keep normal records and do not keep punishing the old behavior.
- You contributed to the conflict → own your part while still naming the needed change.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Motive argument:** detect "they are trying to sabotage me," recover by documenting observable behavior and impact.
- **F2 No written trail:** detect relying on memory, recover by writing neutral notes today.
- **F3 Public confrontation:** detect the conversation becoming a meeting spectacle, recover by moving to private follow-up.
- **F4 Escalation without ask:** detect dumping frustration on a manager, recover by stating the decision or support needed.

## Verification

You have factual documentation, made a direct behavior-focused request when safe, and escalated through manager or HR channels if the conduct continued or was serious.

## Variations

- Remote coworker: rely more on written agreements, meeting notes, and clear handoff definitions.
- Cross-team issue: involve both managers when ownership or priorities conflict.
- Contractor or vendor: use the contract owner rather than informal pressure.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because jobs and reputations are involved. Keep records factual and private, avoid gossip, and use formal channels for protected-class harassment, threats, safety issues, or retaliation.
