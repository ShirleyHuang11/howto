## Goal

Write and send a sincere message that tells someone specifically why they matter.

## Preconditions

- A person you want to appreciate, comfort, congratulate, or reconnect with.
- A private enough channel for the relationship and subject.
- A few true details you can name.

## Steps

1. **Choose the purpose.** [BRANCH: gratitude | care | congratulations | apology-adjacent] Decide what the message should make clearer. → *Expect:* the message has one emotional job.
2. **Name the person directly.** Start with their name or a natural greeting. → *Expect:* the opening feels meant for them.
3. **Use one specific memory or trait.** Mention what they did, how they show up, or a moment you remember. → *Expect:* the message cannot be sent unchanged to anyone else.
4. **Say why it mattered.** Connect the detail to its impact on you or others. → *Expect:* the feeling has evidence.
5. **Keep the tone genuine.** Use your own words, not grand language you would never say aloud. → *Expect:* the sentence sounds like you.
6. **Cut performance.** Remove phrases written to impress an audience or force a reaction. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once sent, private feelings can be forwarded or screenshotted, so do not include secrets or pressure. → *Expect:* the message serves the recipient, not your image.
7. **End simply.** Close with appreciation, care, pride, or a concrete next step if appropriate. → *Expect:* the ending does not demand a perfect reply.
8. **Send it.** Do one final check for name, channel, and tone, then send. → *Expect:* the message leaves drafts and reaches them.

## Decision points

- The message is about harm you caused → use `daily/social/apologize-well` instead of hiding an apology in praise.
- The recipient is grieving → avoid silver linings and focus on presence.
- The message is romantic → be clear without pressuring for a response.
- The channel is public → keep private details out.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Generic praise:** detect "you're amazing" with no detail, recover by adding one concrete example.
- **F2 Performative length:** detect paragraphs proving your sensitivity, recover by cutting to the true part.
- **F3 Hidden demand:** detect "I hope this makes you..." recover by removing pressure.
- **F4 Draft forever:** detect endless polishing, recover with one final read and send.

## Verification

The sent message names a specific detail, says why it matters, uses your natural voice, and does not require the recipient to manage your feelings.

## Variations

- Card: handwriting can be short; specificity does the work.
- Text: two to six sentences is often enough.
- Email: useful for longer appreciations or professional gratitude.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Assume any written message can be saved or shared. Avoid secrets, medical details, workplace confidential information, or emotional pressure disguised as sincerity.
