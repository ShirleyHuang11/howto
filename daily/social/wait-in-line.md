## Goal

You join, hold, and complete a queue correctly — reaching the service point in fair order without conflict.

## Preconditions

- A service point with a visible line, a ticket dispenser, or a virtual/app queue.
- Rough knowledge of what you'll need at the front (documents, payment, order decision) so the wait is spent getting ready.

## Steps

1. **Identify the queue system before joining.** [BRANCH: physical line → find its *end* | numbered-ticket machine → take a ticket | app/virtual queue → register] Ask when ambiguous: "Are you in line?" / "Is this the line for X?" → *Expect:* you know which mechanism governs and where its tail is.
2. **Join at the end.** Stand behind the last person at a culturally sane gap (~arm's length in most places); with a ticket, sit anywhere and watch the counter display. → *Expect:* your position established; late arrivals form up behind you.
3. **Hold your place.** Advance as the line does; keep the gap from yawning open (a big gap invites honest confusion and less-honest insertion). Phone use is fine — inattention that stalls the line is not. → *Expect:* steady position; no one legitimately confused about the order.
4. **Handle interlopers proportionately.** Someone joins ahead: assume confusion first — "Excuse me, the line ends back there." Most comply instantly. Non-compliance: one calm repetition, then let staff handle it; a queue spot is never worth escalation. → *Expect:* order restored or delegated, tone level throughout.
5. **Prepare during the final approach.** Documents/payment/order decision out and ready one or two turns before yours. → *Expect:* when called, you step up and transact without a pocket-dig delay.
6. **Complete your business efficiently and clear the service point.** Post-transaction packing/sorting happens a step aside, not at the counter. → *Expect:* the next person is being served while you finish organizing off to the side.

## Decision points

- Must leave the line briefly (restroom, forgotten item) → tell an adjacent person "Could you hold my spot? Right back" — honored nearly everywhere for short absences; long absences forfeit the spot.
- Companion joining you in line → one person holding for a group is accepted in casual lines, resented in scarce-resource ones (limited releases, last tickets) — read the stakes.
- Ticket number missed while distracted → go to the counter at the next lull and show the ticket; most systems re-slot you rather than restart you.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Two parallel structures formed (blob vs. line):** ask staff to designate, or default to arrival-order courtesy — voice it lightly: "I think they were first."
- **F2 You accidentally cut (didn't see the real end):** apologize and relocate immediately; intent doesn't matter, position does.
- **F3 Line isn't moving at all:** verify service is actually operating (register open? number display advancing?) before sinking more time — ask the person ahead what they've observed.

## Verification

You were served in arrival order, no conflict trailed your interactions, and the queue behind you inherited a clean, unambiguous structure.

## Variations

- `uk`: the line is sacred and self-enforcing; joining ambiguity is nearly always resolved by asking.
- `cn`/dense-crowd systems: physical proximity is tighter and gaps close fast; ticket machines dominate at banks/services precisely to defuse crowding.
- Merge points (traffic, boarding): alternate/zipper merging is the norm — one from each stream.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The single principle under everything: the queue is a fairness machine — keep your inputs honest, keep your reactions proportionate.
