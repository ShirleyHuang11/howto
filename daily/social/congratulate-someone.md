## Goal

Someone receives a timely, sincere congratulations that recognizes their achievement without stealing focus or adding awkward comparison.

## Preconditions

- You know what happened well enough to name it accurately.
- You know the relationship and the right level of formality.
- You can contact them through a medium they use.
- The news is public enough to mention, or you have permission to respond privately.

## Steps

1. **Act while it is fresh.** Send the message the same day if possible, or soon after you learn the news. → *Expect:* the congratulations feels connected to the moment.
2. **Choose the medium.** [BRANCH: close relationship | professional or public relationship] call, voice note, or personal text for closeness, or email, card, comment, or chat for formal contexts. → *Expect:* the medium matches the relationship.
3. **Name the achievement specifically.** Say the promotion, graduation, award, new baby, launch, move, recovery, or milestone. → *Expect:* they can tell you noticed the real event.
4. **Add one sincere detail.** Mention effort, persistence, taste, kindness, courage, skill, or the impact you saw. → *Expect:* the message sounds personal rather than copied.
5. **Keep comparisons out.** Do not mention who failed, who is jealous, how long it took, or how you would have done it. → *Expect:* the focus stays on their good news.
6. **Match the energy.** [BRANCH: big life event | modest win] use warmth and celebration, or a concise "well done" with a specific note. → *Expect:* the tone feels supportive, not inflated or flat.
7. **Offer a next step only if real.** Suggest coffee, a toast, help celebrating, or a small gift only if you intend to follow through. → *Expect:* any offer creates no false obligation.
8. **Send and let them receive it.** Avoid adding corrections, advice, or your own news in the same message. → *Expect:* the final message is clean and centered on them.

## Decision points

- The news is sensitive, such as pregnancy, job change, or recovery → congratulate privately unless they made it public.
- You feel envy or mixed feelings → keep the message shorter and factual rather than pretending excess enthusiasm.
- You are a manager or colleague → be specific about work impact and avoid personal overreach.
- You do not know details → say "I heard the good news" and avoid guessing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Back-handed compliment:** detect phrases like "finally" or "for once" → remove them and make the praise direct.
- **F2 Spotlight shift:** detect your own story taking over → cut it or save it for another conversation.
- **F3 Wrong medium:** detect a public comment on private news → delete if possible and send a private apology.
- **F4 Generic message:** detect it could fit anyone → add one accurate detail about the person or achievement.

## Verification

The sent message names the achievement, includes a sincere specific note, matches the relationship, and contains no comparison, advice, or privacy breach.

## Variations

- `card`: use two or three sentences and sign clearly.
- `work-chat`: keep it brief, specific, and appropriate for the channel.
- `social-media`: comment only on information already shared by the person.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not reveal private news, medical details, pregnancy, job changes, or family updates before the person has shared them. Avoid jokes that depend on embarrassment.
