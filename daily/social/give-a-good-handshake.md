## Goal

A handshake feels respectful, brief, and confident without crushing, lingering, or ignoring cultural context.

## Preconditions

- The other person appears open to a handshake.
- Your hand is clean and dry enough.
- You can switch to a nod or verbal greeting if a handshake is not welcome.

## Steps

1. **Read the cue.** Notice whether the other person extends a hand or leaves space. → *Expect:* you know whether to offer or skip contact.
2. **Offer at chest height.** Extend your right hand with thumb up and palm angled sideways. → *Expect:* the gesture is easy to meet.
3. **Connect web-to-web.** Slide hands until the web between thumb and index finger meets theirs. → *Expect:* palms align instead of catching fingers only.
4. **Grip firmly, not hard.** Close fingers with moderate pressure. → *Expect:* the hand feels held, not squeezed.
5. **Use two pumps.** Move hands once or twice from the elbow, then stop. → *Expect:* the shake is brief and controlled.
6. **Make natural eye contact.** Look at the person while greeting them by name if known. → *Expect:* attention is on the person, not the hands.
7. **Release cleanly.** Let go as the pumps end and return your hand to your side. → *Expect:* neither person has to pull away.
8. **Adapt respectfully.** [BRANCH: handshake accepted | handshake declined] continue normally or switch to a nod and greeting. → *Expect:* the interaction stays comfortable.

## Decision points

- Other person hesitates → do not chase their hand; use a nod.
- Cultural or religious context may limit touch → let the other person lead.
- Your hands are wet or dirty → say hello without contact.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Finger grab:** detect only fingers connected, recover by keeping it brief and aligning earlier next time.
- **F2 Crushing grip:** detect tension or withdrawal, recover by loosening immediately.
- **F3 Limp grip:** detect no palm contact, recover in future by connecting web-to-web before closing fingers.
- **F4 Lingering hold:** detect conversation continuing while hands remain clasped, recover by releasing after two pumps.

## Verification

The handshake includes web-to-web contact, moderate pressure, one or two pumps, eye contact, and a clean release.

## Variations

- Business setting: stand if practical before offering a handshake.
- Casual setting: a nod, wave, or fist bump may fit better.
- Illness concern: decline politely with a smile and verbal greeting.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Respect personal boundaries, health concerns, and cultural norms; a declined handshake is not a problem to solve.
