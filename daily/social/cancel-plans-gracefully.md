## Goal

Plans are canceled early, clearly, and kindly, with the other person left informed instead of stranded or guessing.

## Preconditions

- You know which plan you cannot attend and whether a cost, ticket, ride, or reservation is involved.
- You have a way to contact the person or host directly.
- You can offer a new time if you genuinely want to reschedule.
- You can keep the explanation brief and truthful.

## Steps

1. **Decide promptly.** As soon as you know you cannot go, stop hoping the conflict will fix itself. → *Expect:* the other person gets the maximum possible notice.
2. **Choose the direct channel.** [BRANCH: close or time-sensitive plan | casual low-stakes plan] call or text directly, or send a short message in the original thread. → *Expect:* the message reaches the person who needs to adjust.
3. **State the cancellation first.** Say you cannot make it, naming the plan and date. → *Expect:* the main fact is clear in the first sentence.
4. **Give a brief honest reason.** Use one sentence, such as illness, work conflict, family issue, low energy, or a scheduling mistake. → *Expect:* the reason is understandable without inviting debate.
5. **Apologize once and take responsibility.** Say "I'm sorry for the late change" or "I'm sorry to miss it" without spiraling. → *Expect:* regret is expressed without making them manage your guilt.
6. **Offer repair if needed.** [BRANCH: you want to reschedule | you do not] suggest two replacement windows, or simply wish them a good time. → *Expect:* the next step is easy to accept or decline.
7. **Handle costs and logistics.** Transfer tickets, pay your share, release a reservation seat, or help find a replacement if appropriate. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* canceling a paid booking may trigger fees, so check the policy before pressing cancel. → *Expect:* your cancellation does not create avoidable work or cost for others.
8. **Stop over-explaining.** After they respond, acknowledge once and move on. → *Expect:* the interaction ends cleanly and respectfully.

## Decision points

- You are canceling within a few hours → use the fastest direct channel and mention logistics first.
- The host paid for you → offer repayment or help transferring the spot.
- You often cancel on this person → name the pattern and propose a more reliable plan next time.
- You are canceling because of safety or illness → prioritize clarity over social smoothing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Message is too vague:** detect "something came up" with no plan named → add the specific event and whether you can reschedule.
- **F2 Over-apology shifts burden:** detect multiple apology texts after they accepted → stop and let the repair action speak.
- **F3 Reschedule offer is fake:** detect you do not actually want a new date → do not offer one, and close warmly.
- **F4 Money is unresolved:** detect tickets, deposits, groceries, or rides involved → offer a concrete repayment or transfer plan.

## Verification

The other person has received a clear cancellation before the event time, and any reservation, money, ticket, or reschedule issue has an explicit next step.

## Variations

- `group-chat`: notify the organizer directly, then post a brief note to the group if needed.
- `work-event`: keep the reason professional and mention coverage or handoff.
- `illness`: say you are avoiding exposing others and skip unnecessary detail.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. You do not owe medical, family, or personal details beyond what is needed. Be direct when safety is involved, and do not use a false emergency as a social shortcut.
