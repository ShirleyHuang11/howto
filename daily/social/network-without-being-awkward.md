## Goal

Build a professional connection through curiosity, useful exchange, graceful exits, and timely follow-up.

## Preconditions

- A setting where professional conversation is welcome.
- A short way to describe what you do or what you are exploring.
- A way to follow up, such as email, LinkedIn, or a shared contact.

## Steps

1. **Enter with curiosity, not a pitch.** Prepare two questions about their work, path, or current problem. → *Expect:* the conversation begins with interest.
2. **Introduce yourself briefly.** Say your name, context, and one sentence about your work or goal. → *Expect:* they can place you without hearing your resume.
3. **Ask a real question.** [BRANCH: event | one-on-one] Use the setting as a bridge, then ask about their experience or priorities. → *Expect:* they talk about something specific.
4. **Listen for ways to be useful.** Offer a link, introduction, note, or relevant experience only if it genuinely fits. → *Expect:* you give before asking.
5. **Make one clear ask if appropriate.** Ask for advice, a resource, or permission to follow up, not a job on the spot. → *Expect:* the ask is easy to answer.
6. **Exit gracefully.** Say, "I enjoyed talking with you. I am going to circulate, but may I follow up?" → *Expect:* the ending is polite, not abrupt.
7. **Record the context.** Note where you met, what mattered to them, and any promised follow-up. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not share their contact information, job search, or private comments without permission. → *Expect:* your follow-up will be specific.
8. **Follow up within a few days.** Send a short note with the context, thanks, and any promised item. → *Expect:* the connection moves from memory to relationship.

## Decision points

- They seem busy or closed off → shorten the exchange and exit kindly.
- You need a favor → earn clarity first, then make a small specific ask.
- You promised help → follow through before asking for anything.
- The event has many people → prioritize a few good conversations over collecting names.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Resume dump:** detect talking through your history, recover by asking about them.
- **F2 Transactional ask:** detect leading with what you need, recover by showing context and curiosity first.
- **F3 Awkward lingering:** detect the conversation ending twice, recover with a graceful exit.
- **F4 Follow-up delay:** detect waiting a week, recover by sending a concise note now with honest context.

## Verification

You had a focused conversation, offered or respected value before asking, exited cleanly, and sent a specific follow-up within a few days.

## Variations

- Conference: mention the session or speaker that created the connection.
- Online community: contribute useful comments before direct messages.
- Informational interview: ask for perspective and next resources, not a hidden referral.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Respect confidential work details, job searches, and personal contact information. Do not add people to mailing lists or introductions without consent.
