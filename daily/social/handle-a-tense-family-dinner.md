## Goal

Get through a tense family dinner with your dignity intact, fewer escalations, and a clear exit plan if the evening stops being tolerable.

## Preconditions

- A family meal or gathering where conflict is possible but physical safety is not in immediate danger.
- Transportation or a departure option you control, especially if past gatherings have gone badly.
- One or two topic boundaries decided before you arrive.

## Steps

1. **Set your boundary in advance.** Choose a sentence you can repeat: "I'm not discussing my dating life tonight" or "I'm here for dinner, not a political debate." → *Expect:* you have words ready before adrenaline arrives.
2. **Enter with one neutral contribution.** Bring a side dish, help set the table, ask about travel, or compliment the food. → *Expect:* the first impression is cooperative, not defensive.
3. **Use safe topics early.** Ask about recipes, kids, pets, local events, sports, movies, house projects, or old family stories that do not start fights. → *Expect:* conversation has somewhere to go besides the usual conflict lane.
4. **Redirect at the first hook.** When someone pokes the boundary, answer once and move: "I'm not getting into that tonight. How was your trip?" [BRANCH: direct redirect | softer redirect] use direct for repeat offenders; use softer when someone simply forgot. → *Expect:* the topic changes or the pattern becomes visible.
5. **Take the bathroom break.** If your body heats up, excuse yourself, breathe, text a grounding person, or wash your hands slowly. → *Expect:* you return with enough distance to choose your next sentence.
6. **Do not take the bait.** Refuse insults disguised as questions, old grievances, or "everyone knows" traps. Short lines work: "I see it differently," "I'm not debating this here," "Pass the potatoes?" → *Expect:* you give less fuel than the provocation requests.
7. **Leave before the exit becomes dramatic.** If the boundary keeps being crossed, thank the host and go. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* shouted accusations at a family table can echo for years, so leave before you say the sentence you came to avoid. → *Expect:* you are out of the room before the worst version of the fight.
8. **Debrief later, not in the driveway group text.** Write down what happened, what worked, and what boundary you need next time. → *Expect:* the next plan is based on evidence, not only exhaustion.

## Decision points

- If someone becomes threatening or blocks you from leaving → prioritize safety and call for help.
- If a relative is trying to repair sincerely → consider a private conversation after dinner, not across the table.
- If children are present → lower your volume and model exit over escalation.
- If alcohol is driving the conflict → stop arguing with the alcohol and leave or change rooms.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Boundary is ignored:** detect the same topic returning after one clear refusal → repeat once, then leave the conversation or the room.
- **F2 You snap back:** detect your voice rising or a personal attack leaving your mouth → stop, say "I need a minute," and take the break.
- **F3 Others demand you explain:** detect a chorus of questions → use the broken-record line and do not defend the boundary like a thesis.
- **F4 Host feels embarrassed:** detect apology or tension after you leave → send a short thank-you later and separate appreciation for hosting from refusal to be baited.

## Verification

You stated or upheld at least one topic boundary, used a redirect or break before escalating, and left or stayed by choice rather than pressure.

## Variations

- Holiday dinner: arrange lodging or transport that does not depend on the most volatile person.
- In-law dinner: agree with your partner beforehand on who handles which family member.
- Restaurant dinner: stepping outside for air may be easier than a bathroom break.

## Safety & privacy

Medium social risk because family conflict can affect housing, money, custody, and long-term relationships. Do not disclose private information to win a table argument, and leave immediately if safety becomes the issue.
