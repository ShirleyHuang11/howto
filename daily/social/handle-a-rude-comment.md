## Goal

Respond to a rude comment without escalating unnecessarily or surrendering your dignity.

## Preconditions

- You heard or read the comment clearly enough to decide whether to respond.
- You are physically safe and able to leave if needed.
- The setting allows at least one of ignoring, naming, questioning, or exiting.

## Steps

1. **Pause first.** Take one breath before answering, even if you know what you want to say. → *Expect:* the first response is chosen, not reflexive.
2. **Check the stakes.** Decide whether the comment affects safety, work, reputation, or only annoyance. → *Expect:* your response matches the setting.
3. **Choose an option.** [BRANCH: ignore | name it | question it] pick the lightest response that protects you. → *Expect:* you have a clear lane before speaking.
4. **Ignore cleanly if it is bait.** Let the comment pass and redirect to the task or another person. → *Expect:* the rude person gets no reward from your reaction.
5. **Name it if a boundary is needed.** Say "That was rude" or "Do not speak to me that way" in a calm tone. → *Expect:* the behavior is identified without a speech.
6. **Question it if clarity helps.** Ask "What do you mean by that?" or "Can you say that another way?" → *Expect:* they must either soften, explain, or own the comment.
7. **Return to the point.** Move back to the task, conversation, or next action. → *Expect:* you do not get trapped in a side argument.
8. **Exit if it continues.** End the conversation, leave the room, mute the thread, or involve a moderator or manager. → *Expect:* contact with the rude behavior decreases.

## Decision points

- The comment includes a threat → leave and get help rather than debating.
- The comment happens at work → document the wording, time, witnesses, and impact.
- The person apologizes → accept briefly if you want, then move forward.
- The person claims it was a joke → decide whether the joke excuse changes the boundary; often it does not.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overreaction spiral:** detect raised voices and repeated insults, recover by pausing and exiting.
- **F2 Underresponse regret:** detect that you froze and feel bothered later, recover by following up with a short boundary message.
- **F3 Public pile-on:** detect others joining the rudeness, recover by addressing the group once or leaving.
- **F4 Sarcasm trap:** detect yourself crafting a cutting comeback, recover by using a plain boundary instead.

## Verification

After the exchange, you have either ignored, named, questioned, or exited in a way that reduces harm and preserves your boundary.

## Variations

- Online comment: mute, block, report, or ask one clarifying question; avoid long threads.
- Family setting: use a shorter boundary and repeat it rather than litigating history.
- Customer service setting: stay factual and escalate to policy or a supervisor if needed.

## Safety & privacy

Low to medium risk depending on threat level. Do not trade insults in recorded work channels, and leave immediately if the situation becomes unsafe.
