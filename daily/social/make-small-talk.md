## Goal

A low-stakes conversation with a stranger or acquaintance (waiting rooms, parties, coffee machines) runs comfortably for its natural length and ends warmly.

## Preconditions

- A context that permits talk: shared waiting, an event, a queue, a break room — and a counterpart not signaling "leave me be" (headphones in, book up, closed posture = the answer is no).

## Steps

1. **Open with the shared situation, not a personal probe.** The context is the license: "Quite a queue, huh?" / "How do you know the host?" / "Is the coffee here any good?" → *Expect:* a response whose *length* tells you whether they want conversation (one word = probably not; a sentence + return question = yes).
2. **Follow their thread, not your script.** Whatever their answer contains is the material: they mention a trip → ask about the trip. The skill is listening for the offered hook, not preparing your next line. → *Expect:* the conversation moves to a topic *they* introduced.
3. **Balance the exchange with open questions + small self-disclosures.** Questions that can't be answered "yes/no" ("What's that like?") plus brief matching shares of your own — interviews feel like interrogations without the give-back. → *Expect:* roughly alternating turns; neither monologue nor quiz.
4. **Stay in the shallow-safe zone unless they go deeper first.** Weather, the event, food, local area, weekend plans, light work talk. Follow *their* depth changes; don't initiate politics, money, health, or relationship probes with strangers. → *Expect:* no visible flinch moments; comfort maintained.
5. **Read the ending and land it.** Natural endpoints: the queue moves, drinks refilled, a lull after a completed topic. Close warmly and cleanly: "Well, good luck with the marathon — nice talking to you." → *Expect:* a mutual, unhurried close; nobody trapped.

## Decision points

- They give short answers twice in a row → they're done (or shy) — one more gentle offer, then release: silence beside someone is a fine outcome.
- You blank on what to say → the environment always has material: the venue, the food, the reason you're both here; or return to a hook they dropped earlier ("you mentioned...").
- Recurring counterpart (same barista, same dog-walker) → bank a detail (`daily/social/greet-a-neighbor` step 5) and compound the relationship a little each time.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dead air:** normal, not fatal — the environment-scan question restarts it, or the warm close ends it; both beat visible panic.
- **F2 Stepped on a sore topic:** acknowledge lightly and redirect: "Ah, sorry — so anyway, how's the new place?" Recovery speed matters more than the misstep.
- **F3 Trapped by a monologuer:** bridge-and-exit is legitimate: "That's wild — hey, I need to grab a word with someone before they leave. Good to meet you!"
- **F4 Forgot their name mid-conversation:** survive without it, then the `greet-a-neighbor` F1 confession at the next meeting.

## Verification

The exchange ran at mutual comfort (their engagement told you), stayed at consented depth, and ended with a clean warm close — plus one banked detail if they're a repeat character in your life.

## Variations

- High-context cultures / `jp`: longer comfortable silences, more formulaic openers (weather, season) — the formula *is* the courtesy.
- Professional events: "What brings you here?" is the universal opener; the exit line adds a card/contact exchange when warranted.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Match self-disclosure to acquaintance level — your address, workplace details, and schedule patterns are not small talk with true strangers.
