## Goal

Support a friend through ordinary stress or difficulty by showing up, listening well, offering practical help, and following through.

## Preconditions

- A friend has shared a need, stress, loss, decision, or hard season.
- You have enough capacity to offer support honestly.
- The situation is not an immediate emergency beyond friendship support.

## Steps

1. **Show up promptly.** Send a message, call, visit, or make plans based on what fits the relationship. → *Expect:* they know you noticed and care.
2. **Ask what kind of support they want.** [BRANCH: listening | advice | practical help | distraction] Let them choose if they can. → *Expect:* you are not guessing loudly.
3. **Listen more than you advise.** Reflect what you heard and ask one gentle question. → *Expect:* they feel accompanied, not managed.
4. **Validate without taking over.** Say the situation sounds hard or unfair without making yourself the main character. → *Expect:* their experience stays central.
5. **Offer practical help specifically.** Suggest food, rides, childcare, proofreading, errands, or sitting with them. → *Expect:* help is easy to accept or decline.
6. **Do what you said.** Put reminders on your calendar for promised calls, meals, or check-ins. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not share their private situation with others unless they ask or safety requires it. → *Expect:* support becomes reliable action.
7. **Remember the follow-up.** Check in after the appointment, anniversary, interview, funeral, or hard date. → *Expect:* they are not dropped after the first conversation.
8. **Stay consistent within your limits.** Be honest about what you can do and keep showing up in small ways. → *Expect:* care is sustainable.

## Decision points

- They ask for advice → ask permission before giving it and keep it practical.
- They are in danger or mention self-harm → involve urgent support, crisis resources, or emergency services.
- You are overwhelmed → offer a smaller reliable action instead of disappearing.
- They do not want to talk → respect that and offer a low-pressure check-in later.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Advice reflex:** detect solving before listening, recover by saying, "Do you want ideas or company?"
- **F2 Vanishing after first text:** detect no follow-up, recover by scheduling one concrete check-in.
- **F3 Vague help:** detect "let me know" with no offer, recover by naming two specific options.
- **F4 Privacy leak:** detect wanting to tell others, recover by asking permission or keeping it private.

## Verification

Your friend received timely contact, was listened to before advice, got a specific offer of help, and had at least one remembered follow-up.

## Variations

- Long-distance friendship: support can be calls, deliveries, shared shows, reminders, or admin help.
- Grief: keep showing up after the public attention fades.
- Busy season: small reliable check-ins beat dramatic but inconsistent gestures.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for ordinary support, higher if danger, abuse, or self-harm appears. Keep confidences, but do not keep secrets that would leave someone in immediate danger.
