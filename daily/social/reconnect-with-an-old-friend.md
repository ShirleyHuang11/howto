## Goal

Restart contact with an old friend using a low-pressure message that names the gap and suggests a concrete next step.

## Preconditions

- You have a current way to contact the person.
- The past relationship was safe and welcome enough to reopen.
- You are prepared for no reply or a slower reply than you hope.

## Steps

1. **Choose the channel.** Use the place where you last had normal contact, unless it now feels too public. → *Expect:* the message will reach them without making a scene.
2. **Open lightly.** Start with their name and a simple memory or reason you thought of them. → *Expect:* the message feels personal, not copied.
3. **Name the gap without drama.** Say it has been a long time or you fell out of touch. → *Expect:* the absence is acknowledged without demanding forgiveness.
4. **Ask a low-pressure question.** Use "How have you been?" or a specific question about something they cared about. → *Expect:* they can answer easily if they want to.
5. **Suggest something concrete.** Offer coffee, a call, a walk, or a short catch-up window. [BRANCH: local | remote] → *Expect:* they know what reconnecting could look like.
6. **Give an easy out.** Add that there is no pressure if now is not a good time. → *Expect:* the message does not require emotional labor to refuse.
7. **Send once.** Do not stack follow-ups immediately. → *Expect:* they receive one clean invitation.
8. **Respond at their pace.** If they reply warmly, match their energy and schedule the concrete next step. → *Expect:* the reconnection moves from vague goodwill to a real plan.
9. **Accept silence.** If there is no reply, leave the door open without monitoring them. → *Expect:* your dignity and their autonomy stay intact.

## Decision points

- There was a conflict → include a short apology or accountability before asking to catch up.
- The person is grieving or under stress → make the offer more supportive and less social.
- You only want information or networking help → be honest about the ask instead of disguising it as friendship.
- They reply politely but vaguely → offer one specific time, then let it rest.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too much nostalgia:** detect a message that makes them responsible for your feelings, recover by trimming it to one memory and one invitation.
- **F2 No concrete next step:** detect "we should catch up sometime," recover by naming a call or coffee window.
- **F3 Pressure wording:** detect "you probably forgot me" or "you owe me," recover by removing guilt.
- **F4 Unwanted reopening:** detect a cold or boundary-setting reply, recover by thanking them and not pushing.

## Verification

You sent one respectful message that acknowledges the gap, asks a low-pressure question, and offers a concrete next step.

## Variations

- Text message: shorter is better, with one memory and one ask.
- Email: add a little context if the address may surprise them.
- Social media: avoid public comments for emotionally loaded reconnections.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, unless the relationship involved harm or harassment. Do not bypass blocks, use alternate accounts, or pressure someone who does not reply.
