## Goal

A genuine compliment delivered so it lands: specific, sincere, well-timed, and free of hooks, leaving the receiver a little taller and the relationship a little warmer.

## Preconditions

- Something you genuinely noticed and admire. Manufactured flattery is detectable at any dose and poisons future real ones; the recipe starts with authentic material.
- A moment where receiving is easy: not mid-crisis, not when it forces them to perform gratitude in front of an audience they manage.

## Steps

1. **Catch the specific, not the general.** "Great job" evaporates; "the way you handled that interruption without losing the thread was impressive" sticks, because specificity is the proof you actually paid attention. → *Expect:* a compliment containing at least one concrete detail only a genuine observer would have.
2. **Compliment choices and effort over lottery tickets.** What someone did, made, or chose ("that color combination", "the patience you showed", "this sauce") lands better than what they were born with; effort-praise also survives repetition. → *Expect:* the content pointing at something they can feel ownership of.
3. **Deliver it clean, with a period at the end.** No hook ("...so could you help me move Saturday?"), no comparison that demotes someone else ("unlike Tom's version"), no softener that retracts it ("not bad for a beginner"). Say it, stop, let it exist. → *Expect:* a complete sentence of pure credit, followed by silence or a normal topic, not a request.
4. **Match the register to the relationship and setting.** Colleagues: work-adjacent and public-safe ("your write-up made the decision easy"). Strangers: light and exit-friendly ("great dog, great coat"). Appearance compliments: safest about choices (glasses, jacket, haircut `daily/errands/get-a-haircut` results included) and calibrated to context; when in doubt, compliment the work, not the body. → *Expect:* nothing that would read differently on a loudspeaker.
5. **Consider the upgrade: compliment them to their boss, their partner, or behind their back.** Relayed praise ("your colleague told me you saved that project") is the highest-value form, and back-channel kindness has a way of arriving. → *Expect:* where stakes or shyness make direct praise awkward, the indirect route delivering it better.
6. **Receive their response gracefully.** They may deflect ("oh, it was nothing"); reaffirm once, lightly ("well, it worked on me"), and move on. Do not force them to accept it ceremonially. → *Expect:* the moment closing warm and unlabored.

## Decision points

- Frequency and inflation: a compliment habit is wonderful; a compliment reflex is wallpaper. Scarcity is not required, sincerity is; say the true ones, skip the filler ones.
- Power dynamics: praising upward, keep it about the work to avoid reading as flattery; praising downward, do it publicly where it pays reputational dividends and privately where the person is shy.
- Cross-cultural notes: some cultures deflect compliments as ritual modesty (accepting too readily can feel boastful there); insisting past the ritual once is warm, past twice is pressure.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 It came out backhanded ("you look great today!"):** repair instantly with a laugh at yourself: "That came out wrong — you look great, full stop."
- **F2 The hook slipped in and they visibly recalculated your sincerity:** separate the two explicitly: "The ask is unrelated — the compliment stands either way," and space future asks away from future praise.
- **F3 Appearance compliment landed uncomfortably:** read it, retreat to neutral warmth, and recalibrate that register for that person permanently; no explanation tour needed.
- **F4 You never say the compliments you think:** the pattern to break is silent admiration; the training wheel is the relayed form (step 5) or written form (`communication/write-a-thank-you-note` is a compliment with an envelope), both lower-exposure than face-to-face.

## Verification

The compliment was specific, hook-free, register-matched, and delivered with a period; the receiver's day visibly ticked upward; and nothing about it would embarrass either of you replayed aloud.

## Variations

- Written praise (a two-line email to someone's manager, a review for a small business, a comment on a creator's work) compounds: it is re-readable on their bad days.
- Kids: praise the process ("you kept trying different ways") over the label ("you're so smart"); the process version builds the thing you are praising.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The two live wires are hooks and bodies: keep asks out of compliments, and keep appearance praise to choices and contexts that respect the receiver. Everything else is free warmth left on the table by most people, most days.
