## Goal

Meet someone new in a way that feels respectful, present, and easy to continue.

## Preconditions

- A planned or likely first meeting.
- Basic awareness of the setting and expected formality.
- Enough time to arrive without rushing.

## Steps

1. **Arrive on time or slightly early.** Build in travel margin so your first signal is reliability. → *Expect:* you enter calm rather than apologizing immediately.
2. **Reset your body language.** Stand or sit open, shoulders relaxed, phone away, face oriented toward them. → *Expect:* your posture says available, not guarded.
3. **Greet clearly.** Use a simple hello, your name, and a handshake only if it fits the setting. → *Expect:* both names are exchanged without awkward guessing.
4. **Use their name once.** Say it naturally, then stop before it feels rehearsed. → *Expect:* they know you heard it.
5. **Ask an easy opening question.** [BRANCH: professional | social] Ask about their role, connection to the event, or how they know the host. → *Expect:* they have a low-effort way to begin.
6. **Listen for one detail to follow.** Ask a second question from what they actually said, not from your next talking point. → *Expect:* the conversation becomes specific.
7. **Share briefly in return.** Offer one relevant detail about yourself. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* avoid jokes, gossip, or disclosures that cannot be taken back in a first meeting. → *Expect:* the exchange feels balanced.
8. **Close or continue cleanly.** If parting, say it was good to meet them and name the follow-up if there is one. → *Expect:* the ending is clear and warm.

## Decision points

- You forget their name → ask early with a quick apology rather than pretending.
- They seem quiet → slow down, ask easier questions, and tolerate pauses.
- The setting is formal → use titles or surnames until invited otherwise.
- The meeting has a purpose → move to that purpose after the human opening.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Phone check:** detect glancing down mid-conversation, recover by putting the phone away and re-engaging.
- **F2 Name misuse:** detect uncertainty, recover by asking them to repeat it and saying it back once.
- **F3 Over-talking:** detect long monologues, recover by asking a question and actually pausing.
- **F4 Forced charm:** detect performing instead of listening, recover by getting curious about one real detail.

## Verification

You arrived on time, exchanged names, used open body language, listened for a specific detail, and left a clear path for follow-up if needed.

## Variations

- Job interview: keep warmth, but let competence and preparation carry more weight.
- Neighbor or casual setting: shorter is fine; friendliness beats polish.
- Online first meeting: camera, name, listening, and follow-up still matter.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep early disclosures modest, avoid confidential gossip, and respect physical space and touch preferences.
