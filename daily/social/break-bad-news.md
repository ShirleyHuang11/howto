## Goal

Tell someone difficult news clearly, kindly, and privately while giving them room to react.

## Preconditions

- The news is accurate enough to share, or uncertainty is clearly labeled.
- You have a private setting and enough time not to rush away.
- Any immediate safety or logistics support is identified.

## Steps

1. **Prepare the core sentence.** Write one plain sentence that says what happened without euphemism. → *Expect:* you can say the news in under 20 seconds.
2. **Choose the setting.** Use a private room, quiet call, or direct message only when live conversation is not possible. → *Expect:* the person will not be exposed in front of an audience.
3. **Check availability.** Ask whether they can talk for a few minutes about something serious. → *Expect:* they either make space now or name a better near-term time.
4. **Give a brief warning.** Say that you have bad news and will be direct. → *Expect:* their attention shifts before the main sentence.
5. **Say the news plainly.** Deliver the prepared sentence without overexplaining first. → *Expect:* the key fact is unmistakable.
6. **Let it land.** Stop talking for several seconds, and tolerate silence, tears, anger, or confusion. → *Expect:* they have room to process before details arrive.
7. **Answer only what you know.** Share confirmed details and say "I do not know yet" when needed. → *Expect:* the conversation stays honest instead of padded with guesses.
8. **Offer support.** Ask what they need now, and offer one concrete help option if appropriate. → *Expect:* they know they are not being abandoned with the news.
9. **Close with next contact.** Agree on the next update, call, ride, meal, or person to notify. → *Expect:* there is a small next step after the first shock.

## Decision points

- The person may be unsafe alone → stay with them or contact a trusted support person or emergency service.
- The news concerns a death or serious injury → use voice or in person when possible, not a casual text.
- You are not authorized to share details → say what you can share and who can answer more.
- They ask the same question repeatedly → answer calmly and briefly each time.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rambling preface:** detect when you explain for a minute before the fact, recover by stopping and saying the core sentence.
- **F2 False reassurance:** detect phrases like "it will be fine" when you cannot know, recover with "I am here with you right now."
- **F3 Public exposure:** detect nearby listeners or visible notifications, recover by moving private before continuing.
- **F4 Defensive reaction:** detect yourself arguing with their feelings, recover by naming the impact and returning to support.

## Verification

The person has heard the main fact, has had time to react, and knows at least one concrete next step or support option.

## Variations

- Workplace news: include what changes now, what is still unknown, and where official information will appear.
- Medical family update: separate confirmed facts from clinician interpretation.
- Remote delivery: use a call or video when possible and confirm the person is not driving.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because privacy, mental state, and family or work consequences may be involved. Share only necessary facts, avoid group settings, and escalate if there is immediate danger.
