## Goal

Help another person feel heard without taking over the conversation.

## Preconditions

- A conversation partner who wants to talk.
- A setting where you can give attention without rushing.
- Willingness to hold advice until it is wanted.

## Steps

1. **Put attention where they can see it.** Face the person, put the phone away, and stop side tasks. → *Expect:* your body and eyes show that the conversation has priority.
2. **Invite the topic without steering it.** Ask "What happened?" or "What do you want me to understand?" → *Expect:* they choose the starting point.
3. **Let the first pass run.** Do not interrupt the first full explanation unless you cannot hear or understand. → *Expect:* they finish a thought without having to defend the floor.
4. **Reflect back the core.** Say the short version of what you heard, including the feeling if clear. → *Expect:* they can confirm, correct, or sharpen it.
5. **Ask questions before advice.** Use open questions such as "What part is bothering you most?" or "What have you tried?" → *Expect:* the next answer gives more detail instead of shutting down.
6. **Check before fixing.** [BRANCH: they want advice | they want listening] ask "Do you want ideas, or do you want me to just listen?" → *Expect:* the role is explicit.
7. **Use silence without panic.** Wait a few seconds after an emotional sentence instead of filling the gap. → *Expect:* they either continue or visibly settle.
8. **Name limits gently.** If time is short, say how much time you have and offer a later continuation. → *Expect:* the conversation has a clear boundary without abruptness.
9. **Close by summarizing support.** Mention one thing you understood and one next action only if they named it. → *Expect:* they leave knowing what you heard, not what you imposed.

## Decision points

- They ask directly for advice → give one or two concrete options, then return to listening.
- They repeat the same point several times → reflect the emotion and ask what would feel useful right now.
- They disclose danger to self or others → move from listening to getting immediate help.
- You feel defensive → pause and ask a clarifying question before explaining yourself.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Accidental fixing:** you hear yourself solving, recover by saying you jumped ahead and asking what they need.
- **F2 False reflection:** they say "that's not it," recover by asking them to restate the important part.
- **F3 Phone distraction:** they notice you checking a device, recover by apologizing and putting it out of reach.
- **F4 Advice flood:** they get quiet after suggestions, recover by stopping and asking what landed badly.

## Verification

The other person has spoken more than you, has corrected or confirmed your summary, and knows whether you are listening or advising.

## Variations

- Text chat: reflect in shorter messages and avoid sending a wall of advice.
- Workplace: separate listening from decisions by saying when you are gathering context.
- Conflict: reflect their point before making your own.

## Safety & privacy

Respect private details and do not retell the story without permission. If someone is in immediate danger, privacy yields to getting help.
