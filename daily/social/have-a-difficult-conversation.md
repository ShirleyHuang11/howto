## Goal

A hard topic is raised with care, heard with less defensiveness, and closed with a clear outcome or next step.

## Preconditions

- You can state the point in one or two sentences.
- The other person is not trapped, impaired, working under immediate pressure, or surrounded by an audience.
- You have considered whether the conversation needs privacy, documentation, or a third party.

## Steps

1. **Prepare the point.** Write the main concern, one concrete example, and what you hope changes. → *Expect:* you can explain the conversation without a speech.
2. **Pick the time and place.** Choose privacy, enough time, and a setting where both people can leave or pause. → *Expect:* the context supports attention rather than performance.
3. **Open with care and clarity.** Say why the relationship or work matters, then name the topic directly. → *Expect:* the other person knows this is serious but not an ambush.
4. **Describe facts before interpretations.** Use dates, behaviors, or words you observed, then explain the impact. → *Expect:* the discussion starts from shared reality.
5. **Listen for their account.** Ask what you are missing, let them finish, and reflect the part you understand. → *Expect:* they have a real chance to respond.
6. **Make the request specific.** [BRANCH: ask for change | share a boundary] Ask for a behavior if collaboration is possible; state a boundary if the behavior must stop. → *Expect:* the desired outcome is not vague.
7. **Agree an outcome.** Decide who will do what by when, or agree that no agreement was reached and what happens next. → *Expect:* the close has a recordable result.
8. **Avoid public damage.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* humiliation, threats, and private disclosures can permanently change trust, so do not use an audience as leverage. → *Expect:* the conversation stays as private as the situation allows.
9. **Follow up briefly.** Send a short note if stakes are practical, or check in later if stakes are relational. → *Expect:* the outcome survives beyond the moment.

## Decision points

- If the other person becomes overwhelmed, pause and schedule a return.
- If legal, HR, safety, or medical stakes are present, get appropriate advice before improvising.
- If you only want to vent, choose a friend or journal first, not the person affected.
- If the relationship is unsafe, do not hold a private confrontation.
- If the issue is minor but recurring, frame it as a pattern early.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Conversation starts too vague:** detect it when they ask "what is this about" repeatedly → state the point in one sentence.
- **F2 Defensive loop:** detect it when both people repeat positions → reflect their concern before restating your request.
- **F3 Bad timing:** detect it when interruptions or fatigue dominate → reschedule with a specific time.
- **F4 Outcome evaporates:** detect it when no one can name the next step → write the action down before leaving.

## Verification

At the end, both people know the topic, the impact, and the agreed outcome or next step.

## Variations

- Workplace conversation: keep examples behavioral and document decisions afterward.
- Family conversation: choose a neutral time, not a holiday table.
- Phone conversation: slow down and summarize more because body language is missing.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk. Prioritize physical safety, consent to continue, and privacy; do not disclose sensitive information to bystanders to force a response.
