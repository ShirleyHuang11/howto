## Goal

Arrange a playdate with clear logistics, compatible expectations, allergy awareness, supervision, and a clean pickup plan.

## Preconditions

- The children know each other or have a reasonable connection through school, family, neighbors, or activities.
- A parent or guardian for each child can communicate directly.
- You know the child's schedule, temperament, allergies, medical needs, and comfort with separation.
- The host location is safe enough for the ages involved.

## Steps

1. **Choose a good match.** Consider age, interests, energy level, and whether the children usually play well together. → *Expect:* the invitation is likely to fit both children.
2. **Suggest a short window.** Offer a specific day, start time, end time, and location, especially for a first playdate. → *Expect:* the other caregiver can answer without doing all the planning.
3. **Confirm the supervising adult.** State who will be present and ask who is allowed to pick up the child. → *Expect:* both sides know which adult is responsible.
4. **Ask about allergies and needs.** Ask about food allergies, pets, medications, bathroom help, fears, and any rules around screens or swimming. → *Expect:* safety issues are known before the child arrives.
5. **Agree on food and activities.** [BRANCH: snack provided | no snack] keep snacks simple and avoid risky foods when allergies are uncertain. → *Expect:* nobody is surprised by food, screens, pets, pools, or outings.
6. **Prepare the space.** Put away fragile items, hazards, private documents, weapons, medications, and toys that the host child cannot share. → *Expect:* the play area supports supervision and fewer conflicts.
7. **Start with structure.** Offer one or two activity options, then let the children play while an adult stays within appropriate range. → *Expect:* the children settle into play without needing constant adult direction.
8. **Handle conflict early.** Step in for hitting, exclusion, unsafe climbing, or repeated distress, and redirect before it becomes the whole visit. → *Expect:* small problems are corrected without ending the playdate immediately.
9. **Do the pickup clearly.** Have shoes, belongings, and any artwork or toys ready, share a brief report, and end on time. → *Expect:* the child leaves with the approved adult and no missing essentials.

## Decision points

- If ages or separation comfort differ widely → choose a parent-stays playdate or public park meetup.
- If allergies, pets, pools, or weapons are involved → require explicit caregiver agreement before hosting.
- If the children are struggling after repeated tries → shorten future visits or choose a more structured activity.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Logistics vague:** no end time, address, or pickup adult is clear → send a simple confirmation text before the visit starts.
- **F2 Allergy surprise:** food or pet exposure becomes uncertain → pause snacks or pet contact until the caregiver confirms.
- **F3 Child wants to leave:** homesickness, fear, or conflict appears → comfort briefly, call the caregiver, and arrange early pickup if needed.
- **F4 Pickup delay:** the caregiver is late → keep supervising, contact them, and do not release the child to an unapproved adult.

## Verification

The playdate has a confirmed time, place, supervising adult, allergy or medical notes, activity expectations, and pickup plan.

## Variations

- `first-playdate`: keep it one to two hours and consider having both caregivers stay.
- `public-place`: agree on bathroom trips, meeting point, and what happens if children split interests.
- `older-kids`: include device rules, neighborhood boundaries, and whether they may leave the house.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but supervision, allergies, water, pets, weapons, and pickup authorization matter. Share only necessary health and contact details, and store other families' information respectfully.
