## Goal

A movie night where the film starts within twenty minutes of the stated time, everyone can see and hear, the snacks outlast act one, and the phone-glow problem never takes hold — hosting's easiest win, executed instead of improvised.

## Preconditions

- A screen and sound adequate for the room, tested *today* (the streaming login, the HDMI cable, the soundbar input — whatever chain your setup uses, run two minutes of it before anyone arrives; the pre-flight is the whole difference between hosts).
- Seating audit: a sightline and a soft seat for every invited human; floor cushions count, craned necks do not.
- The film question settled or deliberately structured (see step 2) — "what should we watch?" consumes forty-five minutes when left to the wild.

## Steps

1. **Invite with the two anchors: arrival time and lights-down time.** "Come at 7, movie at 7:45" licenses socializing *and* protects the film; guests self-pace around a known curtain. → *Expect:* an invitation naming both times and the film (or the selection ritual).
2. **Solve selection before the couch does.** Either the host picks (announced in the invite; hosts' privilege, zero debate), or bring a structured ritual: three pre-chosen options and a vote, or rotating dictatorship among the regulars. → *Expect:* the film locked by lights-down minus five, not litigated live across three streaming catalogs.
3. **Stage the room in the last half hour.** Seating arranged toward the screen, blankets deployed, main lights down-dimmable or a lamp plan, subtitles decision pre-made (increasingly the default; ask the room once), volume pre-set to a tested level. Popcorn timed to finish at lights-down (`daily/food/pop-popcorn-on-the-stove` batch one). → *Expect:* a room that converts to cinema in sixty seconds.
4. **Run the descent: snacks landed, drinks in reach, bathroom call, phones down, lights, play.** The pre-roll announcement is beloved when it is brief: "bathroom now or forever hold it; phones to the basket if you're playing hard mode; lights going down." → *Expect:* the film starting within the twenty-minute grace of the stated time, snacks within arm's reach of every seat.
5. **Host through the film with a light hand.** Volume nudges, a pause *only* for genuine emergencies or a democratic snack-refill intermission at the natural midpoint; the host absorbs interruptions (door, spill) so the room doesn't. Chatty guests get the time-honored escalation: laugh once, shush warmly twice. → *Expect:* an uninterrupted act three; spills handled with towels pre-staged (`daily/social/host-a-guest-at-home`'s calm).
6. **Land the ending properly.** Lights up gently *after* the credits' first beat, immediate hot-take round (the best five minutes of any movie night), and the host's closing offers: tea, the last popcorn, and taxis-checked for the far-travelers. → *Expect:* the discussion everyone actually came for, then a `daily/social/host-a-guest-at-home` step-6 graceful release.

## Decision points

- Genre stakes: horror nights need a consent check in the invite (and a lights-on exit ramp for the overwhelmed); kids' nights need the film vetted *by the host* against the youngest viewer, not the box's rating alone.
- Food beyond popcorn: finger food only in dim light (forks and laps end badly); pizza timed to arrive *before* lights-down or at the intermission, never mid-act.
- The pause debate: declare the house policy at pre-roll ("we pause for no one" vs "intermission at the hour"); either works, ambiguity doesn't.
- Weeknight cut: shorter film, earlier lights-down, and the hot-take round capped — the recipe scales down to two people and a Tuesday flawlessly.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tech failure at lights-down:** the pre-flight makes this rare; live recovery order is cable-reseat, input-cycle, restart-app, then the host's backup plan (a second service or the downloaded fallback) — announced with confidence while executing.
- **F2 The selection spiral broke out anyway:** invoke the ritual retroactively: three options, sixty-second vote, done; the host's job is ending the spiral, not winning it.
- **F3 A guest talks through everything:** the warm shush twice, then recruit them to a job (popcorn batch two) at the natural break; structure beats resentment.
- **F4 Half the room asleep by act three:** no failure at all if the blankets did it — pause quietly, offer beds or taxis, and finish the film for the survivors; the sleeper's verdict on the film is also data.

## Verification

The film started inside the grace window on a pre-flighted setup, everyone had seat, sightline, snacks, and subtitle consensus, no phone glowed past the first shush, and the hot-take round happened — the actual deliverable of the whole evening.

## Variations

- Outdoor/projector nights add weather calls, a white wall or sheet (`daily/play/build-a-blanket-fort` engineering transfers), and neighbor-volume diplomacy after ten.
- Series nights ("two episodes, strictly") live or die on the host enforcing the stated count against the auto-play button; announce the number, honor the number.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: dim-room trip hazards (route the cables, `daily/home/vacuum-a-room`'s cord discipline), candle bans near blankets, and one modern courtesy — the group's watch history and hot takes stay in the room, and nobody's sleeping face gets posted (`daily/play/take-a-good-group-photo`'s consent rule applies double at midnight).
