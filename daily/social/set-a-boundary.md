## Goal

State a limit clearly and kindly, then hold it without over-explaining or turning it into a debate.

## Preconditions

- You know the behavior or request you need to limit.
- The setting is safe enough for a direct conversation or message.
- You can choose a consequence you are willing to follow.

## Steps

1. **Name the limit to yourself first.** Write the exact behavior and what you will or will not do. → *Expect:* the boundary is concrete.
2. **Choose the right channel.** [BRANCH: low-stakes | emotionally loaded] Use text for simple logistics, in person or call for important relationships. → *Expect:* the channel fits the weight.
3. **Lead with the boundary.** Say "I cannot lend money" or "I need you to call before visiting." → *Expect:* the limit is visible in the first sentence.
4. **Be direct and kind.** Use calm language without insults, diagnoses, or courtroom evidence. → *Expect:* the person hears a boundary, not an attack.
5. **Give one short reason if useful.** Stop before defending your entire life. → *Expect:* the explanation supports the limit without replacing it.
6. **Name the consequence.** Say what you will do if the boundary is ignored, such as leave, end the call, or stop responding. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not threaten consequences you cannot or will not carry out. → *Expect:* the next step is predictable.
7. **Repeat once if challenged.** Use the same sentence with fewer words. → *Expect:* the discussion does not expand under pressure.
8. **Follow through calmly.** If the line is crossed, do the stated action. → *Expect:* the boundary becomes real.

## Decision points

- There is risk of retaliation or harm → prioritize safety planning and outside support over direct confrontation.
- The person needs clarification → explain the requested behavior, not your worthiness to ask.
- They apologize and adjust → acknowledge it and continue normally.
- They argue your feelings → return to the behavior and the limit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overexplaining:** detect long justifications, recover by returning to one sentence.
- **F2 Soft boundary:** detect "maybe" where you mean no, recover by restating the limit plainly.
- **F3 Consequence bluff:** detect a threat you will not keep, recover by choosing a smaller real action.
- **F4 Debate loop:** detect repeated objections, recover by ending the conversation or changing the subject.

## Verification

The other person has heard the specific limit, the requested behavior, and the consequence, and you acted consistently if the limit was tested.

## Variations

- Workplace boundary: connect it to role, time, and priorities.
- Family boundary: expect more history, but keep the current request simple.
- Text boundary: use short sentences and avoid arguing line by line.

## Safety & privacy

Medium social risk. Boundaries can trigger anger in controlling dynamics, so choose safety, witnesses, or professional support when needed. Do not share private reasons just to prove the boundary is valid.
