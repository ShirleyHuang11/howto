## Goal

A guest (friend for dinner, visiting relative, colleague dropping by) is received into your home and leaves having felt welcome — the hosting script executed without the host dissolving into the kitchen.

## Preconditions

- An agreed visit: time, rough duration, and headcount confirmed; dietary constraints asked *at inviting time* ("anything you don't eat?").
- The home at guest-ready baseline: the rooms they'll see tidied (`embodied/household/tidy-a-room`), bathroom checked (clean, stocked, `daily/home/clean-a-bathroom` recently), somewhere to sit that isn't laundry.

## Steps

1. **Prep the arrival surface an hour out.** Entry clear of shoe-mountains, coat hooks/hangers ready, food/drinks staged so the first ten minutes don't need the kitchen. → *Expect:* opening the door leads somewhere, and the first offer can happen within two minutes.
2. **Receive at the door.** Greet, take coats, state the shoe custom plainly where it applies ("we're a shoes-off house — slippers there if you like"). → *Expect:* guest unburdened and inside without navigating unspoken rules.
3. **Make the first offer immediately.** Drink options named concretely ("tea, coffee, juice, or a beer?") — open-ended "want anything?" gets polite refusals from guests who do. → *Expect:* guest seated with a drink; the visit's rhythm started.
4. **Give the micro-orientation.** Where the bathroom is, and (overnight guests) their room, towels, and the Wi-Fi (`digital/connect-to-wifi`'s guest-network option is made for this). → *Expect:* guest can meet their own needs without asking permission each time.
5. **Host the middle without disappearing.** Food/meal per the plan (`daily/social/order-at-a-restaurant`'s allergy diligence applies at home doubly); batch kitchen tasks so you're present, not performing a service; include the guest in low-stakes helping if they offer twice (people mean it the second time). → *Expect:* the visit is mostly conversation with logistics stitched in — not logistics with conversation gaps.
6. **Read the departure and make it graceful.** Guests signal (glances at time, "well…") — respond by *releasing*, not re-anchoring: "It was so good to have you." Walk them to the door, coats returned, leftovers offered for the road where it fits. → *Expect:* an unhurried exit they chose; nobody trapped by hospitality.

## Decision points

- Guest arrives early while you're mid-prep → radical transparency beats flustered perfection: "You're catching the backstage — sit, talk to me while I finish the salad."
- Something burns / the meal fails → the rescue is social, not culinary: name it lightly, order in or improvise, and the story becomes the evening's best material.
- Overnight guests → the orientation (step 4) expands: keys/coming-and-going rules, breakfast norms ("help yourself, coffee's there"), and hosts announcing their own schedule frees guests from guessing.
- Cultural gift customs → receive offered gifts warmly (opening now vs. later varies by culture — follow the guest's lead); a guest's dish contribution gets served, not shelved.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Conversation stalls into hosting anxiety:** the environment provides (`daily/social/make-small-talk` step 2 works at your own table); food, photos, the guest's current life — and comfortable silence at a table is not a failure.
- **F2 Dietary constraint discovered at serving time:** the ask-at-invite habit failed — triage sides and staples into a workable plate, apologize once without ceremony, log the constraint permanently.
- **F3 Guest overstays past your energy:** hosts are allowed honest closings: "I've got an early one tomorrow — let's do this again soon." Warm and final beats resentful and endless.
- **F4 Home-state embarrassment (mess, repairs):** guests calibrate to warmth, not to skirting boards — the tidy-what-they-see rule is the 20% that buys 80%; drop the pre-visit panic-clean spiral.

## Verification

Guest was received, offered, oriented, fed per constraints, and released gracefully; you were present for most of it; and the honest test (same as lending): they'd come again, and you'd have them.

## Variations

- `jp`: genkan shoe transition is structural, house slippers + separate toilet slippers; guests bring omiyage — reciprocity is part of the loop.
- Drop-in cultures (some `latam`, `mena`, rural traditions): the no-notice knock is normal and the recipe compresses to steps 2–3 executed warmly from a standing start — the staged pantry (biscuits, tea) is the standing preparation.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Allergies are the one hard line (ask, don't quiz). Your home reveals you — that's the point of hosting, and the tidy-what-they-see boundary is also a privacy boundary you're allowed to keep.
