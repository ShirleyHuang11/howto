## Goal

Respond to a crying baby with a calm checklist, safe soothing techniques, and clear escalation when crying may signal illness or danger.

## Preconditions

- The baby is in your care and you can place them on their back in a safe sleep space if you need a break.
- You know the baby's approximate age, feeding plan, diaper supplies, and any medical instructions.
- A thermometer, clean diaper, burp cloth, and feeding supplies are available if needed.
- Emergency services or a pediatric advice line can be contacted if danger signs appear.

## Steps

1. **Check your own state.** Take one slow breath, loosen your shoulders, and put the baby down safely for a minute if you feel close to losing control. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never shake a baby, because shaking can cause brain injury or death. → *Expect:* you can handle the next step without rough motion.
2. **Run the basic checklist.** Check hunger, diaper, tiredness, wind or gas, temperature, tight clothing, hair wrapped around toes or fingers, and signs of illness. → *Expect:* any obvious need is found or ruled out.
3. **Feed or burp if indicated.** Offer the normal feed if hungry, or hold upright and burp after feeding. → *Expect:* sucking, swallowing, burping, or relaxed pauses give you information.
4. **Change and adjust comfort.** Replace a wet or soiled diaper, adjust layers, and move away from bright light or loud noise. → *Expect:* the baby's body is clean, comfortable, and not overheated.
5. **Use the 5 S's safely.** [BRANCH: swaddle if age-appropriate | skip swaddle] use swaddling only before rolling, side or stomach position only while held and awake, shushing, swinging with gentle motion, and sucking with a pacifier if accepted. → *Expect:* the baby has rhythmic input without unsafe sleep positioning.
6. **Hold with steady support.** Support the head and neck, keep movements small, and try walking, rocking, or a carrier if used correctly. → *Expect:* crying may soften, pause, or change pattern.
7. **Try a reset.** Move to a dim room, step outside briefly if weather is safe, or give a warm bath if the baby enjoys baths and can be held securely. → *Expect:* a new environment interrupts the crying cycle without overstimulation.
8. **Take a safe break if needed.** Place the baby on their back in an empty crib or bassinet, leave the room for a few minutes, and call another trusted adult. → *Expect:* the baby remains safe while you regain control.
9. **Escalate when it does not fit normal crying.** Call a pediatrician, nurse line, or emergency service for fever in a young infant, trouble breathing, blue color, limpness, seizure, injury, repeated vomiting, dehydration signs, a bulging soft spot, a new weak cry, or crying that is extreme and cannot be settled. → *Expect:* medical help is involved when crying may be a symptom.

## Decision points

- If the baby is under 3 months with a fever of 100.4 F or 38 C or higher → seek urgent medical advice.
- If you might hurt the baby → put the baby in a safe crib and call someone immediately.
- If the baby has known medical instructions → follow those instructions before general soothing preferences.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Caregiver overwhelmed:** anger, panic, or rough impulses appear → put the baby down on their back in a safe sleep space and call for help.
- **F2 Unsafe sleep position:** baby falls asleep in arms, swing, car seat outside travel, or on a couch → move them to a firm, empty sleep surface on their back.
- **F3 Crying worsens suddenly:** high-pitched cry, injury signs, fever, breathing trouble, or limpness appears → seek urgent medical help.
- **F4 Nothing works:** checklist and soothing fail after a reasonable try → call a pediatric advice line and arrange relief for the caregiver.

## Verification

The baby's immediate needs have been checked, soothing was attempted safely, and either the baby is settling or medical or caregiver support has been contacted.

## Variations

- `newborn`: feed, burp, diaper, safe swaddle, and caregiver breaks are the main tools.
- `reflux`: upright holding after feeds may help, but medication or feeding changes need pediatric guidance.
- `colic`: repeated evening crying can be common, but danger signs and caregiver overload still require help.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because rough handling can permanently injure a baby. Never shake a baby, use safe sleep rules, and get medical help for fever in young infants, breathing problems, blue or gray color, limpness, seizures, dehydration, injury, or inconsolable crying that feels wrong.
