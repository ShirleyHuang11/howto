## Goal

End a romantic relationship clearly and compassionately, without cruelty, false hope, or avoidable confusion.

## Preconditions

- You have decided to end the relationship, not merely test their reaction.
- The setting is safe, private enough, and not during a crisis if avoidable.
- You have considered shared housing, belongings, money, pets, and immediate logistics.

## Steps

1. **Choose the safest respectful channel.** [BRANCH: safe relationship | unsafe or volatile relationship] Meet in person if possible; use phone, text, or support if safety requires distance. → *Expect:* the channel balances respect and safety.
2. **Prepare one clear sentence.** "I care about you, and I am ending our relationship." → *Expect:* the message cannot be mistaken for a negotiation.
3. **Say it early.** Do not build a long preamble that makes them guess. → *Expect:* they know the conversation's purpose.
4. **Be honest without being cruel.** Name the real broad reason, such as compatibility, trust, or wanting different futures, without listing every flaw. → *Expect:* the truth is clear but not weaponized.
5. **Do not be wishy-washy.** Avoid "maybe someday" unless you genuinely mean a defined pause. → *Expect:* the ending is understandable.
6. **Allow their reaction.** They may cry, go quiet, ask questions, or be angry. Listen without surrendering the decision. → *Expect:* their feelings have space, but the decision remains intact.
7. **Set immediate logistics.** Discuss leaving, belongings, contact, shared plans, and who tells whom. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not use threats, public humiliation, outing, or disclosure of private information to force the ending. → *Expect:* the next 24-72 hours are practical.
8. **Leave cleanly.** Repeat the core sentence if needed, then end the conversation rather than relitigating all history. → *Expect:* the breakup has an actual close.

## Decision points

- You fear harm to yourself or them → involve trusted people, crisis resources, or authorities before the breakup conversation.
- You live together → plan temporary sleeping arrangements and key logistics before speaking.
- They ask for another chance → answer once with clarity, then stop debating.
- Children, marriage, or shared assets are involved → get legal or professional guidance.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soft ending:** detect language that implies a pause, recover by saying the relationship is over.
- **F2 Cruel inventory:** detect listing every resentment, recover by returning to the broad reason.
- **F3 Endless closure talk:** detect the same questions cycling, recover by naming what you have answered and ending the conversation.
- **F4 Safety escalation:** detect threats, blocking exits, or self-harm statements, recover by contacting support or emergency help.

## Verification

The other person has heard that the relationship is over, the reason was stated without cruelty, immediate logistics were addressed, and no false hope was offered.

## Variations

- Short dating relationship: a call or clear message may be enough if in-person would be disproportionate.
- Long-term partnership: plan more logistics and expect more than one practical follow-up.
- Unsafe relationship: safety planning outranks etiquette.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because emotions, safety, housing, and private information may be involved. Do not break up in a way that traps either person. Seek urgent help for threats, violence, stalking, or self-harm risk.
