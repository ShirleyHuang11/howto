## Goal

Attend an event as someone's guest in a way that supports your host, respects the occasion, and leaves people glad you came.

## Preconditions

- An invitation from a host, partner, friend, or colleague, plus the event time, location, dress code, and expected role.
- Basic context: whose event it is, why it matters, and any people or topics to handle carefully.
- A plan to get home without relying on your host at the worst possible time.

## Steps

1. **Get briefed before arrival.** Ask your host for names to know, relationships, dress code, gift expectations, and any tensions. → *Expect:* you can recognize a few key people and avoid obvious mistakes.
2. **Arrive as a unit.** Be on time, dressed appropriately, and ready to follow your host's lead for greetings, seating, and introductions. → *Expect:* your host does not have to manage your lateness or confusion.
3. **Learn a few names.** Repeat names once, connect them to context, and use them when saying goodbye. → *Expect:* at least three people feel noticed instead of treated as background.
4. **Help your host lightly.** Offer to hold a bag, get water, make conversation with someone, take a photo, or rescue them from a stuck conversation. [BRANCH: social event | work event] be warmer and looser at social events; be more restrained at work events. → *Expect:* your presence makes the night easier for the person who brought you.
5. **Do not disappear.** Step away for bathroom, calls, or conversation, but tell your host if you will be gone more than a few minutes. → *Expect:* your host never has to search the room anxiously.
6. **Do not overdrink or overperform.** Keep alcohol and jokes below the level where your host becomes responsible for you. → *Expect:* you remain steady, readable, and appropriate to the room.
7. **Respect photos and private context.** Ask before posting, especially at weddings, work events, launches, or family gatherings. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* public posts can reveal attendance, relationships, or news before hosts are ready. → *Expect:* no one has to ask you to remove a photo.
8. **Thank the host and your inviter.** Say goodbye to the event host when practical, then thank the person who invited you on the way home or the next day. → *Expect:* both hosts know you appreciated being included.

## Decision points

- If you know almost no one → ask open, low-risk questions and keep answers brief enough for conversation to move.
- If your host is busy with duties → be self-sufficient but easy to find.
- If someone asks how you know the host → answer simply and do not overshare your host's private life.
- If the event turns uncomfortable → check with your host before leaving unless safety requires immediate exit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 You forget a name:** detect hesitation → ask again warmly: "Remind me of your name?" then use it once.
- **F2 You say too much:** detect your host's look, a pause, or topic shift → stop, pivot to a question, and apologize later if needed.
- **F3 You get separated:** detect you have been gone too long → text your location and rejoin.
- **F4 You drink past your plan:** detect blurred judgment or louder voice → switch to water, stop posting, and arrange a quiet ride home.

## Verification

You arrived prepared, supported your host, stayed appropriate, remained findable, and thanked both your inviter and the event host when possible.

## Variations

- Wedding plus-one: let the couple's timeline lead, avoid taking prime photo positions, and sign the guest book if there is one.
- Work gala: assume everything is professional context, even at the bar.
- Family event: learn relationship labels and follow your host's lead on affection and formality.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, mostly reputational. Avoid oversharing, posting without permission, driving impaired, or making your host responsible for your behavior.
