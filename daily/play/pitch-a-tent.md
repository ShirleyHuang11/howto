## Goal

Pitch a tent on suitable ground so the body, poles, stakes, rain fly, and guy lines form a stable shelter for the expected weather.

## Preconditions

- A complete tent: body, poles, stakes, rain fly, footprint if used, and repair sleeve if available.
- Permission to camp at the chosen site.
- Ground that is not in a drainage channel, under dead branches, or on fragile vegetation.
- Enough daylight or headlamp light to inspect poles, seams, and tension.

## Steps

1. **Choose the site.** Pick flat, durable ground above runoff paths and away from dead limbs, ant nests, and sharp rocks. → *Expect:* the footprint area is level, legal, and safer than the surrounding options.
2. **Orient the tent.** Point the narrow end or strongest side toward the wind and place the door where entry will stay dry and practical. → *Expect:* the layout works with wind, slope, and foot traffic.
3. **Lay out the footprint and body.** Spread the footprint if used, then lay the tent body over it with corners aligned. → *Expect:* fabric lies flat without the footprint sticking out to catch rain.
4. **Assemble the poles.** Let pole sections seat fully without snapping them together under tension. → *Expect:* poles are complete, straight, and fully joined.
5. **Raise the tent.** [BRANCH: clips | sleeves] Insert poles through sleeves or attach clips in the tent maker's order, then lift the structure evenly. → *Expect:* the tent stands without twisted fabric or crossed poles.
6. **Stake it taut.** Stake corners first, then adjust so the floor is smooth but not strained. → *Expect:* the tent holds its shape and the door zips without pulling hard.
7. **Add fly and guy lines.** Attach the fly, tension it off the tent body, stake guy lines at useful angles, and do a wind check by pushing gently on the frame. → *Expect:* the fly sheds water, lines are visible, and the tent springs back under hand pressure.

## Decision points

- Wind increasing: use all guy lines and extra anchors, or move to a more sheltered site.
- Soil too loose for stakes: use rocks, deadman anchors, or different ground.
- Rain expected: keep the fly off the inner tent until ready, then attach quickly and tension well.
- Door hard to zip: reduce corner tension before the zipper is damaged.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sagging fly:** rain fly touches the inner tent, tighten fly corners and guy lines to restore air gap.
- **F2 Bent pole:** a section is mis-seated or overloaded, stop tensioning and reseat or sleeve the pole.
- **F3 Water pooling:** the tent sits in a low spot, move before weather arrives if possible.
- **F4 Stakes pull out:** ground is soft or angle is wrong, reset stakes lower and angled away from the tent.

## Verification

The tent is verified when it stands taut, zips normally, has a separated rain fly, and resists a gentle wind push without shifting stakes.

## Variations

- `freestanding`: assemble first, then lift and move the tent to the best micro-site before staking.
- `sand`: bury anchors horizontally because standard stakes pull out easily.
- `platform`: use deck anchors or cord loops instead of driving stakes.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is medium because poor site choice can expose sleepers to weather, falling branches, or flooding. Keep guy lines visible to avoid trips and never pitch where water, wind, or overhead hazards are already warning you away.
