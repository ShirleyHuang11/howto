## Goal

Make a clear leaf rubbing by placing paper over a leaf vein-side up and rubbing with the side of a crayon.

## Preconditions

- Fresh or dry leaves with raised veins and interesting shapes.
- Plain paper thin enough to reveal texture.
- Crayons with paper wrappers peeled back, colored pencils, or soft graphite.
- A flat table, clipboard, or book cover under the leaf.

## Steps

1. **Choose the leaf.** Pick a leaf with strong veins and no wet dirt, then brush off loose grit. → *Expect:* the leaf is clean enough not to smear the paper.
2. **Place vein-side up.** Lay the underside of the leaf facing up on the flat surface. → *Expect:* the raised vein network can be felt with a fingertip.
3. **Cover with paper.** Put paper over the leaf and hold the sheet still at the corners. → *Expect:* the leaf shape is hidden but does not slide underneath.
4. **Rub with the crayon side.** Turn the crayon sideways and rub lightly across the paper in one direction. → *Expect:* the veins and outline begin appearing before the background fills in.
5. **Build the color.** Add more pressure gradually, keeping strokes broad and even. → *Expect:* the full leaf shape appears without tearing the paper.
6. **Layer colors.** [BRANCH: single-color | layered] Stop after one color for a clean print, or add a second light color from another direction. → *Expect:* the rubbing keeps the vein detail while gaining depth.
7. **Lift and inspect.** Hold the paper flat, remove the leaf, and sign or label the rubbing if desired. → *Expect:* the final image shows the outline, stem, and main veins clearly.

## Decision points

- Paper too thick: switch to lighter paper so texture transfers.
- Leaf too flat: choose a leaf with stronger veins or use the underside.
- Crayon crumbs appear: wipe them away gently before they smear.
- Want a composition: tape several leaves lightly under one sheet before rubbing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Smudged image:** the paper or leaf moved, hold corners more firmly and start on a clean area.
- **F2 Torn paper:** pressure was too high or crayon edge too sharp, use the side and lighter strokes.
- **F3 Weak veins:** the leaf is too smooth, choose oak, maple, elm, or another leaf with raised structure.
- **F4 Wax buildup:** heavy crayon hides detail, place a new sheet and rub with less pressure.

## Verification

The rubbing is verified when the leaf outline and main vein pattern are visible on the paper without major smears or tears.

## Variations

- `autumn`: layer yellow, orange, and red lightly over the same leaf.
- `science`: label each rubbing with the leaf margin, vein pattern, and plant name if known.
- `collage`: overlap several rubbings after testing each leaf separately.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low. Wash hands after handling outdoor leaves, avoid poison ivy and pesticide-treated plants, and do not collect from private or protected areas without permission.
