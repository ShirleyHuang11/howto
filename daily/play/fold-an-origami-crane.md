## Goal

A square sheet of paper is folded into a classic origami crane using disciplined creases, a square base, and a bird base.

## Preconditions

- One square sheet of paper, preferably thin and not glossy.
- Flat table, clean dry hands, and enough light to see crease intersections.
- Patience to align corners exactly before sharpening each crease.

## Steps

1. **Start color side down.** Place the square like a diamond if using colored paper. → *Expect:* the finished color will show outside.
2. **Fold both diagonals.** Fold corner to corner both ways, creasing sharply, then unfold. → *Expect:* an X crease crosses the center.
3. **Fold both book folds.** Fold edge to edge horizontally and vertically, then unfold. → *Expect:* a plus crease crosses the center.
4. **Collapse the square base.** Bring opposite corners together along the existing creases and flatten into a smaller diamond. → *Expect:* open flaps point downward and the closed corner points upward.
5. **Make kite folds.** Fold the lower left and right edges of the top layer to the center line. → *Expect:* the top layer forms a narrow kite.
6. **Crease the top triangle.** Fold the upper triangle down over the kite folds, crease, then unfold all three folds. → *Expect:* a clear horizontal crease caps the kite.
7. **Petal fold upward.** Lift the bottom top layer and fold it upward while the sides collapse inward. → *Expect:* a long diamond forms cleanly without wrinkles.
8. **Repeat on the back.** Turn the model over and repeat the kite folds, top crease, and petal fold. → *Expect:* the bird base has two long points below and two broad flaps above.
9. **Narrow the legs.** Fold lower outside edges to the center on both front and back. → *Expect:* the two lower points become slim.
10. **Form neck and tail.** Inside-reverse-fold each lower point upward, then inside-reverse-fold a small head on one side. → *Expect:* the crane has a neck, head, and tail.
11. **Fold wings down.** Fold each broad flap downward from the top body line. → *Expect:* wings sit even on both sides.
12. **Open the body.** Hold the wings and pull gently apart, then puff the body from below. → *Expect:* the crane stands with a hollow body and balanced wings.

## Decision points

- Paper is rectangular → cut or fold it into a true square before starting.
- Creases do not meet → unfold to the last clean base and realign, because early errors compound.
- Petal fold wrinkles → slow down and let existing creases decide the collapse.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Crooked base:** detect unequal diamond edges; restart or recrease from the square base.
- **F2 Torn paper:** detect whitening or splits at the center; use thinner paper and lighter pressure.
- **F3 Reversed color:** detect the wrong side outside after square base; start color side down next time.
- **F4 Flat crane body:** detect wings but no volume; pull wings apart gently and open the underside pocket.

## Verification

The model has a recognizable crane shape with head, tail, two wings, and a puffed body that holds together without tape.

## Variations

- Small paper: use a toothpick or fingernail for precise creases.
- Decorative paper: avoid thick cardstock because inside reverse folds become bulky.
- Teaching version: pause after the bird base and compare all four points before forming the crane.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Paper cuts are possible; keep sharp cutting tools away once the square is prepared.
