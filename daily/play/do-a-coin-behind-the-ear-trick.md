## Goal

Perform a simple coin trick where a coin appears to come from behind someone's ear.

## Preconditions

- A coin or similar small flat object is available.
- The spectator is comfortable with a hand near their ear.
- A mirror or phone camera is available for practice.

## Steps

1. **Choose the coin.** Pick a coin large enough to control but small enough to hide behind curled fingers. → *Expect:* the coin sits flat against your fingers without showing from the front.
2. **Learn the finger palm.** Hold the coin at the base of your middle and ring fingers, lightly curled into the palm. → *Expect:* your hand looks relaxed from the spectator's view.
3. **Practice empty-looking gestures.** Move your hand naturally while keeping the coin hidden in finger palm. → *Expect:* the coin does not flash in the mirror.
4. **Set up the moment.** Hold the hidden coin in the hand closest to the spectator's ear while talking to them. → *Expect:* your loaded hand is near the reveal position.
5. **Use misdirection.** Ask them to look at your other hand, their hat, or a spot in the air while the loaded hand moves near the ear. → *Expect:* attention shifts away from the hidden coin.
6. **Reach behind the ear.** Slide the coin from finger palm to fingertips as your hand passes behind the ear. → *Expect:* the coin is now pinched at the fingertips but still partly hidden.
7. **Reveal slowly.** Pull the coin into view as if it was found behind the ear. → *Expect:* the spectator sees the coin appear from a place they were not watching.
8. **Reset casually.** Drop the coin into a pocket or hand it over before the spectator asks to inspect your hand. → *Expect:* the trick ends cleanly.
9. **Practice in a mirror.** Repeat the whole trick from the spectator's angle until the palm and reveal look natural. → *Expect:* you can perform without looking at your hidden hand.

## Decision points

- Spectator does not want touch near their ear → reveal from behind their shoulder or from the air instead.
- Coin keeps flashing → use a larger curl in the fingers and slower movement.
- Audience is very close → angle your palm slightly down and keep the trick brief.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Coin drops:** detect a clink or visible fall → laugh it off, pick it up, and restart later.
- **F2 Coin flashes:** detect the spectator looking at the loaded hand → turn the movement into a visible vanish and stop.
- **F3 Awkward reach:** detect the spectator leaning away → ask permission or switch to a no-touch reveal.
- **F4 Stiff hand:** detect tense fingers in the mirror → practice holding the coin while casually pointing and waving.

## Verification

In a mirror or recording, the coin stays hidden until the reveal and the hand looks relaxed throughout the trick.

## Variations

- For children: use a plastic token instead of real money.
- No-touch version: make the coin appear from behind your own ear.
- Repeat version: secretly load a second coin before doing the trick again.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not put small coins near the mouth of a child, and ask before touching or reaching close to someone's face.
