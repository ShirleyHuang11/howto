## Goal

Throw and catch a ball with a partner using a ready position, step-and-throw mechanics, and soft hands.

## Preconditions

- A ball appropriate to the players' age and skill.
- Gloves if using a baseball or softball.
- Open space away from glass, cars, and bystanders.
- Partners agree on distance and throw strength.

## Steps

1. **Choose the ball and glove setup.** [BRANCH: bare hands | glove] use a softer ball barehanded, or wear gloves for hard balls. → *Expect:* each player can close hands or glove around the ball.
2. **Set the distance.** Start 3 to 5 m apart for beginners. → *Expect:* partners can talk normally and see each other's hands.
3. **Take ready position.** Feet shoulder-width, knees soft, hands up near the chest. → *Expect:* the receiver can move left, right, forward, or back.
4. **Grip the ball.** Hold it across the seams if present, with relaxed fingers and thumb underneath. → *Expect:* the ball is secure without a tight fist.
5. **Point the front shoulder.** Turn the non-throwing shoulder toward the target. → *Expect:* the throwing hand moves behind the body comfortably.
6. **Step and throw.** Step toward the partner with the opposite foot and bring the arm forward. → *Expect:* the ball leaves toward the partner's chest.
7. **Follow through.** Let the throwing hand finish across the body and keep eyes on the receiver. → *Expect:* the thrower stays balanced after release.
8. **Receive with soft hands.** Move the glove or hands to meet the ball, then give slightly as it arrives. → *Expect:* the ball settles instead of bouncing out.
9. **Secure the catch.** Close the glove or second hand around the ball. → *Expect:* the ball is controlled before the next throw.
10. **Progress gradually.** Add distance only after five comfortable catches in a row. → *Expect:* throws stay catchable at the new distance.

## Decision points

- If the receiver flinches → switch to a softer ball or stand closer with underhand tosses.
- If throws sail high → aim at the partner's belt and finish the hand lower.
- If throws bounce short → step toward the target and release a little later.
- If using a glove → catch above the waist with fingers up and below the waist with fingers down.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ball bounces out:** detect repeated drops after contact → relax the receiving hand and pull back slightly on contact.
- **F2 Wild throw:** detect throws outside two steps of the receiver → shorten distance and slow the arm until accuracy returns.
- **F3 Face-level throw:** detect the ball rising toward head height → call "heads up," catch defensively, and agree to chest targets only.
- **F4 Sore arm:** detect elbow or shoulder pain → stop throwing hard and switch to short underhand tosses or rest.

## Verification

Partners complete five consecutive catchable throws and catches at the chosen distance with controlled throws, ready hands, and no ball near the face.

## Variations

- Underhand catch: use a gentle arc for young children or tight spaces.
- Grounders: roll the ball and practice stepping in front before fielding.
- Baseball progression: add crow hop footwork only after basic accuracy is reliable.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Match ball hardness to skill, keep throws away from faces and bystanders, and stop before arm soreness becomes pain.
