## Goal

Play a basic game of jacks by scattering the jacks, bouncing the ball once, and picking up sets from onesies through tens.

## Preconditions

- A set of ten jacks and one small bouncy ball.
- Smooth floor, table, or pavement where the ball bounce is predictable.
- Players agree on turn order, fouls, and whether one or two hands may be used.
- Small pieces are kept away from toddlers and pets.

## Steps

1. **Set the play area.** Clear a flat space about an arm's reach wide and remove objects the ball could roll under. → *Expect:* the ball can bounce and be retrieved easily.
2. **Scatter the jacks.** Hold all ten jacks a few inches above the surface and let them fall naturally. → *Expect:* the jacks spread out without leaving the play area.
3. **Start onesies.** Toss the ball up, let it bounce once, pick up one jack, and catch the ball before it bounces again. → *Expect:* one jack is collected cleanly.
4. **Continue the set.** Repeat until all ten jacks have been picked up one at a time. → *Expect:* the player completes onesies or loses the turn on a foul.
5. **Move to higher numbers.** [BRANCH: twos-through-tens | beginner-repeat] Pick up two per bounce for twosies, then three, and so on, or repeat onesies for practice. → *Expect:* the target number for each turn is clear.
6. **Call fouls fairly.** End the turn if the ball bounces twice, a wrong number of jacks is picked up, another jack is moved, or the ball is not caught. → *Expect:* play passes to the next player without argument.
7. **Resume from the level missed.** On the next turn, restart the same number set rather than going back to onesies unless house rules say so. → *Expect:* progress through onesies to tens stays trackable.

## Decision points

- Ball too lively: switch to a less bouncy ball or softer surface.
- Jacks too spread out: scatter lower and inside a marked boundary.
- New players: allow practice rounds with no scoring.
- Competitive play: agree on whether touched jacks count as moved.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Double bounce:** the ball hits twice before catch, turn ends and the player retries that level next turn.
- **F2 Wrong pickup count:** too many or too few jacks are lifted, return them and pass the turn.
- **F3 Moved jack:** a non-target jack shifts, call a foul and reset for the next player.
- **F4 Lost ball:** ball rolls away, pause and clear the area before restarting the turn.

## Verification

The game is verified when players can complete or fairly foul turns using the agreed pickup number from onesies toward tens.

## Variations

- `solo`: count how far you get before the first foul and try to beat that score.
- `easy`: allow the ball to bounce twice for younger players.
- `tabletop`: use a lower bounce and smaller scatter to keep pieces contained.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low. Jacks are small and pointy enough to hurt bare feet, so collect all pieces after play and keep them away from children who might swallow them.
