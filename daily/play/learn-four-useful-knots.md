## Goal

Learn four useful knots by purpose: overhand stopper, square knot for light joining, bowline fixed loop, and two half-hitches for tying to a post.

## Preconditions

- One or two short practice ropes, shoelaces, or paracord pieces.
- A table, chair leg, railing, or smooth post for hitch practice.
- Time to untie and repeat instead of tying once and guessing.
- No life-safety load, suspended weight, animal restraint, or moving vehicle involved.

## Steps

1. **Tie the overhand knot.** Make a loop, pass the working end through it, and pull both sides to make a simple stopper. → *Expect:* one small knot sits near the rope end and is hard to pull through a narrow gap.
2. **Untie and repeat the overhand.** Tie it three more times, each time leaving a longer or shorter tail on purpose. → *Expect:* you can control where the stopper lands.
3. **Tie the square knot.** Cross right over left and tuck, then left over right and tuck, then dress the knot flat. → *Expect:* the two tails lie beside their matching standing parts.
4. **Tie the bowline.** Make the rabbit hole, bring the working end up through it, around the standing part, and back down. → *Expect:* a fixed loop holds its size when pulled by hand.
5. **Tie two half-hitches.** Wrap the rope around a post, make one half-hitch around the standing part, then repeat with a second half-hitch. → *Expect:* the rope grips the post and the two hitches sit neatly beside each other.
6. **Match knot to job.** [BRANCH: stopper | join | fixed-loop | post-tie] Choose overhand for a stopper, square for light same-rope joining, bowline for a fixed loop, or two half-hitches for a post. → *Expect:* each knot is chosen for a clear purpose.
7. **Practice the set.** Tie all four knots in order, inspect them, untie them, and repeat twice more. → *Expect:* the shapes become familiar without needing to force them.

## Decision points

- Need a stopper: use the overhand, but know it can jam tight.
- Need to join two similar ends lightly: use the square knot and check for a granny knot.
- Need a loop that does not slide closed: use the bowline.
- Need to tie to a post or ring: use two half-hitches and keep tension on the standing part.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mixed purposes:** the wrong knot is chosen for the job, stop and name the job before tying again.
- **F2 Granny square knot:** the square knot looks twisted, untie and use right-over-left then left-over-right.
- **F3 Bowline slips loose:** the collar is not dressed, retie with a longer tail and set it firmly.
- **F4 Hitches slide:** the post is slick or the hitches are loose, add tension or choose a more secure hitch.

## Verification

The practice set is verified when you can tie, name, inspect, and correctly assign a use to all four knots without looking up the steps.

## Variations

- `camp`: practice on thicker cord that behaves like guy line or utility rope.
- `craft`: practice with yarn or string, then avoid loading those knots heavily.
- `teaching`: have a partner point to a job and choose the knot before tying.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low because this is practice, but bad knot confidence is a real hazard. Keep the rule simple: these knots are learned on the table before they are trusted outdoors.
