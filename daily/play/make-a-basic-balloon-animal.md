## Goal

A long twisting balloon is inflated and twisted into a basic dog with head, ears, legs, body, and tail.

## Preconditions

- 260-style twisting balloons and a hand pump are available.
- The player has room to twist away from sharp objects.
- Anyone sensitive to latex or popping sounds can step away.

## Steps

1. **Inflate with a tail.** Pump the balloon, leaving 3 to 4 inches uninflated at the end. → *Expect:* air has space to move during twisting.
2. **Tie the nozzle.** Pinch the neck, tie a simple knot, and squeeze the balloon gently to soften pressure. → *Expect:* balloon is sealed and slightly flexible.
3. **Twist the snout.** Make a 2-inch bubble at the knotted end. → *Expect:* the bubble holds when pinched.
4. **Make ears.** Twist two more 2-inch bubbles, then fold them together and twist-lock at the base. → *Expect:* snout points forward and two ears sit above it.
5. **Make neck and front legs.** Twist a short neck bubble, then two equal leg bubbles, fold the legs together, and twist-lock. → *Expect:* front legs hang below the neck.
6. **Make body and back legs.** Twist a body bubble, then two equal back-leg bubbles, fold and twist-lock them. → *Expect:* dog stands with four legs.
7. **Leave the tail.** Let the remaining uninflated end act as the tail. → *Expect:* tail has some soft uninflated balloon at the tip.
8. **Adjust gently.** Bend legs and ears into place without squeezing hard. → *Expect:* the dog shape is recognizable and not overpressurized.

## Decision points

- Balloon feels rock-hard → let a little air out before tying or restart.
- Tail disappears before the final twist → make smaller bubbles next time.
- Twist slips open → add two more turns while holding both bubbles.
- Balloon squeaks loudly → twist slower and use relaxed hands.
- Child is scared of popping → use fewer twists and keep the balloon softer.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Balloon bursts:** detect sudden pop; recover by checking for sharp objects and inflating the next balloon less.
- **F2 No tail left:** detect fully inflated end after legs; recover by shortening body and leg bubbles next time.
- **F3 Bubbles untwist:** detect ears or legs opening; recover by twist-locking paired bubbles together.
- **F4 Uneven legs:** detect dog tipping over; recover by squeezing air between bubbles and retwisting sizes.
- **F5 Latex irritation:** detect itching or rash; recover by stopping and washing hands.

## Verification

The finished balloon has a snout, two ears, four legs, a body, and a soft tail, and it stays twisted for at least 30 seconds.

## Variations

- Giraffe: make the neck much longer and the body shorter.
- Mouse: make tiny ears and a long tail.
- Hat attachment: twist a second balloon loop around the dog only after the dog holds shape.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from latex allergy, choking pieces, and popping near ears. Keep broken balloon scraps away from small children.
