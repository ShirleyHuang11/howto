## Goal

You can toss a basketball with backspin, catch its center on one fingernail, and keep it spinning with light slaps from the free hand.

## Preconditions

- An inflated basketball or similar grippy ball.
- Open indoor or outdoor space away from faces, lamps, shelves, and screens.
- Fingernails trimmed smooth; one slightly longer nail can help but is not required.

## Steps

1. **Mark the spin axis.** Hold the ball with a seam line vertical and imagine a pole running through the top and bottom. → *Expect:* you know where the top center should land on your finger.
2. **Learn the backspin toss.** Hold the ball at chest height with both hands, thumbs behind it, and toss upward while snapping both wrists toward you. → *Expect:* the ball rises with clear backward rotation.
3. **Catch on the fingernail.** Put your index finger under the falling center, nail facing up, and let the ball settle on the nail or fingertip. → *Expect:* the ball spins in place for a moment before sliding.
4. **Keep the finger quiet.** Move the whole hand under the ball instead of poking at it. → *Expect:* the finger stays near the center axis rather than chasing the edge.
5. **Add the free-hand slap.** While the ball spins, brush the side lightly in the direction of spin with the other hand. [BRANCH: gentle slap | hard slap] Gentle adds speed; hard knocks the axis off. → *Expect:* spin lasts longer without the ball jumping sideways.
6. **Reset before it falls.** When the wobble grows, catch the ball with both hands instead of letting it drop. → *Expect:* controlled resets and fewer bruised knuckles.
7. **Build the hold time.** Practice in rounds of ten tosses, counting only spins that balance for at least three seconds. → *Expect:* the best attempts stretch from brief balance to several seconds.

## Decision points

- If the ball slides immediately, the toss is off-center or the spin is too slow.
- If it hurts, switch to the fingernail surface and reduce pressure.
- If the ball keeps drifting, stand still and bring the hand under the center instead of leaning your body.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No backspin:** the ball sits dead and falls → exaggerate the wrist snap toward your body.
- **F2 Edge catch:** the ball rolls off one side → watch the top center and meet it under the spin axis.
- **F3 Over-slap:** the ball jumps away → brush with fingers, not a palm hit.
- **F4 Room damage:** a falling ball hits objects → move to a clearer area before more attempts.

## Verification

The basketball spins balanced on one finger for five seconds, and you add at least one free-hand slap without dropping it.

## Variations

- A smaller rubber playground ball is easier for children and smaller hands.
- Advanced spinners transfer from finger to finger after the axis is stable.
- A damp or dirty ball grips poorly; wipe it before deciding your hands are the problem.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the practice zone clear, avoid spinning near anyone's face, and catch failed attempts early because a full-size ball can still knock things down.
