## Goal

Build a small model volcano and create a foamy eruption using baking soda, dish soap, food color, and vinegar.

## Preconditions

- Adult supervision for children.
- Baking soda, vinegar, dish soap, food coloring, and a small cup or bottle.
- A tray, sink, or outdoor surface that can get wet.
- Paper, clay, or foil to shape a cone around the cup.

## Steps

1. **Protect the area.** Put the cup or bottle on a tray and keep towels nearby. → *Expect:* spills stay contained.
2. **Build the cone.** Shape paper, clay, or foil around the container without covering the opening. → *Expect:* the model looks like a volcano with a clear crater.
3. **Add baking soda.** Spoon two or three tablespoons of baking soda into the crater. → *Expect:* white powder sits at the bottom.
4. **Add soap and color.** Add a small squirt of dish soap and a few drops of food coloring. → *Expect:* the crater holds a thick, colored mixture.
5. **Predict the reaction.** Ask what will happen when acid vinegar meets base baking soda. → *Expect:* players expect gas bubbles and foam.
6. **Pour vinegar slowly.** Add vinegar a little at a time. → *Expect:* foam rises and flows down the cone.
7. **Repeat with changes.** [BRANCH: more foam | slower flow] add more soap for foam, or pour less vinegar for control. → *Expect:* the eruption changes in visible ways.
8. **Explain the gas.** Point out that carbon dioxide bubbles push the soapy liquid upward. → *Expect:* the eruption connects to a concrete chemistry idea.
9. **Clean up fully.** Rinse the tray and throw away soggy paper after the reaction stops. → *Expect:* the workspace is dry and not sticky.

## Decision points

- Indoor setup → use a deep tray and small amounts.
- Outdoor setup → allow a bigger cone, but still collect trash.
- Food coloring stains easily → skip it on porous surfaces.
- Foam reaches the tray edge → stop pouring vinegar and clean before repeating.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Weak eruption:** detect only light fizz → add fresh baking soda and pour vinegar more directly.
- **F2 Overflow mess:** detect foam escaping the tray → stop, wipe, and restart with smaller amounts.
- **F3 Clogged crater:** detect paper or clay blocking the opening → clear the opening before adding vinegar.
- **F4 Stained surface:** detect color on table or hands → wash immediately with soap and water.

## Verification

The volcano produces visible foam from the crater and the tray, tools, and hands are cleaned afterward.

## Variations

- Use washable paint instead of food coloring for a brighter model.
- Compare warm and cool vinegar to see whether bubbling speed changes.
- Try different crater sizes while keeping the same baking soda amount.

## Safety & privacy

Adult supervision is required for young children, and cleanup is part of the activity.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* Food coloring can stain fabric, grout, and unfinished wood, so keep the experiment on a washable tray.
