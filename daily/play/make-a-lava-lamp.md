## Goal

Make a temporary lava-lamp effect in a clear bottle using water, oil, food coloring, and a fizzing tablet reaction.

## Preconditions

- Clear bottle or jar, water, vegetable oil, food coloring, and an effervescent tablet.
- Tray, towel, or washable surface for spills.
- The container is not used for drinking after the activity unless washed thoroughly.
- Children know the mixture and tablet are not food.

## Steps

1. **Set the container on a tray.** Place the clear bottle upright where spills can be wiped easily. → *Expect:* the work area is stable and protected.
2. **Add water.** Fill the bottle about one quarter full with water. → *Expect:* a clear water layer sits at the bottom.
3. **Add oil.** Pour vegetable oil until the bottle is mostly full, leaving air space at the top. → *Expect:* the oil floats above the water in a separate layer.
4. **Add color.** Drop in several drops of food coloring and wait for them to sink into the water layer. → *Expect:* colored drops pass through oil and tint the water below.
5. **Start the fizz.** Break a fizzing tablet into pieces and drop one piece into the bottle. → *Expect:* bubbles carry colored water blobs up through the oil.
6. **Watch and repeat.** [BRANCH: gentle | active] Use one small tablet piece for slow blobs or another piece after fizz slows for more motion. → *Expect:* blobs rise, fall, and separate as bubbles form and pop.
7. **Reuse or dispose.** Let fizz stop, cap loosely for short display, or pour contents into trash according to local guidance for oily waste. → *Expect:* the activity ends without pressure buildup or an oily sink mess.

## Decision points

- Want brighter color: add one drop at a time, since too much makes the water nearly opaque.
- Bottle has a narrow neck: use a funnel and pour slowly.
- Cap needed: wait until fizz stops and never seal an active reaction.
- Reuse planned: keep extra tablet pieces dry for later.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No blobs:** tablet is old or water layer is too small, add fresh tablet or a little more water.
- **F2 Cloudy mixture:** bottle was shaken, let it settle until layers separate again.
- **F3 Overflow:** too much tablet or too little headspace, move to tray and use smaller pieces.
- **F4 Oily spill:** oil reaches the table, wipe with paper towel and wash with dish soap.

## Verification

The lava lamp is verified when colored water blobs rise through the oil with fizzing bubbles and settle back down after bubbles pop.

## Variations

- `glitter`: add a tiny pinch, knowing cleanup becomes messier.
- `color-mixing`: make two bottles with different colors instead of muddying one bottle.
- `small-jar`: halve the amounts and use smaller tablet pieces.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low. Do not drink the mixture, do not seal the bottle during fizzing, keep tablets away from young children, and keep oil off floors where it can make a slip hazard.
