## Goal

A knotted cable, cord, or string of lights returned to a clean line without damage, using the method that works on every tangle from earbuds to extension cords — plus the wrapping habit that retires this recipe from your life.

## Preconditions

- The tangled item and a clear flat surface; tangles fought in mid-air stay tangles.
- Patience calibration: tension is the enemy. Every pull on a random loop tightens somewhere else; the entire method is loosening, then routing ends.

## Steps

1. **Loosen the whole mass first.** Set it on the table and gently massage the clump, working slack into every visible crossing without pulling anything through anything. → *Expect:* a fluffy, open tangle instead of a tight fist; knots that looked hopeless now show daylight.
2. **Find a free end and make it the protagonist.** One end (for lights and extension cords, the plug end) gets followed; the other end and the mass stay passive. → *Expect:* one identified end and the first two or three crossings it passes through visible.
3. **Route the end backward through its crossings, one at a time.** Feed the free end back through each loop it entered, shortest path first, pulling through only enough cable to clear that crossing. Widen loops before threading; never yank the full length through. → *Expect:* each pass audibly simplifies the mass; the free tail growing steadily.
4. **Shake-test between rounds.** Lift the mass by the far end and shake gently: loosened crossings fall out on their own, and the true remaining knots reveal themselves. → *Expect:* free cable lengthening after every shake; the problem shrinking to one or two genuine knots.
5. **Defeat the final knots with the pinch-and-push.** Push cable *into* the knot from both sides to loosen its heart (the counterintuitive move), open the loops with a fingertip or a pen, then thread the end out. → *Expect:* the last knots opening without force; the cable running clean end to end.
6. **Wrap it so this never happens again.** Cables: loose over-under coils or a simple loop secured with velcro/its own tie. Earbuds and small cords: loose figure-eight around fingers, tail wrapped around the middle. Lights: around a cardboard rectangle or spool. → *Expect:* a stored cable whose next deployment takes three seconds, which is the recipe's actual ending.

## Decision points

- Delicate cables (headphones, charging cords): all of the above at half force; copper fatigue lives at the connectors, so tangles get worked mid-cable and connectors never used as handles.
- Multiple cables tangled together: separate the species first (follow each to its ends and lift free the least-entangled one at a time) before untangling any individual.
- Genuinely fused clumps of cheap string: value your minutes honestly; scissors are permitted for garden twine, never for anything with a plug.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pulled a loop tight into a hard knot:** you fed tension into the mass; return to step 1's massage on that knot specifically, and the pinch-and-push (step 5) reopens most sins.
- **F2 Lost track of which end you were routing:** tape or clip a marker on the protagonist end; in light strings, the plug's shape is the marker.
- **F3 The cable kinked or the jacket tore:** force found the weak point; for power cords a damaged jacket is a retirement notice, not a repair opportunity (tape over damaged mains cable is a fire folk-remedy).
- **F4 Same drawer, same tangle, next month:** step 6 was skipped; the tangle is a storage failure, not a cable behavior. One bag of velcro ties reforms an entire household.

## Verification

The cable runs clean end to end with no new kinks, connectors were never used as handles, no force spiked past gentle at any point, and it went back into storage wrapped — the only verification that predicts the future.

## Variations

- Necklace chains: same method under a bright light with two pins as fingers; a drop of baby oil loosens the microscopic knots.
- Rope and garden hose scale the method up: massage, protagonist end, over-under coils; sailors and climbers have entire religions built on step 6.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The one real line: damaged power-cable jackets end cables, and knots near plugs deserve extra gentleness. Everything else is meditation with a prize at the end.
