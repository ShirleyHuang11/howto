## Goal

Whittle a stick into a simple point or shallow spoon shape using a sharp knife, controlled cuts, and a clear safety zone.

## Preconditions

- A sharp folding knife or carving knife that locks open or is held securely.
- A green or straight-grained stick, free of embedded grit, nails, and cracks.
- A seated position, stable footing, and a clear arm-length safety circle.
- First-aid supplies nearby, including clean water, gauze, and pressure bandage.

## Steps

1. **Set the safety circle.** Move other people, pets, loose sleeves, and bags outside arm reach before opening the knife. → *Expect:* no one can step into the blade path without being noticed.
2. **Inspect the knife and stick.** Confirm the blade is sharp, secure, and clean, and trim off loose bark by hand if it peels easily. → *Expect:* the tool and wood feel stable before any cutting starts.
3. **Choose the project.** [BRANCH: point | spoon] Pick a simple point for first practice or a shallow spoon bowl if the wood is soft and you have more time. → *Expect:* the cut plan is clear before the blade touches wood.
4. **Cut away from you.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* keep every stroke moving away from your body, legs, and holding hand; never cut toward skin to save a few seconds. → *Expect:* the blade path ends in open air, not in a body part.
5. **Use long controlled strokes.** Brace the stick, slice thin shavings with the grain, and stop before the blade reaches your support hand. → *Expect:* shavings come off smoothly without the knife jumping.
6. **Rotate the work often.** Take small cuts around the stick for a point, or scrape shallow layers from one face for a spoon depression. → *Expect:* the shape develops evenly and no cut digs deep enough to split the stick.
7. **Stop, close, and clean up.** Close or sheath the knife before setting it down, collect shavings, and check hands for nicks before leaving. → *Expect:* the finished stick is shaped, the blade is secured, and the area is clear.

## Decision points

- Dull knife: sharpen it or stop, because dull blades slip when forced.
- Dry brittle wood: switch sticks if the grain tears or splits.
- Younger carver: use direct adult supervision and a smaller project.
- Any cut to skin: stop carving and handle first aid before touching the knife again.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Knife slips:** the blade jumps instead of slicing, stop and reset grip, posture, and cutting direction.
- **F2 Wood splits:** a deep cut follows the grain too far, shorten the project or choose a new stick.
- **F3 Hand gets tired:** control weakens or strokes get jerky, close the knife and take a break.
- **F4 Bleeding nick:** blood appears or skin opens, apply direct pressure, clean the wound, and seek help for deep cuts.

## Verification

The whittling is verified when the stick has the intended simple shape and the knife is closed or sheathed with no uncontrolled cuts or untreated injuries.

## Variations

- `point`: taper evenly from several sides instead of making one flat spear face.
- `spoon`: use scraping cuts for a shallow bowl and avoid deep gouging without proper tools.
- `bark-on`: leave a handle section uncut so the grip stays comfortable.

## Safety & privacy

Knife risk is medium because sharp tools can injure quickly. The non-negotiables are a sharp secure blade, cuts away from people, no carving when distracted or tired, and first-aid supplies within reach.
