## Goal

Make a paper rocket that launches from a straw or by blowing through a tube, then adjust it for distance.

## Preconditions

- Paper, tape, scissors, a pencil, and a straw.
- Adult supervision for young children using scissors.
- A clear indoor or outdoor launch area.
- Agreement to launch away from faces.

## Steps

1. **Roll the body.** Wrap paper around a pencil to make a snug tube, then tape the seam. → *Expect:* the tube slides off the pencil without collapsing.
2. **Seal the nose.** Pinch or fold one end closed and tape it airtight. → *Expect:* air cannot escape through the front.
3. **Trim the tail.** Cut the open end straight so the straw can fit inside. → *Expect:* the rocket sits on the straw without snagging.
4. **Add fins.** Tape three or four small paper triangles near the tail. → *Expect:* fins are evenly spaced and point backward.
5. **Fit the launcher.** Slide the open end over a straw. → *Expect:* the rocket moves freely but does not wobble too much.
6. **Launch safely.** Aim at open space and blow through the straw in one quick breath. → *Expect:* the rocket flies forward.
7. **Measure distance.** Mark where the rocket lands. → *Expect:* each launch has a comparison point.
8. **Adjust the design.** [BRANCH: nose heavy | tail wobbly] shorten tape at the nose, or straighten and resize fins. → *Expect:* flight becomes straighter or farther.
9. **Repeat and compare.** Change only one feature at a time. → *Expect:* the best version is clear from the landing marks.

## Decision points

- The rocket falls quickly → reduce weight or check that the nose is sealed.
- The rocket spins wildly → straighten fins and make them match.
- The rocket sticks on the straw → loosen the body tube slightly.
- Playing indoors → use soft paper and a short launch lane.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Air leak:** detect weak launch despite strong breath → retape the nose and body seam.
- **F2 Crooked fins:** detect immediate turning → align fins parallel to the body.
- **F3 Tube too tight:** detect rocket stuck on the straw → reroll around a slightly larger pencil.
- **F4 Unsafe aim:** detect launches toward faces or breakables → stop and reset the launch direction.

## Verification

The paper rocket leaves the straw, flies through open space, and improves after at least one measured adjustment.

## Variations

- Use a wider straw for larger rockets.
- Add a paper cone nose for a more pointed front.
- Compete for accuracy by aiming at a floor target instead of distance.

## Safety & privacy

Do not launch at faces, screens, lamps, pets, or fragile objects.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* Tape can tear thin paper when removed, so test-fit before sealing every joint.
