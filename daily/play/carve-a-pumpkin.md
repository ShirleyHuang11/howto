## Goal

A pumpkin with a clean-cut lid, an emptied cavity, and a carved face that reads clearly when lit from inside, achieved without hand injuries.

## Preconditions

- A firm, unblemished pumpkin that sits flat without rocking (soft spots mean rot within days).
- A serrated pumpkin saw or a small serrated knife: short blades beat chef's knives, which wedge and slip in thick rind.
- A large metal spoon or ice-cream scoop, a marker, newspaper or a tray for the mess, and a tea light (LED preferred) or candle with a lighter.
- A stable table at working height; carving on the floor or in your lap invites slips.

## Steps

1. **Wash and dry the pumpkin, then plan the face.** Sketch the design on paper first, or tape a printed template to the flattest side. Chunky shapes survive carving; thin spidery lines break out. → *Expect:* a design where every cutout piece is wider than a finger.
2. **Cut the lid at a 45-degree inward angle.** Draw a hexagon around the stem, add a small notch as a realignment key, and saw with the blade angled toward the center so the lid rests on a ledge. A straight vertical cut makes a lid that falls into the cavity. → *Expect:* the lid lifts out with the stem as a handle and re-seats on its ledge without dropping through.
3. **Scoop the cavity completely.** Scrape seeds and strings out with the spoon, then keep scraping the walls. Thin the wall behind the face area to about 2.5 cm; full-thickness rind is hard to cut and blocks light. → *Expect:* the interior wall feels firm and even; the face wall is noticeably thinner than the back.
4. **Transfer the template.** Poke pinholes along the template lines every few millimeters with a pin or poking tool, then remove the paper and connect the dots with the marker if the holes are hard to see. → *Expect:* the full design visible as a dotted outline on the rind.
5. **Cut the face with short saw strokes, smallest pieces first.** Saw perpendicular to the rind surface, moving the saw, not twisting it. Cut interior details before large outer shapes so the surrounding rind stays rigid. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* every cut is permanent; recheck which side of the line is waste before starting each piece. Always cut away from the hand steadying the pumpkin. → *Expect:* each piece separates along the pinhole line with no cracks radiating past it.
6. **Push cut pieces out from the inside.** Reach into the cavity and press outward with a finger or the spoon handle; prying from the front snaps details off. → *Expect:* pieces pop out cleanly; edges are ragged-free enough to read at a distance.
7. **Light it.** [BRANCH: LED tea light (set inside, switch on, lid on) | real candle (place the candle, light it with a long lighter through the face, then cut a 3 cm chimney hole in the lid before seating it)] → *Expect:* the face glows evenly; with a real candle, no smoke stains forming on the lid underside.

## Decision points

- Carving for or with children → children scoop and design; an adult makes every cut, or the child uses only a dedicated blunt pumpkin-saw under direct supervision.
- Pumpkin must last a week or more → carve no earlier than 3 days before display, smear petroleum jelly on all cut edges, and store it cool at night.
- Design has fine detail your saw cannot follow → switch to surface-etching: shave away only the skin so light glows through the flesh instead of cutting through.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lid falls into the cavity:** the cut was too vertical → re-cut the lid rim at a steeper inward angle, or seat the lid on toothpicks pushed into the rim.
- **F2 A feature breaks off (tooth, eyebrow):** pin it back with toothpicks pushed into both broken faces → the repair is invisible once lit.
- **F3 Saw wedges and stops mid-cut:** the blade is being twisted or the wall is too thick → back the blade out along its entry path and thin the wall from inside before continuing.
- **F4 Pumpkin sags or molds within days:** carved too early or stored warm → soak the pumpkin in cold water with a splash of bleach for an hour, dry it, and move it somewhere cool and shaded.
- **F5 Candle keeps going out with the lid on:** no oxygen path → cut the chimney hole in the lid, or leave the lid off entirely.

## Verification

The lid re-seats on its ledge and the notch key aligns; the cavity is scraped clean; the carved face matches the template with no unintended cracks; a light inside makes the design readable from across the room; all fingers uncut.

## Variations

- Bottom-opening method: cut the opening in the base instead of a lid, lift the pumpkin over the candle to light it; the pumpkin also sags later because the structural top stays intact.
- Roast the seeds: rinse the scooped seeds, toss with oil and salt, roast at 180 °C for 12 to 15 minutes.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from blade slips: the classic injury is the steadying hand in the blade path, so keep it behind or beside the cut, never opposite it. Real candles inside a dry vegetable are open flame; never leave one burning unattended or near curtains, and prefer LED lights where children or pets pass by.
