## Goal

Kick up into a controlled handstand with the feet supported by a wall, hold a hollow body shape briefly, and come down without falling onto the head or neck.

## Preconditions

- A clear wall with 2 m of open floor in front of it.
- Dry hands and a non-slippery floor or exercise mat.
- No shoulder, wrist, neck, or dizziness issue that makes inversion unsafe.
- A spotter nearby if this is the first attempt.

## Steps

1. **Clear the landing zone.** Move chairs, shoes, toys, and hard objects away from the wall. → *Expect:* open floor where hands, feet, and a sideways bail can land.
2. **Warm wrists and shoulders.** Circle wrists, press palms to the floor, and do a few plank shoulder rocks. → *Expect:* wrists feel warm, not sharp or pinched.
3. **Place the hands.** Set palms shoulder-width on the floor, fingers spread, fingertips 15 to 25 cm from the wall. → *Expect:* index fingers point roughly forward and both hands press evenly.
4. **Set the kick-up stance.** Put one foot forward and one foot back like a short lunge, eyes between the hands. → *Expect:* shoulders stack over hands before the kick starts.
5. **Kick with control.** Swing the back leg up while the front leg pushes the floor, aiming one heel to touch the wall first. → *Expect:* one foot taps the wall softly instead of slamming.
6. **Bring the second foot to the wall.** Join the feet and press through the shoulders, keeping elbows straight. → *Expect:* both heels rest on the wall and arms feel locked but not hyperextended.
7. **Find hollow body.** Squeeze ribs down, belly tight, glutes on, and toes pointed lightly into the wall. → *Expect:* lower back stops sagging and the body feels like one long line.
8. **Hold briefly.** Breathe for 3 to 10 seconds, pressing the floor away. → *Expect:* hands stay rooted and the wall carries only the feet.
9. **Bail safely.** [BRANCH: step down | cartwheel bail] step one foot down if balanced, or turn one hand and let the legs cartwheel sideways if tipping. → *Expect:* feet return to the floor without the head touching down.
10. **Reset before repeating.** Shake out wrists and wait until breathing is calm. → *Expect:* the next attempt starts with steady hands, not panic.

## Decision points

- If kicking up feels scary → practice donkey kicks with hands on the floor and only brief foot taps.
- If heels slam the wall → move the hands a little farther away and kick with less speed.
- If the back arches hard → squeeze glutes and ribs down, or shorten the hold.
- If you cannot exit sideways → do not continue alone; use a spotter or a softer setup.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Head touches the floor:** detect pressure on the crown or neck → come down immediately and practice plank or pike holds before trying again.
- **F2 Wrists hurt sharply:** detect stabbing pain or numb fingers → stop, stretch gently, and try another day or use parallettes with guidance.
- **F3 Wall impact is loud:** detect heel slam or wall shake → reduce kick force and aim for one-foot contact before both feet.
- **F4 Cannot bail:** detect freezing upside down → bend one knee, turn the hips to the side, and have the spotter guide the legs down.

## Verification

Both hands stayed planted, both feet touched the wall, the body held for at least 3 seconds, and the exit ended on the feet without head, neck, or shoulder impact.

## Variations

- Chest-to-wall: walk feet up the wall from a plank for stricter alignment and less kicking.
- Soft wall: use a padded gym wall when learning to reduce heel impact.
- Partner drill: a spotter holds one ankle and cues when to step down.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from falling, wrist strain, and neck impact. Keep the area clear, avoid fatigue attempts, and stop immediately if dizziness, pain, or fear prevents a controlled bail.
