## Goal

Throw and catch a flying disc with a partner using a flat backhand release and safe two-hand catches.

## Preconditions

- A soft flying disc in good condition.
- Open grass, beach, or park space away from roads, windows, and crowds.
- At least one partner standing 5 to 10 m away.
- Shoes or footing suitable for short steps and turns.

## Steps

1. **Face the target.** Stand sideways enough that the throwing shoulder points across your body. → *Expect:* the partner is visible and the throwing lane is clear.
2. **Grip the disc for backhand.** Put the thumb on top, fingers curled under the rim, and index knuckle near the edge. → *Expect:* the disc feels secure but the wrist can snap.
3. **Hold it level.** Keep the outer edge neither tipped up nor down. → *Expect:* the top of the disc looks like a flat plate.
4. **Aim at chest height.** Pick a point on the partner's torso rather than the sky. → *Expect:* the throw has a clear target and safe height.
5. **Step and pull across.** Step toward the target while drawing the disc from the far side of the body to the release point. → *Expect:* hips and shoulders turn smoothly, not with a wild arm swing.
6. **Release flat with spin.** Snap the wrist and let the disc leave from a level hand. → *Expect:* the disc spins fast and starts on a straight line.
7. **Follow through.** Let the throwing hand continue toward the target. → *Expect:* the arm finishes forward and the body stays balanced.
8. **Prepare to catch.** The receiver bends knees, keeps eyes on the disc, and moves feet before reaching. → *Expect:* the disc arrives in front of the body, not beside the face.
9. **Catch safely.** [BRANCH: pancake catch | two-hand rim catch] clap the disc between palms, or catch opposite rim edges with two hands. → *Expect:* the disc stops without hitting fingers hard.
10. **Reset distance.** Move closer if throws wobble, farther if five throws arrive cleanly. → *Expect:* both players get repeatable catches.

## Decision points

- If the disc climbs and stalls → release flatter with the nose slightly down.
- If it dives immediately → raise the release angle a little and add more spin.
- If wind is strong → throw lower, harder, and more level, or move to a sheltered area.
- If players are mismatched → shorten the distance and use pancake catches first.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wobbly throw:** detect side-to-side flutter → tighten grip and snap the wrist instead of pushing with the palm.
- **F2 Curves hard left or right:** detect a fast hook → check the outer edge angle at release.
- **F3 Finger jam:** detect painful rim impact → catch with two hands in front of the chest and do not stab one hand at the disc.
- **F4 Unsafe lane:** detect people crossing the path → stop throwing until the lane clears.

## Verification

The pair completes five throws where the disc flies mostly flat, reaches catchable chest height, and is caught with two hands or a pancake catch.

## Variations

- Forehand: use two fingers inside the rim and a sideways wrist flick once backhand is reliable.
- Beach play: throw lower because wind lifts the disc.
- Group circle: call the receiver's name before throwing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid throwing near faces, roads, windows, dogs, or people who have not agreed to play. Stop if fingers, shoulders, or footing feel unsafe.
