## Goal

Make a small friendship bracelet pin by threading beads onto a safety pin in a chosen color pattern, closing it securely, and gifting it safely.

## Preconditions

- Safety pins, small beads that fit the pin wire, and a tray or cloth to stop beads rolling away.
- Optional pliers for gently tightening the pin clasp.
- Recipient is old enough to handle pins and small beads safely.
- Pattern idea, initials, color sequence, or simple stripe plan.

## Steps

1. **Plan the pattern.** Lay beads on the tray in the order they will go onto the pin. → *Expect:* the color or letter sequence is visible before threading starts.
2. **Open the safety pin.** Hold the clasp end steady and open the sharp point away from your face and fingers. → *Expect:* the pin is open without poking anyone.
3. **Thread the beads.** Slide beads onto the open wire one at a time, following the planned order. → *Expect:* the beads sit on the pin wire without forcing.
4. **Check spacing.** Leave enough bare wire near the point for the pin to close fully. → *Expect:* beads do not block the clasp.
5. **Close the pin.** Push the point back into the clasp until it clicks or seats firmly. → *Expect:* the sharp point is covered.
6. **Secure if needed.** [BRANCH: snug | loose] If the clasp feels loose, ask an adult to pinch it slightly with pliers; if snug, leave it alone. → *Expect:* the pin stays closed under a gentle tug.
7. **Gift it.** Attach it to fabric only with permission, or place it in a small envelope with a note. → *Expect:* the recipient gets the bracelet pin without surprise pokes or loose beads.

## Decision points

- Beads do not fit: choose larger-hole beads rather than forcing them.
- Pattern includes letters: lay the word backward or forward depending on how it will hang.
- Recipient is very young: make a keychain or supervised decoration instead.
- Pin will go on clothing: avoid delicate fabric that the point could snag.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Beads spill:** beads roll off the table, collect them before anyone steps on or swallows them.
- **F2 Pin will not close:** too many beads block the clasp, remove one or two and try again.
- **F3 Point exposed:** clasp does not cover the sharp end, discard or repair the pin before gifting.
- **F4 Pattern reversed:** letters or colors face the wrong way, reopen carefully and rethread.

## Verification

The bracelet pin is verified when the bead pattern is correct, the clasp fully covers the point, and a gentle shake does not release beads.

## Variations

- `initials`: use letter beads with spacer beads between names.
- `team-colors`: repeat two or three colors in a stripe.
- `set`: make several pins and link them onto a ribbon or bracelet band.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low, but pins and beads are sharp or small. Keep materials away from toddlers and pets, close every pin before gifting, and do not attach one to another person's clothing without consent.
