## Goal

A hidden message is written with simple invisible ink, dried, and revealed safely with adult-controlled heat or a non-heat alternative.

## Preconditions

- Lemon juice, water, a small bowl, cotton swab or paintbrush, and white paper.
- A lamp, warm iron, or other adult-controlled heat source for lemon ink.
- Adult supervision for heat, plus a clear nonflammable surface.

## Steps

1. **Mix the ink.** Combine lemon juice with a few drops of water. [BRANCH: lemon ink | baking-soda ink] use lemon for heat reveal, baking soda for grape-juice reveal. → *Expect:* a pale liquid sits in the bowl.
2. **Write lightly.** Dip a swab and write on white paper without soaking it. → *Expect:* the message is damp and barely visible.
3. **Let it dry fully.** Wait until the paper looks blank and feels dry. → *Expect:* the message disappears unless viewed at an angle.
4. **Prepare the reveal area.** Move flammable clutter away and put the paper on a heat-safe surface. → *Expect:* only the paper and heat tool are in the work zone.
5. **Reveal with gentle heat.** An adult holds the paper near a warm bulb or passes a warm iron over it with no steam. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overheated paper can scorch or burn, so stop at brown letters and never use an open flame. → *Expect:* the writing turns tan before the blank paper does.
6. **Use the other ink if avoiding heat.** For baking-soda ink, brush grape juice over the dry page. → *Expect:* the hidden message changes color against the paper.
7. **Read and preserve.** Let the page cool or dry, then hold it flat. → *Expect:* the message is legible without touching a hot surface.
8. **Clean up.** Rinse bowls and swabs, and discard sticky paper scraps. → *Expect:* no lemon juice or grape juice remains on the table.

## Decision points

- Children are doing the reveal → choose grape juice over heat.
- Paper curls or browns too fast → move the heat farther away.
- Message is too faint → use less water next time or write a second coat after drying.
- Paper is glossy → switch to plain matte paper.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Message visible early:** detect by shiny or yellow marks after drying → use less liquid and lighter strokes.
- **F2 Paper scorches:** detect by smoke or dark brown patches → stop heating and discard the sheet safely.
- **F3 Ink spreads:** detect by fuzzy letters → use a drier swab and thicker paper.
- **F4 Reveal fails:** detect by no color change → warm more slowly or remake with stronger lemon juice.

## Verification

The dried page first appears blank, then reveals a readable message after the chosen reveal method.

## Variations

- Milk ink: works with gentle heat but can smell if overheated.
- Vinegar ink: similar to lemon juice, usually fainter.
- Secret-map version: draw landmarks first in pencil, then hide the route in ink.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because heat and paper are involved. An adult should control heat, open flames are out, and private messages should not be treated as secure encryption.
