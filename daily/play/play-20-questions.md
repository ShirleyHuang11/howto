## Goal

Play 20 Questions with clear yes-or-no answers, honest clues, and a broad-to-narrow guessing strategy.

## Preconditions

- Two or more players.
- One player willing to think of a thing without changing it.
- Agreement that only yes, no, or sometimes counts as an answer.
- A way to count questions up to 20.

## Steps

1. **Pick the thing.** The answer keeper silently chooses a real or agreed imaginary person, place, object, or idea. → *Expect:* one fixed answer exists.
2. **Set the category if desired.** [BRANCH: open game | category game] play with anything, or announce animal, vegetable, mineral, person, place, or object. → *Expect:* everyone knows the search space.
3. **Ask broad first.** Start with questions that split many possibilities, such as living, human-made, bigger than a chair, or found indoors. → *Expect:* large groups of answers are removed.
4. **Track the count.** Count every yes-or-no question, but do not count clarifying repeats caused by mishearing. → *Expect:* the group knows how many questions remain.
5. **Narrow by traits.** Ask about size, use, location, material, time period, or category. → *Expect:* the answer space becomes small enough to guess.
6. **Answer honestly.** The keeper gives the best truthful answer and says "sometimes" only when yes or no would mislead. → *Expect:* clues remain fair and consistent.
7. **Make a candidate list.** After about ten questions, name two or three likely answers before spending a guess. → *Expect:* players compare possibilities instead of guessing wildly.
8. **Use the final guess.** Ask "Is it..." when the evidence points to one answer. → *Expect:* the keeper confirms a win or says no.
9. **Reveal and rotate.** If the group fails after 20 questions, reveal the thing and choose a new keeper. → *Expect:* the next round starts with a new answer.

## Decision points

- The answer could be interpreted two ways → the keeper should choose the common meaning and stay consistent.
- A question asks two things at once → split it into one yes-or-no question.
- Children are playing → allow category hints before the first question.
- Players want a harder game → count every guess as a question.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Moving target:** detect the keeper changing details midgame → restart with a fixed answer.
- **F2 Non-yes-no question:** detect "what color is it" or similar → rephrase into a yes-or-no form.
- **F3 Bad count:** detect disagreement about remaining questions → use the lower remaining number or restart the count next round.
- **F4 Contradictory answer:** detect a new answer conflicting with an old one → clarify the intended meaning and replace the conflicting response.

## Verification

The round ends with either a correct guess within 20 counted questions or a revealed answer after the twentieth question.

## Variations

- Reverse 20 Questions lets the group answer while one player guesses.
- Theme rounds limit answers to movies, foods, animals, or classroom topics.
- Silent version uses thumbs up, thumbs down, and sideways for sometimes.

## Safety & privacy

Avoid choosing private information about real people as the hidden answer.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* A revealed answer cannot be hidden again for that round, so reveal only when the round is over.
