## Goal

You can play a fair thumb war with the standard grip, chant, pin, and agreed rules.

## Preconditions

- Two willing players with clean hands and no thumb or wrist injury.
- A small table or standing space where arms can rest comfortably.
- Agreement that it is a playful contest, not a strength test.

## Steps

1. **Make the grip.** Face your opponent, hook the four fingers of your right hands together, and leave thumbs upright. → *Expect:* both thumbs can move freely above the joined hands.
2. **Set fair rules.** Agree that elbows stay still, finger hooks do not squeeze painfully, and a pin must last three counts. → *Expect:* both players know what counts before the round starts.
3. **Say the chant.** Move thumbs side to side while saying, "One, two, three, four, I declare a thumb war." → *Expect:* both thumbs begin from neutral after the chant.
4. **Probe for position.** Circle, feint, and lift your thumb to tempt the other thumb into reaching. → *Expect:* the contest is timing-based, not just pushing.
5. **Go for the pin.** Press your thumb across the opponent's thumb when it extends too far. [BRANCH: clean pin | disputed pin] Count aloud for clean pins; reset disputed pins. → *Expect:* a clear hold starts the count.
6. **Hold for three.** Keep steady pressure without twisting the wrist or squeezing fingers. → *Expect:* the opponent's thumb stays pinned through "one, two, three."
7. **Reset politely.** Release immediately after a win or a stop call, shake out hands, and offer another round. → *Expect:* both players want to keep playing.

## Decision points

- If players disagree on the pin count, restart the round with slower counting.
- If one player has shorter thumbs, allow left-hand rematches or best-of-three rounds.
- If anyone feels pain, stop; winning does not matter.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Finger squeeze:** the grip becomes painful → loosen the hooked fingers and restart.
- **F2 Moving elbow:** a player gains leverage by shifting the whole arm → anchor elbows or play seated.
- **F3 Fast count argument:** the pin count is rushed → count in full spoken beats.
- **F4 Wrist twist:** pressure bends the joint sideways → release and reset with thumbs only.

## Verification

A round begins with the chant, one player pins the other's thumb for a clear three-count, and both players agree the grip stayed fair.

## Variations

- Best two out of three reduces lucky wins.
- Left-handed rounds even out a dominant-hand advantage.
- Tournament play can rotate opponents, but keep rounds short to avoid sore thumbs.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wash hands when appropriate, do not play with injured fingers or wrists, stop at pain, and avoid using recorded videos of players without consent.
