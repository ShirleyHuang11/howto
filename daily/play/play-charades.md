## Goal

A group has played several rounds of charades with fair teams, shared gesture conventions, a working timer, and a score both teams accept.

## Preconditions

- 4 or more people (two teams of 2+; with exactly 3-4, play free-for-all where one acts and everyone guesses).
- Paper slips and pens, a bowl or hat, and any timer (phone timer is fine; 60-90 seconds per turn).
- Space where the actor can stand and be seen by all guessers.

## Steps

1. **Split into two balanced teams.** Balance by mixing ages and the naturally theatrical with the reserved, not by friendship clusters. → *Expect:* two teams within one person of each other in size, neither stacked with all the extroverts.
2. **Agree on categories and write slips.** Standard fair set: movies, TV shows, books, songs, famous people. Each player writes 3-5 slips for the OTHER team's bowl. Rule for fairness: if the writer doubts half the acting team would recognize the answer, it does not go in. → *Expect:* two bowls of folded slips; no inside jokes, no obscure picks meant to sabotage.
3. **Teach the gesture conventions before round one.** These are the standard signs and skipping this briefing is the number one cause of chaotic games: category signs (movies: crank an old camera; TV: draw a rectangle; book: open palms; song: hand to mouth like singing; person: hands on hips), then hold up fingers for word count, then fingers again for which word, fingers on forearm for syllables, tug your earlobe for "sounds like", pinch fingers for "small word" (a, the, of), beckoning hands for "keep going, warmer", wave-off for "no, cold". → *Expect:* every player can produce the category signs and the "sounds like" ear tug on request.
4. **State the two hard rules.** The actor may not speak, mouth words, or point at objects in the room; guessers may say anything and shout freely. → *Expect:* verbal agreement, especially from first-timers who will instinctively mouth words.
5. **Run a turn.** Actor draws a slip (no swaps unless the group pre-agreed one free swap per game), timer starts when they signal the category. Actor plays only to their own team. → *Expect:* the turn opens with category sign then word count, before any acting; teams that skip the framing waste half the clock.
6. **Score the turn.** [BRANCH: guessed before the buzzer → 1 point, slip discarded | timer expires → no point, and the slip is read aloud so everyone learns what it was] → *Expect:* score written down immediately; disputed "they said it at the buzzer" calls go to the timer holder, whose call is final.
7. **Alternate teams and rotate actors.** Every player acts before anyone acts twice; nobody may opt out of acting entirely (shy players can take slips late in the game once the room is warm). → *Expect:* actor rotation visibly fair; the same ham is not up every other turn.
8. **End at an agreed line.** Play to a point target (first to 10) or until the bowls empty, then total the score. → *Expect:* a final score both teams heard announced, and slips cleaned up.

## Decision points

- Big age spread in the room → add a "seen by everyone" filter to slip writing, or split categories (kids' bowl and adults' bowl) with matched turns.
- A team argues a slip was unguessable → the slip's writer must act it out for their own team next turn; this rule, announced up front, self-polices slip difficulty.
- Group over ~12 → three teams rotating, or two simultaneous games; waits longer than 5 minutes between turns kill the energy.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Actor talks or mouths words:** guessers or the other team catch it → dead slip, no point, next team plays; enforce gently the first time, firmly after.
- **F2 Endless silent flailing:** actor freezes mid-turn with 30 s left → teammates call for structure: "category again! which word?"; the convention set from step 3 is the recovery tool.
- **F3 Slip is illegible or blank:** actor cannot read the draw → discard, draw again, restart the timer; no penalty.
- **F4 One player dominates guessing:** teammates go passive → institute "first guess from someone new each clue" informally, or seat the dominator as timer holder for a round.

## Verification

Every player acted at least once, all turns were timed with the same limit, the score sheet totals match the announced winner, and no slip disputes are still open.

## Variations

- Reverse charades: the whole team acts while one person guesses; louder, better for parties and kids.
- Themed bowls: one topic only (all holiday movies, all office in-jokes); fun for close groups, unfair for mixed ones.
- No-props extension for hard mode: ban the "sounds like" ear tug so everything must be acted literally.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the acting space clear of coffee tables and drinks (actors lunge). Keep slips kind: no slips that mock a person in the room, and in mixed company keep the topics ones everyone would happily act in front of their family.
