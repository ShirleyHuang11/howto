## Goal

A group plays the license plate game on a trip with clear rules, fair scoring, and enough variants to keep the back seat from becoming a courtroom.

## Preconditions

- A road trip, parking-lot wait, or other passenger situation.
- At least one player who is not driving.
- Paper, notes app, or memory good enough for the chosen scoring.

## Steps

1. **Pick the target.** [BRANCH: states game | alphabet game | numbers game] choose states for long trips, alphabet for shorter drives, numbers for heavy traffic. → *Expect:* everyone knows what counts.
2. **Set the driver rule.** The driver may call plates only when safe, or may be the judge instead of a player. → *Expect:* no one asks the driver to stare at bumpers.
3. **Define a valid spot.** Count only real license plates seen with eyes, not remembered sightings or plates in ads. → *Expect:* every point comes from the current trip.
4. **Call it clearly.** Say the state, letter, or number out loud before another player does. → *Expect:* the group hears the claim.
5. **Record contested finds.** Write down rare states or hard letters immediately. → *Expect:* the score does not depend on heroic memory.
6. **Use fair scoring.** One point per new state, letter, or number, with ties allowed for simultaneous calls. → *Expect:* arguments end at the rule, not at volume.
7. **Add variants when needed.** Try first-to-Z, all states, vanity plate phrases, or team play. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* unsafe distraction cannot be undone, so passengers handle spotting and recording while the driver drives. → *Expect:* the game stays lively without becoming chaotic.
8. **Declare the finish.** End at the destination, rest stop, or completed list. → *Expect:* a winner or shared completed list is recognized.

## Decision points

- Traffic is too dense → switch to alphabet instead of state rarity.
- Players are different ages → use teams or let younger players count partial matches.
- Someone claims a plate no one saw → require one witness or written proof for rare finds.
- Night or bad weather reduces visibility → pause the game or loosen the target.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Driver distraction:** detect by missed road attention → driver stops playing immediately.
- **F2 Duplicate scoring:** detect by the same state counted twice → keep a written checklist.
- **F3 Rule drift:** detect by recurring arguments → restate the chosen variant and restart scoring from the last agreed point.
- **F4 Player boredom:** detect by long silence → switch variants or play in teams.

## Verification

At the agreed endpoint, the group has a checklist or accepted score produced under the rules chosen at the start.

## Variations

- State hunt: try for all states or provinces over a whole vacation.
- Alphabet hunt: find plate letters A through Z in order.
- Cooperative mode: the car wins together when the list fills.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not photograph plates unnecessarily, do not harass other drivers, and keep the driver focused on driving rather than scoring.
