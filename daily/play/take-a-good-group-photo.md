## Goal

A group photo where everyone is visible, lit, and has their eyes open, captured in under two minutes of wrangling — because the photographer's real job is logistics, not artistry.

## Preconditions

- A phone or camera with a charged battery and space, and a group willing to pause for ninety seconds.
- The one lighting fact that decides everything: faces toward the light, sun behind the *camera* (or to its side), never behind the group. Backlit groups become silhouettes with teeth.

## Steps

1. **Pick the spot for light and background, and announce it with authority.** Open shade or soft light beats harsh noon sun (squints) and beats backlight (silhouettes); background two steps behind the group, free of bins, strangers, and poles that grow from heads. Groups follow confident pointing. → *Expect:* the group assembling where you said, light on their faces, clean background.
2. **Arrange by rows in seconds, not minutes.** Taller behind, shorter and seated in front, nobody hidden: the caller's line is "if you can't see the camera, it can't see you." Tighten gaps ("everyone one step in") — groups always stand too far apart, and closeness reads as warmth in the print. → *Expect:* two loose rows, every face with a clear line to the lens, shoulders angled slightly inward.
3. **Frame with margin and hold the camera straight.** Everyone fully in frame with headroom and elbow room (cropping later beats amputating now), horizon level, camera at the front row's face height — not a low angle, which flatters no one in a group. → *Expect:* the whole group plus margin in a level frame.
4. **Run the countdown protocol and take a burst.** Loud and scripted: "Looking here — three, two, one!" and shoot a burst or three quick frames on and after "one." Blinks cluster on the count; the frame after the laugh that follows is often the best photo of the set. → *Expect:* several frames per pose; at least one with all eyes open (the burst is the blink insurance).
5. **Check the review honestly before releasing the group.** Zoom the faces: eyes, focus, nobody mid-sentence. Retake immediately if needed — "one more, Maya blinked!" costs ten seconds now and is impossible tomorrow. → *Expect:* a verified keeper before the group dissolves.
6. **Get the photographer into one.** Swap shooters, use the timer (camera propped or on a ledge, ten seconds, run), or hand the phone to a passerby with the frame pre-composed ("just press this"). The person who organized the photo belongs in the photo. → *Expect:* at least one frame containing everyone, including you.
7. **Share while the group is still assembled.** The group chat drop or airdrop in the moment; the photo everyone waits three weeks for is the photo nobody gets. → *Expect:* the photo in everyone's possession before the goodbyes.

## Decision points

- Big groups (15+): elevation is the cheat — shoot from a chair, steps, or a slope so back rows are faces, not hats; three rows maximum before you need stairs.
- Harsh sun and no shade: sun behind the camera at an angle, or embrace the backlight *deliberately* with exposure tapped on the faces (phones handle this better than folklore says).
- Kids and dogs in the group: shoot the entire time, not on the count; the burst-everything approach is the only strategy with a success rate.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Every frame has one blinker:** the burst was skipped; re-run with the count-then-burst, or the classic fix — "everyone close your eyes... open on three!"
- **F2 Backlit silhouettes discovered later:** rotate the whole arrangement 180 degrees next time; for the rescued frame, phone editors' shadow-lift salvages more than expected.
- **F3 The passerby took one blurry frame of the ground:** pre-compose, lock focus/exposure if the phone allows, mime the button, and ask for "a few"; choose passersby holding their own camera — photographers over-deliver.
- **F4 Someone hates the photo of themselves:** take the "safety" second pose (everyone's choice: one nice, one silly) — the silly one loosens faces for a retake of the nice one, and doubles as the better memory anyway.

## Verification

Faces lit and visible, background clean, at least one all-eyes-open frame verified by zoom before the group dispersed, the photographer appears in one, and the file left your phone into the group's hands.

## Variations

- Formal events: same protocol with a list (couple + each family cluster) run efficiently; the announcer role matters more than the camera.
- Self-timer-only solo groups (tripods, ledges): compose, mark standing spots, ten-second timer, walk don't run, and take three rounds.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Two courtesies with weight: ask before posting identifiable photos of others (the group's photo is the group's, not the internet's by default), and mind what backgrounds reveal (house numbers, kids' school logos) before public sharing.
