## Goal

You can perform a basic skateboard ollie on flat ground: pop the tail, slide the front foot, level the board, and land over the bolts.

## Preconditions

- A skateboard in good condition with tight enough trucks for learning.
- Helmet, wrist guards, knee pads, and elbow pads.
- Flat smooth pavement away from traffic, crowds, curbs, and downhill slopes.
- Basic comfort standing, rolling slowly, and stepping off the board.

## Steps

1. **Gear up and inspect.** Put on pads and helmet, check wheels, trucks, deck cracks, and shoelaces. → *Expect:* nothing loose, sharp, or likely to catch.
2. **Set the stance.** Back foot on the tail pocket, front foot behind the front bolts, shoulders aligned with the board. → *Expect:* knees bend while weight stays centered.
3. **Practice the pop stationary.** Press the tail sharply down with the back foot while jumping up, letting the board snap against the ground. → *Expect:* a crisp pop sound without leaning backward.
4. **Slide the front foot.** As the board rises, drag the outside edge of the front shoe up toward the nose. → *Expect:* grip tape catches the shoe and lifts the board's front.
5. **Level the board.** Tuck knees, guide the nose forward, and let the back foot rise so the tail can come up. → *Expect:* the deck flattens in the air, even if only briefly.
6. **Land over the bolts.** Bring both feet down above the truck bolts with knees bent and shoulders still aligned. → *Expect:* the board lands flat instead of shooting out.
7. **Try tiny rolling ollies.** [BRANCH: stationary stable | stationary unstable] If stable, roll at walking speed; if unstable, repeat stationary reps and step-offs. → *Expect:* the motion stays the same while moving slowly.
8. **Stop before sloppy reps.** End the session when legs are tired or fear makes you stiff. → *Expect:* practice ends with controlled attempts, not crashes.

## Decision points

- If the board shoots forward, your shoulders or feet are behind the board.
- If the tail does not rise, the front foot slide is stopping early or the back foot is pinning the tail down.
- If you cannot step off safely at rolling speed, delay moving ollies.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Credit-card slip:** the board shoots out and you fall backward → keep weight centered and practice on grass or carpet for pop mechanics.
- **F2 No pop:** the tail presses instead of snaps → jump first, then pop as your weight leaves the board.
- **F3 Rocket ollie:** nose rises but tail drags → slide the front foot up and forward, and lift the back knee.
- **F4 Hard slam:** pain, head hit, or wrist impact → stop, assess injury, and do not resume after a head impact without medical guidance.

## Verification

On flat open ground with protective gear, you pop the board, get all four wheels off the ground, land with both feet over the bolts, and roll away.

## Variations

- Grass or carpet reduces rolling and helps isolate the pop, but it changes board feel.
- Holding a rail can teach timing, but release it before counting a real ollie.
- Once consistent, practice over a painted line before trying objects.

## Safety & privacy

High risk: falls can injure wrists, elbows, knees, and head. Wear a helmet and pads, avoid traffic and hills, never practice near pedestrians, and stop after any head hit, sharp pain, or equipment failure.
