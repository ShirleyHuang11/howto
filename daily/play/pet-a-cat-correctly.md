## Goal

A cat petted on its own terms: consent obtained, the approved zones serviced, the warning signs read, and the interaction ended before the famous sudden bite, leaving both parties wanting more.

## Preconditions

- A cat whose petting is plausibly on the table: relaxed posture, in shared space by choice. A cat mid-meal, mid-groom, cornered, or watching birds has declined in advance.
- Your acceptance of the core doctrine: cats issue revocable licenses, not blanket permissions. The entire skill is reading the license's current status.

## Steps

1. **Offer the greeting finger.** Crouch or sit at the cat's level, extend one relaxed finger or knuckle at nose height, half a meter away, and wait. This mimics cat-to-cat nose greeting and hands the decision over. → *Expect:* the cat approaches and sniffs, or does not. No approach is a complete answer; petting does not proceed by ambush.
2. **Let the cat cast the first vote.** After the sniff: a cheek or body rub against your hand is a yes; walking away, a frozen stare, or retreating ears are a no. → *Expect:* an explicit yes before any petting begins.
3. **Pet the approved zones only, with the fur, briefly.** Cheeks, under the chin, base of the ears, and the head's crown are the consensus zones; short strokes in the fur's direction. The belly, tail, paws, and (for many cats) the back near the tail are advanced-license territory that the default license does not include, regardless of how invitingly the belly is displayed — the belly roll is a trust signal, not a request. → *Expect:* leaning-in, purring, slow blinks, a tail held soft; all green.
4. **Watch the license status continuously.** The revocation warnings arrive in order: tail starting to swish or thump, skin rippling along the back, ears rotating sideways/back, a sudden stillness, dilated pupils, a head swiveled toward your hand. → *Expect:* you catching the *first* sign, not the third; the bite everyone calls sudden was announced twice.
5. **Pause at the first warning and let the cat re-vote.** Hand still or withdrawn a few centimeters; the cat resumes contact (license renewed) or disengages (license expired). → *Expect:* an exit with zero teeth involved; ending early is what makes you the favorite visitor.
6. **Close on a high note.** A last chin scratch during clear enjoyment, then stop while it is good; optionally the slow-blink farewell (look at the cat, blink slowly; a returned slow blink is the species' handshake). → *Expect:* a cat that regards you as competent, which among cats is the whole social ladder.

## Decision points

- Unknown cats outdoors: add a check for collar/ear-tip context, skip pick-up ambitions entirely, and be strict about step 1; street cats grade harder.
- Kids and cats: teach the greeting finger and one-hand-only rule before contact; supervise the belly temptation directly, since it is irresistible and always wrong.
- Kittens: licenses are chaotic and teeth are recreational; redirect play-bites to a toy, never to fingers, or the adult cat inherits the habit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bitten or swatted anyway:** withdraw calmly (yanking triggers the grab reflex; press slightly *toward* the bite instead, then remove), no scolding — the warnings were missed, and the cat regards the matter as correctly adjudicated. Broken skin: wash well and take cat bites seriously (`daily/self-care/treat-a-minor-cut`; punctures that swell or redden see a doctor promptly — cat bites infect at high rates).
- **F2 Overstimulation spiral (was purring, now thrashing):** the license expired mid-purr, which is legal; end the session entirely rather than renegotiating.
- **F3 The cat never approaches:** correct outcome, not failure; remain seated, ignore the cat conspicuously, and there is a fair chance it appears in your lap by minute twenty, cats being contrarians of documented record.
- **F4 Allergic party in the room:** hands washed after contact and off the face; the cat's opinion of the sneezing is not recorded here.

## Verification

Consent preceded contact, only default-license zones were petted, the first warning sign paused the session, the interaction ended with the cat willing to return, and no skin was broken on either species.

## Variations

- Dogs invert several rules (many love the chest and rump; consent still applies, hand offered low); the greeting-and-read-continuously skeleton transfers across species and is the actual lesson.
- Therapy and shelter contexts formalize all of this; shelter volunteers will recognize every step above as day-one training.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk concentrated in one fact: cat bites are medically serious out of proportion to their size — wash immediately and escalate swelling to a doctor. Everything else is diplomacy, and the cat is better at it than you; the recipe just narrows the gap.
