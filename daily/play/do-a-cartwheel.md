## Goal

Perform a controlled beginner cartwheel with a hand-hand-foot-foot rhythm and a safe landing.

## Preconditions

- A clear patch of grass, mat, or soft floor with no furniture nearby.
- Comfortable clothes and shoes with grip, or bare feet on a mat.
- Basic ability to support body weight through straight arms for a moment.

## Steps

1. **Choose the direction.** Stand sideways to the travel line and pick the lead leg that feels natural in a lunge. → *Expect:* one foot points forward along the line and the other foot stays behind.
2. **Mark the hand targets.** Imagine two handprints on the line, shoulder-width apart, just past the lead foot. → *Expect:* you know where the first hand and second hand will land.
3. **Practice the rhythm standing low.** Say hand, hand, foot, foot while touching the ground lightly and stepping through. → *Expect:* the sequence feels like a sideways wheel, not a jump straight up.
4. **Start in a deep lunge.** Bend the front knee, raise both arms, and look toward the first hand target. → *Expect:* weight is forward but controlled, with the back leg ready to swing.
5. **Place the first hand.** Reach down with the lead-side hand while the back leg begins to kick up. → *Expect:* first palm lands flat on the line and the hips start to lift.
6. **Place the second hand.** Put the second palm down past the first, keeping arms straight and shoulders strong. → *Expect:* both hands briefly carry weight while legs pass overhead or at a low diagonal.
7. **Land the first foot.** Let the first kicked leg come down on the far side of the line. → *Expect:* the first landing foot touches down before the second foot.
8. **Land the second foot and stand.** Step the second foot down, lift the chest, and finish facing the opposite direction. → *Expect:* the hand-hand-foot-foot rhythm completes without a collapse.
9. **Add height gradually.** Kick a little higher only when the low version stays straight and quiet. → *Expect:* legs rise more without changing the order of contact.

## Decision points

- Fear of going upside down → keep the first attempts low, like a side donkey kick, and use a spotter.
- Wrist discomfort → stop and try a padded mat, wider hands, or wrist warmups before another attempt.
- Body twists forward → point the belly button sideways longer and place both hands on the same line.
- Crowded space → do not attempt it; move to grass or a gym mat.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bent-arm collapse:** detect when elbows buckle, recover by practicing straight-arm weight shifts before trying again.
- **F2 Feet land together:** detect when the rhythm becomes hand-hand-both-feet, recover by saying the rhythm aloud and slowing the kick.
- **F3 Crooked travel path:** detect when you drift off the line, recover by drawing or imagining a straight line for hands and feet.
- **F4 Hard landing:** detect from a loud foot slap or knee jolt, recover by bending knees slightly and lowering the kick height.

## Verification

Hands and feet touch in the order hand, hand, foot, foot, and you finish upright without pain or falling.

## Variations

- Spotter version: a helper stands behind the waist and guides the hips over the hands.
- Wall drill: practice kicking legs up near a wall before trying a full pass.
- Gymnastics version: pointed toes and straighter legs come after the safe rhythm is consistent.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from falls, wrist strain, and collisions. Practice on grass or mats first, keep the area clear, and stop if wrists, head, neck, or knees hurt.
