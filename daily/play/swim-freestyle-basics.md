## Goal

You can swim a short length of basic freestyle with a horizontal body, steady kick, reaching pull, body rotation, and controlled side breathing.

## Preconditions

- A lifeguarded pool, a swim instructor or competent supervisor, and water shallow enough to stand in during drills.
- Comfort putting your face in the water and returning to standing.
- Goggles that fit, plus a lane or open area without crossing swimmers.
- Do not use this recipe to learn in open water, moving water, or alone.

## Steps

1. **Start with pool safety.** Enter shallow water, identify the wall and ladder, and agree with your supervisor when to stop a drill. → *Expect:* you know exactly how to stand, grab the wall, or get help.
2. **Set the body line.** Push gently from the wall with arms extended, face down, eyes toward the pool floor, and body long. → *Expect:* a brief glide with hips near the surface.
3. **Add a small kick.** Kick from the hips with loose knees and relaxed ankles, making small bubbles rather than big splashes. → *Expect:* forward movement without legs dropping.
4. **Learn the reach-and-pull.** One arm reaches forward, catches water with the forearm, pulls past the ribs, then exits near the hip. Switch arms slowly. → *Expect:* each pull moves water backward and your body forward.
5. **Rotate the body.** Roll the ribs and hips slightly with each reach, as if each shoulder takes a turn pointing upward. → *Expect:* the recovering arm clears the water more easily.
6. **Practice side breathing at the wall.** Hold the wall, face down, exhale bubbles, rotate one ear toward the shoulder, sip air, then return face down. → *Expect:* breath happens by rotation, not by lifting the head.
7. **Swim with a breathing pattern.** [BRANCH: breathe every 2 strokes | breathe every 3 strokes] Use every 2 for oxygen comfort, or every 3 for bilateral practice. → *Expect:* the rhythm stays calm for 5 to 10 m.
8. **Connect the short swim.** Push off, kick, alternate pulls, rotate, breathe to the side, and stand before form collapses. → *Expect:* a short freestyle segment that ends under control.

## Decision points

- If you lift your head to breathe, return to wall breathing and side-kick drills.
- If you feel breathless, shorten the swim and exhale steadily underwater; holding air is the common trap.
- If the lane is crowded or supervision is unavailable, do land review or postpone water practice.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sinking legs:** head lifts and hips drop → look down, press the chest slightly into the water, and kick smaller.
- **F2 Windmill arms:** hands slap and pull shallow → slow down and feel the forearm catch water before pulling.
- **F3 Gasp breathing:** air exchange arrives late → exhale bubbles continuously before turning to inhale.
- **F4 Deep-water drift:** the drill moves where standing is impossible → stop at the wall and reset in shallow water.

## Verification

In a supervised pool, you swim 10 m of freestyle, breathe to the side at least twice, and stop by standing or holding the wall.

## Variations

- A kickboard can isolate the kick, but do not let it teach head-up swimming as the default.
- Side-kick drill improves rotation: one arm extended, one arm at the side, body rolled slightly.
- Bilateral breathing is useful for symmetry, while single-side breathing is acceptable for short comfort swims.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because drowning can be silent and fast. Learn supervised in a pool, stay where you can stand while drilling, follow lifeguard rules, leave the water when tired, and never practice breath-holding games.
