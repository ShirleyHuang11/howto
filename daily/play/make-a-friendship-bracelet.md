## Goal

A finished candy-stripe friendship bracelet: even diagonal stripes, tight consistent knots, and a tie-off that fits the wearer's wrist without falling off.

## Preconditions

- Embroidery floss (cotton, 6-strand) in 3-4 colors. Yarn is too stretchy and fuzzy; use real floss.
- Scissors, and a way to anchor the work: tape on a table edge, a safety pin to a cushion or your jeans, or a clipboard.
- The one knot to know: the forward knot, which is simply a double half-hitch (the same "4 shape" knot made twice). Everything below is that knot repeated.

## Steps

1. **Cut and anchor the strands.** Cut one strand per color, each about the length of your fingertip-to-shoulder (roughly 60-70 cm). Gather them, tie a single overhand knot 8-10 cm from one end, and anchor above the knot. → *Expect:* strands hang free below a firm anchor; the 8-10 cm tail above the knot is your future tie, do not trim it.
2. **Order the colors and keep that order.** Arrange strands left to right in your stripe sequence (e.g. red, yellow, green, blue). The leftmost color is about to become the first stripe. → *Expect:* strands separated, not crossed, in a sequence you could re-derive if they tangle.
3. **Learn the forward knot on the first pair.** Hold strand 2 taut with your right hand (the taut strand is the core, this is the secret of even bracelets). With strand 1, make a "4" over strand 2, pass the end through, pull up snug to the top. Then make the identical knot again on the same strand. → *Expect:* two half-hitches = one forward knot; a small bump in strand 1's color sits at the top, and strand 1 now hangs to the RIGHT of strand 2.
4. **Carry the working strand across the row.** Same working strand (old strand 1) makes a forward knot over strand 3, then strand 4. Always double-knot; a single half-hitch flips the colors and is the classic beginner bug. → *Expect:* a full diagonal row of one color; the working strand has traveled to the far right and retires there.
5. **Repeat rows with each new leftmost strand.** The new leftmost color knots across everything. Keep tension constant: taut core strand, knots pulled snug but not strangled, every knot slid up flush against the row above. → *Expect:* diagonal stripes cycling through your color order; edges straight, no gaps (loose) and no curling corkscrew (too tight, see F2).
6. **Check length against the wrist.** Knot until the striped band wraps the wrist with a finger's width of slack. Band-only length is typically 14-15 cm for adults, 12-13 cm for kids. → *Expect:* band circles the wrist comfortably; ties are extra and not counted.
7. **Tie off into a braid.** Tie one overhand knot flush against the last row, then braid the loose strands for 5-7 cm and finish with another overhand knot. Do the same to the starting tail if you left it loose. → *Expect:* a braid on each end that will not unravel; trim strand ends 1 cm past the final knots.
8. **Attach to the wrist.** [BRANCH: permanent (square knot: right-over-left then left-over-right, trim ends; friendship-bracelet tradition is you wear it until it falls off) | removable (loop-and-knot closure: fold the starting tail into a loop when you begin, then pass the end braid through it and knot)] ⚠️ *Irreversible:* the permanent square knot plus trimming; confirm fit and the wearer's consent to the "until it falls off" deal before cutting. → *Expect:* bracelet on the wrist, snug over the wrist bone plus one finger of slack, staying put when the hand shakes.

## Decision points

- First bracelet ever → use exactly 3 colors and thicker floss; more strands multiply every mistake and 3-strand rows build the muscle memory fastest.
- Strand runs short mid-bracelet (one color always depletes fastest since every strand works equally, this usually means uneven starting cuts) → tie on a new strand with a tight double knot positioned on the back face, keep knotting, hide the ends under later rows.
- Want chevrons or diamonds next → learn the backward knot (mirror image "P" shape) after candy stripe is solid; patterns are just choreographed forward/backward knots.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stripes flip color mid-row:** a bump shows the core color instead of the working color → you made a single half-hitch somewhere; unpick back to the flip with a pin and re-knot doubles.
- **F2 Corkscrew curl:** the band twists into a spiral → knots are too tight; ease tension going forward and steam or damp-press the finished band flat under a book overnight.
- **F3 Tangled hanging strands:** rows slow to a crawl → detangle every few rows before knots form; butterfly-bobbin the long ends (small bundles secured with a loose loop) on your next bracelet.
- **F4 Uneven width:** band swells and narrows → tension drift between sessions; before resuming, redo one row consciously matching the snugness of the row above.

## Verification

The band shows unbroken diagonal stripes in the planned color cycle with no color flips, lies flat without corkscrewing, fits the wrist with about one finger of slack, and both ends are braided and knotted so nothing unravels after a gentle tug test.

## Variations

- Loop-and-knot start: begin by folding all strands in half around an anchor loop; gives the removable closure from step 8 automatically.
- Wider band: 6-8 strands (colors may repeat); same knot, longer rows, noticeably slower per row.
- Ankle version: add 4-6 cm of band length and choose the removable closure; permanent knots on ankles snag on socks.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Scissors are the only sharp object. Do not attach permanent bracelets tightly on small children (growth and circulation; leave real slack), and never let long floss strands near babies or pets unattended.
