## Goal

A yo-yo is set to the right string length, thrown into a sleeper, returned with a gravity pull, and optionally walked like a tiny obedient wheel.

## Preconditions

- A working yo-yo with a string loop.
- Clear floor space away from faces, lamps, and drinks.
- A string that can be retied if it is too long.

## Steps

1. **Set string length.** Stand the yo-yo on the floor and bring the string to your belly button, tying a new finger loop there if needed. [BRANCH: long string | short string] shorten long string, leave short string for now. → *Expect:* the loop reaches about navel height.
2. **Wear the loop correctly.** Slip the loop over your middle finger between the first and second knuckle. → *Expect:* the string tightens on your finger without cutting circulation.
3. **Wind the yo-yo.** Wrap the string neatly until the yo-yo is full. → *Expect:* the yo-yo hangs from your hand without loose loops.
4. **Throw the sleeper.** Hold palm up, snap the yo-yo downward, and let it spin at the end of the string. → *Expect:* the yo-yo spins in place instead of climbing back immediately.
5. **Wake it with a tug.** Flick your wrist upward for a gravity pull. → *Expect:* the yo-yo climbs back to your hand.
6. **Try walk the dog.** Throw a sleeper, lower the spinning yo-yo gently onto smooth floor, and let it roll forward. → *Expect:* the yo-yo creeps ahead while still spinning.
7. **Call it back.** Lift the yo-yo off the floor and tug upward before it slows too much. → *Expect:* it returns to your hand, perhaps with comic dignity.
8. **Reset the wind.** Rewind neatly before the next attempt. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* throwing near breakable objects can convert practice into cleanup, so keep the practice lane clear. → *Expect:* the yo-yo is ready for another clean throw.

## Decision points

- Yo-yo returns instantly → throw harder or use a less responsive yo-yo for sleeper tricks.
- Yo-yo will not return → tug sooner, or check that the string is wound in the correct direction.
- String hits the ground → shorten it before practicing.
- Walk the dog scratches floor → stop and use a smooth mat or outdoor surface.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Crooked throw:** detect by wobbling spin → release straight down from the center of your body.
- **F2 Finger pinches:** detect by string pain → loosen the loop and move it closer to the knuckle.
- **F3 Yo-yo dies on floor:** detect by no return after walking → lift earlier and tug before spin fades.
- **F4 String tangles:** detect by knots or uneven winding → unwind fully, remove knots, and rewind.

## Verification

The yo-yo sleeps at the bottom of the string for at least two seconds and returns to your hand on a tug.

## Variations

- Responsive yo-yo: best for gravity pull and beginner returns.
- Unresponsive yo-yo: better for long sleepers but needs a bind return.
- Carpet practice: safer for drops, worse for walk the dog.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Give yourself arm clearance, keep throws below face height, and stop if the string frays or the axle sounds rough.
