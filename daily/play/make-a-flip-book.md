## Goal

A small stack of pages becomes a flip book animation that moves when thumbed from one corner.

## Preconditions

- Sticky notes, index cards, or small paper cut to the same size.
- Pencil or pen, binder clip or rubber band, and a flat drawing surface.
- A simple motion idea, such as a bouncing ball or blooming flower.

## Steps

1. **Choose the flipping corner.** [BRANCH: right-handed flip | left-handed flip] put the drawing near the outer lower corner your thumb will release. → *Expect:* the action area sits where each page will be seen during the flip.
2. **Stack pages squarely.** Align 20 to 40 pages and clip the binding edge. → *Expect:* the stack behaves like one little book.
3. **Draw the first pose.** Make a simple shape near the corner, leaving room for movement. → *Expect:* page one shows the starting position clearly.
4. **Change only a little per page.** On each next page, move the shape a few millimeters or alter one part. → *Expect:* the drawings differ gradually, not by teleportation.
5. **Use onion-skin guessing.** Peek at the previous page edge or hold pages to light to place the next drawing. → *Expect:* the motion path stays smooth enough to follow.
6. **Add timing.** Repeat a drawing for a pause, or make bigger jumps for speed. → *Expect:* the animation has slow and fast moments.
7. **Test the thumb flip.** Hold the binding firmly and let the corner pages slip under your thumb. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* ink is hard to undo, so sketch in pencil until the timing test works. → *Expect:* the drawing appears to move.
8. **Darken and finish.** Trace the best lines, number the backs if useful, and reclip the stack. → *Expect:* the final flip reads from start to finish.

## Decision points

- Motion looks jumpy → add in-between pages with smaller changes.
- Motion is too slow → remove duplicate poses or flip faster.
- Drawings drift out of frame → mark a light corner guide.
- Pages slip unevenly → trim or restack them square.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Backward animation:** detect by motion playing in reverse → flip from the other end or reorder pages.
- **F2 Hidden drawing:** detect by art too close to the binding → redraw nearer the flipping corner.
- **F3 Smudged pages:** detect by gray streaks → let ink dry or place scrap paper under your hand.
- **F4 Weak ending:** detect by the last pose passing unnoticed → hold the final pose for three pages.

## Verification

When flipped from the chosen corner, the stack shows one continuous recognizable motion from first page to last.

## Variations

- Sticky-note version: easy binding, smaller drawing area.
- Photo reference: trace a simple object in different positions.
- Group version: one person draws rough motion, another inks details.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep blades away unless an adult trims paper, and avoid drawing private names or messages if the book will be passed around.
