## Goal

Tie a bowline: a fixed loop that does not slide smaller under ordinary hand loads and can usually be untied after loading.

## Preconditions

- One rope with a free working end and enough tail to finish the knot.
- A practice object, post, or imaginary target for the loop.
- Rope that is not icy, oily, damaged, or so stiff that it cannot dress cleanly.
- Non-critical practice use unless trained and equipped for the specific activity.

## Steps

1. **Size the loop.** Decide how large the finished fixed loop should be and leave enough working end beyond it. → *Expect:* the working end is long enough to go around the standing part and still leave a tail.
2. **Make the rabbit hole.** Form a small loop in the standing part with the standing part on the bottom and the working end side on top. → *Expect:* a small loop sits above the larger loop you want to keep.
3. **Send the rabbit out.** Pass the working end up through the small loop from underneath. → *Expect:* the working end emerges toward you through the hole.
4. **Go around the tree.** Wrap the working end around the standing part, passing behind it and coming back toward the small loop. → *Expect:* the working end has made one turn around the standing part.
5. **Return to the hole.** Pass the working end back down through the small loop the way it came. → *Expect:* the bowline shape is present, with a fixed loop and a short tail.
6. **Dress and set.** Pull the standing part while holding the loop and tail so the knot tightens cleanly without twists. → *Expect:* the collar grips the standing part and the loop stays the chosen size.
7. **Check before use.** [BRANCH: correct | suspect] If the tail is inside the loop and the collar is snug, use it lightly; if the collar is loose or the tail is too short, retie. → *Expect:* the bowline passes a firm hand tug without shrinking.

## Decision points

- Short tail: retie, because a bowline with a tiny tail can work loose.
- Slippery rope: add a backup overhand knot in the tail or choose a more secure variant.
- Ring or post must be captured: pass the rope around the object before tying, not after.
- Critical load: use trained procedures, rated gear, and inspection by someone qualified.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong loop direction:** the knot flips or forms a slip knot, untie and rebuild from the small rabbit-hole loop.
- **F2 Loose collar:** the wrap around the standing part is not snug, dress the collar and set it again.
- **F3 Tail outside pattern:** the working end exits oddly, untie rather than trying to patch it.
- **F4 Loop wrong size:** the fixed loop is too small or too large, loosen before loading and retie to size.

## Verification

The bowline is verified when the loop remains fixed under a firm hand pull, the collar is snug, and the tail is long enough to inspect.

## Variations

- `around-object`: pass the working end around the post first, then tie the same bowline.
- `backup`: add an overhand knot with the tail around the loop side for slippery cord.
- `practice`: tie the knot slowly, then repeat until the shape is recognizable before tightening.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low for practice and light camp chores, but knot failure can become serious when loads rise. Do not use an unverified bowline for climbing, rescue, lifting people, or suspended heavy objects.
