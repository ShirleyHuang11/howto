## Goal

Inflate an air mattress to comfortable firmness without over-inflating it, then deflate and store it without stressing the valve or seams.

## Preconditions

- Air mattress, matching pump or built-in pump, and any required power source or batteries.
- Clear floor or tent space free of thorns, sharp rocks, glass, and heat sources.
- Valve cap or plug present and not cracked.
- Manufacturer instructions available for unusual built-in pumps or pressure limits.

## Steps

1. **Clear the surface.** Sweep or feel the ground and remove anything sharp before unrolling the mattress. → *Expect:* the mattress rests on a clean, smooth area.
2. **Find the valve.** Open the inflation valve and check whether it has a one-way flap, screw cap, or separate deflation port. → *Expect:* you know which opening takes air in and which releases it.
3. **Attach the pump.** [BRANCH: built-in | external | manual] Turn on the built-in pump, connect an external pump nozzle, or use a hand or foot pump that fits snugly. → *Expect:* air enters without loud leaks around the nozzle.
4. **Inflate gradually.** Add air until the mattress rises and feels supportive but still yields slightly under a hand. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* over-pressure can split seams, so do not keep pumping after the mattress is drum-tight. → *Expect:* the surface is firm, not rock hard.
5. **Cap the valve.** Remove the pump while pinching or closing the valve, then seal the cap firmly. → *Expect:* no hiss continues after the valve is closed.
6. **Top up after settling.** Lie on it for a minute, wait several minutes, and add a small amount of air only if hips or shoulders bottom out. → *Expect:* the mattress feels stable under body weight.
7. **Deflate and store.** Open the deflation valve, press air out gently, fold without sharp creases at the valve, and store dry. → *Expect:* the mattress is flat, dry, capped, and ready for its bag or shelf.

## Decision points

- Cold night: expect air pressure to drop and plan a small top-up later.
- Shared mattress: inflate for the heavier sleeper first, then adjust for comfort.
- Built-in electric pump indoors: keep vents clear so the motor does not overheat.
- Leak suspected: listen at the valve before assuming the fabric is punctured.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Valve hiss:** the cap is crooked or the flap is stuck, reseat the valve and check for grit.
- **F2 Seam strain:** edges look stretched or feel hard, release air immediately.
- **F3 Pump mismatch:** nozzle leaks around the valve, use the correct adapter or a different pump.
- **F4 Slow leak overnight:** mattress softens by morning, test valve and seams with soapy water later.

## Verification

The mattress is verified when it supports body weight comfortably, the sealed valve is quiet, and the surface is not over-tight.

## Variations

- `camping-pad`: self-inflating pads need time with the valve open before final breaths or pump air.
- `guest-bed`: place a sheet or blanket on top to reduce squeaks and cold feel.
- `storage`: keep the valve closed loosely after drying so dust stays out.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low, but seam failure can ruin shelter or sleep. Keep the mattress away from flames, heaters, sharp objects, pets' claws, and aggressive over-inflation.
