## Goal

Pack a sleeping bag neatly by zipping, flattening, pushing out air, and either rolling from the foot or using a stuff sack.

## Preconditions

- A dry sleeping bag with no person, phone, flashlight, or clothing still inside.
- Straps, compression sack, or stuff sack that fits the bag.
- Clean ground, tent floor, tarp, or bed to keep the fabric dry.
- Time to air the bag first if it is damp from sleep or condensation.

## Steps

1. **Empty and shake out.** Remove every item from the bag and shake loose dirt away from the zipper. → *Expect:* the bag is empty, dry enough to pack, and free of gritty debris.
2. **Zip and align.** Zip the bag most or all of the way closed and line up the edges. → *Expect:* the bag forms one flat shape instead of a loose blanket.
3. **Flatten the air.** Press from head toward foot with forearms or knees to move trapped air out. → *Expect:* the bag becomes thinner before rolling starts.
4. **Roll from the foot.** Start at the foot end and roll tightly toward the head, pushing air forward as you go. → *Expect:* the roll stays narrow and firm instead of bulging.
5. **Strap the roll.** Wrap built-in straps or separate straps around the roll and tighten evenly. → *Expect:* the roll holds its shape when lifted.
6. **Use the stuff-sack method if preferred.** [BRANCH: roll | stuff-sack] For many backpacking bags, stuff the foot in first and feed fabric steadily rather than rolling. → *Expect:* the bag fills the sack without stressing one seam.
7. **Store correctly.** Pack for travel now, then loosen or hang the bag at home if storage will last more than a short trip. → *Expect:* the bag is compact for transport and not left compressed long-term.

## Decision points

- Damp bag: air it before packing or unpack it again as soon as possible.
- Down insulation: stuffing is often better than repeated tight rolling.
- Synthetic family bag: rolling may fit the factory straps more easily.
- Zipper snag: stop and back the slider up instead of forcing it through fabric.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bulging roll:** trapped air has nowhere to go, unroll halfway and press air toward the open end.
- **F2 Strap pops off:** the roll is too wide or uneven, reroll tighter and center the strap.
- **F3 Zipper catches liner:** fabric enters the slider, reverse gently and pull fabric free.
- **F4 Musty smell later:** bag was stored damp or compressed, dry fully and store loose.

## Verification

The sleeping bag is verified when it is empty, dry enough for travel, secured in its straps or sack, and not under long-term compression.

## Variations

- `car-camping`: roll loosely if trunk space allows and the next setup is soon.
- `backpacking`: use a waterproof pack liner or dry bag in wet conditions.
- `home-storage`: hang or store in a large breathable sack to preserve loft.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low. The main concerns are mildew from packing damp insulation, zipper damage from forcing snags, and accidentally packing valuables inside the bag.
