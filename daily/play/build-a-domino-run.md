## Goal

A domino run topples from start to finish, including curves and branches, with enough reliability that the final fall feels earned instead of lucky.

## Preconditions

- Dominoes, blocks, or upright tiles with flat bottoms.
- A flat floor or table that will not be bumped.
- Space where pets, feet, sleeves, and swinging doors are out of the route.

## Steps

1. **Clear the runway.** Sweep crumbs and move chairs, cords, and wobbling objects. [BRANCH: table build | floor build] choose table for short runs, floor for long drama. → *Expect:* the surface is flat and traffic-free.
2. **Use the spacing rule.** Place each domino about half a domino height from the next one. → *Expect:* a falling domino reaches the next near its upper half.
3. **Build straight sections first.** Stand five to ten dominoes in a line, keeping their faces parallel. → *Expect:* the row looks evenly spaced, like tiny patient doors.
4. **Make curves with smaller angles.** Rotate each domino a little, not one big turn. → *Expect:* the curve bends smoothly and every top edge points at the next target.
5. **Add safety gaps while building.** Leave intentional gaps between sections and bridge them only when the section is tested. → *Expect:* one accidental bump cannot destroy the entire route.
6. **Create reset-proof branches.** Aim one falling domino into two slightly offset starters, then leave a removable blocker until the final run. → *Expect:* each branch can be tested without firing the whole machine.
7. **Run a topple test.** Flick the first domino of each section before connecting sections. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* the full run spends all setup time at once, so test sections before removing safety gaps. → *Expect:* every tested section reaches its endpoint.
8. **Connect and launch.** Fill the safety gaps, remove blockers, and tap the first domino lightly. → *Expect:* the cascade reaches the final domino.

## Decision points

- Dominoes stop short → reduce spacing or aim each top edge more directly.
- Dominoes knock sideways out of line → straighten the path and use smaller curve angles.
- Branch fails on one side → move the splitter closer or offset the two starters less.
- Surface is soft carpet → use heavier blocks or move to a hard surface.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Premature collapse:** detect by a sleeve or hand starting the run early → rebuild only the affected section from the nearest safety gap.
- **F2 Dead curve:** detect by a fall that slides past the next domino → tighten spacing and reduce turn angle.
- **F3 Branch misses:** detect by only one route firing → put the two branch starters closer to the splitter.
- **F4 Wobbly pieces:** detect by dominoes falling before launch → swap warped pieces or change surfaces.

## Verification

One deliberate tap at the start causes the final domino in the planned route to fall.

## Variations

- Timed run: add a stopwatch and compare rebuilds.
- Rube Goldberg start: use a marble or toy car to tap the first domino.
- Collaborative build: assign sections, then connect only after each section passes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the run away from stairs, glass edges, food, pets, and walkways where someone might step on scattered pieces.
