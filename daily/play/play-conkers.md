## Goal

Play conkers by drilling and stringing a horse chestnut, taking turns striking, and following rules that keep eyes and hands out of the swing path.

## Preconditions

- Horse chestnut conkers, string, and a hand drill or supervised drill setup.
- Eye protection for drilling and for play.
- Open outdoor space away from windows, cars, pets, and bystanders.
- Players agree not to use hardened, baked, soaked, or chemically treated conkers unless everyone explicitly allows it.

## Steps

1. **Choose sound conkers.** Pick firm horse chestnuts with no deep cracks, soft spots, or mold. → *Expect:* each conker feels hard and intact.
2. **Drill the hole.** Wear eye protection, secure the conker on scrap wood, and drill straight through the center without holding it in your fingers. → *Expect:* a clean hole passes through without a hand near the bit.
3. **Thread the string.** Push string through the hole and tie a large stopper knot below the conker. → *Expect:* the conker hangs securely from the string.
4. **Set the stance.** One player dangles their conker at arm's length while the striker stands to the side with clear space. → *Expect:* eyes, hands, and faces are outside the swing line.
5. **Strike in turns.** [BRANCH: striker | holder] The striker swings once at the hanging conker, then roles swap after a miss or agreed number of tries. → *Expect:* only one conker moves at a time and no one steps in.
6. **Score breaks.** A player wins when the opponent's conker cracks, falls off the string, or can no longer be used. → *Expect:* the broken conker is removed from play.
7. **Stop on unsafe swings.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* an eye injury cannot be undone, so pause immediately if anyone removes eye protection or moves into range. → *Expect:* play resumes only after the area is safe again.

## Decision points

- Conker cracks during drilling: discard it before play.
- Younger players: use slower swings and larger spacing.
- Crowded area: do not play until bystanders can stay well back.
- Local school or park bans conkers: choose another game there.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 String slips:** knot pulls through the hole, retie with a larger stopper knot or washer.
- **F2 Unsafe swing path:** the conker passes near a face or hand, stop and reset stance farther apart.
- **F3 Drill skids:** bit slides on the glossy shell, secure the conker better and start with light pressure.
- **F4 Disputed hardening:** players suspect treatment, remove that conker or restart with inspected ones.

## Verification

The conkers game is verified when each round uses eye protection, a clear swing path, and an agreed win condition based on a broken or detached conker.

## Variations

- `friendly`: allow each player three misses before swapping roles.
- `tournament`: winning conkers carry a count of defeated opponents.
- `display`: string extra conkers as decorations and do not swing them.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is medium because swinging hard objects can injure eyes and fingers. Wear eye protection, drill with the conker secured on scrap wood, and stop play whenever a person enters the swing zone.
