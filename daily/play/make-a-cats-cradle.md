## Goal

A loop of string is arranged into the opening cat's cradle figure and passed between partners without dropping the pattern.

## Preconditions

- A smooth loop of string about 4 to 5 feet long is available.
- Hands are clean and dry enough for the string to slide.
- Two players can sit or stand facing each other.

## Steps

1. **Set the loop.** Place the string around both hands, behind the thumbs and little fingers. → *Expect:* one strand crosses each palm.
2. **Make the first palm pickup.** Use the right middle finger to pick up the left palm strand from below. → *Expect:* a diagonal strand runs to the right middle finger.
3. **Make the second palm pickup.** Use the left middle finger to pick up the right palm strand from below. → *Expect:* a symmetrical woven X appears between the hands.
4. **Open the cradle.** Spread fingers gently without pulling too tight. → *Expect:* the classic cat's cradle shape sits in the center.
5. **Show the transfer points.** Partner identifies the two crossed X points near the middle. → *Expect:* both players agree which strands will be pinched.
6. **Pinch and lift.** Partner pinches the X points from above, pulls them outward, then dips under the side strings. → *Expect:* string begins moving onto the partner's hands.
7. **Release at the right time.** First player lets go only after the partner's fingers hold the new figure. → *Expect:* the pattern stays suspended.
8. **Continue passes.** [BRANCH: practice the same transfer | learn the next named figure] → *Expect:* players can pass at least once without tangling.

## Decision points

- String is too short → use a longer loop so hands can spread comfortably.
- String is too fuzzy → switch to smoother cord to prevent snagging.
- Pattern tightens painfully → relax hands and restart with less tension.
- Partner cannot see pickup points → lower hands and pause before the transfer.
- A knot sits in the working area → rotate the loop so the knot is near a wrist.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dropped loop:** detect string falling slack; recover by returning to the opening hand setup.
- **F2 Twisted start:** detect uneven strands crossing palms; recover by removing the loop and smoothing it flat.
- **F3 Wrong pickup:** detect no central X after step 3; recover by undoing to the hand loop and repeating slowly.
- **F4 Too much tension:** detect string digging into fingers; recover by bringing hands closer together.
- **F5 Partner tangle:** detect crossed fingers trapped in string; recover by letting go and restarting, not yanking.

## Verification

The opening cat's cradle shows a central woven X, and one partner transfer succeeds while the string pattern remains intact.

## Variations

- Solo practice: make only the opening figure and release.
- Longer loop: works better for adult hands or group demonstrations.
- Figure sequence: continue into soldier's bed, candles, and manger after the first transfer.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep string away from necks, very young children, and tight wrapping around fingers.
