## Goal

You can roll a coin from index knuckle to pinky knuckle, catch it with the thumb, and reset for another pass.

## Preconditions

- A large coin or poker chip; a quarter works, a half-dollar or similar token is easier.
- A table, bed, or carpet below your hand so drops do not disappear.
- Clean dry hands.

## Steps

1. **Set the hand platform.** Hold your palm down with fingers slightly curled, knuckles forming a shallow ridge. → *Expect:* the coin can rest flat on the index finger segment.
2. **Start at the index.** Place the coin on the first segment of the index finger and pinch it lightly against the thumb to begin. → *Expect:* the coin is controlled but ready to tip.
3. **Tip to the middle finger.** Lift the index finger slightly while lowering the middle finger so the coin rolls over the index knuckle. → *Expect:* the coin lands on the middle finger segment.
4. **Walk finger-to-finger.** Repeat the lift-and-lower motion from middle to ring, then ring to pinky. Move slowly enough to see each handoff. → *Expect:* the coin travels as three small rolls, not one fling.
5. **Catch with the thumb.** When the coin reaches the pinky side, curl the thumb underneath and trap the coin against the side of the index finger or palm. → *Expect:* the coin returns under the hand without falling.
6. **Reset to the index.** Slide or flip the coin back to the starting pinch between thumb and index. [BRANCH: visible reset | hidden reset] Visible is fine for practice; hidden looks smoother later. → *Expect:* another roll can begin immediately.
7. **Drill slow cycles.** Practice sets of five slow rolls, counting only clean finger handoffs and thumb catches. → *Expect:* fewer drops because speed is not hiding the weak point.

## Decision points

- If the coin sticks, relax the fingers and reduce downward pressure.
- If the coin flies off, make the knuckle ridge flatter and slow the lift.
- If one handoff fails repeatedly, drill only that two-finger transfer for a minute.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Coin flips upright:** the lifting finger snaps too hard → use smaller finger motion and keep the coin nearly flat.
- **F2 Pinky escape:** the last finger is too curled or too low → raise the pinky earlier and meet the coin.
- **F3 Thumb misses:** the reset hand arrives late → start moving the thumb as the coin reaches the ring finger.
- **F4 Lost coin:** practice over a tray, towel, or bed until drops are rare.

## Verification

You complete three full coin rolls in a row from index to pinky with thumb catches and resets, without dropping the coin.

## Variations

- Poker chips exaggerate the motion and help beginners see the roll.
- Smaller coins look cleaner but demand better timing.
- Once stable, reverse the roll from pinky to index as a separate trick.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not use valuable or sentimental coins during practice, keep coins away from small children, and avoid drilling through finger joint pain.
