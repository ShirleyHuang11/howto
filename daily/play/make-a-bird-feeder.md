## Goal

Make a simple bird feeder from a bottle or pine cone, place it safely, and keep it clean for visiting birds.

## Preconditions

- Adult supervision for cutting, hanging, or using string outdoors.
- A clean plastic bottle or pine cone.
- Birdseed and unsalted fat, nut butter, or another bird-safe binder.
- A hanging spot high enough to reduce cat access.

## Steps

1. **Choose the base.** [BRANCH: bottle | pine cone] use a clean bottle for a refillable feeder, or a pine cone for a simple coated feeder. → *Expect:* the feeder body is dry and ready.
2. **Add access.** For a bottle, have an adult cut small seed openings; for a pine cone, spread binder into the gaps. → *Expect:* birds can reach food without sharp edges.
3. **Make a perch.** Push a pencil, dowel, or sturdy twig through the bottle below openings, or tie string to the pine cone top. → *Expect:* birds have a place to land or cling.
4. **Fill with seed.** Pour seed into the bottle or roll the coated pine cone in seed. → *Expect:* food is held by the feeder.
5. **Check for hazards.** Smooth sharp plastic, trim loose strings, and avoid sticky globs birds could step in. → *Expect:* the feeder has no obvious snags.
6. **Hang it high.** Place it where birds can see it, near cover but away from cat jumping paths. → *Expect:* the feeder hangs steady and out of easy predator reach.
7. **Watch from a distance.** Observe quietly for several days. → *Expect:* birds visit once they find the food.
8. **Clean regularly.** Empty old seed, wash the feeder, and let it dry before refilling. → *Expect:* mold and droppings do not build up.
9. **Remove if unsafe.** Take it down during mold, disease alerts, or repeated predator stalking. → *Expect:* feeding stops until conditions are safe.

## Decision points

- Cats patrol the area → hang higher, move farther from fences, or do not install.
- Seed gets wet → empty and dry the feeder before refilling.
- Local guidance warns against feeding birds → pause feeding.
- Large birds dominate → use smaller openings or a different feeder location.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Moldy seed:** detect clumps, smell, or fuzz → discard seed, wash, dry, and refill lightly.
- **F2 Sharp edge:** detect rough plastic around holes → tape, sand, or recut with adult help.
- **F3 Predator risk:** detect cats waiting below → remove or relocate the feeder.
- **F4 No visitors:** detect no activity after several days → move near shrubs while keeping safe height.

## Verification

The feeder hangs securely, stays clean, contains bird-safe food, and is placed where cats cannot easily reach visiting birds.

## Variations

- Use a toilet-paper tube coated with binder and seed for a short-lived feeder.
- Use sunflower seed for many backyard birds, avoiding salted or seasoned food.
- Add a simple rain cover if seed gets wet quickly.

## Safety & privacy

Adult supervision is required for cutting and hanging, and cleanup protects birds from disease.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* A poorly placed feeder can train birds into a predator trap, so move or remove it if cats wait nearby.
