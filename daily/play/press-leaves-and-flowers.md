## Goal

Press dry leaves and flowers flat between absorbent paper so they can be mounted for a craft, journal, or collection.

## Preconditions

- Leaves or flowers picked legally and respectfully, without taking protected plants.
- Dry weather or specimens that are not wet with rain or dew.
- Plain paper, newspaper, blotting paper, or coffee filters.
- A heavy book, extra weights, and a safe place where the stack can sit for weeks.

## Steps

1. **Pick dry specimens.** Choose leaves and flowers that are fresh, thin, and not muddy or wet. → *Expect:* the pieces feel dry to the touch and are not already wilting into mush.
2. **Prepare the paper layers.** Place one sheet of absorbent paper inside a book or between two flat boards. → *Expect:* the pressing surface is clean and ready before arranging begins.
3. **Arrange the shapes.** Lay each leaf or flower flat with space between pieces, spreading petals and turning the best side up. → *Expect:* no specimen overlaps another or folds under itself.
4. **Cover and close.** Add a second paper sheet, close the book slowly, and keep the pages from sliding. → *Expect:* the specimens stay in position as pressure begins.
5. **Add weight.** [BRANCH: book | board-stack] Use a heavy book alone for small pieces or boards plus weights for larger batches. → *Expect:* even pressure covers the full page.
6. **Wait for drying.** Leave the press undisturbed for two to four weeks, checking only if paper feels damp and needs replacement. → *Expect:* moisture leaves slowly without mold or curled edges.
7. **Mount the pressed pieces.** Lift with tweezers or fingertips, then attach with tiny dots of glue, tape corners, or photo mounts. → *Expect:* the finished leaf or flower lies flat on the chosen backing.

## Decision points

- Thick flower head: split it, press petals separately, or choose a thinner specimen.
- Damp paper after a day: replace the paper so mold does not start.
- Plant may be protected: photograph it instead of picking it.
- Color matters most: press soon after picking and keep out of direct sun.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mold spots:** fuzzy or dark marks appear, discard the specimen and use drier material next time.
- **F2 Wrinkled petals:** petals folded before pressing, rehydrate is not reliable, so press a new flower more carefully.
- **F3 Stuck to paper:** delicate parts tear when lifted, use tweezers and thinner glue or mount with the paper edge hidden.
- **F4 Faded color:** direct light or slow drying dulled the plant, press fresher pieces and store them in shade.

## Verification

The pressing is verified when the mounted specimen is fully dry, flat, intact, and attached without visible damp spots.

## Variations

- `field-journal`: label the backing with date, place, and plant name if known.
- `gift-card`: use one strong leaf or flower as the main decoration.
- `color-study`: press several leaves from the same plant at different seasons.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low, but plant choice matters. Avoid poisonous plants, unknown berries, pesticide-treated areas, private gardens without permission, and protected wildflowers.
