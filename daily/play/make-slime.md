## Goal

Make stretchy play slime from school glue and an activator, adjust the texture, and store it without contaminating food surfaces.

## Preconditions

- White or clear PVA school glue.
- Activator: contact lens solution with boric acid plus baking soda, or diluted borax solution.
- Bowl, spoon, measuring spoon, and airtight container.
- Adult supervision for young children, especially when borax is present.

## Steps

1. **Cover the work surface.** Lay down parchment, a tray, or a washable mat. → *Expect:* spills land on a cleanable surface, not fabric or carpet.
2. **Measure glue.** Pour 1/2 cup glue into the bowl. → *Expect:* glue sits in one puddle with no dried chunks.
3. **Add color or mix-ins.** Stir in food coloring, glitter, or foam beads before activating. → *Expect:* color is even and mix-ins are suspended in the glue.
4. **Add baking soda for lens-solution slime.** Stir in 1/2 teaspoon baking soda per 1/2 cup glue. → *Expect:* glue thickens slightly but still flows.
5. **Add activator slowly.** [BRANCH: lens solution | borax solution] add 1 tablespoon lens solution, or 1 teaspoon borax solution at a time. → *Expect:* strings form and the mixture pulls from the bowl.
6. **Stir until it clumps.** Scrape the sides and fold the mass through the wet spots. → *Expect:* one sticky lump forms around the spoon.
7. **Knead with clean hands.** Pick up the lump and fold it for 2 to 5 minutes. → *Expect:* slime changes from wet and sticky to stretchy and smooth.
8. **Fix stickiness.** Add activator a few drops at a time, then knead before adding more. → *Expect:* slime releases from fingers but still stretches.
9. **Fix stiffness.** Add a pea-size dab of lotion or a few drops of warm water and knead. → *Expect:* slime softens without turning soupy.
10. **Store immediately after play.** Put slime in an airtight container or zip bag. → *Expect:* the container closes fully and slime is not exposed to lint or crumbs.
11. **Wash hands and tools.** Rinse bowls before residue dries, then wash with soap. → *Expect:* hands are clean and no slime remains in the sink drain.

## Decision points

- If making slime with kids → pre-measure activator and keep borax powder handled by an adult.
- If using contact lens solution → confirm the label includes boric acid or sodium borate.
- If slime is for a classroom → avoid glitter that sheds onto floors and choose washable color.
- If anyone has sensitive skin → wear gloves or skip borax-based slime.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soup slime:** detect liquid that will not clump → add activator slowly, stirring after each small addition.
- **F2 Rubber slime:** detect snapping instead of stretching → knead in lotion or warm water a little at a time.
- **F3 Stained hands:** detect strong dye transfer → wash promptly and use less coloring next batch.
- **F4 Eye or mouth exposure:** detect slime, glue, or activator contact → rinse with water, stop play, and contact poison control or medical help if symptoms continue.

## Verification

The slime stretches at least 15 cm without pouring like liquid, releases mostly from clean hands, and is sealed in a labeled container after use.

## Variations

- Clear slime: let it rest overnight after mixing so bubbles rise out.
- Fluffy slime: fold in shaving foam before activator for a lighter texture.
- Borax solution: dissolve 1 teaspoon borax in 1 cup warm water and use only small spoonfuls.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because borax and activators can irritate skin, eyes, and mouths. Do not let children taste slime, handle borax powder, or play on food-prep surfaces that will not be washed.
