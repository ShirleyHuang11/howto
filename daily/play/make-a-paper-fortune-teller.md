## Goal

A square paper fortune teller is folded, labeled, filled with fortunes, and ready to play.

## Preconditions

- One square sheet of paper, pen or markers, and a flat surface are available.
- Scissors are available if starting from rectangular paper.
- Players agree the fortunes are playful and not serious predictions.

## Steps

1. **Make a square.** Fold a rectangular sheet diagonally, trim the extra strip, and open it. → *Expect:* paper has four equal sides.
2. **Mark the center.** Fold diagonally both ways, then open flat. → *Expect:* crease lines cross at the center.
3. **Fold corners inward.** Bring each corner point to the center. → *Expect:* a smaller square forms with four triangular flaps.
4. **Flip and repeat.** Turn the square over and fold all four new corners to the center. → *Expect:* an even smaller square forms.
5. **Fold for fingers.** Fold the square in half one way, open it, then fold in half the other way. → *Expect:* pockets puff open underneath.
6. **Label outside flaps.** Write colors, symbols, or numbers on the four outside flaps. → *Expect:* each outside choice is readable.
7. **Number and fill inside.** Number the eight inner triangles, lift them, and write one fortune under each. → *Expect:* every hidden flap has a short message.
8. **Play a round.** Put thumbs and index fingers into the pockets and move the teller as choices are spelled or counted. → *Expect:* a final flap opens to reveal a fortune.

## Decision points

- Paper tears at the center → start with thinner paper and softer creases.
- Players cannot fit fingers → make a larger square.
- Fortunes are for young children → keep messages kind and silly.
- Game needs faster play → use numbers instead of long color words.
- Paper keeps unfolding → press creases firmly with a fingernail.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rectangle start:** detect uneven flaps; recover by trimming to a true square and refolding.
- **F2 Wrong-side labels:** detect fortunes visible too early; recover by flipping and relabeling the correct flaps.
- **F3 Stiff movement:** detect pockets resisting finger motion; recover by flexing folds open and closed several times.
- **F4 Missing fortune:** detect a blank hidden flap; recover by adding a short message before play.
- **F5 Hurtful fortune:** detect a message targeting someone; recover by replacing it with a neutral or funny line.

## Verification

The fortune teller opens in two directions on the fingers, has four outside choices, eight numbered inner choices, and eight hidden fortunes.

## Variations

- Picture version: use icons or colors for early readers.
- Challenge version: write dares or trivia instead of fortunes.
- Party version: make several with themed prompts.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid sharp scissors for young children without supervision, and do not write private or mean messages inside the flaps.
