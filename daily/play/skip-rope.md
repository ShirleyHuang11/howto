## Goal

A person sizes a jump rope and performs steady basic skips using wrist turns, small jumps, and consistent timing.

## Preconditions

- Jump rope, flat open surface, supportive shoes, and overhead clearance.
- No nearby fragile objects, pets, or people inside the rope arc.
- Body is comfortable with light jumping and balance.

## Steps

1. **Check space.** Stand where the rope can swing overhead and behind you without striking anything. → *Expect:* at least an arm's length of clear space on every side.
2. **Size the rope.** Step on the rope center and pull handles upward. → *Expect:* handles reach roughly armpit height for a beginner.
3. **Adjust if possible.** Shorten or lengthen the rope before jumping. → *Expect:* the rope clears the head and brushes the floor lightly, not heavily.
4. **Set posture.** Stand tall with elbows near ribs and handles slightly forward of hips. → *Expect:* shoulders are relaxed and wrists can rotate freely.
5. **Practice wrist turns.** Swing the rope beside the body or behind-to-front without jumping. → *Expect:* the rope moves from the wrists, not big arm circles.
6. **Find the floor tap.** Swing once and listen for the rope to tap in front of the toes. → *Expect:* the tap happens just before the jump moment.
7. **Jump small.** Hop only 2 to 4 cm as the rope reaches the toes. → *Expect:* the rope passes under both feet with little knee bend.
8. **Reset after misses.** If the rope catches, stop, place it behind heels, and restart calmly. → *Expect:* form returns instead of turning into high jumps.
9. **Link repetitions.** Aim for sets of 5, then 10, then 30 smooth skips. → *Expect:* breathing and rope rhythm become predictable.
10. **Cool down.** Stop before calves or shins ache sharply and walk for a minute. → *Expect:* heart rate settles and legs feel worked, not strained.

## Decision points

- Rope hits the head → rope may be too short or hands are too high.
- Rope catches toes → jump is late, rope is too long, or hands drift behind the body.
- Calf pain builds quickly → reduce session length and surface hardness.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Arm swinging:** detect tired shoulders and wide circles; pin elbows near ribs and turn from wrists.
- **F2 Donkey kicks:** detect heels kicking backward; practice tiny vertical hops without the rope.
- **F3 Rope slaps hard:** detect loud floor strikes; shorten the rope or move hands slightly lower.
- **F4 Trip risk:** detect repeated catching and stumbling; stop, clear the area, and restart with single jumps.

## Verification

The person completes at least 10 consecutive basic skips with small jumps, wrist-driven turns, and no contact with nearby objects.

## Variations

- Beaded rope: gives audible feedback and helps beginners find timing.
- Speed rope: works best after basic timing is stable.
- No-adjust rope: tie temporary knots near handles only if they do not hit the hands.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from falls, overuse, and striking others. Use a clear flat surface and stop for sharp pain, dizziness, or joint instability.
