## Goal

Perform a simple mind-reading card trick using the key-card principle, where you secretly track the card next to the spectator's chosen card.

## Preconditions

- A standard deck of cards with no missing cards.
- A table or standing space where the deck can be handled cleanly.
- One spectator who can follow simple instructions.
- Willingness to practice the setup before presenting it as a trick.

## Steps

1. **Peek the key card.** Secretly look at the bottom card of the deck and remember it without changing your expression. → *Expect:* you know one card the spectator does not know you know.
2. **Invite a selection.** Spread the cards and ask the spectator to pick one, remember it, and show it to others while you look away. → *Expect:* the spectator believes their card is unknown to you.
3. **Cut the deck.** Ask them to place their card on top of the lower half, then place the original bottom half on top. → *Expect:* your key card now sits directly above their chosen card.
4. **Add the patter.** Say you will read tiny clues from their face or from the deck's order while keeping the handling casual. → *Expect:* attention stays on the story, not the method.
5. **Search for the key.** Turn cards face up one by one until you see your key card; the next card is the selection. → *Expect:* the chosen card is identified silently before the reveal.
6. **Reveal cleanly.** [BRANCH: direct | dramatic] Name the card directly or pause on it and ask the spectator to say it aloud. → *Expect:* the named card matches the spectator's memory.
7. **Do not repeat immediately.** Move to another trick or stop while the effect is strong. → *Expect:* no one gets a second identical run to analyze the method.

## Decision points

- Key card forgotten: switch to a joke reveal or restart openly as practice.
- Spectator shuffles after replacing card: the method is broken, so do a different trick.
- Card placed in wrong location: cut again only if it looks natural, otherwise stop.
- Repeat request: decline playfully and offer a different effect.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lost key card:** you cannot remember the bottom card, end with a comic miss and practice again.
- **F2 Spectator flashes too little:** no one confirms the chosen card, ask them to show it clearly before replacement.
- **F3 Deck order disturbed:** cards are shuffled or dropped, abandon the method and reset privately.
- **F4 Reveal rushed:** the effect feels like a procedure, slow down the patter before naming the card.

## Verification

The trick is verified when the spectator's selected card is revealed correctly after you locate the card immediately following your remembered key card.

## Variations

- `table`: spread the face-up cards slowly on the table to find the key.
- `hands-only`: deal face-up into your hand if no table is available.
- `story`: frame the reveal as memory, intuition, or card behavior while the method stays the same.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is low. Do not pressure anyone to reveal private thoughts, and present the trick as entertainment rather than a real claim of mind reading.
