## Goal

Players draw a ring, shoot marbles knuckle-down, and take turns trying to knock target marbles out under agreed rules.

## Preconditions

- A flat dirt, carpet, chalk, or sidewalk area is available.
- Each player has a shooter marble and several target marbles.
- Players agree whether the game is for keeps or friendly return.

## Steps

1. **Draw the ring.** Make a circle 3 to 10 feet wide, scaled to player skill. → *Expect:* all players can see the boundary.
2. **Set target marbles.** Place marbles in a cross, cluster, or center pile inside the ring. → *Expect:* targets are reachable but not touching the edge.
3. **Choose rules.** [BRANCH: keepers, winners keep knocked-out marbles | friendlies, all marbles return after game] → *Expect:* no one is surprised by stakes.
4. **Pick shooting order.** Flip a coin, lag toward a line, or let youngest go first. → *Expect:* turn order is agreed.
5. **Shoot knuckle-down.** Rest a knuckle on the ground, tuck the shooter against a finger, and flick with the thumb. → *Expect:* the shooter leaves from inside or behind the agreed line.
6. **Score a hit.** If a target marble leaves the ring, collect it or set it aside by the rules. → *Expect:* boundary result is visible.
7. **Continue turns.** [BRANCH: shooter stays in ring, shoot from there next turn | shooter leaves ring, shoot from start line] → *Expect:* next position is clear.
8. **End the game.** Stop when all target marbles are out or players reach the agreed round limit. → *Expect:* winner is the player with the most scored marbles.

## Decision points

- Players are new → use a smaller ring and friendly rules.
- Surface is bumpy → smooth dirt or switch to carpet.
- Shooter marble cracks → replace it before continuing.
- A marble stops on the line → decide before play whether the line counts as out.
- Younger players struggle → allow closer shooting or rolling.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rule dispute:** detect arguing over keeps or boundaries; recover by pausing and restating rules before the next shot.
- **F2 Lost marble:** detect a marble leaving the play area; recover by marking a backstop or reducing shot force.
- **F3 Illegal lift:** detect a hand leaving the ground during a shot; recover by replaying the shot.
- **F4 Ring too large:** detect no one hitting targets; recover by shrinking the ring.
- **F5 Choking hazard:** detect toddlers or pets near marbles; recover by collecting marbles and moving play.

## Verification

The ring is marked, turns were taken with knuckle-down shots, and the final score matches the marbles knocked out under the agreed rules.

## Variations

- Ringer: use thirteen target marbles in a cross inside a ten-foot ring.
- Indoor: use string for the ring on carpet.
- Practice: shoot at one target marble without scoring.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but marbles are choking hazards and can roll underfoot. Keep them away from small children and clean up after play.
