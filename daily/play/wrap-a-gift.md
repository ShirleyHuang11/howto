## Goal

A gift wrapped with crisp edges, hidden tape where possible, and a finish that says care rather than struggle, including the escape routes for awkward shapes.

## Preconditions

- The gift, wrapping paper, scissors, and tape; ribbon and a tag optional but transformative.
- A hard flat surface with room to unroll (`daily/home`'s cleared-table doctrine applies; wrapping on carpet is how corners die).
- Awkward-shape honesty check: boxes wrap; balls, plants, and dinosaur-shaped things go in gift bags or boxes first. Fighting geometry is optional.

## Steps

1. **Box the gift if it is not already boxy.** A box (reused is fine) converts every gift into the solvable case; remove or obscure the price first. → *Expect:* a rectangular object with a best face chosen to end up on top, which means it starts face-*down* on the paper.
2. **Cut the paper to size with the roll trick.** Roll the paper around the box once plus a 2 to 3 cm overlap, and ensure the sides have paper equal to about two-thirds of the box's height; cut along a straight edge (paper grids on the back are the cheat that pros use). → *Expect:* a rectangle of paper that wraps fully with modest, not lavish, overlap; excess paper is the enemy of crisp ends.
3. **Wrap the body and seam it.** Bring one edge over, fold the other edge's raw cut under itself by a centimeter (the hidden hem that makes it look professional), overlap, and tape along the fold, or use double-sided tape underneath for the invisible seam. Pull snug before each tape; loose paper wrinkles forever. → *Expect:* a tight cylinder of paper around the box with one clean seam line on the bottom face.
4. **Fold the ends like presents deserve.** At each open end: push the top flap down against the box creating two 45-degree wings, crease the wings sharply against the corners, fold each wing in, then bring the bottom triangle up over them; tape once. Crease every fold with a fingernail. → *Expect:* flat triangular ends with sharp diagonal creases, lying flush; this step is 80 percent of the "professionally wrapped" impression.
5. **Add the ribbon or twine cross and the tag.** Ribbon around both axes, crossing underneath, tied on top; curl ribbon ends with a scissor-blade pull; tag on the ribbon or a corner, naming giver and receiver (the tag prevents the under-tree identity crisis). → *Expect:* a finished parcel whose top face is the chosen best face from step 1.
6. **Quality pass.** Rattle test (contents secured?), corner check (no torn punctures; patch with a deliberate-looking extra fold if so), and price-sticker double-check. → *Expect:* a gift you would be pleased to receive across a table.

## Decision points

- Paper economy: the roll trick prevents both the 40 cm of waste and the 2 cm of heartbreak; when the cut piece turns out short anyway, a deliberate contrasting band of the other paper over the gap reads as design, not error.
- Bags with tissue: for the geometry-refuseniks — gift in bag, two sheets of tissue fluffed and inserted stems-up; ninety seconds, zero shame, and the recipe's fun is optional.
- Kids' gifts: sturdier paper, more tape, no elegance debt; the paper's fate is measured in seconds.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cut the paper too small:** the contrasting-band patch above, or demote this sheet to a smaller gift and re-cut; forcing a short wrap tears at the corners on cue.
- **F2 Wrinkled, baggy faces:** the pull-snug-before-taping rule was skipped; unwrap to the last good fold and re-tension rather than adding compensatory tape, which never once has worked.
- **F3 Tape mummification:** each visible face earns at most one or two pieces; if the count is higher, the folds are doing too little work; revisit steps 3 and 4's hems and creases.
- **F4 Ribbon slid off a corner:** cross it under the box and tighten before the bow, or anchor with a discreet tape dot under the crossing.

## Verification

Edges are creased sharp, the seam is hemmed and single-taped, the ends lie flat with 45-degree folds, the ribbon holds its cross, the tag names both parties, and no price sticker survived.

## Variations

- Furoshiki (`jp` cloth wrapping): a square cloth, two diagonal knots, endlessly reusable and effortlessly elegant; the gateway to never buying paper again.
- Newspaper-and-twine or brown-paper-and-stamps styles turn thrift into aesthetics; crisp folds carry the whole look regardless of paper cost.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk beyond scissors and paper cuts. One privacy note with teeth: gift receipts in, prices off, and for doorstep-delivered gifts, the outer shipping box's label (your address, the contents description) does not belong under anyone's tree.
