## Goal

A flexible cherry stem is tied into a loose knot using tongue, teeth, and patience, then revealed with appropriate theatrical restraint.

## Preconditions

- A clean cherry stem, preferably long, thin, and fresh.
- Clean hands and a place to discard failed stems.
- No braces, mouth injury, or choking concern that would make this a bad idea.

## Steps

1. **Choose a good stem.** [BRANCH: fresh flexible stem | dry brittle stem] use fresh flexible, reject dry brittle. → *Expect:* the stem bends without snapping.
2. **Soften it.** Hold the stem in your mouth for 20 to 30 seconds. → *Expect:* it becomes more pliable and less springy.
3. **Make a U shape.** Use your tongue to bend the stem into a loop against the roof of your mouth. → *Expect:* both ends point forward near your teeth.
4. **Cross the ends.** Press one end over the other with your tongue, using front teeth as a gentle fence. → *Expect:* the stem forms a loose loop with crossed tips.
5. **Push one end through.** Use the tip of your tongue to nudge one stem end through the loop. → *Expect:* a simple overhand knot shape begins.
6. **Pinch and pull.** Hold one end lightly with teeth while your tongue pulls the other end away. → *Expect:* the knot tightens enough to stay formed.
7. **Adjust without biting through.** Work the knot looser or tighter until it sits near the middle. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* biting the stem in half ends that attempt, so use teeth as posts, not scissors. → *Expect:* one knotted stem remains intact.
8. **Reveal it.** Place the stem on a napkin and accept applause, skepticism, or both. → *Expect:* the knot is visible outside your mouth.

## Decision points

- Stem is too short → eat another cherry and select a longer one.
- Stem keeps slipping flat → soften longer and make a larger first loop.
- Teeth hurt or braces catch → stop and tie it with fingers instead.
- Audience demands proof → repeat slowly, but do not turn dinner into a dental exam.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stem snaps:** detect by two pieces in your mouth → discard both pieces and pick a fresher stem.
- **F2 Loop collapses:** detect by a straight stem after crossing → restart with a bigger U shape.
- **F3 Knot tightens on the end:** detect by a tiny knot at the tip → use a longer stem and pull more evenly.
- **F4 Choking worry:** detect by coughing or panic → spit the stem into a napkin immediately.

## Verification

The cherry stem leaves your mouth as one intact stem with a visible overhand knot in it.

## Variations

- Finger practice: tie a few stems by hand first to learn the knot shape.
- Long-stem cherries: much easier and worth shamelessly selecting from the bowl.
- Licorice lace: a larger edible practice tool for the same loop-and-pull idea.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not try this while laughing, walking, driving, or supervising children, and discard stems rather than swallowing them.
