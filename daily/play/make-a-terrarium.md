## Goal

A small terrarium is planted in a glass container with drainage, soil, suitable plants, and a watering plan that does not turn it into soup.

## Preconditions

- A clean glass jar, bowl, or lidded container.
- Pebbles, activated charcoal, potting mix, small plants, and a spoon.
- Bright indirect light, not a hot windowsill.

## Steps

1. **Choose open or sealed.** [BRANCH: open terrarium | sealed terrarium] use open for succulents and cacti, sealed for moisture-loving tropical plants. → *Expect:* the plant choice matches the container style.
2. **Add a drainage layer.** Pour 2 to 4 cm of pebbles into the bottom. → *Expect:* water has a place to collect below the roots.
3. **Sprinkle charcoal.** Add a thin layer of activated charcoal over the pebbles. → *Expect:* the bottom looks dusted, not buried.
4. **Add soil.** Spoon in enough potting mix for the plant roots, usually 5 to 8 cm. → *Expect:* there is room for roots and still room above for leaves.
5. **Plant small and gently.** Make holes, set plants in, and firm soil around roots without crushing stems. → *Expect:* each plant stands upright with its crown above the soil.
6. **Clean the glass.** Brush soil from leaves and wipe the inside wall with a dry cloth or cotton swab. → *Expect:* the view looks like a tiny exhibit, not a jar after a landslide.
7. **Water lightly.** Add water by spoon or spray bottle, aiming at soil, not leaves. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overwatering in a sealed jar can rot roots fast, so add less than feels satisfying. → *Expect:* soil darkens slightly while no puddle rises into the soil layer.
8. **Place and observe.** Put it in bright indirect light and check condensation the next day. → *Expect:* plants remain upright and the glass is mostly clear.

## Decision points

- Heavy condensation all day → open the lid for a few hours.
- Soil dries in an open terrarium → water a spoonful at a time.
- Leaves touch wet glass → trim or move the plant to prevent rot.
- Plant needs full sun outdoors → choose a different plant for glass.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Root rot smell:** detect by sour odor or black stems → remove rotting plants and let the container air out.
- **F2 Mold bloom:** detect by white fuzz on soil → open the container, remove mold, and improve airflow.
- **F3 Plant scorch:** detect by browned leaf patches → move away from direct sun.
- **F4 Swamp bottom:** detect by water above the pebble layer → tip out excess carefully or rebuild with drier soil.

## Verification

After 24 hours, the plants stand upright, the soil is lightly moist, and there is no standing water in the soil layer.

## Variations

- Moss jar: use collected moss only where gathering is allowed.
- Desert bowl: skip the lid, use gritty cactus mix, and water rarely.
- Decorative layer: add sand or stones on top after planting, not before roots are covered.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wash hands after handling soil, avoid toxic plants around children and pets, and do not collect wild plants from protected spaces.
