## Goal

Build a freestanding sandcastle with compact towers, carved details, and a moat that survives normal beach wind and a few small waves.

## Preconditions

- Sand with access to water, ideally near but above the active wave line.
- Bucket, shovel, cup, or hands for carrying and shaping sand.
- Space away from foot traffic and beach towels.
- Awareness of tide direction so the castle is not erased immediately.

## Steps

1. **Pick the site.** Choose damp, packed sand above the current wash line. → *Expect:* footprints hold shape and no wave reaches the spot for several minutes.
2. **Make a water source.** Dig a small pit until water seeps in, or carry water in a bucket. → *Expect:* wet sand can be squeezed into a clump that holds.
3. **Mix sand to packing texture.** Use roughly 8 parts sand to 1 part water, adding water until it feels like heavy brown sugar. → *Expect:* a squeezed ball stays intact but does not drip.
4. **Build a compact base.** Pile wet sand in the center and press it down with hands or the shovel back. → *Expect:* the mound feels dense and does not crumble when tapped.
5. **Pack bucket forms.** Fill a bucket in layers, tamping each layer firmly before adding more. → *Expect:* the bucket feels heavy and the top surface is tight.
6. **Flip the bucket.** Place it upside down on the base, tap the sides, then lift straight up. → *Expect:* a tower form releases with clean sides.
7. **Add more forms.** [BRANCH: one large keep | several towers] repeat with larger or smaller containers, pressing each form into the base. → *Expect:* towers touch the base firmly with no gaps at the bottom.
8. **Carve from the top down.** Shape roof points, walls, windows, and stairs before carving lower details. → *Expect:* loose sand falls away without damaging finished lower areas.
9. **Cut clean edges.** Use a stick, shell, card, or finger to scrape doorways and wall lines. → *Expect:* features look intentional, not finger-smeared.
10. **Dig the moat last.** Scoop a ring around the castle and connect it to the water pit only if you want water in it. → *Expect:* the moat has sloped sides and does not undercut the base.
11. **Reinforce weak spots.** Drip wet sand onto cracks and press gently from both sides. → *Expect:* cracks close and the tower no longer sheds dry grains.

## Decision points

- If sand collapses when unmolded → it is too dry or loosely packed; add water and tamp in thinner layers.
- If the castle slumps → it is too wet or built too tall; widen the base and wait a minute before carving.
- If waves approach → stop carving and build a low berm seaward of the moat.
- If children are sharing tools → assign one bucket for forms and one shovel for digging to avoid collisions.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bucket sticks:** detect suction or tearing during lift → tap the bucket all around and lift slower, straight upward.
- **F2 Tower cracks:** detect vertical splits → patch with dripped wet sand and reduce height above the crack.
- **F3 Base undercuts:** detect moat wall caving inward → move the moat farther out and pack a thicker apron around the castle.
- **F4 Dry crust crumbles:** detect pale loose sand on surfaces → mist or drip water, then wait before carving again.

## Verification

The castle has at least one compact tower, carved visible details, and a moat or boundary trench, and it stands for 5 minutes without hand support.

## Variations

- No bucket: hand-pack a mound, then carve a keep and walls from one solid mass.
- Very fine sand: use less water and more compaction because fine grains slump when saturated.
- Group build: one person hauls water, one packs forms, and one carves top-down.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch for sun exposure, sharp shells, and deep holes near walking paths. Fill large pits before leaving so others do not trip.
