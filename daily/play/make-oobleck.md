## Goal

Make oobleck, a cornstarch and water mixture that feels solid when hit and liquid when moved slowly.

## Preconditions

- Adult supervision for children.
- Cornstarch, water, a bowl, a spoon, and measuring cups.
- A washable table, tray, or outdoor area.
- A trash bag or compost-safe disposal plan, not a sink drain.

## Steps

1. **Measure the powder.** Put two parts cornstarch in the bowl. → *Expect:* a mound of dry white powder fills the bowl.
2. **Add water slowly.** Add about one part water in small pours while stirring. → *Expect:* the mixture changes from powder to thick paste.
3. **Adjust the texture.** [BRANCH: too dry | too runny] add drops of water if crumbly, or cornstarch if soupy. → *Expect:* the mixture flows slowly but resists quick pressure.
4. **Test a fast hit.** Tap the surface quickly with a finger or spoon. → *Expect:* it feels firm for a moment.
5. **Test a slow move.** Rest fingers on the surface and let them sink. → *Expect:* the mixture behaves like a thick liquid.
6. **Squeeze and release.** Roll some oobleck into a ball, then open your hand. → *Expect:* it firms under pressure and then drips.
7. **Name the rule.** Explain that tiny starch particles jam under sudden force and slide during slow motion. → *Expect:* the strange feel has a simple cause.
8. **Contain play.** Keep the bowl over the tray and wipe dropped bits before they dry. → *Expect:* the mess stays local.
9. **Dispose safely.** Scrape oobleck into trash or compost where allowed, then wash residue from tools. → *Expect:* no thick starch goes down the drain.

## Decision points

- Younger children are involved → use a shallow tray and small batch.
- Food coloring is added → protect surfaces and hands from stains.
- The mixture dries out → stir in teaspoons of water.
- The mixture splashes → switch from hitting to slow stretching.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too runny:** detect watery liquid with no firm feel → add cornstarch a spoonful at a time.
- **F2 Too crumbly:** detect dry chunks that will not flow → add water a teaspoon at a time.
- **F3 Drain risk:** detect oobleck near the sink → scrape it into trash before rinsing thin residue.
- **F4 Slippery floor:** detect drops underfoot → stop play and wipe the floor immediately.

## Verification

The batch firms under quick force, flows under slow force, and is disposed of without putting thick oobleck down a drain.

## Variations

- Add washable color for visual contrast.
- Use a speaker or vibrating tray only with adult setup and very small amounts.
- Compare spoon tapping with hand squeezing to feel different force speeds.

## Safety & privacy

Adult supervision and cleanup are required, because wet starch is slippery and can clog plumbing.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* Thick oobleck can harden in pipes, so never pour the batch down the drain.
