## Goal

You can turn a clean sock into a puppet with a working mouth, eyes, hair, and a simple voice or personality.

## Preconditions

- A clean sock long enough to cover your hand.
- Glue, felt or paper, yarn, buttons or craft eyes, and scissors.
- Adult supervision if using hot glue, needles, small parts, or sharp scissors.

## Steps

1. **Test the mouth fold.** Put your hand in the sock with fingers in the toe and thumb under them, then open and close your hand. → *Expect:* the toe bends like a mouth.
2. **Mark the face.** With the sock on your hand, lightly mark where eyes and hair should sit above the mouth. → *Expect:* features land where the audience will see them.
3. **Add the mouth shape.** Cut a felt oval or paper oval and glue it inside the folded mouth area. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* glue can stain or set fast, so confirm placement before pressing. → *Expect:* the mouth has a colored inside when it opens.
4. **Glue on eyes.** Attach craft eyes, buttons, or felt circles above the fold, evenly spaced but not perfectly if you want character. → *Expect:* the puppet has a clear gaze.
5. **Attach yarn hair.** Cut several yarn strands, tie or glue them together at the center, and attach them to the top of the head. → *Expect:* hair moves when the puppet talks.
6. **Give it a voice.** [BRANCH: silly voice | serious narrator] Pick one voice and a favorite phrase, then move the mouth once per syllable. → *Expect:* speech looks connected to hand motion.
7. **Rehearse a tiny scene.** Have the puppet introduce itself, ask a question, and react to an answer. → *Expect:* the puppet feels like a character, not just a decorated sock.

## Decision points

- If the sock is stretchy, use fabric glue or sewing instead of brittle craft glue.
- If making for a young child, avoid buttons and loose small parts.
- If the mouth does not open clearly, move the hand deeper into the toe and exaggerate the thumb motion.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Features slide:** glue has not set → lay the sock flat and let each layer dry before adding the next.
- **F2 Mouth hidden:** decorations sit too low → place eyes and nose above the mouth fold, not on it.
- **F3 Stiff puppet:** too much glue blocks hand movement → keep glue away from the crease line.
- **F4 Loose hair:** yarn peels off → tie a small bundle first, then glue the knot area.

## Verification

The puppet opens and closes its mouth on your hand, has secure eyes and hair, and performs a three-line introduction.

## Variations

- Sewn features last longer than glued features for heavy play.
- Felt eyebrows, glasses, mustaches, or a paper tongue can define the character quickly.
- Two puppets make conversation easier than one puppet monologuing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use child-safe materials for young users, supervise scissors and hot glue, let adhesives dry before play, and do not share photos of children or names without permission.
