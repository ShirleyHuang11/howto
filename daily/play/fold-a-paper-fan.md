## Goal

You can fold a sheet of paper into even accordion pleats, secure the base, and open it into a handheld fan.

## Preconditions

- A rectangular sheet of paper; printer paper, origami paper, or magazine paper works.
- Tape, string, rubber band, or a staple to secure the base.
- A flat surface for sharp creases.

## Steps

1. **Place the paper landscape.** Lay the long edge left to right so the finished fan has width. → *Expect:* the sheet is oriented wider than tall.
2. **Fold the first pleat.** Fold one short edge over by about 1 to 2 cm and crease firmly. → *Expect:* the first strip sets the pleat width.
3. **Flip and repeat.** Turn the paper over, fold the same width back the other way, and crease. → *Expect:* an accordion pattern begins.
4. **Keep widths even.** Continue flipping and folding, using the previous pleat as a guide. [BRANCH: exact pleats | relaxed pleats] Exact looks crisp; relaxed still works if widths stay close. → *Expect:* a stacked strip with alternating folds.
5. **Compress the pleats.** Run a fingernail or ruler along the stack to sharpen all creases. → *Expect:* the strip springs open slightly but keeps its folds.
6. **Fold the base.** Pinch one end of the strip together and fold the bottom 2 to 3 cm upward to make a handle area. → *Expect:* one end becomes a firm grip.
7. **Secure the base.** Tape, tie, band, or staple the folded base. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* stapling makes holes, so confirm the handle end before pressing. → *Expect:* the base stays closed when released.
8. **Open the fan.** Spread the free end into a semicircle and adjust pleats until the shape looks even. → *Expect:* the fan moves air when waved gently.

## Decision points

- If the fan is floppy, use thicker paper or narrower pleats.
- If it will not open wide, the base may be secured too high.
- If pleats drift unevenly, mark light guide dots along both edges before folding.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Crooked stack:** each fold leans more than the last → realign edges every two pleats instead of waiting.
- **F2 Weak handle:** tape or band slides off → fold a deeper base and secure it in two spots.
- **F3 Torn paper:** creases were forced too hard on thin paper → use gentler pressure or a fresh sheet.
- **F4 No airflow:** fan is too small or limp → use larger paper and sharpen the pleats.

## Verification

The fan opens into a stable semicircle, stays secured at the base, and moves noticeable air when waved.

## Variations

- Two folded sheets taped together make a wider fan.
- Decorative paper looks finished without extra coloring.
- A craft stick glued into the base makes a stronger handle.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use staples and scissors carefully, keep small rubber bands away from young children, and avoid waving the fan near faces or candles.
