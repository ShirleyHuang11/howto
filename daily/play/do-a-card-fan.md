## Goal

Spread a deck into a clean card fan, close it without dropping cards, and practice the flourish smoothly.

## Preconditions

- A deck of cards, preferably smooth and not sticky.
- Clean, dry hands.
- A table or soft surface beneath the practice area.
- Permission to use the deck for handling practice.

## Steps

1. **Square the deck.** Tap the cards into a neat block with all edges aligned. → *Expect:* the deck starts compact.
2. **Set the grip.** Hold the deck in the non-dominant hand with the long edge resting across the fingers. → *Expect:* the thumb can move across the top card edges.
3. **Angle the deck.** Tilt the outer corner slightly upward and keep light pressure at the inner corner. → *Expect:* the cards are ready to pivot from one point.
4. **Push with the thumb.** Sweep the thumb across the top edge in a smooth arc. → *Expect:* cards spread one after another instead of in clumps.
5. **Control pressure.** [BRANCH: clumps | gaps] reduce pressure for clumps, or add pressure if cards fly too far apart. → *Expect:* the fan becomes more even.
6. **Support the fan.** Let the fingers underneath hold the curved spread. → *Expect:* the fan stays open without sagging.
7. **Close cleanly.** Push the outer cards back toward the pivot point and square the deck. → *Expect:* the cards return to one packet.
8. **Practice both directions.** Try the fan with the other hand or reverse sweep. → *Expect:* one side feels easier, but both improve.
9. **Add the flourish.** Present the fan briefly, then close it before the spread collapses. → *Expect:* the motion looks intentional and controlled.

## Decision points

- Cards are sticky → use a newer deck or clean hands.
- The fan is uneven → slow down and focus on steady thumb pressure.
- The deck is valuable → practice with a cheaper deck first.
- Hands are small → use half the deck until the movement is comfortable.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Card clumps:** detect several cards moving as one → loosen the thumb and square the deck again.
- **F2 Cards drop:** detect the fan slipping from the fingers → practice over a table and reduce the spread width.
- **F3 Bent corners:** detect pressure at one corner only → support the deck more evenly.
- **F4 Rough close:** detect cards catching or folding → close slowly from the outside edge inward.

## Verification

The deck opens into a visible fan with mostly even spacing, then closes back into a squared packet without dropped or bent cards.

## Variations

- Pressure fan uses more thumb pressure and a stronger pivot for a wider arc.
- Table fan spreads cards on a smooth surface instead of in the hand.
- Half-deck fan is easier for children and beginners.

## Safety & privacy

Practice away from drinks, food, and fragile card decks.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* Bent corners can mark a card permanently, so stop when resistance rises.
