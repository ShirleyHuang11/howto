## Goal

A beginner keeps a hula hoop spinning around the waist by choosing a workable hoop size and using rhythmic weight shifts.

## Preconditions

- Open floor space with breakable objects moved away.
- A hoop that reaches roughly from floor to navel or lower chest when standing upright.
- Comfortable clothes that do not snag.
- Shoes or bare feet with stable traction.
- Enough room to step forward, back, and sideways safely.

## Steps

1. **Choose a beginner-friendly hoop.** Pick a larger, slightly heavier hoop rather than a tiny toy hoop. → *Expect:* hoop reaches at least the navel when vertical.
2. **Stand inside the hoop.** Hold it against the small of the back with both hands. → *Expect:* hoop is level around the waist.
3. **Set the feet.** Place one foot slightly ahead of the other, knees soft. → *Expect:* body can shift forward and back without leaning.
4. **Start with a flat push.** Push the hoop around the waist strongly and level, not upward. → *Expect:* hoop makes one clean rotation before dropping.
5. **Move with a push, not a circle.** Shift weight forward and back, bumping the hoop each time it touches the belly or back. → *Expect:* hoop speeds up from repeated pulses.
6. **Keep the torso tall.** Look forward and keep shoulders relaxed. → *Expect:* hips move under a steady upper body.
7. **Correct dropping early.** If the hoop slides down, bend knees slightly and pulse faster. → *Expect:* hoop climbs or stabilizes before it reaches the thighs.
8. **Restart efficiently.** When it falls, catch it, reset at the back, and push in the easier direction. → *Expect:* restarts take seconds instead of breaking rhythm.
9. **Practice both directions.** Try clockwise and counterclockwise starts after finding the easier side. → *Expect:* one direction feels natural and the other improves slowly.
10. **Stop with control.** Slow the pulses and catch the hoop with a hand. → *Expect:* hoop stops without hitting nearby objects.

## Decision points

- Hoop drops immediately → choose a larger or heavier hoop and push harder at the start.
- Hoop tilts sharply → keep hands level at launch and avoid twisting shoulders.
- Waist hooping hurts → use a lighter hoop or shorter sessions.
- Space is crowded → move outdoors or to a room with more clearance.
- Progress stalls → practice 1-minute sets with frequent clean restarts.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Spinning hips in circles:** the hoop loses speed; switch to front-back or side-side weight shifts.
- **F2 Weak launch:** hoop falls before rhythm starts; push firmly from the back with a level release.
- **F3 Overbending forward:** posture collapses; stand taller and soften knees instead.
- **F4 Hoop hits furniture:** space is too tight; clear a wider circle before continuing.
- **F5 Skin bruising:** hoop is too heavy or sessions are too long; stop and resume another day with less force.

## Verification

The hoop completes several waist rotations while the player maintains balance, uses weight shifts, and can catch or restart it safely.

## Variations

- Side-to-side stance: feet parallel and hips shift left-right instead of front-back.
- Arm hooping: use a lighter hoop and keep the arm extended with small circles.
- Group play: space players far enough apart that falling hoops do not collide.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Clear the area, avoid weighted fitness hoops for children, and stop if pain, dizziness, or bruising develops.
