## Goal

Build a small popsicle-stick catapult with rubber bands, a fulcrum stack, a throwing arm, and a bottle-cap cup for soft projectiles.

## Preconditions

- Popsicle sticks, rubber bands, tape or glue, and a clean plastic bottle cap.
- Soft projectiles only, such as pom-poms, mini marshmallows, or crumpled paper.
- A clear target area away from faces, breakables, pets, and food prep.
- Adult help if hot glue, craft knife, or drilling is used.

## Steps

1. **Stack the fulcrum.** Place six to eight sticks in a neat stack and wrap rubber bands tightly around both ends. → *Expect:* the stack acts like a firm block.
2. **Make the arm pair.** Stack two sticks together and band one end so they open like a narrow mouth. → *Expect:* one end is hinged and the other end can separate.
3. **Insert the fulcrum.** Slide the thick stack between the two arm sticks near the banded end. → *Expect:* the upper stick lifts into a springy throwing arm.
4. **Band the crossing.** Wrap a rubber band in an X around the fulcrum and arm so the stack cannot slide out. → *Expect:* the frame stays together when the arm is pressed.
5. **Attach the cup.** Tape or glue a bottle cap near the free end of the upper arm with the open side up. → *Expect:* the cap holds one soft projectile without wobbling.
6. **Aim safely.** [BRANCH: target | distance-test] Point at a paper cup target or an empty floor zone, never at a person, animal, glass, screen, or light. → *Expect:* the launch path is clear before loading.
7. **Launch lightly.** Press the arm down a short distance, release, and adjust the fulcrum position for more or less range. → *Expect:* the projectile travels toward the target without parts flying loose.

## Decision points

- Need more range: move the fulcrum slightly toward the cup, then test with less pull first.
- Rubber band looks cracked: replace it before it snaps.
- Using glue: let it cure fully before launching.
- Indoor play: use paper targets and soft projectiles only.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Frame slides apart:** the fulcrum moves during launch, add an X band or tape at the crossing.
- **F2 Cup falls off:** adhesive failed, reattach and let glue cure longer.
- **F3 Projectile flies wild:** the cup is crooked or pull is too hard, realign and reduce force.
- **F4 Rubber band snaps:** band is old or overstretched, discard pieces and install a fresh band.

## Verification

The catapult is verified when it launches one soft projectile at a clear target while the frame, bands, and cup remain attached.

## Variations

- `science`: measure distance after moving the fulcrum one centimeter at a time.
- `no-glue`: tape the bottle cap with two crossed strips and test gently.
- `team-game`: score only hits on floor targets, not height or force.

## Safety & privacy

Risk is medium because even small launchers can hit eyes or damage objects. Use soft projectiles, aim low, keep faces out of the launch line, and stop if anyone crosses the target area.
