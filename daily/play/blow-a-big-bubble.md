## Goal

Make a large soap bubble using a flexible wand, a strong bubble solution, and slow movement in still air.

## Preconditions

- Dish soap, water, glycerin or sugar, a shallow tray, string, and two sticks or straws are available.
- Outdoor or easy-clean indoor space is available.
- The area is away from slippery floors, electronics, and open flames.

## Steps

1. **Mix the solution.** Combine 6 parts water, 1 part dish soap, and either 1 part glycerin or 1 spoon of sugar per cup of water. → *Expect:* the mixture is smooth without heavy foam.
2. **Rest the solution.** Let it sit for at least 15 minutes, longer if possible. → *Expect:* bubbles on the surface settle down.
3. **Build the wand.** Tie string between two sticks or straws to make a loop with a smaller top span and a longer bottom span. → *Expect:* the loop hangs open like a loose triangle.
4. **Wet the wand fully.** Dip the entire string loop into the tray and lift it slowly. → *Expect:* a thin soap film fills the loop.
5. **Find still air.** [BRANCH: outdoors | indoors] Stand where there is a faint breeze or still air; avoid fans and gusts. → *Expect:* the film does not rip immediately.
6. **Open the loop.** Hold the sticks apart gently without snapping the film. → *Expect:* the soap film stretches between the strings.
7. **Pull slowly.** Walk backward or move the wand in one slow steady pull. → *Expect:* a tube of bubble film forms and grows.
8. **Close the bubble.** Bring the sticks together or twist the wand slightly when the bubble is large enough. → *Expect:* the bubble pinches off and floats free.
9. **Adjust one variable.** If bubbles pop quickly, add a little glycerin or sugar; if the film is heavy, add water. → *Expect:* the next bubble lasts longer or opens more easily.

## Decision points

- Air is windy → wait, move behind a wall, or use a smaller wand.
- Solution is too foamy → skim foam or let it rest longer.
- Bubble film will not form → add soap a little at a time and re-dip the whole string.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Film tears immediately:** detect holes as soon as the wand lifts → clean the wand, reduce wind, and re-dip.
- **F2 Bubble pops at launch:** detect popping when closing the loop → close more slowly and avoid jerking the sticks.
- **F3 Solution is weak:** detect small bubbles only → add glycerin or sugar and rest the mix.
- **F4 Slippery floor:** detect puddles or soap on walking surfaces → stop and wipe before continuing.

## Verification

A soap film stretches from the wand and pinches off into at least one large free-floating bubble.

## Variations

- Small wand: bend a pipe cleaner or wire loop and use the same solution.
- Giant wand: use longer sticks and cotton string that absorbs solution well.
- Color play: shine light through bubbles rather than adding dye that can stain.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Soap makes floors slippery and can irritate eyes, so keep solution off walkways and rinse skin or eyes with clean water if needed.
