## Goal

An avocado pit is started in water, rooted with patience, and moved to soil once it has enough growth to behave like a houseplant.

## Preconditions

- A fresh avocado pit, rinsed clean without removing the brown skin.
- Three or four toothpicks, a glass, water, and a sunny windowsill with indirect light.
- Potting soil and a small pot for later.

## Steps

1. **Find top and bottom.** The pointy end goes up, and the flatter wider end goes down. [BRANCH: obvious point | round pit] if unsure, put the slightly broader end in water. → *Expect:* the pit has an assigned sky side and water side.
2. **Insert toothpicks.** Push three toothpicks around the middle at a slight downward angle. → *Expect:* the pit can rest on a glass rim without falling in.
3. **Suspend over water.** Fill the glass so the bottom third of the pit sits in water. → *Expect:* the lower end is wet and the upper end is dry.
4. **Place in bright indirect light.** Set the glass where it is warm but not baking. → *Expect:* the water stays visible and the pit is not hot to the touch.
5. **Refresh the water.** Change water every few days and top it up between changes. → *Expect:* the submerged end stays wet and the glass does not smell.
6. **Wait for splitting and roots.** Give it 2 to 8 weeks without declaring failure too early. → *Expect:* the pit cracks, then a root appears from the bottom.
7. **Pot when ready.** Once roots are several centimeters long and a shoot has leaves, plant the pit halfway above soil. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* breaking the main root sets the plant back badly, so handle it by the pit, not the root. → *Expect:* roots are covered and the top half of the pit remains visible.
8. **Set expectations.** Keep soil lightly moist and treat fruit as unlikely indoors. → *Expect:* the plant is a leafy experiment, not a guaranteed guacamole farm.

## Decision points

- Water turns cloudy → change it and rinse the glass.
- Pit molds lightly → wipe it and improve airflow.
- No crack after 8 weeks → start a second pit while keeping the first if it still looks firm.
- Leaves droop after potting → water lightly and keep out of direct hot sun.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pit rots:** detect by mushy texture or sour smell → discard and start with a fresher pit.
- **F2 Root dries out:** detect by shriveled brown root tip → restore water quickly, but expect slower growth.
- **F3 Stem gets leggy:** detect by a long pale shoot → move to brighter indirect light.
- **F4 Soil stays soggy:** detect by fungus gnats or sour smell → let the top soil dry and improve drainage.

## Verification

The potted pit has a visible shoot, intact roots under soil, and new leaves that remain firm for one week.

## Variations

- Direct soil method: plant the pit halfway in moist soil and skip toothpicks.
- Classroom version: label several pits and compare start dates.
- Warmer climates: an outdoor avocado tree still may not match the fruit variety you ate.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Toothpicks are sharp, glass can break, and avocado plant parts can be unsafe for some pets if chewed.
