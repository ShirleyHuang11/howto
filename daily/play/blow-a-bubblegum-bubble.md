## Goal

A visible bubble (5 cm or larger) is blown from a piece of bubblegum and either popped intentionally or pulled back into the mouth without gum ending up on face or clothes.

## Preconditions

- Actual bubble gum (labeled "bubble gum"), not regular chewing gum. Regular gum lacks the film-forming gum base and will only sputter.
- No loose long hair hanging near the mouth, or hair tied back.
- A mirror helps for the first attempts but is not required.

## Steps

1. **Chew the piece until the sugar is mostly gone.** This takes 3 to 5 minutes; two pieces are easier than one for beginners. → *Expect:* the gum is smooth, warm, and stretchy, no longer gritty or intensely sweet.
2. **Flatten the wad against the roof of your mouth with your tongue.** Press it into a rough disc, then move the disc so it sits just behind your front teeth. → *Expect:* a flat pancake of gum you can feel spanning the area behind the teeth.
3. **Make the tongue pocket.** Push the tip of your tongue gently into the center of the flattened disc, stretching a thin cup of gum over the tongue tip (like a sock over a toe). → *Expect:* the tongue tip is wrapped in a thin, translucent-feeling layer of gum; the gum has not torn.
4. **Part your teeth and lips slightly and withdraw the tongue.** Keep the gum sheet anchored by your teeth as the tongue pulls back, leaving a small hollow pocket facing outward. → *Expect:* a small gum membrane sits between slightly open lips.
5. **Blow a slow, steady breath into the pocket.** Use gentle, continuous pressure from the lungs, not a sharp puff. Sharp puffs blow the gum out of your mouth. → *Expect:* the membrane inflates outward and a bubble grows past your lips.
6. **Manage the endgame.** [BRANCH: pop it (bite down and inhale slightly so the film collapses inward onto your lips and teeth) | recover it (stop blowing, suck inward gently, and chew the deflated bubble back into the wad)] → *Expect:* gum is entirely back in the mouth; nothing on skin or clothes.

## Decision points

- Gum tears every time at step 3 → it is not chewed enough or it is chewing gum, not bubble gum; chew longer or switch brands.
- Bubble grows then instantly pops small → film too thick and uneven; re-flatten more thoroughly and stretch a thinner pocket.
- Outdoors on a cold day → gum stiffens fast; keep it warm in the mouth between attempts.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gum shoots out of the mouth:** you puffed instead of breathing steadily → retrieve or discard the gum, start a new piece, and practice exhaling as if fogging a mirror.
- **F2 Bubble pops onto the face:** film got too big or too thin → peel the gum off with the main wad itself (gum lifts gum better than fingers do), then wipe with a dry cloth.
- **F3 Gum in hair or on fabric:** do not pull → work peanut butter, cooking oil, or ice into the spot; oil dissolves the gum base, ice makes it brittle enough to crumble off.
- **F4 No bubble ever forms, only spit sounds:** the pocket has a hole at its edge → rebuild the seal by pressing the gum sheet against the back of the teeth all the way around before blowing.

## Verification

A bubble at least the size of your mouth opening was visible to an observer (or in the mirror), and afterward all gum is back in the mouth or in the trash, with none stuck to face, hair, or clothing.

## Variations

- Double-bubble: once a medium bubble is stable, pause, seal it with your lips, form a second small pocket, and blow a bubble inside or on top of the first. Advanced; expect many failures.
- Contest sizing: for maximum diameter use 2 to 3 pieces, chew 10+ minutes to remove all sugar, and blow in slow pulses, pausing to let the film relax.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not attempt while walking, cycling, or lying on your back (inhaled gum is a choking hazard, especially for children under 5, who should not have gum at all). Discard swallowed-feeling or dirty gum rather than rescuing it.
