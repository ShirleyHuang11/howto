## Goal

Shuffle a standard deck so the cards are mixed, squared, and still easy to handle.

## Preconditions

- A complete deck of playing cards.
- A clean, dry table or a soft play surface.
- Hands clean enough that cards will not stick.
- Enough patience to mix more than once.

## Steps

1. **Square the deck.** Tap the short and long edges on the table until the cards form one block. → *Expect:* the deck sits flat with no corners sticking far out.
2. **Choose a shuffle.** [BRANCH: riffle | overhand] use the riffle for stronger mixing, or the overhand for gentler handling. → *Expect:* you have one clear method for this pass.
3. **Split for a riffle.** Hold half the deck in each hand, inner corners close together, thumbs on the inside edges. → *Expect:* two roughly equal packets meet at one corner.
4. **Release the corners.** Bend only enough for the thumb edges to spring down and interlace. → *Expect:* cards fall in small alternating groups instead of two solid blocks.
5. **Bridge lightly.** Push the interlaced cards inward, arch them just enough to relax together, then let them settle. → *Expect:* one joined deck forms without sharp creases.
6. **Use an overhand pass.** Hold the deck in one hand and pull small packets from the top into the other hand. → *Expect:* several small groups transfer and change order.
7. **Repeat the mix.** Do three to seven passes, alternating riffle and overhand if desired. → *Expect:* top and bottom cards change between passes.
8. **Square and offer.** Tap all four edges, then place the deck where play can continue. → *Expect:* the deck is neat, flat, and ready to cut or deal.

## Decision points

- Cards are new and stiff → prefer overhand shuffles until they loosen.
- Cards are valuable or borrowed → avoid hard bridges and shuffle on a padded surface.
- A game requires a cut → shuffle first, then let another player cut.
- Children are learning → teach the overhand first because it bends cards less.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cards spray apart:** detect cards sliding out of both hands → gather them face down, square, and use smaller packets.
- **F2 Deck bends too much:** detect a permanent bow or corner crease → stop bridging and use overhand passes.
- **F3 Poor mixing:** detect the same top or bottom cards after each pass → split at different places and repeat.
- **F4 Sticky cards:** detect clumps that will not separate → wipe hands, clean the table, and replace damaged cards.

## Verification

The deck is squared, no card is visibly damaged, and several cards from the original top or bottom are no longer together.

## Variations

- Table riffle: place both halves on the table and release the inner corners with thumbs.
- Hindu shuffle: pull packets from the top into the palm, common in some regions.
- No-bend shuffle: make several overhand passes and cuts for delicate cards.

## Safety & privacy

Keep drinks away from the deck, and do not force a bridge if the cards resist.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* A hard bend can permanently crease a card, so use light pressure before trying showy shuffles.
