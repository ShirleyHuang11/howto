## Goal

You can fold a square sheet into sixths, cut patterns that survive the folds, and unfold a six-sided paper snowflake.

## Preconditions

- Thin paper, scissors, and a flat work surface.
- Adult supervision for children using scissors.
- Optional pencil for sketching cut shapes before cutting.

## Steps

1. **Make a square.** Fold one corner of rectangular paper across to align with the opposite edge, then trim the extra strip. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* cutting changes the sheet, so confirm the fold is aligned first. → *Expect:* one neat square.
2. **Fold into a triangle.** Fold the square corner to corner and crease sharply. → *Expect:* a triangle with clean matched edges.
3. **Fold the triangle in half.** Bring the two sharp corners together and crease, then reopen if you need a center guide. → *Expect:* a center line that helps divide the paper.
4. **Fold to sixths.** Bring one side across about one-third, then overlap the other side to make a narrow cone shape with three stacked wedges. → *Expect:* the top forms a 60-degree wedge with mostly aligned edges.
5. **Trim the uneven top.** Cut across the wide end to make a clean wedge tip. → *Expect:* a compact folded wedge ready for pattern cuts.
6. **Cut shapes from the edges.** Snip triangles, curves, diamonds, and notches from both long sides. Do not cut the whole fold through from one side to the other. → *Expect:* holes appear while the wedge still holds together.
7. **Open slowly.** Unfold one layer at a time, easing tight cuts with fingertips instead of pulling hard. [BRANCH: clean unfold | snag] If it snags, reverse and loosen the cut. → *Expect:* a six-sided snowflake appears without tearing.
8. **Flatten the reveal.** Press under a book for a few minutes or smooth gently with clean hands. → *Expect:* the snowflake lies flat enough to display.

## Decision points

- If the folds are bulky, use thinner paper or make fewer tiny cuts.
- If the snowflake falls apart, the cuts crossed the folded spine too completely.
- If the shape looks too solid, add small edge cuts, not one large center bite.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Four-point snowflake:** the paper was folded into quarters → refold using the thirds overlap in step 4.
- **F2 Confetti collapse:** too many cuts severed the structure → leave uncut bridges along each folded edge.
- **F3 Ragged edges:** scissors are dull or paper is too thick → switch paper or make larger simpler cuts.
- **F4 Torn unfold:** impatience catches a thin bridge → open layer by layer and press flat afterward.

## Verification

The unfolded paper has six repeated arms, visible cutout shapes, and remains connected as one piece.

## Variations

- Coffee filters fold easily and make soft round snowflakes.
- Pencil half-shapes along the folded edges make the final symmetry easier to predict.
- Tape the finished flake to a window, or string several as a garland.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use age-appropriate scissors, cut away from fingers, clean up small scraps, and keep sharp tools away from unattended children.
