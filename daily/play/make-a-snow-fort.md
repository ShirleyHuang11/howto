## Goal

Build a small snow fort for play with stable walls, clear exits, and no collapse-prone roof or tunnel.

## Preconditions

- Outdoor snow is available away from roads, driveways, roofs, and plowed snowbanks.
- Warm waterproof clothing, gloves, and boots are worn.
- An adult or capable spotter is present for children.

## Steps

1. **Choose a safe site.** Pick flat ground away from traffic, roofs, trees with heavy snow, and hidden water. → *Expect:* the fort area has clear space on all sides.
2. **Choose the build method.** [BRANCH: pack blocks | pile and hollow] Pack blocks with buckets or tubs, or pile snow and hollow only shallow wall openings. → *Expect:* the method fits the snow texture and available tools.
3. **Mark the footprint.** Draw a circle, U shape, or low rectangle with at least one wide opening. → *Expect:* everyone can see the wall line and exit.
4. **Build thick low walls.** Stack blocks or packed snow with walls at least 30 cm thick and no higher than chest height for the smallest player. → *Expect:* walls stand without leaning inward.
5. **Pack each layer.** Press snow firmly into gaps and stagger block joints like bricks. → *Expect:* joints are filled and the wall does not wobble when tapped lightly.
6. **Follow the safe-roof rule.** Keep the fort open to the sky unless an adult uses a rated structure outside this recipe. → *Expect:* no one is under a snow roof.
7. **Avoid tunnels.** Do not dig crawl tunnels into piles, plow banks, or deep drifts because they can collapse without warning. → *Expect:* all play spaces are open or shallow enough to step out of.
8. **Smooth sharp edges.** Round corners and clear loose chunks from the floor. → *Expect:* the inside has fewer trip points and no hard protrusions.
9. **Check stability before play.** Push lightly on each wall section from outside and repair weak spots. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* collapse can trap or injure a child; stop play if any wall slumps or cracks. → *Expect:* walls remain upright during a gentle check.

## Decision points

- Snow is powdery and will not pack → wait for wetter snow or mist lightly with water in freezing weather.
- Players are very young → build knee-high walls only.
- A roof is requested → use blankets over chairs indoors instead of a snow roof.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wall slumps:** detect leaning or cracking → knock down the weak section and rebuild lower and thicker.
- **F2 Cold hands or wet clothing:** detect numb fingers, shivering, or soaked layers → go inside and warm up before continuing.
- **F3 Hidden hazard:** detect ice, rocks, road salt, or debris in the snow → move to a cleaner area.
- **F4 Unsafe digging:** detect a tunnel, cave, or roof forming → collapse it from outside and return to open-wall play.

## Verification

The fort has stable low walls, at least one open exit, no snow roof, and no crawl tunnel.

## Variations

- Block fort: pack snow into bins, invert, and stack like bricks.
- Pile-and-hollow fort: make a low mound and carve shallow standing spaces, not enclosed rooms.
- Decoration: use sticks, leaves, or food coloring only where they will not create sharp hazards.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from cold exposure and collapse. Supervise children, keep the structure open, and never play in roadside plow piles.
