## Goal

Grow visible sugar or salt crystals from a supersaturated solution on a seed string over several days.

## Preconditions

- Adult supervision, especially if heating water.
- Sugar or salt, clean water, a jar, string, pencil, and spoon.
- A safe place where the jar can sit undisturbed for days.
- Optional food coloring for sugar crystals only.

## Steps

1. **Prepare the seed.** Tie string to a pencil so the string hangs into the jar without touching the bottom. → *Expect:* the string hangs straight in the center.
2. **Make hot water safe.** Have an adult heat water until hot but not dangerously splashing. → *Expect:* the water can dissolve extra solid.
3. **Add solid gradually.** Stir in sugar or salt until no more dissolves and a few grains remain. → *Expect:* the liquid is supersaturated or close to it.
4. **Cool briefly.** Let the solution stop steaming before it goes into the jar. → *Expect:* the jar is warm, not scalding.
5. **Pour and suspend.** Pour the solution into the jar and lay the pencil across the top with the string hanging in. → *Expect:* the string is covered by liquid.
6. **Leave it alone.** Place the jar where it will not be bumped. → *Expect:* evaporation begins slowly.
7. **Check daily.** [BRANCH: crystals forming | no crystals] observe growth if present, or wait another day before changing anything. → *Expect:* small facets appear on the string or jar.
8. **Reveal the crystals.** Lift the string after several days when crystals are large enough to see. → *Expect:* solid crystals cling to the string.
9. **Clean the jar.** Save crystals on paper and dissolve leftover crust with warm water. → *Expect:* the jar is clean and the workspace is dry.

## Decision points

- Sugar crystals are desired → use sugar and avoid tasting lab materials.
- Salt crystals are desired → expect smaller, clearer cubes or crusts.
- No growth after two days → add a seeded string dusted with dry sugar or salt.
- Mold or bad smell appears → discard the batch and wash the jar.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Undersaturated solution:** detect no crystals after several days → remake with more dissolved sugar or salt.
- **F2 String touches jar:** detect crystals forming mostly on glass → recenter the string.
- **F3 Bumped jar:** detect broken or cloudy crystals → leave it still for another day.
- **F4 Hot-water burn risk:** detect children reaching toward hot liquid → stop and let an adult handle pouring.

## Verification

Visible crystals form on the seed string or jar, and all hot-water equipment is cleaned and put away.

## Variations

- Use separate jars to compare sugar and salt crystal shapes.
- Use a pipe cleaner instead of string for more seed points.
- Cover the jar with a coffee filter to reduce dust while allowing evaporation.

## Safety & privacy

Adult supervision is required for hot water, glass jars, and cleanup.
⚠️ *Irreversible:* Do not eat crystals from an experiment jar because tools and hands may have contaminated them.
