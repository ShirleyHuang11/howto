## Goal

Play a fair round of I Spy where players take turns giving clues about visible objects and guessing them.

## Preconditions

- At least two players can see a shared area.
- Players agree whether moving objects are allowed.
- Everyone can hear the clue giver.

## Steps

1. **Set the play area.** Choose a room, car view, park bench view, or waiting area that all players can see. → *Expect:* everyone knows what counts as visible.
2. **Pick the first spy.** Choose the youngest player, rotate clockwise, or let the last winner start. → *Expect:* one person is responsible for the secret object.
3. **Choose a fair object.** The spy silently picks something visible long enough for others to inspect. → *Expect:* the object is real and currently in the shared view.
4. **Give the classic clue.** Say, "I spy with my little eye something..." followed by a color, shape, first letter, texture, or category. → *Expect:* the clue narrows the field without naming the object.
5. **Tune clue difficulty by age.** [BRANCH: young child | older child | mixed ages] use color and size for younger players, letters or functions for older players, and multiple clues for mixed ages. → *Expect:* guessers have a reasonable chance.
6. **Take guesses in turn.** Each player makes one guess per turn, with no grabbing or pointing into another player's face. → *Expect:* guesses move around the group fairly.
7. **Add clues if stuck.** After three wrong rounds, give another true clue such as location, material, or use. → *Expect:* the answer becomes easier without changing.
8. **Reveal and rotate.** When someone guesses correctly, show the object and make that player the next spy. → *Expect:* everyone agrees what the object was.
9. **Use car-trip rules if moving.** Pick objects inside the car or things that stay visible long enough, such as "something blue in the car." → *Expect:* the game does not depend on objects already passed.

## Decision points

- Players are very young → allow pointing after several guesses.
- The object leaves view → cancel the round and let the same spy choose again.
- A clue has multiple valid answers → accept any object that fits or add a clarifying clue.
- One player dominates guesses → require strict turn order.
- The setting needs quiet → use whisper clues and hand-raise guesses.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vanished object:** detect no one can see it anymore, recover by restarting with a stationary object.
- **F2 Impossible clue:** detect a clue only the spy could know, recover by replacing it with visible color, shape, or location.
- **F3 Argument over answer:** detect multiple matching objects, recover by accepting the first matching guess or giving a tie clue.
- **F4 Turn skipping:** detect one player guessing repeatedly, recover by naming the next guesser each turn.
- **F5 Too hard for age:** detect long silence or frustration, recover by adding broader clues or choosing bigger objects.

## Verification

A round succeeds when a guesser identifies the exact visible object and the next spy begins a new round.

## Variations

- Car trip: restrict answers to inside the vehicle or long-lasting outside landmarks.
- Waiting room: use quiet voices and objects within the seating area.
- Pre-readers: use colors, sizes, and "starts like" sounds instead of letters.
- Team play: let pairs discuss quietly before giving one guess.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. In cars, the driver should not be the spy or guesser, and clues should not encourage anyone to unbuckle, block mirrors, or distract the driver.
