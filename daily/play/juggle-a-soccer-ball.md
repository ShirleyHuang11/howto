## Goal

You can keep a soccer ball in the air for a short streak using controlled laces touches, then build toward alternating feet.

## Preconditions

- A properly inflated soccer ball.
- Flat open space with no windows, cars, pets, or bystanders close by.
- Shoes with laces or a firm top surface; bare feet can work but are less forgiving.

## Steps

1. **Pick a soft target area.** Stand on grass, turf, or a court with room for bad rebounds. → *Expect:* missed balls roll harmlessly instead of hitting fragile things.
2. **Find the laces surface.** Point the toe slightly down and lock the ankle so the broad top of the foot meets the lower half of the ball. → *Expect:* your foot feels like a small flat paddle.
3. **Start with a bounce.** Drop the ball, let it bounce once, then tap it upward with the laces to waist height. Catch it. → *Expect:* one clean upward touch you can repeat.
4. **Repeat single touches.** Drop, bounce, touch, catch for 20 reps on the stronger foot, then 20 on the other foot. → *Expect:* the ball rises mostly vertical instead of shooting forward.
5. **Add two touches after one bounce.** Bounce, touch, let the ball fall, touch again, then catch. [BRANCH: same foot | alternate feet] Use the same foot for control or alternate to train symmetry. → *Expect:* two intentional contacts before the catch.
6. **Remove the catch gradually.** Let the ball bounce between touches, then reduce the bounce height until the next touch happens before the ground. → *Expect:* the ball spends more time in the air than on the floor.
7. **Count the streak.** Count only laces touches that keep the ball below chest height and within one step. Reset after a wild chase. → *Expect:* a meaningful record, not a lucky ricochet.
8. **Train the weak foot early.** Finish each session with five attempts starting on the weaker foot. → *Expect:* progress feels uneven but the ball stops avoiding one side.

## Decision points

- If the ball flies forward, your toe is too high or your ankle is loose.
- If every touch is tiny, strike slightly lower on the ball and lift the knee.
- If your streak only works with one foot, spend a round alternating after every bounce.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Locked knee poke:** the ball rockets away → bend the knee and lift from the thigh with a firm ankle.
- **F2 Toe stab:** the ball spins backward and escapes → point the toe down enough to use the laces.
- **F3 Chasing practice:** you count saves across the yard → reset if you need more than one step.
- **F4 Frustration kicks:** missed attempts turn into hard shots → pause, collect the ball by hand, and restart with bounce touches.

## Verification

You complete five controlled juggles in a row, each below chest height and within one step of your starting spot.

## Variations

- Futsal balls are heavier and less bouncy, which can make control practice calmer.
- Thigh juggles are a useful bridge, but keep the main count to feet if that is the skill goal.
- A wall nearby can help retrieval, provided it is not glass or shared with people.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Clear breakables, stop before ankle or hip soreness, and avoid juggling near roads or shared walkways where a bad touch can surprise someone.
