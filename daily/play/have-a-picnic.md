## Goal

A group has eaten a comfortable outdoor meal at a chosen site and left the spot exactly as found: no trash, no food scraps, no damage.

## Preconditions

- A weather forecast checked the same day (wind matters as much as rain; steady wind above ~20 km/h makes plates and napkins airborne).
- A site where picnicking is permitted: public park, beach, or private land with permission. Some parks ban alcohol or open flames; check posted rules.
- A carry kit: blanket with a waterproof underside (or a tarp under a regular blanket), food and drink, cups, a trash bag, wipes or hand sanitizer, and a bag or cooler that closes.
- Food chosen to travel: things that survive an hour unrefrigerated and don't need last-second assembly. Good: hard cheeses, cured meats, bread, whole fruit, pasta or grain salads dressed in oil (not mayo), cookies, nuts. Risky: mayonnaise salads, sushi, cream desserts, anything that must stay below 5 °C for more than an hour without ice.

## Steps

1. **Pack in reverse order of use.** Blanket on top (first thing out), then plates and drinks, food at the bottom; cold items together against an ice pack. Liquids upright and double-checked for sealed lids. → *Expect:* nothing leaking when the bag is tilted; blanket accessible without unpacking.
2. **Choose the exact spot on arrival.** Look for level ground, partial shade, no ant trails or ground-wasp holes, and reasonable distance from paths, trash bins (wasps), and ponds (mosquitoes). Under a tree beats open sun for food longevity. → *Expect:* a flat, dry, shaded patch with no insect traffic across it.
3. **Lay the blanket and weight the corners.** Waterproof side down; corners held by shoes, the cooler, or bags against gusts. → *Expect:* blanket flat and staying put in the wind.
4. **Set out food in stages, not all at once.** Keep the cooler closed between rounds; put out what will be eaten in the next 20 minutes. Keep lids on or cloths over open dishes. → *Expect:* food stays cool and mostly covered; flies and wasps have no open target.
5. **Eat, with one running rule: trash into the bag immediately.** A dedicated open trash bag from minute one prevents the end-of-picnic scatter hunt. Wind-prone wrappers go straight in. → *Expect:* no loose wrappers on or off the blanket at any point.
6. **Manage sun and time on perishables.** Move the blanket if shade shifts. Perishables out of the cooler for over 2 hours (1 hour above 32 °C) get eaten now or thrown out, not repacked. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* food poisoning from repacked warm perishables; when in doubt, throw it out. → *Expect:* nothing marginal goes back in the cooler.
7. **Break camp with a line sweep.** Pack food first, then shake the blanket downwind away from people, then walk the whole area in a slow pass looking for caps, corks, toys, and micro-trash. Crumbs and food scraps go in the trash bag too: "biodegradable" scraps feed pests and are still littering. → *Expect:* the ground shows no trace the group was there.
8. **Carry trash out unless a proper bin exists.** [BRANCH: park bin present and not overflowing (use it) | bin absent or full (the bag rides home with you)] → *Expect:* sealed trash bag disposed of correctly; hands cleaned.

## Decision points

- Rain arrives → food into the closed bag first, people second; a wet blanket dries, soggy food is done.
- Wasps discover the food → don't swat (it recruits more); cover everything, move the sweet drinks 5 m away as a decoy, relocate the blanket if they persist.
- Someone forgot a utensil or opener → bread tears, hard cheese breaks, and a coin or seatbelt-buckle bottle-opener trick exists; improvise before anyone drives back.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ants on the blanket within minutes:** you laid it on a trail → don't fight them; move the blanket 3-5 m and re-inspect the ground first this time.
- **F2 Spilled drink on the blanket:** blot with napkins, tilt the wet zone off the food area, and keep the waterproof side down doing its job.
- **F3 Melted or warm cooler by mid-picnic:** lid was open too long → triage: eat dairy and meat now, keep only commercially sealed items.
- **F4 Overflowing park bin:** never stack trash on top (animals scatter it) → your bag goes home with you; that is the leave-no-trace default anyway.

## Verification

Everyone has eaten; the departure sweep found nothing left on the site (including crumbs, caps, and peels); all trash is in a sealed bag in a proper bin or in your possession; the blanket and kit are packed and drier than the ground you left.

## Variations

- Beach picnic: swap the blanket for a sand-shedding mat, weight everything (constant wind), and triple the sun protection; sand in food is the tax you pay for the view.
- Cold-weather picnic: thermoses of soup or hot drinks replace the cooler problem with the opposite one; sit on insulated pads, not just the blanket.
- Public-grill sites: bring your own fuel and tools, confirm fires are allowed that day (fire-risk bans override posted signs), and douse coals fully before leaving.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall. The real hazards are food-safety (the 2-hour rule in step 6), sun exposure, and allergy cross-contact in shared spreads (label or separate nut-containing items). Leave-no-trace is also a legal matter in many parks: littering fines apply, and feeding wildlife (even accidentally via scraps) harms the animals and future picnickers.
