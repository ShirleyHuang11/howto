## Goal

Play a fair round of hopscotch by drawing a court, tossing a marker, hopping the pattern, and taking turns.

## Preconditions

- Chalk and pavement, or tape and an indoor floor.
- A small marker such as a stone, beanbag, or bottle cap.
- At least one player and enough clear space to hop safely.

## Steps

1. **Draw the court.** Make numbered boxes from 1 to 10, mixing single boxes and side-by-side pairs. → *Expect:* the court has a start end, a turn-around end, and numbers that are easy to read.
2. **Pick the marker.** Use something flat enough to stay put and soft enough not to bounce far. → *Expect:* the marker can land inside a square without rolling across lines.
3. **Set the turn order.** Decide who goes first and whether touching a line counts as a miss. → *Expect:* every player knows the house rules before the first toss.
4. **Toss to square 1.** Stand behind the start line and throw the marker underhand into the first square. → *Expect:* the marker rests inside square 1 and does not touch a boundary line.
5. **Hop out, skipping the marker square.** Hop on one foot in single boxes and land with one foot in each paired box. → *Expect:* you pass square 1 without stepping on it or losing balance.
6. **Turn at the top.** At the last box or pair, pivot carefully and prepare to come back. → *Expect:* you face the start line without stepping outside the court.
7. **Pick up the marker on the return.** Stop in the square before the marker, bend, pick it up, then hop over that square. → *Expect:* marker is in hand and your foot never lands in its square.
8. **Finish the pass.** Hop back over the start line and step out of the court. → *Expect:* the turn is complete with no line touch, missed square, or second foot in a single box.
9. **Advance the target.** On the next successful turn, toss to the next number. [BRANCH: success | miss] → *Expect:* success moves your target up one number, while a miss passes play to the next person.
10. **Win by clearing all squares.** Keep rotating turns until a player completes the highest number. → *Expect:* the winner has finished every numbered target in order.

## Decision points

- The marker lands on a line → count it as a miss if that was agreed before play.
- Younger players struggle with one-foot hops → allow two feet in single boxes as a beginner variation.
- Indoor floor is slippery → use tape for the court and a cloth marker, or play outside.
- Players disagree on a turn → replay the turn once instead of arguing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Marker skids too far:** detect when it leaves the target box, recover by switching to a beanbag or flatter stone.
- **F2 Court numbers confuse players:** detect repeated wrong-direction hops, recover by redrawing arrows or larger numbers.
- **F3 Line stepping dispute:** detect when players call different fouls, recover by restating the rule and applying it from the next turn.
- **F4 Balance loss:** detect when a second foot drops in a single square, recover by ending that turn and trying again next round.

## Verification

A completed turn has a legal toss, correct hop pattern, marker pickup, and return over the start line without a foul.

## Variations

- Spiral court: draw a snail shape and hop inward, then outward.
- Team relay: each player clears one number before tagging the next player.
- Rainy-day version: painter's tape and a soft marker protect indoor floors.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the court away from roads, wet pavement, stairs, and glass; remove chalk afterward if the location requires it.
