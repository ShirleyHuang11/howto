## Goal

Turn browned fond in a pan into a concentrated sauce base without burning it or washing flavor down the sink.

## Preconditions

- A pan with brown fond, not black scorched residue.
- Food has been removed from the pan and is resting.
- Stock, wine, cider, beer, juice, or water is measured nearby.
- A wooden spoon, silicone spatula, or flat whisk is ready.

## Steps

1. **Pause the heat.** Move the pan off heat if the fond is very dark or the fat is smoking; pour off excess fat, leaving a thin film → *Expect:* sizzling calms and browned bits remain on the pan
2. **Choose the liquid.** [BRANCH: wine or cider for acidity | stock for savory body | water for neutral flavor] measure 2 tablespoons to 1/2 cup → *Expect:* liquid is ready before the hot pan needs it
3. **Add liquid safely.** Return to medium heat and pour liquid in from the side. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* alcohol vapor can flare, so turn gas off briefly for wine or spirits → *Expect:* steam rises and the fond starts loosening
4. **Scrape thoroughly.** Drag the spoon across the bottom and corners while the liquid bubbles → *Expect:* brown bits dissolve into the liquid
5. **Reduce.** Simmer until the liquid is glossy and slightly syrupy → *Expect:* volume drops and bubbles look tighter
6. **Taste late.** Taste after reducing, then add salt, acid, or a small knob of butter → *Expect:* flavor is roasted and balanced
7. **Use the base.** Pour into a sauce, spoon over food, or continue with cream or butter → *Expect:* pan is mostly clean and sauce tastes browned, not burnt

## Decision points

- Brown fond smells roasted -> deglaze it; black fond smells acrid -> discard it.
- Wine over a gas burner -> turn flame off for the pour, then relight.
- Adding dairy -> reduce first, then lower heat before cream or butter.
- Nonstick pan -> expect less fond and a lighter sauce.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned fond:** detect black flakes and acrid smoke -> wipe the pan and start a fresh sauce base.
- **F2 Too thin:** detect watery liquid -> simmer in a wide pan until it coats a spoon.
- **F3 Too salty:** detect saltiness after reduction -> dilute with unsalted liquid and reduce only briefly.
- **F4 Greasy surface:** detect oil pooling -> spoon off fat or whisk in a splash of water.

## Verification

The fond has dissolved into a reduced liquid that tastes savory and roasted without burnt bitterness.

## Variations

- No alcohol: use stock plus a few drops of vinegar or lemon.
- Creamy: reduce wine or stock first, then add cream gently.
- Tomato: deglaze with wine, then add crushed tomato and simmer.
- Vegetarian: use mushroom or vegetable fond with miso water or stock.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from steam, hot fat, and alcohol flare.
Keep your face back when liquid hits the pan and turn gas off briefly before adding alcohol.
