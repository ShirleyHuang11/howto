## Goal

Cook frozen vegetables so they are hot, seasoned, and not waterlogged.

## Preconditions

- Frozen vegetables, a pan, steamer basket, sheet pan, or skillet.
- Oil, salt, pepper, and optional aromatics such as garlic, lemon, chili flakes, or herbs.
- A stove or oven and a way to drain excess water if needed.

## Steps

1. **Keep them frozen until cooking.** Do not thaw unless the package specifically says to. → *Expect:* vegetables go from freezer to heat while still icy.
2. **Choose the method.** [BRANCH: roast | steam | saute] Roast for browned edges, steam for tender plain vegetables, or saute for fast seasoning. → *Expect:* method matches the texture you want.
3. **Use high heat for roasting.** Heat oven to 220 C and preheat the sheet pan if safe for the pan. → *Expect:* vegetables sizzle when they hit the pan.
4. **Spread or contain properly.** Roast in one layer, steam in a basket over simmering water, or saute in a wide skillet with a little oil. → *Expect:* vegetables are not piled into a wet mound.
5. **Cook without early stirring.** Let roasting or sauteing vegetables sit for a few minutes before moving them. → *Expect:* moisture cooks off and browning begins.
6. **Drain if steaming.** Stop steaming when vegetables are hot and bright, then shake off water. → *Expect:* vegetables are tender but not sitting in liquid.
7. **Season after moisture leaves.** Add salt, pepper, butter, lemon, sauce, or herbs near the end. → *Expect:* seasoning sticks to the vegetables instead of dissolving into water.
8. **Serve immediately.** Move vegetables to a warm plate or bowl. → *Expect:* texture stays firm enough to bite and color remains bright.

## Decision points

- Vegetables are small like peas or corn → saute or steam briefly rather than roast for a long time.
- Vegetables are large like broccoli florets → roast hotter and longer so centers heat through.
- Pan fills with water → raise heat, spread vegetables wider, or drain before seasoning.
- Package includes sauce → follow package heat instructions, then adjust seasoning after tasting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy vegetables:** detect puddled water and limp texture, recover by draining and cooking uncovered over higher heat.
- **F2 Scorched edges with cold centers:** detect burning outside and icy middle, recover by lowering heat slightly and covering for 1 to 2 min.
- **F3 Bland flavor:** detect watery taste, recover by seasoning after draining and adding acid or fat.
- **F4 Sticking to pan:** detect torn pieces on skillet, recover by waiting before stirring and adding a small amount of oil.

## Verification

The vegetables are hot through, free of standing water, seasoned after cooking moisture is gone, and still have visible structure.

## Variations

- Air fryer: cook from frozen at high heat, shaking once or twice.
- Microwave: use only a little water, drain well, then season after heating.
- Fried rice or pasta: add frozen vegetables directly to the hot pan early enough to drive off water.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from steam and hot pans. Open lids away from your face, and do not handle preheated sheet pans without dry mitts.
