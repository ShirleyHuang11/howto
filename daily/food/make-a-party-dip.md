## Goal

Make a party dip that tastes balanced, holds its texture on the table, and works with sturdy dippers.

## Preconditions

- A dip base such as sour cream, cream cheese, Greek yogurt, mashed beans, or refried beans is available.
- Seasonings, mix-ins, a bowl, spoon, and serving dish are ready.
- Dippers such as chips, crackers, vegetables, or bread are available.

## Steps

1. **Pick the base.** [BRANCH: creamy base | bean base] use sour cream or softened cream cheese for cool dips, or mashed beans for a thicker savory dip. → *Expect:* the base is smooth enough to stir.
2. **Loosen only if needed.** Add a spoonful of milk, lime juice, salsa, or olive oil if the base is too stiff. → *Expect:* a chip can scoop it without snapping.
3. **Season in layers.** Stir in salt, acid, spice, and aromatics such as garlic powder, scallions, herbs, cumin, or hot sauce. → *Expect:* seasoning is evenly distributed with no dry pockets.
4. **Add texture.** Fold in grated cheese, chopped vegetables, herbs, beans, or cooked bacon if using. → *Expect:* mix-ins are small enough to fit on a chip.
5. **Taste and adjust.** Try the dip with the actual dipper, not a plain spoon. → *Expect:* it tastes slightly bold because cold dips dull flavor.
6. **Control thickness.** Add more base if too salty or spicy, or add a small amount of liquid if too dense. → *Expect:* the dip mounds softly and does not run.
7. **Chill to meld.** Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes when time allows. → *Expect:* flavors taste more blended and harsh raw notes soften.
8. **Stir before serving.** Recheck thickness and taste after chilling. → *Expect:* any separated liquid is mixed back in.
9. **Serve with sturdy dippers.** Choose thick chips, pita, crackers, celery, carrots, or toasted bread. → *Expect:* dippers can scoop without breaking.
10. **Refresh safely.** Put out a small bowl and refill from the refrigerator rather than leaving the whole batch out. → *Expect:* the serving bowl stays neat and cool.

## Decision points

- Dip tastes flat → add acid first, then salt, then heat.
- Dip is for a long party → serve in smaller batches and keep backup chilled.
- Dippers are fragile → thin the dip slightly or switch to sturdier dippers.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too salty:** detect harsh salt after tasting with a chip, recover by adding more unsalted base or beans.
- **F2 Too loose:** detect dip sliding off a chip, recover by chilling longer or adding cream cheese, beans, or grated cheese.
- **F3 Raw garlic bite:** detect sharp lingering burn, recover by adding more base and chilling longer.
- **F4 Broken chips:** detect dippers snapping in the bowl, recover by offering vegetables, pita, or thicker chips.

## Verification

The dip can be scooped with the chosen dipper, tastes balanced, and has chilled at least 30 minutes if served cold.

## Variations

- `hot-dip`: transfer to an oven-safe dish and bake until bubbling, then keep warm.
- `vegan`: use mashed beans, tahini, avocado, or plant-based yogurt as the base.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, mostly food handling. Refrigerate dairy, meat, and bean dips promptly, discard dip that sat out more than 2 hours, and label common allergens for guests.
