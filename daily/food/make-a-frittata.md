## Goal

A tender frittata is cooked through, lightly browned on top, and released from the pan in one piece.

## Preconditions

- Eggs, salt, pepper, and 1 to 2 cups cooked or quick-cooking fillings are available.
- An oven-safe nonstick or well-seasoned skillet is available.
- The oven broiler or oven can heat safely, and an oven mitt is within reach.

## Steps

1. **Heat the finish source.** [BRANCH: oven at 350 F | broiler on low or with rack 6 inches from heat] → *Expect:* the oven or broiler is hot before the eggs go into the pan.
2. **Prep the fillings.** Slice vegetables thin, grate cheese, and pat wet items dry. → *Expect:* fillings are bite-size and no puddle of moisture sits on the board.
3. **Saute the fillings.** Cook raw vegetables, potatoes, or meats in oil until tender; season them before adding eggs. → *Expect:* fillings smell cooked and any released liquid has boiled off.
4. **Mix the eggs.** Whisk 6 large eggs with 1/4 cup dairy or water, 1/2 teaspoon salt, and pepper. → *Expect:* mixture is uniform yellow with no streaky whites.
5. **Set the bottom.** Lower heat to medium-low, spread fillings evenly, pour eggs over them, and nudge edges inward for 2 to 4 minutes. → *Expect:* edges thicken while the center still jiggles.
6. **Finish gently.** Move the skillet to the oven or broiler. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* an overheated handle burns skin, so mark it with a towel or mitt before anyone touches it. → *Expect:* top is just set, with faint browning if broiled.
7. **Check doneness.** Insert a thin knife near the center and tilt the pan slightly. → *Expect:* no loose liquid egg runs into the cut.
8. **Slide out.** Rest 2 minutes, loosen the edge with a silicone spatula, and slide onto a plate. → *Expect:* frittata moves as one round without tearing badly.

## Decision points

- Fillings are watery, such as mushrooms or spinach → cook until dry before adding eggs.
- Pan is not oven-safe → keep heat low, cover the pan, and skip the broiler.
- Top browns before center sets → move from broiler to oven heat or cover on the stovetop.
- Frittata is for a crowd → use 8 eggs in a 10-inch skillet and extend finish time.
- Cheese is salty → reduce added salt by about one-third.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rubbery texture:** detect firm, spongy eggs and browned bottom; recover next time with lower heat and shorter finish.
- **F2 Wet center:** detect liquid egg in a center cut; recover by returning to a 350 F oven for 2 minutes.
- **F3 Stuck bottom:** detect resistance when sliding the spatula under the edge; recover by resting 3 minutes and loosening slowly.
- **F4 Burned filling:** detect bitter smell before eggs are added; recover by discarding scorched bits and wiping the pan.
- **F5 Hot handle burn:** detect a bare oven skillet handle; recover by covering it with a towel and warning everyone nearby.

## Verification

A center cut shows fully set egg, the underside is not blackened, and a slice lifts cleanly enough to hold its shape.

## Variations

- Dairy-free: use water instead of milk or cream for a lighter curd.
- Mini version: use 3 eggs in an 8-inch skillet and shorten all cook times.
- Spanish-style: use cooked sliced potatoes and onions, then finish covered on low heat.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot fat, hot metal, and undercooked egg. Keep the oven mitt on the handle after the skillet leaves the oven.
