## Goal

A whole roast chicken with crisp, deeply browned skin and juicy meat throughout, verified safe by thermometer and rested before carving.

## Preconditions

- A whole chicken, 1.5-2 kg. Ideally bought a day ahead: the dry brine (step 1) works best overnight.
- Coarse salt, an instant-read thermometer (the non-negotiable tool of this recipe), an oven-safe pan or roasting tray, ideally with a rack.
- An oven that genuinely reaches 220 °C; kitchen twine optional for trussing.

## Steps

1. **Dry brine, ideally the day before.** Remove the giblet bag from the cavity, pat the bird completely dry with paper towels, and salt it all over (about 1 tsp coarse salt per 500 g), including inside the cavity. Refrigerate uncovered on a rack, breast up, overnight or up to 2 days. The salt seasons deep and the fridge air dries the skin, which is what makes it crisp. Short on time: even 1 hour uncovered helps. → *Expect:* next day the skin looks dry, taut, and slightly translucent, not tacky.
2. **Do not wash the chicken, ever.** Rinsing sprays campylobacter and salmonella around the sink in droplets; cooking kills what washing spreads. → *Expect:* bird goes from fridge to pan with no water involved; anything that touched raw chicken heads to the sink for hot soapy washing.
3. **Bring toward room temperature and prep the oven.** Bird out of the fridge 30-45 minutes ahead; oven to 220 °C (200 °C fan) with the rack in the lower-middle position. Tie the legs together if trussing; tuck the wingtips under the body either way so they do not char. → *Expect:* oven fully preheated; skin patted dry once more if any moisture reappeared.
4. **Roast breast-up without opening the door.** Bird on the rack in the pan, into the oven. High heat renders the skin instead of steaming it. Expected time: roughly 50-70 minutes for a 1.5-2 kg bird. Opening the door "to check" dumps the heat; use the light. → *Expect:* audible sizzling within 15 minutes; skin turning gold by the halfway mark, deep brown near the end.
5. **Temp it in the right places.** When the skin is deep brown and juices at the leg joint look clear, probe the thickest part of the breast (from the neck end, not touching bone) and the inner thigh. Targets: breast 68-71 °C rising with carryover to 74 °C; thigh at least 74 °C, and thighs are better at 80 °C plus. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* undercooked poultry served to the table cannot be un-served; if either reading is low, back in for 10 minutes and re-probe. → *Expect:* both targets met; thermometer probe wiped or washed after touching underdone meat.
6. **Rest 15-20 minutes before carving.** Move the bird to a board or warm platter, tent very loosely with foil (a tight wrap softens the skin you just earned). Resting lets the juices redistribute; carving hot pours them onto the board instead. → *Expect:* only modest juice on the board after resting; pan juices reserved for sauce.
7. **Carve in order: legs, then breasts.** Remove each leg at the hip joint, separate drumstick from thigh, then slice each breast off the keel bone in one piece and cut crosswise. → *Expect:* breast slices moist and opaque throughout; no pink translucence at the bone.

## Decision points

- No time for any dry brine → salt heavily, dry the skin aggressively with towels, and accept slightly less crisp skin; the thermometer steps do not change.
- Skin at deep brown but thigh under temperature → drop the oven to 180 °C and continue; color is done, cooking is not.
- Bird much larger than 2 kg → same method, roughly 20 minutes per 500 g as a planning number, but the thermometer is the only real clock.
- Want vegetables → potatoes and onions under the rack catch the fat, but crowd the pan and they steam; one layer only.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Breast reads done, thigh does not:** normal anatomy. Return the bird legs-toward-the-back-wall (the hotter zone) for 10 minutes; the rested breast tolerates it better than serving raw thigh.
- **F2 Pale, flabby skin:** bird went in wet, oven too cool, or foil tented tightly during the rest. Rescue: 5 minutes under a hot broiler, watched the entire time.
- **F3 Smoking oven from dripping fat:** normal at high heat with a fatty bird. Slide a pan with a little water on the rack below; do not open the door repeatedly.
- **F4 Pink juice or pink flesh at the joints after resting:** re-probe. Under 74 °C in the thigh: carve off the legs and return just them to the oven; the breast stays rested.
- **F5 Cross-contamination slip (raw juice on counter or hands touched the salt jar):** hot soapy water on every touched surface immediately; when in doubt, wash it. This failure is invisible and the one that actually sends people to the doctor.

## Verification

Thermometer confirmed at least 74 °C final in both thigh and breast, skin is browned and crisp, the bird rested 15+ minutes before the first cut, breast slices show no translucent pink, and every surface or tool that touched raw chicken has been washed in hot soapy water.

## Variations

- Spatchcocked: cut out the backbone and flatten; cooks in about 45 minutes at 220 °C with more even breast/thigh timing. Worth learning as the everyday method.
- Herb butter under the breast skin: loosen the skin from the neck end and spread softened butter in; adds flavor at slight cost to crispness.
- `fr` style: start at 240 °C for 15 minutes then 200 °C; baste with pan butter; softer skin, richer pan juices.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk on two fronts. Food safety: raw poultry contaminates everything it touches; never wash the bird, do wash hands, boards, and the thermometer probe, and trust only the thermometer for doneness. Physical: a heavy pan of hot fat coming out of a 220 °C oven wants two mitts, a cleared landing spot, and no one standing behind you.
