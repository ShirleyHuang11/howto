## Goal

A smooth pan gravy thickened with a fat-flour roux and seasoned from drippings or stock.

## Preconditions

- Pan drippings or fat, flour, stock or broth, salt, and pepper.
- Whisk, measuring spoon, saucepan or roasting pan, and heatproof cup.
- Optional fine strainer for extra smooth gravy.

## Steps

1. **Separate usable fat.** Spoon 2 tbsp fat from drippings, or use butter if there is not enough. → *Expect:* fat is measured apart from watery juices.
2. **Keep the flavorful liquid.** Deglaze browned bits with stock or pour pan juices into a cup. → *Expect:* you have about 2 cups liquid total.
3. **Cook the roux.** Heat 2 tbsp fat, whisk in 2 tbsp flour, and cook 1 to 2 minutes. → *Expect:* paste smells nutty and no longer raw-floury.
4. **Whisk in liquid gradually.** Add warm stock a splash at a time at first, whisking smooth before adding more. → *Expect:* paste loosens without lumps.
5. **Simmer to thicken.** Bring to a gentle simmer for 3 to 5 minutes. → *Expect:* gravy coats a spoon and bubbles lazily.
6. **Season after thickening.** Taste, then add salt, pepper, and any reserved pan juices. → *Expect:* savory flavor is clear without harsh salt.
7. **Fix texture before serving.** [BRANCH: too thick | too thin] whisk in stock to loosen, or simmer longer to reduce. → *Expect:* gravy pours in a ribbon.
8. **Strain if needed.** Pour through a fine strainer for a smoother finish. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* heavy salt cannot be removed except by dilution, so season in small pinches. → *Expect:* hot gravy ready in a warm boat or saucepan.

## Decision points

- Drippings are very salty → use low-salt stock and season only at the end.
- No drippings → use butter for the roux and a flavorful stock.
- Want darker gravy → cook the roux a little longer to tan, but do not burn it.
- Need gluten-free → use cornstarch slurry instead of roux; that is a different thickening method.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lumpy gravy:** liquid was added too fast; whisk hard off heat or blend briefly, then strain.
- **F2 Greasy surface:** too much fat; skim with a spoon or blot the surface with a paper towel.
- **F3 Flour taste:** roux was undercooked; simmer several more minutes while whisking.
- **F4 Bland flavor:** stock was weak; reduce longer and add pan juices, pepper, or a splash of soy sauce.

## Verification

The gravy is smooth, hot, savory, and thick enough to coat a spoon while still pouring easily.

## Variations

- `turkey`: use turkey drippings and stock, plus a little sage if desired.
- `beef`: use beef drippings and stock, with black pepper.
- `mushroom`: saute mushrooms in the fat before adding flour.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use caution with hot fat and bubbling gravy, and keep poultry or meat drippings heated thoroughly before serving.
