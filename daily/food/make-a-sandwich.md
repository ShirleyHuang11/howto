## Goal

A structurally sound, tasty sandwich — fillings stay in, bread isn't soggy, and it's cut and ready to eat or pack.

## Preconditions

- Bread (2 slices or a roll), at least one protein or main filling, and any of: cheese, vegetables, condiments.
- A clean cutting board and a spreading knife; a sharp knife for cutting.
- Clean hands (`daily/self-care/wash-hands`).

## Steps

1. **Lay out both bread slices and audit the fillings.** Everything sliced/ready before assembly. Wet things (tomato, pickles) get patted dry with a paper towel. → *Expect:* all components staged; wet items visibly drier.
2. **Apply the moisture barrier.** Spread butter/mayo edge-to-edge on *both* inner faces — the fat layer is what keeps juicy fillings from soaking the bread. → *Expect:* full coverage to the crusts, thin and even.
3. **Build in stability order.** Cheese or large leaf flat on the base → protein → wet/slippery items (tomato, cucumber) in the *middle* of the stack → lettuce/greens on top. Keep the footprint inside the bread's edges. → *Expect:* a level stack, nothing overhanging, wet items sandwiched between dry layers.
4. **Season the inside.** Salt/pepper directly on the tomato layer; condiment accents (mustard, hot sauce) go on now, not pooled on the bread. → *Expect:* seasoning where the flavor lives, bread still dry.
5. **Cap, and compress gently with a flat palm.** → *Expect:* layers seat together; nothing squirts out the sides.
6. **Cut with a sharp knife, decisively.** Diagonal or half; a serrated blade *saws* soft bread rather than pressing (pressing ejects fillings). Claw grip on the holding hand. → *Expect:* clean cut faces, stack intact in both halves.
7. **Serve or wrap.** Eating later: wrap snugly in paper/wrap and refrigerate if it contains meat, egg, or dairy (`daily/food/store-leftovers` windows apply). → *Expect:* sandwich plated, or wrapped tight enough to hold its build.

## Decision points

- Toasted version → toast the bread first and let it cool ~1 min before the barrier layer (hot bread melts mayo into sog).
- Packed lunch eaten in 4+ hours → carry wet items (tomato, dressing) separately and add at eating time; or accept the barrier layer's limits.
- Big appetite vs. structural limit → a taller stack fails at the bite; go wider (two modest sandwiches) not taller.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy bread at eating time:** the barrier (2) or drying (1) was skipped — this sandwich is toast-it-open-faced territory; fix the process next round.
- **F2 Fillings slide out at the first bite:** slippery layer touching slippery layer — rebuild with the friction order of step 3 (leaf against cheese, wet items pinned centrally).
- **F3 Bread tore during spreading:** butter was fridge-hard — microwave the butter 5–8 s or switch to mayo; patch the tear by rotating it to the top slice, barrier-side down.

## Verification

The sandwich holds together through a bite test at the cut face, the bread is dry to the touch outside, fillings are inside the footprint, and the board and knife are cleaned.

## Variations

- Baguette/roll: hollow out a little crumb from the top half to seat fillings; the crust does the structure.
- Grilled cheese: butter goes on the *outside* faces; medium-low pan, flip once when golden.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: knife discipline at step 6 (claw grip, sharp blade, board not hand) and the cold-chain rule for packed meat/egg/dairy are the two real rules.
