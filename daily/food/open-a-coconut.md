## Goal

A mature coconut drained, cracked open, and separated into usable water and flesh without unsafe chopping.

## Preconditions

- Whole mature coconut, screwdriver or skewer, bowl, towel, hammer or heavy pan, and butter knife.
- Stable counter or floor surface that can handle firm tapping.
- Eye protection or a towel wrap if the shell chips easily.

## Steps

1. **Find the soft eye.** Inspect the three eyes and press each with a skewer or screwdriver. → *Expect:* one eye gives more easily than the others.
2. **Open the eye.** Twist through the soft eye and widen the hole. → *Expect:* a clean drain hole reaches the hollow center.
3. **Drain the water.** Invert the coconut over a bowl and shake until flow stops. → *Expect:* coconut water collects without shell pieces.
4. **Check the water.** Smell and taste a small amount if it looks clear. → *Expect:* it smells fresh and mildly sweet, not sour or fermented.
5. **Crack the equator.** Wrap in a towel or hold firmly, then tap around the middle line with a hammer or heavy pan. → *Expect:* a crack forms around the coconut.
6. **Split the shell.** Continue tapping along the crack until the halves separate. → *Expect:* two shell halves expose white flesh.
7. **Pry the flesh.** [BRANCH: chunks | clean shell cups] Use a butter knife to lift chunks, or warm halves briefly to loosen cleaner pieces. → *Expect:* flesh releases with brown skin attached.
8. **Trim and rinse.** Peel brown skin if desired and rinse away shell dust. → *Expect:* coconut flesh is clean and ready to grate, slice, or eat.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* cracking the shell cannot be undone; drain the water first if you want to save it.

## Decision points

- Sour smell or pink, fizzy water → discard the coconut.
- Shell resists cracking → rotate and tap patiently around the equator instead of swinging hard.
- Need fine shreds → pry large pieces first, then grate with the brown skin removed if texture matters.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Eye will not pierce:** wrong eye or dull tool → test the other eyes and use a clean screwdriver.
- **F2 Shell chips fly:** blows are too sharp → wrap in a towel and tap through the cloth.
- **F3 Flesh sticks tight:** coconut is fresh and firm → warm halves in a low oven for a few minutes, then pry again.
- **F4 Water tastes off:** coconut is spoiled → discard water and flesh.

## Verification

Coconut water is drained cleanly, shell is split, and edible flesh is separated without sharp shell fragments.

## Variations

- Young coconut: trim the husk and open the top with a cleaver only if trained and stable.
- Oven assist: bake drained coconut briefly to help the shell crack and flesh release.
- Toasting: shave or grate flesh, then toast slowly for dry applications.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hard shell, impact tools, and shell shards. Do not hold the coconut in your palm while striking hard, and keep fingers outside the impact path.
