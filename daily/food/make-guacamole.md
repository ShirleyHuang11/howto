## Goal

A bowl of chunky, well-seasoned guacamole made from properly ripe avocados, bright with lime and salt, protected from browning until served.

## Preconditions

- 2-3 ripe avocados (the whole recipe lives or dies on ripeness; see step 1).
- Fresh lime (bottled juice is a distant fallback), salt; optional: white or red onion, cilantro, jalapeño or serrano, tomato, garlic.
- A bowl, fork, knife, cutting board, spoon; plastic wrap or a lidded container if not serving immediately.

## Steps

1. **Test ripeness before cutting anything.** Cradle the avocado in your palm and press gently with the whole hand, not a thumbtip (thumb-pressing bruises it): ripe yields slightly, like a relaxed muscle. Flick out the small stem nub: green underneath means ripe, brown means overripe, stuck-tight means underripe. → *Expect:* gentle give plus a green stem scar on every avocado you commit to.
2. **Prep the mix-ins first.** Finely dice onion (rinse the dice in cold water for 30 s to tame raw-onion harshness), mince chili (seeds out for mild), chop cilantro, dice tomato if using (seed it first; tomato water makes guac runny). → *Expect:* small even dice staged in the bowl or beside it; hands washed after chili.
3. **Halve, pit, and scoop the avocados.** Cut lengthwise around the pit, twist the halves apart. Remove the pit with a spoon or by prying with fingertips, not by whacking a knife into it (a top cause of home hand injuries). Scoop the flesh into the bowl with a spoon. → *Expect:* all flesh in the bowl; any grey or stringy patches cut away; pits and skins discarded.
4. **Add lime juice and salt before mashing.** Juice of about half a lime and a generous pinch of salt per avocado; the acid coats the flesh immediately and slows browning from the first second. → *Expect:* juice distributed over the flesh, no dry surfaces.
5. **Mash with a fork, deliberately short of smooth.** Press and fold until it holds together but distinct chunks remain; a food processor makes paste, which is a different (worse) food. → *Expect:* creamy base with visible avocado pieces throughout.
6. **Fold in the mix-ins, then season in rounds.** Taste; adjust lime and salt alternately in small additions until the flavor snaps into focus. Under-salted guacamole tastes flat and "missing something"; the answer is nearly always salt or lime, not more chili. → *Expect:* each adjustment tastes brighter; you stop when one more taste makes you keep eating.
7. **Serve now, or seal against air.** [BRANCH: serving immediately → done | holding → press plastic wrap into full contact with the surface (zero air pockets), or float a thin layer of lime juice or water on top and pour it off later; refrigerate] Browning is oxidation: no air contact, no browning. The pit-in-the-bowl trick only shields the area it touches. → *Expect:* surface fully covered, no trapped air bubbles; fridge if held over 30 minutes.

## Decision points

- Avocados all underripe and needed today → 2-3 days in a paper bag with a banana ripens them; nothing ripens them in hours. Change the menu or buy ripe ones.
- One avocado is overripe with brown streaks → cut away brown, smell it: grassy is fine to blend in, sour or musty means discard that one.
- Crowd-size batch → make it no more than 2-3 hours ahead, surface-sealed and refrigerated; stir once before serving.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Browned surface after holding:** air reached it → scrape off the thin brown layer with a spoon; the green guac underneath is unaffected. Stir a little fresh lime in and re-seal.
- **F2 Watery, loose guac:** unseeded tomato or overripe fruit → stir in a small amount of extra mashed avocado if available, or drain off pooled liquid; seed tomatoes next time.
- **F3 Bitter or rubbery bites:** underripe avocado forced through → no fix once mixed; fish out the hardest chunks; verify ripeness (step 1) before cutting next time.
- **F4 One-note or flat flavor:** seasoning stopped too early → return to alternating small additions of salt and lime; a tiny pinch of cumin or a minced garlic clove adds depth if it still lags.

## Verification

The guacamole is green throughout (no brown streaks), chunky rather than pureed, tastes bright and salted enough that a plain chip's worth is compelling on its own, and any held portion has an airtight surface seal.

## Variations

- Minimalist (avocado, lime, salt only): the test of good fruit; many taquerias serve exactly this.
- Salsa-style: fold in a spoonful of prepared salsa instead of individual mix-ins; faster, wetter.
- No lime on hand: lemon works nearly as well; rice vinegar in a pinch; skipping acid entirely costs both flavor and browning protection.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk for one specific reason: "avocado hand", the knife-into-pit and knife-through-flesh-into-palm injuries that send people to emergency rooms. Pit with a spoon, cut on a board, never in your hand. Wash hands after handling chilies and before touching your face. No privacy exposure.
