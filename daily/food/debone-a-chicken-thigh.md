## Goal

A chicken thigh deboned cleanly, kept flat for even cooking, with skin left on or removed as needed.

## Preconditions

- Bone-in chicken thighs, sharp boning or paring knife, stable board, and bowl for bones.
- Board secured with a damp towel and raw poultry cleanup supplies ready.
- Knife is sharp; forcing a dull knife near bone increases slip risk.

## Steps

1. **Set skin side down.** Place the thigh on the board with the bone side facing up. → *Expect:* the bone is visible or easy to feel through the meat.
2. **Locate the bone.** Run fingers along the center ridge and identify both ends. → *Expect:* you know the exact line to cut.
3. **Cut along one side.** Use short strokes against the bone, keeping fingertips curled back. → *Expect:* one side of the bone is exposed.
4. **Cut along the other side.** Repeat tight to the bone without cutting through the skin. → *Expect:* the bone stands free along both edges.
5. **Scrape under the bone.** Angle the blade against bone and scrape meat away in small strokes. → *Expect:* meat releases while staying in one flat piece.
6. **Free the ends.** Cut around cartilage at each end and lift the bone out. → *Expect:* the thigh opens boneless with minimal meat on the bone.
7. **Trim and flatten.** [BRANCH: skin-on | skin-off] Leave skin for crisping, or peel it off; trim knobs, then open the thigh flat. → *Expect:* thickness is even for cooking.
8. **Save or discard bones.** Move bones to stock or trash and sanitize the board. → *Expect:* raw poultry scraps are contained.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* removing skin changes browning and moisture; confirm skin-on or skin-off before trimming.

## Decision points

- Crispy pan-sear → keep skin on and dry it well.
- Skewers or stir-fry → skin off and cut the flat thigh into even pieces.
- Stock planned → save bones in a freezer bag.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Meat hacked up:** strokes are too long or deep → switch to short scraping cuts against bone.
- **F2 Skin cut through:** blade angle dropped too low → continue, then cook skin-side down carefully.
- **F3 Cartilage remains:** hard white cap is still attached → trim it out before cooking.
- **F4 Raw juices spread:** cleanup lagged → wash and sanitize before handling other ingredients.

## Verification

The thigh is boneless, mostly even in thickness, and the chosen skin status matches the cooking plan.

## Variations

- Bone-out by hand: after side cuts, use fingers to pull meat from bone and knife only for the ends.
- Stuffed thigh: keep the meat as one wide sheet and avoid trimming too aggressively.
- Batch prep: debone all thighs first, then trim all at once for speed.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from raw poultry and close knife work. Use a stable board, sharp knife, claw grip, and full sanitation after handling chicken.
