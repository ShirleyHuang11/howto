## Goal

A bowl of oatmeal at the consistency you actually like, cooked without a boiled-over pot or a cement-lined microwave, topped and on the table in minutes.

## Preconditions

- Rolled oats (old-fashioned) as the default; quick oats cook faster and softer, steel-cut cook much longer and chewier. Know which bag you hold, because the liquid and time change with it.
- Liquid at roughly 2:1 by volume (liquid to oats): water for clean oat flavor, milk or a mix for creamier results.
- A pinch of salt, which is the difference between oatmeal and wallpaper paste, and toppings within reach.

## Steps

1. **Measure oats, liquid, and the salt pinch into the pot.** Half a cup of rolled oats to one cup of liquid feeds one person. Starting everything cold together (rather than adding oats to boiling liquid) gives a creamier result. → *Expect:* pot loaded, ratio known, salt in.
2. **Bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring now and then.** Milk-based pots scorch and boil over more readily than water; medium heat and presence are the deal. → *Expect:* small bubbles at the edges rising to a gentle simmer, not a rolling boil.
3. **Simmer 5 to 7 minutes for rolled oats, stirring occasionally.** More stirring means creamier (the starch releases); less stirring keeps flakes more distinct. Steer by preference. → *Expect:* visible thickening; flakes softening; the spoon starting to leave a brief trail.
4. **Judge doneness by texture, slightly early.** Oatmeal keeps thickening off the heat, so pull it a shade looser than target. → *Expect:* the consistency you like, minus a little.
5. **Rest one minute off the heat, then bowl it.** → *Expect:* final texture landing on target as it sits.
6. **Top with intent.** A fat or protein (nut butter, yogurt, nuts, seeds), a fruit (banana, berries, apple), and a flavor accent (cinnamon, honey, maple, jam). Sweeteners measured after tasting, not before. → *Expect:* a bowl that holds you until lunch, not a sugar delivery system.
7. **Fill the pot with water immediately.** Dried oat residue sets like plaster; a soaking pot cleans itself (`daily/home/wash-dishes-by-hand` will thank you). → *Expect:* pot soaking before the first spoonful.

## Decision points

- Microwave version: large bowl (double the volume you think, for the surge), oats, liquid, salt, 90 seconds, stir, then 30-second bursts to target. The oversized bowl is the entire anti-eruption technology.
- Steel-cut oats: 3:1 liquid, 20 to 30 minutes of gentle simmering, or the overnight-soak shortcut that halves it. A different breakfast timeline, same principles.
- Overnight oats (no cooking): equal oats and milk or yogurt in a jar, fridge overnight, toppings in the morning. The summer branch of this recipe.
- Savory branch: skip sweet toppings; a fried egg (`daily/food/fry-an-egg`), scallions, soy sauce, and chili turn the same pot into congee's cousin.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Boil-over or scorched bottom:** heat was high and attention elsewhere; kill the heat, decant without scraping the burnt layer (the `daily/food/cook-rice` F2 move), and soak the pot long.
- **F2 Gluey wallpaper paste:** overcooked, overstirred, or quick oats treated like rolled. Loosen with a splash of hot milk and lean on toppings; adjust the oat type or timing next round.
- **F3 Watery soup:** undercooked or ratio drift; two more simmering minutes fixes it, and the rest step (5) absorbs the remainder.
- **F4 Bland despite toppings:** the salt pinch was skipped. It is load-bearing; there is no topping-side rescue that fully replaces it.

## Verification

The bowl hits your chosen consistency after the one-minute rest, tastes seasoned before sweeteners, carries a fat or protein alongside the fruit, and the pot is already soaking rather than fossilizing.

## Variations

- `uk` porridge tradition runs saltier and water-based; `us` leans milk and sweet; both fit the same ratios.
- Batch cooking: a large pot portioned into containers reheats all week with a splash of milk (`daily/food/portion-and-freeze-meals` logic at fridge scale).

## Safety & privacy

Low risk beyond a hot pot and the microwave surge, both handled by medium heat, presence, and the oversized bowl. Breakfast should be the least dangerous thing you do all day.
