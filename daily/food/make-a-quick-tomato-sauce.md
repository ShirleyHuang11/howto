## Goal

A bright weeknight tomato sauce is made in about 20 minutes from garlic, olive oil, canned tomatoes, salt, and basil.

## Preconditions

- 1 can whole, crushed, or diced tomatoes.
- Olive oil, garlic, salt, and basil, fresh or dried.
- Saucepan, spoon, and a way to crush tomatoes if using whole tomatoes.

## Steps

1. **Open and check the tomatoes.** Pour tomatoes into a bowl if you need to crush them by hand. → *Expect:* tomatoes smell sweet and acidic, not metallic or fermented.
2. **Warm the oil.** Add 2 tablespoons olive oil to a saucepan over medium-low heat. → *Expect:* oil shimmers lightly but does not smoke.
3. **Soften the garlic.** Add 2 thin-sliced or minced garlic cloves and cook 30 to 60 seconds. → *Expect:* garlic smells fragrant and stays pale gold.
4. **Add tomatoes carefully.** Pour in the canned tomatoes and their juice, then crush large pieces with a spoon. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* garlic turns bitter once burned, so if it browns hard before the tomatoes go in, discard and restart the oil and garlic. → *Expect:* sauce bubbles gently after the first minute.
5. **Season and simmer.** Add 1/2 teaspoon salt, lower heat, and simmer uncovered 12 to 15 minutes, stirring often. → *Expect:* sauce thickens enough to coat the spoon.
6. **Finish with basil.** Tear in fresh basil at the end, or rub in a pinch of dried basil during the last 5 minutes. → *Expect:* sauce smells fresh and no longer tastes flat.
7. **Adjust for pasta.** Taste, then add salt, olive oil, or a splash of pasta water if using with noodles. → *Expect:* sauce clings to pasta instead of pooling as watery tomato juice.

## Decision points

- Tomatoes taste very acidic → simmer 5 minutes longer or add a small pinch of sugar.
- Sauce is too thick → loosen with water or pasta water 1 tablespoon at a time.
- Sauce is too thin → simmer uncovered and stir more often until reduced.
- Want smoother sauce → blend after simmering, away from heat.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt garlic:** detect dark brown garlic or bitter smell; recover by restarting before adding tomatoes.
- **F2 Bland sauce:** detect raw canned flavor; recover with more salt, 1 tablespoon olive oil, and 5 more minutes simmering.
- **F3 Splattering sauce:** detect red spots around the pan; recover by lowering heat and using a splatter screen or partial lid.
- **F4 Watery pasta plate:** detect sauce sliding off noodles; recover by finishing pasta in the pan with sauce and pasta water.

## Verification

The sauce tastes seasoned, coats a spoon, and has simmered long enough that garlic, tomato, oil, salt, and basil taste integrated.

## Variations

- Spicy: add red pepper flakes with the garlic.
- Butter finish: stir in 1 tablespoon butter off heat for a rounder sauce.
- No basil: use a pinch of oregano, but keep it light so it does not dominate.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot splattering sauce and sharp can lids. Keep the simmer gentle and turn pot handles away from the counter edge.
