## Goal

A balanced Old Fashioned with whiskey, sugar, bitters, cold dilution, and citrus oil.

## Preconditions

- Whiskey, aromatic bitters, sugar or simple syrup, ice, and an orange peel.
- A mixing glass or sturdy rocks glass, bar spoon, jigger, and strainer if mixing separately.
- Adults only where alcohol use is legal.

## Steps

1. **Chill the serving glass.** Put the rocks glass in the freezer or fill it with ice while you build the drink. → *Expect:* the glass feels cold before the cocktail goes in.
2. **Dissolve the sweetener.** Add 1 tsp simple syrup, or 1 sugar cube with a few drops of water, to the mixing glass. → *Expect:* syrup is fluid, or the cube is crushed into a wet paste.
3. **Add bitters.** Dash in 2 to 3 dashes aromatic bitters and stir into the sugar. → *Expect:* the sugar smells spiced and no dry sugar sits in the glass.
4. **Measure whiskey.** Add 2 oz bourbon or rye. → *Expect:* the drink base is strong and slightly sweet before dilution.
5. **Add ice and stir.** Fill the mixing glass with solid ice and stir 20 to 30 seconds; do not shake. → *Expect:* the outside chills and the drink loosens without becoming cloudy or foamy.
6. **Serve over one large cube.** Empty any chilling ice from the rocks glass, add a large cube, and strain or pour the cocktail over it. → *Expect:* clear amber drink around cold fresh ice.
7. **Express the peel.** Hold orange peel skin-side down over the glass, squeeze to spray oils, rub the rim, then drop it in or discard. → *Expect:* orange aroma rises before the first sip.
8. **Taste once.** If too hot, stir 5 more seconds with ice; if too sweet, add 1 more dash bitters. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once extra sugar or bitters are added, balance can only be diluted, not removed. → *Expect:* whiskey leads, with sugar and bitters supporting it.

## Decision points

- Bourbon versus rye → bourbon gives round sweetness; rye gives spice and a drier finish.
- Sugar cube versus syrup → syrup integrates faster; a cube gives a slightly textured old-school style.
- No large cube → use several solid cubes and drink sooner because dilution accelerates.
- Serving a guest → ask preference on sweetness before increasing syrup.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Grainy sugar:** cube did not dissolve; stir longer before adding ice next time, and use syrup for consistency.
- **F2 Watery drink:** stirred too long or used wet, melting ice; rebuild with colder ice and shorter stirring.
- **F3 Harsh alcohol burn:** under-diluted; stir with ice 5 to 10 more seconds and retaste.
- **F4 No citrus aroma:** peel was dry or pith-only; cut a fresh strip with visible orange skin.

## Verification

The drink is cold, clear, lightly sweetened, aromatic from orange oil, and not foamy or watered down.

## Variations

- `rye`: use rye whiskey and a lean 1/2 tsp syrup for a drier drink.
- `maple`: replace simple syrup with 1 tsp maple syrup.
- `brandy`: common Wisconsin-style variation, often sweeter and fruit-garnished.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because this contains alcohol. Adults only, drink responsibly, measure pours, and do not drive or operate equipment after drinking.
