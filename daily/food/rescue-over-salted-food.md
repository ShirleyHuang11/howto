## Goal

Reduce the perceived saltiness of a dish enough that it tastes balanced and can be served.

## Preconditions

- The dish is still editable: soup, sauce, stew, grains, vegetables, meat, or a composed mixture.
- Unsalted liquid, plain bulk ingredients, acid, fat, or a mild dairy ingredient is available.
- A clean spoon is available for tasting between fixes.

## Steps

1. **Stop adding salt.** Move salty seasonings, stock cubes, soy sauce, cheese, and cured ingredients away from the work area. → *Expect:* no more salt enters the dish by accident.
2. **Taste a small sample.** Let it cool briefly, then taste from a spoon instead of the pot. → *Expect:* you know whether the dish is slightly salty or harshly salty.
3. **Dilute with unsalted liquid.** [BRANCH: soup or sauce | dry dish] Add water, unsalted stock, tomato puree, milk, or coconut milk to wet dishes; use a small splash at a time. → *Expect:* the liquid level rises and saltiness drops slightly.
4. **Add unsalted bulk.** Stir in plain cooked rice, pasta, potatoes, beans, vegetables, bread crumbs, or extra unsalted main ingredient. → *Expect:* the salty flavor spreads across more food.
5. **Balance with acid.** Add lemon juice, vinegar, wine, or tomato in small amounts when the dish can handle brightness. → *Expect:* the flavor tastes less flat without turning sour.
6. **Round with fat or dairy.** Add butter, olive oil, cream, yogurt, coconut milk, or unsalted cheese when it fits the dish. → *Expect:* sharp saltiness feels softer on the tongue.
7. **Ignore the potato myth.** A raw potato absorbs liquid and some salt with it, but it does not selectively remove enough salt to rescue most dishes. → *Expect:* you choose real dilution or bulk instead of waiting on a potato.
8. **Re-season carefully.** Add herbs, spices, sweetness, heat, or acid before adding any more salt. → *Expect:* the dish tastes balanced without a salty finish.
9. **Record the cause.** Note whether the salt came from stock, brined ingredients, reduction, or double seasoning. → *Expect:* the next batch has a clear prevention rule.

## Decision points

- Dish is only slightly salty → use acid, fat, or a bland side rather than doubling the recipe.
- Dish is aggressively salty → dilute or bulk up first, then rebalance.
- Sauce reduced too far → restore volume with unsalted liquid before tasting again.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Watery result:** detect thin texture after dilution → simmer briefly or add unsalted thickener such as mashed beans, potato flakes, or a slurry.
- **F2 Sour result:** detect sharp acid bite → add more unsalted bulk or a small amount of fat.
- **F3 Still too salty:** detect saltiness after two correction rounds → serve smaller portions over plain grains or repurpose as a seasoning base.
- **F4 Hidden salt source:** detect saltiness rising after cheese, olives, bacon, or soy sauce → stop additions and switch to unsalted ingredients.

## Verification

A cooled spoonful tastes balanced enough to eat without immediate thirst or a harsh salty aftertaste.

## Variations

- Soup: add unsalted stock and more vegetables, noodles, or beans.
- Sauce: add cream, tomato, pasta water without salt, or the unsalted base ingredient.
- Meat or vegetables: chop and fold into unsalted grains, salad, eggs, or soup.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Food safety still applies: keep hot food hot, cold food cold, and discard food left in the danger zone too long.
