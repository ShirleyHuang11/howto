## Goal

Separate yolks from whites cleanly, especially when the whites need to whip into a stable foam.

## Preconditions

- Fresh cold eggs are available.
- Three bowls are ready: catch bowl, whites bowl, and yolks bowl.
- Hands are clean if using the hand method.
- The whites bowl is clean and grease-free.

## Steps

1. **Set bowls.** Put the catch bowl in front and the final bowls to each side → *Expect:* one egg can be inspected before joining the batch
2. **Crack flat.** Tap the egg on a flat surface and open over the catch bowl → *Expect:* shell opens with fewer shards
3. **Pick method.** [BRANCH: shell method | hand method] pass yolk between shell halves or let white drain through fingers → *Expect:* white separates while yolk stays intact
4. **Check white.** Look for any yellow streak before adding to the main whites → *Expect:* catch bowl white is clear
5. **Transfer white.** Pour clean white into the whites bowl → *Expect:* batch whites stay yolk-free
6. **Transfer yolk.** Slide the yolk into the yolks bowl → *Expect:* yolk is whole or only slightly flattened
7. **Repeat singly.** Separate each egg over the catch bowl. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* one broken yolk can ruin a whole whipping batch → *Expect:* main whites remain uncontaminated

## Decision points

- Whites are for meringue -> always use the three-bowl catch system.
- Yolk breaks in catch bowl -> save that egg for scrambling and wash the bowl.
- Hand method -> softer on fragile yolks than sharp shell edges.
- Older eggs -> whites drain fast but yolks break more easily.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Yolk in whites:** detect yellow droplets -> remove tiny spots with shell; if any remains, do not use for whipping.
- **F2 Shell shard:** detect hard fragment -> scoop with a shell half or wet finger.
- **F3 Broken yolk:** detect leaking yolk -> reserve that egg for another use and clean the catch bowl.
- **F4 Greasy bowl:** detect oil film -> wash and dry before adding whipping whites.

## Verification

The whites are free of yolk and shell, and no contaminated egg has been added to the main whites bowl.

## Variations

- Bottle method: suction yolks with a clean plastic bottle.
- Room temperature baking: separate cold first, then let parts warm.
- Carton whites: useful when absolutely yolk-free whites matter.
- Shell method: fastest, but shell edges can pierce weak yolks.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from raw egg handling.
Wash hands and surfaces, and use pasteurized eggs for uncooked dishes served to vulnerable eaters.
