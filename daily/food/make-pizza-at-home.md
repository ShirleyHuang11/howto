## Goal

A pizza with a browned, crisp-bottomed crust and just-melted toppings comes out of a home oven, sliced and ready to eat.

## Preconditions

- Dough: either homemade (flour, water, yeast, salt, olive oil; started 2+ hours ahead) or a store-bought dough ball brought to room temperature for 1 hour.
- An oven that reaches at least 250 °C (as hot as it goes), plus a pizza stone, steel, or an inverted heavy baking tray.
- Toppings prepped: sauce, cheese, and at most 2-3 others. Restraint is the home-oven trick; a loaded pizza steams instead of crisping.
- Parchment paper or a well-floured peel/board to launch from.

## Steps

1. **Make or temper the dough.** [BRANCH: homemade: mix 500 g flour, 325 g water, 7 g yeast, 10 g salt; knead 8 min; rise covered 1.5-2 h | store-bought: rest the ball on the counter 1 h] → *Expect:* dough roughly doubled (homemade) or soft and stretchable without snapping back hard (either path).
2. **Preheat the oven with the stone or tray inside.** Max temperature, stone/steel/inverted tray on a middle-high rack, for a full 45-60 minutes. This is the step people cut short; a cold surface gives a pale, bendy base. → *Expect:* oven at max for the full soak; a drop of water flicked onto the surface skitters off instantly.
3. **Shape by hand, not with a rolling pin.** Press the ball flat, then stretch from the center outward on a floured surface, leaving a 2 cm rim untouched. Rolling crushes the gas bubbles that become the airy rim. → *Expect:* a 28-30 cm round, thin enough in the center to see light through when lifted, rim intact.
4. **Build on parchment or a floured peel.** Thin sauce layer (2-3 tablespoons, spread with the back of a spoon), cheese, then the few toppings, none stacked. Work fast: a wet dough glued to the peel will not launch. → *Expect:* the built pizza still slides when you shake the peel/board gently.
5. **Launch onto the hot surface.** Open the oven, slide the pizza (with its parchment, or with a confident jerk off the peel) onto the stone/tray. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* the stone and rack are at max heat; a hesitant launch folds the pizza onto itself. Commit to the motion and keep forearms clear. → *Expect:* pizza flat on the hot surface, door closed within seconds.
6. **Bake 6-10 minutes, watching from ~5 minutes.** If the oven has a broiler/grill, switching it on for the last 2 minutes browns the top like a real pizza oven. → *Expect:* rim puffed and spotted brown, cheese bubbling, edges of the base lifting slightly.
7. **Retrieve, rest, slice.** Pull it out with a peel or tongs onto a rack or board, wait 2 minutes, then slice. Cutting immediately drags molten cheese off. → *Expect:* bottom shows browning when lifted; slices hold their cheese.

## Decision points

- No stone or steel → preheat a heavy baking tray upside down; the flat back launches easier than the rimmed face.
- Dough snaps back while stretching → cover it and wait 10 minutes; gluten relaxes on its own, forcing it tears holes.
- Wet toppings (fresh mozzarella, mushrooms) → tear mozzarella and drain on paper towel first; add delicate items (basil, arugula, prosciutto) after the bake.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pale, floppy base:** stone not preheated long enough → next round, give the full 45-60 minutes; for this pizza, finish it 2 minutes in a dry hot skillet on the stovetop.
- **F2 Pizza stuck to the peel at launch:** shake test failed → lift the edge, throw flour underneath, or slide parchment under it and launch paper and all.
- **F3 Soggy center under the toppings:** overloaded or over-sauced → blot standing liquid, return to the stone 2-3 more minutes; use less next time.
- **F4 Top burnt, bottom pale:** rack too high or broiler too early → move the stone one rack lower and only broil at the end.

## Verification

The underside shows brown spots (lift a slice to check), the rim is puffed and holds its shape when a slice is picked up, cheese is melted without a pool of liquid in the center, and the oven is off.

## Variations

- `thin-crackery`: roll instead of hand-stretch, dock with a fork, skip the rim; bake the same way.
- Pan pizza: no stone needed; press dough into an oiled cast-iron or heavy pan, top, bake at max heat; forgiving for beginners.
- Grill method: stretch smaller rounds, grill one side bare, flip, top the cooked side, lid down until melted.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: the oven, stone, and rack sit at maximum temperature for over an hour, and the launch step puts hands near them. Use dry thick mitts (wet cloth conducts heat instantly) and keep children away during launch and retrieval. Flour dust near an open flame is best avoided; shape away from the stovetop.
