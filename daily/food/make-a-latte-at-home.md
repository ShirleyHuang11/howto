## Goal

A hot latte is made with espresso or strong coffee, warmed milk, and a foam layer that tastes balanced instead of watery.

## Preconditions

- Espresso machine, moka pot, Aeropress, pod machine, or strong brewed coffee.
- Milk or unsweetened alternative milk that can foam.
- Mug, spoon, and either a steam wand, handheld frother, French press, or lidded microwave-safe jar.

## Steps

1. **Warm the mug.** Fill the mug with hot tap water while you prepare the coffee, then dump it out. → *Expect:* the mug feels warm and will not cool the drink immediately.
2. **Brew the coffee base.** Use 1 double espresso shot, about 2 oz, or 3 to 4 oz of very strong coffee. → *Expect:* the coffee smells concentrated and has no grounds floating in it.
3. **Measure the milk.** Use about 6 oz milk for a classic 1:3 espresso-to-milk latte, or 4 oz for a stronger drink. → *Expect:* the milk amount fits the mug with at least 1 inch of headroom.
4. **Heat and texture the milk.** [BRANCH: steam wand | handheld frother | jar-and-microwave] steam until hot but not boiling, froth after warming with a handheld frother, or shake cold milk in a half-full jar, remove the lid, then microwave 30 to 45 seconds. → *Expect:* milk is hot, lightly foamy, and still smells sweet, not cooked.
5. **Tap and swirl the milk.** Tap the pitcher or jar once, then swirl until the foam and milk look glossy. → *Expect:* large bubbles shrink and the surface looks smooth.
6. **Pour milk into coffee.** Hold back the foam with a spoon at first, pour the hot milk into the coffee, then spoon foam over the top. → *Expect:* the drink is pale brown with a thin cap of foam.
7. **Taste and adjust.** Add sweetener or a pinch of cocoa only after tasting the plain latte. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once syrup or sugar is stirred in, the sweetness cannot be removed, so add half the amount first. → *Expect:* the latte tastes balanced, warm, and not scorched.

## Decision points

- Coffee tastes weak → use less milk next time or brew a smaller, stronger coffee base.
- Milk smells eggy, burnt, or skin forms on top → it overheated; start the milk again at lower heat.
- Foam collapses instantly → use fresher milk, a narrower vessel, or a barista-style oat milk.
- Drink is too hot to sip → wait 2 minutes rather than adding cold milk that thins the texture.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Watery latte:** detect a pale drink with little coffee aroma; recover by adding another espresso shot or reducing milk next time.
- **F2 Big soap bubbles:** detect dry foam with large bubbles; recover by tapping, swirling, and spooning off the driest top foam.
- **F3 Jar overflow:** detect milk climbing in the microwave; recover by stopping, using a larger jar, and reheating in shorter bursts.
- **F4 Scalded milk:** detect a cooked smell or film; recover by discarding the milk and reheating a fresh portion gently.

## Verification

The mug contains hot coffee mixed with roughly 2 to 3 parts textured milk to 1 part espresso or strong coffee, with a smooth foam layer and no scorched flavor.

## Variations

- Iced latte: skip frothing, pour espresso over ice, then add cold milk at the same ratio.
- No frother: warm milk in a small pot and whisk hard for 30 seconds before pouring.
- Stronger cafe-style cup: use a double shot with only 4 to 5 oz milk.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot liquid, steam, and glass jars. Never microwave a sealed jar or a metal lid, and open hot containers away from your face.
