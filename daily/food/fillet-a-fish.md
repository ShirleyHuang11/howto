## Goal

A whole fish broken into clean fillets, with pin bones removed and skin kept on or taken off as needed.

## Preconditions

- A scaled, gutted fish; a sharp flexible fillet knife; tweezers or pliers; and a stable board.
- Board secured with a damp towel; wet fish slides, and a moving board makes knife work unsafe.
- Knife is sharp enough to glide along bone; sharp-is-safe because force causes slips.

## Steps

1. **Dry and position the fish.** Pat the fish dry and lay it belly facing you, head to your non-knife side. → *Expect:* the fish grips the board instead of skating.
2. **Cut behind the gill plate.** Angle the knife behind the gill and pectoral fin until you touch the backbone. → *Expect:* a clean diagonal cut reaches bone without sawing through it.
3. **Turn onto the spine.** Rotate the blade flat toward the tail and make short strokes along the backbone. → *Expect:* the top edge of the fillet starts releasing in one sheet.
4. **Ride the rib cage.** Keep the blade flexible and close to bone, lifting the fillet with your claw hand as you cut. → *Expect:* ribs show cleanly with little flesh left on them.
5. **Finish through the tail.** Keep the blade flat and slice out through the tail end. → *Expect:* one attached fillet comes free with even thickness.
6. **Repeat the second side.** Flip the fish and repeat behind the gill, along the spine, over the ribs, and out the tail. → *Expect:* two fillets and one mostly clean frame.
7. **Remove pin bones.** Run fingertips from head end to tail, then pull bones in their natural angle with tweezers. → *Expect:* no sharp pin bones catch your fingertip.
8. **Skin off if desired.** [BRANCH: skin-on | skin-off] For skin-off, start at the tail, grip skin with a towel, and slide the knife flat between skin and flesh. → *Expect:* skin separates in one strip and flesh remains smooth.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once skinned, the crisp-skin option is gone; confirm the cooking method first.

## Decision points

- Round fish versus flat fish → round fish use the spine-and-rib path above; flat fish need four fillets from the center line.
- Delicate fish → use shorter strokes and less lift so the fillet does not tear.
- Skin for cooking → keep skin on for pan-searing, grilling, or holding fragile flesh together.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Knife catches in bones:** blade angle is too steep → flatten the blade and restart with shorter strokes.
- **F2 Ragged fillet:** knife is dull or sawing too hard → hone or sharpen, then let the flexible blade ride bone.
- **F3 Pin bones remain:** fingertip catches a point → pull again from head toward tail until smooth.
- **F4 Fish slips:** surface is wet → dry fish and board, then reset the damp towel under the board.

## Verification

Fillets are free of pin bones, trimmed cleanly from the frame, and either skin-on or skin-off to match the intended cooking method.

## Variations

- Small fish: kitchen shears can help open the belly and trim fins before knife work.
- Salmon side: remove pin bones first, then portion across the grain.
- Stock: save clean frames, heads without gills, and fins for fish stock.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from sharp blades, slippery fish, and bones. Use a stable board, claw grip, sharp knife, and cut away from the holding hand.
