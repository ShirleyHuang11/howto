## Goal

A watermelon cut safely into wedges, batons, or rindless cubes with stable flat surfaces throughout.

## Preconditions

- Washed watermelon, long sharp knife, stable board, damp towel, and large tray or bowl.
- Counter has enough room for the melon and knife stroke.
- Board is secured; heavy melons become dangerous when they roll.

## Steps

1. **Wash the rind.** Rinse and dry the outside before cutting through it. → *Expect:* dirt from the rind will not drag into the flesh.
2. **Trim a small base.** Slice a thin piece from one side if the melon rolls badly. → *Expect:* melon sits more steadily.
3. **Halve the melon.** Cut through the center with long steady strokes, keeping the other hand clear. → *Expect:* two halves expose the red flesh.
4. **Place flat side down.** Put one half cut-side down before any smaller cuts. → *Expect:* the half is stable and safe to cut.
5. **Choose the cut.** [BRANCH: wedges | batons | cubes] Pick wedges for hand eating, batons for parties, or rindless cubes for bowls. → *Expect:* the cut plan is set before slicing.
6. **Make wedges or batons.** Slice the half into wide slabs, then cut slabs into wedges or stick-like batons. → *Expect:* pieces have rind handles if desired.
7. **Make rindless cubes.** For cubes, cut the half into slabs, remove rind from each slab, then crosscut into cubes. → *Expect:* cubes are bite-size and rind-free.
8. **Chill or serve.** Move pieces to a tray and wipe juice from the board. → *Expect:* melon is portioned and the board is no longer slick.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once rind is removed, pieces lose their handle; decide serving style first.

## Decision points

- Picnic or kids → wedges with rind are easiest to hold.
- Buffet bowl → rindless cubes are cleaner to serve.
- Very large melon → cut quarters first so every piece has a flat side.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Melon rolls:** no flat surface exists → trim a shallow base before continuing.
- **F2 Knife binds:** blade is too short or dull → use a longer sharp knife and cut in sections.
- **F3 Pieces are uneven:** slabs varied in width → square the slab edges before cubing.
- **F4 Board floods with juice:** tray is missing → pause, drain juice, and dry the board.

## Verification

Watermelon is cut into the chosen shape, pieces are stable and even, and no cutting was done on a rolling surface.

## Variations

- Mini watermelon: halve, then grid-cut each half for quick sticks.
- Seeded watermelon: remove visible seeds after cutting slabs.
- Salad cubes: cut smaller and drain before mixing with salt or cheese.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from heavy fruit, slick juice, and a long knife. Use flat-side-down cutting, a sharp blade, stable board, and claw grip whenever holding pieces.
