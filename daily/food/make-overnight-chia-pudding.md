## Goal

Chia pudding thickened overnight with an even gel texture and toppings added at serving.

## Preconditions

- Chia seeds, milk or plant milk, sweetener, salt, and a jar or bowl.
- Spoon or small whisk.
- Refrigerator space.

## Steps

1. **Measure the ratio.** Use 3 tbsp chia seeds to 1 cup milk for spoonable pudding. → *Expect:* seeds are measured before liquid hydrates them.
2. **Mix liquid first.** Stir milk with 1 to 2 tsp sweetener, a pinch of salt, and vanilla if using. → *Expect:* sweetener dissolves evenly.
3. **Add chia while stirring.** Sprinkle seeds into the liquid rather than dumping them in one clump. → *Expect:* seeds disperse throughout the jar.
4. **Stir again after 10 minutes.** Break up any seed clusters along the sides and bottom. → *Expect:* mixture looks evenly speckled, not clumped.
5. **Chill overnight.** Cover and refrigerate at least 6 hours, preferably overnight. → *Expect:* seeds swell and suspend in a thick gel.
6. **Check texture.** Stir before serving. → *Expect:* pudding mounds softly on the spoon.
7. **Fix if needed.** [BRANCH: too thick | too thin] loosen with milk, or stir in 1 tsp chia and wait 30 minutes. → *Expect:* texture returns to spoonable.
8. **Top and serve.** Add fruit, nuts, granola, jam, or yogurt just before eating. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* soggy crunchy toppings cannot be made crisp again, so add them last. → *Expect:* pudding is cold with fresh toppings.

## Decision points

- Using canned coconut milk → thin with water or milk so the pudding is not waxy.
- Want smoother texture → blend the mixture before chilling.
- Meal prep → portion into jars but leave crunchy toppings separate.
- Unsweetened plant milk → add a little extra sweetener or fruit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Seed clumps:** chia was dumped or not restirred; whisk hard, wait 15 minutes, and stir again.
- **F2 Runny pudding:** ratio had too much liquid; add 1 tsp chia per cup and chill longer.
- **F3 Too stiff:** too many seeds or thick milk; loosen with milk 1 tbsp at a time.
- **F4 Bitter or stale taste:** old chia seeds; discard if rancid and use fresher seeds.

## Verification

The pudding is cold, evenly gelled, spoonable, and has no dry seed clumps at the bottom or edges.

## Variations

- `coconut-mango`: use light coconut milk and top with mango.
- `cocoa`: whisk 1 tbsp cocoa into the milk before adding chia.
- `yogurt`: replace half the milk with yogurt for tang and thickness.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep refrigerated, use clean jars, and eat within 4 days for best texture and freshness.
