## Goal

A smooth bechamel-style white sauce is made from butter, flour, and milk, ready for pasta, vegetables, casseroles, or cheese sauce.

## Preconditions

- Butter, flour, milk, salt, pepper, saucepan, whisk, and measuring spoons are available.
- Milk is warm or at least not refrigerator-cold if quick thickening is desired.
- The cook can stay at the stove and whisk continuously for several minutes.

## Steps

1. **Measure the base ratio.** Use 2 tablespoons butter, 2 tablespoons flour, and 1 cup milk for medium sauce. → *Expect:* ingredients are measured before heat is on.
2. **Melt the butter.** Heat butter in a saucepan over medium-low until foamy, not browned. → *Expect:* butter is liquid with small bubbles.
3. **Cook the roux.** Whisk in flour and cook 1 to 2 minutes. → *Expect:* paste smells lightly nutty and no raw flour dust remains.
4. **Add milk gradually.** Pour in a small splash of warm milk while whisking hard, then add the rest in several additions. → *Expect:* each addition smooths out before the next one goes in.
5. **Simmer to thicken.** Keep whisking over medium heat until bubbles break slowly at the surface. → *Expect:* sauce coats the back of a spoon.
6. **Choose thickness.** [BRANCH: thin sauce, add 2 to 4 tablespoons milk | thick sauce, simmer 1 to 3 minutes longer] → *Expect:* sauce runs, ribbons, or mounds according to the target use.
7. **Season.** Add salt, pepper, and a small pinch of nutmeg if wanted. → *Expect:* sauce tastes savory rather than flat.
8. **Add cheese off heat.** Remove from heat and stir in grated cheese a handful at a time. → *Expect:* cheese melts smoothly without oily separation.

## Decision points

- Sauce is for lasagna → keep it loose enough to spread.
- Sauce is for croquettes or filling → cook thicker until it mounds.
- Milk is cold → add it in smaller splashes to prevent lumps.
- Cheese sauce is needed → use low heat and avoid boiling after cheese is added.
- Gluten-free sauce is needed → use a tested gluten-free flour blend or cornstarch slurry variation.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lumpy sauce:** detect flour balls or grainy patches; recover by whisking hard, then straining if needed.
- **F2 Scorched bottom:** detect brown flecks and bitter smell; recover by pouring unburned sauce into a clean pan without scraping.
- **F3 Too thick:** detect paste-like sauce; recover by whisking in warm milk one spoonful at a time.
- **F4 Too thin:** detect sauce running like milk after simmering; recover by simmering longer or adding a small roux.
- **F5 Cheese splits:** detect oil beads; recover by removing from heat and whisking in a splash of milk.

## Verification

The sauce is smooth, hot, and thick enough to coat a spoon while leaving no floury taste.

## Variations

- Light sauce: use 1 tablespoon each butter and flour per cup of milk.
- Thick sauce: use 3 tablespoons each butter and flour per cup of milk.
- Mustard cheese sauce: whisk in a small spoon of Dijon before adding cheddar.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, with hot dairy as the main hazard. Stir from the bottom so sauce does not scorch and spatter.
