## Goal

Onions are cooked low and slow into a deeply browned, sweet, jammy pile without scorching or pretending it takes 10 minutes.

## Preconditions

- Yellow, white, or red onions, a sharp knife, cutting board, wide heavy pan, fat, salt, and water or stock for deglazing.
- At least 45 minutes of stove attention. Recipe claims of 10 to 15 minutes usually mean browned, not caramelized.
- Ventilation if onion fumes bother your eyes or throat.

## Steps

1. **Slice evenly.** Cut onions pole to pole into 3 to 5 mm slices so pieces cook at the same pace. → *Expect:* a loose pile of similar-width onion strips.
2. **Use a wide pan.** Set a heavy skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat and add enough oil or butter to coat the bottom. → *Expect:* fat shimmers or butter foams without browning hard.
3. **Load and salt.** Add onions with a pinch of salt and stir to coat. → *Expect:* the pan looks crowded at first, then onions begin to soften and shed water.
4. **Cook off moisture.** Stir every few minutes while steam rises and the pile collapses. → *Expect:* onions turn translucent and reduce to less than half their starting volume.
5. **Lower the heat.** When browning begins, reduce to medium-low or low. → *Expect:* sizzling becomes quiet and steady rather than sharp.
6. **Let color build slowly.** Stir every 3 to 5 minutes, scraping the bottom each time. → *Expect:* pale gold becomes tan, then brown, with no black flakes.
7. **Deglaze when fond sticks.** Add 1 to 2 tablespoons water, stock, wine, or vinegar and scrape until the browned film dissolves. → *Expect:* dark sticky spots lift into the onions and the pan bottom looks clean.
8. **Repeat patiently.** Continue cooking and deglazing as needed until 45 to 60 minutes have passed for a full pan. → *Expect:* onions are soft, glossy, and deep brown throughout.
9. **Finish seasoning.** Taste and adjust salt; add a small splash of vinegar only if the onions taste flat. → *Expect:* sweetness is balanced and not greasy.
10. **Use or store.** Move onions to a dish or jar. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once onions scorch, bitterness cannot be removed, so reduce heat at the first black specks. → *Expect:* pan is off heat and onions are ready for burgers, soup, toast, or storage.

## Decision points

- Pan is dry but onions are not brown → add a spoon of water, not more oil, then continue.
- Onions brown before softening → heat is too high or pan is too small. Lower heat and deglaze.
- Need French onion soup depth → cook until very dark brown, but stop before black.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt bottom:** detect black spots and bitter smell; transfer unburned onions without scraping black residue, then continue in a clean pan.
- **F2 Pale after 30 minutes:** detect soft but beige onions; widen the pan, raise heat slightly, and stop stirring constantly.
- **F3 Greasy result:** detect oil pooling; spoon off excess fat and finish with acid.
- **F4 Crunchy onions:** detect raw bite in thick slices; add water, cover for 5 minutes, then uncover and resume browning.

## Verification

The onions are uniformly soft, glossy, sweet, and brown, with no acrid smell or black scorched pieces.

## Variations

- Butter plus oil: butter gives flavor, oil gives a little heat tolerance.
- Baking soda shortcut: a tiny pinch speeds browning but can make onions mushy and soapy.
- Batch cooking: three large onions reduce to roughly one cup.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot fat, steam, and long unattended stove time. Stay nearby, especially during the final darker stage.
