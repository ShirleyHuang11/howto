## Goal

Cold pizza is reheated so the base crisps, the cheese melts, and the slice avoids the rubbery texture of microwave-only reheating.

## Preconditions

- Refrigerated pizza that still smells fresh.
- Skillet with lid for best results, or oven/toaster oven.
- A few drops of water for the skillet steam trick.

## Steps

1. **Check the slice.** Smell it and look for mold, slime, or dried-out toppings. → *Expect:* pizza looks and smells like normal leftovers.
2. **Use the skillet first.** Put a slice in a dry skillet over medium-low heat. → *Expect:* the crust begins to warm and faintly sizzle after 1 to 2 minutes.
3. **Crisp the base.** Let the slice sit until the bottom feels firm when nudged with a spatula. → *Expect:* crust underside is crisp, not floppy.
4. **Add the water trick.** Put 1 teaspoon water into the empty side of the pan, not on the pizza, and cover for 1 to 2 minutes. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* water hitting hot oil can splatter, so add only a small amount to a clear part of the pan. → *Expect:* steam melts the cheese while the base stays crisp.
5. **Uncover and dry.** Remove the lid and heat 30 seconds more if the crust softened. → *Expect:* steam escapes and the bottom crisps again.
6. **Oven option.** [BRANCH: skillet | oven] for several slices, bake on a preheated tray at 375 F for 7 to 10 minutes. → *Expect:* cheese bubbles and crust edges revive.
7. **Avoid microwave alone.** Use the microwave only for 15 to 20 seconds before skillet finishing if the slice is very thick. → *Expect:* toppings warm faster without turning the crust leathery.

## Decision points

- Thin slice → lower heat and shorter time to avoid scorching.
- Thick Sicilian slice → microwave briefly first, then skillet or oven.
- Lots of wet toppings → uncover longer so steam can escape.
- Reheating many slices → oven is more consistent than crowding a skillet.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt crust:** detect dark bitter bottom; recover by lowering heat and moving the next slice sooner.
- **F2 Cold cheese:** detect unmelted center; recover by adding the water trick and covering another minute.
- **F3 Soggy base:** detect limp crust after steaming; recover by uncovering and heating dry until it firms.
- **F4 Unsafe leftovers:** detect sour smell, mold, or unknown age; recover by discarding the pizza.

## Verification

The slice is hot through the center, cheese is melted, and the bottom crust is crisp enough to hold its shape when lifted.

## Variations

- Toaster oven: place on rack or hot tray at 350 to 375 F until bubbling.
- Microwave plus mug of water: helps texture slightly, but still will not crisp the crust.
- Covered grill: works like an oven if the heat is indirect and moderate.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot pans and questionable leftovers. Do not reheat pizza that has sat at room temperature for many hours.
