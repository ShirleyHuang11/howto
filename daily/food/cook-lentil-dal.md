## Goal

Lentils are rinsed, simmered with aromatics, and finished with tempered spices for a soft, flavorful dal.

## Preconditions

- Lentils such as red, yellow moong, masoor, or split pigeon peas.
- Onion, garlic, ginger, turmeric, salt, water, fat, and spices for tempering.
- Pot, spoon, and small skillet for the finish.

## Steps

1. **Pick and rinse lentils.** Check 250 g lentils for stones, then rinse until water is mostly clear. → *Expect:* no grit and less foaming in the pot.
2. **Choose water by lentil.** [BRANCH: red or yellow split: 750 ml water | whole brown or green: 1 L water] → *Expect:* enough water for a soft stew, not a dry pilaf.
3. **Start aromatics.** Simmer lentils with water, turmeric, sliced onion, garlic, and ginger. → *Expect:* foam rises during the first few minutes.
4. **Skim and simmer.** Skim foam, lower heat, and cook partly covered. → *Expect:* gentle bubbles, not violent boiling.
5. **Cook until soft.** Red lentils take 15-25 minutes; split pigeon peas and whole lentils may take 35-60 minutes. → *Expect:* lentils collapse or mash easily against the pot.
6. **Adjust body.** Mash some lentils with a spoon and add hot water if too thick. → *Expect:* dal pours slowly and thickens as it sits.
7. **Salt after softening.** Add salt and acid only after lentils are tender. → *Expect:* flavor wakes up without toughening undercooked lentils.
8. **Temper spices.** Heat ghee or oil in a small skillet, bloom cumin, mustard seed, chili, garlic, or curry leaves for 30-60 seconds. → *Expect:* spices sizzle and smell fragrant, not burnt.
9. **Pour tempering into dal.** Stir hot spiced fat through the pot or spoon it over bowls. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* wet curry leaves can spit in hot oil; dry them and keep your face back. → *Expect:* aroma intensifies immediately.

## Decision points

- Need a very smooth dal → use red lentils or blend after cooking.
- Lentils stay hard → they may be old; keep simmering with more water and delay salt or acid.
- Want richer dal → finish with butter, ghee, or coconut milk after lentils soften.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scorched bottom:** detect by sticking or burnt smell → stop stirring the bottom, pour good dal into a clean pot.
- **F2 Watery dal:** detect by lentils sinking in thin liquid → simmer uncovered and mash a portion.
- **F3 Burnt tempering:** detect by black spices or acrid smell → discard the tempering and make it again.
- **F4 Bland taste:** detect by flat flavor → add salt, lemon, and a fresh tempering rather than more dry spice.

## Verification

The lentils are fully tender, the dal has a spoonable stew texture, and the tempered spices smell fragrant without burnt bitterness.

## Variations

- `tadka-dal`: keep the simmered lentils simple and put most flavor in the final tempering.
- `coconut`: use coconut milk for part of the water and finish with lime.
- `pressure-cooker`: cook rinsed lentils with water 6-12 minutes under pressure depending on type.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from boiling liquid and spitting hot oil. Use a deep enough pot, dry washed herbs before frying, and cool leftovers quickly before refrigerating.
