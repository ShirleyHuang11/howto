## Goal

Garlic cloves peeled quickly, using the shake-in-a-jar method for batches or the smash method for a few cloves.

## Preconditions

- Garlic head, lidded jar or two metal bowls, stable board, and chef's knife.
- Cloves are dry; damp skins cling.
- Knife is sharp and board is secured if using the smash method.

## Steps

1. **Break the head.** Press the heel of your hand on the garlic head to separate cloves. → *Expect:* cloves loosen from the root cluster.
2. **Choose the method.** [BRANCH: jar batch | smash few] Use a jar for many cloves, or smash for one to five cloves. → *Expect:* the setup matches the amount.
3. **Trim only if needed.** Cut off stubborn root ends before peeling very tight cloves. → *Expect:* skins have an opening to release.
4. **Shake in a jar.** Put cloves in a hard lidded jar and shake hard for 15 to 30 seconds. → *Expect:* skins crack and many cloves come out bare.
5. **Separate skins.** Dump onto the board and pick out peeled cloves, then reshake stubborn ones. → *Expect:* most skins are loose flakes.
6. **Smash a few cloves.** Lay the knife flat over a clove and press with your palm, keeping fingers away from the edge. → *Expect:* the skin splits and slides off.
7. **Clean up.** Remove papery skins from the board before cutting garlic. → *Expect:* peeled cloves are ready without dry flakes mixed in.
8. **Use promptly.** Refrigerate peeled extra cloves in a sealed container for short storage. → *Expect:* garlic stays moist but not slimy.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* smashed cloves are bruised and stronger; choose jar peeling when you need whole pristine cloves.

## Decision points

- Whole cloves for roasting or pickling → jar method wins.
- Minced garlic next → smash method wins because it starts the breakdown.
- Very fresh sticky garlic → trim roots and expect a second shake.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Skins do not loosen:** jar is too full or cloves are damp → dry cloves and shake in smaller batches.
- **F2 Jar leaks:** lid is loose → switch to two nested metal bowls or tighten the lid.
- **F3 Clove crushes too much:** smash was too hard → use lighter pressure or jar method next time.
- **F4 Skin flakes cling:** static or moisture → rub cloves between dry towels.

## Verification

The needed cloves are free of papery skin, with whole or smashed texture chosen intentionally.

## Variations

- Two-bowl method: trap cloves between metal bowls and shake like a cocktail shaker.
- Silicone tube: roll cloves inside the tube for quiet small-batch peeling.
- Pre-peeled cloves: inspect for sour smell or slimy texture before using.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from knife handling and glass breakage. Use a sturdy jar, keep the blade edge away from your palm, and clean papery skins before they scatter.
