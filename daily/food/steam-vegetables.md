## Goal

Vegetables are steamed above simmering water until bright, tender, and not waterlogged.

## Preconditions

- Fresh or frozen vegetables, washed and trimmed.
- Pot with lid, steamer basket or metal colander that fits, and water.
- Tongs or a spoon plus a towel or mitt for the hot lid.

## Steps

1. **Cut evenly.** Trim vegetables into similar-size pieces, keeping dense stems smaller than tender tops. → *Expect:* pieces will cook at the same pace.
2. **Add shallow water.** Pour 2-3 cm water into the pot, below the basket bottom. → *Expect:* basket sits over water, not in it.
3. **Bring to a simmer.** Heat until steady steam forms but water is not splashing hard. → *Expect:* steam rises when the lid is lifted briefly.
4. **Load the basket.** Add vegetables in a loose layer, placing thick pieces at the bottom. → *Expect:* steam can circulate through gaps.
5. **Cover and time.** [BRANCH: greens 2-4 min | broccoli or beans 4-6 min | carrots or potatoes 8-15 min] → *Expect:* color brightens and pieces begin to soften.
6. **Check early.** Pierce one thick piece with a knife or taste one safely cooled bite. → *Expect:* tender resistance, not raw crunch or collapse.
7. **Stop the cooking.** Remove the basket or spread vegetables on a plate; season with salt, fat, acid, or herbs. → *Expect:* vegetables stop steaming aggressively and keep color.
8. **Dry if needed.** Let watery vegetables drain for 30 seconds before saucing. → *Expect:* dressing clings instead of sliding off.

## Decision points

- No basket → use a heatproof colander over a pot if the lid still closes enough to trap steam.
- Cooking mixed vegetables → start dense items first, then add tender ones later.
- Want crisp-cold vegetables → transfer immediately to ice water, then drain well.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vegetables submerged:** detect by pieces sitting in water → pour water out until below the basket and resume.
- **F2 Overcooked dull color:** detect by olive or gray color → cool quickly and use in soup, puree, or fried rice.
- **F3 Pot boiled dry:** detect by sizzling or scorched smell → turn off heat, move pot off burner, and add water only after cooling.
- **F4 Uneven cooking:** detect by soft tops and hard stems → cut stems smaller or add them earlier next time.

## Verification

Vegetables are bright-colored, hot through, and pierced easily while still holding their shape.

## Variations

- `microwave`: place vegetables in a covered microwave-safe bowl with 1-2 tbsp water and vent carefully.
- `frozen`: steam from frozen and add 1-3 minutes; do not thaw first.
- `asian-greens`: steam stems first, then leaves for the final minute.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from steam burns. Open lids away from your face, keep hands clear of the steam plume, and use stable pot handles turned inward.
