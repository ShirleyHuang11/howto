## Goal

Chicken is seasoned by wet or dry brining, then dried so it cooks juicy with browned skin.

## Preconditions

- Raw chicken, salt, refrigerator space, and a rimmed tray or food-safe container.
- For wet brine: water and optional sugar, herbs, or spices.
- Paper towels and a clean sink or counter area that can be sanitized.

## Steps

1. **Choose wet or dry.** [BRANCH: wet: dissolve salt in water | dry: salt directly on meat] → *Expect:* method fits your space and desired skin texture.
2. **Use the right salt ratio.** Wet brine: 50-60 g salt per 1 L water; dry brine: 1-1.5% salt by chicken weight. → *Expect:* measured salt, not a random handful.
3. **Add optional flavor.** Add sugar at half the salt weight for browning, plus herbs or spices if desired. → *Expect:* seasoning is dissolved or evenly scattered.
4. **Submerge or coat.** Wet brine chicken fully under liquid; dry brine salt evenly over all sides and under loose skin if possible. → *Expect:* all thick parts contact salt.
5. **Refrigerate by size.** Pieces need 1-4 hours; a whole chicken needs 8-24 hours. → *Expect:* chicken stays below 4 °C the whole time.
6. **Remove from brine.** Lift chicken out and discard wet brine. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* used brine contains raw chicken juices; do not reuse it for sauce. → *Expect:* contaminated liquid is poured away safely.
7. **Rinse only if needed.** If wet-brined heavily, rinse briefly; dry-brined chicken usually should not be rinsed. → *Expect:* surface is not crusted with excess salt.
8. **Dry thoroughly.** Pat very dry and rest uncovered in the refrigerator 1-12 hours if time allows. → *Expect:* skin feels tacky-dry, not wet.
9. **Cook with less added salt.** Season other components lightly because the chicken is already salted. → *Expect:* final dish is seasoned through, not salty on the surface only.

## Decision points

- Want crisp skin → dry brine and refrigerate uncovered.
- Want insurance for lean breast meat → wet brine gives more margin against dryness.
- Chicken is pre-brined or kosher → reduce or skip added salt.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Chicken too salty:** detect after cooking → serve with unsalted sides and use less salt or shorter time next round.
- **F2 Rubbery skin:** detect by wet pale skin → dry uncovered longer and roast with higher initial heat.
- **F3 Uneven seasoning:** detect by bland thick breast center → brine longer or loosen skin to salt directly.
- **F4 Cross-contamination:** detect by raw juices on surfaces → wash with hot soapy water, then sanitize.

## Verification

Chicken has been salted by measured ratio, kept refrigerated, removed from brine, and dried before cooking.

## Variations

- `buttermilk`: use salted buttermilk for fried or roast chicken, then scrape off excess before cooking.
- `quick-pieces`: salt cut pieces dry and rest 45-90 minutes uncovered.
- `aromatic-wet`: simmer herbs and spices in part of the water, then cool completely before adding chicken.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from raw poultry contamination. Keep chicken cold, prevent splashes, wash hands after touching it, and sanitize anything contacted by raw juices.
