## Goal

Proper hot chocolate from real cocoa (or real chocolate), smooth and lump-free, hot but drinkable, and dressed for the occasion — a full tier above the packet, for three minutes more work.

## Preconditions

- Per mug: 1 cup (250 ml) milk (dairy or oat carry it best), 1 to 2 tablespoons cocoa powder *or* 30 to 50 g chopped dark chocolate, 1 to 2 teaspoons sugar (to taste; skip if using sweet chocolate), and a pinch of salt — the quiet ingredient that makes chocolate taste more like itself.
- A small saucepan and a whisk; a microwave variant exists in the decision points.

## Steps

1. **Make the slurry first — this is the anti-lump technology.** In the cold pan or the mug: cocoa, sugar, salt, and just 2 to 3 tablespoons of the milk, stirred into a smooth, thick paste. Cocoa whisked into hot milk directly forms armored lumps; the slurry surrenders instantly. → *Expect:* a glossy, lump-free chocolate paste.
2. **Add the remaining milk gradually, whisking.** Thin the paste with a splash, whisk smooth, then the rest. → *Expect:* uniformly chocolate-colored milk with nothing clinging to the whisk.
3. **Heat on medium, whisking often, to steaming — never boiling.** Milk scorches on the bottom and skins on top when boiled; steaming with small bubbles at the rim is the ceiling (`daily/food/make-oatmeal`'s milk-vigilance applies). → *Expect:* steam rising, tiny rim bubbles, and a bottom the whisk confirms is clean.
4. **Chocolate-bar variant: melt off the heat.** If using chopped chocolate instead of (or with) cocoa: heat the milk first, remove from the stove, add the chocolate and let it sit 30 seconds, then whisk smooth. → *Expect:* fully melted chocolate; the residual heat does it without scorching risk.
5. **Finish with the ten-second froth.** A vigorous final whisk (or a milk frother pulse) aerates the surface into a light foam. → *Expect:* a foamy crown; the difference between hot milk and a beverage.
6. **Pour, top per the occasion, and mind the first sip.** Marshmallows, whipped cream, a dusting of cocoa or cinnamon; the mug is at scald temperature (⚠️ the `daily/food/make-tea` first-sip test doubly applies for kids: an ice cube or a minute's wait for theirs). → *Expect:* a dressed mug, first sip taken cautiously, subsequent sips taken rapidly.

## Decision points

- Microwave route: slurry in the mug, milk in, 60 to 90 seconds, whisk, 30 more; the slurry rule is what makes the microwave version respectable.
- Grown-up variants: a shot of espresso (mocha territory), a pinch of chili and cinnamon (Mexican style), a drop of peppermint or orange extract, or the winter-cabin additions that need no recipe.
- Batch for a crowd: scale in the pan and hold on the lowest heat with occasional whisks; a skin forming means the hold is too hot.
- Cocoa vs drinking chocolate honesty: pure cocoa + your own sugar beats pre-mixed "drinking chocolate" on both flavor control and cost; the packet's only advantage is dissolving fast, which the slurry just took away.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lumps anyway:** slurry skipped or rushed; a fine sieve or a stick-blender pulse rescues the batch, and the slurry gets its dignity back next time.
- **F2 Scorched bottom (burnt notes in every sip):** heat too high, whisking too rare; decant without scraping (`daily/food/cook-rice` F2's move), and accept the pan's soak.
- **F3 Skin on top:** formed during an unwhisked hold; whisk it back in (it dissolves) or lift it off if skin-averse diners are present, and keep the hold cooler.
- **F4 Too rich or too sweet:** thin with hot milk; the reverse (too thin) fixes with a teaspoon of the slurry's ingredients pasted in a cup and whisked back — never dry powder into the hot pan.

## Verification

Smooth to the last sip with a clean pan bottom, steamed-not-boiled, frothed, seasoned with the salt pinch, and the kid-mug tested to drinkable before small hands got it.

## Variations

- Thick European sipping chocolate (`es` churros-grade, `it` cioccolata densa): double the chocolate, add a teaspoon of cornstarch to the slurry, and simmer a minute to pudding-adjacent glory — a spoon beverage.
- Vegan builds: oat milk froths and carries cocoa beautifully; coconut milk brings its own agenda, which is sometimes the point.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk is the standard hot-milk suite: scorch vigilance at the stove and scald patience at the mug, both already trained by this corpus's tea and oatmeal recipes. The rest is winter working as designed.
