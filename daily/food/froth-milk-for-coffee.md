## Goal

Turn cold milk into glossy microfoam that pours with coffee instead of sitting as dry bubbles.

## Preconditions

- Cold milk or barista plant milk is ready and smells fresh.
- Steam wand, frother, french press, or clean jar are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Start cold.** Fill pitcher or jar no more than halfway with cold milk → *Expect:* milk has room to expand
2. **Choose method.** [BRANCH: steam wand | frother | jar method] use clean equipment → *Expect:* tool is ready
3. **Add air early.** Keep wand or frother near the surface, or shake jar before heating → *Expect:* volume increases
4. **Texture.** Sink the tip deeper and swirl; microwave shaken jar with lid off → *Expect:* foam turns glossy
5. **Stop warm.** Stop around 140 to 150 F or when pitcher is hot but holdable → *Expect:* milk smells sweet
6. **Polish.** Tap and swirl to pop large bubbles → *Expect:* surface looks like wet paint
7. **Pour now.** Pour steadily into coffee. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overheated milk tastes cooked and will not become sweet again → *Expect:* foam flows with milk

## Decision points

- Latte art -> use steam wand and whole or barista milk.
- Cappuccino -> add slightly more air.
- Jar method -> remove lid before microwaving.
- Plant milk -> choose barista style.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dry bubbles:** detect soap foam -> add less air and swirl more.
- **F2 No foam:** detect flat milk -> use colder or higher-protein milk.
- **F3 Scalded:** detect cooked smell -> start over for coffee.
- **F4 Overflow:** detect climbing milk -> stop and use less.

## Verification

milk is warm, glossy, and made of fine bubbles

## Variations

- French press: pump warm milk 10 to 20 times.
- Iced: froth cold without heating.
- Oat milk: barista cartons foam best.
- No thermometer: stop when pitcher is barely too hot to hold.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from steam, hot milk, and pressure in closed containers.
Never microwave a sealed jar and keep hands clear of steam vents.
