## Goal

Corn on the cob is hot, sweet, tender-crisp, and ready for butter and salt.

## Preconditions

- Fresh corn ears, a large pot, tongs, water, butter, and salt.
- A sink or trash bowl for husks and silk.
- Enough stovetop space to handle a pot of boiling water safely.

## Steps

1. **Check freshness.** Choose ears with green husks, moist silk, and plump kernels under the tip. → *Expect:* kernels look full, not shriveled.
2. **Decide when to husk.** [BRANCH: husk before boiling for clean water | husk after a 1-minute blanch if silk is stubborn] → *Expect:* the chosen workflow fits your cleanup space.
3. **Bring plain water to a boil.** Fill the pot with enough water to cover the ears, but do not salt it. → *Expect:* water reaches a rolling boil with room for corn.
4. **Add corn carefully.** Lower ears into the water with tongs and cover until the boil returns. → *Expect:* the water bubbles again within 1 to 2 minutes.
5. **Time 3 to 5 minutes.** Cook fresh sweet corn briefly once the boil returns. → *Expect:* kernels brighten and smell sweet.
6. **Test doneness.** Lift one ear and pierce a kernel with a fork tip. → *Expect:* the kernel is tender but still pops with juice.
7. **Drain and season.** Move corn to a platter, spread butter while hot, then salt at the table. → *Expect:* butter melts across the kernels and salt sticks.
8. **Serve promptly.** Keep covered with foil or a towel if waiting a few minutes. → *Expect:* ears stay hot without continuing to boil.

## Decision points

- Corn is very fresh and small → use the 3-minute end of the range.
- Corn is older or large → use 5 minutes, then test before draining all ears.
- Water was salted by mistake → continue, but expect slightly firmer skins.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tough kernels:** detect chewy skins, recover by shortening cook time next batch and salting after cooking.
- **F2 Watery flavor:** detect bland kernels, recover by draining immediately and buttering while hot.
- **F3 Silk remains:** detect strands between kernels, recover by rubbing with a damp towel before serving.
- **F4 Splash burn:** detect water jumping when ears drop, recover by lowering ears slowly with tongs.

## Verification

At least one kernel from the thickest ear is hot, juicy, and tender when pierced, with no raw starchy center.

## Variations

- Milk-and-butter boil: add after cooking or use gently, since dairy can foam.
- Frozen corn: boil 5 to 7 minutes from frozen.
- Grilling finish: boil 3 minutes, dry, then char briefly on a hot grill.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from boiling water and steam. Keep children away from the stove, use tongs, and turn pot handles inward.
