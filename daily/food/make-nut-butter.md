## Goal

Smooth nut butter made from roasted nuts, with enough patience to pass through the crumbly stage.

## Preconditions

- Raw or roasted nuts, salt, and optional neutral oil.
- Food processor or high-power blender with a tamper.
- Baking sheet and oven if roasting.

## Steps

1. **Roast for flavor.** Spread raw nuts and roast at 325 °F for 8 to 12 minutes, stirring once. → *Expect:* nuts smell toasted and are lightly darker inside.
2. **Cool briefly.** Let nuts cool 5 minutes so they are warm, not scorching. → *Expect:* nuts can be handled and still feel slightly warm.
3. **Load the processor.** Add at least 2 cups nuts so the blade has enough volume to work. → *Expect:* nuts sit above the blade line.
4. **Blitz to meal.** Process until nuts become coarse crumbs. → *Expect:* mixture looks sandy and climbs the bowl sides.
5. **Scrape and keep going.** Stop, scrape sides, then process through the dry clumpy phase. → *Expect:* crumbs clump, then start releasing oil.
6. **Process to smooth.** Run in 1-minute bursts, resting the motor if hot. → *Expect:* texture changes from paste to glossy flowing butter.
7. **Season at the end.** Add 1/4 tsp salt per 2 cups nuts, then blend and taste. → *Expect:* nuttiness is clearer without tasting salty.
8. **Jar and store.** Spoon into a clean jar and refrigerate for longer storage. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a burned-out motor or scorched nuts cannot be fixed, so pause if the machine smells hot. → *Expect:* jarred butter with oil either incorporated or ready to stir later.

## Decision points

- Butter refuses to smooth → add 1 tsp neutral oil at a time, but only after several minutes of processing.
- Almonds versus peanuts → almonds take longer and may need more scraping.
- Want crunchy butter → reserve 1/3 cup chopped roasted nuts and stir them in at the end.
- Using salted roasted nuts → skip added salt until after tasting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stuck as crumbs:** not enough processing time or volume; scrape, continue, and add a teaspoon of oil only if needed.
- **F2 Machine overheating:** motor smells hot or shuts off; stop 10 minutes, remove some paste if overloaded, then pulse.
- **F3 Bitter flavor:** nuts were over-roasted or rancid; discard if rancid, or balance mild over-roast with honey.
- **F4 Too salty:** salt added early or heavily; blend in unsalted nut butter to dilute.

## Verification

The nut butter is spreadable, glossy after stirring, and tastes toasted with salt balanced to the nut flavor.

## Variations

- `peanut`: roast peanuts until fragrant; skins can stay on for deeper flavor.
- `almond`: expect 10 to 15 minutes of processing and more heat buildup.
- `cashew`: blends faster and becomes naturally sweet and creamy.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch motor heat, avoid touching processor blades, and label clearly if serving where nut allergies are possible.
