## Goal

An avocado halved, pitted with a spoon, and scooped without the knife-whack injury risk known as avocado hand.

## Preconditions

- Ripe avocado, sharp knife, spoon, stable board, and towel.
- The avocado yields slightly to pressure but is not mushy.
- You will not strike the pit with a knife; that ER-visit cut pattern is avoidable.

## Steps

1. **Stabilize the avocado.** Place it on a towel or board, not in your palm. → *Expect:* the fruit does not roll.
2. **Cut around the pit.** Hold with a claw grip and rotate the avocado against the knife until the cut circles lengthwise. → *Expect:* the blade hits pit all the way around.
3. **Twist open.** Set the knife down, hold both halves, and twist gently in opposite directions. → *Expect:* halves separate with the pit in one side.
4. **Inspect the pit half.** Put the half on the board with the pit facing up. → *Expect:* your hand is not behind or under the pit.
5. **Spoon out the pit.** Slide a spoon between pit and flesh, then lever the pit out. → *Expect:* pit releases without any blade strike.
6. **Scoop the flesh.** Run the spoon between flesh and skin in one smooth curve. → *Expect:* flesh comes out in a clean half.
7. **Slice or dice on the board.** [BRANCH: slices | dice] Put flesh flat-side down and cut with claw grip. → *Expect:* pieces are even and fingers stay clear.
8. **Discard the pit safely.** Put pit and skin in compost or trash before continuing. → *Expect:* the board is clear and no slippery scraps remain.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not knife-whack the pit; a slip can drive the blade into the palm and cause avocado hand.

## Decision points

- Very ripe avocado → spoon pit removal is easiest and preserves flesh.
- Firm avocado → use a larger spoon and work around the pit gradually.
- Presentation slices → scoop first, then slice on the board for better finger safety.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pit will not release:** spoon angle is too shallow → circle the pit with the spoon and lift from several points.
- **F2 Flesh tears:** avocado is overripe → scoop gently and use for mash or guacamole.
- **F3 Avocado rolls while cutting:** it is unsupported → put it on a towel and cut slowly.
- **F4 Knife contacts hand:** unsafe position → stop, set fruit on board, and restart with claw grip.

## Verification

The pit is removed by spoon, the flesh is scooped or cut on the board, and no knife strike toward the hand occurred.

## Variations

- Halves for stuffing: scoop out only a little flesh and leave the skin as a bowl.
- Cubes in skin: cut shallow grid lines only if the half rests on the board, then scoop.
- Guacamole: scoop directly into a bowl and mash after pit removal.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because avocado hand can sever tendons or nerves. Keep the fruit on the board, use a sharp knife only for the perimeter cut, and spoon out the pit.
