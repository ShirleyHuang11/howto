## Goal

A baked potato with crisp, salty, edible skin and a fluffy interior that yields completely to a fork, ready to split and top.

## Preconditions

- Russet (baking) potatoes, ideally of similar size so they finish together. Waxy potatoes bake up dense, not fluffy.
- An oven, a wire rack or baking sheet, neutral oil, coarse salt, a fork or skewer.
- About 50-75 minutes of oven time depending on size; this is not a recipe to rush.

## Steps

1. **Scrub under running water.** Russets grow in dirt and the skin is part of the dish; scrub with a brush or rough sponge, then dry completely with a towel. A wet skin steams and stays leathery. → *Expect:* visibly clean skin, no dirt in the eyes, surface dry to the touch.
2. **Pierce each potato 6-8 times with a fork.** Space the holes around the whole surface. This vents steam; an unpierced potato can genuinely burst in the oven and coat it in starch. → *Expect:* shallow fork marks all around; no need to stab deep.
3. **Oil and salt the skin.** Rub a thin coat of oil over the whole potato, then roll or sprinkle with coarse salt. The oil crisps the skin; the salt seasons it and draws surface moisture out. → *Expect:* a thin even sheen with salt clinging to it.
4. **Bake hot and unwrapped, directly on the rack.** 220 °C (425 °F), potatoes straight on the oven rack or on a wire rack over a sheet for airflow all around. Never wrap in foil: foil traps steam and gives you a boiled texture with soggy skin. → *Expect:* oven at temperature before the potatoes go in; a sheet on the rack below to catch salt and drips.
5. **Bake 50-70 minutes without opening the oven early.** Medium russets take about 55 minutes; large ones over an hour. → *Expect:* the kitchen smells of baked potato in the last third; skin looks dry, wrinkled at the shoulders, and slightly puffed away from the flesh.
6. **Run the done test.** Squeeze with an oven mitt: a done potato gives easily under gentle pressure. Confirm with a skewer or thin knife: it should glide to the center with zero resistance. A thermometer reading 95-99 °C in the center is the precise version. → *Expect:* effortless skewer, soft squeeze. Any firmness at the center means 10 more minutes.
7. **Split immediately and fluff.** Cut a cross in the top, push the ends toward each other so it blossoms open, and rough up the interior with a fork so steam escapes. Waiting keeps steam inside and turns fluffy flesh dense. → *Expect:* steam rising, interior white and fluffy; add butter and salt while hot so they melt in.

## Decision points

- Potatoes vary a lot in size → test each individually; pull small ones early and give the giant one 15 extra minutes.
- Short on time → microwave 5-6 minutes first, then finish in the hot oven 20 minutes for the skin. Pure microwave gives a steamed skin (see Variations).
- No rack space / cooking many → a baking sheet works; flip once at the halfway mark so the contact side does not harden flat.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hard center at serving time:** undercooked; the squeeze test was skipped. Back into the oven; 10-minute increments until the skewer glides. Microwave rescue works if guests are already seated.
- **F2 Leathery, soft skin:** foil-wrapped, oiled too heavily, or oven too cool. Unwrap and give it 10 minutes at 230 °C to partially recover crispness.
- **F3 Dense, gummy interior:** waxy potato variety or split too long after baking. Not recoverable; mash it with butter and call it intentional.
- **F4 Burst potato in the oven:** piercing was skipped. Turn off the oven, let it cool, clean up; the surviving flesh is still edible.
- **F5 Hollow or dark center revealed on splitting:** a field defect in the potato, not your error; cut away and use the rest.

## Verification

A skewer passes to the center of every potato with no resistance (or center reads 95-99 °C), the skin is dry and crisp enough to eat, and the split interior is steaming, fluffy, and separates into flakes under a fork.

## Variations

- `us` steakhouse style: bake at 200 °C for up to 90 minutes for extra-thick crackly skin on very large russets.
- Sweet potatoes: same method at 200 °C; they are done softer and often weep caramelized syrup at the pierce holes, which is normal.
- Pure microwave (8-10 minutes, turned halfway): acceptable interior, steamed skin; use only when the oven is not an option.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall. The two contact hazards: the oven rack during in-and-out, and the potato itself, which holds scalding steam; open it away from your face and hands. Always pierce before baking.
