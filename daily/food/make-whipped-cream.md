## Goal

Whip cold cream to soft or stiff peaks while stopping before it turns grainy and starts becoming butter.

## Preconditions

- Heavy cream or whipping cream has at least 30 percent fat.
- Bowl and whisk are cold.
- Sugar, vanilla, or flavoring is measured if using.
- The dessert or drink is ready because whipped cream softens as it sits.

## Steps

1. **Chill tools.** Use a refrigerator-cold bowl and whisk, or freeze them for 10 minutes → *Expect:* tools feel cold to the touch
2. **Start slow.** Pour in cold cream and beat on low until foamy → *Expect:* cream bubbles without splashing
3. **Add flavor.** Add sugar and vanilla once cream thickens slightly → *Expect:* flavoring disappears evenly
4. **Increase speed.** Beat on medium while moving around the bowl → *Expect:* whisk tracks briefly hold
5. **Check peaks.** [BRANCH: soft peaks for folding | stiff peaks for piping] lift the whisk often → *Expect:* peak tips slump for soft or stand for stiff
6. **Stop smooth.** Slow down near the target. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* separated butter grains will not become silky whipped cream again → *Expect:* surface is satin-smooth
7. **Hold cold.** Serve now or refrigerate covered for a few hours → *Expect:* cream holds shape without weeping

## Decision points

- Cream below 30 percent fat -> it may foam but will not hold peaks.
- Warm kitchen -> set the bowl over ice while whipping.
- Folding into mousse -> stop at soft peaks for easy blending.
- Piping -> take it to stiff peaks but check every few seconds.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Over-whipped:** detect grainy clumps -> fold in a splash of cold cream if only slightly grainy, otherwise make butter.
- **F2 Will not thicken:** detect foam after minutes -> chill everything and verify fat percentage.
- **F3 Splattering:** detect cream leaving bowl -> lower speed or use a deeper bowl.
- **F4 Weeping:** detect liquid in the bowl -> re-whisk briefly by hand and serve soon.

## Verification

The cream holds the chosen peak stage, remains smooth, and has no butter grains or watery separation.

## Variations

- Stabilized: add mascarpone, gelatin, or powdered milk for longer holding.
- Chocolate: fold in cooled melted chocolate at soft peaks.
- Citrus: use zest rather than much juice to protect the foam.
- Savory: omit sugar for soups, vegetables, or coffee.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, mainly spoilage and mess.
Keep cream cold and refrigerate leftovers promptly.
