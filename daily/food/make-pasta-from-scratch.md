## Goal

Fresh egg pasta dough is mixed, kneaded, rested, rolled thin, cut, and cooked quickly.

## Preconditions

- Flour, eggs, salt, and a clean wide work surface.
- Rolling pin or pasta machine, knife or cutter, and a pot of salted boiling water.
- Extra flour or semolina for dusting cut pasta.

## Steps

1. **Measure the ratio.** Use 100 g flour per large egg, plus a pinch of salt per egg. → *Expect:* one serving per egg for a main course.
2. **Make the well.** Mound flour, dig a wide crater, and crack eggs into the center. → *Expect:* walls high enough to hold the eggs.
3. **Beat eggs inward.** Use a fork to whisk the eggs while slowly pulling flour from the inner wall. → *Expect:* a thick slurry forms before the wall breaks.
4. **Bring dough together.** Scrape loose flour into the paste and press until a rough ball forms. → *Expect:* most flour is absorbed, with only dry crumbs left aside if not needed.
5. **Knead firmly.** Push, fold, and rotate for 8-10 minutes. → *Expect:* dough changes from rough to smooth, dense, and slightly springy.
6. **Rest wrapped.** Wrap tightly or cover with an inverted bowl for 30 minutes. → *Expect:* dough softens and rolls without snapping back.
7. **Roll in stages.** [BRANCH: pasta machine | rolling pin] Start thick, fold and reroll a few times, then go thinner one setting at a time. → *Expect:* a long sheet with even thickness and no wet patches.
8. **Cut the pasta.** Dust the sheet, fold loosely, and slice into ribbons, or run through a cutter. → *Expect:* strands separate when tossed with a little flour.
9. **Cook fast.** Boil in well-salted water for 60-120 seconds, stirring immediately. → *Expect:* pasta floats, tastes tender with slight bite, and does not clump.
10. **Sauce immediately.** Transfer with tongs into warm sauce with a splash of cooking water. → *Expect:* sauce coats the pasta instead of pooling underneath.

## Decision points

- Dough is too dry to knead → wet hands and continue rather than adding water by the spoonful.
- Dough is sticky after resting → dust the outside only; too much flour makes tough pasta.
- Making filled pasta → roll thinner than ribbons and keep sheets covered so edges seal.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Egg leaks from the well:** detect by egg running across the counter → scoop flour into the leak and switch to bowl mixing.
- **F2 Ragged sheets:** detect by torn edges in the machine → fold, dust lightly, and pass through the widest setting again.
- **F3 Pasta clumps:** detect by strands sticking in water → stir during the first 20 seconds and use more boiling water next time.
- **F4 Mushy texture:** detect by swollen soft noodles → drain earlier; fresh pasta cooks much faster than dried.

## Verification

Cooked pasta is tender, separate, lightly springy, and coated with sauce within two minutes of draining.

## Variations

- `semolina`: replace up to half the flour with fine semolina for more bite.
- `egg-yolk-rich`: use 1 whole egg plus 2 yolks per 150 g flour for a richer dough.
- `no-machine`: roll small pieces at a time until you can faintly see your hand through the sheet.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Raw eggs and raw flour can carry pathogens, so wash hands, clean the counter, and cook the pasta before tasting more than a tiny texture check.
