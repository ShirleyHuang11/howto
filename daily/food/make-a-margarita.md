## Goal

A bright shaken margarita with tequila, fresh lime, orange liqueur, salt, and enough ice dilution.

## Preconditions

- Blanco tequila, fresh limes, orange liqueur, salt, and ice.
- Cocktail shaker, jigger, strainer, knife, and a rocks or coupe glass.
- Adults only where alcohol use is legal.

## Steps

1. **Chill the glass.** Put the glass in the freezer or fill it with ice while preparing the drink. → *Expect:* glass is cold by the time the cocktail is strained.
2. **Salt the rim selectively.** Rub a lime wedge around half the rim, dip that side in salt, and shake off excess. → *Expect:* salt sticks in a thin band, with an unsalted side for control.
3. **Juice fresh lime.** Squeeze 1 oz lime juice and strain seeds if needed. → *Expect:* juice smells sharp and fresh, not bottled or dull.
4. **Measure the base.** Add 2 oz blanco tequila, 1 oz lime juice, and 3/4 oz orange liqueur to the shaker. → *Expect:* the ratio is tart before dilution.
5. **Adjust sweetness if needed.** [BRANCH: dry | sweeter] keep as is for dry, or add 1/4 oz agave syrup for sweeter. → *Expect:* sweetness choice is made before ice melts.
6. **Shake hard with ice.** Fill shaker with ice, seal, and shake 10 to 15 seconds. → *Expect:* shaker frosts or gets very cold and the drink sounds lighter.
7. **Strain immediately.** Strain into the prepared glass, either up or over fresh ice. → *Expect:* cold, slightly cloudy drink with no spent shaker ice shards unless desired.
8. **Garnish and serve.** Add a lime wheel or wedge. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* extra salt or syrup cannot be removed cleanly once mixed, so add lightly. → *Expect:* aroma is lime-forward and the first sip is tart, cold, and balanced.

## Decision points

- Fresh lime unavailable → make a different drink; bottled lime is the main failure point.
- Cointreau versus triple sec → Cointreau is drier and stronger; basic triple sec may need less agave.
- On the rocks → strain over fresh ice, not the broken ice from the shaker.
- Too tart for the drinker → add agave in 1 tsp increments and shake briefly with fresh ice.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too sour:** lime was large or orange liqueur was dry; add 1 tsp agave syrup and shake again briefly.
- **F2 Too sweet:** too much liqueur or syrup; add 1/4 oz lime and a few ice cubes, shake briefly.
- **F3 Flat and warm:** under-shaken or delayed straining; shake with fresh ice until very cold.
- **F4 Salt overload:** rim was fully wet or dipped too deeply; wipe half the rim clean with a damp towel.

## Verification

The margarita is very cold, tart first, lightly orange-sweet, and the salt rim accents the drink without dominating it.

## Variations

- `tommy`: omit orange liqueur and use 1/2 oz agave syrup.
- `mezcal`: replace half or all tequila with mezcal for smoke.
- `spicy`: shake with one thin jalapeno slice, then double strain.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because this contains alcohol. Adults only, drink responsibly, measure pours, and avoid driving or operating equipment after drinking.
