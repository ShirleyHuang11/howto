## Goal

Maximum clean lemon juice extracted, with seeds caught and zest saved first when needed.

## Preconditions

- Lemon, cutting board, knife, bowl or measuring cup, and fork, reamer, or citrus press.
- Fine strainer or clean fingers ready to catch seeds.
- Microplane available if the recipe needs zest.

## Steps

1. **Zest before cutting.** If zest is needed, grate only the yellow peel before juicing. → *Expect:* zest is fluffy and has no bitter white pith.
2. **Warm the lemon.** Microwave 10 to 15 seconds or place in warm water for a few minutes. → *Expect:* lemon feels slightly warm, not hot.
3. **Roll firmly.** Press the lemon under your palm and roll on the counter to break internal membranes. → *Expect:* fruit softens and gives under pressure.
4. **Cut crosswise.** Use a sharp knife and stable board to halve the lemon around its equator. → *Expect:* two juicy halves expose the segments.
5. **Choose a tool.** [BRANCH: fork | reamer] Use a fork for quick work, or a reamer or press for higher yield. → *Expect:* tool fits into the cut face.
6. **Juice over a strainer.** Twist the fork or reamer while squeezing the lemon half. → *Expect:* juice flows and seeds stay out of the bowl.
7. **Scrape membranes.** Rotate and press the half until the flesh looks collapsed. → *Expect:* little juice remains in the pulp.
8. **Measure and use.** Pour juice through the strainer and use soon for brightest flavor. → *Expect:* clean juice is seed-free and fragrant.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once juiced, zesting is awkward and low-yield; zest first if there is any chance you need it.

## Decision points

- Hard lemon → warm and roll longer before cutting.
- Need clear juice → strain through a fine sieve.
- Seeds in hand juicing → hold cut side up and let juice run around the lemon, trapping seeds inside.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Low juice yield:** lemon was cold or dry → warm, roll, and use a reamer.
- **F2 Seeds fall in:** strainer was skipped → fish them out before mixing.
- **F3 Bitter flavor:** pith got grated into zest → use lighter pressure and discard white-heavy zest.
- **F4 Juice sprays:** squeezing angle is uncontrolled → lower the lemon into the bowl and twist slowly.

## Verification

Juice is seed-free, zest is saved if needed, and the squeezed halves are mostly collapsed.

## Variations

- Lime: same technique, often needs more rolling because it is firmer.
- Citrus press: place cut side down in most hinged presses unless the tool specifies otherwise.
- Meyer lemon: expect sweeter, more floral juice with thinner skin.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep cuts on a stable board, avoid rubbing citrus juice into cuts or eyes, and wash waxed fruit before zesting.
