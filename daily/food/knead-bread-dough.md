## Goal

Bread dough is developed until elastic enough to trap gas, with hydration handled by feel instead of drowning it in bench flour.

## Preconditions

- Mixed dough that has rested 10 to 20 minutes after flour and water met.
- Clean counter, clean hands, bench scraper if available, and a lightly floured or unfloured work surface.
- Recipe hydration understood: wetter dough should feel tacky and alive, not like modeling clay.

## Steps

1. **Assess stickiness before adding flour.** Touch the dough with a clean finger and pull away slowly. → *Expect:* tacky dough stretches slightly; soupy dough coats the finger.
2. **Set the dough on the bench.** Use minimal flour for standard dough or wet hands for high-hydration dough. → *Expect:* the dough releases from the bowl in one mass.
3. **Push with the heel.** Press the dough away from you using the heel of one hand. → *Expect:* the dough elongates without tearing into crumbs.
4. **Fold back.** Fold the far edge toward you over the center. → *Expect:* the dough stacks on itself and traps a little tension.
5. **Turn a quarter turn.** Rotate the dough 90 degrees and repeat the push-fold-turn rhythm. → *Expect:* each cycle works a new side of the dough.
6. **Use the scraper, not handfuls of flour.** If the dough sticks, scrape it up and keep moving. → *Expect:* the bench clears and the dough gradually sticks less.
7. **Rest if it fights.** If the dough snaps back hard, cover it for 5 minutes. → *Expect:* the dough relaxes and stretches farther after the pause.
8. **Check texture at 8 to 10 minutes.** Gather the dough into a ball and feel the surface. → *Expect:* the surface is smoother, elastic, and slightly tacky.
9. **Perform the windowpane test.** Pinch off a small piece and stretch it thin between fingers. → *Expect:* it forms a translucent membrane before tearing, or it tears cleanly after a little more kneading.
10. **Shape for proofing.** Tuck the dough into a taut ball and place it seam-side down in a lightly oiled bowl. → *Expect:* the dough holds a rounded shape and springs back slowly when poked.

## Decision points

- Dough is very wet by design → use slap-and-fold or stretch-and-fold sets instead of heavy bench flour.
- Dough tears during windowpane → knead 2 minutes more, then test again after a short rest.
- Dough is stiff and dry → wet your hands and knead in moisture gradually rather than dumping water into the center.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Floury dense dough:** detect a dry, cracked surface; mist or wet hands, knead briefly, and stop adding bench flour.
- **F2 Endless stickiness:** detect batter-like spread after resting; add flour one tablespoon at a time until the dough holds shape.
- **F3 Torn gluten:** detect rough tearing after aggressive kneading; cover and rest 10 minutes so gluten relaxes.
- **F4 Overworked enriched dough:** detect greasy smearing from warm butter; chill 10 minutes, then resume gently.

## Verification

The dough is smooth, elastic, slightly tacky, and passes a windowpane test or stretches thin before a clean tear.

## Variations

- Stand mixer: knead on low to medium-low until the dough clears the bowl and passes the same windowpane check.
- No-knead dough: time and folds replace continuous kneading.
- Rye-heavy dough: expect less windowpane because rye gluten behaves differently.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep hands, counter, and raw flour dust controlled; raw flour is not ready-to-eat.
