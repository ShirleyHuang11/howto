## Goal

Lentils are cooked without soaking until tender, with the type of lentil matched to the dish and the salt managed for good texture.

## Preconditions

- Dry lentils, water or stock, salt, and optional aromatics.
- Pot, strainer, spoon, and timer.
- A surface for sorting the lentils before rinsing.
- Knowledge of the lentil type: red, yellow, green, brown, black, or French green.

## Steps

1. **Sort and rinse.** Spread lentils briefly to check for stones, then rinse under cool water → *Expect:* rinse water runs mostly clear and no grit remains.
2. **Choose the cooking goal.** [BRANCH: red for soft puree | green or brown for intact grains] match the lentil to soup, salad, curry, or side dish → *Expect:* the selected lentil type fits the desired final texture.
3. **Add liquid.** Put lentils in a pot with about 3 parts water or stock to 1 part lentils → *Expect:* lentils are covered by at least 2 cm of liquid.
4. **Add aromatics if using.** Add bay leaf, garlic, onion, or herbs, but keep acidic ingredients out for now → *Expect:* aromatics are submerged and easy to remove later.
5. **Simmer gently.** Bring to a boil, then reduce to a gentle simmer with small bubbles → *Expect:* lentils move slightly without violently bouncing.
6. **Salt at the right time.** Salt early for seasoned lentils if they are fresh, or halfway through if you are worried they are old and tough → *Expect:* the cooking liquid tastes lightly seasoned.
7. **Check doneness.** Taste red lentils after 10 minutes and green or brown lentils after 18 minutes → *Expect:* lentils are tender at the center with the texture your dish needs.
8. **Drain or hold.** Drain lentils for salad, or keep them in their liquid for soup and stew → *Expect:* the finished lentils are not sitting in unwanted excess liquid.

## Decision points

- Red or yellow lentils → expect them to collapse into a soft texture.
- Brown or green lentils → expect them to hold shape if simmered gently.
- French green or black lentils → use for salads where intact lentils matter.
- Acidic tomatoes, vinegar, or lemon are part of the dish → add them after the lentils are tender.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lentils stay hard:** detect firm centers after the normal time, recover by simmering longer and delaying acidic additions.
- **F2 Lentils burst:** detect many split skins, recover by lowering the heat and checking earlier next time.
- **F3 Watery dish:** detect thin liquid after lentils are done, recover by draining or simmering uncovered.
- **F4 Bland lentils:** detect flat taste, recover with salt, fat, acid, or spices after cooking.
- **F5 Grit in pot:** detect sandy texture, recover by rinsing more thoroughly and sorting carefully next time.

## Verification

Three lentils from different parts of the pot are tender at the center and match the intended texture: creamy for red, intact for green or brown.

## Variations

- Lentil salad: drain, cool briefly, then dress while warm.
- Dal: use red or yellow lentils and cook until they break down.
- Soup: start with more liquid and leave the lentils in the pot.
- Pressure cooker: reduce liquid and time according to the appliance manual.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch steam when removing the lid, keep the simmer controlled, and refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours.
