## Goal

Cook a skin-on salmon fillet with crisp skin, moist flesh, and doneness that is easy to confirm.

## Preconditions

- Skin-on salmon fillets are thawed and pin bones are removed.
- A skillet, neutral oil, salt, paper towels, and a thin spatula are available.
- A thermometer is available if precise doneness is required.

## Steps

1. **Dry the fish.** Pat the skin and flesh very dry with paper towels, especially along the skin edges. → *Expect:* the skin feels tacky rather than wet.
2. **Season just before cooking.** Salt the flesh and skin lightly and let the fillet sit only while the pan heats. → *Expect:* salt is visible and no liquid pools on the fish.
3. **Heat the pan.** Add a thin film of oil to a skillet over medium-high heat until the oil shimmers. → *Expect:* the oil moves quickly and a corner of fish would sizzle on contact.
4. **Start skin down.** Place the fillet skin-side down, laying it away from you to avoid splatter. → *Expect:* the skin sizzles immediately.
5. **Press flat briefly.** Hold the fillet down with a spatula for 10 seconds so the skin does not curl. → *Expect:* the skin stays in full contact with the pan.
6. **Cook 70 percent skin-side down.** Leave it mostly undisturbed for 4 to 7 minutes, lowering heat if the skin darkens too fast. → *Expect:* the opaque cooked band climbs about two thirds up the side.
7. **Flip briefly.** Turn the fillet and cook the flesh side for 30 to 90 seconds. → *Expect:* the top surface turns lightly opaque but does not dry out.
8. **Check doneness.** [BRANCH: thermometer | flake test] aim for 50 C to 52 C for medium, 63 C for fully cooked, or flesh that flakes with slight translucency in the center. → *Expect:* the thickest part matches the chosen doneness.
9. **Rest skin up.** Move the fish to a plate skin-side up for 2 minutes. → *Expect:* carryover heat finishes the center and the skin stays crisp.
10. **Serve promptly.** Plate with sauce beside the skin, not poured over it. → *Expect:* the skin remains crisp when served.

## Decision points

- Fillet is skinless → cook presentation side down first and shorten the first side.
- Fillet is thicker than 3 cm → lower heat and extend skin-side cooking before the brief flip.
- Skin sticks when you try to flip → wait another 30 to 60 seconds for the crust to release.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy skin:** detect pale flexible skin, recover by drying better and using a hotter pan with less crowding next time.
- **F2 Burned skin:** detect black bitter patches, recover by lowering heat and scraping only the worst spots before serving.
- **F3 Dry salmon:** detect chalky flesh and white albumin beads everywhere, recover by pulling earlier and resting off heat.
- **F4 Torn fillet:** detect flesh separating during flip, recover by using a thinner spatula and waiting until the skin releases.

## Verification

The salmon skin is browned and crisp, and the thickest part reaches the chosen temperature or flakes cleanly.

## Variations

- `oven-finish`: after crisping skin, transfer the pan to a 190 C oven for thick fillets.
- `glazed-salmon`: add sugary glaze only after flipping so it does not burn under the skin.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot oil and undercooked fish for vulnerable eaters. Keep raw fish cold, wash surfaces after contact, and choose 63 C when serving anyone who needs lower foodborne-illness risk.
