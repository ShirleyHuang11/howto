## Goal

Pizza dough is mixed, developed, fermented, divided into balls, and ready to stretch without tearing.

## Preconditions

- Flour, water, yeast, salt, and optionally olive oil.
- Bowl, scale if available, cover, and room in the refrigerator for cold fermentation.
- Clean counter space for kneading and shaping.

## Steps

1. **Choose the schedule.** [BRANCH: same-day: 500 g flour, 325 g water, 7 g yeast, 10 g salt | cold ferment: 500 g flour, 325 g water, 2 g yeast, 10 g salt] → *Expect:* ingredients match the time you actually have.
2. **Mix flour and water first.** Stir flour and water until rough, then rest 15-20 minutes before adding yeast and salt. → *Expect:* dough starts stretchy before kneading.
3. **Add yeast and salt.** Sprinkle yeast and salt over the dough, pinch and fold until evenly distributed. → *Expect:* no visible salt piles or yeast granules.
4. **Knead until elastic.** Work dough 8 minutes by hand or 5 minutes on low speed; add only dustings of flour. → *Expect:* dough is tacky, smooth, and springs back when pressed.
5. **Bulk ferment.** Cover the bowl. Same-day dough rises 1.5-2 hours at room temperature; cold dough rests 30 minutes, then refrigerates 24-72 hours. → *Expect:* dough increases in volume and shows small bubbles.
6. **Divide into balls.** Cut into 2 large or 3 smaller pieces; tuck edges underneath while rotating to make tight balls. → *Expect:* each ball has a smooth top and sealed bottom.
7. **Proof before stretching.** Same-day balls rest 30-60 minutes; refrigerated balls warm covered for 1-2 hours. → *Expect:* dough feels relaxed and does not feel cold in the center.
8. **Flour and press the center.** Dust both sides, press from the middle outward, leaving the rim untouched. → *Expect:* a flat center with a thicker outer ring.
9. **Stretch by gravity.** Lift the dough over knuckles and rotate gently, pausing when it resists. → *Expect:* a thin round that expands without tearing.
10. **Use promptly.** Transfer to parchment or a floured peel and top lightly. → *Expect:* dough still slides and has not stuck to the work surface.

## Decision points

- Want flavor and easier digestion → choose cold ferment; time does more work than extra yeast.
- Need pizza tonight → same-day works, but use warm room temperature and expect milder flavor.
- Dough snaps back → cover 10 minutes so gluten relaxes before stretching again.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dough too sticky:** detect by paste on hands → rest 10 minutes, oil hands lightly, and dust the bench instead of dumping flour in.
- **F2 Dough did not rise:** detect by no bubbles after expected time → yeast may be dead; move warmer and wait once, then remix if still flat.
- **F3 Torn center:** detect by holes while stretching → pinch holes closed, rest 5 minutes, and make a smaller pizza.
- **F4 Dry skin on dough:** detect by leathery surface → cover better and knead the skin back in before balling.

## Verification

Each dough ball stretches into a thin center with an intact rim and enough elasticity to transfer without tearing.

## Variations

- `neapolitan-style`: use 60-62% water and skip oil for a tender, blistered rim in very high heat.
- `pan-pizza`: use 70-75% water and proof in an oiled pan instead of stretching in the air.
- `no-scale`: use about 4 cups flour, 1 1/3 cups water, 2 tsp salt, and yeast adjusted to schedule.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wash hands and surfaces after handling raw flour, keep dough covered during long fermentation, and discard if it smells rotten rather than yeasty or alcoholic.
