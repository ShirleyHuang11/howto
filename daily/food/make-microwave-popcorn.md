## Goal

Microwave popcorn is popped with minimal burning, safe steam handling, and an option for a plain-paper-bag DIY version.

## Preconditions

- Commercial microwave popcorn bag, or plain paper lunch bag plus popcorn kernels.
- Microwave with a turntable or rotating habit of pausing and shaking.
- Bowl for serving.

## Steps

1. **Read the bag direction.** Place the commercial bag correct-side up, or add 1/4 cup kernels to a plain paper bag and fold the top twice. → *Expect:* bag lies flat and can expand.
2. **Set time generously, then listen.** Use the popcorn button only if it works well on your microwave, otherwise set 2 to 3 minutes. → *Expect:* popping starts after a short delay.
3. **Listen for the gap.** Stop when pops slow to about 2 seconds between pops. → *Expect:* most kernels have popped and there is no burnt smell.
4. **Wait before opening.** Let the bag sit closed for 15 to 30 seconds. → *Expect:* steam settles and popping stops.
5. **Open away from your face.** Tear or unfold the bag with the opening pointed away from hands and eyes. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* steam burns happen instantly, so vent the first puff away from skin. → *Expect:* hot steam escapes without hitting you.
6. **Pour into a bowl.** Shake gently so unpopped kernels stay near the bottom of the bag. → *Expect:* popped corn moves to the bowl and hard kernels are visible before serving.
7. **Season lightly.** Add salt, melted butter, or oil in small amounts and toss. → *Expect:* seasoning sticks without soaking the popcorn.

## Decision points

- Microwave has hot spots → pause halfway and rotate the bag.
- Smell turns smoky → stop immediately, remove when safe, and ventilate.
- DIY bag starts to unfold → stop and refold tighter before continuing.
- Many kernels remain but corn smells fine → try 10 more seconds next batch, not the same hot bag.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt popcorn:** detect smoke or bitter smell; recover by stopping, discarding, and shortening time next batch.
- **F2 Many old maids:** detect lots of unpopped kernels; recover by using fresher kernels or a slightly longer cook time next batch.
- **F3 Greasy bag leak:** detect oil on the turntable; recover by cleaning after it cools and using a bowl under suspect bags.
- **F4 Steam burn risk:** detect swelling hot bag; recover by waiting longer and opening away from your face.

## Verification

The bowl contains mostly popped kernels, no active smoke, no scorched smell, and the bag was opened away from the body after popping slowed to 2-second gaps.

## Variations

- Plain-bag DIY: 1/4 cup kernels in a folded paper lunch bag, no staples, optional 1 teaspoon oil.
- Lower-salt: use plain kernels and season after popping.
- No turntable: rotate the bag once during the first minute if popping is uneven.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from steam, hot oil, and small hard kernels. Never use staples, foil, or unknown coated bags in the microwave.
