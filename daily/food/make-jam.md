## Goal

Fruit, sugar, acid, and pectin are cooked to a safe set, packed into sterilized jars, sealed, and stored correctly.

## Preconditions

- Ripe fruit, sugar, lemon juice, and pectin if the fruit is low-pectin or the recipe calls for it.
- Clean jars, new lids, wide pot, ladle, funnel, tongs, and towels.
- A tested canning recipe if storing at room temperature.

## Steps

1. **Sterilize jars.** Wash jars and keep them hot in simmering water or a low oven. → *Expect:* hot clean jars ready when jam is done.
2. **Prepare fruit.** Wash, trim, pit, and crush or chop fruit evenly. → *Expect:* measured fruit quantity matches the recipe.
3. **Combine by ratio.** For classic jam, use about equal weights fruit and sugar, plus 1-2 tbsp lemon juice per kg fruit; add pectin as directed. → *Expect:* sugar begins dissolving into fruit juices.
4. **Bring to a hard boil.** Use a wide pot and stir until the mixture boils even when stirred. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* boiling sugar sticks to skin and causes deep burns; use long sleeves and keep children away. → *Expect:* large glossy bubbles across the surface.
5. **Cook to setting point.** Boil until 104-105 °C or until the wrinkle test passes. → *Expect:* jam thickens and bubbles become slower and heavier.
6. **Do the wrinkle test.** Drop jam on a chilled plate, wait 30 seconds, then push with a finger. → *Expect:* surface wrinkles instead of flowing like syrup.
7. **Fill jars hot.** Ladle into hot jars, leaving the headspace specified by the recipe, usually about 6 mm. → *Expect:* rims are clean and jars are filled evenly.
8. **Seal and process.** Apply lids fingertip-tight and process in a boiling-water bath for the tested time. → *Expect:* jars are covered by boiling water and processed fully.
9. **Cool undisturbed.** Lift jars out upright and rest 12-24 hours. → *Expect:* lids ping down and do not flex after cooling.
10. **Label and store.** Store sealed jars in a cool dark place; refrigerate any unsealed jar. → *Expect:* jars are dated and failed seals are not shelved.

## Decision points

- Low-acid fruit or unusual additions → use a tested canning recipe, not an improvised shelf-stable method.
- No water-bath canner → make refrigerator jam and store cold.
- Jam is for immediate use → sterilize clean jars, fill, cool, and refrigerate without shelf-stable claims.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Jam will not set:** detect by runny chilled test → reboil with more pectin or use as syrup.
- **F2 Jam scorches:** detect by brown flecks or burnt smell → stop stirring from the bottom and transfer unburnt jam to a clean pot.
- **F3 Jar fails to seal:** detect by lid flexing after 24 hours → refrigerate and use soon, or reprocess with a new lid.
- **F4 Mold appears:** detect by fuzzy growth or gas → discard the whole jar without tasting.

## Verification

Sealed jars have concave non-flexing lids after 12-24 hours, and any unsealed jar is clearly refrigerated.

## Variations

- `low-sugar`: use pectin labeled for low-sugar jam and follow its packet ratios.
- `freezer-jam`: skip water-bath processing and store in freezer-safe containers.
- `seedless`: cook fruit, strain pulp, then measure juice or puree before adding sugar and pectin.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from hot sugar burns and unsafe preservation. Never touch spilled jam while hot, never invert jars as a substitute for processing, and discard bulging, moldy, or fermenting jars.
