## Goal

A ramekin-baked egg with set whites, a jammy yolk, cream, seasoning, and toast for dipping.

## Preconditions

- Egg, butter or oil, cream or milk, salt, pepper, and toast.
- Oven-safe ramekin, small baking dish, and kettle or hot tap water for a water bath.
- Oven and mitts.

## Steps

1. **Heat the oven.** Set oven to 375 °F. → *Expect:* oven preheats while the ramekin is prepared.
2. **Butter the ramekin.** Grease the inside lightly with butter or oil. → *Expect:* bottom and sides have a thin sheen.
3. **Add cream.** Pour 1 tbsp cream or milk into the ramekin. → *Expect:* a shallow layer covers the bottom.
4. **Crack in the egg.** Add one egg without breaking the yolk. → *Expect:* yolk sits intact in the center or slightly off-center.
5. **Season lightly.** Add a pinch of salt, pepper, and optional herbs or cheese. → *Expect:* seasoning sits on top without burying the egg.
6. **Use a water bath.** Place ramekin in a baking dish and add hot water halfway up the ramekin sides. → *Expect:* ramekin is stable and surrounded by hot water.
7. **Bake until just set.** Bake 10 to 14 minutes, checking early. → *Expect:* whites are opaque at the edges and yolk still jiggles slightly.
8. **Rest and serve.** Remove carefully, rest 2 minutes, and serve with toast soldiers. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* an overbaked yolk turns firm, so pull the egg while the center still jiggles. → *Expect:* spoon breaks into creamy white and jammy yolk.

## Decision points

- No cream → use milk, or skip it and add a small knob of butter.
- Adding vegetables → pre-cook watery vegetables so they do not flood the ramekin.
- Multiple ramekins → start checking at 10 minutes and remove each as it sets.
- Prefer firm yolk → bake 2 to 4 minutes longer.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Watery ramekin:** raw vegetables or too much milk; spoon off liquid and pre-cook add-ins next time.
- **F2 Rubbery white:** baked too long or too hot; serve with toast and shorten next time.
- **F3 Raw white:** underbaked center; return to oven 2 minutes and recheck.
- **F4 Broken yolk before baking:** still usable, but expect a firmer set; crack into a small cup first next time.

## Verification

The baked egg has opaque set whites, a yolk that is still soft or jammy, and toast ready for dipping.

## Variations

- `herb`: add chives, parsley, or dill before baking.
- `cheese`: sprinkle parmesan or gruyere over the cream.
- `tomato`: add a spoon of thick tomato sauce under the egg.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Handle the water bath carefully, use oven mitts, and cook eggs more firmly for people at higher foodborne-illness risk.
