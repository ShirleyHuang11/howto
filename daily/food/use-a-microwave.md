## Goal

Food or drink is heated to the intended temperature in a microwave oven, in a safe container, without splatter, cold spots, or hazards.

## Preconditions

- A working microwave on a stable surface, its interior reasonably clean.
- The food is in, or can be moved to, a microwave-safe container: glass, ceramic, or plastic marked microwave-safe. **Never metal, foil, or sealed containers.**

## Steps

1. **Check the container.** [BRANCH: microwave-safe → proceed | metal/foil/unmarked thin plastic/sealed jar → transfer to a safe container first] → *Expect:* no metal anywhere (including foil lids, gold-rimmed plates, twist ties).
2. **Prepare the food for even heating.** Spread food out; pierce anything sealed in skin or membrane (potatoes, sausages, egg yolks); loosen or vent any lid; cover splatter-prone food with a microwave cover or damp paper towel. → *Expect:* food vented and loosely covered.
3. **Place the container on the turntable and close the door.** Off-center placement improves evenness on turntable models. → *Expect:* door clicks shut; interior light off.
4. **Set power and time.** Default full power for reheating liquids and plates; use 50–70 % power for dense leftovers, defrosting, or anything that exploded on you before. Start short: 1 min for a mug, 2 min for a plate. → *Expect:* display shows the set time; microwave hums and the turntable rotates when started.
5. **Stir or rotate at the halfway point.** Open the door, stir liquids and stir/flip food, resume. Microwaves heat unevenly — this step removes cold spots. → *Expect:* partially heated food; steam beginning to appear.
6. **Finish heating and let it stand 30–60 s before removing.** Standing time evens out the temperature. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* scalds — steam vents when lifting covers; open covers away from your face. → *Expect:* food steaming; container may be hot — use a cloth if needed.
7. **Test the temperature at the center before eating/serving.** → *Expect:* center is hot, not just the edges; if lukewarm in the middle, heat in further 30 s increments with stirring.

## Decision points

- Reheating rice, meat, or soup for food safety → heat until steaming hot throughout (not merely warm), stirring at intervals.
- Defrosting → use the defrost program or 30 % power, in steps, turning the item; cook immediately after.
- Plain water for tea → add a non-metal object (wooden stirrer) or use short bursts: superheated still water can flash-boil when moved.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sparks/arcing:** stop immediately (open door or Stop). Remove the metal source. The oven is usually fine; inspect for burn marks before reuse.
- **F2 Splatter explosion (sauce, yolk):** stop, let cool, wipe the interior with a damp cloth; re-cover food properly and resume at lower power.
- **F3 Runs but food stays cold:** the magnetron may have failed (hum but no heat) — test with a cup of water for 1 min; if cold, the oven needs repair, not more time.
- **F4 Smoke or burning smell:** stop, keep the door *closed* until smoke subsides (starves flames), unplug; discard the food.

## Verification

The food's center is steaming hot (or defrosted through, for defrost tasks), the container came out intact, and the microwave interior is as clean as it started.

## Variations

- Flatbed (no turntable) models rely on internal stirrers — the halfway stir in step 5 matters even more.
- High-power (1000 W+) vs compact (700 W) ovens: package instructions assume a stated wattage — scale time up for weaker ovens.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: steam scalds, superheated liquids, and metal arcing are the three hazards; steps 1, 6, and the water rule cover them. Never run the microwave empty, and never bypass a broken door latch.
