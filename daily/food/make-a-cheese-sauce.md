## Goal

A smooth cheese sauce is built from roux, warm milk, and cheese melted off heat.

## Preconditions

- Butter, flour, milk, grated melting cheese, salt, pepper, and optional mustard.
- Small saucepan, whisk, and a low-to-medium heat source.
- Cheese grated from a block if possible; pre-shredded cheese can melt less smoothly.

## Steps

1. **Warm the milk.** Heat 500 ml milk until warm but not boiling. → *Expect:* milk steams lightly and will blend into roux faster.
2. **Make the roux.** Melt 30 g butter, whisk in 30 g flour, and cook 1-2 minutes. → *Expect:* paste smells nutty and loses raw flour smell without browning much.
3. **Add milk gradually.** Whisk in warm milk a splash at a time at first, then in larger additions. → *Expect:* sauce stays smooth and thickens after each addition.
4. **Simmer to thicken.** Cook 3-5 minutes, whisking, until it coats a spoon. → *Expect:* no floury taste and bubbles rise slowly.
5. **Turn off heat.** Remove pan from burner before adding cheese. → *Expect:* sauce is hot enough to melt cheese but not boiling.
6. **Melt cheese gently.** Stir in 150-200 g grated cheese by handfuls. → *Expect:* each handful disappears before the next is added.
7. **Season last.** Add salt, pepper, mustard, hot sauce, or nutmeg to taste after cheese is in. → *Expect:* sauce tastes balanced, not flat or overly salty.
8. **Adjust texture.** [BRANCH: too thick: whisk in warm milk | too thin: simmer plain sauce before more cheese] → *Expect:* sauce flows slowly from a spoon.
9. **Hold warm, not hot.** Keep over very low heat or a warm water bath. → *Expect:* sauce remains glossy and does not split.

## Decision points

- Using aged hard cheese → mix with a better melting cheese such as cheddar, fontina, or gruyere.
- Sauce will bake in pasta → leave it slightly looser because pasta absorbs liquid.
- Need gluten-free → use cornstarch slurry instead of roux and simmer until thick.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lumpy roux sauce:** detect by flour lumps → strain or blend, then return to low heat.
- **F2 Grainy cheese:** detect by sandy texture → remove from heat and whisk in a splash of warm milk.
- **F3 Split oily sauce:** detect by grease pooling → whisk in 1-2 tsp lemon juice or a spoon of hot bechamel.
- **F4 Too salty:** detect after cheese melts → dilute with unsalted milk or fold into unsalted pasta or vegetables.

## Verification

The sauce is smooth, glossy, seasoned, and coats a spoon without visible oil separation or flour lumps.

## Variations

- `mac-and-cheese`: combine with cooked pasta while loose, then bake briefly with crumbs.
- `nacho-style`: add evaporated milk and a little sodium citrate if available for extra stability.
- `mornay`: use gruyere and parmesan over a classic bechamel base.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot dairy splatter and saucepan burns. Stir gently, keep handles turned inward, and refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours.
