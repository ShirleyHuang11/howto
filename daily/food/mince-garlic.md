## Goal

Garlic peeled and minced evenly, with an optional salt paste for sauces, dressings, and marinades.

## Preconditions

- Garlic cloves, sharp chef's knife, stable board, and optional kosher salt.
- Board secured with a damp towel.
- Knife is sharp; sharp-is-safe because less pressure means fewer slips.

## Steps

1. **Separate cloves.** Pull the needed cloves from the head and leave skins on for the smash. → *Expect:* loose cloves are ready on the board.
2. **Smash and peel.** Lay the flat of the knife over one clove, heel of your hand on top, and press firmly. → *Expect:* skin cracks and slips off easily.
3. **Trim rough ends.** Cut away hard root nubs with fingers in a claw grip. → *Expect:* peeled cloves have no dry root bits.
4. **Slice first.** Cut each clove lengthwise into thin slices so the mince starts even. → *Expect:* slices lie flat instead of rolling.
5. **Rock-chop.** Gather slices, place your free hand flat on the knife spine, and rock the tip through the pile. → *Expect:* garlic becomes small, even pieces.
6. **Scrape and repeat.** Use the knife spine to gather garlic, then chop again until fine. → *Expect:* no large chunks remain.
7. **Make paste if needed.** [BRANCH: mince | salt-paste] Sprinkle a pinch of kosher salt and drag the blade flat across the garlic to smear it. → *Expect:* salted garlic turns sticky and smooth.
8. **Add late to heat.** Put minced garlic into hot fat only when other ingredients are ready. → *Expect:* garlic sizzles briefly and smells sweet, not bitter.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* burnt garlic turns harsh quickly; remove it and restart rather than trying to hide the flavor.

## Decision points

- Raw dressing → use salt paste so garlic disperses smoothly.
- Stir-fry or saute → keep a slightly coarser mince so it does not burn instantly.
- Large batch → peel all cloves first, then slice all, then rock-chop in piles.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Clove rolls under knife:** it was not smashed or halved → flatten it before slicing.
- **F2 Mince is uneven:** pile spread out → scrape together with the spine and chop in a tighter mound.
- **F3 Garlic tastes metallic:** it sat too long after mincing → mince closer to cooking time.
- **F4 Garlic burns:** heat was too high or timing too early → discard burnt bits, lower heat, and restart.

## Verification

Garlic pieces are evenly minced or pasted, skins and root nubs are removed, and the garlic has not browned before the recipe needs it.

## Variations

- Microplane: fastest for raw sauces, but stronger and wetter than knife mince.
- Garlic press: acceptable for cooked dishes, though it bruises garlic more aggressively.
- Confit cloves: mash with a fork instead of mincing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from the blade and strong odors. Use claw grip, keep the board stable, and wash hands or rub with stainless steel to reduce lingering smell.
