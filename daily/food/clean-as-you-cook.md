## Goal

The kitchen finishes the meal in near-serving condition, because cleaning was woven into the cooking's dead time instead of stacked into a post-dinner mountain.

## Preconditions

- A cooking session about to start, and the acceptance that this is a habit recipe: the steps are practices, not a sequence you run once.
- Starting conditions matter: an empty sink and an empty or ready dishwasher (`embodied/kitchen/load-a-dishwasher`). Cleaning-as-you-cook dies immediately if the sink is already full.

## Steps

1. **Set up the stations before heat.** A "garbage bowl" or the bin pulled near the board for scraps, hot soapy water standing in the sink or a basin, and a damp wipe-cloth parked at the counter's edge. → *Expect:* trash, wash, and wipe each have a destination within arm's reach.
2. **Practice: every wait is a wash.** Water coming to a boil, onions sweating, oven preheating: those minutes wash the knife, the board, the measuring cups. Small items go straight into the standing hot water and take seconds each. → *Expect:* dead time consumed by two-minute cleaning bursts; no idle staring at pots.
3. **Practice: done-with-it means dealt-with-it.** The moment a tool or bowl finishes its last job, it goes to the dishwasher or the soak, not to the counter. The counter is workspace, not storage. → *Expect:* the counter holding only what the meal still needs.
4. **Practice: wipe spills at commission time.** Splatter, flour, and drips wipe off in one pass while fresh and cook onto surfaces if left (`embodied/kitchen/wipe-down-a-countertop` at micro scale). → *Expect:* each spill's lifespan under a minute.
5. **Practice: pots start soaking the moment they are empty.** Serve, then immediately fill the pan with hot water (never cold water into a screaming-hot pan; warping and spitting) and a drop of soap. → *Expect:* by the first bite, every pan is soaking rather than setting.
6. **Close the loop after eating.** With steps 1 to 5 done, the aftermath is ten minutes: plates and soaked pans into the wash flow (`daily/home/wash-dishes-by-hand` or the machine), surfaces wiped, sink emptied and rinsed. → *Expect:* the kitchen at baseline the same evening, and tomorrow-you not negotiating with a sink mountain.

## Decision points

- Complex or multi-dish cooking: prep everything first (mise en place) and wash the whole prep wave before the stove phase begins; the practices then run on a clean second lap.
- Two people: one cooks, one runs the wash-and-wipe practices; this is the fastest configuration a home kitchen has.
- Genuinely no dead time (stir-fry pace): let it go during the sprint and run one consolidated burst at the first simmer; the principle is using real gaps, not inventing them.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Looked up and the counter is chaos anyway:** the done-means-dealt-with practice slipped; run one two-minute reset burst now (everything to bin, soak, or dishwasher) and resume.
- **F2 Standing wash water gone cold and greasy:** refresh it once mid-session; grim water washes nothing.
- **F3 Burnt-on pan discovered at cleanup:** it skipped its soak window; boil an inch of water with a spoon of baking soda in it for a few minutes and the residue lifts.
- **F4 The habit never sticks:** shrink it to the two highest-leverage practices (empty starting sink, pots soak at empty) and let the rest accrete; partial habit beats abandoned system.

## Verification

At serving time the counter holds only serving items, every pan is soaking, spills died young, and the post-meal close-out took ten minutes rather than an evening.

## Variations

- Baking sessions: the standing-water practice does the most work (batter tools rinse easily wet, terribly dry), and flour responds to dry-wipe-then-damp-wipe in that order.
- Tiny kitchens: the garbage bowl and one-in-one-out counter discipline are the whole game; there is no counter slack to absorb sloppiness.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Two physical notes: no cold water into hot pans, and wipe floor drips immediately since a greasy kitchen floor is `daily/home/sweep-and-mop-a-floor`'s F4 waiting to happen.
