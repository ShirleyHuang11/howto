## Goal

Tender fruit poached in a light syrup, with a reduced liquid for serving.

## Preconditions

- Firm pears, peaches, apricots, or similar fruit.
- Sugar, water, lemon, and optional spices such as vanilla, cinnamon, or star anise.
- Saucepan wide enough to hold fruit in one layer.

## Steps

1. **Make light syrup.** Combine 2 cups water, 1/2 cup sugar, a strip of lemon peel, and optional spices. → *Expect:* sugar dissolves as the liquid warms.
2. **Prepare the fruit.** Peel pears if desired, halve and core pears, or halve and pit peaches. → *Expect:* pieces are similar size for even cooking.
3. **Bring syrup to a bare simmer.** Heat until small bubbles appear at the edges. → *Expect:* liquid is hot but not boiling hard.
4. **Add fruit gently.** Lower fruit into the syrup and use parchment or a small plate to keep it submerged. → *Expect:* fruit sits mostly under the liquid.
5. **Poach slowly.** Simmer gently 10 to 25 minutes depending on ripeness and fruit type. → *Expect:* fruit softens without falling apart.
6. **Test doneness.** Pierce the thickest part with a paring knife. → *Expect:* knife slides in with slight resistance, not crunch.
7. **Remove fruit and reduce syrup.** Lift fruit out, then simmer liquid until lightly syrupy. → *Expect:* bubbles look a little thicker and flavor concentrates.
8. **Serve or chill.** Spoon reduced syrup over fruit warm or cold. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* boiling hard can shred ripe fruit, so keep heat gentle once fruit is in. → *Expect:* intact tender fruit with glossy syrup.

## Decision points

- Pears are underripe → poach longer at a very gentle simmer.
- Peaches are ripe → shorten cooking and consider leaving skins on until after poaching.
- Want wine-poached fruit → replace part of the water with wine and simmer syrup first to mellow alcohol.
- Serving cold → cool fruit in syrup so it absorbs more flavor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fruit falling apart:** simmer was too hard or fruit was overripe; serve as compote with syrup.
- **F2 Fruit still crunchy:** underpoached; return to syrup and cook 5 more minutes.
- **F3 Syrup too sweet:** reduce less and add lemon juice to sharpen.
- **F4 Bland syrup:** spices were weak or reduction short; simmer the liquid longer after removing fruit.

## Verification

The fruit is intact, tender to the center, flavored by the syrup, and served with a lightly thickened poaching liquid.

## Variations

- `pear-vanilla`: use vanilla, lemon peel, and a small cinnamon stick.
- `peach-basil`: add basil only after cooking so it stays fresh.
- `red-wine`: use red wine, sugar, citrus peel, and warm spices.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use care with hot syrup, keep the simmer gentle, and refrigerate poached fruit in syrup if not serving soon.
