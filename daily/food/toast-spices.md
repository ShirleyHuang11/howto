## Goal

Heat spices just until their aroma blooms, then cool them before volatile oils scorch.

## Preconditions

- Whole or ground spices is ready and smells fresh.
- Dry skillet, plate, and grinder if using whole spices are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Choose form.** [BRANCH: whole spices | ground spices] keep the batch in one loose layer → *Expect:* spices can move freely
2. **Warm pan.** Heat a dry skillet over medium-low → *Expect:* pan feels warm above the surface
3. **Add spices.** Pour spices into the dry pan → *Expect:* spices contact the surface evenly
4. **Move constantly.** Shake or stir so nothing sits in one hot spot → *Expect:* color changes evenly
5. **Use aroma.** Stop when aroma blooms and color deepens slightly → *Expect:* smell is strong, not smoky
6. **Cool fast.** Tip onto a plate. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* scorched spices turn bitter quickly → *Expect:* spices stop darkening
7. **Grind after.** Grind whole spices once warm, not steaming → *Expect:* powder smells fresh

## Decision points

- Whole spices -> toast before grinding.
- Ground spices -> lower heat and shorter time.
- Smoke -> remove immediately and judge bitterness.
- Oil tempering -> use a different lower-heat fat method.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned:** detect smoke or black flecks -> discard.
- **F2 Uneven:** detect pale and dark pieces -> stir more and lower heat.
- **F3 Weak:** detect little aroma -> spices may be old.
- **F4 Clumping:** detect steam in grinder -> cool longer.

## Verification

spices smell stronger and are slightly darker, not smoky

## Variations

- Cumin and coriander: toast whole until one shade darker.
- Ground chile: toast briefly with ventilation.
- Seeds: use the same method but watch popping.
- Microwave: short bursts on a plate, stirring often.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from smoke and irritating chile fumes.
Ventilate, keep your face back, and cool spices before grinding.
