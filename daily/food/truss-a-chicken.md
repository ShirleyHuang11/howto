## Goal

A chicken tied into a compact shape so the legs, wings, and breast cook more evenly and present cleanly.

## Preconditions

- Whole chicken, butcher's twine, scissors, stable board, and paper towels.
- Giblets removed and cavity checked.
- Hands and surfaces ready for raw poultry cleanup.

## Steps

1. **Dry the chicken.** Pat the skin dry and place breast-side up with legs facing you. → *Expect:* skin is tacky enough for twine to grip.
2. **Cut twine.** Pull a piece about 3 feet long so you have enough to loop and tie. → *Expect:* twine reaches around legs and body without strain.
3. **Center under the tail.** Slide the middle of the twine under the tail end or just beneath the leg joints. → *Expect:* both twine ends are even.
4. **Loop over the legs.** Bring twine over the ankles, pull them together, and cross the strands. → *Expect:* legs close over the cavity without crushing the bird.
5. **Run under the wings.** Pull the crossed strands along the sides, tuck wing tips under, and pass twine under the wings. → *Expect:* wings sit tight against the breast.
6. **Tie at the neck end.** Pull snug and knot securely near the neck skin or breast top. → *Expect:* chicken holds a compact oval shape.
7. **Trim loose twine.** Cut tails short enough that they will not burn or dangle. → *Expect:* no twine trails hang near the pan.
8. **Choose tightness.** [BRANCH: roast | grill] Tie snug for roasting; tie slightly firmer for rotisserie or grilling so parts do not flop. → *Expect:* limbs are controlled but skin is not deeply indented.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once roasting starts, retie only with heatproof tools; confirm the truss before it goes in the oven.

## Decision points

- Stuffed cavity → keep the leg tie secure enough to hold filling in place without packing it tight.
- Spatchcocking → do not truss; flattening is the opposite strategy for faster cooking.
- No twine → make small skin slits near the tail and tuck ankles through, then tuck wings under.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Legs spring apart:** twine missed the ankles → unwrap and loop directly over the narrow leg ends.
- **F2 Wings scorch:** tips were not tucked → fold tips behind the shoulders before tying.
- **F3 Twine burns:** loose ends hang near heat → trim them or tuck under the knot.
- **F4 Bird cooks unevenly:** truss is very loose → retie before cooking so legs and wings sit close.

## Verification

The chicken is compact, legs are crossed or closed over the cavity, wings are tucked, and the twine knot holds when lifted gently.

## Variations

- Simple leg tie: tie only the ankles for casual roasting.
- Rotisserie: use two ties, one at legs and one around wings, for stronger hold.
- Herb bundle: place herbs in the cavity before tying.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, with raw poultry contamination as the main hazard. Wash hands, scissors, board, and counters after tying.
