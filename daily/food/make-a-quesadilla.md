## Goal

Make a crisp tortilla filled with melted cheese and optional fillings that stays together when cut and eaten.

## Preconditions

- Tortillas, shredded or thin-sliced cheese, a skillet, and a spatula.
- Optional cooked fillings such as beans, chicken, peppers, onions, or mushrooms.
- A plate or cutting board for resting before slicing.

## Steps

1. **Choose the build style.** [BRANCH: folded tortilla | two-tortilla stack] Use folded for easier flipping and two-tortilla for a larger serving. → *Expect:* the tortilla size and portion match the skillet.
2. **Warm the pan.** Heat a skillet over medium heat for 1 to 2 min. → *Expect:* a drop of water sizzles and evaporates within a few seconds.
3. **Choose dry pan or light oil.** Use a dry pan for a flexible tortilla, or wipe in a thin film of oil for a crisper surface. → *Expect:* pan surface is evenly hot and not puddled with oil.
4. **Lay down cheese as glue.** Put cheese on the tortilla first, then add small amounts of fillings, then a little more cheese. → *Expect:* cheese touches both tortilla and filling so the layers can bind.
5. **Cook the first side.** Place the quesadilla in the pan and press lightly with the spatula. → *Expect:* the bottom shows golden spots and the cheese at the edge starts to melt.
6. **Flip confidently.** Slide the spatula under the center, support the top with fingertips or another spatula if needed, then turn it over in one motion. → *Expect:* filling stays inside and the browned side faces up.
7. **Finish melting.** Cook the second side over medium or medium-low heat until the tortilla browns and the cheese fully melts. → *Expect:* edges look sealed and the center feels soft when pressed.
8. **Rest before cutting.** Move to a board and wait 1 to 2 min before slicing. → *Expect:* cheese firms slightly so the filling does not slide out.
9. **Cut and serve.** Slice with a knife or pizza wheel and serve with salsa, crema, or hot sauce. → *Expect:* wedges hold together when lifted.

## Decision points

- Cheese is not melting before the tortilla browns → lower heat and cover the pan for 30 to 60 seconds.
- Filling is wet → drain or pat it dry before adding, or the tortilla will steam instead of crisp.
- Tortilla is large and fragile → use the folded style so the crease helps control the flip.
- Cheese choice matters → use a meltable cheese such as Monterey Jack, cheddar, mozzarella, Oaxaca, or a blend.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned tortilla:** detect dark bitter patches, recover by lowering heat and using less oil for the next one.
- **F2 Cold center:** detect unmelted cheese after cutting, recover by returning wedges to the pan on low heat with a lid.
- **F3 Filling spills during flip:** detect loose pieces falling out, recover by pushing them back in, using less filling, and adding cheese near the edge.
- **F4 Soggy quesadilla:** detect limp tortilla and steam, recover by cooking uncovered and drying fillings next time.

## Verification

The tortilla is browned on both sides, the cheese is melted through the center, and a cut wedge can be lifted without the filling falling out.

## Variations

- Corn tortillas: make smaller quesadillas and use slightly more oil to prevent cracking.
- Oven batch: bake on a hot sheet pan at 220 C until crisp, flipping once.
- Breakfast: add scrambled egg only after it is cooked and not wet.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot pan surfaces and hot cheese. Keep the handle clear of the edge and let cheese cool briefly before biting.
