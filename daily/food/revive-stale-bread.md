## Goal

Refresh stale bread with moisture and oven heat, or turn it into croutons if it is too far gone.

## Preconditions

- Stale bread with no mold is ready and smells fresh.
- Water, hot oven, sheet pan, and tongs are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Inspect.** Check for mold on crust and cut faces → *Expect:* bread is stale but safe
2. **Heat oven.** Preheat to 350 to 375 F → *Expect:* oven is hot
3. **Dampen crust.** Wet the crust quickly without soaking cut faces → *Expect:* surface is moist
4. **Bake.** Place directly on rack or hot sheet → *Expect:* steam refreshes crumb
5. **Time by size.** Bake rolls 5 to 8 minutes and larger loaves 12 to 15 → *Expect:* bread is warm through
6. **Rest.** Wait 2 minutes before slicing. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* cutting too hot vents steam and gums the crumb → *Expect:* crumb settles
7. **Use soon.** Serve warm or make croutons if it fades → *Expect:* texture is pleasant

## Decision points

- Sliced sandwich bread -> toast instead of wetting.
- Mold -> discard the bread.
- Leathery crust -> dampen more and bake longer.
- Refresh fails -> cube for croutons.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Waterlogged:** detect soggy crumb -> bake longer at lower heat.
- **F2 Hard center:** detect cold middle -> return for 3 to 5 minutes.
- **F3 Burned:** detect bitter dark crust -> lower heat and trim.
- **F4 Mold:** detect fuzzy spots -> discard.

## Verification

bread is warm through with crisp crust and flexible crumb

## Variations

- Microwave: damp towel 10 seconds, then toast.
- Air fryer: lower heat and check early.
- Croutons: cube, oil, season, bake crisp.
- Breadcrumbs: blitz if eating quality is poor.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot racks and steam.
Use tongs and discard moldy bread because heating does not make moldy bread safe.
