## Goal

A grilled cheese sandwich has crisp browned bread, fully melted cheese, and no scorched butter.

## Preconditions

- Two slices of sandwich bread, sliced cheese or grated cheese, and softened butter.
- A skillet with a lid, spatula, plate, and stovetop.
- Optional: a pinch of salt, mustard, tomato, or other thin filling.

## Steps

1. **Choose cheese that melts.** Use American, cheddar, Monterey Jack, Swiss, mozzarella, or a mix; grate firm cheese if the slices are thick. → *Expect:* cheese pieces are thin enough to soften before the bread burns.
2. **Butter the outside faces.** Spread butter all the way to the corners on one side of each bread slice. → *Expect:* each outside face has a thin even butter coat with no dry corners.
3. **Build the sandwich cold.** Put cheese between the unbuttered faces, keeping fillings thin and dry. → *Expect:* butter is on the outside, cheese is centered, and no wet filling touches the pan.
4. **Preheat low-medium.** Set the skillet over low-medium heat for 1 to 2 minutes. → *Expect:* a butter crumb sizzles gently instead of smoking.
5. **Start covered.** Place the sandwich in the pan, cover with a lid, and cook 2 to 4 minutes. → *Expect:* the bottom turns golden while trapped heat starts melting the cheese.
6. **Flip once.** Slide the spatula fully under the sandwich, flip in one motion, and cover again. → *Expect:* the first side stays intact and evenly browned.
7. **Finish uncovered if needed.** Remove the lid for the last 30 to 60 seconds if the bread needs more crispness. → *Expect:* both sides are crisp and the cheese looks glossy at the edge.
8. **Rest and cut.** Move to a plate, wait 1 minute, then cut with a serrated knife. → *Expect:* cheese stretches or oozes lightly without flooding the plate.

## Decision points

- Bread browns before the cheese melts → lower the heat and use the lid longer.
- Cheese melts but bread stays pale → remove the lid and raise heat slightly for the final minute.
- Using mayo instead of butter → spread a very thin outside layer because mayo browns faster.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned first side:** detect dark patches or smoke, recover by lowering heat, scraping off bitter spots, and finishing slowly.
- **F2 Cold cheese center:** detect stiff cheese when cut, recover by returning halves to the pan under a lid for 1 minute.
- **F3 Soggy bread:** detect limp bread from steam or wet fillings, recover by finishing uncovered on low heat.
- **F4 Cheese leaks out:** detect bubbling cheese on the pan, recover by nudging it back with the spatula before it hardens.

## Verification

The bread is golden and crisp on both outside faces, and the cheese is melted across the center when the sandwich is cut.

## Variations

- Tomato: seed and blot slices first so the bread does not steam.
- Extra-crisp: sprinkle a little grated parmesan on the buttered outside before cooking.
- Large sandwich: use lower heat and a longer covered melt period.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot fat and a hot skillet. Keep the handle turned inward, do not leave butter smoking, and let spilled cheese cool before touching it.
