## Goal

Rice is cooked into a creamy risotto with distinct grains, warm stock added gradually, and a loose wave on the plate.

## Preconditions

- Arborio, carnaroli, or vialone nano rice.
- Warm stock in a small pot or heatproof measuring cup.
- Wide pan, wooden spoon or silicone spatula, butter, hard cheese, and optional aromatics.
- Time to stand at the stove for active stirring.

## Steps

1. **Warm the stock.** Heat stock until steaming, then keep it just below a simmer → *Expect:* stock is hot enough that it does not cool the rice when ladled in.
2. **Sweat aromatics.** Cook onion or shallot gently in butter or oil until translucent, not browned → *Expect:* the pan smells sweet and the pieces look glossy.
3. **Toast the rice.** Add dry rice and stir for 1 to 2 minutes until the edges look translucent → *Expect:* grains feel hot and sound faintly glassy against the pan.
4. **Deglaze if using wine.** Add wine and stir until the pan is almost dry [BRANCH: wine | no wine] → *Expect:* the sharp alcohol smell fades.
5. **Ladle stock gradually.** Add enough warm stock to barely cover the rice, stirring often until mostly absorbed → *Expect:* the spoon leaves a temporary trail across the pan.
6. **Keep stirring.** Continue adding stock one ladle at a time, stirring constantly enough to release starch and prevent sticking → *Expect:* the mixture gets creamier while the grains stay visible.
7. **Taste for doneness.** Start tasting around 16 minutes and stop when the center has a small firm point → *Expect:* rice is tender but not mushy.
8. **Check the wave.** Shake the pan; add stock if the risotto sits stiff, or cook briefly if it floods the pan → *Expect:* the surface flows slowly like a soft wave.
9. **Finish with mantecatura.** Off heat, beat in cold butter and grated cheese until glossy → *Expect:* risotto looks shiny and loose, not oily or clumpy.

## Decision points

- Stock is salty → use less added salt until the end.
- Rice is done before texture is creamy → stir in a splash of stock and finish immediately.
- Rice is hard but pan is dry → add more hot stock and keep stirring.
- Serving is delayed → leave risotto looser than final texture, because it tightens as it sits.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Chalky center:** detect hard white cores, recover by adding hot stock and cooking 2 more minutes.
- **F2 Gluey risotto:** detect paste-like rice with broken grains, recover by loosening with stock and stirring more gently next time.
- **F3 Scorched bottom:** detect brown bits and bitter smell, recover by moving the unburned top to a clean pan without scraping the bottom.
- **F4 Greasy finish:** detect separated butter or oil, recover by beating in a spoonful of hot stock off heat.
- **F5 Stiff plate:** detect risotto mounding like mashed potatoes, recover with warm stock until it relaxes.

## Verification

When spooned onto a warm plate, the risotto spreads slowly into a shallow pool while the rice grains remain distinct.

## Variations

- Mushroom risotto: cook mushrooms separately and fold them in near the end.
- Seafood risotto: use light stock and add seafood only when the rice is nearly done.
- Vegetable risotto: blanch firm vegetables first, then fold in after mantecatura.
- No wine: start stock additions directly after toasting.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot liquid and steam. Keep the stock pot stable, ladle toward the pan, and do not leave the rice unattended once stock additions begin.
