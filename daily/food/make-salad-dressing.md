## Goal

A balanced vinaigrette is emulsified, seasoned, and tested on an actual leaf so it tastes right on salad, not just on a spoon.

## Preconditions

- Oil, acid such as vinegar or lemon juice, salt, pepper, mustard, and a bowl or jar.
- Clean salad leaf for tasting.
- Optional aromatics: minced shallot, garlic, herbs, honey, or grated cheese.

## Steps

1. **Start with the ratio.** Measure 3 parts oil to 1 part acid for a classic vinaigrette. → *Expect:* the base tastes bright but not harsh once mixed.
2. **Add binder.** Add a small spoon of Dijon or other prepared mustard. → *Expect:* the mustard disappears into the acid and gives the dressing body.
3. **Season the acid first.** Stir salt and pepper into the vinegar or lemon before adding oil. → *Expect:* salt dissolves instead of sitting as grains.
4. **Add optional flavorings.** Mix in shallot, garlic, herbs, honey, or zest in small amounts. → *Expect:* the dressing smells seasoned but not dominated by one raw ingredient.
5. **Emulsify slowly.** Whisk while drizzling oil, or shake everything in a jar for 15 seconds. → *Expect:* the dressing turns cloudy and slightly thick.
6. **Taste on a leaf.** Dip a lettuce leaf or salad ingredient into the dressing and eat it. → *Expect:* acid, salt, and oil taste balanced on the food.
7. **Adjust deliberately.** [BRANCH: too sharp → add oil or honey | too flat → add acid or salt | too oily → add acid and mustard] → *Expect:* the next leaf tastes better, not just stronger.
8. **Dress lightly.** Toss salad with part of the dressing, then add more only if needed. → *Expect:* leaves are glossy, not dripping.
9. **Hold leftovers.** Store extra dressing in a covered jar and shake before reuse. → *Expect:* dressing is contained, labeled if needed, and can re-emulsify.

## Decision points

- Tender greens → use less dressing and toss gently by hand or tongs.
- Grain, bean, or potato salad → season warmer or denser ingredients more assertively.
- Raw garlic tastes hot → let it sit in the acid for 5 minutes or use less next time.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Broken emulsion:** detect clear oil floating on top; add mustard to a clean bowl and whisk the broken dressing in slowly.
- **F2 Too acidic:** detect puckering after a leaf taste; add oil, a pinch of sugar, or more salad.
- **F3 Underseasoned salad:** detect watery, dull leaves; add salt to the bowl and toss again.
- **F4 Soggy greens:** detect limp leaves and dressing pooled below; add more undressed greens or serve immediately.

## Verification

The dressing coats salad ingredients lightly, tastes balanced on a leaf, and can be shaken back together if it separates.

## Variations

- Creamy vinaigrette: add yogurt, tahini, mayonnaise, or grated cheese.
- Citrus dressing: use lemon, lime, or orange juice and taste because acidity varies.
- Jar method: useful for storage, but mince aromatics fine so they distribute.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Refrigerate dressings with fresh garlic, dairy, or herbs, and discard if they smell fermented or mold appears.
