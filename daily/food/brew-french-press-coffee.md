## Goal

Full-bodied french press coffee with no technique anxiety and no mud in the cup, plus a press that gets cleaned instead of becoming the kitchen's guilt object.

## Preconditions

- A french press (cafetiere) with an intact mesh, coarse-ground coffee (like breadcrumbs; fine grind is what makes mud), and water just off the boil (`daily/food/boil-water`, then 30 seconds).
- Ratio around 1:15: for a small press, roughly 30 g (four rounded tablespoons) of coffee to 450 g of water.

## Steps

1. **Preheat the press with a swirl of hot water, then discard it.** Cold glass steals brewing heat. → *Expect:* warm empty press.
2. **Add the coffee, then all the water, and give one gentle stir.** Make sure no dry island of grounds floats unwetted. → *Expect:* all grounds saturated; a crust forming on top is normal.
3. **Rest the lid on with the plunger pulled all the way up, and steep four minutes.** The lid holds heat; the timer holds you back. → *Expect:* four untouched minutes.
4. **Press slowly and only halfway-firmly.** Steady pressure over ten seconds or so; the plunger should meet mild resistance. ⚠️ Forcing a stuck plunger can blast hot coffee out of the spout; if it resists hard, pull up slightly and press again slower. → *Expect:* the mesh gliding down, grounds pinned below, no eruption.
5. **Pour all the coffee out immediately.** Coffee left standing on the grounds keeps extracting into bitterness; decant the whole press into cups or a carafe now. → *Expect:* press emptied within a minute of pressing; the last few sips left behind if you dislike sediment.
6. **Clean the press today.** Grounds into the bin or compost (not the sink drain; `daily/home/unclog-a-drain` exists because of habits like that), then rinse and wash the beaker and mesh. → *Expect:* a clean press drying, mesh free of packed grounds.

## Decision points

- Taste tuning uses the same two levers as every brew method: bitter means coarser or shorter; weak and sour means finer (slightly; stay coarse territory) or a minute longer. One change per brew.
- Bigger batches scale linearly and steep the same four minutes.
- The press also makes cold brew (fridge overnight at double strength) and doubles as a tea pot, which is one more reason the mesh gets a real wash.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Muddy, silty cups:** grind too fine or the press plunged fast and stirred fines upward. Coarser grind, slower press, and stop pouring before the dregs.
- **F2 Plunger stuck or spouting:** too fine a grind packing the mesh, or too much coffee. Release upward, press glacially, and fix the grind next time.
- **F3 Bitter by cup two:** the decant rule (step 5) was skipped and the second cup kept brewing. Pour everything out at pressing time from now on.
- **F4 Mesh clogged with old oils, coffee tasting stale-rancid:** the press needs a deep clean: disassemble the plunger layers, wash with hot soapy water, and it drinks like new.

## Verification

Four-minute steep, slow successful press, entire brew decanted immediately, cups carry body without mud, and the press is clean with the grounds in the bin rather than the drain.

## Variations

- `daily/food/brew-pour-over-coffee` is the clarity-forward sibling; the press is the body-forward one with zero pouring skill required, which makes it the better guest-house and office method.
- Metal and plastic presses trade the glass beaker's fragility for durability; brewing is identical.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from one specific move: never force a resisting plunger on a vessel of near-boiling liquid. Everything else is a timer and a bin.
