## Goal

A batch of chocolate chip cookies with crisp edges and soft centers, evenly baked, cooled enough to hold their shape.

## Preconditions

- Oven that reaches 180 °C / 350 °F; at least one flat baking tray and parchment paper (or a silicone mat).
- Ingredients: butter, white and brown sugar, egg, vanilla, flour, baking soda, salt, chocolate chips. Butter softened to room temperature (bends without cracking, does not look greasy), not melted.
- Mixing bowl, sturdy spoon or mixer, oven mitts, cooling rack or spare counter space.

## Steps

1. **Preheat the oven to 180 °C / 350 °F, rack in the middle.** Start this first; a cold oven ruins spread and texture. → *Expect:* preheat indicator confirms temperature before any tray goes in.
2. **Cream the softened butter with both sugars for a full 2-3 minutes.** Beat until the mixture is visibly lighter in color and fluffy, not just combined. This aeration is what the leavening builds on; undercreamed dough bakes into dense pucks. → *Expect:* pale, fluffy mixture that has grown in volume, no butter streaks.
3. **Beat in the egg and vanilla.** Mix just until uniform. → *Expect:* smooth, glossy batter with no liquid pooling at the bowl edge.
4. **Add flour, baking soda, and salt; mix until the last flour streak disappears, then stop.** Overmixing develops gluten and toughens the cookie. → *Expect:* a cohesive soft dough; no dry pockets, no long beating after they vanish.
5. **Fold in the chocolate chips with a few strokes.** → *Expect:* chips distributed through the dough, dough still soft.
6. **Portion onto the parchment-lined tray with 5 cm / 2 in between balls.** Roughly golf-ball size, 8-12 per standard tray. Cookies spread to 2-3x their raw diameter; crowding welds them into one sheet. → *Expect:* evenly spaced balls with clear gaps on all sides.
7. **Bake 9-12 minutes and pull when the centers still look slightly underdone.** Edges set and golden, centers pale and puffy. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overbaking. Carryover heat finishes the centers on the hot tray; a cookie that looks fully done in the oven is dry once cooled. → *Expect:* golden edges, soft-looking middles that seem one minute early.
8. **Rest the cookies on the tray 5 minutes, then move to a rack.** They are too soft to lift at first. → *Expect:* cookies firm enough to slide off intact with a spatula.
9. **Repeat with remaining dough on a cooled tray.** [BRANCH: second tray ready and cool → load it | only one tray → wait until it is cool to the touch] A hot tray melts the dough before baking starts and causes overspreading. → *Expect:* second batch spreads the same as the first.

## Decision points

- Dough is sticky and cookies spread into puddles → chill the dough 30 minutes before portioning; also check the butter was not melted.
- Want chewier cookies → shift the sugar ratio toward brown sugar and pull a minute earlier; crispier → more white sugar, a minute longer.
- Fan/convection oven → drop the set temperature by 15-20 °C or expect faster, darker edges.
- Baking two trays at once → swap and rotate them halfway through, or bake sequentially for evenness.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cookies spread into one flat sheet:** butter was too warm or trays were hot → chill remaining dough 30 min, use a room-temperature tray; cut apart the merged batch while warm.
- **F2 Dense, cakey pucks:** creaming was skipped or too short, or flour was overmeasured → for the next batch cream fully and spoon flour into the cup rather than scooping.
- **F3 Burnt bottoms, pale tops:** tray too low or dark metal tray running hot → move rack to middle, double up trays or add parchment, drop 10 °C.
- **F4 First batch overbaked:** they looked "done" in the oven → recalibrate: pull the next tray when centers still look wet-ish, and trust the 5-minute tray rest.

## Verification

Cookies are uniformly golden at the edges, centers soft but set (a cooled cookie bends slightly before breaking), no cookies fused together, none burnt underneath, and the oven is off.

## Variations

- Chill the portioned dough 24-72 hours before baking: deeper flavor and better texture; bake straight from the fridge, adding a minute.
- Flaky salt pressed on top just before baking for a sweet-salty finish.
- No mixer: creaming by hand works; it takes 4-5 minutes of vigorous beating with a wooden spoon.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from oven burns: mitts for every tray transfer, and set the hot tray only on a trivet or cold burner. Raw dough contains raw egg and raw flour; taste at your own judgment and keep it from young children. No privacy exposure.
