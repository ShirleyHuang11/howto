## Goal

A cheese board with three contrasting cheeses at full room-temperature flavor, arranged with accompaniments so guests can serve themselves without instructions.

## Preconditions

- Three cheeses spanning textures: one soft (brie, camembert, fresh goat), one semi-firm (manchego, gouda, gruyère), one hard or blue (aged cheddar, parmesan, stilton). About 80-100 g per person total across all three.
- A board or platter, one knife per cheese, and small bowls for wet items.
- Accompaniments from at least three groups: something crunchy (crackers, baguette), something sweet (honey, fig jam, grapes), something acidic or briny (cornichons, olives), optionally nuts and cured meat.

## Steps

1. **Take the cheese out of the fridge 45-60 minutes before serving.** This is the step that matters most and the one most often skipped. Cold cheese is muted and waxy; fat needs room temperature to carry flavor. Leave wrappers on while tempering so surfaces do not dry out. → *Expect:* soft cheese yields under a gentle press; hard cheese has lost its fridge chill.
2. **Choose for contrast, not theme.** Three is the sweet spot: enough variety, no crowding, one knife each. Confirm the trio spans soft/semi-firm/hard and ideally mixes milk types (cow, goat, sheep). → *Expect:* three cheeses that differ visibly in color and texture on the counter.
3. **Anchor the board with the cheeses first.** Place them spaced apart (roughly a triangle), each with its own knife. Pre-cut a third of the hard cheese into shards and break the first wedge; an untouched board intimidates guests, a started one gets eaten. → *Expect:* three stations, each knifed, hard cheese partially cut, first cuts made.
4. **Fill in accompaniments around the anchors.** Bowls for wet items (honey, jam, olives) so nothing bleeds across the board; crackers in a fan or a separate basket so they stay crisp; grapes in small snippable clusters; sweet things nearer the blue/strong cheese, briny near the rich soft one. → *Expect:* no bare wood showing, wet items contained, every cheese has a natural pairing within reach.
5. **Label anything unusual.** A small flag or card for the blue and anything pungent spares guests a surprise. → *Expect:* strong cheeses identified at a glance.
6. **Serve, and hold back reserves.** Put out about two-thirds of everything; refill from the fridge-tempered reserve as the board wears down rather than crowding it all on at once. → *Expect:* the board still looks composed an hour in.

## Decision points

- Guests include pregnant people or the immunocompromised → prefer pasteurized cheeses and label raw-milk ones; when unsure, check the label.
- Hot day or outdoor table → temper for 30 minutes only and keep soft cheese in partial shade; past 2 hours out, retire soft cheeses.
- One guest count double-dipping knives across cheeses → normal at parties; the reason each cheese gets its own knife from the start, with the blue's knife never migrating.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cheese served fridge-cold:** guests get wax, not flavor → pull the board back for 20 minutes near (not on) a warm oven or in the warmest room; slice the hard cheese thinner, which tempers in minutes.
- **F2 Soft cheese collapsing and running:** over-tempered or very ripe → transfer it into a small bowl and present it as intended-for-spooning; it tastes the same.
- **F3 Crackers gone soft:** they sat against wet items → replace from the packet, keep the rest in a separate dry basket.
- **F4 Blue cheese flavoring everything:** its knife wandered → give it a fresh dedicated knife and a corner position with a bowl boundary.

## Verification

Each cheese yields correctly for its type at a touch (soft gives, hard shears clean), every cheese has its own knife and a reachable pairing, wet items are in bowls with no run-off on the board, and strong cheeses are labeled.

## Variations

- `fr` course order: serve the board after the main and before dessert, with baguette rather than crackers.
- Budget board: one good cheddar cut three ways (shards, cubes, thin slices) with strong accompaniments still reads as generous.
- Vegan board: cashew-based soft "cheese", smoked tofu, and hummus follow the same texture-contrast logic.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall. The real considerations: soft and fresh cheeses left out beyond ~2 hours (less in heat) become a food-safety risk, raw-milk cheeses matter to pregnant guests, and nuts on the board should be announced for allergies. Knives on a crowded table point handles-out.
