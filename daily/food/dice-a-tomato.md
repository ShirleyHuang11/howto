## Goal

A tomato diced cleanly into even pieces, with seeds kept for juiciness or removed for drier dishes.

## Preconditions

- Ripe tomato, serrated knife or very sharp chef's knife, paring knife, and stable board.
- Board secured with a damp towel because tomato juice makes surfaces slick.
- A bowl is ready if you want to save seeds and juice.

## Steps

1. **Wash and dry.** Rinse the tomato and dry the skin so it does not slide. → *Expect:* tomato feels clean and less slippery.
2. **Core the stem.** Use a paring knife to cut a shallow cone around the stem scar. → *Expect:* tough core lifts out with minimal flesh.
3. **Create a stable side.** Halve the tomato through the core line and set cut-side down. → *Expect:* each half sits flat on the board.
4. **Choose seed handling.** [BRANCH: seeds-in | seeds-out] Keep seeds for salsa or salad; scoop them out for bruschetta or omelets. → *Expect:* tomato moisture matches the dish.
5. **Cut planks.** With a serrated knife, saw gently into even slices while holding a claw grip. → *Expect:* skin cuts cleanly without crushing flesh.
6. **Cut sticks.** Stack one or two planks and cut into strips the width of your dice. → *Expect:* strips stay aligned and even.
7. **Cut crosswise.** Rotate strips and cut into cubes using short saw strokes. → *Expect:* dice fall cleanly with limited juice loss.
8. **Drain if needed.** Move dice to a sieve or towel for very wet tomatoes. → *Expect:* excess juice drains without smashing the dice.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* squeezing seeds out crushes texture; decide seeds-in or seeds-out before dicing.

## Decision points

- Very ripe tomato → serrated knife wins because it grips the skin.
- Firm Roma tomato → chef's knife works if genuinely sharp.
- Sauce prep → rough dice is fine because pieces will cook down.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Skin tears instead of cuts:** knife is dull or not serrated → switch to serrated or sharpen.
- **F2 Dice turn watery:** seeds and gel were left in → drain briefly and salt later.
- **F3 Tomato rocks:** round side is down → cut in half and work from the flat side.
- **F4 Pieces are crushed:** too much downward force → use longer, lighter strokes.

## Verification

Tomato dice are roughly even, core is removed, and seed level matches the intended use.

## Variations

- Cherry tomatoes: halve first, then quarter; do not try tiny grid cuts.
- Concasse: blanch, peel, seed, then dice for refined sauces.
- Canned whole tomatoes: crush by hand instead of dicing unless texture matters.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but tomato skin encourages slips. Use a sharp or serrated knife, stable board, flat side down, and claw grip.
