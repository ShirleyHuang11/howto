## Goal

Cut citrus segments free of peel, pith, and membrane while catching the juice.

## Preconditions

- Orange, grapefruit, lemon, or similar citrus is ready and smells fresh.
- Sharp knife, board, and bowl are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Top and tail.** Slice off both ends until flesh shows → *Expect:* fruit stands flat
2. **Remove peel.** Cut downward following the curve → *Expect:* peel and pith come off in strips
3. **Trim pith.** Shave remaining white patches → *Expect:* surface is glossy
4. **Hold over bowl.** Work over a bowl to catch juice → *Expect:* juice is saved
5. **Cut first side.** Slice just inside one membrane → *Expect:* knife reaches the center
6. **Cut second side.** Slice along the next membrane → *Expect:* segment releases
7. **Squeeze core.** Squeeze membranes over the bowl. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* squeezed membranes cannot yield neat segments → *Expect:* juice is captured

## Decision points

- Grapefruit -> use a very sharp knife for thick membranes.
- Tiny citrus -> peel by hand unless presentation matters.
- Need slices -> do not supreme.
- Need dressing -> segment over the dressing bowl.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pith:** detect white patches -> shave before segmenting.
- **F2 Ragged:** detect torn pieces -> sharpen knife.
- **F3 Juice loss:** detect puddles -> hold over bowl.
- **F4 Membrane:** detect chewy edges -> trim finished pieces.

## Verification

segments are clean and juice is collected

## Variations

- Blood orange: protect boards from staining.
- Lemon: remove seeds carefully.
- Serrated knife: works for grapefruit if sharp.
- Zest: zest unwaxed fruit before peeling.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from knife cuts and citrus juice on small cuts.
Use a sharp knife, cut away from your palm, and rinse irritated skin.
