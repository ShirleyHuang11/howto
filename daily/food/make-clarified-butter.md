## Goal

Butter separated into clear golden butterfat with water cooked off and milk solids left behind.

## Preconditions

- Unsalted butter, small heavy saucepan, spoon, heatproof jar, and fine strainer or cheesecloth.
- Low heat source available; rushing browns the milk solids.
- Jar is clean and dry because water shortens storage life.

## Steps

1. **Cut the butter.** Cube butter so it melts evenly in the pan. → *Expect:* pieces are similar size.
2. **Melt slowly.** Set over low heat and let butter melt without stirring hard. → *Expect:* foam rises and yellow fat separates.
3. **Hold a gentle heat.** Keep heat low enough that bubbling is quiet, not frying. → *Expect:* moisture bubbles off steadily.
4. **Skim the foam.** Spoon white foam from the surface into a discard bowl. → *Expect:* the top looks clearer and more golden.
5. **Watch the layers.** Let milk solids settle to the bottom while the middle layer turns transparent gold. → *Expect:* pale solids sit below clear butterfat.
6. **Pour carefully.** [BRANCH: ladle | pour] Ladle or pour the golden layer through a strainer into the jar, stopping before solids leave the pan. → *Expect:* jar fills with clear butterfat.
7. **Leave the solids.** Keep the last cloudy spoonfuls and browned bits out of the jar. → *Expect:* pan holds milk solids and the jar stays clear.
8. **Cool and store.** Cool uncovered briefly, then cover and refrigerate for longer storage. → *Expect:* clarified butter firms into a clean yellow fat.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* browned solids make ghee-like flavor; stop early for neutral clarified butter.

## Decision points

- Neutral saute fat → remove from heat before solids brown.
- Nutty ghee → cook longer until solids turn golden brown and smell toasted.
- Very clear result → strain through damp cheesecloth after skimming.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Butter browns too fast:** heat is too high → pull pan off heat and pour clear fat away from solids.
- **F2 Cloudy jar:** solids slipped in → let settle, then decant the clear top again.
- **F3 Foam overflows:** pan is too small → lower heat and transfer to a larger pan.
- **F4 Water pops later:** moisture remained → cook slightly longer next batch until bubbling slows.

## Verification

The jar contains clear golden butterfat with no visible white foam or milk solids.

## Variations

- Ghee: continue cooking until milk solids brown and the aroma turns nutty, then strain.
- Microwave: melt gently in bursts, settle, then skim and pour.
- Salted butter: works, but salt concentrates in the discarded solids and flavor is less predictable.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot fat. Use low heat, dry tools, and avoid adding water to melted butterfat because it can sputter.
