## Goal

Tender meatballs are mixed with a panade, shaped evenly, cooked by searing and simmering or baking, and checked for doneness.

## Preconditions

- Ground meat, bread or breadcrumbs, milk, egg, salt, aromatics, and sauce or baking tray.
- Mixing bowl, spoon, clean hands, and thermometer if available.
- Refrigerator space if mixing ahead.

## Steps

1. **Make the panade.** Soak 60 g breadcrumbs or torn bread in 120 ml milk for 5 minutes. → *Expect:* bread becomes a soft paste that keeps meatballs tender.
2. **Season the binder.** Mix egg, salt, garlic, herbs, cheese, and pepper into the panade before adding meat. → *Expect:* seasoning is evenly dispersed.
3. **Add meat gently.** Add 500 g ground meat and fold with fingers or fork just until combined. → *Expect:* mixture holds together without becoming smeared and pasty.
4. **Cook a test bite.** Fry a teaspoon of mixture and taste for salt. → *Expect:* seasoning can be corrected before shaping all of it.
5. **Shape evenly.** Roll 30-45 g portions with damp hands. → *Expect:* meatballs are similar size and cook evenly.
6. **Choose cooking path.** [BRANCH: sear-then-simmer | bake] Sear in oil before simmering in sauce, or bake on a rimmed tray at 220 °C. → *Expect:* browned exterior or firm set surface.
7. **Cook through.** Simmer 15-25 minutes or bake 15-20 minutes depending on size. → *Expect:* centers reach 71 °C for beef, pork, or mixed meat; 74 °C for poultry.
8. **Rest briefly.** Let meatballs sit 5 minutes in sauce or on the tray. → *Expect:* juices settle and texture firms.
9. **Serve or store.** Serve hot, or cool quickly and refrigerate. → *Expect:* leftovers are packed within 2 hours.

## Decision points

- Mixture feels loose → chill 15 minutes before adding more breadcrumbs.
- Want very tender meatballs → use higher-fat meat and avoid compacting during shaping.
- Cooking in sauce → brown first for flavor, but keep simmer gentle so they do not toughen.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tough meatballs:** detect by rubbery bite → mix less next time and increase panade.
- **F2 Falling apart:** detect by crumbling in pan → chill mixture, add egg or breadcrumbs, and turn less aggressively.
- **F3 Raw center:** detect by low thermometer reading or pink poultry → continue cooking gently and recheck.
- **F4 Greasy sauce:** detect by oil pooling → skim fat from the top before serving.

## Verification

Meatballs are evenly shaped, tender when cut, and cooked to the safe internal temperature for the meat used.

## Variations

- `italian-style`: beef and pork, parmesan, parsley, garlic, and tomato sauce.
- `swedish-style`: smaller meatballs with allspice or nutmeg, served with cream gravy.
- `turkey`: add extra panade and oil because poultry is lean.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from raw meat and hot fat. Wash hands after mixing, avoid splatter while searing, and do not hold cooked meatballs at room temperature for more than 2 hours.
