## Goal

A pineapple trimmed, peeled, eyes removed, cored, and cut into spears or chunks.

## Preconditions

- Ripe pineapple, sharp chef's knife, paring knife, stable board, and bowl.
- Board secured with a damp towel; pineapple juice makes it slick.
- Knife is sharp enough to remove skin with controlled downward cuts.

## Steps

1. **Trim top and base.** Lay pineapple on its side and cut off the crown and bottom. → *Expect:* two flat ends make the fruit stand stable.
2. **Stand it upright.** Put the broad flat base on the board and hold the top with a claw grip. → *Expect:* pineapple does not wobble.
3. **Cut down the skin.** Slice from top to bottom following the curve, removing peel in strips. → *Expect:* yellow flesh shows with some brown eyes remaining.
4. **Remove the eyes.** Use a paring knife to cut shallow diagonal channels following the spiral rows of eyes. → *Expect:* tough brown spots are gone with minimal flesh loss.
5. **Quarter lengthwise.** Cut the peeled pineapple from top to bottom into four wedges. → *Expect:* each wedge has a strip of pale core at the inner edge.
6. **Cut out the core.** Lay each wedge flat and slice away the firm inner core. → *Expect:* only tender yellow flesh remains.
7. **Choose final shape.** [BRANCH: spears | chunks] Leave long wedges as spears, or cut crosswise into bite-size pieces. → *Expect:* pieces are even for serving.
8. **Collect juice and clean.** Move pieces to a bowl and wipe the board before it gets slippery. → *Expect:* fruit is ready and the work surface is safe.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* deep peel cuts waste fruit; follow the curve and remove eyes separately.

## Decision points

- Very ripe pineapple → cut slightly thicker pieces because soft flesh crushes easily.
- Decorative rings → peel whole, remove eyes, slice rounds, then punch out cores.
- Grilling → spears are easier to turn and less likely to fall through grates.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pineapple rocks:** ends are not flat → recut a thin slice from the base.
- **F2 Too much flesh removed:** peel cut is too deep → switch to thinner skin strips and remove eyes after.
- **F3 Eyes remain:** spiral rows were missed → trim with a paring knife before serving.
- **F4 Knife slips in juice:** board is wet → wipe the board and reset the damp towel underneath.

## Verification

Pineapple pieces have no tough skin, eyes, or core, and are cut evenly into the chosen shape.

## Variations

- Ring method: peel upright, slice rounds, then core each round.
- Boat presentation: quarter with leaves attached, remove core, cut flesh free, then cube in the shell.
- Salsa dice: cut spears into small cubes and drain excess juice.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from a large slick fruit and sharp knife. Use a stable flat base, claw grip, and controlled downward cuts.
