## Goal

Crisp, evenly seasoned croutons from stale bread, suitable for salads, soups, or snacking.

## Preconditions

- Stale bread, olive oil or melted butter, salt, pepper, and optional garlic or herbs.
- Knife, cutting board, bowl, and baking sheet or skillet.
- Oven or stovetop.

## Steps

1. **Cut even cubes.** Slice bread into 1/2 to 3/4 inch cubes. → *Expect:* pieces are similar size so they crisp together.
2. **Dry fresh bread if needed.** If bread is soft, leave cubes out 20 minutes or toast briefly at low heat. → *Expect:* surfaces feel dry, not squishy.
3. **Season with fat.** Toss 4 cups bread with 3 tbsp oil or butter, 1/2 tsp salt, pepper, and seasonings. → *Expect:* bread is lightly coated, not soaked.
4. **Choose cooking method.** [BRANCH: oven | pan] use a 375 °F oven for batches, or a skillet over medium heat for small amounts. → *Expect:* one cooking path is set before browning starts.
5. **Cook in one layer.** Spread on a baking sheet or keep cubes moving in the pan. → *Expect:* cubes touch heat directly instead of steaming in a pile.
6. **Turn for even color.** Bake 10 to 15 minutes, stirring once, or pan-toast 6 to 10 minutes. → *Expect:* multiple sides become golden.
7. **Cool completely.** Move croutons to a plate or leave on the sheet off heat. → *Expect:* centers finish drying and the texture crisps as they cool.
8. **Store dry.** Transfer to an airtight container only when fully cool. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* burned garlic powder or blackened bread tastes bitter and cannot be fixed. → *Expect:* crisp croutons that rattle in the container.

## Decision points

- Dense bread → cut smaller or bake longer at slightly lower heat.
- Delicate sandwich bread → use less oil and turn gently.
- Fresh garlic → add near the end in a pan, or use garlic-infused oil for oven batches.
- Want soup croutons → larger cubes are fine if fully dried.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy croutons:** too much oil or crowded pan; return to a 325 °F oven until dry.
- **F2 Uneven browning:** pieces varied in size or were not turned; remove browned pieces and keep pale ones cooking.
- **F3 Too salty:** seasoning was heavy; toss with unsalted toasted bread cubes to dilute.
- **F4 Burnt edges:** heat too high; discard black pieces and lower heat next batch.

## Verification

Croutons are golden on several sides, crisp after cooling, and seasoned enough to taste good alone.

## Variations

- `caesar`: add garlic, parmesan after baking, and black pepper.
- `sourdough`: use olive oil and bake until deeply golden.
- `butter-herb`: use melted butter with dried thyme or parsley.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch hot oil and sheet pans, and let croutons cool before sealing so trapped steam does not soften them.
