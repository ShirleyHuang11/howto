## Goal

Extract flavor and body from bones or scraps into a clear stock by starting cold and simmering gently.

## Preconditions

- Fresh bones, carcass, shells, or vegetable scraps is ready and smells fresh.
- Large pot, cold water, aromatics, strainer, and containers are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Start cold.** Cover bones or scraps with cold water by 1 to 2 inches → *Expect:* solids are submerged
2. **Heat slowly.** Bring toward a simmer over medium → *Expect:* foam rises
3. **Skim.** Remove gray scum with a ladle → *Expect:* surface looks cleaner
4. **Add aromatics.** Add onion, carrot, celery, herbs, or peppercorns → *Expect:* aromatics are submerged
5. **Simmer low.** Hold only a few bubbles at the surface → *Expect:* stock trembles without boiling
6. **Strain gently.** Pour through a strainer without pressing. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* pressing spent solids clouds stock → *Expect:* liquid runs clear
7. **Cool quickly.** Use shallow containers and refrigerate or freeze → *Expect:* stock chills promptly

## Decision points

- Chicken -> simmer 2 to 4 hours.
- Beef -> roast bones first and simmer longer.
- Vegetable -> simmer 45 to 90 minutes.
- Clear soup -> never hard boil.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cloudy:** detect milky liquid -> use as cooking stock and simmer lower next time.
- **F2 Weak:** detect watery taste -> reduce strained stock.
- **F3 Greasy:** detect fat cap -> chill and lift it off.
- **F4 Unsafe cooling:** detect hours at room temp -> discard if safety is doubtful.

## Verification

stock tastes savory and is strained and cooled safely

## Variations

- White stock: do not roast bones.
- Brown stock: roast bones and onions.
- Seafood: simmer shells only 30 to 45 minutes.
- Pressure cooker: natural-release before opening.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot liquid and food-safety errors.
Cool stock quickly, refrigerate within 2 hours, and discard stock with a spoiled smell.
