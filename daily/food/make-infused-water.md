## Goal

Cold water lightly flavored with fruit, herbs, cucumber, or citrus without bitterness or mushy solids.

## Preconditions

- Clean drinking water, washed produce, and a pitcher or bottle.
- Knife, cutting board, and fridge space.
- Ice if serving immediately.

## Steps

1. **Wash the produce.** Rinse fruit, herbs, and cucumber under cool water. → *Expect:* visible grit is gone and leaves look fresh.
2. **Slice for surface area.** Cut citrus, cucumber, berries, or stone fruit into thin slices or halves. → *Expect:* cut surfaces are exposed without pulverizing the fruit.
3. **Crush herbs lightly.** Clap mint, basil, or rosemary between your hands or press once with a spoon. → *Expect:* herbs smell stronger but are not shredded.
4. **Combine with cold water.** Add about 1 cup produce to 1 quart water. → *Expect:* ingredients move freely and are fully submerged.
5. **Steep cold.** Refrigerate 1 to 4 hours; use citrus-heavy blends closer to 1 hour. → *Expect:* water takes on aroma and faint color while staying clear.
6. **Taste and remove bitter items.** Pull citrus rinds or strong herbs once the flavor is enough. → *Expect:* flavor is refreshing, not pithy or medicinal.
7. **Serve over ice.** Pour gently so soft fruit stays behind if desired. → *Expect:* glass has clean flavored water and a few attractive pieces.
8. **Refill once.** Add more cold water to the same solids one time, then discard after the second batch. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* discard fruit water left warm for more than 2 hours; do not chill it again for later. → *Expect:* second infusion is lighter but still pleasant.

## Decision points

- Citrus with peel → shorter steep prevents bitter pith flavor.
- Soft berries → halve or lightly press them, but do not mash unless you want cloudy water.
- Herbs seem strong → use fewer stems and remove earlier.
- Serving all day → keep pitcher refrigerated and pour portions as needed.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bitter water:** citrus peel steeped too long; remove citrus, dilute with cold water, and add fresh cucumber.
- **F2 Weak flavor:** pieces are too large or steep was short; slice thinner and wait another hour cold.
- **F3 Cloudy or pulpy water:** fruit was mashed; strain through a fine sieve and use gentler cuts next time.
- **F4 Slimy fruit:** held too long; discard, wash the pitcher, and restart with fresh produce.

## Verification

The water is cold, clean-tasting, lightly aromatic, and the solids are fresh-looking or removed before storage.

## Variations

- `cucumber-mint-lime`: remove lime after 1 hour; cucumber and mint can stay longer.
- `strawberry-basil`: halve berries and bruise basil lightly.
- `orange-rosemary`: use one small rosemary sprig per quart to avoid resin flavor.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wash produce, keep infused water cold, and discard after 24 hours or sooner if it smells off.
