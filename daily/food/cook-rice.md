## Goal

A batch of rice cooked to fluffy, separate (or intentionally sticky) grains — not burnt, crunchy, or gluey.

## Preconditions

- White rice (long-grain assumed; see Variations), a pot with a tight lid or a rice cooker.
- Water and a measuring cup (any consistent cup works — the ratio is what matters).

## Steps

1. **Measure the rice.** ~¾ cup dry per hungry person. → *Expect:* rice in a bowl or the cooker pot, quantity known in "cups".
2. **Rinse until the water runs mostly clear.** Cover with cold water, agitate with fingers, pour off the cloudy water; repeat 2–3×. → *Expect:* rinse water goes from milky to nearly clear — this is what prevents gluey rice.
3. **Add water by ratio.** Long-grain white: 1 : 1.5 (rice : water). Rice cooker: fill to the matching numbered line instead. → *Expect:* correct water level; a pinch of salt in if desired.
4. **Bring to a boil uncovered, then drop to the lowest heat and lid on.** [BRANCH: rice cooker → press cook and skip to step 6] → *Expect:* brief visible boil, then a quiet simmer under a closed lid.
5. **Simmer ~15 min without lifting the lid.** The trapped steam is doing the cooking; every peek costs it. → *Expect:* faint hissing that quiets near the end; no burnt smell (burnt smell → F2).
6. **Rest 10 minutes off the heat, lid still on.** Rice cooker: after it clicks to "keep warm", same rest. → *Expect:* untouched pot; the rest lets moisture equalize so the bottom isn't wet and the top isn't dry.
7. **Fluff with a fork or paddle and serve.** Gentle folding, not stirring. → *Expect:* grains separate and tender all the way through.

## Decision points

- Brown rice → more water (~1 : 1.8) and ~40 min simmer; everything else identical.
- Short-grain / sushi rice → rinse more thoroughly, ratio near 1 : 1.1, and stickiness is the goal, not a failure.
- Doubling the batch → ratio holds, but simmer time grows a little; the finger-joint method (water one first-knuckle above the leveled rice) scales automatically.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Crunchy center at the rest's end:** add 3–4 tbsp hot water, lid on, 5 more minutes on lowest heat, rest again.
- **F2 Burnt smell mid-simmer:** heat too high — kill the heat, do NOT stir (stirring drags the burnt layer through), transfer the unburnt top to a new pot with a splash of water and finish; soak the pot.
- **F3 Gluey mush:** too much water or unrinsed starch — usable for congee/fried-rice-tomorrow; fix ratio and rinse next round.
- **F4 Boiled over foamy water:** lid was on during the initial boil — boil uncovered first (step 4), or wipe and continue at lower heat.

## Verification

Grains are tender through the center, distinct (unless sticky was intended), nothing is burnt to the pot beyond a light film, and quantity matches the plan.

## Variations

- Rice cooker cultures (`jp`, `cn`, `kr`): the cooker + its water lines make steps 3–6 automatic — the rinse is still on you.
- Pilaf method: toast the rinsed rice in oil first, then add hot water/stock — same ratio and rest.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk only around the stove: an unattended simmer is still an attended appliance — stay in earshot, and mind the steam burst when first lifting the lid.
