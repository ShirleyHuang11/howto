## Goal

Make refrigerator pickles with hot vinegar brine, aromatics, cooling, and chilled storage.

## Preconditions

- Fresh vegetables and a clean heatproof jar is ready and smells fresh.
- Vinegar, water, salt, sugar, and aromatics are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Prep vegetables.** Wash, trim, and slice evenly → *Expect:* pieces fit the jar
2. **Pack jar.** Add aromatics and vegetables without crushing → *Expect:* jar is full but loose
3. **Mix brine.** Use equal parts vinegar and water with salt and sugar → *Expect:* seasoning dissolves partly
4. **Heat brine.** Bring just to a simmer → *Expect:* brine is clear and steaming
5. **Pour brine.** Cover vegetables fully. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* hot brine can crack weak glass, so use a heatproof jar → *Expect:* vegetables are submerged
6. **Cool.** Let steaming stop before lidding → *Expect:* jar closes without pressure
7. **Refrigerate.** Chill 2 hours for thin slices or overnight for thick pieces → *Expect:* pickles taste tangy

## Decision points

- Crisp cucumber -> use firm small cucumbers and do not boil them.
- Sweeter pickle -> increase sugar but keep vinegar.
- Shelf-stable goal -> use a tested canning recipe, not this one.
- Vegetables float -> add weight or more brine.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Floating:** detect exposed pieces -> add clean weight.
- **F2 Too sharp:** detect harsh vinegar -> add a little sugar next time.
- **F3 Soft:** detect limp texture -> use fresher vegetables and chill faster.
- **F4 Spoilage:** detect fizz, mold, slime, or rot -> discard.

## Verification

vegetables are submerged in cold tangy brine

## Variations

- Red onion: ready in 30 minutes to 2 hours.
- Carrot: slice thin and rest overnight.
- Spicy: add chile or pepper flakes.
- Rice vinegar: mild for cucumber and radish.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot brine and refrigerated food safety.
These are not shelf-stable preserves; keep refrigerated and use within about 2 to 4 weeks.
