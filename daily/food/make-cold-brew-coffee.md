## Goal

A clean, low-bitterness cold brew concentrate that can be diluted over ice or with milk.

## Preconditions

- Coarsely ground coffee, filtered water, a jar or pitcher, and a fine strainer.
- A paper filter, nut milk bag, or clean cloth for the final pass.
- Fridge space for the brew container.

## Steps

1. **Grind coarse.** Use coffee about as coarse as raw sugar, not espresso or drip-fine. → *Expect:* distinct gritty particles; very little powder at the bottom.
2. **Measure a concentrate ratio.** Combine 1 part coffee by weight with 5 parts cold water, such as 100 g coffee and 500 g water. → *Expect:* all grounds are wet but the mixture is still thick.
3. **Stir until fully saturated.** Scrape the dry clumps from the sides and bottom. → *Expect:* no pale floating islands of dry coffee remain.
4. **Cover and refrigerate.** Steep 12 to 18 hours in the fridge. → *Expect:* the liquid darkens slowly and smells sweet rather than sharp.
5. **Strain the grounds.** Pour through a fine strainer without pressing hard on the bed. → *Expect:* most grounds stay behind and the coffee flows steadily.
6. **Filter for clarity.** Run the strained brew through paper, cloth, or a nut milk bag. → *Expect:* fewer silty particles and a smoother mouthfeel.
7. **Dilute to drink.** Start with 1 part concentrate to 1 part water, milk, or ice, then adjust. → *Expect:* coffee tastes strong but not muddy or harsh.
8. **Store cold.** Transfer concentrate to a clean bottle and refrigerate up to 5 days. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* discard brew left warm for hours or smelling fermented; do not try to rescue it. → *Expect:* sealed cold brew labeled or easy to identify.

## Decision points

- Drinking black → filter more carefully; sediment is more obvious without milk.
- Want ready-to-drink cold brew → brew at 1:8 coffee to water instead of 1:5 and dilute less.
- Using pre-ground drip coffee → shorten to 10 to 12 hours and expect more bitterness.
- No scale → use about 1 cup coarse grounds to 3 cups water for concentrate.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bitter and dusty:** grind was too fine or steep was too long; dilute, add milk, and use coarser coffee next batch.
- **F2 Weak and tea-like:** ratio was too dilute or steep was short; steep 2 more hours if grounds are still present, or reduce dilution.
- **F3 Slow clogged filter:** fines blocked the paper; pre-strain through mesh first, then change to a fresh filter.
- **F4 Sour or fermented smell:** brew was held too warm or too long; discard and sanitize the container.

## Verification

The finished concentrate pours clear enough to leave little grit in the glass, tastes rounded after dilution, and is stored covered in the fridge.

## Variations

- `generic-mellow`: use 1:6 coffee to water and steep 14 hours for a softer concentrate.
- `generic-bold`: use 1:4.5 and dilute more at serving.
- `decaf`: same ratio and time; freshness matters more because stale decaf tastes flat quickly.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep the brew refrigerated after steeping, use clean containers, and discard any batch that smells alcoholic, fizzy, or moldy.
