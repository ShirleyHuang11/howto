## Goal

Soft, sweet roasted garlic cloves that squeeze out like jam for spreading, sauces, or dressings.

## Preconditions

- Whole garlic heads, olive oil, salt, foil or a covered baking dish.
- Oven, knife, cutting board, and a small spoon or fork.
- Heat-safe surface for cooling.

## Steps

1. **Heat the oven low.** Set oven to 325 °F. → *Expect:* steady moderate heat that softens garlic without burning the edges.
2. **Trim the top.** Slice about 1/4 inch off the stem end to expose most clove tips. → *Expect:* cut faces of many cloves are visible.
3. **Set garlic on foil.** Place each head cut-side up on foil or in a small baking dish. → *Expect:* heads sit upright enough to hold oil.
4. **Oil and season.** Drizzle 1 to 2 tsp olive oil per head and add a pinch of salt. → *Expect:* oil seeps between exposed cloves.
5. **Wrap or cover tightly.** Seal foil around the head or cover the dish. → *Expect:* garlic is enclosed so steam stays inside.
6. **Roast until jammy.** Bake 40 to 60 minutes depending on size. → *Expect:* cloves are golden, soft, and easily pierced.
7. **Cool before squeezing.** Rest 10 minutes, then squeeze from the root end or lift cloves with a fork. → *Expect:* cloves slide out as soft paste.
8. **Use or store.** Mash into butter, vinaigrette, mashed potatoes, soup, or spread on toast. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* if garlic is blackened and acrid, the bitterness cannot be removed. → *Expect:* sweet paste stored in a clean container if not used right away.

## Decision points

- Garlic browning too fast → lower oven to 300 °F and keep it covered.
- Need individual cloves → peel cloves, oil them in a small covered ramekin, and check after 25 minutes.
- Want caramelized edges → uncover for the final 5 minutes only after cloves are soft.
- Storing with oil → refrigerate promptly and use within a few days.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dry cloves:** not enough oil or loose wrap; mash with a little olive oil and cover tighter next time.
- **F2 Still sharp:** under-roasted; rewrap and bake 10 to 15 minutes more.
- **F3 Burnt tips:** oven too hot or exposed cloves; trim bitter tips and use only the soft interior.
- **F4 Hard to squeeze:** top was not cut deeply enough; separate cloves and remove skins by hand.

## Verification

The cloves are golden, spreadable, sweet-smelling, and squeeze from the skins with light pressure.

## Variations

- `herb`: tuck in thyme or rosemary before wrapping.
- `batch`: roast several heads at once and freeze mashed portions.
- `quick-cloves`: roast peeled cloves covered in oil at 325 °F until soft.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use oven mitts, cool before handling, and refrigerate garlic stored in oil rather than leaving it at room temperature.
