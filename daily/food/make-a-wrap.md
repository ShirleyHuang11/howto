## Goal

Make a tidy wrap that rolls tightly, holds its filling, and stays firm enough to eat by hand.

## Preconditions

- A large flour tortilla or flexible flatbread is available.
- Fillings are cooked, drained, and cooled enough to handle.
- A clean board, knife, and napkin or parchment are ready.

## Steps

1. **Warm the tortilla.** Heat it in a dry pan for 10 to 20 seconds per side or microwave it under a damp towel for 10 seconds. → *Expect:* the tortilla bends without cracking.
2. **Place it on a dry board.** Set the warm tortilla flat with the best-looking side facing down. → *Expect:* the side that will show outside is clean and unbroken.
3. **Make a sauce barrier.** Spread sauce in a thin stripe through the center, leaving 5 cm bare at the edges and avoiding watery puddles. → *Expect:* the sauce is controlled and not soaking the tortilla.
4. **Layer dry ingredients first.** Put lettuce, rice, cheese, or other dry pieces over the sauce before wet fillings. → *Expect:* wet ingredients are buffered from the tortilla.
5. **Add the main filling.** Keep the mound in a narrow horizontal band below center and use less than you think. → *Expect:* the tortilla still has clear side flaps and a top flap.
6. **Choose the fold.** [BRANCH: closed-end wrap | open-end wrap] fold both sides in for a sealed wrap, or fold only one side for a deli-style open top. → *Expect:* the filling is contained before rolling.
7. **Fold and tuck.** Pull the bottom edge up over the filling, tuck it back under the mound with your fingertips, and keep tension as you roll. → *Expect:* the first turn is snug and the filling is compressed, not squeezed out.
8. **Finish the roll.** Continue rolling away from you until the bare top edge seals against the outside. → *Expect:* the wrap forms a firm cylinder.
9. **Set seam side down.** Rest the wrap on its seam for 1 minute, or toast that side briefly in a dry pan. → *Expect:* the seam stays closed when released.
10. **Cut cleanly if needed.** Use a sharp knife and gentle sawing motion through the middle. → *Expect:* the halves show compact layers without filling falling out.

## Decision points

- Tortilla cracks while folding → warm it longer or switch to a fresher tortilla.
- Filling is juicy → drain it first and use rice, greens, or cheese as the layer touching sauce.
- Wrap is for later → keep tomatoes, pickles, and thin sauces separate until serving.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overfilled wrap:** detect bulging sides and splitting tortilla, recover by removing one third of the filling and rolling again.
- **F2 Soggy bottom:** detect wet translucent spots, recover by blotting wet fillings and moving sauce away from the bare edges.
- **F3 Loose roll:** detect a wrap that uncoils when set down, recover by reopening and repeating the fold-and-tuck roll with more tension.
- **F4 Filling escapes at ends:** detect pieces falling out while eating, recover by using the closed-end fold next time.

## Verification

The wrap rests seam side down and can be lifted with one hand without unrolling or dripping.

## Variations

- `grilled-wrap`: toast seam side down first, then rotate until the outside has light brown spots.
- `small-tortilla`: make a taco fold instead of forcing a full closed wrap.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep perishable fillings cold if packing the wrap, and avoid leaving meat, dairy, or egg fillings at room temperature for more than 2 hours.
