## Goal

Cook butter until its milk solids turn nutty brown, then stop the carryover heat before the solids burn.

## Preconditions

- Butter is cut into even pieces.
- A light-colored pan is available so the solids are visible.
- A heatproof spatula or whisk is ready.
- A cool bowl is nearby if the butter must stop cooking fast.

## Steps

1. **Melt evenly.** Put butter in the light pan over medium heat → *Expect:* butter melts and pale solids are visible
2. **Cook through foam.** Stir while the butter foams and crackles → *Expect:* sound is wet and lively as water cooks off
3. **Watch the bottom.** Scrape constantly where milk solids settle → *Expect:* specks move from pale to tan
4. **Lower at nutty.** When the smell turns nutty, lower heat or lift the pan → *Expect:* color keeps deepening from carryover
5. **Stop at brown.** Remove from heat at chestnut brown. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* black milk solids cannot be fixed, so stop slightly early → *Expect:* specks are brown, not black
6. **Pour out.** Scrape butter and solids into a cool bowl unless using immediately → *Expect:* color stops darkening
7. **Use with solids.** Spoon or measure the butter with the brown bits included → *Expect:* flavor is toasted and nutty

## Decision points

- Foam hides color -> stir and check the solids under the foam.
- Dark pan -> use aroma and spoon checks, then switch pans next time.
- Baking recipe -> chill brown butter if the recipe needs solid butter.
- Salted butter -> taste before adding more salt.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned butter:** detect black specks and acrid smoke -> discard because bitterness dominates.
- **F2 Under-browned:** detect pale solids and plain butter smell -> return to medium-low heat.
- **F3 Carryover burn:** detect darkening after heat is off -> pour into a cool bowl faster.
- **F4 Separated sauce:** detect oily pooling after adding liquid -> whisk in a teaspoon of water.

## Verification

The butter smells nutty, contains evenly browned milk solids, and has no black specks or harsh smoke smell.

## Variations

- Sage: add leaves after foaming slows and remove when crisp.
- Pasta: loosen with pasta water so the butter coats instead of greasing.
- Baking: weigh after browning or replace evaporated water if precision matters.
- Large batch: use a wider pan and moderate heat for even browning.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot fat and splatter as water boils out of butter.
Use a pan with headroom and pour into a heatproof bowl away from your hand.
