## Goal

A mango is cut away from its flat pit into clean pieces with minimal waste and safe knife control.

## Preconditions

- A ripe mango, cutting board, sharp knife, spoon, and optional peeler.
- The mango is washed and the board is stable.
- Hands are dry enough to grip the slippery fruit.

## Steps

1. **Find the pit direction.** Stand the mango stem-end up and notice the wider, flatter sides. → *Expect:* the flat pit runs parallel to the broad cheeks.
2. **Slice off the first cheek.** Cut about 1/4 inch from center along one broad side, following the pit. → *Expect:* a thick oval cheek comes away with little hard white pit.
3. **Slice off the second cheek.** Repeat on the other broad side. → *Expect:* two cheeks and a pit strip remain.
4. **Trim the pit strip.** Cut narrow side pieces from around the pit. → *Expect:* most usable flesh is removed from the hard center.
5. **Choose a cutting method.** [BRANCH: hedgehog scoring for cubes | peel-the-strip method for slices] → *Expect:* the method matches how ripe and soft the mango is.
6. **Score cheeks for cubes.** Cut a grid through the flesh but not the skin, then turn the cheek inside out. → *Expect:* cubes stand up like a hedgehog pattern.
7. **Remove the pieces.** Slice cubes off the skin, or peel a strip and slice flesh directly from it. → *Expect:* edible pieces drop cleanly onto the board.
8. **Clean up sticky surfaces.** Wipe the board and knife before they get slick. → *Expect:* knife handle and board are no longer slippery.

## Decision points

- Knife hits hard pit → angle outward slightly and follow the curve.
- Mango is very soft → use a spoon to scoop flesh from the skin instead of neat cubes.
- Mango is firm → peel first, then cut strips from the pit with firmer control.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pit in the cheek:** detect a hard white patch, recover by shaving around it with short strokes.
- **F2 Cut through skin:** detect cubes separating too early, recover by scooping with a spoon.
- **F3 Slippery grip:** detect fruit sliding under the hand, recover by drying hands and placing a towel under the board.
- **F4 Wasted side flesh:** detect orange flesh on the pit strip, recover by trimming narrow strips lengthwise.

## Verification

The pit is mostly bare, the edible mango is in bite-size pieces or slices, and no large hard pit fragments remain.

## Variations

- Ataulfo mango: smaller and flatter, so cut closer to the center.
- Green mango: peel first and cut firmer matchsticks for salads.
- Serving in skin: leave hedgehog cheeks intact and eat with a spoon.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from a knife on slippery fruit. Keep fingers curled, dry the handle often, and never cut toward your palm.
