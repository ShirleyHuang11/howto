## Goal

Shrimp are thawed, cleaned, and cooked quickly until juicy, opaque, and curled into a loose C shape.

## Preconditions

- Raw shrimp, fresh or frozen, are available.
- A colander, paper towels, knife or deveining tool, skillet, oil, salt, and tongs are available.
- The cook can wash hands and clean surfaces after handling raw seafood.

## Steps

1. **Thaw safely.** [BRANCH: overnight in refrigerator | sealed bag under cold running water for 10 to 20 minutes] → *Expect:* shrimp bend easily and have no icy core.
2. **Peel if needed.** Remove shells, leaving tails on only if wanted for serving. → *Expect:* flesh is exposed and shells are set aside for disposal or stock.
3. **Devein.** Cut a shallow line along the back and lift out the dark vein with a knife tip. → *Expect:* the back is clean or only a harmless clear line remains.
4. **Dry thoroughly.** Pat shrimp on both sides with paper towels. → *Expect:* surfaces feel tacky, not wet.
5. **Heat the pan.** Warm a skillet over medium-high heat, then add a thin film of oil. → *Expect:* oil shimmers and a shrimp sizzles immediately on contact.
6. **Cook briefly.** Add shrimp in one layer, salt lightly, and cook 1 to 2 minutes per side. → *Expect:* the first side turns pink and opaque around the edges.
7. **Stop at C shape.** Flip once and remove shrimp when curled like a C, not a tight O. → *Expect:* centers are opaque and the curve is relaxed.
8. **Rest off heat.** Move shrimp to a plate and season with lemon, butter, herbs, or sauce. → *Expect:* carryover heat finishes them without a rubbery texture.

## Decision points

- Shrimp smell strongly of ammonia → discard them.
- Shrimp are very small → cook closer to 1 minute total.
- Shrimp are jumbo → reduce heat slightly and cook until the thickest part turns opaque.
- Pan is crowded → cook in batches so they sear instead of steam.
- Sauce is sugary → add it after shrimp are nearly done to avoid scorching.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overcooked O shape:** detect tight rings and rubbery bite; recover by chopping into salad or tacos with sauce.
- **F2 Watery pan:** detect simmering liquid and no browning; recover by removing shrimp, drying pan, reheating, and cooking in batches.
- **F3 Gritty vein:** detect dark line or sandy bite; recover by splitting and rinsing the back before cooking remaining shrimp.
- **F4 Raw center:** detect translucent gray at the thickest point; recover by returning to hot pan for 30 seconds.
- **F5 Seafood contamination:** detect raw juices on tools; recover by washing tools, board, hands, and counter with hot soapy water.

## Verification

Every shrimp is opaque through the thickest part and curled into a C shape, with no translucent raw gray areas.

## Variations

- Boiled shrimp: simmer salted water, add shrimp, and pull them as soon as they turn opaque.
- Garlic butter: cook garlic only after lowering heat so it does not burn.
- Frozen cooked shrimp: thaw and warm briefly, since they are already cooked.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from raw seafood contamination and shellfish allergy. Keep raw shrimp cold until cooking and serve promptly.
