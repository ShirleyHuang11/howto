## Goal

A drinkable, balanced smoothie from whatever fruit is on hand, blended in the right order, with the blender cleaned in the thirty seconds when cleaning is still easy.

## Preconditions

- A blender with a seated, intact blade and a lid that fits.
- A rough formula rather than a recipe: liquid, a base fruit, something creamy or protein-rich, and optionally greens or flavor accents. Frozen fruit makes the texture; all-fresh fruit plus ice is the fallback.

## Steps

1. **Load in the blending order: liquid first, then soft, then frozen on top.** A cup or so of liquid (milk, plant milk, juice, water) at the blades, then yogurt or banana, then greens, then frozen fruit and ice last. Liquid-first is what lets the vortex form instead of the blades spinning in a jam. → *Expect:* jar loaded bottom-light to top-heavy, filled no more than two-thirds.
2. **Lid on, hand on the lid, start low.** Low speed until a vortex forms and pulls the top layers down, then ramp to high. → *Expect:* a visible vortex; contents circulating rather than a frozen island spinning above the blades.
3. **Blend on high for 30 to 60 seconds until visually smooth.** Green flecks disappearing is the sign the greens are actually blended rather than confetti. → *Expect:* uniform color and texture, no chunks knocking.
4. **Check the consistency and adjust once.** Too thick: splash more liquid, pulse. Too thin: a handful more frozen fruit or ice, pulse. → *Expect:* pourable but spoon-coating, or whatever your target is, reached in one adjustment.
5. **Taste and balance.** Too tart: half a banana, a date, or a small drizzle of honey. Too sweet or flat: a squeeze of citrus. → *Expect:* balanced flavor before it leaves the jar.
6. **Pour and rinse-blend immediately.** Serve, then put warm water and a drop of dish soap in the jar, run it 20 seconds, rinse. ⚠️ Blade jars are the cut-risk moment of the kitchen sink; the self-clean cycle exists so hands never chase residue around the blade. → *Expect:* clean jar drying, hands never inside with the blade.

## Decision points

- Meal vs snack: a meal smoothie carries protein (yogurt, protein powder, nut butter) and fat, or hunger returns in an hour; a snack smoothie can be fruit and liquid.
- Greens for skeptics: spinach disappears behind banana and berries; kale does not disappear behind anything.
- Weak blender: smaller frozen pieces, more liquid, longer low-speed phase, and patience beat burning the motor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Blades spinning, nothing moving (the air pocket):** stop, shake the jar or stir with the tamper (never a spoon while running), add a splash of liquid, restart low. The liquid-first order prevents most of this.
- **F2 Leak at the base or lid:** blade assembly or lid was cross-threaded; stop, wipe, re-seat properly. A leaking blender at speed redecorates kitchens.
- **F3 Motor hot and straining:** stop and rest it; too full, too frozen, or too dry. Halve the load and add liquid.
- **F4 Gray-brown sludge from overambitious mixing:** red fruits plus greens plus everything is the classic. It tastes fine; drink it in an opaque cup, and next time keep the palette to two or three stars.

## Verification

The vortex formed, the result is smooth with no flecks or chunks, consistency and taste were adjusted once each, and the jar was self-cleaned before the smoothie was finished.

## Variations

- Smoothie bowls: less liquid, tamper work, toppings after; the same order rules at thicker settings.
- Batch prep: freezer bags of pre-portioned fruit turn every morning into liquid-plus-one-bag (`daily/food/portion-and-freeze-meals` applied to breakfast).

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk in two places: fingers never in the jar with the blade (the self-clean cycle is the substitute), and the lid held during the first seconds of every blend. The rest is fruit.
