## Goal

Build a glossy sauce in the searing pan with fond, aromatics, deglazing liquid, reduction, and cold butter.

## Preconditions

- Seared meat, fish, tofu, or mushrooms is ready and smells fresh.
- Stock, wine, lemon, vinegar, herbs, and cold butter are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Assess fond.** Remove the cooked item and pour off excess fat, leaving 1 to 2 teaspoons → *Expect:* brown fond remains without deep grease
2. **Soften aromatics.** Add shallot, garlic, or herbs over medium-low heat → *Expect:* aromatics smell sweet, not scorched
3. **Deglaze.** Add wine, stock, or water and scrape the pan → *Expect:* fond dissolves into the liquid
4. **Reduce.** Simmer until slightly syrupy → *Expect:* spoon leaves a short trail
5. **Add juices.** Pour in resting juices from the plate → *Expect:* sauce tastes more like the main food
6. **Mount butter.** Turn heat low or off and whisk in cold butter. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* boiling after butter can break the sauce → *Expect:* sauce turns glossy
7. **Season and serve.** Add salt or acid, then spoon over food → *Expect:* sauce coats rather than floods

## Decision points

- Black fond -> discard and start in a clean pan.
- Flat flavor -> add lemon or vinegar before more salt.
- No fond -> use stock, mustard, or browned butter for body.
- Butter separates -> remove from heat and whisk in cold water.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scorched aromatics:** detect dark garlic -> wipe out before deglazing.
- **F2 Thin sauce:** detect watery texture -> reduce longer before butter.
- **F3 Broken sauce:** detect grease slick -> whisk in cold water off heat.
- **F4 Raw alcohol:** detect sharp wine smell -> simmer longer before mounting.

## Verification

sauce is glossy and tastes like the seared food

## Variations

- Mustard: whisk Dijon in before butter.
- Cream: add after deglazing and reduce gently.
- Herb: add tender herbs at the end.
- No alcohol: use stock plus a splash of vinegar.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot fat, steam, and alcohol vapor.
Turn gas off while adding alcohol and avoid tasting with utensils that touched raw meat.
