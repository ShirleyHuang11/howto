## Goal

Egg whites whipped to glossy stiff peaks that stand upright without being dry or broken.

## Preconditions

- Fresh eggs or separated egg whites, cream of tartar or lemon juice, and sugar if the recipe calls for it.
- Clean dry metal or glass bowl and whisk, hand mixer, or stand mixer.
- No yolk, oil, butter, or dish soap residue in the bowl.

## Steps

1. **Clean the tools.** Wipe bowl and whisk with vinegar or lemon, then dry well. → *Expect:* surfaces feel squeaky clean with no grease.
2. **Separate carefully.** Crack eggs one at a time, keeping yolk out of the whites. → *Expect:* whites are clear and free of yellow streaks.
3. **Warm slightly.** Let whites sit 20 to 30 minutes if time allows. → *Expect:* whites are closer to room temperature and whip faster.
4. **Start foaming.** Beat on low to medium until bubbles cover the surface. → *Expect:* whites look loose and foamy, not yet white.
5. **Add stabilizer.** Add 1/8 tsp cream of tartar per 2 to 3 whites, or a few drops lemon juice. → *Expect:* foam becomes finer and more stable.
6. **Build to soft peaks.** Increase to medium-high until lifted whisk forms peaks that fold over. → *Expect:* whites are white, glossy, and still flexible.
7. **Finish to stiff peaks.** Beat in short bursts, checking often. → *Expect:* peaks stand upright with a slight curl and the bowl can tilt without sliding.
8. **Stop before dry.** Use immediately or fold into the next mixture. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* over-whipped whites turn grainy and leak liquid; they cannot return to glossy stiff peaks. → *Expect:* foam is smooth, shiny, and ready to fold or bake.

## Decision points

- Any yolk breaks into the white → remove with shell immediately, or start over if it spreads.
- Adding sugar → add gradually at soft peaks, not at the beginning.
- Whipping by hand → use a large balloon whisk and expect more time.
- Copper bowl → skip cream of tartar because copper already stabilizes whites.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Whites will not foam:** grease or yolk is present; start over with a clean bowl.
- **F2 Grainy curds:** over-whipped; for some batters, whisk in one fresh white briefly to loosen, then use immediately.
- **F3 Weeping liquid:** foam sat too long or over-whipped; rewhip briefly only if still glossy, otherwise restart.
- **F4 Collapsed after folding:** folded too roughly; use wide gentle strokes and stop when streaks just disappear.

## Verification

The whites are glossy and hold upright peaks when the whisk is lifted, with no watery liquid pooling in the bowl.

## Variations

- `meringue`: add sugar slowly at soft peaks and beat until glossy and sugar dissolves.
- `souffle`: stop at firm but not dry peaks so folding stays easy.
- `carton-whites`: check that the carton says suitable for whipping.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use clean eggs, avoid raw egg whites for people at higher foodborne-illness risk unless the recipe will cook them fully.
