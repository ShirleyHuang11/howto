## Goal

Frozen dumplings are cooked through without thawing, either tender from boiling or crisp-bottomed by the potsticker steam-fry method.

## Preconditions

- Frozen dumplings still frozen solid.
- Pot of water for boiling, or a nonstick or well-seasoned skillet with a lid for potstickers.
- Oil, water, tongs or spatula, and a plate.

## Steps

1. **Choose the cooking style.** [BRANCH: boil for soft dumplings | steam-fry for potstickers] pick boiling for soups and delicate wrappers, or steam-fry for crisp bottoms. → *Expect:* pan or pot choice matches the texture you want.
2. **Keep dumplings frozen.** Take out only the amount you will cook and return the rest to the freezer. → *Expect:* dumplings are firm and separate, not thawed or sticky.
3. **Boil option: start rolling water.** Fill a pot with enough water for dumplings to move freely and bring it to a boil. → *Expect:* large bubbles break the surface continuously.
4. **Boil option: add and stir.** Slide dumplings in, stir gently once, and simmer 6 to 10 minutes depending on size. → *Expect:* dumplings float, wrappers turn slightly translucent, and filling feels firm.
5. **Potsticker option: oil the cold skillet.** Add 1 tablespoon neutral oil, arrange dumplings flat-side down with space between them, then heat over medium. → *Expect:* bottoms sizzle within 2 minutes.
6. **Potsticker option: steam then crisp.** Add 1/3 cup water, cover immediately, cook 6 to 8 minutes, uncover, then cook until water evaporates and bottoms brown. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* hot oil and water splatter when combined, so add water from the side and cover right away. → *Expect:* wrappers are tender and bottoms release golden and crisp.
7. **Serve with sauce.** Mix soy sauce with vinegar and chili oil or sesame oil, then plate dumplings while hot. → *Expect:* dumplings hold shape and sauce is ready before they cool.

## Decision points

- Dumplings are homemade and larger than store-bought → add 2 to 4 minutes and verify the center is hot.
- Wrapper breaks in boiling water → lower the heat to a gentler simmer and stop stirring.
- Potstickers stick hard → let them brown another minute, then loosen with a thin spatula.
- Unsure about doneness → cut one open; filling should be steaming and opaque all the way through.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mushy wrappers:** detect swollen, tearing skins; recover by draining immediately and reducing cook time next batch.
- **F2 Raw center:** detect pink meat or cold filling; recover by returning dumplings to simmering water or covered skillet for 2 to 3 minutes.
- **F3 Burnt bottoms:** detect black patches or bitter smell; recover by moving edible dumplings to a plate and lowering heat for the next batch.
- **F4 Clumped dumplings:** detect frozen pieces stuck together; recover by cooking as a cluster and separating gently after wrappers soften.

## Verification

At least one dumpling cut open has a steaming-hot, fully opaque filling, and the batch has either tender boiled wrappers or crisp browned potsticker bottoms.

## Variations

- Soup dumplings: boil gently and lift with a slotted spoon so fragile wrappers do not burst.
- Air fryer: use only if the package lists it, since many dumplings dry out before the filling heats.
- Sauce shortcut: soy sauce plus rice vinegar in equal parts works for most frozen dumplings.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot water, steam, and oil splatter. Cook meat-filled dumplings fully and keep raw-filling utensils away from ready food.
