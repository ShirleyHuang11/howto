## Goal

Bring a curdled or split sauce back to a smooth, glossy texture when it is still safe and worth saving.

## Preconditions

- The sauce has not burned and does not smell spoiled.
- A whisk, bowl, spoon, and optional blender or immersion blender are available.
- Warm water, stock, cream, butter, mustard, egg yolk, or another suitable emulsifier is available.

## Steps

1. **Remove the sauce from heat.** Take the pan off the burner and stop simmering or boiling. → *Expect:* bubbling slows within a few seconds.
2. **Identify the split.** [BRANCH: oily separation | grainy dairy or egg curdling] Look for fat pooling on top, or small protein curds in a thin liquid. → *Expect:* you know which texture problem you are fixing.
3. **Start a rescue bowl.** Put one tablespoon of warm water, stock, cream, or another compatible warm liquid in a clean bowl. → *Expect:* the bowl has a small smooth base.
4. **Whisk in sauce gradually.** Add the broken sauce one spoonful at a time while whisking constantly. → *Expect:* the rescue bowl turns smooth before each new spoonful.
5. **Use an emulsifier if needed.** Whisk in a small amount of mustard, egg yolk, butter, cream, or starch slurry that fits the recipe. → *Expect:* loose fat droplets pull back into the sauce.
6. **Blend for stubborn separation.** Pour the sauce into a blender or use an immersion blender in a tall container, starting low and venting steam safely. → *Expect:* the sauce becomes more uniform and glossy.
7. **Return gentle heat only if needed.** Warm the repaired sauce over low heat, stirring often and avoiding a boil. → *Expect:* the sauce is hot enough to serve but not bubbling hard.
8. **Fix thickness and seasoning.** Add warm liquid if too thick, or reduce gently if too thin, then taste. → *Expect:* texture and seasoning are balanced.
9. **Note why it split.** Record whether the cause was high heat, adding fat too fast, cold ingredients, too much acid, or over-reduction. → *Expect:* the next batch has a prevention cue.

## Decision points

- Sauce smells burned or spoiled → discard it instead of repairing.
- Egg or dairy sauce curdled from high heat → use the rescue bowl and low heat, not direct boiling.
- Butter or oil sauce split → add liquid or emulsifier first, then whisk in the broken sauce slowly.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fat keeps pooling:** detect shiny oil on top after whisking → start again with fresh warm liquid and add the sauce more slowly.
- **F2 Grainy texture remains:** detect visible curds after blending → strain through a fine sieve and adjust seasoning.
- **F3 Sauce turns thin:** detect watery texture after rescue liquid → reduce gently or add a compatible thickener.
- **F4 Blender overflow:** detect container filled more than halfway with hot sauce → blend in smaller batches with the lid vented.

## Verification

The sauce coats a spoon evenly, shows no large oil pools or curd clumps, and stays smooth for one minute off heat.

## Variations

- Hollandaise or beurre blanc: start with warm water and whisk in the split sauce slowly.
- Cheese sauce: use low heat, a splash of milk, and avoid boiling after cheese is added.
- Pan sauce: whisk in cold butter off heat to restore shine.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Hot sauce can burn skin, and sealed blending of hot liquid can force the lid off, so vent and blend small amounts.
