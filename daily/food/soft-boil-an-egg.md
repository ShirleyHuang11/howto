## Goal

An egg with set whites and a warm runny or jammy yolk, ready to peel, spoon, or serve with toast.

## Preconditions

- Eggs, saucepan, water, slotted spoon, timer, and bowl of ice water.
- Eggs are not cracked before cooking.
- Heat source can maintain a gentle boil.

## Steps

1. **Boil the water first.** Fill the pan deep enough to cover eggs and bring to a steady boil. → *Expect:* bubbles rise actively across the pan.
2. **Lower the eggs.** Use a slotted spoon to place eggs gently into the water. → *Expect:* shells stay intact on the bottom.
3. **Start the timer.** Cook 6 minutes for runny yolk or 7 minutes for jammy yolk. → *Expect:* water returns to a gentle boil.
4. **Control the boil.** Reduce heat slightly if eggs bounce hard. → *Expect:* eggs move gently without cracking.
5. **Ice bath immediately.** Transfer eggs to ice water as soon as the timer ends. → *Expect:* cooking stops and shells cool.
6. **Rest briefly.** Chill 2 to 3 minutes for spooning, or 5 minutes for peeling. → *Expect:* egg is cool enough to handle.
7. **Open the egg.** [BRANCH: spoon | peel] Tap off the top and spoon from shell, or crack all over and peel under water. → *Expect:* white is set and yolk remains soft.
8. **Verify doneness.** Cut one egg open if timing a new stove or egg size. → *Expect:* yolk is dippy or jammy as intended.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* extra cooking firms the yolk; use a timer and ice bath on time.

## Decision points

- Refrigerator-cold large egg → 6 to 7 minutes works for most kitchens.
- Extra-large egg → add about 30 seconds.
- Room-temperature egg → check at the lower end because it cooks faster.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shell cracks in water:** egg hit the pan or boiled too hard → lower gently and reduce to a gentler boil next time.
- **F2 Whites are runny:** time was short → simmer another egg 30 to 60 seconds longer.
- **F3 Yolk is hard:** egg cooked too long or skipped ice bath → reduce time and chill immediately.
- **F4 Peeling tears white:** egg is too fresh or warm → peel under water after a longer ice bath.

## Verification

The white is fully set and the yolk is runny at 6 minutes or jammy at 7 minutes, confirmed by opening one egg.

## Variations

- Steamed eggs: steam covered for similar timing and often easier peeling.
- Ramen egg: cook 6.5 to 7 minutes, peel, then marinate chilled.
- Toast soldiers: serve spooned eggs in cups with strips of toast.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from boiling water and possible undercooked egg. Use a spoon to lower eggs, avoid splashes, and choose fully cooked eggs for vulnerable eaters.
