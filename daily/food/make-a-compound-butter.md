## Goal

Seasoned butter blended with herbs, garlic, citrus zest, or spices, chilled into a sliceable log.

## Preconditions

- Unsalted or lightly salted butter, flavorings, salt, and parchment or wax paper.
- Bowl, fork or spatula, knife, and refrigerator.
- Butter softened to cool room temperature.

## Steps

1. **Soften the butter.** Leave butter out until it dents easily but is not greasy. → *Expect:* fork presses through with light resistance.
2. **Prepare flavorings finely.** Mince herbs, grate garlic, zest citrus, or measure spices. → *Expect:* pieces are small enough to distribute evenly.
3. **Mash butter smooth.** Work the butter in a bowl before adding flavorings. → *Expect:* texture is pliable and lump-free.
4. **Fold in seasonings.** For 1 stick butter, add 1 to 2 tbsp herbs, 1 small grated garlic clove, zest, and salt to taste. → *Expect:* color and aromatics are evenly streaked through.
5. **Taste and adjust.** Spread a little on bread or a warm spoon to check salt and intensity. → *Expect:* flavor is slightly stronger than plain butter because it will be used sparingly.
6. **Shape into a log.** Spoon onto parchment, roll tightly, and twist the ends. → *Expect:* butter forms an even cylinder without large air pockets.
7. **Chill until firm.** Refrigerate 1 hour or freeze 20 minutes for quick slicing. → *Expect:* log holds its shape when unwrapped.
8. **Slice and use.** Cut coins for steak, vegetables, toast, pasta, or rice. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* raw garlic gets stronger as it sits, so do not overload it for long storage. → *Expect:* slices melt evenly over hot food.

## Decision points

- Serving immediately → a small bowl is fine; rolling is for storage and neat portions.
- Using raw garlic → use less than you think, or roast it first for sweetness.
- Sweet butter → use honey, cinnamon, citrus zest, or vanilla instead of herbs.
- Freezing → wrap the parchment log in a freezer bag and label the flavor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Butter melted:** room was too warm; chill 10 minutes, then reshape.
- **F2 Flavor too harsh:** raw garlic or zest is intense; blend in more plain butter.
- **F3 Uneven chunks:** herbs were too coarse; let butter soften and remix with finer cuts.
- **F4 Too salty:** add unsalted butter to dilute and retaste.

## Verification

The compound butter is evenly seasoned, firm enough to slice, and melts into food without gritty pockets of seasoning.

## Variations

- `garlic-herb`: parsley, chives, grated garlic, and black pepper.
- `lemon-dill`: lemon zest, dill, and a small squeeze of lemon for fish or potatoes.
- `honey-cinnamon`: honey, cinnamon, and a pinch of salt for toast.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep butter refrigerated, use clean utensils, and do not leave garlic butter at room temperature for extended periods.
