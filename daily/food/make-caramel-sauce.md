## Goal

Sugar is cooked to amber caramel, loosened with warm cream, finished with butter and salt, and cooled into a pourable sauce.

## Preconditions

- Granulated sugar, water if using wet caramel, warm heavy cream, butter, salt, and a heavy light-colored saucepan.
- Long-handled whisk or heatproof spatula, oven mitt, and a clear counter space.
- No children, pets, or distractions near the stove.

## Steps

1. **Warm the cream.** Heat 240 ml cream until steaming, then keep nearby off the burner. → *Expect:* cream is warm enough to reduce seizing.
2. **Start the sugar.** [BRANCH: wet: combine 200 g sugar with 60 ml water | dry: sprinkle 200 g sugar evenly in the pan] → *Expect:* sugar begins melting evenly over medium heat.
3. **Dissolve without crystals.** For wet caramel, swirl rather than stir once boiling; brush pan sides with water if crystals appear. → *Expect:* syrup turns clear before coloring.
4. **Cook to amber.** Watch closely as color shifts from pale gold to honey to amber. → *Expect:* caramel smells nutty, not burnt, and is deep amber.
5. **Stop the heat.** Remove pan from heat before adding dairy. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* caramel above 160 °C causes severe burns and cannot be wiped off safely while hot. → *Expect:* pan is off the burner and tilted away from you.
6. **Add warm cream slowly.** Pour in a small stream while whisking; it will bubble violently and steam. → *Expect:* bubbling rises then settles without overflowing.
7. **Finish with butter and salt.** Whisk in 30-45 g butter and 1/2 tsp salt until glossy. → *Expect:* sauce is smooth and slightly thinner than final texture.
8. **Cool safely.** Pour into a heatproof jar and let cool uncovered until warm, then lid and refrigerate. → *Expect:* sauce thickens as it cools.
9. **Rewarm to serve.** Warm gently in short bursts or a water bath if too thick. → *Expect:* sauce pours from a spoon in a ribbon.

## Decision points

- Want less bitterness → stop at medium amber; carryover heat darkens it.
- Sugar crystallizes before coloring → add a splash of water and dissolve again, or restart if grainy throughout.
- Sauce is for drizzling cold desserts → cook slightly lighter because chilling makes bitter notes stronger.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt caramel:** detect by smoke, black color, or harsh smell → discard once cool; burnt sugar cannot be fixed.
- **F2 Seized lumps:** detect by hard sugar clumps after cream → return to low heat and stir until dissolved.
- **F3 Sauce too thin:** detect after full cooling → simmer briefly to reduce, stirring constantly.
- **F4 Sauce too thick:** detect by spoon standing up → whisk in warm cream 1 tbsp at a time.

## Verification

The cooled sauce is glossy, smooth, amber, lightly salted, and pours from a spoon without grainy crystals.

## Variations

- `salted-caramel`: increase salt gradually and taste after cooling, when sweetness is less aggressive.
- `bourbon`: add 1 tbsp off heat after cream settles.
- `dairy-free`: use full-fat coconut cream and plant butter, expecting coconut flavor.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from hot sugar and steam. Do not taste until cooled, do not catch spills with your hand, and soak hardened caramel in hot water after the pan cools.
