## Goal

A car stocked with snacks that survive hours in a warm cabin, can be eaten one-handed without showering the seats in crumbs, and leave no trash rolling around the footwells at the end of the trip.

## Preconditions

- Trip length and headcount known (plan roughly one snack event per 2 hours of driving per person).
- A small cooler or insulated bag with ice packs, frozen the night before.
- One rigid bin or box that fits within arm's reach of the front passenger.
- A trash receptacle: a plastic cereal container or lidded tub lined with a grocery bag works far better than a loose bag.

## Steps

1. **Build the snack list against the no-melt rule.** Cut anything with a chocolate coating, gummy candy, and hard cheese unless it will live in the cooler. A parked car in the sun exceeds 45 °C in under an hour. → *Expect:* every item on the list is either cabin-stable (pretzels, jerky, nuts, dried fruit, crackers) or assigned to the cooler.
2. **Apply the no-crumb rule to anything the driver will eat.** Cut flaky pastries, powdered donuts, and open-bag chips from the driver's share; favor grapes, pretzel rods, string cheese, and granola bars that snap clean. → *Expect:* the driver's snacks can each be eaten with one hand and no lap napkin.
3. **Portion into individual containers or zip bags per person.** Shared bags get passed, spilled, and fought over; portions do not. → *Expect:* one labeled portion set per passenger, no full-size open bags in the cabin.
4. **Pack the cooler zone.** Ice packs on the bottom, perishables (cheese, yogurt, cut fruit, sandwiches) in the middle, one ice pack on top; drinks go in a second cooler or the door pockets so snack-cooler openings stay rare. → *Expect:* cooler closes fully; perishables are in contact with a cold pack, not floating above it.
5. **Pack the dry zone bin.** Cabin-stable snacks upright in the rigid bin, heaviest at the bottom, napkins and wipes tucked down one side. → *Expect:* nothing shifts when the bin is tilted 30 degrees.
6. **Stage the trash plan.** Lined rigid container in the front footwell or console area, wipes clipped nearby; announce the rule that wrappers go in immediately, not into door pockets. → *Expect:* every seat can reach either the trash container or a person who can.
7. **Load the car last, cooler out of the sun.** Cooler on the floor behind a front seat (shaded, and the floor stays coolest), dry bin within passenger reach, water bottles per seat. → *Expect:* driver can be handed any snack without anyone unbuckling.
8. **Empty the trash at every fuel stop.** Swap the liner bag and wipe hands before rolling out again. → *Expect:* trash container starts each leg empty.

## Decision points

- Trip crosses a hot afternoon with the car parked (attraction stops) → move all cooler items to a hard cooler with block ice, or plan to buy perishables fresh at each stop instead.
- Toddlers on board → drop whole grapes and nuts (choking risk); substitute quartered grapes prepped at home and puff-style snacks.
- No cooler available → run the trip entirely on cabin-stable items and buy cold drinks at stops.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Melted mess:** chocolate or cheese found liquefied in the dry bin → quarantine the item in a zip bag, wipe the bin, recheck the rest of the bin against the no-melt rule at the next stop.
- **F2 Warm cooler:** ice packs thawed and contents at cabin temperature for more than 2 hours → discard dairy, meat, and cut fruit; do not "risk it" with mayonnaise-based items.
- **F3 Crumb explosion:** open bag tipped on a seat → do not brush crumbs into the seat rail gap; use a stiff card to sweep them onto a napkin at the next stop, then demote that snack to stops-only.
- **F4 Sticky hands on the wheel:** driver ate something that needed a napkin → wipes are staged in the console for exactly this; pull the next snack from the no-crumb list only.
- **F5 Smell contamination:** banana peel or similar left in the closed car → the rigid lidded trash container is the fix; a loose bag lets odors out, the sealed tub does not.

## Verification

At the destination: no perishable item spent more than 2 hours above cooler temperature, the seats and footwells are free of crumbs and wrappers, the trash container holds all waste and closes, and nobody had to unbuckle to reach food.

## Variations

- Electric cooler plugged into the 12 V socket: no ice packs needed, but confirm the socket stays powered while parked or the cooler dies at every stop.
- Winter trips: melt risk disappears, but keep drinks from freezing in the trunk overnight.
- Motorcycle or bike variant: everything must pass the no-melt rule; there is no cooler zone.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The real hazards are choking-risk foods for small children and food poisoning from perishables held warm; both are handled by the toddler decision point and the 2-hour rule. Never let the driver dig for a snack: hand it over.
