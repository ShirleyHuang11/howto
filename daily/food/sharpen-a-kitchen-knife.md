## Goal

A dull kitchen knife is restored to a working edge with the tools you actually own, tested safely, and the truth absorbed that a sharp knife is the safe knife (`daily/food/cut-an-onion`'s first precondition, delivered).

## Preconditions

- The dull knife, cleaned and dried.
- One sharpening tool: a pull-through sharpener (easiest), a whetstone (best results, real learning curve), or a honing steel (which does not sharpen at all; it realigns an edge between sharpenings; knowing the difference is half this recipe).
- A stable board or counter with a damp towel underneath, and full attention. No distractions while metal meets abrasive.

## Steps

1. **Diagnose before sharpening.** The paper test: slice a held sheet of paper. Clean slice: the knife is fine, hone it and stop. Tears or slides off a tomato skin: proceed to actual sharpening. → *Expect:* an honest verdict; most "dull" knives that were recently sharpened just need honing (step 5).
2. **Pull-through sharpener path: anchor it, then draw the knife through with light, even pulls.** Coarse slot first (3 to 5 pulls, heel to tip, light downward pressure), then the fine slot the same way. → *Expect:* even resistance each pull; a faint gritty sound; no sawing back and forth.
3. **Whetstone path: soak or wet the stone per its type, then hold the blade at roughly 15 to 20 degrees and sweep.** Edge trailing, moderate pressure away and light pressure back, covering heel to tip in overlapping strokes; equal strokes per side; coarse side until a burr forms along the whole edge (a faint catch you can feel with a careful thumb across, never along, the edge), then the fine side to refine it. → *Expect:* a consistent angle held throughout; a burr raised on each side then polished off.
4. **Deburr and clean.** A few very light alternating strokes on the fine surface, then wash and dry the knife; abrasive dust does not belong in food. → *Expect:* clean blade, no metal-slurry residue.
5. **Hone as the finishing pass and as the between-times habit.** Steel held point-down on the board, blade at the same shallow angle, 4 to 6 light alternating strokes per side, heel to tip. → *Expect:* the edge aligned; this thirty-second habit, done weekly, delays the next real sharpening by months.
6. **Test the result the safe way.** Paper slice, or a tomato: a sharp knife bites the skin at zero pressure. ⚠️ Never test an edge with a thumb along the blade or by casual chopping at speed; the newly sharp knife is exactly when old dull-knife force habits cause cuts. → *Expect:* clean paper slice; the first real cutting session done deliberately slower than usual.

## Decision points

- Serrated knives, badly chipped edges, and expensive Japanese blades: send them to a professional service; pull-throughs damage serrations and hard steels, and a pro sharpening costs less than the knife.
- Tool choice honestly: the pull-through gets any household to acceptably sharp; the whetstone is worth learning if you cook daily; the steel alone is maintenance, not repair.
- Cadence: hone weekly, sharpen when the paper test fails (a few times a year for a home kitchen).

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Knife seems duller after the pull-through:** pressure too heavy or sawing in the slot chewed the edge. Light single-direction pulls only; finish on the fine slot.
- **F2 Whetstone angle wandering:** the classic beginner issue; a marker line on the bevel shows where you are actually grinding (marker gone where you touched). Slow down; consistency beats speed.
- **F3 Cut yourself testing or first-use:** `daily/self-care/treat-a-minor-cut`, and adopt the deliberate-first-session rule above; the knife did not get dangerous, the force calibration was stale.
- **F4 Edge sharp but chips appearing:** the edge was made too acute for the steel or the board is glass/stone. Resharpen at a slightly wider angle and cut on wood or plastic only.

## Verification

The paper test passes cleanly, the knife is washed of grit, the honing steel is stationed where the knife lives, and the first cutting session afterward was consciously slower with the claw grip intact.

## Variations

- `jp` single-bevel knives and their whetstone rituals are their own discipline with steeper stakes; the marker trick and pro services remain the safe entries.
- Electric sharpeners behave like guided pull-throughs: convenient, consistent, and slightly hungry for your blade's metal.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk at two moments: fingers versus the edge during burr checks and testing (across, never along), and the first post-sharpening session while muscle memory still expects dullness. A stable tool, a damp towel base, and unhurried strokes cover the rest.
