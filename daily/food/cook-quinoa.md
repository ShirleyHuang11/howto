## Goal

Quinoa is rinsed, cooked, rested, and fluffed into tender separate grains without bitterness or a soggy bottom.

## Preconditions

- Quinoa, water or broth, fine-mesh strainer, saucepan with lid, fork, and measuring cup.
- Clean sink for rinsing.
- Optional salt, oil, or aromatics.
- Burner that can hold a low simmer.
- Heat-safe surface for resting the covered pot.

## Steps

1. **Measure quinoa.** Use the serving amount needed, commonly 1 cup dry quinoa for about 3 cups cooked. → *Expect:* dry quinoa is measured before rinsing.
2. **Rinse away bitterness.** Put quinoa in a fine-mesh strainer and rinse under cool running water for 30-60 seconds while rubbing lightly. → *Expect:* runoff looks clearer and the grains smell less grassy.
3. **Drain thoroughly.** Shake the strainer and let excess water drip off. → *Expect:* quinoa is damp, not carrying a pool of rinse water.
4. **Toast if desired.** Warm drained quinoa in the dry saucepan for 1-2 minutes, stirring. → *Expect:* grains smell nutty and no longer hiss with moisture.
5. **Add liquid at the right ratio.** Use 1 3/4 to 2 cups liquid per 1 cup rinsed quinoa, plus a pinch of salt. → *Expect:* liquid level covers the grains evenly.
6. **Bring to a boil, then reduce.** Heat uncovered until bubbling, then cover and lower to a gentle simmer. → *Expect:* lid is on and only faint steam escapes.
7. **Cook without stirring.** Simmer for 12-15 minutes until most liquid is absorbed. → *Expect:* small steam holes appear and the pan sounds quieter.
8. **Rest off heat.** Turn off the burner and let the covered pot sit 5-10 minutes. → *Expect:* remaining steam finishes hydration without scorching.
9. **Check doneness.** Look for translucent grains with tiny white germ rings visible. → *Expect:* grains are tender with a slight pop, not chalky.
10. **Fork-fluff.** Gently lift and separate the grains with a fork, scraping the bottom lightly. → *Expect:* quinoa loosens into separate grains and steam escapes.

## Decision points

- Quinoa still tastes bitter → rinse longer next time and consider buying pre-rinsed quinoa from a fresher package.
- Using broth → reduce added salt until after cooking.
- Cooking red or black quinoa → expect a firmer bite and possibly 2-4 extra minutes.
- Want salad quinoa → use the lower liquid ratio and cool spread on a tray.
- Want porridge texture → use more liquid and stir near the end.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy quinoa:** too much liquid or no rest; uncover and cook on low 1-3 minutes, then rest again.
- **F2 Crunchy centers:** not enough liquid or time; add 2 Tbsp water, cover, and steam 5 minutes.
- **F3 Burned bottom:** heat was too high after covering; transfer unburned top grains without scraping the scorched layer.
- **F4 Clumped grains:** stirred during cooking or skipped fluffing; fork-fluff while warm and spread briefly to steam off.
- **F5 Bitter flavor:** saponins remained; mask lightly with acid and oil for this batch, rinse better next time.

## Verification

Quinoa is tender, has visible germ rings, tastes clean rather than bitter, and separates when lifted with a fork.

## Variations

- Rice cooker: use rinsed quinoa and the same liquid ratio, then rest 5 minutes after the cooker switches.
- Instant Pot: use 1 cup quinoa to 1 cup liquid, cook briefly under pressure, then natural release.
- Pilaf style: toast with aromatics before adding liquid.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot steam and boiling liquid. Open the lid away from your face and move the pot with dry handles or mitts.
