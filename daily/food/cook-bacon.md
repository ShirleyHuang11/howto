## Goal

Bacon is cooked to the target crispness with rendered fat managed safely and no grease sent down the drain.

## Preconditions

- Raw bacon, paper towels, tongs, and a plate.
- For stovetop: a cold heavy skillet large enough to hold strips mostly flat.
- For oven: a rimmed sheet tray, foil or parchment, and optional wire rack.
- A heat-safe jar, can, or lined trash container for cooled grease.
- A working exhaust fan or open window if smoke alarms are sensitive.

## Steps

1. **Choose the cooking method.** [BRANCH: stovetop for 2-8 slices with close control | oven tray for a full package or hands-off batch] → *Expect:* the pan or tray size matches the bacon volume without tight stacking.
2. **Separate the strips.** Peel slices apart while cold, keeping torn pieces grouped by thickness. → *Expect:* strips lie flat and similar-thickness pieces are ready to cook together.
3. **Start stovetop bacon in a cold pan.** Lay strips in the cold skillet with slight gaps, then set heat to medium-low. → *Expect:* fat begins to look glossy before loud sizzling starts.
4. **Render before browning.** Let the bacon slowly release fat for 5-8 minutes, moving curled edges flat with tongs. → *Expect:* a shallow pool of clear fat forms and the meat shrinks evenly.
5. **Turn and adjust heat.** Flip when the underside is lightly browned; reduce heat if fat spits hard or edges darken before centers firm. → *Expect:* steady sizzle, not smoke, and both sides color gradually.
6. **Use the oven-tray method when batching.** Line a rimmed tray, arrange strips in one layer, and bake at 400°F until browned. → *Expect:* bacon renders into the lined tray and cooks evenly in about 15-22 minutes.
7. **Pull just before final crispness.** Transfer strips to paper towels when they look one shade paler than the target. → *Expect:* carryover heat crisps the bacon as surface grease drains.
8. **Save or dispose of grease correctly.** Pour warm, not smoking, grease into a heat-safe container. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* grease down a sink can clog plumbing; confirm the drain path is avoided before pouring. → *Expect:* grease is contained off the pan and no liquid fat enters the sink.
9. **Degrease after cooling.** Wipe the cooled skillet or tray with paper towel before washing with hot soapy water. → *Expect:* only a thin film remains before the item reaches the sink.
10. **Store leftovers promptly.** Refrigerate cooked bacon once it is no longer steaming. → *Expect:* leftovers are in a covered container, not sitting warm on the counter.

## Decision points

- Thick-cut bacon → use lower heat and more time so fat renders before the outside scorches.
- Very lean bacon → add 1 tsp water to the cold pan to slow the first browning and help rendering.
- Bacon is needed crumbled → cook slightly crisper than strip-serving texture, then crumble after cooling.
- Smoke appears → lower heat, move the pan off the burner briefly, and turn on ventilation.
- Saving bacon fat → strain cooled liquid fat into a clean jar and refrigerate.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned edges with chewy centers:** heat was too high; lower heat and finish slowly, or chop and use as bacon bits.
- **F2 Bacon sticks to the pan:** wait 30-60 seconds before forcing it; rendered fat usually releases the strip.
- **F3 Grease splatter:** moisture or high heat is driving pops; reduce heat and use a splatter screen, not a sealed lid.
- **F4 Oven grease spills:** tray was not rimmed or was tilted; turn off heat, let it cool, then clean before restarting on a rimmed tray.
- **F5 Drain contamination:** if grease entered the sink, run hot water and dish soap briefly, then avoid repeating; do not assume it is harmless.

## Verification

Bacon is cooked to the desired crispness, the heat source is off, and all rendered grease is cooling in a container or solidified in trash rather than in the drain.

## Variations

- Turkey bacon: less fat renders, so use a lightly oiled pan and shorter cooking time.
- Candied bacon: add sugar or syrup only for oven cooking on a lined rimmed tray.
- Extra-flat strips: bake on a rack or press gently with a bacon press on the stovetop.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot grease, smoke, and slippery spills. Keep children away from the stove, never add water to hot grease, and let fat cool before handling containers.
