## Goal

A single-serving cake mixed and cooked in a mug, tender rather than rubbery or overflowing.

## Preconditions

- Microwave-safe mug holding at least 12 oz.
- Flour, sugar, cocoa or vanilla, baking powder, milk, oil or melted butter, and a pinch of salt.
- Spoon, measuring spoons, and a microwave.

## Steps

1. **Choose a large mug.** Use a straight-sided mug no more than half full after mixing. → *Expect:* plenty of headroom for the batter to rise.
2. **Mix dry ingredients first.** In the mug, stir 4 tbsp flour, 2 tbsp sugar, 1 tbsp cocoa or 1/2 tsp vanilla later, 1/4 tsp baking powder, and a pinch of salt. → *Expect:* no pockets of white flour or cocoa remain.
3. **Add wet ingredients.** Stir in 3 tbsp milk and 1 tbsp neutral oil or melted butter until just combined. → *Expect:* batter is glossy and spoonable, not dry paste.
4. **Scrape the bottom corners.** Drag the spoon around the mug base where flour hides. → *Expect:* no dry streaks come up on the spoon.
5. **Stop mixing early.** Do not beat once the batter is uniform. → *Expect:* batter still looks slightly loose and not elastic.
6. **Microwave in short timing.** Cook on high 60 seconds in a standard microwave. → *Expect:* top rises and looks mostly set with a tiny damp spot in the center.
7. **Rest and check.** Wait 1 minute; carryover heat finishes the center. → *Expect:* cake pulls slightly from the sides and the center is fudgy, not liquid.
8. **Add 10-second bursts only if needed.** [BRANCH: wet center | set center] cook wet batter briefly, or stop if set. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overcooked mug cake turns rubbery and cannot be softened except with sauce. → *Expect:* spoon meets tender cake with a moist crumb.

## Decision points

- Mug is more than half full before cooking → split into two mugs to prevent overflow.
- Want chocolate lava style → press a square of chocolate into the center before microwaving.
- Microwave is powerful → start at 45 to 50 seconds and rest before judging.
- Batter seems thick → add milk 1 tsp at a time until spoonable.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overflow:** mug was too small or batter overfilled it; stop, wipe the microwave, and use a larger mug next time.
- **F2 Rubbery texture:** overmixed or overcooked; add ice cream or sauce, and reduce time next batch.
- **F3 Dry flour pockets:** bottom corners were not scraped; eat around them and scrape more thoroughly next time.
- **F4 Raw center:** undercooked past a fudgy spot; microwave 10 seconds, rest, and recheck.

## Verification

The mug cake is risen, set at the edges, slightly moist in the center, and can be spooned without raw batter pooling.

## Variations

- `vanilla`: omit cocoa, use 1/2 tsp vanilla, and add 1 tbsp extra flour.
- `peanut-butter`: swirl 1 tbsp peanut butter into the top before cooking.
- `vegan`: use plant milk and neutral oil.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use only microwave-safe mugs, handle the hot mug with care, and let steam escape before the first bite.
