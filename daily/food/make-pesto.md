## Goal

A bright green basil pesto is made with balanced herb, nut, cheese, garlic, and oil flavors without unnecessary bruising or browning.

## Preconditions

- Fresh basil leaves, olive oil, hard cheese, nuts, garlic, salt, and lemon or acid if desired.
- Food processor, blender, mortar and pestle, or knife and cutting board.
- Clean jar or bowl for storage.
- Optional ice water if blanching basil for color.
- Awareness of nut and dairy allergies for anyone eating it.

## Steps

1. **Pick and inspect basil.** Remove thick stems, wilted leaves, and gritty pieces. → *Expect:* leaves are fragrant, dry or lightly damp, and free of dirt.
2. **Prevent early browning.** Pat basil dry and chill the bowl or blade if the kitchen is hot. → *Expect:* basil stays green before chopping.
3. **Toast nuts only if wanted.** Warm pine nuts, walnuts, or almonds lightly, then cool fully. → *Expect:* nuts smell sweet and are not dark or oily.
4. **Start with garlic, nuts, and salt.** Pulse or pound them first to break down hard pieces. → *Expect:* a coarse paste forms before basil is added.
5. **Add basil gently.** Pulse in short bursts or pound with a twisting motion instead of grinding continuously. → *Expect:* basil breaks down without turning hot, blackened, or watery.
6. **Balance cheese and oil.** Add grated Parmesan or Pecorino, then drizzle olive oil until the pesto loosens. → *Expect:* sauce looks glossy and spoonable, not dry or swimming.
7. **Taste for salt, sharpness, and bitterness.** Add more cheese for savoriness, oil for softness, lemon for brightness, or nuts for body. → *Expect:* no single ingredient dominates.
8. **Use the no-processor method if needed.** Mince basil with a sharp knife, then fold with crushed garlic, chopped nuts, cheese, and oil. → *Expect:* texture is rustic but cohesive.
9. **Cover against air.** Press pesto into a jar and cover the surface with a thin oil layer. → *Expect:* exposed green surface is sealed from oxygen.
10. **Serve or store quickly.** Refrigerate for short storage or freeze in small portions. → *Expect:* pesto remains bright and aromatic.

## Decision points

- Basil tastes bitter → add a little more cheese or oil, and avoid overprocessing.
- Pesto is for hot pasta → loosen with pasta water off heat so cheese does not clump.
- Nut-free pesto needed → use sunflower seeds or omit nuts, then adjust oil for body.
- Dairy-free pesto needed → omit cheese and add nutritional yeast or extra nuts for savoriness.
- Long storage needed → freeze rather than relying on refrigerator color.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Brown pesto:** basil oxidized or overheated; stir in lemon and cover with oil, then use soon.
- **F2 Oily separated pesto:** oil was added too fast; fold in more chopped basil, cheese, or nuts.
- **F3 Harsh garlic bite:** raw garlic was too strong; add more basil and oil, or let it rest 20 minutes.
- **F4 Gritty texture:** cheese or nuts are too coarse; pulse briefly or pound longer before adding more oil.
- **F5 Salty pesto:** cheese and salt stacked up; dilute with unsalted basil, nuts, and oil.

## Verification

Pesto is green, cohesive, spoonable, and balanced enough that a small taste shows basil first with supporting garlic, nut, cheese, salt, and oil.

## Variations

- Classic Genovese: basil, pine nuts, garlic, Parmigiano-Reggiano or Pecorino, olive oil, and salt.
- Herb blend: replace part of the basil with parsley, mint, arugula, or spinach.
- Freezer cubes: portion into an ice cube tray, freeze, then move cubes to a sealed bag.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but disclose nuts and dairy. Refrigerate promptly, use clean utensils, and do not leave garlic-in-oil mixtures at room temperature.
