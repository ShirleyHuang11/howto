## Goal

A vacuum-sealed or over-tightened jar lid opened without injury, drama, or shattered glass, using the escalation ladder that ends every kitchen standoff.

## Preconditions

- A stubborn jar and dry hands. Wet or oily hands lose before starting; wash and dry both hands and the lid first.
- Knowing the enemy: new jars resist because of vacuum (the safety button on the lid is sucked down); opened jars resist because of dried food gluing the threads. The ladder below handles both.

## Steps

1. **Set the base grip and try once, properly.** Jar at counter height, base hand wrapping the jar low and pressed down onto a non-slip surface (a damp cloth under it), lid hand over the top, turn counterclockwise with your whole shoulder, not the wrist. → *Expect:* many "stubborn" jars open here, with a pop as the vacuum breaks. No movement: proceed up the ladder.
2. **Add friction.** Rubber glove, silicone trivet, or a rubber band stretched around the lid's rim; regrip and repeat. → *Expect:* your grip stops slipping at the same torque; a good fraction surrender to friction alone.
3. **Break the vacuum directly.** Slide a spoon's tip under the lid's lip and lever gently until you hear the hiss, or tap the lid's edge firmly a few times on the counter (rotating between taps), or smack the jar's *bottom* with a flat palm at 45 degrees (the water-hammer trick). → *Expect:* the hiss or the button popping up; after it, the lid turns with two fingers.
4. **Heat the lid for the glued-threads case.** Hot tap water run over the lid (not the glass) for 30 seconds: metal expands faster than glass and dried syrup softens. Dry the lid, then friction grip and turn. ⚠️ Boiling water on a cold glass jar risks cracking it; hot tap water is the ceiling. → *Expect:* previously-opened honey and sauce jars give up here.
5. **Use leverage tools if the ladder is still climbing.** A jar-opener cone, an oil-filter strap wrench, or the under-cabinet V-grip if the kitchen has one; these multiply torque beyond any grip. → *Expect:* mechanical advantage ends the contest.
6. **Open away from your face and wipe the threads before re-closing.** The pop can spray (fermented or pressurized contents especially); point the lid away. After use, wipe the jar's threads clean so future-you never meets step 4 again. → *Expect:* jar open, face dry, threads wiped for next time.

## Decision points

- Bulging lid, spraying contents, or a safety button that was already up on a *new* jar: that is spoilage pressure, not stubbornness; do not eat it (`daily/food/read-food-labels`'s discard asymmetry applies with prejudice).
- Weak grip, arthritis, or one-handed contexts: skip straight to step 5's tools; the strap wrench and V-grip exist precisely so this task needs no grip strength, and they are a genuinely great gift for anyone who struggles.
- Twist-off beer and soda caps are a different thread and a different recipe; don't strap-wrench those.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lid deformed by pliers or brute force (the ladder was skipped):** a bent lid grips harder; work the spoon-lever (step 3) around the full circumference to release it, and let the ladder's order do the thinking next time.
- **F2 Glass cracked:** stop immediately; contents are contaminated with possible fragments; discard jar and contents wrapped (`daily/home/sort-recycling`'s glass rule), sweep per the glass protocol.
- **F3 Contents erupted on opening:** shaken or pressurized; wipe now (`embodied/kitchen/wipe-down-a-countertop`), and open suspect jars over the sink at arm's length.
- **F4 The jar wins anyway:** hand it to someone else *after* your vacuum-break — the infuriating physics is that your steps 1 to 3 loosened it for their effortless victory. Announce this loudly; the recipe's dignity depends on it.

## Verification

The jar is open, hands and face are unsprayed, the glass is intact, the lid is unmangled and re-closeable, and the threads are wiped so this jar never earns its own sequel.

## Variations

- Ketchup-packet and foil-seal battles are cousins with their own tricks (tear at the notch, use scissors without shame).
- Restaurant/host setting: step 3's palm-smack is quiet and looks deliberate; the counter-tap is louder; choose your theater.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from two directions: glass under force (the ladder exists so force stays low) and spray in the face (open away, especially anything fermented). The victory belongs to physics, not grip strength, which is the entire point of writing it down.
