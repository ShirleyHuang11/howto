## Goal

A plate of fried rice with separate, lightly chewy grains, distinct egg pieces, and a savory toasted-soy edge, made in one pan in under ten minutes of actual cooking.

## Preconditions

- Cold cooked rice, ideally day-old from the fridge. Fridge-dried grains separate; fresh rice steams into mush. (No day-old rice → see Decision points.)
- A wok or the widest heavy skillet available, a spatula or wok ladle, and a high-heat oil (peanut, canola).
- Everything prepped and within arm's reach before the heat goes on: 2 beaten eggs, aromatics (garlic, scallion whites), any add-ins (peas, diced ham, leftover chicken) cut small, soy sauce, salt, scallion greens. There is no time to chop once the wok is hot.

## Steps

1. **Break up the cold rice.** Wet your hands and crumble the clumps into loose grains in a bowl. Clumps that go in whole come out as clumps. → *Expect:* mostly individual grains, no fist-sized lumps.
2. **Heat the empty wok until it just smokes, then add oil.** High heat throughout; a timid burner is why home fried rice tastes steamed. Swirl in 1-2 tablespoons of oil. → *Expect:* a wisp of smoke off the bare metal, then oil that shimmers and flows fast.
3. **Cook the egg first and remove it.** [BRANCH: egg-first (default): pour in the beaten egg, scramble 20-30 seconds to barely set, take it out to a bowl | egg-last coating: skip here, see step 6] Egg-first gives distinct golden pieces; leaving egg in for the whole stir-fry overcooks it to rubber. → *Expect:* soft just-set curds out of the pan, still glossy.
4. **Flash the aromatics, then the add-ins.** More oil if the pan is dry; garlic and scallion whites for 10-15 seconds only, then cooked add-ins for ~1 minute. → *Expect:* loud sizzle, fragrant garlic that is not browning past golden.
5. **Add the rice and toss until every grain is hot.** Spread it, let it sit 20-30 seconds to toast, then toss; repeat. Press clumps flat with the spatula back. 2-3 minutes total. → *Expect:* grains hopping loose in the toss, faint toasty smell, no steam-wet sheen.
6. **[BRANCH: egg-last chosen]** Push rice aside, pour beaten egg into the gap, half-set it, then toss so egg coats the grains golden. → *Expect:* rice coated pale gold rather than carrying distinct egg pieces.
7. **Soy sauce at the edge of the wok, not on the rice.** Drizzle 1-2 tablespoons down the hot side wall so it sears and caramelizes on the metal before being tossed through. Poured directly on the pile, it soaks in and makes wet, patchy rice. → *Expect:* an instant sharp sizzle and a toasted-soy smell, then even pale-brown color after tossing.
8. **Return the egg, season, finish.** Egg pieces back in, salt to taste, scallion greens, optional sesame oil off the heat. Toss twice and plate immediately. → *Expect:* separate grains on the plate, egg in visible pieces, nothing pooling.

## Decision points

- No day-old rice → cook rice with slightly less water, then spread it on a tray in the freezer for 15-20 minutes uncovered; usable, though a notch softer than true day-old.
- Electric or weak burner → cook in two half batches; crowding a weak burner drops the pan temperature below frying.
- Rice was left out at room temperature overnight (not refrigerated) → discard it; see Safety.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mushy, clumping rice:** rice too fresh or pan overcrowded → spread the batch thin on the hot pan and leave it untouched 1-2 minutes at a time to dry and crisp; split into batches.
- **F2 Rice sticking and scorching to the pan:** not enough oil or heat applied to a cold pan → scrape off, re-oil the hot pan, continue; hot-pan-then-oil prevents it.
- **F3 Flat gray taste:** soy went on the pile and heat was low → push rice aside, re-drizzle soy on the bare hot metal, toss through the sizzle.
- **F4 Rubbery egg bits:** egg rode along for the whole cook → remove egg early next time; for now, fold in a fresh soft-scrambled egg at the end.

## Verification

Grains fall separately off the spatula, egg appears as distinct pieces (or an even golden coat, if egg-last), color is uniform pale brown with no white wet patches, and the plate shows no liquid after a minute.

## Variations

- `cn-cantonese`: minimal soy, white pepper, lard if available; egg-last coating style.
- `th`: fish sauce plus a little sugar instead of most of the soy; serve with lime and cucumber.
- Vegetarian: crumbled firm tofu fried until dry stands in for meat; season slightly more.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from two sources. High-heat oil: keep the handle turned in, never add watery ingredients to smoking oil in volume, and smother (never water) an oil flare with a lid. Rice safety: cooked rice held at room temperature grows Bacillus cereus, whose toxin survives re-frying; refrigerate rice within an hour of cooking and reheat it fully to steaming.
