## Goal

A whole egg is poached with a set white, liquid yolk, and neat shape, then landed on toast without waterlogging it.

## Preconditions

- Fresh eggs. The fresher the egg, the tighter the white stays around the yolk.
- A shallow saucepan, slotted spoon, small bowl or ramekin, timer, and paper towel.
- Water, a splash of plain vinegar, and toast or another landing surface ready before the egg finishes.

## Steps

1. **Choose the freshest egg.** Use an egg with a firm shell and a recent date; older eggs are better for boiling than poaching. → *Expect:* the egg cracks into a bowl with a tall yolk and a thick white that does not spread like water.
2. **Bring water to a bare simmer.** Fill the pan 6 to 8 cm deep and heat until small bubbles cling to the bottom with only a few breaking the surface. → *Expect:* the water is hot and moving gently, not boiling hard.
3. **Add vinegar.** Stir in about 1 tablespoon vinegar per liter of water. → *Expect:* the water smells faintly sharp but remains clear.
4. **Crack into a cup.** Crack the egg into a small bowl, not directly into the pan, and discard shell fragments. → *Expect:* the yolk is intact and the egg can slide out in one motion.
5. **Make a gentle whirlpool.** Stir the center of the pan in one direction, then stop before it becomes a tornado. → *Expect:* the water rotates slowly enough that the egg will gather, not shred.
6. **Slide in the egg.** Lower the cup close to the water and pour the egg into the center of the swirl. → *Expect:* the white wraps around the yolk within 10 seconds.
7. **Time the poach.** Keep the water at a bare simmer for 3 minutes for a runny yolk, 4 minutes for a slightly thicker yolk. → *Expect:* the white turns opaque while the yolk still looks rounded and soft.
8. **Lift and test.** Scoop the egg with a slotted spoon and nudge the white gently with a fingertip or spoon edge. → *Expect:* the white feels set and the yolk wobbles under it.
9. **Drain before serving.** Rest the spoon briefly on a folded paper towel or clean kitchen towel. → *Expect:* loose water leaves the egg instead of soaking the toast.
10. **Land on toast.** Set the egg on buttered or dry toast and season immediately. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* cutting the egg releases the yolk, so place it exactly where it will be served first. → *Expect:* the egg sits centered on toast with no puddle around it.

## Decision points

- Egg white spreads into ribbons → the egg is old or the water is too turbulent. Use a fresher egg, strain watery white through a fine sieve, or poach in a ladle.
- Cooking several eggs → skip the whirlpool after the first egg; use a wide pan and place eggs in separate zones.
- Serving later → undercook by 30 seconds, chill in cold water, then rewarm in hot water for 1 minute.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shredded white:** detect by cloudy water and stringy fragments; lower the simmer, remake a gentler whirlpool, and use a fresh egg.
- **F2 Broken yolk:** detect yellow leaking into the water; continue cooking for a firm broken-yolk egg or start over if presentation matters.
- **F3 Rubbery egg:** detect a hard bounce and chalky yolk edge; shorten the next egg by 30 to 60 seconds and keep the water below a boil.
- **F4 Wet toast:** detect a soggy ring under the egg; drain the egg on towel before landing and toast the bread darker next time.

## Verification

The egg has an opaque set white, a warm soft yolk, and is served on toast without standing water.

## Variations

- No vinegar: use a very fresh egg and accept a looser white.
- Neater restaurant shape: strain the cracked egg through a fine sieve before poaching.
- Firm yolk: cook 5 minutes and verify the center barely wobbles.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot water and undercooked egg. Use pasteurized eggs for pregnancy, immune compromise, or anyone advised to avoid runny eggs.
