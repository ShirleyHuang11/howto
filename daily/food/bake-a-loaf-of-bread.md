## Goal

A basic yeasted loaf is mixed, kneaded, risen, shaped, baked with steam, cooled, and checked for doneness.

## Preconditions

- Bread flour or all-purpose flour, instant or active dry yeast, salt, water, and a clean work surface.
- A bowl, towel or wrap, loaf pan or baking sheet, oven, and a way to add steam such as a hot tray.
- Time for two rises; rushing yeast gives dense bread.

## Steps

1. **Mix the dough.** Stir 500 g flour, 325 g warm water, 7 g yeast, and 10 g salt until no dry flour remains. → *Expect:* a shaggy, sticky mass that pulls loose from the bowl in patches.
2. **Rest before kneading.** Cover for 15 minutes so the flour hydrates. → *Expect:* dough feels less sandy and stretches farther before tearing.
3. **Knead to build gluten.** Knead 8-10 minutes by hand, pushing and folding, or 5-6 minutes on low in a mixer. → *Expect:* dough becomes smoother, elastic, and tacky rather than wet.
4. **Check the gluten.** Pinch off a piece and stretch it thin. [BRANCH: thin sheet forms | tears instantly] → *Expect:* a translucent window, or a clear need for 2 more minutes of kneading.
5. **First rise.** Put dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise at room temperature 1-2 hours. → *Expect:* dough roughly doubles and a fingertip dent fills back slowly.
6. **Shape the loaf.** Pat into a rectangle, fold thirds like a letter, roll tight, and pinch the seam. → *Expect:* a taut log with surface tension and the seam underneath.
7. **Second rise.** Place in a greased pan or seam-down on parchment, cover, and proof 45-75 minutes. → *Expect:* dough looks puffy and springs back slowly when touched.
8. **Preheat and prepare steam.** Heat oven to 220 °C with an empty metal tray on the lower rack. → *Expect:* oven fully hot and tray ready before the loaf goes in.
9. **Slash and bake with steam.** Score the top, load the loaf, pour 1 cup hot water into the lower tray, and close the door. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* steam burns skin fast; keep face back and use a long-spouted cup. → *Expect:* an immediate burst of steam and the loaf beginning to expand.
10. **Finish the bake.** Bake 30-40 minutes, lowering to 200 °C after 15 minutes if browning too fast. → *Expect:* deep golden crust and an internal temperature near 93 °C.
11. **Tap-test and cool.** Tip loaf out and tap the bottom; cool on a rack at least 1 hour before slicing. → *Expect:* a hollow sound, dry base, and no gummy crumb when cut.

## Decision points

- Dough is slack after kneading → rest 10 minutes before adding flour; hydration often solves itself.
- Kitchen is cold → proof in the turned-off oven with the light on, not over direct heat.
- Crust is browning before the center is done → tent loosely with foil and keep baking.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dense loaf:** detect by little rise and tight crumb → extend proof next time and verify yeast is alive in warm water.
- **F2 Torn top:** detect by random side bursts → score deeper and proof slightly longer.
- **F3 Gummy middle:** detect by sticky knife and damp crumb → return whole loaf to a 180 °C oven for 10-15 minutes if not sliced much.
- **F4 Burned crust:** detect by black patches or bitter smell → lower rack, tent with foil, and reduce next bake by 10 °C.

## Verification

The cooled loaf sounds hollow when tapped, has a browned crust, and slices with an open, set crumb rather than raw dough.

## Variations

- `whole-wheat`: replace up to half the flour with whole wheat and add 25-40 g extra water.
- `overnight`: use 2 g yeast, refrigerate after mixing, then shape and proof the next day.
- `enriched`: add 30 g butter and 25 g sugar for a softer sandwich loaf.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot oven surfaces, steam, and heavy pans. Use dry mitts, vent steam away from your face, and keep raw flour off ready-to-eat food because it is not sterile.
