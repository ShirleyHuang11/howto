## Goal

Use ethylene in a paper bag to speed ripening for fruit that continues ripening after harvest.

## Preconditions

- Unripe avocado, banana, pear, peach, plum, kiwi, mango, or tomato is ready and smells fresh.
- Paper bag and ripe banana or apple if needed are ready.
- A clean work surface is available.
- Storage or serving container is ready.

## Steps

1. **Confirm type.** Use only fruit that ripens after harvest → *Expect:* fruit type is suitable
2. **Inspect.** Reject moldy, cut, or badly bruised fruit → *Expect:* fruit is clean
3. **Bag.** Place in a paper bag and fold loosely → *Expect:* bag breathes
4. **Boost ethylene.** Add a ripe banana or apple if speed matters → *Expect:* ethylene source is inside
5. **Keep temperate.** Set on counter away from sun → *Expect:* fruit does not sweat
6. **Check daily.** Smell and press gently near stem or shoulder → *Expect:* aroma rises and flesh softens
7. **Stop ripe.** Remove and refrigerate if needed. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overripe fruit ferments or molds quickly → *Expect:* ripening slows

## Decision points

- Berries, grapes, citrus, pineapple, and watermelon -> they will not get sweeter.
- Hard avocado tonight -> bagging cannot work miracles in hours.
- Hot kitchen -> check twice daily.
- Bruised fruit -> use sooner or separately.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mold:** detect fuzz -> discard affected fruit and bag.
- **F2 Condensation:** detect wet paper -> open bag more.
- **F3 Still hard:** detect no change after 2 days -> fruit may be immature.
- **F4 Overripe:** detect alcohol smell -> use immediately if not spoiled.

## Verification

fruit is ripe, fragrant, and removed before rot

## Variations

- Avocado: ripe when it yields near the stem.
- Pear: ripen counter, then refrigerate.
- Tomato: keep at room temperature, stem-side down.
- Banana: bag together to speed, separate to slow.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, mostly food waste and mold exposure.
Use paper rather than plastic and inspect daily for mold or fermented odor.
