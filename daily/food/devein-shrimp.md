## Goal

Shrimp cleaned of the dark digestive tract, with shells left on or removed as the recipe needs.

## Preconditions

- Raw shrimp, a sharp paring knife or kitchen shears, a bowl for waste, and cold running water.
- A stable cutting board or tray; wet shrimp are slippery, so keep towels nearby.
- Clean hands and a plan to keep raw seafood away from ready-to-eat food.

## Steps

1. **Keep shrimp cold.** Work from the refrigerator in small batches, leaving the rest chilled. → *Expect:* shrimp feel cold and firm, not warm or limp.
2. **Choose shell status.** [BRANCH: shell-on | peeled] For shell-on shrimp, leave the shell attached and slit through it; for peeled shrimp, pull off shell segments and legs first. → *Expect:* each shrimp is ready for a shallow cut along the back.
3. **Expose the back line.** Hold the shrimp curved-side up, fingers curled in a claw away from the blade. → *Expect:* the dark line along the outer curve is visible or traceable.
4. **Make a shallow back slit.** Use the tip of a sharp knife to cut 1/8 inch deep from head end toward tail, following the curve. → *Expect:* the flesh opens slightly without cutting the shrimp in half.
5. **Lift the vein.** Slide the knife tip, skewer, or fingertip under the dark tract and pull it out in one piece when possible. → *Expect:* the groove is clear or only tiny fragments remain.
6. **Rinse briefly.** Pass the slit under cold water and wipe out fragments with a fingertip. → *Expect:* no gritty dark line remains in the back cut.
7. **Butterfly when wanted.** [BRANCH: normal | butterfly] Stop after the shallow slit for normal prep, or deepen the cut almost to the belly for butterflied shrimp. → *Expect:* butterflied shrimp open flat but remain hinged.
8. **Dry before cooking.** Pat shrimp dry on towels before seasoning or searing. → *Expect:* surfaces are dry enough to brown instead of steam.
   - ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a deep butterfly cut cannot be undone; confirm the recipe wants flattened shrimp first.

## Decision points

- Tiny shrimp → skip individual deveining if the vein is pale and the recipe is casual; it is edible but can be gritty.
- Shell-on cooking → slit through the shell with shears for flavor and easier eating.
- Tail presentation → leave tails on for cocktail or grilling, remove them for pasta, tacos, or stir-fry.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vein breaks:** dark fragments stay in the groove → rinse under cold water and wipe with the knife tip or fingertip.
- **F2 Shrimp turns mushy:** batch warmed up → return it to the refrigerator and work smaller batches.
- **F3 Cut goes through the shrimp:** the slit was too deep → use that shrimp as a butterfly piece and cut the rest shallower.
- **F4 Raw juices spread:** board or towel got contaminated → wash, sanitize, and replace towels before touching other food.

## Verification

Each shrimp has a clean back groove with no gritty dark tract, stays cold, and is dry before cooking.

## Variations

- Kitchen shears: cut through shell and flesh in one pass, then lift the vein.
- Frozen shrimp: thaw overnight in the refrigerator or sealed under cold water, then dry before cutting.
- Head-on shrimp: remove the vein from the back while keeping head and shell intact for presentation.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but raw shellfish can carry pathogens and allergens. Wash hands, boards, and knives after contact, and keep shrimp cold until cooking.
