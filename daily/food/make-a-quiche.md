## Goal

A quiche with a crisp crust and just-set custard is baked, cooled slightly, and sliced cleanly.

## Preconditions

- Pie dough in a 23 cm tart or pie pan, chilled.
- Eggs, cream or milk, cheese, cooked fillings, salt, pepper, and oven.
- Pie weights, dried beans, or rice for blind-baking.

## Steps

1. **Chill the lined crust.** Fit dough into the pan, trim, dock lightly, and chill 20-30 minutes. → *Expect:* firm dough that does not slump when touched.
2. **Blind-bake weighted.** Line with parchment, fill with weights, and bake at 190 °C for 15 minutes. → *Expect:* edges set and bottom no longer raw-looking.
3. **Finish the empty crust.** Remove weights and bake 8-12 minutes more until pale golden. → *Expect:* dry, lightly colored bottom that resists sogginess.
4. **Prepare fillings.** Cook watery vegetables, bacon, or mushrooms first and cool them. → *Expect:* fillings are flavorful and not leaking liquid.
5. **Mix custard ratio.** Whisk 1 large egg per 120 ml dairy, using 3 eggs plus 360 ml cream or milk for a standard pie. → *Expect:* smooth custard with no streaks.
6. **Fill the shell.** Scatter cheese and fillings in the crust, then pour custard to just below the rim. → *Expect:* solids are evenly distributed and not mounded above liquid.
7. **Bake gently.** Bake at 175 °C for 30-40 minutes. → *Expect:* edges puff slightly while the center still has a small wobble.
8. **Stop at just-set.** Remove when a knife 2 cm from the edge comes out mostly clean. → *Expect:* center jiggles like soft gelatin, not liquid.
9. **Rest before slicing.** Cool 15-30 minutes on a rack. → *Expect:* custard firms enough for neat wedges.

## Decision points

- Using milk instead of cream → expect a lighter custard and avoid too many watery fillings.
- Crust edge browns early → cover edge with foil and continue baking the center.
- Fillings taste bland before baking → season them now; custard will dilute the flavor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Soggy crust:** detect by pale wet bottom → blind-bake longer next time; for this quiche, reheat slices on a hot baking sheet.
- **F2 Curdled custard:** detect by watery scrambled texture → bake lower and pull earlier next time.
- **F3 Overflow:** detect by custard near rim before moving → ladle some out before lifting the pan.
- **F4 Wet filling pockets:** detect by liquid around vegetables → cook and drain fillings more thoroughly.

## Verification

The cooled quiche slices cleanly, with a crisp bottom crust and custard set to the center without watery seepage.

## Variations

- `crustless`: grease the dish well and bake the filling directly, reducing dairy slightly.
- `mini`: use muffin tins and bake 15-22 minutes after blind-baking any crusts.
- `classic-lorraine`: use bacon, gruyere, cream, eggs, pepper, and a little nutmeg.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from hot fat, hot custard, and oven burns. Refrigerate leftovers within 2 hours because egg custard is perishable.
