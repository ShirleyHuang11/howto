## Goal

Nuts or seeds are toasted evenly until fragrant, then cooled off the heat before they burn.

## Preconditions

- Raw or untoasted nuts or seeds are available.
- A dry skillet or sheet pan is available.
- A plate, bowl, or cool tray is ready before heating starts.

## Steps

1. **Choose a method.** [BRANCH: dry skillet for small amounts | oven at 325 F for larger amounts] → *Expect:* the pan size lets nuts sit mostly in one layer.
2. **Remove excess dust.** Shake out broken skins or fine powder that can burn first. → *Expect:* pieces look clean and similar in size.
3. **Heat gently.** For skillet, set medium-low heat; for oven, preheat and line a tray if desired. → *Expect:* pan is warm but not smoking.
4. **Spread in one layer.** Add nuts or seeds without oil unless the recipe calls for it. → *Expect:* pieces touch the hot surface evenly.
5. **Watch constantly.** Stir or shake every 30 seconds in a skillet, or stir every 3 minutes in the oven. → *Expect:* color deepens gradually and aroma turns nutty.
6. **Pull early.** Remove when one shade darker and fragrant. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* carryover heat can burn them, so do not leave them in the hot pan. → *Expect:* pieces are toasted but not dark brown or smoking.
7. **Cool off heat.** Dump immediately onto the waiting cool plate or tray. → *Expect:* sizzling stops and color stops darkening.
8. **Use or store.** Cool fully before chopping or sealing in a container. → *Expect:* texture is crisp and aroma is clean.

## Decision points

- Seeds are tiny, such as sesame → use a skillet and stir constantly.
- Nuts are large, such as walnuts → chop after toasting to avoid burned small edges.
- Nuts are already roasted → warm briefly only if extra aroma is needed.
- Recipe needs salted nuts → salt while warm so seasoning sticks.
- Nuts smell rancid before toasting → discard them.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burned batch:** detect smoke, black specks, or bitter smell; recover by discarding because bitterness spreads.
- **F2 Uneven color:** detect pale centers and dark edges; recover by lowering heat and stirring more often.
- **F3 Soggy storage:** detect steam in the container; recover by reopening and cooling completely.
- **F4 Hot-spot scorching:** detect one pan area darkening fast; recover by moving the pan and stirring through the hot spot.
- **F5 Allergens spread:** detect shared tools with allergy risk; recover by washing tools and surfaces before other food prep.

## Verification

The nuts or seeds smell fragrant, taste crisp, and show even light browning with no blackened pieces.

## Variations

- Microwave: heat in 30-second bursts on a plate, stirring between bursts.
- Sweet spiced: add sugar or syrup only after dry toasting, then cool spread apart.
- Breadcrumb mix: toast nuts first, then add crumbs for the final minute.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but nuts burn fast and smoke can trigger alarms. Keep allergen cross-contact in mind when serving others.
