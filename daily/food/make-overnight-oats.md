## Goal

Rolled oats are soaked in a jar until tender, with toppings timed so the final breakfast has the intended texture.

## Preconditions

- Rolled oats, milk or another liquid, salt, and a jar or lidded container.
- Refrigerator space for at least 4 hours.
- Optional yogurt, chia seeds, sweetener, fruit, nuts, or spices.
- Measuring cup or a consistent scoop.

## Steps

1. **Choose the base ratio.** Use 1 part rolled oats to 1 part liquid for spoonable oats, or add 1/4 part yogurt for creamier oats → *Expect:* the jar has enough headroom for stirring and toppings.
2. **Season the dry oats.** Add a pinch of salt and any dry spices before the liquid → *Expect:* spices are dispersed instead of clumped on top.
3. **Add liquid and dairy.** Pour in milk, plant milk, or water, then add yogurt if using → *Expect:* oats are fully wet with no dry pockets at the bottom.
4. **Stir thoroughly.** Scrape the corners and sides of the jar with a spoon → *Expect:* the mixture looks even from top to bottom.
5. **Time the toppings.** [BRANCH: soft toppings overnight | crunchy toppings morning] add dried fruit now, but keep nuts, granola, and crisp fruit separate → *Expect:* toppings that should soften are submerged and crunchy items stay dry.
6. **Refrigerate.** Seal the jar and chill for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight → *Expect:* the jar is cold and the oats have absorbed most liquid.
7. **Fix the texture.** Stir before eating, then add liquid for thick oats or extra oats/chia for loose oats → *Expect:* the spoon moves easily and the mixture is not soupy unless desired.
8. **Finish cold or warm.** Eat cold, or microwave in a microwave-safe bowl with extra liquid if you prefer warm oats → *Expect:* the final bowl is creamy and evenly mixed.

## Decision points

- Using quick oats → reduce liquid slightly and expect a softer texture.
- Using steel-cut oats → soak longer or use a recipe designed for them.
- Adding chia seeds → add extra liquid, since chia thickens aggressively.
- Packing for travel → keep wet fruit and crunchy toppings in separate small containers.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too thick:** detect a stiff paste, recover by stirring in milk 1 tablespoon at a time.
- **F2 Too loose:** detect free liquid after stirring, recover by adding oats, chia, or yogurt and resting 10 minutes.
- **F3 Bland oats:** detect flat cereal flavor, recover with salt first, then sweetener, fruit, or spice.
- **F4 Slimy texture:** detect gluey oats, recover by using rolled oats instead of quick oats next time.
- **F5 Soggy toppings:** detect limp nuts or granola, recover by adding a fresh crunchy topping at serving.

## Verification

After chilling and stirring, the oats are hydrated throughout, cold, and thick enough to hold on a spoon.

## Variations

- High-protein: replace part of the liquid with Greek yogurt.
- Fruit-on-bottom: put jam or berries under the oats, then stir in the morning.
- Chocolate: add cocoa powder with the dry oats and extra liquid to prevent dryness.
- Warm version: transfer to a bowl before microwaving so the jar is not overheated.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep dairy-based oats refrigerated, use a clean spoon, and discard after 3 to 4 days or sooner if the smell or texture changes.
