## Goal

A whole chicken has its backbone removed, is flattened, roasted evenly, rested, and ready to carve.

## Preconditions

- Whole chicken, sturdy kitchen shears, cutting board, rimmed sheet pan, salt, and oven.
- Paper towels and sanitizer for raw poultry cleanup.
- Optional instant-read thermometer.

## Steps

1. **Dry and orient the chicken.** Pat dry and place breast-side down with legs toward you. → *Expect:* backbone runs along the top center facing you.
2. **Cut one side of the backbone.** Use shears to cut from tail to neck along one side of the spine. → *Expect:* ribs crack cleanly and shears stay close to the backbone.
3. **Cut the other side.** Repeat on the opposite side and remove the backbone. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* keep fingers ahead of the shear blades out of the cutting path. → *Expect:* backbone is free for stock or discard.
4. **Flatten the bird.** Flip breast-side up and press firmly on the breastbone with both hands. → *Expect:* a crack or give, and the chicken lies flatter.
5. **Tuck and season.** Tuck wing tips behind shoulders, salt all sides, and oil lightly if roasting now. → *Expect:* even surface with legs splayed outward.
6. **Preheat and arrange.** Heat oven to 220 °C and place chicken skin-side up on a rack or vegetables. → *Expect:* thighs and breasts are exposed to the same heat.
7. **Roast fast and even.** Roast 35-50 minutes depending on size. → *Expect:* skin browns and juices collect in the pan.
8. **Check doneness.** Measure thick breast at 68-71 °C and thigh at 79-85 °C, or verify clear juices at the thigh. → *Expect:* no pink juices at the joint and skin crisping.
9. **Rest before carving.** Rest 10-15 minutes uncovered. → *Expect:* juices settle and carving does not flood the board.

## Decision points

- Shears cannot cut the bones → use a sharp heavy knife only if comfortable, otherwise ask a butcher to spatchcock it.
- Breast browns too fast → shield breast loosely with foil while thighs finish.
- Want extra crisp skin → dry-brine uncovered overnight before roasting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Jagged cuts:** detect by meat hacked away with spine → cut closer to the backbone on the next bird; it is still roastable.
- **F2 Uneven cooking:** detect by breast done before thigh → point legs toward hotter back of oven or shield breast.
- **F3 Soggy skin:** detect by pale rubbery skin → pat dry, salt earlier, and roast on a rack.
- **F4 Undercooked joint:** detect by pink juices at thigh → return to oven and check every 5 minutes.

## Verification

The chicken lies flat, the backbone is removed, breast and thigh have reached safe doneness, and the bird rested before carving.

## Variations

- `grill`: cook skin-side up over indirect heat, then crisp briefly skin-side down.
- `brick-chicken`: press with a foil-wrapped skillet for extra skin contact.
- `butterflied-parts`: split further into halves after removing the backbone for faster cooking.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from sharp shears, raw poultry, and hot roasting fat. Keep the board stable, sanitize raw-contact surfaces, and use a thermometer rather than guessing doneness.
