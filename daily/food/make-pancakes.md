## Goal

A stack of fluffy pancakes from scratch, with the batter rested, the first-pancake sacrifice understood, and the bubble-reading skill that makes every flip land golden.

## Preconditions

- The base ratio (memorize once, own it forever): 1 cup flour, 1 tablespoon sugar, 2 teaspoons baking powder, a pinch of salt, 1 egg, 1 cup milk, 2 tablespoons melted butter or oil. Feeds two to three people; scales linearly.
- A nonstick or well-seasoned pan (`daily/food/fry-an-egg`'s pan skills transfer whole), a spatula wider than your pancake ambitions, and butter or neutral oil for the pan.

## Steps

1. **Mix dry and wet separately, then combine *briefly*.** Whisk the dry ingredients; whisk egg, milk, and melted butter in another bowl; pour wet into dry and stir just until no dry streaks remain. Lumps are correct. Overmixing develops gluten and makes rubber frisbees — the single most important sentence in pancake literature. → *Expect:* a thick, pourable, visibly lumpy batter.
2. **Rest the batter 5 to 10 minutes while the pan heats on medium.** The rest hydrates the flour and lets the baking powder start; the pan wants medium, not hot (`fry-an-egg` step 1's smoking-pan rule applies). → *Expect:* batter slightly puffed; a water-drop on the pan dances rather than instantly vanishing.
3. **Grease lightly and pour the first pancake — the designated sacrifice.** A small knob of butter wiped around with a paper towel; pour about a third-cup from a low height into the center. The first pancake calibrates pan temperature and is traditionally eaten standing up by the cook. → *Expect:* a slowly spreading round; gentle sizzle, not a sear.
4. **Read the bubbles for the flip.** Wait for bubbles to form across the surface *and begin popping and staying open*, with the edges looking set and slightly dry — typically 2 to 3 minutes. This visual, not a timer, is the flip signal. → *Expect:* open bubble holes across the middle; an edge lifted with the spatula shows golden brown.
5. **Flip once, decisively, and cook the second side briefly.** Slide the spatula fully under, one confident wrist turn, no height ambitions. Side two takes half the time. Do not press the pancake down; the fluff you squash never comes back. → *Expect:* an even golden second side in 60 to 90 seconds; the pancake puffing, not hissing flat.
6. **Stack, hold, and repeat with adjusted heat.** Finished pancakes stack on a plate (in a low oven or under a towel if serving all at once); re-grease lightly every second or third pancake; nudge the heat down as the pan accumulates warmth. → *Expect:* colors converging batch by batch on uniform gold; the stack growing faster than the audience can be restrained.
7. **Serve warm with the toppings ceremony.** Butter melting between layers, syrup or the household's ruling toppings; pancakes wait for no one — the eaters assemble before the last flip. → *Expect:* a warm stack meeting an assembled table.

## Decision points

- Buttermilk upgrade: swap the milk for buttermilk and add ¼ teaspoon baking soda; tangier, fluffier, and the reason diner pancakes taste like that.
- Add-ins (blueberries, chocolate chips, banana): scatter onto the wet top surface *after* pouring, not into the batter bowl — even distribution and no purple batter.
- Thin-pancake cultures: crêpes and `uk` pancakes are a different, restful batter (no raising agent, more milk, swirled thin); this recipe's fluffy doctrine does not apply there, and both traditions are correct.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Pale and greasy or scorched outside, raw inside:** pan too cool or too hot respectively; the water-drop test and the sacrificial first pancake exist to catch this before pancake three.
- **F2 Rubbery, tough pancakes:** overmixed (see the most important sentence); there is no rescue for this batch beyond syrup and humility — next batch, stir ten fewer strokes.
- **F3 First side perfect, second side leopard-spotted:** excess butter pooling; the paper-towel wipe (step 3) is the fix, giving film instead of puddles.
- **F4 Batter thickened to paste while resting:** loosen with a splash of milk to pourable; batters with baking powder also fade after an hour — cook within the morning, which has never once been a problem.

## Verification

The batter went lumpy-and-rested, the bubbles called every flip, no pancake was pressed, the stack runs uniform gold, and the cook got the first pancake plus the last word on when they were ready.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk is the standard stove suite: hot pan, butter spatter, and an audience underfoot; `daily/food/fry-an-egg`'s spatter rules and the no-unattended-pan law govern. The rest is Sunday morning working exactly as intended.
