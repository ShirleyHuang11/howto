## Goal

An omelette is softly set, folded cleanly, and filled without raw egg or cold filling pockets.

## Preconditions

- Two or three eggs, salt, butter or oil, and a nonstick or well-seasoned pan.
- A fork or whisk, bowl, spatula, plate, and stovetop.
- Fillings are cooked or safe to eat as-is.

## Steps

1. **Warm the fillings first.** Heat mushrooms, onions, meat, or greens separately and keep cheese ready. → *Expect:* fillings are warm and not watery.
2. **Beat the eggs.** Whisk eggs with a pinch of salt until yolks and whites are fully combined. → *Expect:* the mixture pours as one yellow liquid with no clear streaks.
3. **Heat the pan gently.** Put the pan over medium heat, add butter, then drop to low once the butter foams. → *Expect:* butter is melted and quiet, not brown or smoking.
4. **Add eggs and pause.** Pour eggs into the pan and wait 10 seconds for the bottom to start setting. → *Expect:* a pale rim appears around the edge.
5. **Push and tilt.** Push set egg from the edge toward the center while tilting raw egg into the empty space. → *Expect:* curds form while loose egg keeps flowing to the pan surface.
6. **Stop while glossy.** Spread the top gently and stop stirring before it looks dry. → *Expect:* the omelette is mostly set with a slightly shiny top.
7. **Add filling to one side.** Place warm fillings and cheese on the half farthest from the handle. → *Expect:* filling sits in a low mound and the empty half can fold over it.
8. **Fold and slide.** Loosen the edge, fold the empty half over the filling, and slide onto a plate seam-side down. → *Expect:* the omelette lands in one folded piece.

## Decision points

- Eggs run faster than they set → keep heat low and continue push-and-tilt.
- Bottom browns before the top sets → remove pan from heat for 20 seconds, then finish with residual heat.
- Fillings are wet → drain or blot before adding.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scrambled texture:** detect many small broken curds, recover by stopping stirring earlier next time and folding what remains.
- **F2 Raw center:** detect clear egg when folding, recover by covering pan off heat for 30 seconds.
- **F3 Torn fold:** detect a split at the hinge, recover by sliding it open-face onto the plate.
- **F4 Cold filling:** detect cool cheese or vegetables, recover by warming fillings before the next omelette.

## Verification

The omelette is folded, holds together on the plate, and shows no clear raw egg when the thickest part is cut.

## Variations

- French style: use lower heat, smaller curds, and fold into thirds while glossy.
- Diner style: cook slightly firmer and brown the outside lightly.
- Dairy: add 1 teaspoon water or milk per egg for a softer texture.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot pans and undercooked egg. Wash hands after handling raw eggs, and avoid raw or runny egg for people who need fully cooked foods.
