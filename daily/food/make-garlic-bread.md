## Goal

Bread is toasted with a garlic-butter spread so it comes out either crisp-edged or soft and pull-apart, by deliberate choice.

## Preconditions

- Bread such as baguette, Italian loaf, sandwich rolls, or sturdy sliced bread.
- Soft butter or olive oil, garlic, salt, and optional parsley or cheese.
- Oven, toaster oven, or broiler-safe tray.

## Steps

1. **Choose crisp or soft.** [BRANCH: crisp open-face | soft wrapped loaf] use split bread for crisp edges, or a scored loaf wrapped in foil for soft centers. → *Expect:* bread is cut to match the texture you want.
2. **Make garlic butter.** Mash 4 tablespoons soft butter with 1 minced garlic clove, a pinch of salt, and parsley if using. → *Expect:* garlic is evenly spread through the butter.
3. **Spread to the edges.** Coat the cut sides thinly and evenly, reaching the corners. → *Expect:* no dry patches remain on the eating surface.
4. **Bake for control.** Heat oven or toaster oven to 375 F and bake 8 to 10 minutes for crisp bread or 10 to 12 minutes wrapped for soft bread. → *Expect:* butter melts in and the bread smells toasted.
5. **Broil only to finish.** If you want more browning, broil 30 to 90 seconds while watching constantly. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* garlic bread can go from golden to burnt in seconds under a broiler, so do not walk away. → *Expect:* edges are golden, not black.
6. **Rest briefly.** Move bread to a board and wait 1 minute before slicing. → *Expect:* butter stops bubbling and slices hold together.
7. **Serve hot.** Cut into portions and serve with sauce, soup, or salad. → *Expect:* bread is warm, garlicky, and not greasy on the bottom.

## Decision points

- Bread is very soft or thin → use lower heat and shorter time so it does not dry out.
- Garlic flavor is too sharp → cook the garlic in butter for 1 minute before spreading next time.
- Want cheese → add grated parmesan before baking or mozzarella only for the final few minutes.
- Feeding people who dislike raw garlic bite → use garlic powder or roasted garlic.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Burnt top:** detect black garlic or bitter smell; recover by scraping off the darkest bits or cutting away the top.
- **F2 Soggy bottom:** detect butter pooling under bread; recover by returning it cut-side up to the oven for 2 minutes.
- **F3 Dry bread:** detect hard, pale slices; recover by brushing lightly with melted butter and warming wrapped in foil.
- **F4 Uneven garlic:** detect one harsh bite and one plain bite; recover by mixing the spread longer before applying.

## Verification

The bread is hot and evenly seasoned, with the chosen texture: crisp browned edges for open-face bread or soft buttery centers for wrapped bread.

## Variations

- Olive oil version: use 3 tablespoons olive oil instead of butter and add a pinch more salt.
- Roasted garlic: mash several roasted cloves into butter for sweeter flavor.
- Air fryer: cook at 350 F for 3 to 5 minutes, checking early.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot trays, knives, and fast broiler browning. Use oven mitts and keep parchment away from direct broiler heat.
