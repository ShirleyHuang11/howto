## Goal

Inspect a used car enough to decide whether to walk away, negotiate, or pay for a mechanic pre-purchase inspection.

## Preconditions

- You can see the car in daylight on dry ground.
- The seller allows a test drive and inspection of documents, VIN plates, body, engine bay, tires, and interior.
- You have a flashlight, paper towel, tire tread gauge if available, and a phone for photos.
- You know the market value and common problems for the model.

## Steps

1. **Match identity documents.** Compare the VIN on the dashboard, door jamb, title or registration, listing, and history report. → *Expect:* all VIN records match exactly.
2. **Review history and title status.** Check accident history, branded title, mileage records, ownership gaps, recalls, and service records. → *Expect:* the story is consistent with the odometer and seller claims.
3. **Inspect panels and paint.** Look for uneven gaps, overspray, mismatched paint, rust, cracked glass, water stains, and repaired structural areas. → *Expect:* body condition matches the price and history.
4. **Check tires and brakes visually.** Look for uneven tread wear, old date codes, mismatched tires, curb damage, and visible brake rotor scoring. → *Expect:* tire and brake condition does not hide immediate major cost.
5. **Open the hood cold.** [BRANCH: cold engine | already warm] prefer cold start; check oil, coolant, belts, hoses, battery, leaks, and smells before running. → *Expect:* fluids look plausible and no fresh leak is obvious.
6. **Start and observe.** Watch for smoke, rough idle, warning lights, delayed engagement, unusual exhaust smell, and overheating. → *Expect:* warning lights turn off and the idle stabilizes.
7. **Test drive deliberately.** Drive varied roads and test steering, braking, acceleration, transmission shifts, suspension noise, cruise, climate, and electronics. → *Expect:* the car behaves consistently under normal use.
8. **Book a mechanic inspection.** If still interested, arrange an independent pre-purchase inspection before paying. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* buying without title and mechanical checks can leave you with unrecoverable repair costs. → *Expect:* a mechanic can inspect or you decide to walk away.

## Decision points

- VINs do not match → walk away unless a legitimate clerical error is documented by authorities.
- Seller refuses independent inspection → treat it as high risk and leave.
- Fluid is milky, burnt, empty, or contaminated → require mechanic review before any offer.
- Airbag, ABS, check engine, or battery warning stays on → diagnose before negotiating.
- Rust affects frame or suspension mounts → avoid unless a specialist confirms it is repairable.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Washed engine bay:** detect by unusually clean wet engine hiding leaks, recover by inspecting after a drive and asking for service records.
- **F2 Rolled-back mileage concern:** detect by history mileage conflicts or worn interior, recover by checking official records and walking away if unresolved.
- **F3 Flood damage:** detect by musty smell, silt, corrosion under seats, or electrical issues, recover by avoiding the purchase.
- **F4 Missed hidden defect:** detect by uncertainty after your inspection, recover by paying for a mechanic pre-purchase inspection.

## Verification

VIN, title story, visible condition, cold start, test drive, and mechanic inspection plan all support either a walk-away or a justified offer.

## Variations

- Hybrid or electric vehicle: check battery health reports, charging behavior, and high-voltage warning messages.
- Performance car: inspect modifications, tire wear, brake heat damage, and service intervals more closely.
- Older car: expect wear, but prioritize rust, title, safety systems, and parts availability.

## Safety & privacy

Meet in a safe public place and do not crawl under a car supported only by a jack. Vehicle histories and documents contain personal data, so photograph only what you need.
