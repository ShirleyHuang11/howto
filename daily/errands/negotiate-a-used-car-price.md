## Goal

Negotiate a used car price using market value, inspection findings, and an out-the-door number you are willing to pay.

## Preconditions

- You know the vehicle year, trim, mileage, condition, title status, and comparable local listings.
- You have test-driven the car and reviewed its history report or VIN record.
- You know your maximum budget including taxes, fees, registration, repairs, and insurance.
- You are willing to leave if the deal does not meet your conditions.

## Steps

1. **Research fair value.** Compare guide values, sold listings where available, dealer listings, private listings, mileage, options, and title status. → *Expect:* you have a realistic price range, not one number.
2. **List leverage items.** Note tires, brakes, fluids, warning lights, cosmetic damage, missing keys, accident history, overdue services, and inspection findings. → *Expect:* every price objection is tied to evidence.
3. **Decide your walk-away number.** Include purchase price, taxes, dealer fees, registration, immediate repairs, insurance, and financing cost. → *Expect:* you know the highest out-the-door price you will accept.
4. **Ask for the seller's best basis.** Have the seller explain their price and included fees before making your offer. → *Expect:* hidden costs or confidence in pricing become visible.
5. **Make a calm lower offer.** [BRANCH: private seller | dealer] start below your target with evidence, while staying polite and specific. → *Expect:* the seller counters, accepts, or refuses without confusion.
6. **Negotiate out-the-door.** Focus on the final total including taxes, document fees, add-ons, delivery, registration, and required repairs. → *Expect:* the number you compare is the total amount due.
7. **Use inspection results carefully.** Ask for a price reduction, repair before sale, or credit for mechanic-confirmed issues. → *Expect:* the seller addresses concrete defects rather than vague complaints.
8. **Pause before committing.** Leave or take time if the seller pressures you, changes terms, or asks for a rushed deposit. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* signing purchase documents or paying a nonrefundable deposit can lock the deal, so verify title, price, and conditions first. → *Expect:* any agreement is written and matches your walk-away number.

## Decision points

- The seller refuses inspection → lower your offer heavily or walk away.
- The title is branded, missing, or not in the seller's name → do not proceed without resolving title risk.
- Dealer adds mandatory extras → include them in the out-the-door price or reject the deal.
- Financing changes the price → compare the cash price and financed total separately.
- The seller meets your number → confirm terms in writing before payment.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Anchored too high:** detect by negotiating from asking price only, recover by returning to comparable values and repair costs.
- **F2 Hidden fees:** detect by final paperwork exceeding the negotiated price, recover by asking for an itemized out-the-door worksheet.
- **F3 Emotional overbid:** detect by exceeding your walk-away number, recover by taking a break and reviewing alternatives.
- **F4 Inspection ignored:** detect by defects not reflected in price or repair terms, recover by restating repair estimates or walking away.

## Verification

The agreed written out-the-door price is at or below your maximum and accounts for title status, condition, fees, and known repairs.

## Variations

- Certified used car: warranty value may justify some premium, but compare coverage details.
- Private sale: taxes and registration may be paid later, so include them in your true total.
- Auction or no-haggle store: negotiation may be limited to fees, trade value, or walking away.

## Safety & privacy

Negotiation exposes financial limits and identity details if financing is involved. Keep your walk-away number private and do not pay until title and seller identity are verified.
