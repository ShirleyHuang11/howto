## Goal

Usable things you no longer need reach an organization that can actually use them, in the condition and manner that helps rather than burdens, and the donate-pile leaves your life for good.

## Preconditions

- A gathered donate pile (`daily/home/organize-a-closet` step 6 feeds this errand) that passes the honesty test: would you give this to a friend? Donation is not a bin with extra steps; stained, broken, and incomplete items burden charities with disposal costs.
- A chosen destination matched to the goods: charity shops (clothes, books, homewares), shelters (specific needs lists), food banks (unopened, in-date food), textile banks (worn-out fabric, which is recycling rather than donation), community groups and give-away boards (furniture, oddities).

## Steps

1. **Sort by destination, not just by "donate".** Wearable clothes to the shop pile; ragged textiles to the textile bank pile; books, kitchenware, toys per the venue's accepted list (posted on their sites; many refuse electronics, cots, helmets, and mattresses for safety and law). → *Expect:* piles matched to venues that accept them, and a small honest trash pile that stops pretending.
2. **Prepare the goods like they matter.** Clothes washed (`daily/home/do-the-laundry`) and folded; sets complete and bagged together (game pieces counted, cables with their device); pockets checked (`daily/home/organize-a-closet`'s pocket rule: keys and cards retire with coats constantly). → *Expect:* bags a stranger would be pleased to open.
3. **Check the venue's intake mode before hauling.** Shop drop-off hours, donation-bin locations, or booked collections for furniture; food banks post current-needs lists (they drown in pasta and starve for toiletries, typically). → *Expect:* a confirmed destination, open hours, and for big items a booking.
4. **Deliver during staffed hours where possible.** Hand bags to staff rather than stacking them at a closed door: doorstep dumping is fly-tipping in law and in effect, whatever the intention. → *Expect:* goods handed over, and for taxed jurisdictions a receipt where deductions apply (ask; `us` itemizers especially).
5. **Big furniture and appliances: let collectors come.** Charity furniture services collect free or cheaply and refuse honestly (fire-safety labels on sofas matter legally in some countries); give-away boards move the rest with a "collection only" post and a doorstep handover. → *Expect:* the item leaving through a door you did not carry it through alone.
6. **Close the loop the same day.** The car unloads at the venue, not back into the garage; the give-away post gets deleted once collected; the receipt files where receipts live. → *Expect:* the donate pile extinct, not migrated.

## Decision points

- Sell vs donate: items with real resale value and your time's price decide it (`shopping/sell-an-item-online` for the sell path); the mixed strategy (sell the two valuable things, donate the bag) beats letting the whole pile wait on eBay ambitions.
- Sentimental donations (a late relative's clothes): charities handle these with practiced grace, and keeping one representative item often unlocks releasing the rest.
- Disaster and appeal donations: send what the appeal lists, or money; spontaneous goods convoys mostly create sorting burdens at the far end.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Venue refused items:** their list is law: re-route refused goods to the right stream (textile bank, electronics recycling `daily/errands/recycle-electronics`, or the honest bin), not their doorstep after hours.
- **F2 The bag rode in the car trunk for a month:** the same-day rule failed; put the venue on the next grocery route (`daily/errands/buy-groceries` chains errands well) and treat the drop as the trip's first stop, not its maybe.
- **F3 Something donated is missed later:** the honesty test's price, paid rarely; charity shops occasionally still have it that week, and the lesson calibrates the next purge rather than souring it.
- **F4 Give-away no-shows:** endemic; state "first collector wins, doorstep pickup, I won't hold items" in the post and relist without hurt feelings.

## Verification

Every pile reached a venue that accepts its kind, in cleaned complete condition, during staffed hours or by booked collection, with receipts where relevant, and by nightfall the house, the car, and the hallway hold none of it.

## Variations

- Community fridges, little free libraries, and Buy Nothing groups make hyper-local donation a walk rather than a drive.
- Workplace collections (coat drives, food drives) compress steps 3 and 4 into a lobby box with a deadline.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The pocket-and-data rule is the one with teeth: check pockets, wipe donated phones and laptops to factory state (`digital/export-your-data` first), and shred documents found in desk donations rather than gifting your identity along.
