## Goal

Evaluate a car on a varied route without sales pressure and decide whether it deserves further consideration.

## Preconditions

- You have a valid license and permission from the seller or dealer.
- Insurance or dealer coverage for the test drive is clear.
- You know the asking price, trim, mileage, and key options.
- You can drive a route with parking, low speed streets, rough pavement, turns, and highway or faster roads.

## Steps

1. **Request a cold start.** Ask that the car not be warmed up before you arrive, then start it yourself if allowed. → *Expect:* the engine starts cleanly without smoke, long cranking, or warning lights.
2. **Set the route.** [BRANCH: dealer route | your route] include stop-and-go traffic, turns, bumps, braking, parking, and higher-speed driving if allowed. → *Expect:* the seller agrees to enough variety for evaluation.
3. **Check controls before moving.** Adjust seat, mirrors, steering wheel, climate, visibility, infotainment basics, and driver assists. → *Expect:* you can sit comfortably and see safely.
4. **Listen at low speed.** Drive slowly with the radio off and note clunks, squeaks, steering play, brake noise, or transmission hesitation. → *Expect:* the car moves smoothly without obvious mechanical complaints.
5. **Test acceleration and braking.** Accelerate moderately, brake progressively, and check whether the car pulls, shudders, or smells hot. → *Expect:* power and braking feel predictable.
6. **Drive at speed.** On a safe road, hold steady speed and feel for vibration, wind noise, tracking issues, or gear hunting. → *Expect:* the car tracks straight and feels settled.
7. **Park and inspect again.** Try reverse, tight turns, parking sensors, camera, and look underneath for new drips. → *Expect:* parking systems work and no fresh leak appears.
8. **Leave without pressure.** Tell the seller you need time to review notes and compare options. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not place a nonrefundable deposit from test-drive excitement until price, inspection, title, and financing are checked. → *Expect:* you leave with notes, not an impulse commitment.

## Decision points

- The seller refuses a meaningful drive → treat it as a warning and walk away.
- Warning lights remain on → require diagnosis before negotiating.
- The car was warmed before arrival → ask for maintenance records and consider a second cold visit.
- You feel unsafe or pressured → return immediately and end the drive.
- You like the car → schedule an independent pre-purchase inspection before buying.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sales pressure:** detect by urgent discount or deposit demand, recover by leaving and deciding later.
- **F2 Hidden mechanical noise:** detect by radio on or seller talking over sounds, recover by turning audio off and asking for quiet.
- **F3 Route too short:** detect by only a loop around the block, recover by requesting more varied roads or declining.
- **F4 Unsafe vehicle:** detect by poor brakes, steering, tires, or warning lights, recover by stopping the test and returning carefully.

## Verification

You have driven the car on varied roads, checked cold-start behavior, and recorded any concerns before discussing purchase terms.

## Variations

- Private sale: confirm insurance and avoid leaving valuables or original documents with the seller.
- Electric vehicle: test charging port, range estimate, regenerative braking, and battery health screen.
- Manual transmission: check clutch engagement, gear synchros, hill starts, and reverse.

## Safety & privacy

Drive legally and keep control of your license and keys. Do not let a seller rush you into deposits, financing, or personal-data forms before you decide.
