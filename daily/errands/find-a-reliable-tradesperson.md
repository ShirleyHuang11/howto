## Goal

Select a reliable tradesperson for home work by comparing scope, reputation, licensing where relevant, schedule, and payment terms before money changes hands.

## Preconditions

- A clear description of the problem or project.
- Photos, measurements, model numbers, or access details that help quote the job.
- Budget range and timing constraints.
- Knowledge of whether the trade is licensed in your area.

## Steps

1. **Define the job.** Write the issue, location, desired result, and known constraints. → *Expect:* every contractor hears the same scope.
2. **Collect candidate names.** Ask neighbors, building managers, local groups, or trade directories. → *Expect:* at least five possible contacts before filtering.
3. **Screen basics.** Check website, reviews, years in business, photos of similar work, and contact information. → *Expect:* obvious bad fits are removed.
4. **Check license and insurance where relevant.** Search the local license database or ask for license and insurance proof. → *Expect:* regulated trades can be matched to a current credential.
5. **Request three quotes.** Send the same scope and photos to at least three candidates. → *Expect:* responses can be compared on the same job.
6. **Ask for written scope.** Require materials, labor, cleanup, exclusions, timeline, and warranty in writing. → *Expect:* quote details are not just a single price.
7. **Compare more than price.** Weigh responsiveness, clarity, reviews, schedule, and deposit terms. → *Expect:* the best choice is defensible, not only cheapest.
8. **Limit deposits.** Avoid large upfront payments before materials or work begin. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* money sent by cash, wire, or instant transfer may be hard to recover. → *Expect:* payment schedule matches visible progress or material purchase.
9. **Confirm access and logistics.** Agree on date, arrival window, parking, pets, shutoffs, and cleanup. → *Expect:* both sides know how the workday will run.
10. **Keep records.** Save messages, quote, license proof, receipts, and photos before work starts. → *Expect:* there is a paper trail if the job changes.

## Decision points

- If the job involves electrical, gas, structural, or major plumbing → prioritize licensing, permits, and insurance over speed.
- If one quote is far lower → ask what is excluded before accepting.
- If a contractor pushes immediate cash payment → pause and get another quote.
- If emergency work is needed → still get the written price and scope before approval.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No written quote:** detect verbal-only pricing → ask for text or email before scheduling.
- **F2 Bad license match:** detect expired, wrong name, or wrong trade → remove the candidate from consideration.
- **F3 Scope creep:** detect added work without price → stop and request a written change order.
- **F4 Deposit pressure:** detect demand for most of the price upfront → offer a smaller materials deposit or choose another provider.

## Verification

You have chosen one tradesperson with a written scope, checked reputation and license where relevant, compared at least three quotes or documented why fewer were available, and agreed to payment terms.

## Variations

- Small handyman task: licensing may not apply, but written scope and review checks still matter.
- Condo or rental: get building or landlord approval before scheduling.
- Insurance claim: use insurer requirements for estimates, photos, and approved vendors.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from home access, money, and poor workmanship. Do not leave valuables exposed, do not pay large cash deposits, and keep all agreements in writing.
