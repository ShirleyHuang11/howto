## Goal

Sell your car privately with an honest listing, safer buyer screening, scam-aware payment, and proper title transfer.

## Preconditions

- You have the title or payoff instructions, registration, maintenance records, spare keys, and lien information if any.
- The car is clean enough to photograph and show.
- You know the market value, loan payoff, and minimum acceptable price.
- You can meet buyers in a public place or bank during daylight.

## Steps

1. **Clean and document the car.** Wash it, remove personal items, gather service records, title status, manuals, keys, and take clear photos. → *Expect:* the car can be represented accurately.
2. **Price with evidence.** Compare similar mileage, trim, condition, accident history, and local listings. → *Expect:* your asking price has a defensible range.
3. **Write an honest listing.** Include VIN if comfortable, mileage, title status, maintenance, defects, tire condition, features, and reason for sale. → *Expect:* serious buyers can pre-screen the car.
4. **Screen inquiries.** [BRANCH: serious buyer | suspicious buyer] answer normal questions, reject overpayment, shipping-agent, code-verification, and rushed-payment messages. → *Expect:* you schedule only plausible buyers.
5. **Meet safely.** Use a public lot, police exchange zone, or bank, bring another person if possible, and verify license before any test drive. → *Expect:* the meeting location and driver identity feel controlled.
6. **Handle test drives.** Ride along or set clear terms, keep possession of your keys and documents, and avoid leaving the buyer alone with the car. → *Expect:* the car returns and no documents are missing.
7. **Use scam-safe payment.** Prefer verified bank cashier process, in-bank transfer, or cleared electronic payment before releasing title and keys. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* signing over title before verified payment can permanently lose the car. → *Expect:* funds are verified by your bank, not just a screenshot or email.
8. **Transfer title and notify authorities.** Complete bill of sale, odometer statement, lien release if needed, plates, release of liability, and registration cancellation as local rules require. → *Expect:* ownership and liability are no longer tied to you.

## Decision points

- The car has a lien → get lender payoff and title release instructions before listing as ready to sell.
- Buyer wants shipping or overpayment refund → refuse because this is a common scam pattern.
- Buyer wants to pay by personal check → wait until funds fully clear before transfer.
- Test drive request feels unsafe → decline or meet at a police exchange location.
- Price negotiation is reasonable → use documented defects and market value, not urgency.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fake payment:** detect by overpayment, third-party pickup, screenshots, or pressure before bank verification, recover by stopping the sale.
- **F2 Title problem:** detect by missing title, lien, name mismatch, or error, recover by replacing or correcting title before accepting payment.
- **F3 Post-sale liability:** detect by tickets, tolls, or notices after sale, recover by filing release of liability and keeping bill of sale copies.
- **F4 Unsafe meeting:** detect by buyer refusing public meeting or ID check, recover by canceling and blocking the buyer.

## Verification

Payment is verified, title transfer paperwork is complete, release of liability is filed if required, and copies of sale documents are saved.

## Variations

- Dealer instant offer: lower price may be worth reduced meeting and payment risk.
- Loan payoff sale: meet at the lender or follow escrow-style payoff instructions.
- Plates stay with seller: remove plates before the buyer leaves if local law requires it.

## Safety & privacy

Private sales carry scam, theft, and identity risks. Meet in daylight, verify payment through your own bank, redact unneeded document data, and do not release title early.
