## Goal

Complete a blood donation appointment safely and know what aftercare and deferral rules apply.

## Preconditions

- You meet the donation organization's age, weight, health, travel, medication, and interval rules.
- You have photo identification and any donor card or appointment confirmation.
- You have eaten a real meal and can drink water before and after donating.
- You can avoid strenuous exercise, alcohol, and heavy lifting for the rest of the day.

## Steps

1. **Check eligibility before booking.** Review the donor center's current rules for age, weight, illness, pregnancy, tattoos, travel, medications, and prior donations. → *Expect:* no obvious deferral applies, or you know what to ask about.
2. **Schedule the appointment.** [BRANCH: whole blood | platelets or plasma] choose the donation type, time, and location you can complete without rushing. → *Expect:* you have a confirmed slot and estimated visit length.
3. **Prepare your body.** Hydrate well, eat iron-rich food, sleep enough, and avoid arriving hungry. → *Expect:* you feel steady and have urinated normally before leaving.
4. **Check in honestly.** Present ID and answer the health history questions truthfully and privately. → *Expect:* staff either clears you or explains a deferral.
5. **Complete the mini-physical.** Let staff check temperature, pulse, blood pressure, and hemoglobin. → *Expect:* your readings pass the center's thresholds or donation stops.
6. **Donate while still.** Sit or lie back, keep the arm relaxed, and tell staff immediately if you feel dizzy, hot, nauseated, or anxious. → *Expect:* blood flows into the collection bag while staff monitors you.
7. **Recover before leaving.** Keep the bandage on, drink fluids, eat the snack, and sit until staff says you can stand. → *Expect:* you can stand and walk without lightheadedness.
8. **Follow aftercare.** Avoid heavy lifting with that arm, intense exercise, and alcohol for the advised period. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once donated blood leaves your body, you cannot undo the temporary fluid and iron loss. → *Expect:* the puncture site stays dry and you feel normal.

## Decision points

- You feel sick, feverish, or exposed to infection → reschedule instead of donating.
- You are deferred → ask whether it is temporary, permanent, and what date you may return.
- You faint or nearly faint → stay lying down and let staff manage recovery before leaving.
- You take medication → disclose it; do not decide privately that it is irrelevant.
- You plan hard training or manual work that day → choose another date.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Low hemoglobin:** detect by failed finger-stick or screening result, recover by asking when to retry and discussing iron intake with a clinician if frequent.
- **F2 Vasovagal reaction:** detect by sweating, nausea, tunnel vision, or faintness, recover by lying flat, elevating legs, and following staff instructions.
- **F3 Bleeding bandage:** detect by blood soaking through gauze, recover by applying firm pressure and raising the arm.
- **F4 Post-donation weakness:** detect by dizziness after leaving, recover by sitting down, drinking fluids, eating, and calling the center if it persists.

## Verification

You have completed check-in, screening, donation or documented deferral, and can leave the site alert with aftercare instructions.

## Variations

- Platelet donation: expect a longer appointment and machine return cycles.
- Mobile drive: bring layers because temporary sites can be colder or warmer than clinics.
- First-time donor: ask staff to explain each step before needle placement.

## Safety & privacy

Health history is sensitive medical information. Answer privately and honestly, and treat dizziness or bleeding as a reason to stop and get staff help.
