## Goal

Have a suit adjusted so the jacket and trousers fit cleanly for your body and intended shoes.

## Preconditions

- You have the suit, shirt, belt or suspenders, and shoes you plan to wear with it.
- The suit is clean enough for a tailor to handle.
- You know the event date and how much mobility you need.
- You can return for pickup and a final check.

## Steps

1. **Wear the right base layers.** Put on the dress shirt, shoes, and belt or suspenders that match the suit use. → *Expect:* the tailor measures the suit in the same setup you will wear.
2. **State the fit goal.** Tell the tailor whether you want classic, slim, conservative business, wedding, or comfort-first fit. → *Expect:* the tailor understands the target silhouette and deadline.
3. **Ask what is adjustable.** Confirm likely changes such as trouser hem, waist, seat, taper, sleeve length, jacket waist, collar roll, and button stance limits. → *Expect:* the tailor explains what can and cannot be altered cleanly.
4. **Stand naturally for measurements.** Keep feet flat, arms relaxed, and posture normal while the tailor measures and marks. → *Expect:* pins or chalk reflect your usual stance rather than a posed one.
5. **Test movement while pinned.** Sit, reach forward, button the jacket, put hands in pockets, and walk. → *Expect:* pinned fabric looks clean without pulling, twisting, or restricting normal movement.
6. **Approve the alteration plan.** [BRANCH: simple hem | major tailoring] confirm each change, cost, and pickup date before leaving. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* cutting fabric cannot be fully undone, so confirm length and taper before work starts. → *Expect:* the ticket lists the agreed alterations.
7. **Pick up and re-check.** Try on the suit at pickup with the same shoes and shirt, not over casual clothes. → *Expect:* hems, sleeves, waist, and jacket shape match the pinned plan.
8. **Request corrections immediately.** Point out puckers, uneven hems, tightness, or loose areas before taking the suit away. → *Expect:* the tailor either fixes it on the spot or writes a follow-up ticket.

## Decision points

- Jacket shoulders are too wide or narrow → consider a different suit because shoulder work is expensive and risky.
- Sleeves have functioning buttonholes → shortening may be limited or require shoulder work.
- Trousers need more room than seam allowance allows → ask whether fabric can be let out safely.
- The event is soon → choose only high-impact alterations the tailor can finish in time.
- The quote approaches replacement cost → compare against buying a better-fitting suit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overly tight fit:** detect by pulling across buttons, thighs, or seat, recover by asking to let seams out or reduce taper.
- **F2 Uneven hem:** detect by one trouser break sitting lower, recover by trying with event shoes and having it re-marked.
- **F3 Missed deadline:** detect by pickup date after your event, recover by asking for partial priority work or another tailor.
- **F4 Permanent cut too short:** detect by sleeves or hems above the agreed length, recover only if extra fabric remains in the seam allowance.

## Verification

The suit lies smooth while standing, permits sitting and walking, and the tailor ticket is closed with no unresolved fit issue.

## Variations

- Off-the-rack suit: expect hems, waist, and sleeves to be the most common changes.
- Vintage suit: fabric may be fragile and seam allowances may be larger or inconsistent.
- Formalwear rental: alterations may be limited to temporary hems and basic adjustments.

## Safety & privacy

Pins are sharp and fitting rooms involve body measurements. Wear appropriate underlayers and speak up if pinning or fit feels unsafe or intrusive.
