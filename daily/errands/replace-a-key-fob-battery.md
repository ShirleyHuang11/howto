## Goal

Replace a weak key fob battery while preserving the case, buttons, and battery orientation.

## Preconditions

- You have the key fob, a small flat tool or coin, and a clean table.
- A replacement coin-cell battery is available or can be bought after reading the old battery type.
- Hands are dry and small parts can be kept away from children and pets.
- The vehicle is nearby for testing after reassembly.

## Steps

1. **Remove the mechanical key if present.** Press the release and slide the key blade out of the fob. → *Expect:* the hidden seam or pry slot is easier to access.
2. **Find the case seam.** Look for a notch, slot, or split line instead of prying at a random edge. → *Expect:* you know where the case is designed to open.
3. **Pry the case gently.** Twist a coin or plastic pry tool in the slot while supporting both halves. → *Expect:* the case begins to separate without cracking.
4. **Open over the table.** Lift the cover slowly so buttons, gasket, or circuit board pieces do not fall. → *Expect:* the inside layout remains in place.
5. **Note the battery type.** Read the printed code, such as CR2032 or CR2025, before removing it. → *Expect:* the replacement type is confirmed.
6. **Record the orientation.** Check whether the plus side faces up or down. → *Expect:* you know exactly which face of the new battery should be visible.
7. **Swap the battery.** Slide the old cell out and insert the new one with the same orientation. → *Expect:* the battery sits flat under the contact tabs.
8. **Snap the case shut.** Align the halves and press around the edges until clips seat. → *Expect:* the seam is even and no buttons are trapped.
9. **Reinstall the mechanical key.** Slide it back until it clicks. → *Expect:* the key blade is locked into the fob.
10. **Test close and at range.** Press lock or unlock near the car, then from several parking spaces away. → *Expect:* the car responds reliably at both distances.

## Decision points

- The battery type is unreadable → search by fob model or vehicle year before buying a cell.
- The case will not open → look for a hidden screw under the key blade or logo cover.
- A rubber gasket falls out → reseat it in its groove before closing the case.
- The fob still does not work → try the second fob or check whether re-sync instructions are required.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong battery type:** detect by a cell that is too thick, loose, or not listed on the old cell → recover by installing the exact printed type.
- **F2 Reversed battery:** detect by no response after replacement → recover by reopening and matching the plus and minus orientation.
- **F3 Cracked case clip:** detect by a gap that will not close → recover with a replacement shell before the circuit board is exposed to moisture.
- **F4 Weak range remains:** detect by working only beside the door → recover by cleaning contacts gently or having the fob tested.

## Verification

The fob locks or unlocks the vehicle from normal walking distance and the case is fully closed with all buttons moving freely.

## Variations

- Screw-back fob: remove the screw first and keep it in a small dish.
- Rechargeable smart key: confirm it uses a replaceable coin cell before prying.
- Push-button start vehicle: test both remote buttons and in-car start detection.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but coin-cell batteries are dangerous if swallowed. Keep old and new cells away from children, avoid shorting the battery with metal tools, and do not expose the circuit board to liquid.
