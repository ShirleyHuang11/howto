## Goal

Handle a minor car accident safely, document the facts, and start police or insurance reporting when needed.

## Preconditions

- The crash appears minor and there is no immediate life-threatening injury.
- You can move to a safe place or wait safely for help.
- You have a phone, driver's license, vehicle registration, and insurance information if available.

## Steps

1. **Check for injuries and danger.** Look for pain, bleeding, smoke, fire, traffic exposure, or leaking fuel. → *Expect:* urgent medical or fire risks are identified.
2. **Call emergency services if anyone is hurt or danger remains.** Give location, lane, vehicle count, and hazards. → *Expect:* responders are dispatched when needed.
3. **Move to safety if vehicles are drivable.** Use hazard lights and pull to a shoulder, parking lot, or side street. → *Expect:* people are away from moving traffic.
4. **Stay at the scene.** Do not leave until information is exchanged and any required report is handled. → *Expect:* you remain reachable and legally present.
5. **Exchange information.** Share name, phone, license plate, driver's license details as locally required, insurance company, and policy number. → *Expect:* each driver has enough information to file a claim.
6. **Document with photos.** Photograph vehicle positions, damage, plates, road signs, skid marks, weather, and insurance cards if offered. → *Expect:* factual evidence is saved.
7. **Get witness and police details.** Ask witnesses for contact information and record the responding officer or report number if police attend. → *Expect:* independent references are available.
8. **Avoid admitting fault.** Stick to facts like direction, lane, signals, and what happened. → *Expect:* no speculative or blame statement is made.
9. **Notify your insurer.** Report the accident promptly, even if damage seems small. → *Expect:* the insurer gives claim instructions.

## Decision points

- Injury, impaired driver, hit-and-run, blocked traffic, public property damage, or major damage → call police or emergency services.
- Other driver refuses to share information → call police and record plate, vehicle description, and location.
- You feel unsafe interacting → stay in your locked vehicle if safe and wait for police.
- Local law requires a report above a damage threshold → file the required report by the deadline.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Delayed injury symptoms:** detect pain, headache, dizziness, or stiffness later → seek medical care and update the claim.
- **F2 Missing information:** detect incomplete driver or policy details → use photos, plate number, and police report to help the insurer.
- **F3 Roadside argument:** detect escalating behavior → disengage, move to safety, and call police.
- **F4 Vehicle unsafe to drive:** detect leaking fluid, airbag deployment, rubbing tire, or warning lights → call roadside assistance or a tow.

## Verification

Everyone is safe, required emergency or police calls are made, driver and insurance information is exchanged, photos are saved, and your insurer has been notified.

## Variations

- Rental car: contact the rental company and follow its accident-report instructions.
- Company vehicle: notify the fleet or employer contact before authorizing repairs.
- No cell service: move only as far as needed to call, or ask a passerby to call emergency services.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from roadside traffic and later medical issues. Share required accident information, but do not hand over unnecessary personal documents or post accident details publicly.
