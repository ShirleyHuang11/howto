## Goal

Clean a car interior so trash, dust, crumbs, fingerprints, and odors are reduced without damaging electronics or trim.

## Preconditions

- The car is parked in a safe, ventilated place with doors able to open fully.
- You have trash bags, a vacuum, microfiber cloths, interior cleaner, glass cleaner, and a soft brush.
- Personal papers and valuables can be sorted privately before cleaning.
- The engine is off and infotainment screens are cool.

## Steps

1. **Declutter first.** Remove trash, receipts, cups, toys, and loose gear from seats, pockets, and the floor. → *Expect:* only items that belong in the car remain.
2. **Sort personal items.** Put registration, insurance cards, keys, and electronics somewhere safe before using cleaners. → *Expect:* documents and devices are not mixed with trash.
3. **Pull out mats.** Shake mats away from the car and set them aside for vacuuming or washing. → *Expect:* floor carpet and mat surfaces are exposed.
4. **Vacuum high to low.** Vacuum seats, console gaps, floor, under pedals, and trunk areas before wiping surfaces. → *Expect:* crumbs and grit are removed from fabric and crevices.
5. **Brush the vents.** Use a soft brush while vacuuming near the vent slats. → *Expect:* dust lifts from vents without bending slats.
6. **Wipe hard surfaces.** Spray cleaner onto a cloth, not directly onto screens or switches, then wipe dash, console, handles, and cup holders. → *Expect:* surfaces look even and not wet.
7. **Clean buttons and seams.** Wrap the cloth over a fingertip or soft brush for knobs, seams, and cup holder edges. → *Expect:* sticky spots are gone without liquid pooling.
8. **Treat upholstery lightly.** [BRANCH: cloth | leather | vinyl] use the material-appropriate cleaner and avoid soaking seams. → *Expect:* stains lift without dark wet patches.
9. **Clean glass last.** Spray glass cleaner on a cloth and wipe inside windows, mirrors, and windshield. → *Expect:* fingerprints and haze are removed after other dust is finished.
10. **Air out and replace mats.** Let damp areas dry with doors open, then reinstall dry mats securely. → *Expect:* mats are clipped or flat and pedals move freely.

## Decision points

- Electronics or screens are dusty → use a barely damp microfiber cloth and no direct spray.
- A spill smells sour or moldy → remove mats and dry the area fully before closing the car.
- Pet hair remains after vacuuming → use a rubber brush or damp glove before vacuuming again.
- A cleaner changes the surface color → stop and rinse the cloth with water before continuing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cleaner pooled in controls:** detect by wet edges around buttons → recover by blotting immediately and letting the area dry before powering accessories.
- **F2 Streaky glass:** detect by haze at an angle → recover with a clean dry microfiber towel and smaller amounts of cleaner.
- **F3 Overwet upholstery:** detect by dark damp patches or squish under pressure → recover by blotting, ventilating, and using a fan if available.
- **F4 Loose floor mat:** detect by mat sliding toward pedals → recover by clipping it in or removing it until it can be secured.

## Verification

The cabin has no loose trash, no wet electronics, clear interior glass, dry secured mats, and pedals that move without obstruction.

## Variations

- Touchscreen-heavy car: use screen-safe cleaner or plain microfiber only.
- Rubber floor mats: wash outside the car and reinstall only when fully dry.
- Shared vehicle: return seat, mirror, and safety items to the expected positions.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but cleaners can irritate skin and liquid can damage electronics. Ventilate the car, never soak switches or screens, and remove private documents before vacuuming at a public station.
