## Goal

Take a dog outside for exercise, toileting, and enrichment while keeping the dog, other people, and other animals safe.

## Preconditions

- The dog has a fitted collar or harness, leash, and visible ID tag or microchip information.
- Poop bags, water for longer walks, and weather-appropriate gear are ready.
- You know any medical limits, heat sensitivity, reactivity, or leash laws that apply.
- The dog is healthy enough for the planned distance and surface.

## Steps

1. **Fit the gear.** Attach the leash to a secure collar or harness, check that the dog cannot back out, and confirm ID is on the dog. → *Expect:* the leash connection holds with gentle tension.
2. **Pack the basics.** Bring poop bags, keys, phone if needed, and water for heat, long routes, puppies, seniors, or flat-faced breeds. → *Expect:* you can clean up and handle delays without returning immediately.
3. **Check heat and pavement.** Touch the pavement with the back of your hand for several seconds and choose shade, grass, booties, or a shorter walk if it is too hot. → *Expect:* the route will not burn paws or overheat the dog.
4. **Start with attention.** Ask for a simple cue or pause at the door so the dog exits without bolting. → *Expect:* the first movement outside is controlled.
5. **Use loose-leash basics.** Keep the leash short enough for safety but slack when possible, reward the dog near your side, and stop or change direction when pulling hard begins. → *Expect:* the dog learns that slack leash movement keeps the walk going.
6. **Allow sniffing breaks.** Build in safe sniff stops away from traffic, yards, and hazards. → *Expect:* the dog gets mental enrichment without dragging you.
7. **Follow greeting rules.** [BRANCH: greeting allowed | greeting skipped] ask the other handler before dog-to-dog contact, keep leashes loose, and pass by if either dog stiffens, stares, barks, or pulls. → *Expect:* greetings happen only with mutual permission and calm bodies.
8. **Clean up promptly.** Pick up feces with a bag, tie it closed, and carry it to a trash bin. → *Expect:* the sidewalk or grass is left clean.
9. **Return and check the dog.** Offer water, check paws for cuts or debris, and remove gear after the dog is indoors. → *Expect:* the dog settles with no limping, coughing, or heat distress.

## Decision points

- If weather is hot, icy, smoky, or stormy → shorten the walk, choose indoor enrichment, or change the route.
- If an off-leash dog approaches → create distance, keep your dog close, and avoid wrapping the leash around your hand.
- If your dog is reactive → skip greetings and use distance, treats, and planned quiet routes.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dog pulls hard:** the leash stays tight and your arm is braced → stop, wait for slack, reward near you, and restart at a slower pace.
- **F2 Overheating:** heavy panting, drooling, weakness, or glassy eyes appear → move to shade, offer water, cool gradually, and seek veterinary help if signs persist.
- **F3 Paw injury:** limping, bleeding, or lifting a paw appears → end the walk, inspect gently, and call a vet for cuts, burns, or embedded objects.
- **F4 Unsafe greeting:** growling, stiff posture, or tangled leashes appear → separate calmly, move away, and skip further greetings.

## Verification

The dog returns home safely exercised or toileted, waste is disposed of, and no heat, paw, leash, or greeting problem remains unresolved.

## Variations

- `puppy`: keep walks short, avoid unknown dog-heavy areas until vaccines are current, and reward check-ins often.
- `senior-dog`: favor steady surfaces, shorter distances, and more sniff time.
- `night-walk`: use lights or reflective gear and choose well-lit crossings.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk comes from traffic, heat, leash injuries, bites, and lost pets. Follow local leash rules, keep identification on the dog, and do not let strangers or dogs greet without permission.
