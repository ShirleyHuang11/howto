## Goal

You hold a library card, can find and borrow materials (physical and digital), and return them on time — unlocking the most underused free institution in most towns.

## Preconditions

- A public library serving your area (residency usually defines eligibility).
- ID and proof of address for registration (library sites list exactly what counts).

## Steps

1. **Register once.** At the desk (or online-start): ID + address proof → a library card and a PIN/password. Ask the two questions that unlock everything: "What's my loan limit and period?" and "Which apps do you use for e-books/audiobooks?" → *Expect:* a card in hand, online account credentials, and the digital-app names written down.
2. **Find things via the catalog, not wandering (unless wandering is the point).** The catalog terminal/website/app: search title/author/topic → note the call number and shelf area, or the item's status (on loan → place a hold). → *Expect:* a call number in hand or a hold placed with a pickup notification promised.
3. **Read the shelf logic once and it works everywhere.** Fiction: alphabetical by author. Non-fiction: numbered by subject (Dewey-style) — the call number walks you to the shelf, the range labels on shelf-ends do the navigation. → *Expect:* the item found at its call number, or a librarian asked (they *enjoy* being asked; it's the job).
4. **Borrow at the desk or self-checkout kiosk.** Card + items, kiosk scans and prints/emails the due-date slip. → *Expect:* items on your account with known due dates — calendar them or turn on the library's email/app reminders now.
5. **Use the digital branch from your sofa.** Install the apps from step 1 (Libby/OverDrive-class), sign in with the card number + PIN, borrow e-books/audiobooks/magazines instantly — they return *themselves* at expiry. → *Expect:* a working digital shelf; the no-late-fees-ever tier of library use unlocked.
6. **Return physical items on or before the due date.** The returns slot/bin (any branch, usually, and 24/7 slots exist) — or renew online *before* the date if nobody's waiting. → *Expect:* account back to zero items overdue; holds for others honored.

## Decision points

- The book you want isn't in the system → ask about inter-library loan (they'll borrow it from another system for you) or suggest-a-purchase — both are normal patron powers people don't use.
- Fines anxiety (a real reason people avoid libraries) → many systems have abolished late fines; ask — and the digital branch structurally can't accrue them.
- Beyond books → most cards also unlock: study rooms, printing/scanning, kids' programs, language courses, museum passes, tool/seed libraries, and databases (newspapers, genealogy) — one "what else does my card do?" at the desk pays for years.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lost the card:** report it (someone borrowing on your card is your fine risk), get a replacement — usually trivial and cheap; the number changes, update the apps.
- **F2 Item lost or damaged by you:** tell the desk before the system tells you — replacement cost or a fee, handled far more gently than card-limbo avoidance.
- **F3 Hold expired unclaimed:** it happens; re-place it — but turn on pickup notifications this time.
- **F4 Overdue spiral (books under the bed for months):** return them anyway, today — libraries want the books back more than the fines; amnesty is common and the desk has seen far worse.

## Verification

Card active, one physical item successfully borrowed-and-returned on time, the digital app signed in with something checked out, due-date reminders on, and you know your library's answer to "what else does my card do?"

## Variations

- University towns: public and academic libraries are different systems — public cards sometimes grant academic-library reading access; ask.
- `us`/`uk`/`de` systems map closely to this recipe; `jp` municipal libraries too (registration follows residency registration).

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Borrowing records are protected with unusual seriousness by library ethics; guard the card like a mild credential (F1), and the rest is upside.
