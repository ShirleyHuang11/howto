## Goal

Laundry washed and dried at a laundromat, machines claimed and released by the local etiquette, and the two hours spent rather than lost.

## Preconditions

- Sorted laundry in bags (`daily/home/do-the-laundry` steps 1 and 2 done at home: pockets emptied, colors split), detergent, and the payment the venue takes: coins, a store card, or its app. Check the venue's payment mode before hauling bags.
- Something to do for the cycle gaps: book, laptop, errands nearby. The wash is about 30 to 40 minutes, the dry 30 to 60.

## Steps

1. **Scout on arrival.** Find free washers of the right size (bigger drums cost more, take duvets), check posted prices and any house rules, locate carts and folding tables. → *Expect:* a machine chosen, price known, cart nearby if hauling between machines.
2. **Inspect the drum before loading.** A quick look and sniff: strangers' left socks, bleach puddles, or a gasket full of grime move you one machine over. → *Expect:* a clean, empty drum.
3. **Load, dose, and pay.** Clothes in loosely (the home three-quarters rule), detergent in the drawer or drum per the machine's marking, select cycle and temperature, feed the payment, start. → *Expect:* door locked, cycle running, and the end time noted on your phone.
4. **Stay close or come back on time.** The cardinal etiquette: machines are shared infrastructure and a finished load squatting in a washer blocks everyone. Set an alarm for the cycle end. → *Expect:* you at your machine within minutes of the beep.
5. **Move wet laundry to a dryer promptly.** Shake pieces out (`daily/home/do-the-laundry` step 7), clean the dryer's lint filter first, load loosely, choose medium heat unless the labels say low, pay and start. Overloaded dryers run twice for the same money. → *Expect:* lint screen cleaned, drum loose, alarm reset for the dry time.
6. **Check dryness before hauling home.** Waistbands and seams are the last dry; damp means one more short paid cycle beats mildew at home. → *Expect:* genuinely dry clothes.
7. **Fold at the tables, claim everything, and leave the station clean.** Folding warm at the venue (`embodied/household/fold-a-tshirt`) beats a wrinkled bag; sweep the drum and gasket with a hand for strays (fitted sheets eat socks), take your detergent, wipe your table. → *Expect:* folded bags, empty machines, nothing of yours left behind.

## Decision points

- Peak times: evenings and weekends run full; mid-morning weekdays are the empty hours if your schedule allows.
- The someone-else's-finished-load dilemma: wait a few minutes past the beep, then moving it gently to the top of the machine or a cart is accepted etiquette in most places; do it as you would want yours handled.
- Wash-and-fold service: many laundromats sell drop-off by the kilo; the errand becomes two visits of five minutes, worth pricing when time is the scarce currency.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Machine ate the payment or died mid-cycle:** note the machine number, use the posted phone number or attendant; refunds are routine. Wet hostage clothes justify a polite call, not a crowbar.
- **F2 Came back late to an emptied washer:** your load is on top or in a cart per etiquette; check tables and carts before assuming theft, which is rare but is why valuables do not go to laundromats.
- **F3 A stranger's red sock in your whites:** the drum inspection (step 2) failed or the mover mixed loads; `daily/home/do-the-laundry` F1's rewash-while-wet advice applies at home.
- **F4 Dryer heat scorched or shrank something:** the label-read rule survives venue changes; medium default, low for anything with elastic or wool, and the short-cycle top-up beats one long roast.

## Verification

Everything that arrived leaves washed, dried through at seams, folded, with the lint screen cleaned, machines emptied promptly, and no sock left in a drum or on a table.

## Variations

- Shared building laundry rooms run the same recipe with a booking sheet or app instead of scouting, and the etiquette stakes rise with repeated neighbors (`daily/social/greet-a-neighbor` compounds here).
- `jp` coin laundries often include detergent automatically; check before double-dosing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep wallet and keys on your person, not on a folding table; the machines' lost-and-found is honest, but why test it.
