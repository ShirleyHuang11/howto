## Goal

Receive a seasonal flu vaccine at a pharmacy and leave with a record that can be used for school, work, or medical files.

## Preconditions

- A pharmacy that offers flu vaccination in your area.
- Photo ID if required by the pharmacy.
- Insurance card or payment method if not covered.
- Knowledge of major allergies, recent vaccines, and prior vaccine reactions.

## Steps

1. **Choose walk-in or appointment.** [BRANCH: walk-in | appointment] check the pharmacy website or call for vaccine hours, or book a time online. → *Expect:* you know when the immunizing pharmacist is available.
2. **Bring documents.** Pack ID, insurance card, and any vaccine form from school or work. → *Expect:* documents are easy to reach at check-in.
3. **Wear accessible clothing.** Choose a short sleeve or loose sleeve that exposes the upper arm. → *Expect:* the shoulder can be uncovered without undressing in the pharmacy.
4. **Check in at the pharmacy counter.** Say you are there for a flu shot and give your name or appointment time. → *Expect:* staff provide a consent form or direct you to a waiting area.
5. **Complete screening honestly.** List allergies, illness today, pregnancy status if relevant, and past reactions. → *Expect:* the pharmacist confirms whether vaccination can proceed.
6. **Confirm cost before the shot.** Ask what insurance covers or what the cash price is. → *Expect:* you know whether payment is due before vaccination.
7. **Choose the arm.** Pick the non-dominant arm unless you prefer the other side for sleeping or work. → *Expect:* the pharmacist has a clear injection site on the upper arm.
8. **Receive the shot.** Relax the shoulder and let the arm hang loose. → *Expect:* a quick pinch, then a bandage or cotton on the site.
9. **Wait if instructed.** Stay nearby for 10 to 15 minutes if the pharmacy asks, especially after prior reactions. → *Expect:* no immediate dizziness, breathing trouble, or rash.
10. **Get the record.** Ask for printed or digital proof showing vaccine name and date. → *Expect:* you leave with documentation or know where it appears online.
11. **Do aftercare.** Move the arm normally, drink water, and use usual pain relief only if appropriate for you. → *Expect:* soreness is mild and improves over 1 to 2 days.

## Decision points

- If you feel ill with fever → ask the pharmacist whether to delay.
- If you have egg allergy, immune concerns, or prior severe reaction → consult the pharmacist or clinician before consent.
- If insurance rejects the claim → ask for cash price, discount programs, or another in-network pharmacy.
- If work or school needs proof → verify the record includes date, lot if available, location, and vaccine type.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No vaccinator on duty:** detect staff cannot give shots now → book the next slot or try another nearby pharmacy.
- **F2 Missing insurance card:** detect billing cannot proceed → use the insurer app, call the insurer, or ask for cash price.
- **F3 Lightheaded after shot:** detect dizziness, sweating, or nausea → sit or lie down and tell staff immediately.
- **F4 Record missing:** detect no printed or portal proof → request immunization documentation before leaving.

## Verification

You received the flu shot, have no urgent reaction before leaving, and possess a dated vaccination record from the pharmacy.

## Variations

- `us`: many pharmacies bill medical or pharmacy insurance differently, so bring both cards if you have them.
- Employer clinic: the same consent and record steps apply, but check-in may be through workplace health staff.
- High-dose or special formulation: older adults or specific groups may be offered a different flu vaccine.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because health information, insurance data, and a medical injection are involved. Share screening details accurately, keep the vaccine record private, and seek urgent help for breathing trouble, swelling, or widespread hives.
