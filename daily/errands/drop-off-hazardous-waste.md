## Goal

Dispose of household hazardous waste through an approved facility or collection event without spilling it or putting it in regular trash.

## Preconditions

- You have waste such as paint, solvents, pesticides, cleaners, batteries, fluorescent bulbs, oil, fuel, or electronics.
- Containers are closed, upright, and not actively leaking.
- You can check local waste authority rules for accepted items and drop-off times.
- You have a box, tray, or bin to keep containers stable in the vehicle.

## Steps

1. **Sort the waste by type.** Separate chemicals, paint, batteries, electronics, bulbs, oil, and sharps if present. → *Expect:* incompatible items are not mixed in one loose pile.
2. **Find the correct program.** [BRANCH: permanent facility | one-day event] use the local waste authority site or phone line to confirm location, hours, residency rules, and quantity limits. → *Expect:* you have an address, date, and accepted-item list.
3. **Keep original labels.** Leave waste in original containers when possible, and label unknown containers with what you believe they contain. → *Expect:* staff can identify each item without opening it.
4. **Seal and contain.** Tighten lids, tape weak caps if needed, and place containers upright in a lined box or tray. → *Expect:* nothing tips or leaks when the box is gently moved.
5. **Load safely.** Put the box in a ventilated cargo area away from passengers, pets, food, and direct sun. → *Expect:* containers remain upright and separated during the drive.
6. **Follow site instructions.** Stay in the directed lane, keep children and pets inside the vehicle, and let staff unload if required. → *Expect:* staff accepts the items or directs you to the right station.
7. **Never abandon rejects.** If an item is refused, ask for the correct disposal route before leaving. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* dumping hazardous waste can contaminate drains, soil, and workers' equipment. → *Expect:* every refused item has a next destination.
8. **Clean up the transport area.** Remove liners, ventilate the vehicle, and wash hands after the drop-off. → *Expect:* there is no residue, odor, or loose debris left behind.

## Decision points

- The facility does not accept electronics → use an electronics recycler or retailer take-back program.
- Paint is fully dry latex paint → local rules may allow regular trash, but confirm first.
- A container is leaking → double-contain it and call the facility before transporting.
- Waste is from a business → household programs may reject it, so use a commercial hauler.
- You have lithium batteries → tape terminals and keep them from touching metal.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Closed facility:** detect by locked gates or a date mismatch, recover by keeping waste secured and rescheduling through the official calendar.
- **F2 Unknown chemical:** detect by missing label or unclear container, recover by labeling "unknown" and asking staff how to handle it.
- **F3 Spill in vehicle:** detect by wet box, odor, or visible residue, recover by ventilating, wearing gloves, and following the product label or poison control advice.
- **F4 Item rejected:** detect by staff refusal, recover by noting the recommended recycler, pharmacy, or special program.

## Verification

All accepted hazardous items have been handed to the approved program and no hazardous item was placed in a regular bin.

## Variations

- Batteries: many stores accept household batteries separately from hazardous waste events.
- Medications: pharmacies or law enforcement take-back boxes may be the proper route.
- Large electronics: some programs require appointments or fees for televisions and monitors.

## Safety & privacy

Never pour chemicals down drains or put hazardous waste in normal trash. Remove personal data from electronics before recycling when practical.
