## Goal

Renew your vehicle registration before the deadline and place any new sticker or document where required.

## Preconditions

- You have the renewal notice or vehicle identification details.
- Insurance, inspection, emissions, and taxes are current if your location requires them.
- You have payment for fees, penalties, and mailing or processing charges.
- The registration address is correct or can be updated during renewal.

## Steps

1. **Read the renewal reminder.** Check the plate number, VIN, expiration date, fee, and any missing requirement listed. → *Expect:* you know the deadline and what blocks renewal.
2. **Gather documents.** Have registration notice, plate number, VIN, odometer if requested, insurance proof, inspection result, emissions result, and payment. → *Expect:* the renewal form can be completed without searching mid-process.
3. **Choose the renewal channel.** [BRANCH: online | mail or in-person] use online if eligible, or choose a licensing office, kiosk, or mail option if documents need review. → *Expect:* you are using an accepted channel for your vehicle.
4. **Confirm vehicle and owner details.** Verify name, address, plate, VIN, and vehicle description before paying. → *Expect:* the renewal shows the correct vehicle and mailing address.
5. **Pay the fees.** Submit the required fee and save the receipt or confirmation number. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* renewal fees and penalties may not be refundable, so confirm the plate and VIN first. → *Expect:* payment is accepted and a confirmation appears.
6. **Receive the proof.** Download, print, or wait for the registration card, sticker, decal, or electronic record. → *Expect:* you have temporary or final proof of renewal.
7. **Install the sticker correctly.** Clean the plate or windshield area and apply the new sticker exactly where local rules specify. → *Expect:* the displayed month or year matches the renewed period.
8. **Store the document.** Put the registration card in the vehicle or approved digital wallet, and discard old copies that could confuse a stop. → *Expect:* the vehicle carries current proof.

## Decision points

- Renewal is blocked by inspection or emissions → complete that requirement first.
- The address is wrong → update it before submitting so documents are mailed correctly.
- The deadline has passed → renew promptly and check whether late fees or enforcement apply.
- You sold the vehicle → do not renew it; complete sale or plate surrender steps instead.
- Online renewal is unavailable → use the notice instructions for office, kiosk, or mail.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Payment accepted but no confirmation:** detect by missing receipt, recover by checking email, account history, or agency support before paying again.
- **F2 Sticker lost in mail:** detect by delivery window passing, recover by requesting a replacement with the confirmation number.
- **F3 Wrong vehicle renewed:** detect by mismatched plate or VIN, recover by contacting the agency immediately with receipt details.
- **F4 Blocked requirement:** detect by renewal rejection, recover by completing inspection, insurance update, taxes, or fines.

## Verification

The vehicle record, registration card, or sticker shows a current expiration date for the correct plate and VIN.

## Variations

- Fleet or leased vehicle: the leasing company may handle registration or require proof before renewal.
- Kiosk renewal: sticker may print immediately if no review is needed.
- Digital registration: some places accept an app record, but paper may still be useful.

## Safety & privacy

Registration renewal exposes address, payment, VIN, and plate data. Use official agency sites and avoid driving after expiration unless local law allows a grace period.
