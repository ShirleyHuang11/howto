## Goal

Replace a worn windshield wiper blade with the correct size so it clears water without streaking or scratching the glass.

## Preconditions

- The car is parked, turned off, and the windshield is cool enough to touch.
- You have replacement wiper blades in the correct lengths for the driver and passenger sides.
- The package adapter matches the arm style, such as J-hook, side-pin, or bayonet.
- A folded towel is available to protect the glass if an arm drops.

## Steps

1. **Measure or confirm the blade size.** Check the owner's manual, parts lookup, or the old blade length before opening the package. → *Expect:* each side's blade length is known and the new blades match.
2. **Lift one wiper arm carefully.** Raise it until it holds itself away from the windshield, then lay a folded towel under the bare arm path. → *Expect:* the arm is stable and the glass is protected.
3. **Find the release tab.** Rotate the blade slightly and look for the tab, latch, or button where the blade meets the arm. → *Expect:* the locking point is visible before you pull.
4. **Release the old blade.** [BRANCH: tab | button | clip] press the tab, push the button, or open the clip while sliding the blade off the arm. → *Expect:* the blade separates without bending the arm.
5. **Keep control of the arm.** Hold the metal arm with one hand while the blade is off. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a dropped bare arm can crack or scratch the windshield, so do not let it snap down. → *Expect:* the arm stays raised and controlled.
6. **Install the new blade.** Slide or click the new blade into the same connector path until the lock seats. → *Expect:* you hear or feel a firm click and the blade cannot slide free with a light tug.
7. **Lower the blade onto the towel.** Guide the arm down slowly, then remove the towel after the rubber rests on the glass. → *Expect:* the rubber edge sits flat against the windshield.
8. **Repeat on the other side.** Match the second blade to its side because left and right lengths may differ. → *Expect:* both new blades are installed and locked.
9. **Test with washer fluid.** Turn the key to accessory mode if needed and run the washers with one wipe cycle. → *Expect:* both blades sweep smoothly without chatter or hitting trim.
10. **Do the smear check.** Look through the cleaned area in daylight or with headlights on glass. → *Expect:* no broad streaks, missed arcs, or oily smears remain.

## Decision points

- The new blade is a different length than the old one → stop and verify against the door sticker, manual, or parts catalog before installing.
- The connector does not click → try the included adapter that matches the arm style, not force.
- The wiper arm cannot stay raised → keep one hand on it and work slowly over the towel.
- The blade smears after testing → clean the windshield and rubber edge once before judging the blade defective.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong blade length:** detect by overhang, trim contact, or a shorter wipe path → recover by exchanging for the listed size.
- **F2 Loose connector:** detect by blade sliding when tugged → recover by reinstalling until the latch clicks or using the correct adapter.
- **F3 Arm snaps down:** detect by a hard hit on the towel or glass → recover by inspecting for chips before using the wiper.
- **F4 Persistent smear:** detect by a hazy stripe after washer fluid → recover by cleaning glass, then replace the blade if the stripe follows the rubber.

## Verification

Both wiper blades are locked to the arms, sweep the windshield without contact or chatter, and leave a clear view after one washer cycle.

## Variations

- Rear wiper: the arm may have a small plastic cover that flips up before the blade slides out.
- Beam blade: the connector may be covered by a hinged cap that must close fully after installation.
- Winter blade: confirm the bulkier frame clears the hood edge before driving.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because a spring-loaded bare wiper arm can damage glass or pinch fingers. Keep the car off, control the arm at all times, and avoid touching the sharp edges of broken old rubber or metal inserts.
