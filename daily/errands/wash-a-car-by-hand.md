## Goal

The car's exterior is clean and dried spot-free, with no new swirl marks in the paint, and the wash gear is rinsed and stored.

## Preconditions

- Shaded parking spot (or an overcast day) with a hose or water source; the panels are cool to the touch.
- Two buckets (ideally with grit guards), car-wash soap (not dishwashing liquid, which strips wax), a wash mitt, a wheel brush, and several clean microfiber drying towels.
- Local rules permit driveway washing (some municipalities restrict runoff).

## Steps

1. **Stage in shade and let the panels cool.** Sun-heated paint flash-dries soap and water into etched spots. → *Expect:* panels cool to the back of your hand; car out of direct sun.
2. **Set up the two buckets.** Bucket A: soap solution per the bottle's dilution. Bucket B: plain rinse water. Keep them a step apart so you never dunk into the wrong one. → *Expect:* one sudsy bucket, one clear bucket, grit guards seated at the bottoms.
3. **Do the wheels first with dedicated tools.** Wheel brush and its own water; wheels carry brake dust and grit that must never touch the paint mitt. → *Expect:* wheel faces and barrels clean; wheel brush set aside, out of the paint workflow.
4. **Pre-rinse the whole car top-down.** Loosen and float off loose dirt before anything touches the paint. → *Expect:* visible dirt sheeting off; standing water on all panels.
5. **Wash top-down in sections: roof, glass, hood and trunk, upper sides, lower sides last.** Load the mitt in bucket A, wash one section with straight-line strokes (not circles), then dunk and scrub the mitt against the grit guard in bucket B before reloading. → *Expect:* rinse-bucket water getting dirty while the soap bucket stays clean; lower panels always done last.
6. **Rinse each section before its soap dries.** In warm weather work panel-by-panel: wash one, rinse it, move on. → *Expect:* no dried soap haze anywhere; water sheeting cleanly off rinsed sections.
7. **Final rinse with a gentle free-flowing stream (nozzle off).** A low-pressure sheet of water pulls most droplets off the panel and halves the drying work. → *Expect:* water sheeting off in a film, leaving only scattered droplets.
8. **Dry immediately with clean microfiber towels, top-down.** Blot or drag lightly; swap to a dry towel once one is saturated. Do not let the car air-dry. → *Expect:* glass and dark panels streak-free and spot-free when viewed at a low angle.
9. **Empty buckets and machine-wash the mitt and towels (no fabric softener).** Softener ruins microfiber absorbency. → *Expect:* gear clean and drying; buckets empty and stored.

## Decision points

- Mitt or towel touches the ground → it is done for the day; a single embedded stone grinds scratches into the clearcoat. Swap to a spare, launder the dropped one.
- Bird droppings or sap found → soak with soapy water for a minute and lift gently; scrubbing dried droppings scratches, and their acidity means sooner is better.
- No shade available → wash in early morning or evening, one small panel at a time, rinsing and drying each before moving on.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Water spots after drying:** hazy mineral rings appear as panels dry → re-wet the panel and dry promptly with a clean towel; for etched-in spots use a dedicated water-spot remover, not more scrubbing.
- **F2 Swirl marks visible in sunlight:** fine circular scratches from a gritty mitt or circular strokes → prevention only (two buckets, straight lines, drop-it-lose-it rule); existing swirls need machine polishing, which is beyond this recipe.
- **F3 Dried soap haze:** dull streaks where soap sat too long → re-rinse the panel thoroughly and re-dry; work smaller sections next time.
- **F4 Rinse water beading dirty off the mitt:** the rinse bucket has become as dirty as the mitt → dump and refill bucket B mid-wash; a filthy rinse bucket defeats the whole method.

## Verification

Walk around the car at a low viewing angle in good light: no dirt lines at panel bottoms or behind wheels, no soap residue, no water spots on glass or dark paint, and the wash mitt never touched the ground or the wheel gear.

## Variations

- Waterless/rinseless wash products: for water-restricted areas or apartments; only safe on lightly dusty cars, never on visibly gritty ones.
- Two-bucket plus foam pre-soak: a foam cannon or pump sprayer layer before step 4 further reduces mitt-borne grit on very dirty cars.
- Winter/road-salt regions: add a thorough underbody and wheel-arch rinse; salt trapped there causes rust, not cosmetic damage.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall: mind slippery wet pavement and soap runoff regulations. The only real asset at stake is the paint itself, which the two-bucket discipline and the dropped-mitt rule exist to protect.
