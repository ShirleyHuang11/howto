## Goal

Open a car insurance claim with accurate crash information, a claim number, and a clear next step for repair or payout.

## Preconditions

- Everyone is out of immediate danger and emergency services have been called if needed.
- You have policy information, license, registration, and contact details for other involved drivers.
- You can safely document the scene without standing in traffic.
- You know your deductible or excess may apply.

## Steps

1. **Make the scene safe first.** Move to a safe location if possible, turn on hazards, and call emergency services for injuries, blocked lanes, suspected impairment, or major damage. → *Expect:* people are safer than the vehicles.
2. **Exchange required information.** Get names, phone numbers, driver licenses, plate numbers, insurance details, vehicle descriptions, and witness contacts. → *Expect:* you can identify every involved party.
3. **Document the scene.** Photograph vehicle positions, damage, plates, road signs, signals, skid marks, debris, weather, and injuries if appropriate. → *Expect:* photos show context before vehicles are moved or repaired.
4. **Avoid admitting fault.** Stick to facts with drivers, police, tow operators, and insurers. → *Expect:* you have not guessed blame or accepted private pressure.
5. **Contact your insurer.** [BRANCH: app or web | phone] report the date, time, location, vehicles, people, police report number if any, and what happened. → *Expect:* the insurer opens or prepares a claim.
6. **Record the claim number.** Save the claim number, adjuster contact, repair instructions, rental rules, and upload link. → *Expect:* you can refer to one claim record in later calls.
7. **Ask about deductible and coverage.** Confirm whether collision, comprehensive, liability, uninsured motorist, rental, towing, or glass coverage applies. → *Expect:* you know what costs may come out of pocket.
8. **Follow repair and inspection steps.** Use approved estimates, photos, repair shops, or adjuster appointments as instructed. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* settling or signing a release can close rights, so read payout and injury documents before accepting. → *Expect:* the next claim action is scheduled or submitted.

## Decision points

- Anyone is injured → prioritize medical help and police documentation before claim convenience.
- The other driver asks to avoid insurance → still document fully and consider reporting promptly.
- Your car is unsafe to drive → tow it rather than driving with leaking fluids, airbag deployment, or damaged tires.
- Damage is below deductible → ask whether opening a claim still affects premiums before proceeding.
- Police report is required locally → obtain the report number or filing instructions.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missing other driver info:** detect by incomplete name, plate, or insurance, recover with photos, police report, witness details, or insurer investigation.
- **F2 Claim delayed:** detect by no claim number or adjuster contact, recover by calling the claims line with your policy number.
- **F3 Disputed facts:** detect by conflicting stories, recover by submitting photos, witness names, dashcam footage, and police report.
- **F4 Surprise deductible:** detect by repair shop asking for payment, recover by confirming coverage and deductible with the adjuster.

## Verification

The insurer has issued a claim number and you have documented the next required action, deductible status, and contact channel.

## Variations

- Single-vehicle damage: comprehensive may apply for theft, animals, weather, or vandalism.
- Glass claim: some policies route windshield damage through a separate vendor.
- Total loss: expect title, loan payoff, valuation, and release paperwork.

## Safety & privacy

Crash scenes are dangerous and claim records contain identity, location, vehicle, and medical data. Share details only with police, insurers, medical staff, and legitimate repair providers.
