## Goal

Record your organ donor decision in the official registry and make sure your family knows your preference.

## Preconditions

- You know your legal name, date of birth, address, and identification details used by your local registry.
- You can access the official donor registry, motor vehicle office, health card system, or approved app.
- You understand that registration can usually be changed later.
- You have time to tell a close family member or emergency contact afterward.

## Steps

1. **Find the official registry.** Use a government health, motor vehicle, or recognized donation organization site for your location. → *Expect:* the page clearly identifies the registry authority.
2. **Review what registration means.** Read which organs and tissues may be donated and when donation decisions are considered. → *Expect:* you understand that donation is evaluated only after death or for specific living donation programs.
3. **Enter your identity details.** Provide the required name, birth date, address, ID number, or account login. → *Expect:* the form accepts your identity information.
4. **Select your donor preference.** [BRANCH: register broadly | limit donation] choose the default donor option or any allowed limits for organs, tissues, research, or transplant use. → *Expect:* the selected preference is shown before submission.
5. **Submit the registration.** Confirm the details before final submission. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* this records a legal medical preference until you update it, so verify your identity and choice first. → *Expect:* the registry shows a confirmation page, email, card, or app status.
6. **Save proof accessibly.** Add the donor status to your license, wallet card, health app, or personal records if offered. → *Expect:* you can find evidence of your donor status quickly.
7. **Tell your family.** Tell your next of kin or emergency contact that you registered and what you want. → *Expect:* at least one person likely to be contacted knows your decision.
8. **Note how to change it.** Save the registry link or account method for updating or withdrawing later. → *Expect:* you know where to edit the preference if your decision changes.

## Decision points

- You cannot confirm the site is official → stop and use a government or recognized donation organization link.
- Your jurisdiction requires family authorization → registration still helps, but the family conversation is essential.
- You want to donate while alive, such as kidney or marrow → use a separate living donor or marrow registry process.
- You are worried about cost → donor registration costs nothing to you or your family for donation recovery.
- You have medical conditions → register anyway if you wish; clinicians decide medical suitability later.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Identity mismatch:** detect by form rejection or failed lookup, recover by matching your ID exactly or using the motor vehicle office.
- **F2 Family unaware:** detect by no one close knowing your choice, recover by having a direct conversation and adding it to emergency records.
- **F3 Myth-based hesitation:** detect by concern that care will be reduced, recover by reading official medical ethics explanations that treatment teams and donation teams are separate.
- **F4 Lost proof:** detect by no card, app status, or account access, recover by logging in again or requesting a replacement card.

## Verification

The official registry, license record, donor card, or app shows your current donor preference and a family contact has been told.

## Variations

- Driver license renewal: many places let you register or update donor status during license transactions.
- Health app: some phones can display donor status with emergency medical information.
- Opt-out countries: local law may presume donation unless you record a refusal, so check the rule.

## Safety & privacy

Registration exposes identity and health-adjacent preference data to the registry. Use official channels, and know that donor registration does not create a bill for donation recovery.
