## Goal

A car parked in a multi-story garage, its location retrievable hours later, and the exit paid and cleared without holding a queue at the barrier.

## Preconditions

- A garage whose height limit clears your vehicle (posted at entry; roof boxes and vans read this sign as law) and whose prices you glanced at before committing.
- The car's fuel or charge sufficient for queue idling on busy days.

## Steps

1. **Enter and take the ticket, or badge in.** Pull close to the machine, take the ticket (or tap the card/app the garage uses), wait for the barrier to fully rise before moving. → *Expect:* barrier up, ticket in hand; the ticket goes with you, never stays in the car (payment machines and some exit checks need it, and thieves read tickets as receipts).
2. **Drive the ramps at walking-pace caution.** Headlights on, hard lefts and rights around blind pillars, yield to reversing cars and pedestrians surging from stairwells. → *Expect:* steady progress upward; no surprises at the ramp bends.
3. **Choose a spot for the return trip, not just the entry.** Near a stair or lift core beats near the entrance ramp; wider spots beat tight pillar-side ones (door dings live by pillars); note lighting if returning late. → *Expect:* a spot you can reverse out of and find again.
4. **Park straight, fold mirrors where tight, and record the location.** Photo of the level marker and row sign (or drop a pin in the garage's app); valuables out of sight or with you. → *Expect:* a photo or note that answers "level, color, row" hours later; a cabin showing nothing worth breaking a window for.
5. **Before leaving the garage: find and pay at the machine (pay-on-foot systems).** Most garages want payment at the walk-in machine *before* returning to the car; the ticket validates for a short exit window. [BRANCH: pay-on-foot machine → pay now, keep the validated ticket | pay-at-exit barrier → pay when leaving | app/plate-recognition → nothing to do] → *Expect:* the system's mode identified on the way in or at the machine, and a validated ticket where that mode applies.
6. **Return, load, and exit within the window.** Retrieve the car (the photo does its work), reverse out with a full look for walkers, follow exit arrows, insert the validated ticket or tap at the barrier. → *Expect:* barrier up on the first try; no reversing against one-way ramps.

## Decision points

- Long stays and events: prices step brutally at thresholds; check the daily maximum and event flat rates before entering, and compare the garage across the street for a minute.
- EV bays, family bays, disabled bays: they are enforced and cameras watch; the convenience of misusing one prices poorly against the fine.
- Height-limit anxiety mid-ramp: stop before, not after, the scraping sound; garages post limits honestly and antennas are the only forgiving part of a roof.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lost ticket:** the machines and intercoms have a lost-ticket flow; expect the day-maximum charge and possibly showing the car's registration. The photo from step 4 helps prove entry time in disputes.
- **F2 Cannot find the car:** the photo, or the garage's find-my-car kiosk (many scan plates); failing both, walk one systematic loop per level near your entry stairwell rather than random diagonals.
- **F3 Exit window expired (coffee after paying):** re-validate at the nearest machine for the small difference; barriers reject expired tickets, not people.
- **F4 Barrier refuses the paid ticket:** use the intercom button; attendants release remotely for machine faults constantly. Do not tailgate the car ahead through a barrier, which ends in dented pride at best.

## Verification

The car sits straight in a findable, exit-friendly spot recorded by photo; payment matched the garage's mode; and the exit barrier rose on the first presentation with no queue collected behind you.

## Variations

- Automated stack and valet garages replace steps 2 to 4 with a handover bay and a claim ticket; the record-the-location instinct becomes guard-the-ticket.
- Street-level lots run the same recipe minus ramps, with the pay-and-display slip on the dashboard as the mode.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk split between low-speed metal (pillars, ramps, reversing neighbors) and property (nothing visible in the cabin, ticket on your person). Pedestrian-you at night prefers the lit stair core you parked beside on purpose.
