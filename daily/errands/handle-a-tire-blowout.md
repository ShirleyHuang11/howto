## Goal

Keep control of the vehicle during a tire blowout and stop in a safe location.

## Preconditions

- You are driving and hear a bang, feel a sudden pull, or notice flapping tire noise.
- You can keep both hands on the steering wheel.
- Traffic and road conditions allow gradual speed reduction.

## Steps

1. **Grip the wheel firmly.** Hold the vehicle straight and resist the pull toward the failed tire. → *Expect:* the vehicle stays in its lane as much as possible.
2. **Do not brake hard.** Ease off the accelerator and avoid sudden steering. → *Expect:* speed begins dropping without weight shifting abruptly.
3. **Keep the vehicle pointed straight.** Make only small corrections to stay in the lane. → *Expect:* swerving is minimized.
4. **Let the car coast down.** Allow speed to fall before changing lanes or pulling over. → *Expect:* the vehicle becomes easier to control.
5. **Signal when you have control.** Turn on hazard lights and signal toward a shoulder or safe exit. → *Expect:* nearby drivers can see you are slowing.
6. **Move off the travel lane gradually.** Steer to a wide shoulder, exit ramp, parking lot, or other safe area. → *Expect:* the car leaves traffic without a sharp maneuver.
7. **Stop on level ground if possible.** Park away from traffic, set the parking brake, and keep wheels turned away from the road if on a slope. → *Expect:* the vehicle is stable.
8. **Exit only if safe.** Leave from the side away from traffic, or stay buckled with hazards on if the roadside is unsafe. → *Expect:* people are not standing next to traffic.
9. **Call roadside assistance if needed.** Request a tow or tire change, especially on highways, narrow shoulders, or bad weather. → *Expect:* help is arranged without roadside exposure.

## Decision points

- Blowout occurs at highway speed → focus on steering and coasting before attempting the shoulder.
- There is no safe shoulder → continue slowly to the nearest exit or wide pull-off if the vehicle remains controllable.
- Vehicle is in an active lane and cannot move → stay belted if safer and call emergency services.
- Tire change would place you near traffic → call roadside assistance instead.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hard braking skid:** detect sliding or loss of control → release brake pressure, steer straight, and let speed fall.
- **F2 Overcorrection:** detect fishtailing or lane departure → reduce steering input and look where you want to go.
- **F3 Unsafe stopping place:** detect narrow shoulder or poor visibility → remain in the vehicle if safer and call for help.
- **F4 Secondary damage:** detect grinding, sparks, or wheel damage → stop as soon as safe and tow rather than driving farther.

## Verification

The vehicle is stopped out of traffic or emergency help is called, hazard lights are on, passengers are protected from traffic, and the failed tire is handled by a safe change or tow.

## Variations

- Front tire blowout: steering pull may be strong, so hold straight and avoid abrupt correction.
- Rear tire blowout: the vehicle may sway, so keep steering smooth and do not brake hard.
- Motorcycle: keep a firm grip, avoid sudden braking, and use training-specific emergency procedures.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from loss of control and roadside exposure. Prioritize steering and safe stopping over saving the tire, and call emergency services if the vehicle blocks traffic.
