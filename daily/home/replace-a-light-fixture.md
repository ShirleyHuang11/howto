## Goal

An existing light fixture is replaced with a compatible new fixture that is grounded, mounted securely, and controlled safely by the same switch.

## Preconditions

- You have the new fixture, bulbs within rating, ladder, screwdriver, wire connectors, voltage tester, and a camera.
- The electrical box is firmly mounted and rated for the fixture weight.
- ⚠️ Turn the breaker off first and test dead before touching wires; call a licensed electrician for aluminum wiring, damaged insulation, no ground where required, unfamiliar wiring, or ceiling fans.

## Steps

1. **Turn off the breaker.** Switch off the circuit breaker, not just the wall switch. → *Expect:* the fixture no longer turns on.
2. **Test for power.** Use a non-contact tester and, if available, a contact meter at the fixture box. → *Expect:* all fixture conductors test dead.
3. **Remove shade and bulbs.** Take off glass parts and bulbs before loosening the fixture. → *Expect:* the fixture is lighter and safer to handle.
4. **Document wiring.** Photograph wire colors, connectors, and the ground connection before disconnecting anything. → *Expect:* you have a reference image.
5. **Disconnect old fixture.** Support the fixture, remove wire connectors, separate fixture wires, and lower it. → *Expect:* house wires remain accessible in the box.
6. **Inspect the box.** Check that the box is tight, not cracked, and has a grounding path. → *Expect:* the box can safely support the new fixture.
7. **Attach mounting bracket.** Install the new bracket using the screws supplied for the electrical box. → *Expect:* the bracket sits tight and level.
8. **Connect ground first.** Connect green or bare fixture ground to the house ground or grounded metal box as instructed. → *Expect:* ground is mechanically secure.
9. **Connect neutral and hot.** [BRANCH: black-white wiring | marked fixture leads] connect neutral to neutral and switched hot to fixture hot using approved wire connectors. → *Expect:* no copper is exposed below connectors.
10. **Mount the fixture.** Tuck wires carefully, fasten the fixture to the bracket, and install bulbs within wattage rating. → *Expect:* the fixture is secure and clear of pinched wires.
11. **Restore power and test.** Turn the breaker on, then use the switch. → *Expect:* the fixture turns on and off normally with no buzzing, smell, or flicker.

## Decision points

- Any wire remains live after breaker off → stop and call an electrician.
- Wires are aluminum, brittle, burned, or cloth-insulated → stop and call an electrician.
- Electrical box moves or is not fixture-rated → repair or replace the box before installing.
- Extra red, multiple blacks, or unlabeled switch loops appear → use testing knowledge or call an electrician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Breaker trips:** breaker trips when restored, turn it off and inspect for crossed hot-neutral, pinched wires, or call an electrician.
- **F2 Fixture flickers:** light flickers or buzzes, turn breaker off and check connectors, bulb compatibility, and dimmer compatibility.
- **F3 No ground present:** fixture requires ground but none exists, stop and consult a licensed electrician.
- **F4 Fixture sags:** canopy or body moves, turn breaker off and correct the bracket or box support.

## Verification

The breaker stays on, the wall switch controls the fixture reliably, the fixture is firmly mounted, and no conductor is exposed or energized when it should be off.

## Variations

- LED fixtures: check dimmer compatibility before assuming an old dimmer will work.
- Heavy fixtures: use a properly rated ceiling box and support hardware.
- Bathrooms and damp areas: use fixtures rated for the location.

## Safety & privacy

High risk: household electrical work can cause shock, fire, or code violations. Breaker off, test dead, connect ground, and call a licensed electrician whenever wiring, grounding, box rating, or circuit behavior is uncertain.
