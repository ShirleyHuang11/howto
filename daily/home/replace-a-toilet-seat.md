## Goal

An old toilet seat is removed and a matching new seat is installed squarely without rocking or scratching the bowl.

## Preconditions

- You have the new seat, screwdriver, adjustable wrench or pliers, penetrating oil, cloth, cleaner, and gloves.
- The replacement seat matches the bowl shape, usually round or elongated.
- Clean and dry the hinge area before starting.
- Call a plumber or handyman if bolts are inaccessible, spinning inside the bowl, or fused to hidden hardware.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the shape.** Compare the new seat with the bowl before opening all hardware. → *Expect:* the seat follows the rim shape and bolt spacing.
2. **Open bolt caps.** Flip up the plastic caps behind the seat using a fingernail or flat screwdriver wrapped in cloth. → *Expect:* bolt heads are visible.
3. **Hold the underside nut.** Reach below the bowl rim and grip the nut while turning the bolt from above. → *Expect:* the bolt starts backing out.
4. **Use a rusted-bolt trick.** [BRANCH: plastic nut | metal nut] cut a plastic nut carefully with locking pliers or use penetrating oil on a metal nut, then turn slowly. → *Expect:* the old fastener releases without cracking porcelain.
5. **Remove the old seat.** Lift the seat and bolts away, keeping washers for comparison only. → *Expect:* the hinge deck is bare.
6. **Clean the mounting area.** Scrub around the bolt holes and dry the porcelain. → *Expect:* the new hinges sit flat.
7. **Position the new seat.** Drop the new bolts through the hinge holes and bowl holes with any supplied washers in the correct order. → *Expect:* the seat is centered over the rim.
8. **Tighten evenly.** Alternate sides by hand, then snug with a tool while holding alignment. → *Expect:* both hinges are firm and parallel.
9. **Check for rocking.** Lift, lower, and sit lightly on the closed lid if it is rated for sitting. → *Expect:* the seat does not slide, twist, or rock.

## Decision points

- Bolt spins but will not loosen → grip the nut from below or cut only the fastener, not the porcelain.
- Seat overhangs the bowl front → exchange for the other bowl shape before tightening.
- Soft-close hinge binds → loosen, realign, and retighten.
- Bowl cracks or hardware is hidden inside a skirted toilet → stop and call a pro.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scratched porcelain:** tool marks appear, stop and add cloth padding before continuing.
- **F2 Loose after tightening:** seat shifts during use, add supplied washers and tighten both sides evenly.
- **F3 Rusted nut will not move:** penetrating oil does not work, cut the nut or call a handyman.
- **F4 Hinge cap will not close:** bolt head sits crooked, loosen and reseat the hardware.

## Verification

The seat is centered, both hinge caps close, the lid opens freely, and the seat does not move when pressed side to side.

## Variations

- Quick-release seats: remove the top caps and release buttons before accessing bolts.
- Skirted toilets: use top-mount hardware supplied with the seat.
- Bidet seats: turn water off and disconnect the bidet line before replacing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: porcelain chips easily and old hardware can have sharp rust. Wear gloves, pad tools, avoid overtightening, and wash hands and tools after working near the toilet.
