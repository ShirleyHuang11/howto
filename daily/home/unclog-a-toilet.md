## Goal

A clogged toilet is cleared without overflow, chemical drain cleaners, or damage to the bowl.

## Preconditions

- Flange plunger, gloves, towels, and access to the toilet shutoff valve.
- A toilet bowl with enough water to cover the plunger cup, but not so much that plunging will spill.
- No chemical drain cleaner already added. If chemicals are present, stop and ventilate.

## Steps

1. **Stop a rising bowl.** If water is climbing, lift the tank lid and close the flapper or turn the wall valve clockwise. → *Expect:* the bowl water stops rising.
2. **Set the water level.** Remove water with a cup if the bowl is near the rim, or add water if the plunger cup will not be covered. → *Expect:* water sits below the rim with the plunger cup submerged.
3. **Use the right plunger.** Pull the flange out from the cup so it can seal inside the toilet outlet. → *Expect:* the rubber flange points downward like a sleeve.
4. **Seat the plunger.** Lower it slowly to burp air out, then press the flange into the outlet. → *Expect:* the cup seals and resists movement.
5. **Plunge with control.** Push gently first, then use firm short strokes without breaking the seal. → *Expect:* water pulses but stays in the bowl.
6. **Pull sharply once.** After 10 to 20 strokes, pull up to create suction. → *Expect:* the water drops, gurgles, or debris moves.
7. **Test with a small flush.** Turn water on if needed and press the handle briefly, ready to stop the flapper. → *Expect:* water drains normally and does not rise toward the rim.
8. **Repeat once if needed.** Re-seat the plunger and run another controlled cycle. → *Expect:* each cycle improves drainage or reveals that plunging is not enough.
9. **Escalate to a toilet auger.** If two good plunging rounds fail, use a closet auger, not chemicals. → *Expect:* the auger meets, hooks, or pushes through the blockage.
10. **Clean up.** Flush twice only after drainage is normal, rinse the plunger in clean bowl water, and disinfect contact surfaces. → *Expect:* toilet refills normally and the floor is dry.

## Decision points

- Water level is high → lower it before plunging to avoid sewage on the floor.
- Multiple drains are backing up → stop. The issue may be the main line, not the toilet.
- Hard object suspected → use an auger or call a plumber because plunging can wedge it deeper.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No seal:** detect splashing and weak resistance; angle the flange deeper and add water to cover the cup.
- **F2 Overflow starts:** detect water rising fast; close the flapper or shutoff valve immediately and wait for the level to drop.
- **F3 Chemical exposure:** detect bleach or drain-cleaner smell; do not plunge, ventilate, and follow product emergency instructions.
- **F4 Repeated clog:** detect normal clearing followed by frequent recurrence; reduce paper load and schedule inspection.

## Verification

A full flush drains at normal speed, the bowl refills to its usual level, and no wastewater is on the floor.

## Variations

- Low-flow toilet: use more patient suction strokes because the trapway may be narrow.
- No plunger: wait 10 minutes, add warm water and dish soap, then obtain a flange plunger.
- Septic system: avoid antibacterial cleaners and chemical drain products.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from wastewater and chemical exposure. Wear gloves, wash hands thoroughly, and never mix cleaning chemicals in or near the bowl.
