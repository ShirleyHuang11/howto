## Goal

The door swings through its full arc silently, the hinges are lubricated at the pin (where the squeak actually lives), and no lubricant is left to drip on the floor or stain clothing.

## Preconditions

- A lubricant suited to hinges: white lithium grease, silicone spray, or 3-in-1 oil. Penetrating spray (WD-40 type) works for days, not months; use it only if nothing else is on hand.
- A rag or paper towels, and a flat-head screwdriver or a nail plus a hammer for lifting hinge pins.
- Cardboard or a towel under the door to catch drips.

## Steps

1. **Find the noisy hinge.** Swing the door slowly with a hand resting on each hinge in turn; the squeaking hinge vibrates under your fingers. Most doors have 2-3 hinges and usually only one is guilty. → *Expect:* the squeak localized to a specific hinge.
2. **Stage the area.** Lay cardboard or a towel under the hinge side; drape a rag below the hinge itself. → *Expect:* floor and lower door face protected from drips.
3. **Lift the hinge pin partway.** [BRANCH: pin has a gap under its head → lever it up with a flat-head screwdriver | flush pin → set a nail against its bottom tip through the hole under the hinge barrel and tap upward with a hammer] Raise it 2-3 cm; do not remove it fully or the door sags onto the remaining hinges. → *Expect:* pin standing proud of the barrel; door still hanging normally.
4. **Apply lubricant to the exposed pin and into the barrel.** A thin coat on the pin shaft plus a small amount into the open barrel top. More is not better; excess only migrates out later. → *Expect:* pin visibly coated, no lubricant running down the door face.
5. **Tap the pin back down and seat it.** Hammer or palm until the head sits on the barrel. → *Expect:* pin flush, hinge closed up.
6. **Work the door.** Swing it fully open and closed 10-15 times to carry the lubricant through the barrel. → *Expect:* squeak fades within the first few swings and then disappears.
7. **Wipe all excess.** Rag over the hinge faces, barrel, and any drips on door or frame. Lubricant left on the surface collects dust and marks clothes brushing past. → *Expect:* hinge looks dry to the touch; rag has the residue.
8. **Repeat for any other hinge that still speaks.** Re-run step 1; a second hinge often starts squeaking once the loudest one is silenced. → *Expect:* full silent swing.

## Decision points

- Pin will not move (painted over or rusted in) → score the paint seam around the head with a utility knife first; if still stuck, lubricate from the top of the barrel without lifting the pin and work the door longer.
- Squeak persists with hinges silent → the noise is elsewhere: latch scraping the strike plate, door rubbing the frame or floor, or the hinge screws loose in the jamb. Tighten screws first.
- Rust visible on the pin when lifted → wipe the pin clean with the rag before lubricating; heavy pitting means the hinge is dying and replacement is the real fix.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Squeak returns within a week:** the lubricant was penetrating spray, not grease → repeat with white lithium grease or silicone, which persist.
- **F2 Door sags or binds after the work:** a pin was removed fully and re-seated badly, or jamb screws are loose → drive the pin fully home; tighten hinge screws (replace a stripped screw with a 75 mm one that reaches the wall stud).
- **F3 Oil stain spreading below the hinge:** too much lubricant migrating out → wipe daily for a few days; on unfinished wood, dust the residue with talc or chalk to lift it.
- **F4 Hammer tap chips paint off the pin head:** cushion the blow through the rag or use a plastic mallet; touch up paint later if it matters.

## Verification

Open and close the door slowly through its full arc: no sound from any hinge, the door neither sags nor rubs, and a finger run over each hinge comes away clean.

## Variations

- Ball-bearing or sealed hinges (common on heavy exterior doors): pins are often non-removable; lubricate the barrel seams from outside and work the door.
- Rising butt hinges (door lifts as it opens): grease the spiral bearing surface at the barrel top, the actual wear point.
- No lubricant in the house: rubbing a bar of soap or candle wax on the exposed pin works as a stopgap.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep fingers clear of the hinge knuckles while swinging the door, support the door if more than one pin is ever out at a time, and ventilate briefly when using aerosol sprays. No personal data or money involved.
