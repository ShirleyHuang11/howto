## Goal

A corked still wine bottle is opened without a corkscrew, using the least risky available substitute and accepting that this is a backup plan, not a party trick.

## Preconditions

- A still wine bottle with a cork, not sparkling wine.
- At least one substitute tool: a long screw and pliers, or a sturdy shoe and solid wall.
- Eye protection or glasses if using impact, plus a towel and clear work area.

## Steps

1. **Choose the safer method first.** [BRANCH: screw and pliers | shoe against wall | push-in last resort] prefer screw and pliers when available, because glass impact is harder to control. → *Expect:* one method is chosen before anyone starts improvising.
2. **Remove the foil.** Cut or peel the capsule so the cork top and bottle lip are visible. → *Expect:* the cork can move without snagging foil.
3. **Use screw and pliers if available.** Drive a long screw into the cork center until 2 cm remains above the cork, then pull the screw upward with pliers. → *Expect:* the cork rises slowly and remains mostly intact.
4. **Set up the shoe method safely.** Put the bottle base inside the heel cup of a flat-soled sturdy shoe, keep the bottle horizontal, and choose a solid wall that cannot dent or shatter. → *Expect:* the shoe cushions the bottle base and your hands stay away from the impact point.
5. **Tap, do not smash.** Hit the shoe sole flat against the wall in short controlled taps, checking the cork after every few taps. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* glass can break and wine can spray, so stop immediately if you see cracks, chips, or leakage. → *Expect:* the cork creeps outward a few millimeters at a time.
6. **Pull the cork before it flies.** Once enough cork is exposed to grip, twist and pull it out by hand. → *Expect:* the cork comes free without launching across the room.
7. **Push in only as the last resort.** If extraction fails, use the blunt handle of a wooden spoon to push the cork into the bottle slowly over a sink. → *Expect:* the cork drops in and wine may rise or spill slightly.
8. **Strain the first pour.** Pour through a clean strainer if cork fragments or dust are present. → *Expect:* drinkable wine reaches the glass without grit.

## Decision points

- Bottle is sparkling or pressurized → do not use these methods.
- Wall or shoe feels flimsy → stop and use the screw method or borrow a corkscrew.
- Cork is old and crumbly → extraction may fail, so prepare a strainer before continuing.
- Bottle shows any crack → discard the contents and handle glass as broken.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cork will not move:** detect after 30 controlled taps or a firm screw pull → switch methods rather than increasing force.
- **F2 Screw tears out:** detect by cork crumbs around the screw hole → drive a second screw beside the first and pull both together.
- **F3 Wine sprays:** detect by wet cork or sudden spurts → move over a sink and slow every motion.
- **F4 Glass damage:** detect by crack, chip, or leak → stop, wrap the bottle in a towel, and discard safely.

## Verification

The bottle is open, no glass is damaged, and the wine can be poured without cork grit after straining if needed.

## Variations

- Key method: risky for hands and cork integrity; use only if the cork is soft and you can angle the key deeply.
- Pump method: some needle pumps work, but overpressure can fail glass or cork.
- Picnic solution: asking a neighbor for a corkscrew beats most improvised physics.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from broken glass and impact. The shoe method needs the safety caveat: use gentle taps against a solid surface, keep the bottle cushioned, check constantly, and abandon the method at the first sign of glass damage.
