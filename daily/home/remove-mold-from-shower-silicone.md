## Goal

Black or pink surface mold on shower silicone is killed, lifted, rinsed away, and the joint is left dry enough to judge whether it needs re-caulking.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: gloves, eye protection, old toothbrush or grout brush, paper towels or cotton coils, bleach gel or a bleach bathroom mold remover, water, sponge.
- Ventilation: bathroom fan on and window or door open.
- The shower has not recently been treated with vinegar, acid descaler, ammonia, or another cleaner.
- ⚠️ Never mix cleaners. Bleach plus acid or ammonia can make toxic gas.

## Steps

1. **Ventilate and clear the shower.** Turn on the fan, open the door or window, remove bottles, and put on gloves and eye protection. → *Expect:* steady airflow, bare silicone, and no skin contact with cleaner.
2. **Rinse loose soil first.** Wet the silicone with plain water and wipe away soap film so bleach reaches the mold instead of the scum. → *Expect:* the bead looks cleaner but stained areas remain.
3. **Apply bleach gel to the silicone.** Run a thin bead of gel directly along the stained silicone, then press paper towel or cotton onto vertical spots to keep it wet. → *Expect:* cleaner stays in contact with the mold instead of sliding down.
4. **Let it dwell.** Leave the gel for 20-30 minutes with air moving. [BRANCH: stain lightens | stain unchanged] if unchanged, extend once to 45 minutes. → *Expect:* dark spotting fades or turns brown-gray.
5. **Scrub gently along the bead.** Use the brush with light pressure so you do not tear the silicone edge. → *Expect:* loosened mold wipes off without gouging the sealant.
6. **Rinse completely.** Flush the area with plenty of water, then wipe with a clean wet sponge until no slick cleaner remains. → *Expect:* silicone feels clean, not slippery or bleachy.
7. **Dry and inspect.** Towel-dry the joint, keep the fan running, and check for black growth inside the silicone, peeling, gaps, or softness. → *Expect:* surface mold is gone or clearly identified as embedded damage.
8. **Prevent the return.** Squeegee after showers, keep bottles off the bead, run the fan 20 minutes, and leave the curtain or door open. → *Expect:* the joint dries between uses.

## Decision points

- Stain disappears after one dwell → keep the silicone and focus on drying habits.
- Black remains inside translucent or torn silicone → remove and re-caulk with mold-resistant bathroom silicone.
- Silicone lifts from tile or tub → re-caulk, because cleaner cannot fix a failed water seal.
- Natural stone nearby → avoid bleach contact on stone and rinse overspray immediately.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sharp chemical smell:** leave the room, ventilate, and do not resume until the smell clears → rinse with water only once safe.
- **F2 Silicone tears during scrubbing:** stop scrubbing and plan re-caulking → torn sealant can leak behind the wall.
- **F3 Mold returns within days:** treat it as a moisture problem → improve fan use, drying, and check for leaks behind the joint.
- **F4 Bleach spot on fabric:** rinse fabric immediately with cold water → accept that color loss may be permanent.

## Verification

The silicone is rinsed, dry to the touch, free of removable mold, and any remaining embedded staining is marked for re-caulking rather than more scrubbing.

## Variations

- Bleach pen: works for small corners, but keep the same dwell, rinse, and ventilation rules.
- Oxygen bleach: safer around some surfaces, but slower and less effective on established silicone mold.
- Rental home: photograph failed silicone before re-caulking or asking maintenance.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from chemicals and ventilation. ⚠️ Use only one cleaner in the session, never mix bleach with vinegar, descaler, ammonia, toilet cleaner, or unknown residues.
