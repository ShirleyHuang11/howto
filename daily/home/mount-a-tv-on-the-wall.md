## Goal

A TV is mounted level on a wall using hardware rated for the TV weight, wall structure, and VESA pattern, with cables routed safely.

## Preconditions

- TV, compatible wall mount, VESA screws, level, stud finder, drill, bits, tape measure, and helper.
- The mount's manual and the TV weight from the TV label or specifications.
- Knowledge of the wall type: wood studs, metal studs, masonry, or drywall only.
- Safe cable plan that does not run standard power cords inside the wall.

## Steps

1. **Confirm compatibility.** Match the TV's VESA pattern and weight to the mount rating → *Expect:* the mount manual lists a pattern and weight rating that cover the TV.
2. **Choose viewing height.** Mark the desired screen center and account for where the mount brackets attach to the TV → *Expect:* the wall marks represent final screen height, not just bracket height.
3. **Find structure.** Locate studs or masonry and mark fastener positions [BRANCH: studs | masonry | drywall-only] → *Expect:* marks align with solid structure or rated anchors appropriate for the load.
4. **Plan cable route.** Choose surface raceway, in-wall rated cables, or a recessed power kit installed as allowed locally → *Expect:* power and signal cables have a legal, accessible path.
5. **Attach brackets to TV.** Use the correct VESA screws and spacers so screws do not bottom out or press the panel → *Expect:* brackets sit flat and screws are snug without flexing the TV back.
6. **Drill pilot holes.** Drill at the marked locations using the mount manual's bit size → *Expect:* holes are straight and centered in the marked support.
7. **Fasten wall plate.** Lag into studs or use rated anchors for the wall type; ⚠️ *Irreversible:* drilling holes can hit wires or pipes, so confirm location before drilling → *Expect:* wall plate is tight and level.
8. **Hang with a helper.** Lift the TV onto the wall plate and engage safety screws, locks, or straps → *Expect:* the TV is fully seated and cannot lift off accidentally.
9. **Connect and check.** Connect cables, tilt or swivel gently, and verify clearance → *Expect:* the screen remains level and cables do not pinch.

## Decision points

- Studs are not where the mount needs them → use a wider mount, a mounting board secured to studs, or a different location.
- Wall is metal stud → use hardware rated for metal studs and the TV weight, not wood lag screws.
- Wall is drywall only → use only anchors explicitly rated for the TV and mount load, or avoid mounting there.
- Fireplace location seems attractive → check heat, neck angle, and cable access before committing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missed stud:** detect a lag that spins or pulls out, recover by stopping and relocating into solid structure.
- **F2 TV sits crooked:** detect visible tilt, recover by loosening adjustment screws and leveling the mount or bracket hooks.
- **F3 Wrong VESA screws:** detect screws bottoming out or staying loose, recover with correct length screws and spacers.
- **F4 Cable pinch:** detect no signal or stressed cable bends, recover by re-routing with more slack.
- **F5 Wall damage:** detect cracking drywall or loose anchors, recover by removing load and repairing before remounting.

## Verification

The TV remains level, all mount locks are engaged, and a gentle controlled tug does not move the wall plate or lift the TV off the mount.

## Variations

- Masonry wall: use masonry bits and anchors rated for the wall material.
- Full-motion mount: use stronger structure because extended arms increase leverage.
- Rental home: use a freestanding stand unless wall holes are allowed.
- Soundbar: plan its bracket and cable path before final TV height is set.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from falling equipment, hidden utilities, and electrical code issues. Use a helper for lifting, never bury a normal power cord in a wall, and hire a professional if structure or wiring is uncertain.
