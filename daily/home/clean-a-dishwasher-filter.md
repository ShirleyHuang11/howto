## Goal

The dishwasher filter is free of food debris, the spray arms are unobstructed, and the filter is locked back in place.

## Preconditions

- Dishwasher is empty, cool, and not mid-cycle.
- You have gloves, dish soap, a soft brush, a cloth, and the appliance manual if the filter latch is unfamiliar.
- The sink strainer or bin is ready for trapped food scraps.

## Steps

1. **Remove the lower rack.** Slide it out and set it on a towel or stable floor space. → *Expect:* the filter assembly at the bottom is easy to reach.
2. **Unlock the cylindrical filter.** Turn the handle in the marked direction and lift it out. [BRANCH: twist-lock | lift-out] follow the printed arrows or manual for your model. → *Expect:* the main filter is loose without force.
3. **Lift the flat filter plate.** Remove the lower mesh or plate if your model has one. → *Expect:* the sump area is visible.
4. **Rinse debris away.** Rinse food from the filters under warm water, keeping solids out of the sink drain. → *Expect:* the mesh openings are visible.
5. **Brush the mesh gently.** Use dish soap and a soft brush for greasy film, then rinse clean. → *Expect:* water flows through the filter without pooling.
6. **Check the sump and spray arms.** Wipe loose debris from the bottom and clear spray arm holes with a toothpick if blocked. → *Expect:* spray arms spin freely and holes are open.
7. **Refit and lock the filter.** Replace the flat plate, seat the cylinder, and twist until it locks. → *Expect:* the filter does not lift out when tugged lightly.
8. **Run a rinse cycle.** Start a short rinse with no dishes and listen for abnormal grinding. → *Expect:* water drains cleanly and no error appears.

## Decision points

- Broken glass is in the sump → wear cut-resistant gloves and lift pieces out with care before rinsing.
- Filter is greasy after normal rinsing → soak it in warm soapy water for ten minutes, then brush again.
- Spray arm will not spin → remove it only if the manual shows how, otherwise clear visible holes in place.
- Odor returns quickly → clean door seals and run a dishwasher cleaner after the filter is confirmed seated.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Filter will not unlock:** detect a stuck handle, recover by checking the lock direction and soaking dried food around the rim.
- **F2 Filter is reinstalled crooked:** detect rocking or a gap, recover by removing both parts and seating the flat filter first.
- **F3 Food returns onto dishes:** detect grit after a cycle, recover by confirming the filter is locked and spray arms are clear.
- **F4 Drain error appears:** detect standing water or an error code, recover by checking the filter, then the drain hose and disposal knockout if recently installed.

## Verification

The filter is clean, locked, spray arms spin freely, and a rinse cycle drains without grit or error.

## Variations

- `self-cleaning-filter`: some older models have grinders, but visible screens still need inspection.
- `hard-water`: mineral scale may need a dishwasher-safe descaler after debris is removed.
- `heavy-cooking`: clean weekly when sauces, rice, or seeds are common.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch for broken glass, sharp bones, and hot water, and never run the dishwasher with the bottom filter removed.
