## Goal

Fruit flies are trapped, their breeding source is removed, and the kitchen is set up to prevent a rebound.

## Preconditions

- Apple cider vinegar, dish soap, a small cup or jar, and plastic wrap or paper cone.
- Trash bags, sponge, and access to sink drains.
- Produce and recycling can be inspected.

## Steps

1. **Find the source.** Check ripe fruit, trash, recycling, compost, damp cloths, and forgotten potatoes or onions. → *Expect:* at least one feeding or breeding spot is found or ruled out.
2. **Remove spoiled food.** Bag rotten produce and take it outside, not just into an indoor bin. → *Expect:* the strongest smell source leaves the kitchen.
3. **Wash sticky surfaces.** Wipe counters, bin lids, bottle rings, and fruit bowls. → *Expect:* no sweet residue remains where flies land.
4. **Set a vinegar trap.** Add apple cider vinegar and one drop dish soap to a cup, then cover with poked plastic wrap or a paper cone. → *Expect:* flies can enter and cannot easily escape.
5. **Place traps near activity.** Put traps near fruit bowls, sinks, or bins, away from open windows. → *Expect:* flies gather around the trap within hours.
6. **Check drains.** Pour boiling water down suspect drains and scrub the visible rim. → *Expect:* drain slime and odor are reduced.
7. **Repeat for two days.** Refresh traps daily and keep counters dry. → *Expect:* fly count drops each day.
8. **Prevent return.** Refrigerate ripe fruit, empty compost often, and rinse bottles before recycling. → *Expect:* no new swarm forms after traps are removed.

## Decision points

- Tiny flies come from a drain → treat as drain flies and clean the drain more aggressively.
- Flies appear near houseplants → let soil dry and use sticky plant traps.
- You cannot remove compost daily → freeze scraps until pickup.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Trap ignored:** detect no flies after 12 hours, recover by moving it closer to the source and using fresh vinegar.
- **F2 Rebound swarm:** detect more flies after initial success, recover by finding a missed source behind bins or appliances.
- **F3 Drain breeding:** detect flies rising from a covered drain test, recover by scrubbing the drain and repeating hot-water rinses.
- **F4 Open food attractant:** detect flies on fruit despite traps, recover by refrigerating or sealing produce.

## Verification

No fruit flies are visible around fruit, sink, trash, or traps during a 2-minute kitchen check.

## Variations

- Wine trap: use a splash of red wine if vinegar is unavailable.
- Paper cone: tape a cone into a jar so the narrow end stops above the liquid.
- Commercial trap: still remove the source, since traps alone do not stop breeding.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep traps away from children and pets, label jars if they look drinkable, and handle spoiled food in sealed bags.
