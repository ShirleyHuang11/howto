## Goal

Locate likely water leak sources and decide whether to shut off water or call a professional.

## Preconditions

- You can safely access sinks, toilets, appliances, and the water meter if your home has one.
- No active electrical hazard is present near standing water.
- You have a flashlight, paper towels, and food coloring or dye for toilet testing.

## Steps

1. **Look for urgent hazards.** Check for water near outlets, breaker panels, ceilings, or sagging drywall. → *Expect:* hazards are identified before touching plumbing.
2. **Stop using water.** Turn off faucets, appliances, irrigation, humidifiers, and ice makers. → *Expect:* no intentional water use is happening.
3. **Check the water meter.** Watch the low-flow indicator or record the meter reading for 10 to 15 minutes. → *Expect:* movement with all water off suggests an active supply-side leak.
4. **Inspect visible fixtures.** Wipe supply valves and trap joints under sinks, then look for new droplets. → *Expect:* wet joints show the likely leak location.
5. **Check toilets with dye.** Put dye in the tank, wait 10 minutes without flushing, then inspect the bowl. → *Expect:* colored bowl water indicates a leaking flapper or flush valve.
6. **Check appliances.** Inspect water heater, washing machine hoses, dishwasher base, refrigerator line, and HVAC condensate drain. → *Expect:* pooling, corrosion, or damp flooring points to a source.
7. **Trace stains and sounds.** Follow ceiling stains, wall dampness, musty smells, or hissing toward the nearest plumbing line. → *Expect:* the likely source area narrows.
8. **Shut off water if the leak is active.** Close the fixture valve or the water main if water is escaping continuously. → *Expect:* flow slows or stops.
9. **Call a professional when the source is hidden or damage is spreading.** Contact a plumber, building maintenance, or water mitigation company. → *Expect:* the issue is handed to someone equipped to open walls or dry structures.

## Decision points

- Meter moves but no fixture is wet → suspect a hidden supply leak and call a plumber.
- Meter is still but staining grows → suspect roof, drain, condensation, or intermittent appliance leak.
- Water is near electrical equipment → stay clear, shut power only from a dry safe location, and call emergency service or an electrician.
- You live in a multi-unit building → notify building management because the leak may come from another unit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Meter inaccessible:** detect locked pit or shared meter → check fixture valves and call the utility, landlord, or plumber.
- **F2 Toilet dye false result:** detect recent flush or cleaner tablets → repeat with clean tank water and no flushing.
- **F3 Hidden leak continues:** detect meter movement after closing fixture valves → shut the main and call a plumber.
- **F4 Mold odor appears:** detect musty smell or visible growth → stop moisture and call a remediation professional for large areas.

## Verification

The suspected leak source is identified or isolated, water flow has stopped or been shut off, and a professional has been called for hidden, electrical, structural, or continuing leaks.

## Variations

- Apartment: you may not have a private meter, so rely on fixture isolation and maintenance reporting.
- Well system: pump cycling while no water is used can indicate a leak.
- Smart meter: use the utility portal or app to check continuous-flow alerts.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from electrical contact, contaminated water, and structural damage. Photos may include personal belongings, so keep claim documentation focused on affected areas.
