## Goal

Household waste is separated into the locally correct streams — recyclables clean and sorted, contaminants kept out — and each stream is in its right bin.

## Preconditions

- You know (or will look up now) the *local* rules: municipal websites/bin lids list accepted materials — recycling is intensely local, and wrong guesses contaminate whole batches.
- Separate containers or bags for at least: recycling, general waste; plus glass/organics where collected separately.

## Steps

1. **Learn the local accept-list once, and post it.** Find your municipality's list; stick a printout by the bins. → *Expect:* you can answer "does this town take greasy pizza boxes / plastic film / glass in the bin?" from the sheet, not vibes.
2. **Sort each item by material.** Paper/cardboard | plastics (check the accepted resin numbers) | metal cans | glass (often its own drop-off) | organics | everything else → general waste. → *Expect:* every item lands in a stream on the list — unlisted items default to general waste, not hopeful recycling ("wishcycling" ruins loads).
3. **Empty and rinse containers.** A quick cold rinse and shake for cans, bottles, tubs — food residue is the #1 contaminant. Caps: follow local rules (often: plastic caps back on the bottle; metal lids inside the can, crimped). → *Expect:* no container with visible food/liquid goes in.
4. **Flatten cardboard; keep paper dry.** Break boxes flat; wet or food-soaked paper (used tissues, greasy box bottoms) is general waste/organics. → *Expect:* cardboard flat-stacked; only clean dry fiber in the paper stream.
5. **Keep the known saboteurs out.** Plastic bags/film (jam sorting machines — return to supermarket collection points instead), batteries and electronics (fire risk — dedicated drop-offs), broken glass/ceramics (wrap, general waste unless a glass point takes it), clothing (textile banks). → *Expect:* none of these in the household recycling bin.
6. **Bag or bin per local convention and set out on the right day.** Some systems want loose recyclables, some want specific bags. → *Expect:* each stream in its container, out on its collection day (`daily/home` calendar note helps).

## Decision points

- Genuinely unsure about an item after checking the list → general waste is the *correct* choice; one wrong item can contaminate a truckload of good recycling.
- Deposit-return region (bottle deposits) → deposit bottles/cans go back to the return machine for the refund, not the bin.
- Hazardous items (paint, chemicals, medications) → never in any household bin; municipal hazardous-waste points, and pharmacies for medications.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Battery accidentally binned:** retrieve it if findable — bin-truck and facility fires from batteries are common and serious; set up a dedicated battery jar so it stops happening.
- **F2 Collection refused (tagged/left behind):** the tag states why — re-sort out the named contaminant and re-present next cycle.
- **F3 Smelly recycling bin:** rinsing (step 3) lapsed — rinse the bin itself, restart the habit.

## Verification

Every stream's bin holds only its listed materials, containers are empty-and-rinsed, no bags/batteries/food lurk in the recycling, and the posted list means the next sort needs no guessing.

## Variations

- `de`: multi-stream (Gelbe Tonne, Papier, Bio, Rest, and Pfand deposit returns) — the posted-list step is essential.
- `jp`: municipality-specific schedules with burnable/non-burnable/PET/etc. and designated bags; days and bag types are enforced.
- Single-stream regions (`us` typical): everything recyclable in one bin — the clean-and-dry and saboteur rules still decide what belongs.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: wrap broken glass before binning, keep batteries out of everything, and shred or tear documents with personal data before they enter the paper stream.
