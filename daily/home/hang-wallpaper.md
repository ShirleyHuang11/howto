## Goal

Wallpaper drops are measured, pasted, hung plumb, smoothed flat, and trimmed neatly at edges.

## Preconditions

- Wall is clean, dry, sound, and sized or primed if the paper maker requires it.
- You have wallpaper, paste or ready-pasted paper supplies, plumb line or level, brush, smoother, knife, sponge, table, and bucket.
- Pattern repeat, batch number, and hanging direction have been checked.

## Steps

1. **Plan the first line.** Mark a plumb line near the starting corner rather than trusting the corner itself. → *Expect:* a vertical guide is visible.
2. **Measure and cut with overlap.** Cut the first drop to wall height plus top and bottom overlap. → *Expect:* the drop is long enough for trimming.
3. **Paste the paper or wall.** [BRANCH: paste-the-paper | paste-the-wall] follow the roll label exactly for paste location and soak time. → *Expect:* adhesive is even with dry edges avoided.
4. **Book the paper if required.** Fold pasted sides together without creasing and wait the listed time. → *Expect:* the paper relaxes and expands evenly.
5. **Hang the first drop plumb.** Align one edge to the guide, leaving overlap at ceiling and skirting. → *Expect:* the drop hangs straight with extra at both ends.
6. **Smooth out bubbles.** Brush from the center outward, wiping paste from the face with a damp sponge. → *Expect:* the surface lies flat without trapped air.
7. **Trim edges sharply.** Press into corners with a straightedge and cut with a fresh blade. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm the paper has not shifted before cutting. → *Expect:* ceiling and base edges are clean.
8. **Match and hang the next drop.** Align the pattern at eye level first, butt seams without overlap, smooth, and trim. → *Expect:* the seam is tight and the pattern continues.

## Decision points

- Pattern repeat wastes more paper than expected → recalculate drops before cutting the next length.
- Bubble will not smooth out → lift the nearest edge while paste is wet and re-lay the paper.
- Seams gap as paper dries → check that booking time and paste coverage match the roll instructions.
- Wall is powdery or glossy → stop and prime or size before hanging more paper.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 First drop is not plumb:** detect a drifting seam, recover by lifting it while wet and resetting to the guide.
- **F2 Paste stains the face:** detect shiny or cloudy marks, recover by wiping gently with clean water before it dries.
- **F3 Blade tears paper:** detect ragged trim, recover by changing blades and trimming against a firm straightedge.
- **F4 Pattern mismatch grows:** detect offset motifs at seams, recover by aligning at eye level and accepting tiny variation near ceiling or floor.

## Verification

The hung drops are plumb, seams are butted, bubbles are smoothed, and trimmed edges are clean after the paste begins to set.

## Variations

- `non-woven-paper`: often paste-the-wall and skip booking unless the label says otherwise.
- `vinyl-paper`: use the adhesive recommended for heavier coverings.
- `feature-wall`: start from the visual center so the pattern balances.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from blades, ladders, and adhesive on finished surfaces. Keep blades capped between cuts and confirm placement before each irreversible trim.
