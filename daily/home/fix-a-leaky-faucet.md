## Goal

A dripping sink faucet stops leaking after the worn washer, cartridge, or O-ring is identified and replaced without flooding the cabinet.

## Preconditions

- You can reach the shutoff valves under the sink or the main water shutoff.
- You have towels, a small screwdriver, adjustable wrench, replacement washers or cartridge, plumber's grease, and a bowl for parts.
- Turn the water off first; if a shutoff will not close, pipes are corroded, or the faucet body is cracked, call a licensed plumber.

## Steps

1. **Shut off the water.** Turn both under-sink valves clockwise, then open the faucet to drain pressure. → *Expect:* water slows to drips and stops.
2. **Protect the drain and finish.** Plug the drain, lay a towel in the basin, and wrap shiny parts before using a wrench. → *Expect:* dropped screws cannot fall into the drain.
3. **Remove the handle.** Pry off the cap if present, remove the set screw, and lift the handle straight up. → *Expect:* the valve stem or cartridge top is visible.
4. **Identify the valve type.** [BRANCH: compression | cartridge] compression faucets have a threaded stem and rubber washer at the bottom; cartridge faucets pull out as a plastic or brass cartridge. → *Expect:* you know which replacement path to use.
5. **Service a compression stem.** Unscrew the packing nut, remove the stem, replace the bottom washer, and replace the O-ring if it is flattened or cracked. → *Expect:* fresh rubber sits flat with no splits.
6. **Service a cartridge valve.** Note the cartridge orientation, remove the retaining clip or nut, pull the cartridge straight out, and match it exactly at the store if needed. → *Expect:* the new cartridge matches length, tabs, and ports.
7. **Lubricate seals lightly.** Apply a thin film of plumber's grease to O-rings only, not to ceramic discs or threaded seats unless the maker says so. → *Expect:* seals slide without bunching.
8. **Reassemble in order.** Reinstall the stem or cartridge, retaining hardware, handle, and cap without forcing cross-threaded parts. → *Expect:* the handle moves normally through its range.
9. **Restore water slowly.** Open the shutoff valves a quarter turn at a time while watching the faucet and cabinet. → *Expect:* no spray, seep, or sudden cabinet leak.
10. **Test hot and cold.** Run each side, close the faucet firmly, and wait two minutes. → *Expect:* the spout and handle base stay dry.

## Decision points

- Leak comes from the spout → replace the washer on a compression faucet or the cartridge on a cartridge faucet.
- Leak comes from around the handle → replace the packing O-ring or cartridge seal.
- Shutoff valve leaks when touched → stop and call a plumber before continuing.
- Faucet uses ceramic discs, wall valves, or a single-control shower valve → use the manufacturer's cartridge instructions or call a plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Water will not shut off:** flow continues after both valves are closed, close the main shutoff or stop and call a plumber.
- **F2 Handle stuck:** handle will not lift after the screw is out, use penetrating oil and a handle puller instead of prying against the sink.
- **F3 New part leaks:** dripping continues after replacement, reopen the faucet and check orientation, seat damage, and exact part match.
- **F4 Cabinet leak appears:** water drips from supply connections, close the valves immediately and tighten only the disturbed connection.

## Verification

With the faucet closed after a full hot and cold test, no drip forms at the spout, handle base, supply valves, or cabinet connections for five minutes.

## Variations

- Two-handle faucets: service only the leaking hot or cold side if the drip temperature identifies it.
- Single-handle cartridge faucets: photograph each clip and retaining nut before removal.
- Older compression faucets: a damaged valve seat may need a seat wrench or plumber replacement.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: water can damage cabinets and floors. Shut water off before disassembly, keep towels ready, and call a licensed plumber for seized shutoffs, corroded pipes, wall leaks, or any leak you cannot stop immediately.
