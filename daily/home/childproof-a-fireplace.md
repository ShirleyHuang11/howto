## Goal

Reduce a child's access to fireplace heat, tools, ash, sharp edges, and ignition controls while keeping fire safety equipment ready.

## Preconditions

- The fireplace is completely cool before installing guards or moving tools.
- You have a fireplace gate or guard rated for the fireplace type and the child's age.
- Smoke and carbon monoxide alarms are installed in appropriate areas.
- Matches, lighters, fuel, and fireplace tools can be moved out of reach.

## Steps

1. **Cool and clear the area.** Confirm there are no hot embers, hot glass, or active flames before working. → *Expect:* the fireplace can be touched safely around the exterior.
2. **Choose a stable barrier.** Select a gate or guard that surrounds the hearth and cannot be tipped by a child. → *Expect:* the barrier fits the fireplace width and floor layout.
3. **Install the gate or guard.** Anchor or brace it according to the product instructions. → *Expect:* the guard stays in place when pushed lightly.
4. **Block hot access.** Keep the guard closed whenever the fireplace is hot or cooling. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* burns can happen in seconds, so no child should have access to hot glass, metal, or embers. → *Expect:* a child cannot reach the hot surface from the room side.
5. **Use the screen correctly.** Close the mesh or glass screen when the fireplace is in use. → *Expect:* sparks and rolling logs are contained behind the screen.
6. **Move tools away.** Store pokers, shovels, brushes, and log holders behind a locked barrier or in an adult-only area. → *Expect:* sharp and heavy tools are out of reach.
7. **Secure ignition items.** Move matches, lighters, starter blocks, remote controls, and gas keys to locked storage. → *Expect:* a child cannot start the fireplace.
8. **Check hearth edges.** Add edge padding only if it is heat-safe and placed where it will not contact hot surfaces. → *Expect:* sharp corners are reduced without creating a fire hazard.
9. **Test alarms.** Press the test button on smoke and carbon monoxide alarms near sleeping and living areas. → *Expect:* alarms sound and batteries or power are working.
10. **Set the house rule.** Teach that the fireplace area is adult-only and supervise even when barriers are installed. → *Expect:* the barrier is treated as backup, not the only protection.

## Decision points

- The fireplace has hot glass doors → use a guard that prevents touching glass even after flames are out.
- The guard cannot be anchored → choose a wider freestanding model with anti-tip design or do not use the fireplace around children.
- Gas controls are exposed → add a child-resistant cover or keep the gas key locked away.
- An alarm fails its test → replace batteries or the alarm before using the fireplace.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Barrier tips or slides:** detect by movement during a light push → recover by anchoring correctly or replacing it with a stable guard.
- **F2 Tool access remains:** detect by tools within reach through or around the guard → recover by storing tools in a locked closet or high cabinet.
- **F3 Hot surface reachable:** detect by an arm-width gap near glass or metal → recover by widening or repositioning the barrier.
- **F4 Alarm not working:** detect by no sound during test → recover by replacing batteries, checking power, or replacing the unit.

## Verification

A child cannot reach the fireplace opening, hot glass, tools, fuel, ignition controls, or sharp hearth edges, and smoke and carbon monoxide alarms pass their tests.

## Variations

- Wood-burning fireplace: remove ash only when fully cool and store it in a metal container outside.
- Gas fireplace: keep the remote and gas key inaccessible between uses.
- Electric fireplace: block hot vents and controls even if there is no flame.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because burns, carbon monoxide, fire, and sharp tools can cause serious harm. Barriers do not replace supervision, and a fireplace should not be used if alarms fail, vents are blocked, or a child can reach hot areas.
