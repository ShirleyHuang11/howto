## Goal

Windows cleaned streak-free on both reachable sides, frames and sills included, without anyone leaning out of anything to reach the unreachable.

## Preconditions

- Glass cleaner (or the classic: water with a splash of vinegar and a drop of dish soap), two lint-free cloths or a squeegee with a microfiber, and a separate rag for frames and sills.
- An overcast day or shaded timing beats bright sun, which flash-dries cleaner into streaks before you can wipe it.
- **The reach rule set in advance:** you clean what you can reach standing on the floor or a stable step stool indoors. Outside upper-floor glass belongs to extendable tools or professionals, never to leaning out (`daily/home/change-a-lightbulb`'s ladder sanity, with gravity's stakes raised).

## Steps

1. **Start dry: vacuum or brush the frames, tracks, and sill.** Wet cleaning over grit makes mud and scratches glass. Sliding-window tracks give up their debris to a vacuum crevice tool or a dry brush. → *Expect:* dry dust and dead flies gone before any liquid appears.
2. **Wash the frames and sill first with the separate rag.** Frames drip dirty water; doing them after the glass undoes the glass. → *Expect:* clean frames, with drips landing on still-dirty glass where they cost nothing.
3. **Apply cleaner to the glass modestly.** A few sprays, or a wrung-out cloth of the vinegar mix; pooling liquid is what runs into streaks and frame corners. → *Expect:* a thin even film across the pane.
4. **Squeegee top-to-bottom in overlapping strokes, wiping the blade between strokes.** No squeegee: wipe in one deliberate pattern (say, horizontal), then buff with the dry second cloth in the perpendicular pattern. The two-direction trick makes any remaining streak announce which pass caused it. → *Expect:* glass clearing behind each stroke; blade or cloth wiped each pass.
5. **Detail the edges and corners.** A dry cloth-wrapped finger along the pane's perimeter picks up the liquid the main strokes pushed there. → *Expect:* no drying pool at any corner.
6. **Repeat on the other side where reachable, then step back in raking light.** Streaks hide straight-on and show at an angle. → *Expect:* both reachable faces clear; any streak found gets one buff with the dry cloth.

## Decision points

- Which side is the dirty one: a streak's direction tells you (your two-direction patterns from step 4 differ per side on purpose, one horizontal inside, one vertical outside, so a diagonal glance assigns blame instantly).
- Frequency honestly: twice a year for most homes, quarterly for street-facing city glass; sills and tracks more often since they collect regardless.
- Hard-water spots and years of neglect: neat vinegar dwelling a few minutes, then the normal recipe; true mineral etching is permanent and stops being a cleaning problem.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Streaks everywhere:** too much product, a dirty cloth, or the sun. Re-do with a barely-damp pass and a clean dry buff, in shade.
- **F2 Lint snow on the glass:** the cloth is the wrong kind; paper towels and fluffy towels shed. Microfiber or crumpled newspaper (the old trick that still works) replace them.
- **F3 Dirty drips from above after finishing:** the frames-first order (step 2) was skipped, or an upper neighbor's sill drains onto yours; one spot-buff fixes the pane, and the order fixes the future.
- **F4 A window that will not come clean is fogged *between* panes:** that is a failed double-glazing seal, not dirt; no cleaner reaches it. It goes on the `housing/report-a-repair-to-your-landlord` list or the homeowner's quote list.

## Verification

Glass reads clear in raking light from both reachable sides, corners are dry, frames and tracks hold no mud lines, and no part of the job involved a body further out of a window than a forearm.

## Variations

- `de`/`nl` tilt-turn windows rotate to present the outside face indoors, which converts upper-floor outsides into reachable surfaces; learn the handle positions before wrestling the sash.
- Extendable water-fed poles do multi-story outsides from the ground, and their pure-water systems skip the squeegee entirely.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk is a single word: falls. The reach rule and a stable stool are the whole safety story; every other failure in this recipe costs only re-wiping.
