## Goal

A struggling houseplant is diagnosed for water, light, pests, and roots, then stabilized with the smallest useful intervention.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: scissors or pruners, gloves, watering can, towel, fresh mix and pot if root rot is suspected.
- The plant can be moved to a bright work area for inspection.
- You can accept that some plants are too far gone to recover.

## Steps

1. **Check soil moisture first.** Probe 3-5 cm deep, not just the surface. [BRANCH: dry | wet | evenly moist] match the next action to the reading. → *Expect:* a real moisture diagnosis.
2. **Read the light symptoms.** Look for leggy growth, leaning, scorch, or pale leaves. → *Expect:* light is identified as too low, too harsh, or probably acceptable.
3. **Inspect leaves and stems.** Check undersides, nodes, and soil for pests, webbing, scale, or sticky residue. → *Expect:* pests are found or reasonably ruled out.
4. **Slide out the root ball if soil is wet or sour.** Look for firm pale roots versus black mushy roots. → *Expect:* root health is known before more watering.
5. **Trim only dead material.** Cut crisp brown leaves and mushy stems with clean scissors; leave green tissue that still feeds the plant. → *Expect:* plant is cleaner without losing viable growth.
6. **Correct the main cause.** [BRANCH: underwatered | overwatered | low light | pests] soak and drain dry plants, dry and repot rotten ones, move light-starved plants, isolate and treat pests. → *Expect:* one primary fix is in place.
7. **Stabilize conditions.** Keep temperature steady, avoid fertilizer, and water only when the plant's soil target is reached. → *Expect:* no extra stress is added.
8. **Wait before judging.** Monitor weekly for new growth or firming leaves. → *Expect:* recovery is measured over weeks, not hours.

## Decision points

- Stems are mushy at the crown → salvage cuttings if possible and discard rotten base.
- All leaves are gone but stems are green → wait and water carefully.
- Roots are mostly gone → take cuttings or down-pot into barely moist mix.
- Plant is pest-infested near others → isolate immediately.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 You keep watering a wet plant:** leaves yellow faster and soil smells sour → stop, unpot, trim rot, and repot in airy mix.
- **F2 Sunburn after moving:** bleached or crispy patches appear → move to indirect light and acclimate slowly.
- **F3 Pests spread:** nearby plants show spots or sticky leaves → isolate the group and clean shelves.
- **F4 No green tissue remains:** plant is gone → discard soil, clean the pot, and start over.

## Verification

The plant has one diagnosed primary problem, dead tissue removed, corrected water or light conditions, and at least one viable green stem, leaf, or root to monitor.

## Variations

- Succulents: recovery usually means more light and less frequent water.
- Ferns: humidity and consistent moisture matter more than drying cycles.
- Winter: growth may pause, so judge by stability rather than fast new leaves.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wear gloves for sap, isolate pest plants, and discard diseased material in trash rather than compost when infection is suspected.
