## Goal

A tight ring is removed from a finger without cutting skin, worsening swelling, or delaying urgent care.

## Preconditions

- Cold water, soap or oil-based lubricant, dental floss or thin string, and a helper are available.
- The finger is not severely injured, numb, blue, or rapidly swelling.
- The person can stop immediately if pain or circulation worsens.

## Steps

1. **Check circulation.** Look for color, warmth, sensation, and ability to move the fingertip. → *Expect:* finger is pink or normal-colored and responsive.
2. **Reduce swelling.** Raise the hand above heart level for 5 to 10 minutes. → *Expect:* throbbing eases or swelling softens.
3. **Cool the finger.** Run cold water over the hand or apply a cool pack briefly. → *Expect:* finger feels cooler but not numb.
4. **Lubricate well.** Coat the finger and ring with soap, oil, lotion, or petroleum jelly. → *Expect:* ring rotates slightly.
5. **Twist and pull.** Rotate the ring while easing it over the knuckle. → *Expect:* ring advances a little without sharp pain.
6. **Use floss wrap.** Thread floss under the ring toward the hand, then wrap the finger snugly from ring to fingertip. → *Expect:* swollen tissue is compressed evenly.
7. **Unwind through the ring.** Pull the under-ring floss end so the wrap unwinds and walks the ring over the knuckle. → *Expect:* ring moves outward with each turn.
8. **Stop if unsafe.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* delayed care can cost circulation, so seek urgent help if the finger turns blue, numb, or very painful. → *Expect:* either ring is off or medical removal is chosen promptly.

## Decision points

- Finger is blue, gray, numb, or injured → skip home methods and seek emergency care.
- Ring is valuable but finger is worsening → prioritize the finger over the ring.
- Floss causes severe pain → unwrap immediately and stop.
- Knuckle is the only tight point → use more lubricant and twist slowly.
- Swelling follows a bite or allergic reaction → seek medical advice quickly.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Swelling increases:** detect tighter skin after attempts; recover by elevating and stopping repeated pulling.
- **F2 Floss too tight:** detect numbness or color change; recover by unwrapping at once.
- **F3 Skin tears:** detect bleeding or raw skin; recover by cleaning gently and seeking care if ring remains stuck.
- **F4 Ring edge digs in:** detect a groove deepening near the ring; recover by using lubrication or medical cutting.
- **F5 Panic pulling:** detect fast painful tugging; recover by pausing, cooling, and using a controlled method.

## Verification

The ring is off, the finger has normal color and sensation, and any skin irritation is minor and clean.

## Variations

- Dental tape: use it like floss if regular floss snaps.
- Window cleaner: some clinicians use it as lubricant, but avoid broken skin.
- Ring cutter: jewelers or urgent care can cut metal when home removal is unsafe.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because circulation can be compromised. Do not persist with home methods when numbness, blue color, injury, or severe swelling appears.
