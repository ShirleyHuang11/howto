## Goal

A non-electric bidet attachment is mounted under the toilet seat and connected to the toilet supply without leaks.

## Preconditions

- You have the bidet attachment, tee valve, bidet hose, washers, screwdriver, adjustable wrench, towel, and bucket.
- The toilet shutoff valve works and the existing seat can be removed.
- Turn water off first and do not install on damaged, corroded, or leaking supply parts.
- Call a plumber if the supply valve will not close, the toilet uses rigid pipe, or local code requires backflow protection beyond the kit.

## Steps

1. **Shut off water.** Turn the toilet shutoff valve clockwise, then flush to drain most tank water. → *Expect:* the tank does not refill.
2. **Place a towel.** Put a towel and bucket below the fill-valve connection under the tank. → *Expect:* small spills are contained.
3. **Disconnect the supply at the tank.** Loosen the nut where the supply line meets the toilet fill valve. → *Expect:* the tank inlet threads are exposed.
4. **Install the tee valve.** Fit the tee valve with the correct washer onto the tank inlet and hand-tighten straight. → *Expect:* the tee sits squarely below the tank.
5. **Reconnect the toilet supply.** Attach the existing supply line to the bottom or side of the tee as instructed. → *Expect:* the supply line is not kinked or twisted.
6. **Remove the toilet seat.** Open bolt caps, loosen the seat bolts, and lift the seat away. → *Expect:* the bowl mounting holes are open.
7. **Mount the bidet plate.** Place the attachment brackets over the bolt holes, reinstall the seat, and align the nozzle guard centered in the bowl. → *Expect:* the seat rests flat.
8. **Connect the bidet hose.** Attach one end to the tee and the other to the bidet inlet with washers in place. → *Expect:* both hose nuts thread by hand.
9. **Restore water slowly.** Open the shutoff a quarter turn at a time while watching all connections. → *Expect:* no dripping appears at the tee, hose, or tank.
10. **Adjust and test.** Turn the bidet control briefly while seated aside or with the seat lowered as the manual directs. → *Expect:* spray starts, stops, and aims into the bowl.

## Decision points

- Supply line is brittle, cracked, or too short → replace it before pressurizing the bidet.
- Tee valve will not thread by hand → stop, check washer placement, and avoid cross-threading.
- Nozzle hits the bowl rim → loosen the seat and recenter the mounting plate.
- Any leak continues after reseating washers → turn water off and call a plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tee leak:** water beads at the tank inlet, shut off water, remove the tee, check washer seating, and reinstall.
- **F2 Hose leak:** drip appears at a hose nut, hand-tighten plus a small wrench snug without crushing the washer.
- **F3 Seat rocks:** attachment plate adds uneven height, realign brackets and tighten both seat bolts evenly.
- **F4 Spray misses bowl:** nozzle angle is wrong, adjust the mounting plate or pressure control.

## Verification

After five minutes under pressure and three short spray tests, the seat is stable, the spray lands in the bowl, and every connection is dry.

## Variations

- Warm-water attachments: also connect to the sink hot-water line only if the kit supports it and routing is safe.
- Skirted toilets: use top-mount seat hardware if underside bolts are inaccessible.
- Electric bidet seats: follow outlet and GFCI requirements and use a licensed electrician if a receptacle is needed.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: pressurized supply leaks can damage floors. Shut water off before disconnecting lines, test slowly, keep the nozzle sanitary, and follow local plumbing backflow rules.
