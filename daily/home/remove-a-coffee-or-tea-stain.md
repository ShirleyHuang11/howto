## Goal

A fresh or set coffee or tea stain is lifted from washable fabric or carpet without spreading it or heat-setting the tannins.

## Preconditions

- Clean white cloths or paper towels.
- Cold water, mild dish soap, and white vinegar.
- Access to a hidden test spot on the fabric or carpet.

## Steps

1. **Blot the spill.** Press with a dry white cloth, working from the outside toward the center. → *Expect:* liquid transfers to the cloth without rubbing the stain wider.
2. **Flush with cold water.** Run cold water through the back of washable fabric, or dab cold water onto carpet. → *Expect:* the brown color lightens.
3. **Mix cleaner.** Combine 1 cup cold water, 1 teaspoon dish soap, and 1 tablespoon white vinegar. → *Expect:* solution is slightly soapy and not heavily sudsy.
4. **Test first.** Dab the mix on a hidden area and wait 5 minutes. → *Expect:* no color lifts onto the cloth and the surface texture stays normal.
5. **Treat the stain.** Apply the solution with a cloth, blot, then rinse with cold water. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* heat can set coffee and tea stains, so do not use hot water, a dryer, or an iron until the stain is gone. → *Expect:* stain fades after each blotting pass.
6. **Handle carpet moisture.** Press dry towels over carpet and weigh them down for 10 minutes if needed. → *Expect:* carpet feels damp, not soaked.
7. **Air dry and inspect.** Let the area dry fully before judging the result. → *Expect:* remaining shadow is easier to see once dry.

## Decision points

- Fabric is silk, wool, or dry-clean-only → stop and use a professional cleaner.
- Stain contains milk or sugar → remove residue with dish soap before vinegar treatment.
- Old stain remains after one round → repeat cold treatment before laundering.
- Carpet backing is getting wet → stop adding liquid and focus on blotting dry.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stain spreads:** detect a larger wet brown ring; recover by blotting from the outer edge inward with less liquid.
- **F2 Dye transfer:** detect fabric color on the cloth; recover by rinsing with cold water and stopping home treatment.
- **F3 Sticky residue:** detect tacky fibers after drying; recover by rinsing with plain cold water and blotting dry.
- **F4 Heat-set mark:** detect stain after dryer use; recover with repeated vinegar solution, but expect lower success.

## Verification

After full air drying, the treated area is the same color as the surrounding surface or is clearly lighter with no soap residue or wet ring.

## Variations

- Upholstery: use as little liquid as possible and blot often to avoid wetting cushion foam.
- White cotton: oxygen bleach may help after the soap and vinegar pass, if the care label allows it.
- Mug ring on table linens: soak in cold water and dish soap before laundering.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from mild cleaners. Do not mix vinegar with chlorine bleach, and test colored textiles before treating the visible stain.
