## Goal

Chewing gum is hardened or loosened, removed from the surface, and cleaned without spreading the sticky residue.

## Preconditions

- Ice, a plastic bag, spoon or dull scraper, cloths, dish soap, and optional cooking oil are available.
- The affected item can tolerate cold, light scraping, or spot cleaning.
- A small hidden area can be tested before using oil or solvent.

## Steps

1. **Identify the surface.** [BRANCH: fabric | carpet | hair | hard surface] → *Expect:* the removal method matches the material.
2. **Harden gum when possible.** Hold ice in a sealed bag against the gum, or place a small item in the freezer. → *Expect:* gum turns stiff and less stretchy.
3. **Scrape gently.** Use a spoon edge, dull knife, or plastic scraper to lift the hardened gum. → *Expect:* pieces break away without cutting fibers or scratching finish.
4. **Repeat cold cycles.** Re-freeze any gum that softens while scraping. → *Expect:* remaining gum stays brittle enough to chip off.
5. **Use oil for hair.** Work cooking oil, peanut butter, or conditioner into hair gum, then slide strands free. → *Expect:* gum loosens and pulls away without cutting hair.
6. **Treat residue.** For fabric or carpet, dab with dish soap solution after bulk gum is gone. → *Expect:* sticky shine fades without spreading.
7. **Rinse and blot.** Use a clean damp cloth, then a dry towel. → *Expect:* soap and loosened residue transfer to the towel.
8. **Dry and inspect.** Let the area dry fully before judging the stain. → *Expect:* no tacky spots remain under a fingertip.

## Decision points

- Fabric is delicate or dry-clean-only → stop after freezing and take it to a cleaner.
- Gum is in long hair → use oil first instead of ice to reduce pulling.
- Carpet pile is tall → scrape with the pile direction to avoid fuzzing.
- Surface is unfinished wood or stone → avoid oil unless tested.
- Color transfers to cloth → stop wet cleaning and use professional care.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gum smears:** detect a wider sticky area; recover by re-hardening with ice before scraping again.
- **F2 Fabric fuzzes:** detect raised fibers from scraping; recover by switching to a spoon and lighter pressure.
- **F3 Oil stain:** detect darkened fabric after hair-style oil treatment; recover with dish soap and warm-water blotting.
- **F4 Carpet residue returns:** detect tackiness after drying; recover with another soap dab and rinse cycle.
- **F5 Scratched hard surface:** detect dull scrape marks; recover by stopping metal tools and using plastic only.

## Verification

The surface feels non-sticky when dry, and no visible gum pieces remain in fibers, hair, or surface grooves.

## Variations

- Upholstery: use ice and blotting only, with minimal water.
- Shoes: freeze the shoe in a bag, then flex the sole to crack gum off.
- School desk or tile: harden with ice and lift with a plastic card.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from minor tool scratches and skin irritation from cleaners. Keep oils away from eyes when working gum out of hair.
