## Goal

Chewing gum is removed from a shoe sole or tread with minimal residue and without spreading the sticky mess into a larger biography.

## Preconditions

- The shoe can be removed and handled over a protected surface.
- A freezer bag, ice pack, dull scraper, old toothbrush, and dish soap are available.
- Peanut butter or cooking oil is available only if freezing leaves residue.

## Steps

1. **Keep the gum contained.** Do not step around on carpets or car mats. [BRANCH: fresh soft gum | old hard gum] chill soft gum first, scrape hard gum carefully. → *Expect:* the gum stays on the sole area you can clean.
2. **Freeze the gum.** Put the shoe in a bag sole-side up in the freezer for 1 to 2 hours, or press an ice pack on the gum for 10 minutes. → *Expect:* the gum becomes firm and less stretchy.
3. **Peel the main piece.** Use fingers, a spoon edge, or a dull scraper to lift the gum from one side. → *Expect:* a large piece comes away instead of smearing.
4. **Pick the tread channels.** Work a toothpick or old toothbrush through grooves where gum remains. → *Expect:* the tread pattern is visible again.
5. **Use oil for residue.** Rub a tiny amount of peanut butter or cooking oil into stubborn gum, wait two minutes, then scrape again. → *Expect:* sticky residue loosens into crumbs or streaks.
6. **Wash the sole.** Scrub with dish soap and warm water to remove oil and sugar. → *Expect:* the sole feels rubbery, not slick.
7. **Dry and inspect.** Pat dry, flex the sole, and check the tread under bright light. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* oil can stain suede, fabric, or porous uppers, so keep it only on the sole. → *Expect:* no gum strings stretch between tread blocks.
8. **Clean the tools.** Wrap gum scraps in paper and discard them. → *Expect:* no sticky residue remains on the work surface.

## Decision points

- Gum is on the upper, not the sole → avoid oil and test any cleaner in a hidden spot.
- Shoe has foam or suede near the gum → use ice and patient scraping only.
- Peanut allergy is present → use cooking oil, mineral oil, or skip the oil trick.
- Tread is deeply lugged → freeze twice rather than digging hard.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gum smears:** detect by wider shiny sticky patches → stop, refreeze, and scrape once hard.
- **F2 Oil slick remains:** detect by slippery tread → scrub with dish soap twice and rinse well.
- **F3 Scraper gouges sole:** detect by carved rubber marks → switch to a spoon or plastic card.
- **F4 Gum odor lingers:** detect after drying → wash with baking-soda paste, then rinse.

## Verification

The shoe sole is dry, not sticky, and leaves no gum mark when pressed on scrap paper.

## Variations

- Street cleanup: use ice from a drink cup and a disposable spoon.
- White soles: follow with a mild baking-soda scrub for discoloration.
- Work boots: remove laces and clean aggressively, but still avoid metal blades.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep oily cleaners off walking surfaces until washed away, and avoid peanut products where allergies or shared spaces make them unsafe.
