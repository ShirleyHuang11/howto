## Goal

A load of dirty clothes is washed, dried, and put away — clean, undamaged, un-shrunk, and nothing dyed pink.

## Preconditions

- A washing machine (home or laundromat) and detergent.
- A drying path: dryer, rack, or line.
- Enough dirty laundry for a load, care labels legible.

## Steps

1. **Sort the pile.** Split by color: darks / lights-whites; pull out anything labeled hand-wash/dry-clean, and anything new and deeply dyed (new red items bleed — first washes go alone or with darks). → *Expect:* two or more piles; delicates and bleed-risks separated.
2. **Prep the garments.** Empty every pocket (a tissue ruins a load; a pen ruins a machine), zip zippers, turn prints and dark jeans inside out. → *Expect:* pockets flat-empty; zips closed.
3. **Load the drum loosely.** Fill at most ~¾ — clothes need tumble room to get clean. → *Expect:* a hand fits flat between the load and the drum top.
4. **Dose detergent per its cap markings and the load size.** Into the drawer's main compartment (or pod straight into the drum, *under* the clothes). More detergent ≠ cleaner; excess leaves residue. → *Expect:* dose at or below the cap's line for a normal load.
5. **Choose the cycle and temperature.** Default: normal/cotton cycle, 30–40 °C. Care label overrides: the tub symbol's number is the max temperature; a hand in the tub = hand-wash cycle. → *Expect:* cycle set consistent with the most delicate item in the load.
6. **Start, and note the finish time.** → *Expect:* door locks, water sound begins; you know when to come back — wet clothes left sitting sour within hours.
7. **Move to drying promptly at cycle end.** [BRANCH: dryer — medium heat, clean the lint filter *first*, no items with elastic/wool that the label bans | rack/line — shake each piece out, hang with air space] ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a hot dryer shrinks wool and elastic permanently — when in doubt, rack-dry it. → *Expect:* lint filter cleaned; load drying by the chosen path.
8. **Fold or hang while just-dry (or slightly warm).** This is when wrinkles surrender (`embodied/fold-a-tshirt` for the fold). Put everything away. → *Expect:* empty basket, stocked drawers, no chair-mountain of clean laundry.

## Decision points

- Stains → pre-treat before washing (dab detergent on the spot, 10 min) — a dryer's heat *sets* stains, so check stained items *before* they go in the dryer and rewash if still marked.
- Laundromat → bring detergent, coins/app, and stay with or time your machines; removing a stranger's finished load is accepted etiquette done gently onto the folding table.
- Musty machine smell → run an empty hot cycle with washing-machine cleaner or vinegar; leave the door ajar between loads.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 A color bled (everything pink):** do NOT dry — rewash the victims immediately with detergent while wet; color-run remover products exist; identify and quarantine the bleeder.
- **F2 Tissue confetti everywhere:** shake outside, then dryer with a clean filter picks off most; lint roller for the rest. Pocket check exists for this.
- **F3 Wool sweater came out child-sized:** heat + agitation felted it — mostly unrecoverable (soaking in hair-conditioner water can relax it slightly). The label read would have caught it.
- **F4 Load smells sour after sitting wet:** rewash with detergent; don't just dry it — drying locks the smell in.

## Verification

Clothes are clean (spot-check former stains), fully dry, correctly sized (nothing shrunk), sorted into their storage, the lint filter is clean, and the machine door is ajar to dry.

## Variations

- Hand-wash items: basin, cool water, gentle detergent, press — never wring — and roll in a towel before flat-drying.
- `jp`/`eu` compact machines default to cold-ish washes and line drying; dryer-shrink risk shifts to the radiator-vs-rack choice.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to property, not people: the pocket check, the care-label read, and the dryer-heat decision are where money is lost. Keep detergent pods away from children — they look like candy.
