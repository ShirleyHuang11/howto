## Goal

Tarnished silver is cleaned, rinsed, dried, and stored to slow new tarnish.

## Preconditions

- Silver item, aluminum foil, baking soda, hot water, bowl, tongs, soft cloths, and optional silver polish are available.
- The item is solid silver or silver plate without glued stones, pearls, porous gems, or intentionally dark patina.
- A soft work surface is ready to prevent scratches.

## Steps

1. **Inspect the item.** Look for loose parts, plating wear, stones, or deliberate dark detail. → *Expect:* you know whether a bath or hand polish is safer.
2. **Choose a method.** [BRANCH: foil bath for plain pieces | paste-and-buff for detailed or delicate pieces] → *Expect:* the cleaning method fits the object.
3. **Prepare the foil bath.** Line a bowl with foil, add silver touching the foil, sprinkle baking soda, and pour hot water over it. → *Expect:* faint sulfur smell or bubbling appears.
4. **Wait briefly.** Let the item sit 1 to 3 minutes, turning so tarnished areas contact solution. → *Expect:* tarnish lightens without aggressive rubbing.
5. **Use paste if needed.** Apply silver polish or baking soda paste with a soft cloth, rubbing gently. → *Expect:* black tarnish transfers to the cloth.
6. **Rinse thoroughly.** Rinse under warm water, avoiding soaking handles or glued parts. → *Expect:* no gritty baking soda or polish remains.
7. **Dry completely.** Buff with a soft lint-free cloth, reaching seams and crevices. → *Expect:* surface is dry and brighter.
8. **Store against tarnish.** Wrap in anti-tarnish cloth or keep dry with low airflow. → *Expect:* silver is protected from moisture and sulfur exposure.

## Decision points

- Item has pearls, opals, glued stones, or hollow handles → avoid hot-water bath.
- Patina gives detail to engraving → polish raised surfaces only.
- Heavy black tarnish remains → repeat short sessions rather than one long soak.
- Silver plate shows yellow base metal → stop polishing to avoid more plating loss.
- Food-contact item was polished with commercial paste → rinse extra well before use.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cloudy film:** detect white residue after drying; recover by rinsing again and buffing dry.
- **F2 Lost patina:** detect dark detail fading; recover by stopping and avoiding bath methods next time.
- **F3 Scratches:** detect fine lines from abrasive cloth; recover by switching to microfiber and gentle pressure.
- **F4 Loose handle:** detect wobble after soaking; recover by drying immediately and avoiding water in joints.
- **F5 Tarnish returns fast:** detect yellowing within days; recover by storing dry in anti-tarnish wrap.

## Verification

The silver is visibly brighter, dry to the touch, and leaves no black residue on a clean cloth after final buffing.

## Variations

- Jewelry: use a small foil-lined cup and avoid porous stones.
- Flatware: wash with mild soap after polishing before eating with it.
- Museum or heirloom pieces: clean minimally and ask a conservator before removing patina.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot water and mild chemicals. Ventilate if sulfur smell is strong and keep polishing products away from children.
