## Goal

A hard floor (tile, wood, laminate, vinyl) is swept free of debris and mopped clean, drying streak-free with nobody slipping on it.

## Preconditions

- Broom + dustpan (or vacuum on hard-floor mode), a mop and bucket (or flat/spray mop), and a floor-appropriate cleaner.
- The floor area reasonably clear of movable obstacles (chairs up or aside).

## Steps

1. **Clear and prep the area.** Chairs on the table or out; rugs shaken outside and set aside; pick up cables and stray items. → *Expect:* an open floor with reachable corners.
2. **Sweep the whole area toward one collection point.** Work from the far corner toward the exit/center; short overlapping strokes; get under furniture edges and along baseboards where debris drifts. → *Expect:* one debris pile; visibly clear floor elsewhere.
3. **Dustpan the pile.** Brace the pan, sweep in, then *back the pan up a few centimeters and sweep the leftover line again* — the eternal thin line takes two passes. → *Expect:* pile gone including the line; pan emptied to the bin.
4. **Prepare the mop.** Bucket: warm water + cleaner *dosed per its label* (over-dosing causes the sticky/streaky floor). Wring the mop to damp, not dripping — especially over wood/laminate, where standing water is the enemy. → *Expect:* mop damp enough to sheen the floor, not puddle it.
5. **Mop from the far corner toward the exit.** Figure-eight strokes with a string mop / straight overlapping lanes with a flat mop; re-dip-and-wring when the head drags dirty. Don't mop yourself into a corner. → *Expect:* uniform thin wet sheen behind you; visibly dirty water building in the bucket.
6. **Change water when it turns murky.** Mopping with dirty water just relocates soil in an even gray film. → *Expect:* final passes done with clean-ish water.
7. **Block the area and let it dry.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* wet-floor slips hurt real people — keep household members (and yourself) off it for the 10–20 min dry, ventilate to speed it. → *Expect:* floor dries streak-free; no footprints through the wet zone.
8. **Rinse the mop and dustpan, dump the bucket in a toilet/utility drain (not the kitchen sink), and restore the furniture.** → *Expect:* tools clean and drying; room back in order.

## Decision points

- Wood floors → confirm the cleaner says wood-safe and the mop is well-wrung; unfinished or oiled wood may want dry methods only.
- Sticky spot / dried spill mid-sweep → don't grind at it dry: park a wet cloth on it during steps 2–4 and it wipes up at mop time.
- Vacuum instead of broom → faster and doesn't launch dust; use the hard-floor head (beater bar off) to avoid scattering and scratching.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Streaks/haze after drying:** over-dosed cleaner or dirty final water — re-mop with plain warm water only.
- **F2 Water in a floorboard seam / warping worry:** towel-dry immediately and fan the area; adjust the wring standard upward for that floor.
- **F3 Swept dust just moved to the air and resettled:** strokes too energetic — sweep low and short, or switch to the vacuum; damp-mopping after (which you're doing) catches the resettle.
- **F4 Slipped or nearly did on the wet floor:** the blocking rule (7) failed — physically obstruct the doorway next time; socks on wet tile is the classic fall.

## Verification

Corners and baseboard lines hold no debris, the dried floor shows neither streaks nor gray film under raking light, tools are rinsed and stored, and furniture is back with no wet-floor incident recorded.

## Variations

- Big open areas: sweep and mop in halves so you always stand on dry.
- Spray-mop systems: steps 4–6 collapse into cartridge + pad; pads wash with `daily/home/do-the-laundry`.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk = the wet floor itself (step 7's blocking rule) plus lifting the bucket with knees, not back; never mix cleaning chemicals (specifically bleach + anything acidic/ammonia) in one bucket.
