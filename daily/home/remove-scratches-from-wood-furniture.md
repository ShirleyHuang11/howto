## Goal

Light scratches in wood furniture are cleaned, color-matched, blended, and protected while deep damage is identified before overworking the finish.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: soft cloths, mild soap, water, matching wax stick or furniture marker, walnut for dark raw scratches, paste wax or compatible polish.
- Know whether the surface is solid wood, veneer, lacquer, oil, or polyurethane if possible.
- Test any marker, wax, or polish on a hidden spot first.

## Steps

1. **Clean the area gently.** Wipe with a barely damp cloth and mild soap, then dry immediately. → *Expect:* dust and oils are gone, making the scratch edge visible.
2. **Judge the depth.** Run a fingernail across the mark. [BRANCH: nail does not catch | nail catches] treat light scratches; plan repair for deep ones. → *Expect:* a clear light-versus-deep call.
3. **Choose the color fill.** Match a wax stick or marker to the darker grain, not the lightest highlight. → *Expect:* test mark blends on a hidden area after buffing.
4. **Fill light scratches sparingly.** Rub walnut, wax, or marker along the scratch, then wipe across the grain to remove excess. → *Expect:* color settles in the pale line, not smeared over the whole surface.
5. **Buff with a clean cloth.** Use small circles, then finish with the grain. → *Expect:* shine evens out and the repair looks less obvious from standing height.
6. **Protect the finish.** Apply a small amount of compatible wax or polish if the whole area looks dull. → *Expect:* the repaired patch matches the surrounding sheen.
7. **Stop before sanding.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not sand veneer or finished furniture unless you accept changing the surface. → *Expect:* no finish is removed accidentally.

## Decision points

- White line only in the finish → wax or polish can hide it well.
- Raw pale wood visible → color fill first, then wax.
- Gouge, lifted veneer, water bubble, or missing finish → a furniture repair or refinishing task, not scratch hiding.
- Antique or valuable piece → stop after cleaning and ask a conservator.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Marker too dark:** wipe immediately with a cloth lightly dampened with mineral spirits if finish-safe → switch to wax or a lighter marker.
- **F2 Wax haze remains:** buff longer with a clean cloth → remove excess with mild soap if needed.
- **F3 Scratch still catches:** do not keep coloring it → fill or refinish is required.
- **F4 Finish becomes sticky:** incompatible polish was used → stop, clean gently, and let it cure before adding anything else.

## Verification

From normal viewing distance, the scratch is blended in color and sheen, with no sticky residue, haze, or new finish damage.

## Variations

- Dark walnut furniture: a fresh walnut rubbed into a pale scratch can be enough for tiny marks.
- Painted wood: use touch-up paint, not wood marker.
- Dining table: let wax or polish cure before placing hot dishes or papers on it.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because wrong products can permanently affect finishes. Ventilate when using solvent-based markers or polish, and keep oily cloths flat to dry before disposal.
