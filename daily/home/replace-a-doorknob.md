## Goal

A doorknob set is replaced, the latch faces the right way, and the door opens, closes, and latches smoothly.

## Preconditions

- Replacement knob set, screwdriver, and optional tape measure.
- Door is open and supported so it cannot lock you out.
- New hardware matches the door thickness and backset.

## Steps

1. **Open the door.** Keep it open for the whole job and block it from swinging. → *Expect:* both sides of the knob are reachable.
2. **Find interior screws.** Look on the interior rose or under a small cover plate. → *Expect:* mounting screws or a release slot are visible.
3. **Remove old knobs.** Unscrew interior screws and pull both knobs apart. → *Expect:* the spindle and knob halves come free.
4. **Remove the latch.** Unscrew the latch plate on the door edge and slide the latch out. → *Expect:* an empty edge bore remains.
5. **Install new latch.** Insert it with the slanted face toward the strike plate. → *Expect:* the latch plate sits flush and the latch bevel faces closing direction.
6. **Check handedness.** [BRANCH: privacy passage knob | keyed entry knob] orient lock buttons, keyway, and interior side correctly. → *Expect:* the correct side faces the room or hallway.
7. **Attach knobs.** Feed spindle through the latch, align posts, and tighten screws evenly. → *Expect:* knobs sit flat without binding.
8. **Test alignment.** Close the door slowly and turn the knob several times. → *Expect:* latch retracts, enters the strike, and releases cleanly.

## Decision points

- Latch plate is too large or small → reuse the old latch only if it fits the new knob.
- Latch hits the strike high or low → adjust strike plate or tighten hinge screws.
- Door has no visible screws → look for a small release slot on the knob neck.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Locked out risk:** detect door closing during work, recover by wedging it open before removing hardware.
- **F2 Backwards latch:** detect latch catching on close, recover by removing and rotating the latch.
- **F3 Knob binds:** detect stiff turning after tightening, recover by loosening and realigning screws.
- **F4 Strike misses:** detect latch rubbing the frame, recover by tightening hinges or shifting the strike plate.

## Verification

With the door closed, the knob turns freely, the latch catches in the strike, and the door opens again from the intended side.

## Variations

- Privacy knob: verify the emergency release hole faces outside the room.
- Entry knob: test the key before closing the door.
- Older mortise lock: do not force a tubular latch set into a mortise pocket.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, but do not close a keyed or privacy door until the new knob has been tested from both sides. Keep small screws away from children.
