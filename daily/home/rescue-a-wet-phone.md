## Goal

Reduce damage to a wet phone by powering it down, drying it with air and desiccant, and delaying charging until hidden moisture has had time to leave.

## Preconditions

- The phone has been removed from water or heavy moisture.
- A towel, clean dry area, and silica gel packets or commercial desiccant are available if possible.
- You can leave the phone unused for up to 48 hours.

## Steps

1. **Remove it from liquid.** Pick up the phone, keeping ports pointed downward if water is draining. → *Expect:* the phone is no longer exposed to water.
2. **Power it off.** Shut it down immediately if it is still on; do not test apps or buttons. → *Expect:* the screen is off and the phone is not vibrating or ringing.
3. **Disconnect accessories.** Remove the case, cable, earbuds, and SIM tray if accessible without forcing anything. → *Expect:* trapped water has more paths to evaporate.
4. **Dry the outside.** Blot with a lint-free towel and avoid shaking hard, heating, or blowing into ports. → *Expect:* visible surface water is gone.
5. **Position ports downward.** Stand or lean the phone so charging and speaker openings face down for 30 minutes. → *Expect:* any loose drops can drain out.
6. **Use real desiccant.** Place the phone in a sealed container with silica gel packets or a commercial drying pouch. → *Expect:* desiccant surrounds the phone without touching ports with dust.
7. **Skip rice.** Do not bury the phone in rice; it dries poorly and can leave starch or grains in ports. → *Expect:* the phone stays in clean air or silica, not food.
8. **Wait 48 hours.** Leave the phone powered off and uncharged for two full days. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* charging a wet phone can short components; confirm the wait is complete and ports look dry first. → *Expect:* the phone has had time to dry internally.
9. **Inspect ports.** Check the charging port, speakers, and SIM slot with a flashlight, without inserting metal tools. → *Expect:* no droplets, lint clumps, or corrosion are visible.
10. **Power on carefully.** Try turning it on, then charge only if it boots normally and the port is dry. → *Expect:* the phone starts without heat, smoke, crackling, or repeated shutdowns.

## Decision points

- Phone was in salt water, pool water, soda, or mud → power off and seek repair quickly because residue keeps corroding.
- Phone gets hot, smells odd, or swells → stop using it and move it to a nonflammable surface.
- Important data is not backed up → after it boots, back up before normal use.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 It will not power on:** detect no response after 48 hours, recover by charging briefly with a known-good cable only if the port is dry, then seek repair.
- **F2 Moisture warning:** detect a charging-port alert, recover by unplugging and waiting longer with the port exposed to air.
- **F3 Distorted speaker:** detect muffled sound, recover by waiting longer and gently tapping with speaker openings downward.
- **F4 Corrosion signs:** detect green, white, or rusty residue, recover by stopping use and getting professional service.

## Verification

After at least 48 hours powered off, the phone boots normally and the ports show no visible moisture before charging.

## Variations

- `water-resistant-phone`: still dry it fully because seals degrade and resistance is not waterproofing.
- `non-removable-sim`: leave sealed parts alone and focus on external drying and repair service.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because charging or overheating can permanently damage the phone or battery. Do not use heat, ovens, hair dryers, or rice, and protect private data by backing up only after the phone behaves normally.
