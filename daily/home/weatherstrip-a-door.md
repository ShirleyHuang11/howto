## Goal

A drafty door is sealed with weatherstripping that fits the actual gaps while still letting the door close and latch.

## Preconditions

- Weatherstripping material, scissors or utility knife, tape measure, cleaner, and rag.
- Optional door sweep for a bottom gap.
- The door and frame are dry.
- Permission to attach adhesive, nails, or screws to the door frame.
- A sheet of paper or incense stick for testing drafts.

## Steps

1. **Find the draft.** Close the door and feel around edges, or watch smoke movement near the frame. → *Expect:* leaky sides or bottom are identified.
2. **Measure the gaps.** Check top, latch side, hinge side, and bottom separately. → *Expect:* each gap has an approximate width and length.
3. **Choose type by gap.** [BRANCH: foam tape for small even gaps | V-strip for narrow moving edges | rubber bulb for larger gaps | sweep for bottom] → *Expect:* material compresses enough without blocking closure.
4. **Clean the contact surface.** Wipe dust, grease, and loose paint from the frame. → *Expect:* adhesive area is dry and smooth.
5. **Cut pieces to length.** Cut top and side pieces square, leaving no stretched ends. → *Expect:* pieces match the frame sections before backing is removed.
6. **Apply from one end.** Peel backing gradually and press weatherstrip onto the stop or frame where the closed door contacts it. → *Expect:* strip is straight and firmly adhered.
7. **Seal corners carefully.** Butt pieces together without overlapping bulky corners. → *Expect:* light is blocked at corners without bunching.
8. **Install the bottom sweep if needed.** Position it so it touches the threshold lightly, then fasten. → *Expect:* sweep brushes or compresses against the threshold.
9. **Test closure.** Close and latch the door several times. → *Expect:* latch catches without slamming or excessive force.
10. **Paper-test the seal.** Close a sheet of paper in several spots and pull gently. → *Expect:* paper has light resistance where the seal is working.

## Decision points

- Door will not latch after installation → material is too thick or placed too far toward the door.
- Gap changes along the edge → use adjustable rubber or a door stop adjustment rather than uniform foam.
- Bottom gap is large → install a sweep or threshold seal instead of stacking side tape.
- Exterior door has water intrusion → prioritize drainage and threshold condition before sealing tightly.
- Renter situation → use removable adhesive products unless alterations are allowed.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Adhesive peels:** surface was dirty, cold, or damp; clean, warm, and reapply with fresh strip.
- **F2 Door binds:** strip is oversized; move it slightly or switch to thinner material.
- **F3 Draft remains at corners:** pieces do not meet; add a small fitted patch without creating a lump.
- **F4 Sweep drags hard:** it is mounted too low; loosen screws and raise it slightly.
- **F5 Condensation appears:** sealing changed airflow; improve ventilation and check humidity.

## Verification

The door closes and latches normally, paper has light resistance around sealed edges, and no obvious draft or daylight remains at the treated gaps.

## Variations

- Metal doors: magnetic weatherstripping may work if the frame is compatible.
- Older wood doors: nail-on bronze V-strip is durable and tolerates uneven movement.
- Garage entry doors: use fire-rated materials if required by local code.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from blades, ladders, and pinched fingers. Keep ventilation needs in mind, especially near combustion appliances that require makeup air.
