## Goal

A closet emptied, honestly purged, and rebuilt into zones you can maintain, with the outgoing clothes actually leaving the house instead of migrating to a bag in the hallway.

## Preconditions

- A free block of two to three hours; a half-organized closet is worse than either extreme.
- Staging space (the bed), three containers or bags labeled keep, donate, and discard, and a fourth for elsewhere (things that live in other rooms).
- Matching hangers help more than any other purchase, but the recipe works without buying anything.

## Steps

1. **Empty it completely, one shelf or rod at a time.** Everything onto the bed by section. A full emptying is what makes the purge honest; sorting in place preserves every hiding spot. → *Expect:* bare closet, clothes staged in rough groups, dust visible where it always was.
2. **Clean the empty closet.** Vacuum the floor, wipe shelves and rods (`daily/home/dust-a-room` logic in a box). → *Expect:* clean surfaces; this two-minute step is why emptying was worth it.
3. **Purge each item with two questions.** Worn in the last year? Would you buy it again today? Two yeses: keep. Two nos: donate or discard (stained, torn beyond repair). One yes: a maybe-box with a date six months out; unopened by then, it donates itself. → *Expect:* real piles forming; the keep pile visibly smaller than what came out.
4. **Rebuild in zones by frequency and kind.** Eye level and easy reach: what you wear weekly. Hanging: things that wrinkle (shirts, dresses, jackets). Folded shelves or drawers: knits and tees (`embodied/household/fold-a-tshirt`, filed vertically so every item shows). High shelves: seasonal and rare (luggage, formal wear). Floor: shoes on a rack or in rows, not a heap. → *Expect:* a layout a half-asleep version of you can navigate.
5. **Group within zones.** By category first (all trousers together), then by color if you enjoy it. One-in-one-out lives or dies on being able to see what you own. → *Expect:* like with like; gaps visible where categories are thin or bloated.
6. **Evacuate the exits today.** Discard to the bin now; donate bag into the car or by the door with a calendar slot this week (`daily/errands/donate-used-goods` is its own errand; schedule it); elsewhere-box distributed. ⚠️ *Irreversible in effect:* the donate bag that lingers three weeks quietly reopens; leaving the house is part of this recipe, not an afterthought. → *Expect:* by tonight, only keep items remain on the premises, staged or shelved.

## Decision points

- Seasonal rotation: in small closets, off-season clothes box up for the high shelf or under-bed, and this recipe runs twice a year at the swap.
- Sentimental items that are not clothing-in-use (the concert shirt, the wedding tie): a single memory box, size-capped, honors them better than a working closet hiding them.
- Shared closets: divide territory first, purge only your own side, and negotiate shared zones explicitly; organizing someone else's clothes is a relationship event, not a favor.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stalled mid-pile, bed covered, energy gone:** finish triage of what is on the bed into the four containers even if crudely, rebuild tomorrow; the one unrecoverable state is sleeping next to the pile and shoveling it back.
- **F2 Purge paralysis (everything is a maybe):** the maybe-box with its date is the pressure valve; use it liberally rather than litigating each shirt for five minutes.
- **F3 Closet reverts within a month:** the zones did not match your real habits; move the weekly-wear zone to wherever your hands actually go, and add a hamper or hook for the worn-once-not-dirty layer that otherwise colonizes the chair.
- **F4 Donate bag still in the hallway:** put it in the car now; the car completes errands that hallways absorb.

## Verification

The closet holds only keep items in navigable zones, every shelf and rod is clean beneath them, the maybe-box has a date, the discard is in the bin, and the donate bag is in the car or already gone.

## Variations

- Drawers-only setups (dressers) run the same recipe with vertical filing doing the zone work.
- Kids' closets: lower the daily zone to their height and purge outgrown sizes by the calendar, not by negotiation.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Check pockets before any bag leaves (keys, cards, and cash retire with clothes constantly), and shred anything with personal data found in coat pockets rather than donating it along.
