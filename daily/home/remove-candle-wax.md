## Goal

Candle wax is hardened, lifted, and any oily residue is blotted away from wood, fabric, or carpet.

## Preconditions

- Ice in a plastic bag, dull scraper, paper towels or plain brown paper, iron or hair dryer, and mild soap are available.
- The surface can tolerate cold and controlled warmth.
- Flame is out and the wax has cooled enough to touch.

## Steps

1. **Let wax harden.** Wait until wax is no longer liquid before touching it. → *Expect:* wax is opaque and firm.
2. **Chill the wax.** Hold bagged ice on the wax for several minutes. → *Expect:* wax becomes brittle and shrinks slightly.
3. **Scrape bulk wax.** Lift hardened pieces with a plastic scraper or spoon. → *Expect:* thick wax flakes off without gouging the surface.
4. **Choose residue treatment.** [BRANCH: wood, use gentle heat and cloth | fabric or carpet, use paper and low iron] → *Expect:* the method protects the material.
5. **Heat and blot.** Place paper over residue and apply warm iron or hair dryer briefly. → *Expect:* wax transfers into the paper.
6. **Move to clean paper.** Repeat with fresh paper until no new wax mark appears. → *Expect:* paper stays mostly clean after heating.
7. **Clean the oily trace.** Dab fabric or carpet with mild dish soap solution, or wipe sealed wood with a barely damp cloth. → *Expect:* greasy shine fades.
8. **Dry and restore.** Blot dry, then polish sealed wood if the finish looks dull. → *Expect:* surface is dry and even-looking.

## Decision points

- Wax is on unfinished wood → avoid heat-and-blot that can drive oil deeper.
- Fabric is synthetic → use the lowest iron setting or a hair dryer to avoid melting fibers.
- Wax is colored → test first because dye may stain after heat.
- Carpet backing is damp → stop and dry thoroughly before more cleaning.
- Surface finish softens with heat → stop heat and return to chilling and scraping.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dye stain remains:** detect color after wax is gone; recover with fabric-safe stain remover or professional cleaning.
- **F2 Wax spreads under heat:** detect a larger translucent patch; recover by chilling again and using less heat.
- **F3 Wood finish dulls:** detect a hazy spot; recover with furniture polish only after residue is removed.
- **F4 Carpet fibers mat:** detect flattened pile; recover by drying, then lifting pile with fingers or a soft brush.
- **F5 Paper sticks:** detect paper bonding to wax; recover by cooling and peeling slowly before reheating.

## Verification

No raised wax can be felt, blotting paper no longer picks up wax, and the cleaned area is dry.

## Variations

- Glass holder: freeze briefly, pop wax out, then wash with hot soapy water.
- Table linen: scrape first, blot with paper, then launder if care label allows.
- Brick or stone: chill and scrape, then use absorbent powder for oily residue.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot irons and delicate finishes. Keep heat moving and never apply an iron directly to carpet or wax.
