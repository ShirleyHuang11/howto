## Goal

A necktie is tied in a neat four-in-hand knot, with the wide end at the belt line and a small dimple under the knot.

## Preconditions

- A necktie long enough to reach your belt after tying.
- A collared shirt, mirror, and two free hands.
- The top button is fastened if the tie is for a formal look.

## Steps

1. **Drape the tie around your neck.** Put the wide end on your dominant-hand side and the narrow end on the other side. [BRANCH: standard tie | extra-long tie] start with the wide end about 30 cm lower for standard, farther down for extra-long. → *Expect:* the wide end hangs longer than the narrow end.
2. **Cross wide over narrow.** Lay the wide end across the front of the narrow end just below your collar. → *Expect:* an X shape sits centered at your throat.
3. **Wrap behind and across.** Pass the wide end behind the narrow end, then bring it across the front again. → *Expect:* a horizontal band of fabric covers the front of the knot.
4. **Send wide end up through the neck loop.** Bring the wide end from underneath, up through the opening between collar and tie. → *Expect:* the wide end points upward, with the front band still visible below.
5. **Tuck through the front loop.** Feed the wide end down through the front band you made, keeping the knot loose enough for the fabric to slide. → *Expect:* the wide end emerges flat in front, forming the four-in-hand knot.
6. **Shape the dimple.** Pinch the fabric just below the knot with one finger while pulling the wide end downward. → *Expect:* a clean crease forms under the center of the knot.
7. **Tighten to the collar.** Hold the narrow end and slide the knot upward until it meets the collar. → *Expect:* the knot sits snugly without choking you.
8. **Check the length.** The wide end should touch the top or middle of the belt buckle, hiding the narrow end behind it. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* tugging hard can stretch delicate silk, so loosen and retie instead of yanking. → *Expect:* the tie hangs straight, with no tail peeking out below.

## Decision points

- Wide end lands too short → start again with the wide end lower.
- Wide end lands below the belt → start again with the wide end higher.
- Knot looks bulky → tighten each wrap gently before the final tuck.
- Collar gap shows → slide the knot up while holding the narrow end steady.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Twisted blade:** detect by a spiral or visible seam on the front → untwist the wide end before the final tuck.
- **F2 Flat knot:** detect by a limp triangle with no center crease → pinch the dimple before the final tightening.
- **F3 Narrow end escapes:** detect by a tail visible beside or below the wide end → use the keeper loop or a tie bar.
- **F4 Neck squeeze:** detect by pressure when swallowing → lower the knot slightly or unbutton after the event.

## Verification

The knot is centered at the collar, the wide end reaches the belt buckle, and the narrow end stays hidden behind it.

## Variations

- Skinny tie: make the starting height difference smaller because the knot uses less fabric.
- Thick tie: keep the wraps loose until the final pull so the knot does not become a fist.
- Open collar: tie normally, then lower the knot one finger width for a relaxed look.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid tight pressure around the neck, especially for children, costumes, or long periods of wear. Remove the tie before sleeping, climbing, machine work, or anything where loose fabric could catch.
