## Goal

The mattress surface is vacuumed, spot-treated, deodorized, dried fully, and protected without soaking the interior.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: vacuum with upholstery tool, mild dish soap or enzyme cleaner, cold water, white cloths, baking soda, soft brush, mattress protector.
- A dry room with airflow and enough time for the mattress to dry before bedding returns.
- Bedding has been removed and laundered separately.

## Steps

1. **Strip and inspect the bed.** Remove sheets, protector, and pillows, then check the label for cleaning limits. → *Expect:* bare mattress and known fabric warnings.
2. **Vacuum slowly.** Use the upholstery tool over the top, sides, seams, and tufting in overlapping passes. → *Expect:* dust, hair, and grit are removed from seams.
3. **Spot-treat stains lightly.** Blot with a barely damp cloth and a small amount of cleaner. [BRANCH: protein stain | general soil] use enzyme cleaner for protein, mild soap for general soil. → *Expect:* stain transfers to the cloth without spreading.
4. **Rinse the spot with a damp cloth.** Blot with plain cold water, then press dry towels over the area. → *Expect:* no visible suds and the spot feels only slightly damp.
5. **Deodorize with baking soda.** Sprinkle a thin, even layer over the surface and brush it lightly into fabric texture. → *Expect:* powder sits dry across the mattress, not clumped into wet patches.
6. **Let it sit.** Leave baking soda for at least 1 hour, longer for odor, with a fan or open window. → *Expect:* the room smells fresher and the surface stays dry.
7. **Vacuum again thoroughly.** Remove all baking soda from the top, sides, and seams. → *Expect:* no powder puffs when you pat the mattress.
8. **Dry fully before covering.** Leave the mattress uncovered until cool dampness is gone, then add a clean protector and bedding. → *Expect:* fabric feels dry and bedding is not trapping moisture.
9. **Rotate if the mattress allows it.** Turn head-to-foot unless the maker says not to. → *Expect:* wear pressure is redistributed.

## Decision points

- Fresh liquid spill → blot first, then clean; never pour water into the mattress.
- Urine, sweat, or blood → enzyme cleaner and cold water; heat can set protein stains.
- Musty odor after drying → suspect deep moisture, poor ventilation, or mold, not normal dirt.
- Pillow-top or no-flip mattress → rotate only, do not flip.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mattress feels wet inside:** stop covering it and run fans longer → sleep elsewhere if needed to avoid trapping moisture.
- **F2 Cleaning ring appears:** feather the edge with a barely damp cloth → dry evenly with airflow.
- **F3 Baking soda clumps:** the surface was too wet → vacuum what you can and extend drying before another pass.
- **F4 Odor persists:** repeat deodorizing once → if odor remains deep, use a protector or consider replacement.

## Verification

The mattress is dry, vacuumed clean, has no loose baking soda, stains are reduced without wet patches, and a clean protector is installed.

## Variations

- Memory foam: use less liquid and more drying time, because foam holds moisture.
- Allergy routine: vacuum both sides and wash bedding hot if the fabric permits.
- Moving day: clean after moving, not before, unless the mattress can dry uncovered.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid soaking, avoid harsh solvents, and keep personal items or documents away while bedding and mattress contents are exposed.
