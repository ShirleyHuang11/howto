## Goal

A charcoal grill is lit outdoors with a chimney starter, coals are brought to ash-gray readiness, and heat zones are arranged safely for cooking.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: charcoal, chimney starter, lighter cubes or newspaper, long lighter, heat-resistant gloves, grill tool.
- Grill is outdoors on a stable, nonflammable surface away from siding, branches, and dry leaves.
- Vents are open and ash from the previous cook is removed.
- ⚠️ Never use a charcoal grill indoors, in a garage, in a tent, or near an open window because carbon monoxide can kill.

## Steps

1. **Open vents and set the chimney.** Put the chimney on the charcoal grate, not on a table or deck. → *Expect:* airflow can feed the fire from below.
2. **Load fuel.** Fill the chimney with the amount of charcoal needed, usually half for small cooks and full for high heat. → *Expect:* charcoal sits contained above the starter space.
3. **Light the starter.** Place a lighter cube or loosely crumpled newspaper under the chimney and light it with a long lighter. → *Expect:* flame pulls upward through the chimney.
4. **Wait for ash-gray coals.** Let coals burn 15-25 minutes until top pieces show gray edges and orange glow below. → *Expect:* smoke drops and coals look mostly ashed over.
5. **Pour coals carefully.** Wearing gloves, lift the chimney by its handle and pour slowly onto the charcoal grate. → *Expect:* coals land where intended without sparks spilling outside the grill.
6. **Arrange heat zones.** [BRANCH: direct grilling | two-zone cooking] spread coals evenly for direct heat, or bank to one side for hot and cooler zones. → *Expect:* cooking area has the heat pattern you need.
7. **Install grate and preheat.** Set the cooking grate on, close the lid briefly, then brush the grate clean. → *Expect:* grate is hot and ready for food.
8. **Control air and keep watch.** Adjust vents for heat and keep the grill attended until coals are fully out after cooking. → *Expect:* fire stays controlled and outdoors.

## Decision points

- Wind is strong → move to a more sheltered outdoor spot or postpone.
- Need slower cooking → use two-zone setup with food away from direct coals.
- Coals are black on top → wait longer before pouring or cooking.
- Lighter fluid is available → skip it; chimney lighting is cleaner and more controllable.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Chimney will not catch:** starter lacked airflow or fuel → relight with dry newspaper or a starter cube under open vents.
- **F2 Flames leap after pouring:** grease or too much starter is burning → close lid partly and control with vents.
- **F3 Coals cool too soon:** not enough charcoal or vents restricted → add lit coals from a second chimney if needed.
- **F4 Grill is used in enclosed space:** carbon monoxide risk is immediate → move outdoors and leave the enclosed area.

## Verification

The grill is outdoors, coals are ash-gray and glowing, heat zones are arranged, vents work, and no charcoal is burning in an enclosed or poorly ventilated space.

## Variations

- Lump charcoal: lights faster and burns hotter, so check readiness earlier.
- Briquettes: more consistent for longer cooks.
- Kettle grill: bank coals to one side and place the lid vent over the food side for two-zone cooking.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from fire and carbon monoxide. ⚠️ Never light or cool charcoal indoors, never add lighter fluid to lit coals, and keep children, pets, and combustibles away.
