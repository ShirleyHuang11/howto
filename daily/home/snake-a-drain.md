## Goal

A sink, tub, or shower drain blockage is cleared with a hand auger while avoiding pipe damage and unsafe chemical exposure.

## Preconditions

- You have a hand drum auger or hand crank snake, rubber gloves, bucket, towels, and eye protection.
- No chemical drain cleaner has been used; if it has, do not snake or open the drain until a plumber advises.
- This is one fixture only. Multiple blocked fixtures or sewage backup means stop and call a plumber.

## Steps

1. **Protect the area.** Put on gloves and eye protection, remove standing water with a cup if needed, and place towels nearby. → *Expect:* you can work without splashing dirty water onto floors.
2. **Remove the stopper or strainer.** Unscrew or lift the drain cover and pull out reachable hair or debris first. → *Expect:* the auger has a clear entry point.
3. **Choose the access point.** [BRANCH: sink | tub or shower] for sinks, remove the P-trap if practical and feed into the wall stub; for tubs, feed through the overflow when possible. → *Expect:* the cable enters the pipe without scraping finished surfaces.
4. **Feed cable gently.** Loosen the thumb screw, push cable in a few inches at a time, and stop at resistance. → *Expect:* resistance feels like a clog or bend, not a hard metal stop.
5. **Crank through the obstruction.** Tighten the thumb screw, rotate clockwise while applying light forward pressure, then loosen and feed more cable. → *Expect:* the cable advances or begins to grab material.
6. **Decide break or retrieve.** [BRANCH: break up | retrieve] crank to break soft buildup, or reverse slightly and pull back if hair or wipes catch on the tip. → *Expect:* either water drops or clog material comes out on the cable.
7. **Withdraw slowly.** Pull cable back while rotating, wiping it into a bucket or trash bag as it emerges. → *Expect:* debris is contained and the cable does not whip.
8. **Repeat once if needed.** Feed again until the cable passes the previous resistance with less effort. → *Expect:* the pipe path feels open for several feet.
9. **Reassemble access parts.** Replace the trap, stopper, or overflow cover with washers seated correctly. → *Expect:* fittings are snug and aligned.
10. **Flush the drain.** Run warm water for several minutes while checking any opened joints. → *Expect:* water drains steadily and joints stay dry.

## Decision points

- Hard stop that does not yield → stop to avoid puncturing or disconnecting old pipe.
- Cable returns with roots, mud, or sewage odor → call a drain professional for the main line.
- Drain clears but nearby fixtures gurgle → the blockage may be vent or main-line related.
- You are using a powered auger → use manufacturer training or hire a pro, because torque can injure wrists and pipes.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cable stuck:** cable will not withdraw, rotate counterclockwise gently while pulling without yanking.
- **F2 Trap leaks after reinstall:** droplets form at slip nuts, reseat washers and hand-tighten squarely.
- **F3 Clog returns quickly:** water slows again within days, snake again aiming to retrieve material and add a strainer.
- **F4 Dirty splash:** wastewater splashes skin or eyes, wash immediately and disinfect the area.

## Verification

The fixture drains a full basin or several minutes of running water without backing up, gurgling, or leaking at any disturbed fitting.

## Variations

- Kitchen sink: grease clogs may need repeated hot water after mechanical clearing, but do not pour boiling water into PVC.
- Bathroom sink: removing the pop-up stopper often solves the clog before the auger is needed.
- Shower drain: a plastic hair tool may be enough if the clog is near the grate.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: wastewater contains pathogens, and force can damage old or plastic pipe. Never snake after chemical cleaner, never force a hard obstruction, and call a plumber for sewage backup, multiple blocked drains, or fragile piping.
