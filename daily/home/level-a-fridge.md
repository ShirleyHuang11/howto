## Goal

The refrigerator is level side-to-side with a slight backward tilt so the doors close by themselves and seals land evenly.

## Preconditions

- Fridge is installed, loaded normally, and reachable at the front leveling feet or rollers.
- You have a spirit level, wrench or screwdriver specified by the manual, and a flashlight.
- The floor is clear so the door can swing safely while you test it.

## Steps

1. **Check door behavior first.** Open each door halfway and let go gently. → *Expect:* you see whether the door self-closes or drifts open.
2. **Measure side-to-side level.** Place the level on a front shelf or cabinet edge. → *Expect:* the bubble shows any left-right lean.
3. **Find the front adjusters.** Remove the toe grille if needed and identify the feet or roller screws. [BRANCH: threaded feet | roller screws] turn only the adjusters your model provides. → *Expect:* both front adjusters are accessible.
4. **Set side-to-side level.** Raise the low front side in small turns until the bubble centers. → *Expect:* the cabinet no longer leans left or right.
5. **Add slight backward tilt.** Raise both front adjusters evenly until the front is just higher than the back. → *Expect:* doors begin to swing closed from a partly open position.
6. **Check seal contact.** Close the doors on a strip of paper at several points and pull gently. → *Expect:* the paper has light resistance around the gasket.
7. **Reinstall the toe grille.** Clip the grille back without blocking airflow. → *Expect:* the front looks finished and ventilation openings are clear.

## Decision points

- Door slams hard → reduce the backward tilt slightly.
- Door still hangs open → raise both front feet a little more and confirm no shelf item is blocking it.
- Gasket has gaps after leveling → clean the gasket, warm a warped section gently by hand, or replace it.
- Built-in fridge has no visible feet → use the manufacturer's leveling procedure.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Adjuster will not turn:** detect stuck threads or a stripped screw, recover by unloading the door and trying again with the correct tool.
- **F2 Fridge rocks after adjustment:** detect movement when pushing a front corner, recover by lowering the opposite foot until both touch.
- **F3 Door alignment worsens:** detect uneven door tops, recover by consulting hinge adjustment instructions after cabinet leveling.
- **F4 Toe grille blocks airflow:** detect a grille seated against dust or flooring, recover by cleaning and clipping it into its original slots.

## Verification

The fridge is level side-to-side, slightly high at the front, and each door self-closes from a partly open position without rocking.

## Variations

- `french-door`: test both doors and the center mullion because one door may need hinge adjustment.
- `garage-floor`: sloped floors may need shims rated for appliances.
- `wheel-only-model`: front roller screws may both level and lock the cabinet.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not tip the fridge far, crush water lines, or block the lower airflow path while adjusting the front.
