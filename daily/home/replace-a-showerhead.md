## Goal

An old showerhead is removed and a new one seals on the shower arm without leaks or scratched finishes.

## Preconditions

- The shower is off and the shower arm is firmly attached to the wall.
- You have the new showerhead, plumber's tape, cloth, adjustable wrench or pliers, and a small brush.
- Turn water off at the shower controls before working; if the shower arm twists in the wall or leaks behind the escutcheon, call a plumber.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the shower is off.** Close the shower valve fully and let water drip out of the old head. → *Expect:* no active flow remains.
2. **Protect the finish.** Wrap a cloth around the old showerhead nut before using a wrench. → *Expect:* tool jaws do not touch chrome directly.
3. **Unscrew the old head.** Turn the showerhead counterclockwise while supporting the shower arm with your other hand. → *Expect:* the head separates without the wall arm rotating.
4. **Clean the threads.** Remove old tape, mineral crust, and debris from the shower arm threads with fingers or a soft brush. → *Expect:* bare threads are visible all around.
5. **Inspect the shower arm.** Check for cracks, severe corrosion, or looseness at the wall. → *Expect:* the arm feels solid and usable.
6. **Apply new tape.** Wrap plumber's tape clockwise around the male threads two to three turns, keeping the first thread clear. → *Expect:* tape lies flat and tight in the tightening direction.
7. **Install the new showerhead.** Thread it on by hand until snug and square. → *Expect:* it turns smoothly without cross-threading.
8. **Tighten only if needed.** [BRANCH: rubber washer seal | threaded metal seal] hand-tighten washer-style heads; give metal-thread heads a small wrench nudge through a cloth. → *Expect:* the connection is snug without strain.
9. **Leak test.** Turn the shower on gradually and watch the joint for one minute. → *Expect:* water exits only through the spray face.
10. **Adjust spray and final check.** Cycle spray settings and angle the head. → *Expect:* no joint drip appears under normal movement.

## Decision points

- Shower arm rotates while loosening → stop before breaking the pipe in the wall.
- New head includes a rubber washer → do not overtighten, because the washer makes the seal.
- Joint drips after tape and snug tightening → remove, rewrap cleanly, and reinstall squarely.
- Low flow after replacement → check for shipping plugs, filter screens, or local flow restrictor rules.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cross-threading:** head binds after one turn, back it off and restart by hand.
- **F2 Wall-arm leak:** water appears at the wall opening, shut off the shower and call a plumber.
- **F3 Scratched finish:** wrench marks appear, stop using bare tools and continue with a cloth or strap wrench.
- **F4 Weak spray:** flow is poor, remove the head and clear debris from the inlet screen.

## Verification

With the shower running for one minute on each spray mode, the showerhead joint and wall escutcheon stay dry except for normal spray splash.

## Variations

- Handheld shower kits: mount the bracket according to its weight rating and check hose washers at both ends.
- Rain heads: support the shower arm while tightening because the larger head adds leverage.
- Hard-water homes: soak removable screens in vinegar only if the manufacturer allows it.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: water behind the wall can cause hidden damage. Keep force low, support the shower arm, and call a plumber if the arm turns, cracks, or leaks at the wall.
