## Goal

An HVAC or furnace air filter is found, sized correctly, installed in the right airflow direction, and scheduled for regular replacement.

## Preconditions

- Replacement filter with matching length, width, depth, and suitable MERV rating.
- Access to the return grille, furnace slot, or air handler.
- Step stool if the return is high.
- Trash bag for the dirty filter.
- System can be turned off at the thermostat while changing the filter.

## Steps

1. **Turn the system off.** Set the thermostat mode to off before opening any grille or slot. → *Expect:* fan noise stops or does not start during the change.
2. **Find the filter location.** Check return air grilles, the furnace side slot, or the air handler cabinet. → *Expect:* a rectangular filter or empty filter track is visible.
3. **Read the old filter size.** Look at the printed edge for dimensions such as 16x20x1. → *Expect:* replacement size matches all three numbers.
4. **Note the airflow arrow.** Identify which way air moves, usually from room return toward furnace or blower. → *Expect:* arrow direction is understood before removal.
5. **Remove the old filter carefully.** Slide it out without shaking dust into the room. → *Expect:* dirty side faces the incoming air side.
6. **Inspect the slot.** Look for collapse, heavy dust, or a missing cover. → *Expect:* filter track is clear and ready.
7. **Insert the new filter.** Align the printed arrow with airflow toward the equipment. → *Expect:* filter slides fully into place without bending.
8. **Close the grille or cover.** Latch panels and covers securely. → *Expect:* no open gap lets air bypass the filter.
9. **Restart the system.** Turn the thermostat back to the previous mode. → *Expect:* air flows normally with no rattling filter noise.
10. **Record the cadence.** Write the date on the filter edge or set a reminder for 1-3 months, adjusted for pets, smoke, dust, or allergy needs. → *Expect:* next replacement date is visible or scheduled.

## Decision points

- No old filter is present → measure the slot and consult the equipment manual before running long-term.
- Filter resists insertion → size or direction may be wrong; do not crush it into place.
- High-MERV filter restricts airflow → use the rating recommended by the system or technician.
- Multiple returns have filters → replace all filters on the same cycle.
- Filter is wet → investigate condensation or leaks before restarting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong size:** gaps or buckling allow bypass; buy the exact printed or measured size.
- **F2 Arrow reversed:** dust loads the wrong side and airflow suffers; remove and reinstall correctly.
- **F3 Cover will not close:** filter is too thick or off track; reseat or get the right depth.
- **F4 Filter turns black quickly:** soot, candles, smoke, or combustion issues may be present; inspect sources and service equipment.
- **F5 System sounds strained:** filter may be too restrictive; switch to an approved rating and check for blocked returns.

## Verification

The new filter is fully seated with its airflow arrow pointing toward the HVAC equipment, the cover is closed, and a replacement date is recorded.

## Variations

- Washable filter: rinse only if designed for washing, dry completely, then reinstall in the correct direction.
- Apartment wall unit: access may be behind a front panel rather than a duct return.
- Smart thermostat: add a filter reminder using runtime hours if available.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use a stable stool, avoid touching blower components, and turn the system off so dust is not pulled through an open slot.
