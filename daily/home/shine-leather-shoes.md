## Goal

Leather shoes are cleaned, conditioned, polished, and buffed to a neat shine without smearing polish onto socks, floors, or cuffs.

## Preconditions

- Smooth leather shoes, not suede or fabric.
- Shoe brush, soft cloths, matching polish, optional leather conditioner, and a few drops of water.
- Newspaper, towel, or other protected work surface.

## Steps

1. **Remove loose dirt.** Brush the uppers, seams, welt, and sole edge. [BRANCH: dusty shoes | muddy shoes] dry-brush dust, but let mud dry first and brush it off. → *Expect:* no grit remains to scratch the leather.
2. **Wipe the leather.** Use a barely damp cloth, then let the shoe surface dry. → *Expect:* the leather looks clean and matte.
3. **Condition if thirsty.** Rub a small amount of conditioner into dry or creased leather and wait a few minutes. → *Expect:* the leather darkens slightly and feels less papery.
4. **Apply polish in circles.** Wrap a cloth around two fingers and work a thin layer of polish over the shoe. → *Expect:* color looks even, with no thick clumps in seams.
5. **Let it dry.** Rest the shoes until the polish hazes. → *Expect:* the surface looks duller and no polish lifts easily onto a fingertip.
6. **Buff briskly.** Use a horsehair brush or clean cloth with fast light strokes. → *Expect:* a clear shine appears across the toe and quarters.
7. **Add a water-shine on the toe.** Touch one drop of water to the toe, add a tiny polish dot, and circle gently. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* heavy water or aggressive rubbing can dull delicate finishes, so test on a small area first. → *Expect:* the toe becomes glossier without streaks.
8. **Dry between layers.** Repeat thin toe layers only after the prior layer sets, then give a final buff. → *Expect:* the shoe shines and feels dry, not tacky.

## Decision points

- Shoe is suede, nubuck, or patent leather → use a material-specific method, not wax polish.
- Polish color is uncertain → use neutral polish or test inside the heel edge.
- Leather looks cracked → condition first and skip mirror shine.
- Soles are wet → dry the shoes before polish so moisture does not cloud the finish.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cloudy shine:** detect by gray haze after buffing → let it dry longer and buff again with a clean cloth.
- **F2 Uneven color:** detect by patchy panels → apply a very thin second coat to the light areas.
- **F3 Polish on stitching:** detect by colored buildup in seams → brush seams with a dry toothbrush.
- **F4 Sticky shoe:** detect by polish transferring after buffing → too much polish was used, so wipe down and wait.

## Verification

Both shoes are clean, evenly colored, dry to the touch, and reflect light on the toes after buffing.

## Variations

- Cream polish: better for color nourishment, usually softer shine.
- Wax polish: better for toe shine and weather resistance.
- Quick shine: clean, one thin polish coat, five-minute dry, then buff.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Ventilate around strong-smelling products, keep polish away from carpets and pets, and do not use household oils that can go rancid or soften leather glue.
