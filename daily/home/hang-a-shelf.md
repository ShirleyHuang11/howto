## Goal

A wall shelf is mounted level, fastened to structure or appropriate anchors, and loaded within its safe weight limit.

## Preconditions

- Shelf, brackets or mounting rail, screws, drill, level, pencil, tape measure, and stud finder.
- Wall type known or investigated: drywall over studs, masonry, plaster, tile, or hollow door.
- Expected load estimated, including shelf weight plus stored items.

## Steps

1. **Estimate the load.** Add the shelf weight and what will sit on it, then include a safety margin. → *Expect:* you have a target load category: light, medium, or heavy.
2. **Find studs or structure.** Use a stud finder, outlet clues, or small test holes where hidden by the bracket. → *Expect:* stud edges or masonry locations are marked.
3. **Choose fasteners by load.** [BRANCH: stud screws for heavy | rated hollow-wall anchors for medium | small anchors for light] → *Expect:* fastener ratings meet or exceed the planned load.
4. **Mark bracket height.** Hold the shelf or rail where it belongs and mark one hole. → *Expect:* the first mark is at the intended height and clear of hazards.
5. **Level the layout.** Use a level to mark the remaining holes from the first mark. → *Expect:* marks line up horizontally and match the bracket spacing.
6. **Check for hidden services.** Avoid drilling directly above outlets, switches, plumbing fixtures, or known pipes. → *Expect:* the drill path is reasonably clear.
7. **Drill pilot holes.** Use a bit sized for the screw or anchor, keeping the drill square to the wall. → *Expect:* holes are clean and at the marks.
8. **Install anchors or screws.** Set anchors flush or drive screws into studs, leaving brackets snug but adjustable. → *Expect:* brackets hold position without spinning.
9. **Level and tighten.** Place the level on the shelf, adjust, then tighten all fasteners. → *Expect:* the bubble is centered and brackets no longer shift.
10. **Load gradually.** Add items in stages, heaviest near brackets. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overloaded anchors can tear drywall, so stop if the shelf sags or fasteners move. → *Expect:* the shelf remains level and quiet under normal load.

## Decision points

- Heavy books, dishes, or tools → fasten into studs or masonry, not just drywall anchors.
- Tile wall → use tile bit and avoid cracking by drilling slowly without hammer mode.
- Rental wall → confirm rules and choose removable or low-damage mounting only for light loads.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missed stud:** detect screw spinning or pulling out with white dust; move to the actual stud or use a properly rated anchor.
- **F2 Shelf not level:** detect bubble off-center; loosen one bracket, adjust, and retighten before loading.
- **F3 Anchor blows out:** detect a crater or loose anchor; patch the hole and relocate the fastener.
- **F4 Hidden obstruction:** detect metal, pipe-like resistance, or sudden spark risk; stop drilling and choose a new location.

## Verification

The shelf is level, fasteners are tight, and the planned load sits on it without sagging, shifting, or wall damage.

## Variations

- Masonry: use masonry bits and anchors rated for concrete, brick, or block.
- Plaster: predrill carefully and prefer studs because plaster can crumble around anchors.
- Floating shelf: follow the rail spacing exactly because small errors become visible.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from falling objects, dust, and hidden electrical or plumbing lines. Wear eye protection and keep heavy items off until the mount is verified.
