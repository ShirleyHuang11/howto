## Goal

Grout lines are cleaned without damaging tile, and the surface is evaluated for sealing or mold treatment.

## Preconditions

- Grout brush or stiff nylon brush, baking soda, water, spray bottle, towels, and gloves.
- Optional hydrogen peroxide or a grout-safe commercial cleaner.
- Ventilation if using stronger cleaners.
- Knowledge of tile type, especially natural stone that can be damaged by acid.
- Time for paste to dwell before scrubbing.

## Steps

1. **Identify dirt versus mold.** Dirt is usually tan or gray and wipes lighter; mold is spotty black, green, or pink and returns in damp zones. → *Expect:* the stain type has a likely cause.
2. **Dry sweep or vacuum.** Remove loose grit before wet cleaning. → *Expect:* grout lines are free of sand that could scratch tile.
3. **Mix a mild paste.** Combine baking soda with enough water to make a spreadable paste. → *Expect:* paste clings to vertical grout without running.
4. **Apply and dwell.** Press paste into grout lines and let it sit 10-15 minutes. → *Expect:* paste remains damp and begins lifting surface soil.
5. **Brush along the grout.** Scrub in short strokes along the line, then cross it lightly at stubborn spots. → *Expect:* soil releases into the paste without tile scratching.
6. **Boost if needed.** Mist hydrogen peroxide onto the paste for stained grout, avoiding incompatible stone surfaces. → *Expect:* mild bubbling or lightening appears at stains.
7. **Rinse thoroughly.** Wipe with clean water until no gritty cleaner remains. → *Expect:* tile feels smooth and grout lines are visible.
8. **Dry completely.** Use towels and ventilation to remove moisture. → *Expect:* grout color can be judged without wet darkening.
9. **Seal porous grout.** Apply grout sealer after cleaning and full drying if water soaks in quickly. → *Expect:* sealer wets the grout line, then excess is wiped from tile.
10. **Set a moisture habit.** Squeegee showers or dry splash zones after use. → *Expect:* cleaned grout stays dry longer between uses.

## Decision points

- Natural stone tile → avoid vinegar and acidic cleaners unless the stone manufacturer allows them.
- Mold appears deep or fuzzy → use a mold-specific cleaner and fix moisture before cosmetic cleaning.
- Grout is cracked or missing → cleaning will not seal it; repair grout first.
- Colored grout → test cleaner in a hidden spot for fading.
- Water beads on grout → sealer is still working and may not need reapplication.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Grout still dark after drying:** stain is absorbed or mold is deeper; repeat once, then consider oxygen bleach or regrouting.
- **F2 Tile haze remains:** cleaner residue dried on tile; wipe with clean water and buff dry.
- **F3 Brush scratches tile:** tool is too abrasive; stop and switch to nylon, then test hidden areas.
- **F4 Mold returns fast:** ventilation or leaks are unresolved; dry the area and address moisture source.
- **F5 Sealer streaks on tile:** excess was not wiped promptly; buff immediately or use the remover recommended by the sealer.

## Verification

After drying, grout lines are visibly cleaner, free of gritty residue, and any porous cleaned grout is sealed or scheduled for sealing.

## Variations

- Floor grout: work in small sections and mop rinse water frequently.
- Shower grout: prioritize drying and ventilation after cleaning.
- Commercial cleaner: follow dwell time exactly and never mix cleaners.

## Safety & privacy

Low to medium risk from cleaners and slippery wet tile. Wear gloves, ventilate, and never mix bleach with ammonia, vinegar, or unknown products.
