## Goal

Stop a toilet bowl from overflowing, then clear or isolate the cause without making the spill worse.

## Preconditions

- The toilet is accessible and the floor is safe to stand on.
- You have towels, gloves, and a toilet plunger if available.
- You can reach either the tank or the water shutoff valve behind the toilet.

## Steps

1. **Stop new water fast.** Remove the tank lid and lift the flapper closed, or push the flush handle back up if it is stuck. → *Expect:* water stops entering the bowl.
2. **Shut the toilet supply valve.** Turn the small valve near the wall clockwise until it stops. → *Expect:* the tank no longer refills.
3. **Stabilize the spill.** Place towels around the base and keep people from walking through wastewater. → *Expect:* water is contained and the floor is less slippery.
4. **Check the bowl level.** Wait until the water level drops below the rim before plunging. → *Expect:* there is room for plunger movement without more overflow.
5. **Plunge with a toilet plunger.** Seal the flange over the drain and make steady push-pull strokes for 20 to 30 seconds. → *Expect:* water level drops or gurgles as the clog moves.
6. **Test with a small controlled flush.** Reopen the valve partway, let the tank fill, and flush only if the bowl is below half full. → *Expect:* water drains normally without rising toward the rim.
7. **Keep the water off if the toilet still rises.** Close the valve again and stop testing. → *Expect:* no additional water enters the toilet.
8. **Clean and disinfect the area.** Wear gloves and use an appropriate disinfectant on hard surfaces. → *Expect:* contaminated water is removed and surfaces are drying.

## Decision points

- Bowl is already at the rim → stop water first and do not flush again.
- Valve will not turn or leaks when touched → lift the flapper, then shut off the home water supply and call a plumber.
- Wastewater reached carpet, walls, or lower floors → document with photos and call property management or a mitigation professional.
- Repeated clogs happen without excess paper → arrange plumbing service.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Supply valve stuck:** detect valve will not move by hand → do not force it hard; shut the water main and call a plumber.
- **F2 Plunging splashes:** detect contaminated splatter → pause, lower force, reseal the plunger, and disinfect afterward.
- **F3 Bowl rises on test flush:** detect water climbing again → close the valve immediately and stop flushing.
- **F4 Leak at toilet base:** detect water appearing around the base after flushing → keep the valve closed and call a plumber.

## Verification

The supply is controlled, the bowl no longer rises, a cautious test flush drains normally, and any wastewater has been cleaned or isolated for professional cleanup.

## Variations

- Dual-flush toilet: use the tank flapper or supply valve rather than pressing more buttons.
- Concealed cistern: find the access panel for the shutoff, or shut the water main if you cannot reach it.
- Rental housing: notify the landlord promptly if water escaped the bathroom.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from contaminated water and slip hazards. Wash hands thoroughly, keep children and pets away, and photograph damage before cleanup if insurance or landlord reporting may be needed.
