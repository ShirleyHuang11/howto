## Goal

A heavy mirror is hung level on hardware rated for its weight, anchored into studs or suitable heavy-duty wall anchors.

## Preconditions

- You know the mirror's approximate weight and have hardware rated above that weight.
- You have a stud finder, level, tape measure, pencil, drill, bits, screws, anchors or cleat, and a helper for large mirrors.
- ⚠️ Heavy glass can injure people and damage walls; if the wall type, weight, or hidden utilities are uncertain, call a professional installer.

## Steps

1. **Weigh or verify the mirror.** Check the label or weigh yourself with and without the mirror. → *Expect:* you have a weight number for hardware selection.
2. **Inspect the hanger.** [BRANCH: D-rings | French cleat | wire] use two D-rings or a cleat for heavy mirrors; avoid light picture wire unless rated by the maker. → *Expect:* the mirror has sound attachment points.
3. **Choose the height.** Mark the desired top edge and centerline with painter's tape. → *Expect:* the placement is visible before drilling.
4. **Find studs.** Use a stud finder, then confirm by measuring likely 16 inch or 24 inch spacing. → *Expect:* at least one stud location is marked.
5. **Choose anchors.** Use screws into studs where possible; use toggle or heavy hollow-wall anchors only if rated for the wall and mirror weight. → *Expect:* selected hardware rating exceeds the mirror weight.
6. **Mark hanger points.** Measure from the mirror top to the D-rings or cleat, transfer points to the wall, and check level. → *Expect:* holes align with the mirror hardware.
7. **Drill pilot holes.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm height, level, and no hidden electrical or plumbing before drilling. → *Expect:* clean holes sized for screws or anchors.
8. **Install mounting hardware.** Drive stud screws or set anchors exactly as directed, leaving screw heads proud only if the hanger requires it. → *Expect:* hardware does not wobble under hand pressure.
9. **Hang with a helper.** Lift the mirror onto both points or the cleat without sliding across the wall. → *Expect:* both sides catch securely.
10. **Level and tug-test.** Adjust if allowed, then apply a gentle downward and outward test. → *Expect:* the mirror stays level and firmly seated.

## Decision points

- No stud where needed → use a properly rated toggle anchor, a cleat spanning studs, or choose a new location.
- Wall is masonry → use masonry anchors and bits rated for the load.
- Mirror lacks rated hardware → add a manufacturer-approved hanger or use a professional framer.
- Mirror is too large to control alone → stop until a helper is available.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stud reading uncertain:** marks vary on repeated scans, test nearby with a small pilot hole or use a different location.
- **F2 Anchor spins:** anchor turns instead of tightening, remove it and install a larger rated anchor in a fresh hole.
- **F3 Mirror sits crooked:** one point is higher, lift off and correct hardware position instead of forcing the hanger.
- **F4 Wall bows or crumbles:** surface deforms under load, remove the mirror and use studs, masonry anchors, or a pro.

## Verification

The mirror remains level after a gentle tug test, with all weight carried by rated hardware and no visible wall movement.

## Variations

- Plaster walls: drill carefully through plaster and anchor into studs or use plaster-rated toggles.
- Rental housing: get permission before drilling large anchor holes.
- Bathroom mirrors: use corrosion-resistant hardware and avoid drilling where pipes or wires may run.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: falling glass can cause serious cuts. Use a helper, eye protection while drilling, rated hardware, and stop if you may hit electrical wiring, plumbing, or weak wall material.
