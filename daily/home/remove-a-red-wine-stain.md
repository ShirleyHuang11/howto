## Goal

Remove or lighten a fresh red wine stain without spreading it, bleaching the item unexpectedly, or heat-setting the color.

## Preconditions

- Clean white cloths or paper towels, cold water, salt, dish soap, and 3 percent hydrogen peroxide are available.
- The stained item can tolerate water, or its care label has been checked.
- A hidden test area is available for peroxide or any cleaner.

## Steps

1. **Blot immediately.** Press a clean white cloth onto the stain and lift straight up; do not rub. → *Expect:* wine transfers to the cloth without enlarging the stain.
2. **Move to a protected surface.** Put a towel under fabric or blot carpet from the outside toward the center. → *Expect:* liquid does not spread to clean areas.
3. **Flush with cold water.** Run cold water through the back of washable fabric, or dab cold water onto carpet and upholstery. → *Expect:* some red color loosens and dilutes.
4. **Choose a treatment.** [BRANCH: salt for fresh wet stain | dish-soap plus peroxide for washable colorfast fabric] pick salt when the stain is still wet, or peroxide mix after testing. → *Expect:* the method matches the material risk.
5. **Use salt if fresh.** Cover the damp stain with a thick layer of salt and let it draw wine out for 10 minutes. → *Expect:* the salt turns pink or clumpy.
6. **Test peroxide mix.** Mix 1 part dish soap with 2 parts 3 percent hydrogen peroxide and dab a hidden area first. → *Expect:* the test spot does not lighten after 5 minutes.
7. **Apply treatment gently.** Dab the chosen treatment onto the stain, working from edge to center. → *Expect:* red color lifts onto the cloth or into the salt.
8. **Rinse cold.** Rinse washable fabric in cold water or blot carpets with a clean damp cloth. → *Expect:* cleaner residue and loosened wine are removed.
9. **Check before drying.** Inspect the item in good light before using heat. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* dryer heat or ironing can set remaining red pigment; confirm the stain is gone first. → *Expect:* no visible red remains before any heat is used.
10. **Launder or air dry.** Wash fabric according to its care label or let carpet air dry after blotting. → *Expect:* the treated area dries without a ring.

## Decision points

- Item is silk, wool, leather, or dry-clean-only → blot, keep it cool, and contact a cleaner.
- Peroxide lightens the test spot → stop and use cold water plus professional cleaning instead.
- Stain is old and dry → expect improvement, not guaranteed removal, and repeat cold treatments before laundering.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Spread stain:** detect a larger pink halo, recover by blotting from the outside inward with a dry cloth.
- **F2 Bleached fabric:** detect a lighter test spot, recover by stopping peroxide and rinsing cold immediately.
- **F3 Heat-set mark:** detect a brown or purple shadow after drying hot, recover by soaking cold and repeating, but expect limited success.
- **F4 Sticky residue:** detect a tacky patch, recover by rinsing or blotting with plain cold water until clean.

## Verification

The stained area shows no visible red in good light before the item is exposed to dryer heat or ironing.

## Variations

- `carpet`: use repeated damp blotting instead of running water through the backing.
- `table-linen`: stretch over a bowl and pour cold water from the back of the stain.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from fabric damage. Test peroxide first, never mix cleaners with bleach, and avoid heat until the stain is fully removed.
