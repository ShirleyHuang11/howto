## Goal

Store off-season clothing so it stays clean, dry, labeled, and ready to wear next season.

## Preconditions

- Clothes are ready to sort by season, fabric, and condition.
- You have laundry supplies, breathable garment bags or bins, labels, and cedar blocks or sachets if desired.
- A cool, dry, dark storage area is available.
- Damaged or stained items can be repaired, cleaned, donated, or discarded before storage.

## Steps

1. **Sort by season and use.** Group coats, sweaters, swimwear, formalwear, and everyday items separately. → *Expect:* each pile has a clear storage purpose.
2. **Inspect for stains and damage.** Check collars, cuffs, underarms, hems, and pockets. → *Expect:* items needing cleaning or repair are separated.
3. **Clean before storing.** Wash or dry-clean clothes according to care labels and dry them fully. → *Expect:* no food, sweat, perfume, or moisture is stored with fabric.
4. **Empty every pocket.** Remove tissues, coins, makeup, receipts, and keys. → *Expect:* pockets are flat and nothing can stain or tear fabric.
5. **Choose breathable storage.** [BRANCH: folded | hanging] use breathable bins or cotton bags for folded items, and garment bags for hanging pieces. → *Expect:* fabric can avoid trapped moisture.
6. **Fold heavy knits.** Fold sweaters and heavy knits instead of hanging them. → *Expect:* shoulders will not stretch during storage.
7. **Add cedar safely.** Place cedar blocks or sachets near clothes, not loose mothballs against skin-contact fabric. → *Expect:* pest deterrent is present without chemical residue on clothing.
8. **Label containers.** Write the season, contents, and date on each bin or bag. → *Expect:* items can be found without opening every container.
9. **Store cool, dry, and dark.** Put containers away from sunlight, damp floors, heaters, and exterior leaks. → *Expect:* storage conditions reduce fading, mildew, and pests.
10. **Check after a week.** Open one container briefly to confirm there is no damp smell. → *Expect:* clothes smell clean and dry.

## Decision points

- Clothes are damp or warm from the dryer → let them cool and dry fully before sealing.
- A stain remains after washing → treat again before storage because stains can darken over time.
- Storage area is humid → use a different location or add a moisture absorber outside breathable garment bags.
- Mothballs are being considered → avoid direct contact with clothes and skin, and follow the label in a ventilated space.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Mildew smell:** detect by musty odor when opening a container → recover by washing affected items and moving storage to a drier area.
- **F2 Pest damage:** detect by holes, webbing, or shed insect skins → recover by laundering, vacuuming the storage area, and isolating affected items.
- **F3 Stretched shoulders:** detect by hanger bumps on knits → recover by steaming lightly and folding knits next time.
- **F4 Lost category:** detect by unlabeled bins or mixed seasons → recover by relabeling and grouping contents before returning them.

## Verification

All stored clothes are clean, fully dry, labeled by contents, and placed in a cool, dry, dark location using breathable containers.

## Variations

- Formal clothing: use padded hangers and breathable garment bags.
- Vacuum bags: use only for short-term space saving because compression can crease or flatten some fabrics.
- Shoes and accessories: clean and dry them before storing separately from delicate clothing.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid mothballs touching clothes worn against skin, keep cedar and chemical repellents away from children, and remove personal papers or valuables from pockets before storage.
