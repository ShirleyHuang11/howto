## Goal

A sticking wood door is trimmed only where it rubs, sealed at the bare edge, and tested so it closes without scraping.

## Preconditions

- You have paper, pencil, screwdriver, painter's tape, sandpaper, hand plane or sanding block, drop cloth, and paint or clear sealer.
- The hinge screws are tight and the door frame is not visibly shifting.
- Remove only small amounts of wood at a time.
- Call a carpenter if the frame is out of square, the door is metal or fiberglass, or the door must be cut near glass or fire-rating labels.

## Steps

1. **Confirm the rub.** Close the door slowly and slide paper around the top, latch side, and bottom gaps. → *Expect:* the tight area catches the paper.
2. **Mark the high spot.** Rub pencil on the sticking edge or mark shiny scrape points with painter's tape. → *Expect:* the material to remove is clearly bounded.
3. **Check hinges first.** Tighten hinge screws before removing wood. → *Expect:* any hinge-caused sag is corrected or ruled out.
4. **Decide removal method.** [BRANCH: light rub | heavy rub] sand a light rub in place or remove the door and plane a heavier high spot. → *Expect:* the tool choice matches the amount of interference.
5. **Support the door.** If removing it, pull hinge pins with the door blocked and place it on padded supports. → *Expect:* the door edge is stable for work.
6. **Remove small passes.** Plane or sand only the marked area, working with the grain and checking often. → *Expect:* a thin shaving or dust comes off evenly.
7. **Keep the edge square.** Hold the plane flat and avoid rounding the door face. → *Expect:* the edge remains straight and the reveal stays even.
8. **Seal bare wood.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* before reinstalling, confirm enough wood has been removed, then seal exposed wood with paint or clear finish. → *Expect:* raw wood is covered against swelling.
9. **Rehang and test.** Reinstall hinge pins, close the door, and repeat only if it still rubs. → *Expect:* the door closes without scraping.

## Decision points

- Rub disappears after hinge tightening → do not plane the door.
- Door rubs seasonally at one swollen edge → sand lightly and seal well.
- Door hits the floor along the full width → check flooring clearance before cutting the bottom.
- Fire-rated door label may be affected → call a qualified door technician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too much wood removed:** gap becomes wide, add weatherstrip or consult a carpenter.
- **F2 Tear-out appears:** plane chips the grain, switch direction or use finer sanding.
- **F3 Door still sticks:** rub mark moved, repeat paper test and remove only the new high spot.
- **F4 Bare edge swells later:** sticking returns after humidity, dry the area and reseal all exposed wood.

## Verification

The door closes and latches from a normal push, with paper sliding through the repaired gap and no exposed raw wood left unsealed.

## Variations

- Painted doors: score paint at the edge first to reduce chipping.
- Exterior doors: preserve weatherstrip contact while removing the minimum wood.
- Pocket doors: remove trim only if necessary and call a pro if rollers are failing.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: planing is permanent and tools can cut skin. Mark carefully, remove little material, wear eye protection, and seal bare wood before use.
