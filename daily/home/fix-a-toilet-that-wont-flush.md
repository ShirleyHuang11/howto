## Goal

A toilet that will not flush is restored by finding whether the problem is the handle chain, flapper, tank water level, or bowl clog.

## Preconditions

- You can reach the toilet shutoff valve and have towels nearby.
- You have a plunger, bucket, rubber gloves, flashlight, and an adjustable wrench if the handle is loose.
- Turn water off first before working inside the tank if parts are broken, stuck, or spraying.
- Call a plumber if sewage backs up into other drains, the bowl overflows repeatedly, or the toilet rocks at the floor.

## Steps

1. **Stop any overflow risk.** Turn the shutoff valve clockwise if the bowl is rising or the tank is refilling nonstop. → *Expect:* water stops entering the tank.
2. **Open the tank.** Lift the lid with two hands and set it flat on a towel. → *Expect:* the handle arm, chain, flapper, fill valve, and water level are visible.
3. **Check the handle and chain.** Press the handle while watching the chain lift the flapper. [BRANCH: chain slack | chain disconnected] shorten slack to about 1/2 inch or reconnect it to the handle arm. → *Expect:* the flapper lifts fully when the handle is pressed.
4. **Check the flapper seal.** Press the flapper down, then lift it by hand and inspect for warping, slime, or a broken hinge. → *Expect:* the flapper moves freely and seats flat over the drain opening.
5. **Replace a bad flapper.** Turn water off, flush to drain the tank, unhook the old flapper, clip on a matching one, and reconnect the chain. → *Expect:* the new flapper covers the flush valve without twisting.
6. **Check the tank fill line.** Compare the water level with the mark on the overflow tube or tank wall. → *Expect:* water sits about 1 inch below the overflow tube or at the fill mark.
7. **Raise low tank water.** Adjust the float screw or float cup upward a small amount, then let the tank refill. → *Expect:* the next flush has a stronger surge.
8. **Test for a bowl clog.** If the tank flushes but the bowl drains weakly, add enough water to cover the plunger cup and plunge with firm straight strokes. → *Expect:* the bowl water pulls down sharply.
9. **Flush with observation.** Restore water, wait for the tank to fill, flush once, and watch both tank and bowl. → *Expect:* the tank empties quickly and the bowl clears.

## Decision points

- Handle moves but nothing lifts → repair the chain or handle arm before checking clogs.
- Flapper lifts but water swirls weakly → raise tank water level or check for a restricted rim feed.
- Tank empties normally but bowl remains full → treat it as a clog and stop flushing until cleared.
- Multiple fixtures drain poorly → stop and call a plumber because the blockage may be in the branch or main drain.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bowl starts to overflow:** water rises close to the rim, close the shutoff valve and wait before plunging.
- **F2 Chain binds:** handle stays down or flapper remains open, shorten or reroute the chain so it hangs free.
- **F3 Flapper still leaks:** tank slowly refills after repair, clean the flush valve seat or replace the flapper with the exact size.
- **F4 Plunger does not clear clog:** water level stays high after repeated plunging, use a toilet auger or call a plumber.

## Verification

After two flushes, the handle lifts the flapper, the bowl clears fully, the tank refills to the fill line, and no water keeps running.

## Variations

- Dual-flush toilets: check both buttons and the manufacturer-specific flush cartridge.
- Pressure-assist toilets: do not open the sealed pressure vessel, call a plumber for internal failures.
- Low-flow toilets: use a toilet auger for clogs instead of repeated flushing.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: overflow can damage floors and expose sewage. Shut water off before tank repairs, wear gloves for clogs, disinfect tools, and call a plumber for recurring backups or leaks at the floor.
