## Goal

A shrunken wool sweater is relaxed with conditioner, gently stretched, and reshaped flat so it regains some usable size.

## Preconditions

- Lukewarm water, hair conditioner or wool wash, towels, basin, measuring tape, and flat drying space are available.
- The sweater is wool or animal fiber, not felted into a dense mat.
- The care label and current measurements are checked before starting.

## Steps

1. **Measure the sweater.** Record chest width, sleeve length, and body length. → *Expect:* target areas for stretching are clear.
2. **Fill a basin.** Use lukewarm water and mix in 1 to 2 tablespoons hair conditioner. → *Expect:* water feels slippery, not hot.
3. **Soak without agitation.** Submerge the sweater for 20 to 30 minutes. → *Expect:* fibers feel relaxed and heavy with water.
4. **Lift carefully.** Support the whole sweater when removing it from the basin. → *Expect:* wet fabric does not stretch unevenly from its own weight.
5. **Press out water.** Roll in a towel and press, without wringing. → *Expect:* sweater is damp but not dripping.
6. **Stretch gradually.** Tug small sections to the target measurements, working side to side and sleeve by sleeve. → *Expect:* fabric gives slightly without ripping or thinning.
7. **Reshape flat.** Lay on a dry towel and square the shoulders, hem, cuffs, and side seams. → *Expect:* sweater matches a wearable outline.
8. **Dry in place.** Leave flat until fully dry, replacing wet towels if needed. → *Expect:* shape holds without hanger marks.

## Decision points

- Sweater is fully felted → expect only minor improvement.
- Fibers resist stretching → soak 10 minutes longer rather than pulling harder.
- Sweater grows too much in one area → pat it back into shape while damp.
- Dye bleeds into water → remove promptly and rinse gently.
- Garment is valuable → use a professional cleaner before experimenting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No size recovery:** detect measurements unchanged after soaking; recover by accepting the limit or repurposing the sweater.
- **F2 Uneven sleeves:** detect one sleeve longer; recover by re-dampening and matching both sleeves to the shorter target.
- **F3 Misshapen shoulders:** detect drooping or points; recover by laying flat and pressing seams into place.
- **F4 Conditioner residue:** detect slick feel after drying; recover with a brief cool rinse and flat dry.
- **F5 Fiber damage:** detect thinning or holes; recover by stopping stretch and mending only after dry.

## Verification

After drying flat, at least one target measurement is closer to the desired size and the sweater lies evenly without twists.

## Variations

- Baby shampoo: use it in place of conditioner for a lighter rinse.
- Blocking wires: pin edges to measurements for controlled drying.
- Minor cuff shrinkage: soak and stretch only the cuff area.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk to people, but the garment may not fully recover. Avoid hot water, wringing, and hanging while wet.
