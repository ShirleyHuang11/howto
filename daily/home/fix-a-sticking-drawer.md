## Goal

A drawer opens and closes smoothly without scraping, jumping its track, or needing excessive force.

## Preconditions

- The drawer can be emptied and removed or pulled mostly out.
- Candle wax, bar soap, screwdriver, and flashlight are available.
- Heavy or fragile contents can be set aside safely.

## Steps

1. **Empty the drawer.** Remove contents and check for items jammed behind it. → *Expect:* the drawer is light and moves farther than before.
2. **Find the rub.** Pull slowly and watch where wood, runners, or slides scrape. → *Expect:* the sticking point is visible or audible.
3. **Remove loose debris.** Vacuum crumbs, screws, labels, or splinters from tracks and corners. → *Expect:* tracks look clear.
4. **Tighten hardware.** Snug loose slide screws, knobs, and front-panel fasteners. → *Expect:* parts no longer wiggle under hand pressure.
5. **Wax wood runners.** Rub candle wax or dry bar soap along wooden contact surfaces. → *Expect:* a thin slick film appears on the runners.
6. **Reseat or shim.** [BRANCH: slide is loose | cabinet is racked] tighten screws or add a thin shim behind the low side. → *Expect:* drawer front sits more evenly.
7. **Test with light load.** Slide the empty drawer in and out 5 times. → *Expect:* travel improves without scraping or jumping.
8. **Reload lightly.** Put contents back with heavy items spread evenly. → *Expect:* the drawer still moves with one hand.

## Decision points

- Drawer sticks only when full → reduce weight or move heavy items lower.
- Wood swells in humid weather → dehumidify first and avoid sanding unless sticking is constant.
- Metal slide is bent → replacement is usually faster than reshaping.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hidden obstruction:** detect a sudden hard stop, recover by removing the drawer and clearing the back.
- **F2 Loose slide:** detect side-to-side wobble, recover by tightening screws or replacing stripped screw holes.
- **F3 Overloaded drawer:** detect smooth empty movement but stiff loaded movement, recover by removing weight.
- **F4 Swollen wood:** detect seasonal rubbing along broad wood surfaces, recover with wax first and sand only the high spot if needed.

## Verification

The drawer travels fully open and fully closed 5 times with its normal load and without scraping, binding, or jumping the runner.

## Variations

- Ball-bearing slides: clean first, then use a dry lubricant made for slides.
- Antique wood drawer: use wax, not oil, to avoid staining contents.
- Kitchen drawer: check for utensils wedged over the back rail.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from pinched fingers and falling drawers. Support the drawer with both hands when removing it, and keep tools out of food-contact areas.
