## Goal

Stop a small, contained kitchen fire without spreading it or blocking your escape.

## Preconditions

- The fire is small, visible, and still limited to a pan, appliance, towel, or nearby surface.
- You have a clear exit path and another person can call emergency services if needed.
- You know where the stove controls, breaker, and fire extinguisher are.

## Steps

1. **Check your escape path first.** Stand between the fire and an exit, and keep others away from the kitchen. → *Expect:* you can leave in one movement if the fire grows.
2. **Turn off the heat if you can reach the control safely.** Use the knob or switch without leaning over flames. → *Expect:* burner, oven, or appliance heat source is off.
3. **Cover a pan fire.** Slide a metal lid or baking sheet over the pan from the side, then leave it covered. → *Expect:* flames shrink or stop as oxygen is cut off.
4. **Smother a small grease fire only with baking soda if no lid fits.** Use handfuls from a box for a small pan fire. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* never pour water on burning oil or grease, because it can explode burning fat across the room. → *Expect:* flames reduce without splashing.
5. **Handle an oven fire by keeping the door closed.** Turn the oven off and do not open the door until fully cool. → *Expect:* flames stay contained and fade.
6. **Handle an appliance fire by cutting power.** Unplug only if the plug is reachable without touching fire, smoke, or water; otherwise shut the circuit breaker. → *Expect:* appliance power is off and you are not in contact with energized equipment.
7. **Use a fire extinguisher only if the fire is still small.** Use a Class B or multipurpose ABC extinguisher for grease, and only from your exit side. → *Expect:* extinguisher stream reaches the base of the fire while the exit remains behind you.
8. **Call the fire service if flames do not go out quickly.** Leave, close the door behind you if possible, and call emergency services from outside. → *Expect:* everyone is out and responders are being dispatched.
9. **Wait before uncovering or moving cookware.** Leave a smothered pan covered until cool. → *Expect:* no re-ignition when the area is monitored from a safe distance.

## Decision points

- Fire is larger than a small pan, spreading to cabinets, or producing heavy smoke → evacuate and call the fire service.
- Your exit is not clear → leave immediately and call emergency services.
- Burning material is electrical or appliance-related → cut power first if safe, then use a Class C or ABC extinguisher.
- Clothing catches fire → stop, drop, and roll, then cool burns with running cool water and seek medical care.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Water added to grease:** detect violent flare-up or splatter → evacuate immediately, close the door if safe, and call the fire service.
- **F2 Lid removed too soon:** detect reignition when oxygen returns → slide the lid back on if safe, otherwise leave and call.
- **F3 Fire extinguisher empties:** detect no pressure or no agent discharge → evacuate, close the door, and call emergency services.
- **F4 Smoke exposure:** detect coughing, dizziness, or poor visibility → get outside and ask emergency responders or medical services for guidance.

## Verification

The heat source is off, flames are out, smoke is clearing, cookware remains covered until cool, and emergency services have been called if the fire spread, restarted, or could not be controlled within seconds.

## Variations

- Induction cooktop: turn off the cooktop and remove power if the controls still respond.
- Deep fryer: use the lid if designed for it, or a Class K extinguisher in commercial kitchens.
- Microwave: keep the door closed, stop the microwave, unplug if safe, and wait for smoke to clear.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from burns, smoke inhalation, and fire spread. Do not fight a fire if you are unsure, if smoke is heavy, or if the fire is between you and the exit. Report fire damage honestly to insurance or property management.
