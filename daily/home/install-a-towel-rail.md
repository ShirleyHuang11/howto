## Goal

A towel rail is mounted level, fixed into anchors suited to the wall, and strong enough for normal towel pull.

## Preconditions

- You have the rail kit, level, pencil, tape measure, drill, bits, anchors, screwdriver, and pipe or wire detector.
- The wall area is dry and the rail will not block doors, switches, vents, or shower screens.
- You know the wall type, such as stud, tile, masonry, or hollow drywall.

## Steps

1. **Choose the location.** Hold the rail at usable height and check clearance for hanging towels. → *Expect:* the rail fits without hitting nearby fixtures.
2. **Scan for pipes and wires.** Use a detector and consider plumbing routes near sinks, showers, and radiators. → *Expect:* the fixing points avoid likely services.
3. **Mark level fixing points.** Use the template or brackets, then check with a level. → *Expect:* marks are level and spaced to match the rail.
4. **Select wall anchors.** [BRANCH: stud | hollow wall | masonry or tile] use screws into studs, cavity anchors for hollow walls, and masonry or tile bits for solid surfaces. → *Expect:* fixings match the wall material.
5. **Drill pilot holes.** Drill straight at the marked points, using tape on tile and the correct bit size. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm the marks and service scan before drilling. → *Expect:* holes are clean and correctly spaced.
6. **Set anchors and brackets.** Insert anchors flush and screw brackets tight without cracking tile or plaster. → *Expect:* brackets do not wiggle.
7. **Attach the rail.** Fit the rail onto brackets and tighten grub screws or end caps. → *Expect:* the rail is level and fully seated.
8. **Test the pull.** Pull down and outward with moderate hand force, then hang a towel. → *Expect:* the rail stays firm and the towel hangs clear.

## Decision points

- Detector shows a service near a mark → move the rail or choose a different fixing line.
- Tile starts to crack → stop, switch to a tile bit, and drill without hammer mode.
- Hollow wall anchor spins → remove it and use a larger rated cavity fixing.
- Rail will carry wet bath sheets → prefer studs or stronger anchors over light plastic plugs.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rail is crooked:** detect a visible slope, recover by loosening one bracket and correcting only if holes allow it.
- **F2 Anchor pulls out:** detect movement during pull test, recover with a rated cavity anchor or stud fixing.
- **F3 Screw hits metal or pipe:** detect hard unexpected resistance, recover by stopping immediately and relocating after inspection.
- **F4 Bracket cover rattles:** detect loose trim after mounting, recover by tightening the grub screw or reseating the cover.

## Verification

The towel rail is level, brackets are firm, and it holds a wet towel plus a moderate hand pull without movement.

## Variations

- `tile-wall`: use masking tape, a tile bit, and no hammer action until through the tile.
- `heated-towel-rail`: electrical or plumbing connections require the appropriate qualified trade.
- `rental-home`: use approved fixings only with permission, because towel rails take repeated pulling.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from drilling into hidden services and cracked tile. Scan first, drill slowly, and stop immediately if you hit unexpected resistance.
