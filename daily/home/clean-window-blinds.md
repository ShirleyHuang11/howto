## Goal

Window blinds are dusted on both faces, spot-cleaned or washed as appropriate, and dried before being raised or stacked.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: microfiber cloth or dusting mitt, old clean sock, vacuum brush attachment, mild dish soap, towel.
- Blind material is known: vinyl, aluminum, faux wood, real wood, or fabric.
- A stable step stool is available if the top rail is above comfortable reach.

## Steps

1. **Lower the blinds fully.** Close the slats in one direction without forcing the tilt wand or cord. → *Expect:* one continuous face is exposed.
2. **Dust the closed face.** Wipe from top to bottom with a microfiber cloth or vacuum brush. → *Expect:* loose dust is removed instead of falling onto already-clean slats.
3. **Reverse the slats.** Tilt them closed the other way. → *Expect:* the opposite dusty edges face you.
4. **Dust the second face.** Repeat the top-to-bottom pass. → *Expect:* both sides look evenly clean in daylight.
5. **Use the sock trick for grime.** Put a damp sock over your hand, pinch one slat at a time, and slide from center to edge. → *Expect:* finger oils and gray lines transfer to the sock.
6. **Deep-wash removable vinyl or aluminum slats if needed.** [BRANCH: removable washable blinds | wood or fabric] wash washable blinds in a tub with mild soap; spot-clean wood or fabric only. → *Expect:* sticky residue lifts without soaking delicate material.
7. **Rinse and dry.** Wipe soap with clean water, then towel each wet slat before raising. → *Expect:* no drips, no soap film, and cords stay dry.
8. **Reset the blinds.** Raise or open them only after the slats are dry. → *Expect:* slats stack cleanly without trapping moisture.

## Decision points

- Real wood blinds → use barely damp cloths and dry immediately.
- Fabric blinds → vacuum only unless the maker allows spot cleaning.
- Greasy kitchen blinds → add a drop of dish soap to the sock water, then rinse.
- Bent metal slat → straighten gently once; repeated bending creases it.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dust streaks into mud:** cloth was too wet → dry-dust first, then clean with less water.
- **F2 Slat bends:** support it from underneath → reshape slowly with flat fingers.
- **F3 Cord gets wet or soapy:** blot and leave blinds lowered until dry → avoid staining or mildew.
- **F4 Blind will not tilt:** stop forcing the wand → check for tangled cords or jammed slats.

## Verification

With blinds lowered and tilted both ways, slats show no dust lines, sticky spots, drips, or damp stacking.

## Variations

- Vertical blinds: wipe each vane from top clip to bottom weight while supporting the clip.
- Mini blinds: the sock trick is faster than individual cloth folds.
- Allergy cleaning: vacuum the sill and floor after dusting.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use a stable stool, keep cords away from children, and close street-facing blinds before cleaning private rooms.
