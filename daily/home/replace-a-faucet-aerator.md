## Goal

A clogged or damaged faucet aerator is removed, cleaned or replaced, and refitted so the faucet has an even splash-free stream.

## Preconditions

- You have a towel, bowl, old toothbrush, vinegar, replacement aerator if needed, rubber jar gripper, and pliers.
- You can close the drain or cover it so small parts cannot fall in.
- Turn the faucet off before removing the aerator.
- Call a plumber if the faucet spout is cracked, threads are stripped, or water leaks from inside the faucet body.

## Steps

1. **Cover the drain.** Close the stopper or place a towel over the drain opening. → *Expect:* washers and screens cannot disappear.
2. **Try hand removal.** Grip the aerator with a rubber pad and turn it counterclockwise from below. → *Expect:* the aerator loosens without tool marks.
3. **Use padded pliers if stuck.** Wrap the aerator with cloth and turn gently with pliers. → *Expect:* the metal ring moves without scratching the finish.
4. **Keep the parts in order.** Lay out the shell, washer, screen, and flow restrictor in removal order. → *Expect:* reassembly order is clear.
5. **Clean reusable parts.** Soak mineral-crusted parts in vinegar for 20 minutes, then brush the screen and rinse. → *Expect:* holes are open and debris is gone.
6. **Match a replacement.** [BRANCH: male threads | female threads] choose the same diameter and thread direction as the old aerator. → *Expect:* the new aerator starts by hand.
7. **Reinstall by hand.** Seat the washer, thread the aerator clockwise, and stop when snug. → *Expect:* the aerator sits square to the spout.
8. **Flow test.** Turn on cold water slowly, then hot water. → *Expect:* the stream is even with no spray at the threads.
9. **Tighten only if needed.** If the threads seep, add a small padded-plier snug. → *Expect:* seepage stops without crushing the washer.

## Decision points

- Aerator will not unscrew → add vinegar around the joint and wait before trying again.
- Threads start crooked → remove and restart by hand to prevent cross-threading.
- Flow remains weak with aerator removed → the restriction is upstream and may need faucet or supply-line service.
- Aerator is hidden inside the spout → use the maker's removal key.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Finish scratched:** pliers mark the ring, stop and add thicker cloth or replace the aerator.
- **F2 Washer missing:** water sprays around threads, install the correct rubber washer.
- **F3 Cross-threading:** aerator tilts or binds, remove it and restart gently by hand.
- **F4 Debris returns:** flow clogs again quickly, flush the faucet briefly with the aerator removed.

## Verification

With the aerator installed, hot and cold water run in a centered stream and no water beads around the aerator threads.

## Variations

- Pull-down faucets: check the spray head manual because the aerator may be inside the nozzle.
- Cache aerators: use the plastic key that matches the notches.
- Hard-water homes: periodic vinegar soaking may be enough without replacement.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: small parts are easy to lose and faucet finishes scratch quickly. Cover the drain, use padded tools, and do not force stuck threads.
