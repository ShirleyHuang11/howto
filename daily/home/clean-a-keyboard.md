## Goal

A keyboard is powered off, cleared of crumbs and dust, wiped or keycap-cleaned, and fully dried before reconnecting.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: microfiber cloth, compressed air or hand blower, cotton swabs, 70 percent isopropyl alcohol, keycap puller if keys are removable.
- You can disconnect or power off the keyboard and computer.
- You know whether the keyboard is mechanical, laptop, membrane, or low-profile.

## Steps

1. **Power off and disconnect.** Shut down or unplug the keyboard, and remove batteries if wireless. → *Expect:* no keypresses register while cleaning.
2. **Photograph the layout.** Take a picture before removing any keycaps. → *Expect:* key positions can be restored correctly.
3. **Shake out loose debris.** Hold the keyboard upside down over a trash bin and tap gently. → *Expect:* crumbs and grit fall out without tools.
4. **Blow between keys.** Use short bursts of air at an angle, not straight down. → *Expect:* dust moves out from under keys instead of deeper inside.
5. **Clean key surfaces.** Wipe keys with a cloth lightly dampened with alcohol. → *Expect:* oils lift and no liquid drips between keys.
6. **Remove keycaps only if appropriate.** [BRANCH: removable keycaps | laptop or fragile keys] pull removable caps carefully; wipe around fixed keys with swabs. → *Expect:* access improves without breaking scissor switches.
7. **Treat sticky keys.** Work a barely damp alcohol swab around the sticky key edge, pressing and releasing it repeatedly. → *Expect:* key movement improves without flooding the switch.
8. **Dry before reconnecting.** Let everything air-dry fully, reinstall keycaps from the photo, then reconnect power. → *Expect:* keys are dry, seated, and responsive.

## Decision points

- Liquid spill just happened → disconnect immediately and dry; do not test keys while wet.
- Laptop keyboard → avoid keycap removal unless the maker documents it.
- Mechanical keyboard with removable caps → wash caps separately and dry overnight.
- Battery corrosion → stop and handle as corrosion cleanup, not routine keyboard cleaning.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Keycap will not snap back:** compare stabilizers and orientation to neighboring keys → reseat gently without forcing.
- **F2 Key repeats or misses after cleaning:** it may still be damp → disconnect and dry longer.
- **F3 Air blows debris under another key:** turn the keyboard and blow from a different angle → shake again.
- **F4 Legend fades or surface gets tacky:** cleaner is too harsh → stop and use water-damp microfiber only.

## Verification

The keyboard is dry, debris-free, all removed keycaps are back in place, and every commonly used key registers normally after reconnecting.

## Variations

- White keyboards: clean more often with less liquid to avoid gray grime in texture.
- Shared keyboards: wipe high-touch keys with alcohol after debris cleaning.
- External laptop-style keyboards: treat keycaps as fragile unless the model confirms removal.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep liquids away from ports, do not inhale compressed-air propellant, and close private documents before testing keys.
