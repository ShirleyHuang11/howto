## Goal

A stuck or separated zipper moves again without torn fabric, bent teeth, or a slider mangled beyond repair.

## Preconditions

- The garment, bag, or boot can be laid flat or held steady.
- A pencil, candle wax, bar soap, or zipper lubricant is available.
- Small pliers are available for slider adjustment if the zipper is separated.

## Steps

1. **Stop pulling hard.** Hold the fabric near the slider and take tension off the zipper. [BRANCH: jammed fabric | dry teeth | separated teeth] identify the visible problem before adding force. → *Expect:* the slider stops worsening the snag.
2. **Free trapped fabric.** If cloth is caught, pull the fabric gently backward while nudging the slider the opposite direction. → *Expect:* the cloth loosens without a new tear.
3. **Lubricate the teeth.** Rub graphite from a pencil, candle wax, or dry bar soap along both sides of the teeth near the slider. → *Expect:* the teeth look lightly coated, not greasy.
4. **Work the slider gently.** Move it a few millimeters down, then up, repeating small motions instead of one heroic yank. → *Expect:* travel increases a little each pass.
5. **Realign the slider.** If teeth separate behind the slider, unzip to the bottom, reseat both sides evenly, and zip up while keeping the tape straight. → *Expect:* the teeth interlock behind the slider.
6. **Tighten a loose slider.** For a metal slider that closes but leaves teeth open, squeeze the back plates very slightly with pliers. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* too much pressure can crack or freeze the slider, so make tiny adjustments and test between squeezes. → *Expect:* the slider grips the teeth more firmly.
7. **Clean excess lubricant.** Wipe wax or soap from nearby fabric with a dry cloth. → *Expect:* the zipper moves while the garment looks presentable.
8. **Run a full test.** Zip from bottom to top and back once at normal speed. → *Expect:* smooth motion with no skipped or split section.

## Decision points

- Plastic tooth is missing → lubrication will not fix it; replace the zipper or use a repair kit.
- Slider pull tab broke → thread a key ring or cord as a temporary pull.
- The item is expensive leather or formalwear → stop before pliers and use a tailor.
- Salt or dirt is visible → brush and wipe the zipper before lubricating.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fabric re-snags:** detect by cloth entering the slider again → flatten the fabric path and zip while pulling the cloth away.
- **F2 Teeth keep splitting:** detect by a clean opening behind the slider → tighten the slider slightly or replace it.
- **F3 Lubricant stains:** detect by dark marks near the zipper → switch to graphite or dry soap and blot the mark.
- **F4 Slider locks solid:** detect by no motion after lubrication → stop pulling and replace the slider.

## Verification

The zipper travels its full useful length twice and the teeth remain interlocked after the slider passes.

## Variations

- Outdoor gear: rinse grit from the zipper and dry it before wax.
- Luggage: empty the bag first so packed contents do not press sideways on the teeth.
- Boots: support the ankle fabric so the slider is not fighting a curve.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep pliers away from skin and delicate fabric, and do not force a zipper on someone while they are wearing it unless they can say stop immediately.
