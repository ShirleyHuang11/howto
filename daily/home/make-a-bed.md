## Goal

The bed is neatly made — sheets taut, duvet/blanket squared, pillows plumped — ready to be pleasant to get into tonight.

## Preconditions

- The bed is empty of people, laptops, and laundry.
- Bedding present: fitted (or flat) bottom sheet, duvet/comforter or top sheet + blanket, pillows.

## Steps

1. **Strip back and air for a moment.** Throw the duvet fully back over the footboard; let the sheet breathe while you do step 2. → *Expect:* bottom sheet exposed end to end.
2. **Re-tension the bottom sheet.** Pull each corner of the fitted sheet back over its mattress corner; smooth wrinkles outward from the center with flat palms. → *Expect:* drum-tight surface, corners seated, no ripples.
3. **Square the top layer.** Spread the duvet/top sheet evenly: equal overhang left and right, top edge where you like it (at the headboard, or folded back a hand's width). → *Expect:* symmetric overhangs; label end at the foot.
4. **Smooth the top layer from center outward.** Long sweeping strokes toward the edges and foot. → *Expect:* flat surface, no trapped lumps of last night.
5. **Plump and place the pillows.** Grab each pillow at both ends, compress-and-release a few times, drop it square against the headboard, open ends outward. → *Expect:* pillows full-looking and aligned, not deflated pancakes.
6. **Final square-up.** Step to the foot of the bed and check symmetry; tug where uneven; fold the throw/blanket across the foot if used. → *Expect:* even lines from the one viewing angle that shows everything.

## Decision points

- Duvet shifted inside its cover (all the filling in one corner) → fix now or it bugs you all week: grip the inside corners through the cover and shake it square, or re-seat with the corner ties.
- Sheet-change day (roughly weekly, or twice-monthly minimum) → this recipe becomes: strip all layers, fresh set on, old set to `daily/home/do-the-laundry`.
- Shared bed, one side occupied by a sleeper → make your half; diplomacy over symmetry.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Fitted sheet pops off a corner repeatedly:** it's on rotated (long side on the short edge) — check the tag (usually marks a corner/side), or note which corner has the seam shape; sheet straps solve chronic cases.
- **F2 Everything's smooth but it looks lumpy:** the flaw is a layer down — strip to the offender (bunched mattress protector, twisted duvet) and rebuild from there; you can't smooth over a lower-layer lump.
- **F3 Pillow stays flat after plumping:** it's done — pillows have a lifespan (1–2 years); fold-test: a pillow that stays folded is dead.

## Verification

From the foot of the bed: bottom sheet invisible and tight, top layer symmetric with smooth surface, pillows plump and square — and tonight-you will find no crumbs or twisted layers inside.

## Variations

- Hospital corners (flat top sheet): tuck the foot, fold each side's hanging sheet up at 45°, tuck the drape, drop and tuck the triangle — crisp hotel edges.
- Hot climates / minimalist: fold the duvet into thirds at the foot; the made state is "folded and squared", not "spread".

## Safety & privacy

Essentially risk-free; the only caution is lifting mattress corners with your legs, not your lower back, on heavy mattresses.
