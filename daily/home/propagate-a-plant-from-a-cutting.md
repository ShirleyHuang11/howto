## Goal

A healthy plant cutting is taken below a node, rooted in water or soil, monitored for weeks, and potted on after roots are ready.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: clean scissors or pruners, small jar or nursery pot, water or moist potting mix, optional rooting hormone, label.
- Parent plant is healthy, pest-free, and actively growing.
- You know what a node looks like: the bump or joint where a leaf or aerial root emerges.

## Steps

1. **Choose a healthy stem.** Pick non-flowering growth with at least two nodes and several leaves. → *Expect:* cutting material is firm, green, and pest-free.
2. **Cut below a node.** Make a clean cut 1-2 cm below the node using sanitized scissors. → *Expect:* the cutting has a node near the base where roots can form.
3. **Remove lower leaves.** Strip leaves that would sit underwater or under soil. → *Expect:* bare lower stem and leaves only above the rooting zone.
4. **Choose the rooting method.** [BRANCH: water | soil] put the node in clean water for easy viewing, or into moist mix for fewer transition problems. → *Expect:* the node is covered by the rooting medium.
5. **Place in bright indirect light.** Keep it warm and out of harsh sun. → *Expect:* leaves stay firm without scorching.
6. **Maintain moisture and cleanliness.** Change water every few days, or keep soil lightly moist but not soggy. → *Expect:* stem base stays fresh, not slimy.
7. **Wait for roots.** Check weekly and resist tugging soil cuttings. → *Expect:* roots appear in 2-8 weeks depending on plant and season.
8. **Pot on when roots are ready.** Move water cuttings when roots are 3-5 cm long, or soil cuttings when new growth appears. → *Expect:* cutting enters a small draining pot and remains upright.

## Decision points

- Cutting wilts immediately → reduce leaf area or cover loosely for humidity.
- Stem is succulent or cactus → let the cut callus before soil rooting.
- Root nodes are aerial roots, as with pothos or monstera → include at least one in the medium.
- Woody herbs like rosemary → soil plus humidity usually works better than water.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stem turns black or mushy:** rot has started → cut above the rot and restart with clean tools if a healthy node remains.
- **F2 Leaves yellow in water:** water is stale or light is too low → change water and move brighter.
- **F3 No roots after weeks:** season or plant type may be slow → refresh the cut and try soil or rooting hormone.
- **F4 Cutting collapses after potting:** transition shock → keep soil evenly moist and light indirect for a week.

## Verification

The cutting has healthy roots or new growth, is potted in a small draining container, and shows firm leaves after several days.

## Variations

- Pothos and philodendron: water rooting is simple and visible.
- Snake plant: leaf sections must keep their original up direction.
- Basil and mint: pinch tips after rooting to encourage branching.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Some houseplants have irritating or toxic sap, so wash hands, keep cuttings away from pets, and ask permission before taking a cutting from someone else's plant.
