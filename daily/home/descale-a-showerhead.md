## Goal

Mineral scale is loosened from a showerhead so water sprays evenly through the jets again.

## Preconditions

- You have white vinegar, a plastic bag, rubber band or twist tie, old toothbrush, cloth, and access to the shower.
- The showerhead finish tolerates vinegar; check the maker guidance for brass, nickel, stone, or specialty finishes.
- The shower can be left unused during the soak.
- The bathroom has ventilation if vinegar odor bothers you.

## Steps

1. **Check the spray pattern.** Run the shower briefly and note blocked jets or sideways spray. → *Expect:* the clogged areas are identified before cleaning.
2. **Fill a bag with vinegar.** Add enough white vinegar to cover the face of the showerhead without overflowing. → *Expect:* the bag can surround the spray plate with liquid over the jets.
3. **Tie the bag over the head.** Lift the bag around the showerhead and secure it tightly with a rubber band or twist tie. → *Expect:* the jet face is submerged and the bag hangs without slipping.
4. **Soak the scale.** Leave it for 30 to 60 minutes, shorter for delicate finishes. [BRANCH: chrome or plastic | delicate finish] → *Expect:* white crust softens and the vinegar may turn cloudy.
5. **Remove and brush.** Take the bag off, dip the toothbrush in vinegar, and scrub each jet face gently. → *Expect:* loosened flakes come away without scratching the finish.
6. **Work stubborn jets.** Rub rubber nozzles with a thumb or brush across mineral plugs. → *Expect:* blocked holes open visibly.
7. **Flush hot water.** Run the shower on warm or hot for 1 to 2 minutes while standing clear of spray. → *Expect:* grit and vinegar rinse out, then water runs clear.
8. **Wipe dry.** Dry the fixture and surrounding wall so vinegar does not sit on metal or stone. → *Expect:* the showerhead is clean, dry, and free of vinegar smell.

## Decision points

- If the showerhead is removable, unscrew it and soak only the head in a bowl for better contact.
- If vinegar is unsafe for the finish, use the manufacturer-approved descaler.
- If spray is weak from every hole, check the flow restrictor or household pressure instead of scrubbing longer.
- If scale returns quickly, plan a monthly wipe-down or install a water softening solution.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bag leaks:** detect dripping vinegar, recover by reducing liquid and resecuring above the head.
- **F2 Finish dulls:** detect discoloration or haze, recover by stopping, rinsing immediately, and using mild soap only.
- **F3 Jets still blocked:** detect sideways streams after flushing, recover by repeating a shorter soak or removing the head.
- **F4 Showerhead loosens:** detect wobble at the arm, recover by hand-tightening gently and checking for leaks.
- **F5 Vinegar smell remains:** detect odor after drying, recover by flushing longer with warm water and ventilating.

## Verification

After flushing, water exits most jets in a straight even pattern, with no vinegar dripping from the fixture and no new leak at the shower arm.

## Variations

- Rain showerheads: support the bag from above so its weight does not strain the arm.
- Handheld heads: lay the head face-down in a bowl instead of tying a bag.
- Heavy limescale: repeat short soaks rather than leaving acid overnight on unknown finishes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid mixing vinegar with bleach or other cleaners, protect sensitive finishes, and stand aside when flushing loosened grit.
