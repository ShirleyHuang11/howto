## Goal

Use a portable fire extinguisher on a small fire only when it is safe to attempt.

## Preconditions

- The fire is small, contained, and not producing heavy smoke.
- You have a clear exit behind you and can leave immediately.
- The extinguisher is charged and labeled for the fire class.

## Steps

1. **Decide whether to fight the fire.** [BRANCH: small and contained | growing, smoky, or blocking exit] fight only the first case. → *Expect:* you have chosen evacuation if conditions are unsafe.
2. **Call or assign a call before using the extinguisher.** Tell someone to call the fire service, or call yourself if alone and leaving. → *Expect:* emergency help is on the way if the fire is not instantly controlled.
3. **Confirm the extinguisher class.** Use ABC for most home fires, B for flammable liquids, C for energized electrical equipment, and never use a water extinguisher on grease or live electrical equipment. → *Expect:* the label matches the burning material.
4. **Stand with the exit behind you.** Keep 6 to 8 feet of distance if the extinguisher label allows it. → *Expect:* your path out is clear and the fire is in front of you.
5. **Pull the pin.** Hold the extinguisher upright and break the tamper seal. → *Expect:* the handle can now be squeezed.
6. **Aim at the base.** Point the nozzle or hose where the fuel is burning, not at the top of the flames. → *Expect:* the discharge path targets the source of the fire.
7. **Squeeze the handle.** Discharge a short burst, then continue if the agent is reaching the base. → *Expect:* extinguishing agent flows steadily.
8. **Sweep side to side.** Move across the base until flames are out. → *Expect:* flames shrink and disappear without pushing fire toward you.
9. **Back away and watch.** Do not turn your back on the area until you are clear. → *Expect:* the fire stays out or you leave if it restarts.

## Decision points

- Fire is above shoulder height, spreading, or in a wall or ceiling → evacuate and call the fire service.
- Extinguisher label does not match the fire → do not use it; evacuate and call.
- Smoke reaches face level or visibility drops → leave immediately.
- You are not confident within a few seconds → close the door if safe and evacuate.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong extinguisher class:** detect label mismatch or worsening fire → stop, evacuate, and call the fire service.
- **F2 No discharge:** detect no agent when squeezing → leave and use another extinguisher only if it is between you and the exit.
- **F3 Fire re-ignites:** detect renewed flames after discharge → repeat once if safe, otherwise evacuate.
- **F4 Exit compromised:** detect smoke or flame behind you → leave by the nearest safe route and warn others.

## Verification

The fire is out, the area is watched for re-ignition from a safe exit path, and the fire service is called for any fire that spread, produced heavy smoke, or required an extinguisher.

## Variations

- Kitchen grease: use a Class B, ABC, or Class K extinguisher as labeled; a lid is often safer for a small pan fire.
- Electrical equipment: cut power if safe before extinguishing, but do not delay evacuation.
- Shared building: pull the alarm if the fire may affect other units.

## Safety & privacy

High risk from smoke inhalation, burns, and choosing the wrong agent. A portable extinguisher is for escape and very small fires, not for firefighting in a room filling with smoke.
