## Goal

Old wallpaper is removed without gouging the wall, adhesive residue is washed away, and the surface is left dry for painting or new paper.

## Preconditions

- Power to nearby outlets is protected, covers are removed only if you can do so safely, and floors are covered.
- You have a scorer, steamer or soak solution, scraper, sponge, bucket, towels, and gloves.
- You have checked a hidden area for loose plaster or delicate drywall paper.

## Steps

1. **Protect the room.** Move furniture, tape plastic at the base, and cover floors with towels or drop cloths. → *Expect:* water and paste scrapings have a landing zone.
2. **Score the surface lightly.** Perforate washable or painted paper without digging into the wall. → *Expect:* moisture can reach adhesive through small holes.
3. **Steam or soak a test patch.** [BRANCH: steamer | soak solution] warm the paper until adhesive softens, keeping water away from outlets. → *Expect:* a corner lifts with moderate pressure.
4. **Scrape at a shallow angle.** Slide the scraper under softened paper, working in strips. → *Expect:* paper releases while the wall face stays intact.
5. **Repeat in small sections.** Wet only what you can strip before it cools or dries. → *Expect:* progress stays controlled and the wall is not saturated.
6. **Wash adhesive residue.** Scrub remaining paste with warm water and a sponge, changing water often. → *Expect:* the wall no longer feels slick or slimy.
7. **Let the wall dry fully.** Ventilate the room and wait before sanding, painting, or repapering. → *Expect:* the wall is dry, dull, and firm.

## Decision points

- Wall face starts tearing → stop soaking that area and scrape less aggressively.
- Layers are multiple or painted over → score more carefully and expect several passes.
- Mold or damp staining appears → identify the moisture source before covering the wall again.
- Adhesive will not wash off → use a wallpaper paste remover and rinse afterward.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Gouged wall:** detect scraper dents, recover with filler after the wall dries and sand smooth.
- **F2 Drywall paper lifts:** detect fuzzy brown paper, recover by sealing the area with primer before finishing.
- **F3 Steamer drips into outlet:** detect moisture near electrics, recover by stopping, drying the area, and keeping power off until safe.
- **F4 Paste residue ruins paint:** detect tacky wall after drying, recover by rewashing and priming before paint.

## Verification

No wallpaper or slick paste residue remains, and the wall is dry and firm before any new finish is applied.

## Variations

- `rental-home`: confirm permission before stripping finishes.
- `plaster-wall`: tolerates moisture better than drywall but old skim coats can still loosen.
- `peelable-paper`: top vinyl layer may peel dry, leaving backing that still needs soaking.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from steam burns, blades, wet floors, and water near outlets. Work cold near electrics and keep the scraper angle shallow.
