## Goal

A cabinet knob or pull is removed and replaced so the new hardware sits straight, secure, and aligned with the existing hole spacing.

## Preconditions

- New knob or pull with matching screws.
- Screwdriver that fits the screw head.
- Tape measure for pulls with two holes.

## Steps

1. **Measure before buying or installing.** For a pull, measure center-to-center distance between the two screw holes. → *Expect:* new hardware matches the existing hole spacing.
2. **Open the cabinet.** Hold the old knob or pull with one hand and access the screw from inside. → *Expect:* screw head is visible and reachable.
3. **Remove old hardware.** Turn the screw counterclockwise and keep the screw if it may fit the new piece. → *Expect:* old knob comes away without tearing the finish.
4. **Clean the contact spot.** Wipe grime or compressed dust around the hole. → *Expect:* surface under the old hardware looks clean and dry.
5. **Install the new knob.** Insert the screw from inside and thread the knob or pull by hand first. → *Expect:* screw catches without cross-threading.
6. **Align and tighten.** Square the knob or pull, then tighten by hand plus a small nudge with the screwdriver. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overtightening can crush the cabinet face or strip the hardware threads, so stop when it no longer twists. → *Expect:* hardware is firm and straight.
7. **Check all matching doors.** Compare height and angle with nearby knobs or pulls. → *Expect:* the replacement visually lines up with the set.

## Decision points

- Pull holes do not match → choose different hardware or use a backplate, rather than drilling casually.
- Screw is too long → add a washer inside or use the shorter screw supplied.
- Screw is too short → use a longer machine screw with the same thread.
- Finish has a faded outline → choose hardware with a larger base or clean the whole door.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Cross-threading:** detect rough resistance in the first turns; recover by backing out and starting straight by hand.
- **F2 Loose knob:** detect spinning after tightening; recover with the correct screw length or a small washer.
- **F3 Crooked pull:** detect one side higher; recover by loosening, aligning, and retightening evenly.
- **F4 Finish chip:** detect flaking around the hole; recover by stopping drilling and covering with a backplate if needed.

## Verification

The new knob or pull is straight, does not rotate under normal hand pressure, and matches the existing cabinet hole spacing without new unwanted holes.

## Variations

- Single knob: hole spacing is not an issue, but screw length still matters.
- Two-hole pull: use the exact center-to-center measurement, commonly listed in millimeters.
- New cabinet door: use a hardware jig before drilling any fresh holes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hand tools and sharp hardware edges. Protect cabinet finishes from screwdriver slips and do not drill new holes unless placement is confirmed.
