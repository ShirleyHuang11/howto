## Goal

A dresser, bookcase, TV stand, or similar furniture is secured to wall studs with anti-tip hardware so it cannot tip forward easily.

## Preconditions

- You have the furniture maker's anti-tip kit or a rated strap kit, stud finder, drill, screwdriver, pencil, and measuring tape.
- The furniture is assembled, placed on a stable floor, and unloaded enough to move safely.
- Tip-over risk is serious for children; if the wall cannot accept anchors or the furniture is unstable, use a professional installer.

## Steps

1. **Empty and stabilize.** Remove heavy loose items, close drawers, and push the furniture near its final position. → *Expect:* it can be moved without tipping.
2. **Read the kit rating.** Confirm the strap or bracket is intended for furniture anti-tip use, not light picture hanging. → *Expect:* hardware is suitable for the furniture size.
3. **Find a stud.** Use a stud finder behind the furniture and mark the stud center above the furniture top or through the back gap. → *Expect:* a stud location is clearly marked.
4. **Choose attachment points.** [BRANCH: strap kit | L-bracket] straps usually connect high on the furniture; brackets connect the furniture back or top rail to the wall. → *Expect:* the anchor point is high and solid.
5. **Mark holes.** Hold the wall bracket on the stud mark and the furniture bracket on a solid rail, not thin backer board. → *Expect:* screw marks are aligned with strong material.
6. **Drill pilots.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm the furniture location and avoid hidden wiring or plumbing before drilling. → *Expect:* pilot holes match the screw size.
7. **Fasten to the wall stud.** Drive the wall screws into the stud, not just drywall. → *Expect:* the bracket or strap mount cannot pull out by hand.
8. **Fasten to furniture.** Attach the furniture-side hardware into a solid rail or manufacturer-marked point. → *Expect:* screws bite firmly without splitting.
9. **Set strap tension.** Move the furniture back and tighten the strap so it is snug but not bowing the furniture. → *Expect:* the furniture sits flush and restrained.
10. **Check the tip resistance.** Pull gently from the top front while another adult steadies the piece. → *Expect:* the anchor prevents forward tip movement.

## Decision points

- No stud behind the unit → move the furniture, use a cross rail spanning studs, or use a manufacturer-approved heavy-duty wall anchor.
- Furniture back is thin hardboard → attach to a solid top rail or side rail instead.
- Tall furniture on carpet wobbles → level it before anchoring.
- Child-accessible drawers remain climbable → keep heavy items low and secure drawers if needed.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Screw misses stud:** screw spins or pulls out, relocate to the stud center and patch the bad hole later.
- **F2 Strap too loose:** furniture moves forward several inches, shorten or retension the strap.
- **F3 Furniture screw strips:** screw will not tighten, move to a stronger rail or use the maker's specified fastener.
- **F4 Wall material crumbles:** anchor area fails, stop and get a stronger anchoring method or installer.

## Verification

With drawers closed and shelves loaded normally, the furniture cannot tip forward during a gentle top-front pull because the anti-tip strap or bracket holds it to the wall.

## Variations

- Bookcases: anchor near the top and use more than one strap for wide units.
- Dressers: install drawer stops or keep heavy items in lower drawers.
- Masonry walls: use masonry-rated anchors and bits instead of drywall screws.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: furniture tip-over can seriously injure children and adults. Anchor to studs whenever possible, keep children away during installation, and never rely on drywall alone for tall or heavy furniture.
