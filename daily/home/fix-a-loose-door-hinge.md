## Goal

A loose door hinge is tightened or anchored so the door swings cleanly and the hinge no longer pulls from the jamb or door edge.

## Preconditions

- You have a screwdriver, drill driver if needed, 3 inch wood screws, toothpicks or hardwood slivers, wood glue, utility knife, and painter's tape.
- The door is supported by at least one other hinge.
- Do not remove all hinge screws at once unless the door is blocked or supported.
- Call a carpenter if the jamb is split, the door frame moves, or the door is too heavy to support safely.

## Steps

1. **Find the loose side.** Open the door halfway and watch whether the hinge leaf moves on the jamb or door. → *Expect:* the loose screw locations are identified.
2. **Tighten intact screws.** Drive each loose screw clockwise by hand or with a low-torque driver. → *Expect:* screws pull the hinge leaf flat.
3. **Test the swing.** Open and close the door slowly. → *Expect:* the latch side no longer drops or rubs.
4. **Replace a jamb screw.** If a top hinge screw spins, remove one jamb screw and install a 3 inch screw angled slightly toward the framing stud. → *Expect:* the screw bites firmly and pulls the hinge tight.
5. **Patch a stripped hole.** [BRANCH: toothpick-glue | longer screw] fill a stripped door-edge hole with glue-coated toothpicks or use a longer same-diameter screw where solid wood is available. → *Expect:* the screw tightens without spinning.
6. **Trim the patch.** Let glue set enough to hold, cut toothpicks flush, and reinstall the screw. → *Expect:* the hinge leaf sits flat over the repair.
7. **Check hinge alignment.** Make sure hinge barrels form a straight vertical line and screws are seated flat. → *Expect:* the hinge pin is not binding.
8. **Tighten remaining hinges.** Repeat only for screws that are loose, one at a time. → *Expect:* the door stays supported throughout.
9. **Final swing test.** Open, close, and latch the door several times. → *Expect:* the door swings freely and latches without lifting.

## Decision points

- Screw tightens and holds → no filler is needed.
- Screw spins in place → use the toothpick-glue repair or a longer screw.
- Door still sags after the top hinge is secured → inspect lower hinges and jamb movement.
- Jamb is cracked or separated from wall framing → call a carpenter.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Screw strips head:** driver slips, switch to a hand screwdriver or replace the screw.
- **F2 Hinge binds:** door squeaks or resists after tightening, loosen slightly and realign the leaf.
- **F3 Repair pulls out:** patched hole fails immediately, drill and plug with a hardwood dowel or call a pro.
- **F4 Door drops:** hinge support is inadequate, block the door and stop removing screws.

## Verification

The hinge leaves stay flush, no screw spins, and the door opens, closes, and latches three times without rubbing caused by hinge movement.

## Variations

- Hollow-core doors: use the toothpick repair only where there is wood at the hinge stile.
- Exterior doors: use corrosion-resistant screws and maintain weatherstrip alignment.
- Heavy solid doors: support the door with shims before removing any hinge screw.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: doors can pinch fingers or fall if unsupported. Work one screw at a time, keep hands clear of hinge gaps, and get help for heavy doors.
