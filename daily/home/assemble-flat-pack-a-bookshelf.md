## Goal

The flat-pack bookshelf is assembled square, shelves are secure, and any tall unit is anchored to prevent tip-over.

## Preconditions

- You have clear floor space, the full kit, instructions, required tools, and the wall anchor hardware for tall units.
- A second person is available for tall or heavy panels.
- You know the wall type for anchoring, such as stud, masonry, or hollow drywall.

## Steps

1. **Inventory every part.** Lay panels, cams, dowels, screws, shelf pins, back panel, and anchors against the parts list. → *Expect:* no part count is short before assembly begins.
2. **Read the instructions fully.** Identify panel orientation, finished edges, and steps that require two people. → *Expect:* front, back, top, and bottom are clear.
3. **Start cam-lock hardware correctly.** Insert dowels and cam bolts straight, then align cams with their openings facing the bolt path. → *Expect:* panels draw together when cams turn.
4. **Build the frame loosely.** Join side, top, bottom, and fixed shelves before final tightening. [BRANCH: cam locks | screws] snug cams by rotation, snug screws by hand pressure. → *Expect:* the frame stands in the intended shape.
5. **Square with the back panel.** Measure diagonals or align corners before fastening the back panel. → *Expect:* the diagonals match and shelves sit parallel.
6. **Fasten the back panel.** Nail, screw, or slide the panel as instructed, keeping edges flush. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm the unit is square before driving nails or staples. → *Expect:* the frame becomes rigid and square.
7. **Install shelves and caps.** Add adjustable shelf pins at matching heights and press covers into exposed holes. → *Expect:* shelves sit level and do not rock.
8. **Anchor tall units.** Attach the anti-tip bracket to the unit and a stud, masonry anchor, or rated hollow-wall anchor. → *Expect:* the top cannot be pulled forward by hand.

## Decision points

- A panel is reversed → stop at once, loosen hardware, and correct orientation before locking more cams.
- Cam spins without tightening → the bolt is not seated or the cam opening is facing the wrong way.
- Back panel does not fit squarely → adjust the frame before fastening it.
- Wall anchor hardware does not match the wall → buy the correct anchor before loading the shelf.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shelf leans:** detect unequal diagonals or slanted shelves, recover by loosening the frame and refastening the back square.
- **F2 Cam blows out the panel:** detect crumbling around a cam hole, recover with manufacturer replacement parts or furniture repair blocks.
- **F3 Unit tips forward:** detect movement when pulled at the top, recover by unloading it and installing a proper anti-tip anchor immediately.
- **F4 Missing hardware:** detect a short part count, recover by pausing and requesting replacements rather than substituting random screws.

## Verification

The bookshelf is square, shelves sit on matched supports, and any tall unit is anchored so the top cannot tip forward under a firm hand pull.

## Variations

- `masonry-wall`: use masonry bits and anchors rated for the load.
- `rental-home`: ask permission for wall anchors, but do not use an unanchored tall bookshelf as a substitute.
- `wide-unit`: assemble near its final location because moving it can rack the frame.

## Safety & privacy

High risk because tall furniture can tip over and injure people. Anchor tall units before loading them, keep children away during assembly, and do not climb the shelves.
