## Goal

A toilet tank fills to the correct line, flushes cleanly, and stops running after the float is adjusted.

## Preconditions

- You can remove the tank lid and reach the fill valve.
- You have a towel, flashlight, and screwdriver if the float uses an adjustment screw.
- Know where the toilet shutoff valve is before starting.
- Call a plumber if the shutoff valve leaks, the fill valve is cracked, or the tank has visible cracks.

## Steps

1. **Remove the tank lid.** Lift it with two hands and place it flat on a towel away from the toilet. → *Expect:* the float, fill valve, overflow tube, and water line are visible.
2. **Find the fill level mark.** Look for a molded water line on the tank wall or a mark on the overflow tube. → *Expect:* the target level is identified.
3. **Compare the current level.** Let the tank finish filling and see whether water is below the mark, at the mark, or flowing into the overflow tube. → *Expect:* you know whether the float must move up or down.
4. **Identify the float type.** [BRANCH: ball float | cup float] a ball float sits on a metal arm; a cup float rides around the fill valve shaft. → *Expect:* you know which adjustment method fits.
5. **Lower a high ball float.** Bend the metal arm slightly downward, moving slowly so the ball sits lower. → *Expect:* the fill valve shuts off before water enters the overflow tube.
6. **Raise a low ball float.** Bend the arm slightly upward if the flush is weak from low tank water. → *Expect:* the tank fills closer to the marked line.
7. **Adjust a cup float.** Turn the screw or squeeze the release clip, then slide the float down to stop running or up for a stronger flush. → *Expect:* the float locks at the new height.
8. **Flush and refill.** Flush once, watch the refill, and listen after the fill valve shuts off. → *Expect:* water stops at the mark with no hiss or trickle.
9. **Fine tune once.** Make a small second adjustment only if the level is still more than 1/2 inch off. → *Expect:* the final level is stable.

## Decision points

- Water runs into the overflow tube → lower the float until the valve shuts off below the tube rim.
- Bowl flush is weak and no clog is present → raise the float to the fill mark without exceeding it.
- Float adjustment changes nothing → the fill valve may be worn and should be replaced.
- Shutoff valve drips when touched → stop and call a plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Water keeps running:** the fill valve hisses after float adjustment, lower the float again or replace the fill valve.
- **F2 Float rubs the tank:** the float sticks against the wall, rotate or reposition the fill valve if possible.
- **F3 Weak flush remains:** the level is correct but bowl action is poor, check the flapper, chain, or clog.
- **F4 Arm breaks:** an old ball-float arm cracks, turn water off and replace the fill valve assembly.

## Verification

After two flushes, tank water stops at the fill mark or about 1 inch below the overflow tube, and the toilet is silent for five minutes.

## Variations

- Newer fill valves: adjustment may be a small plastic screw on top of the float cup.
- Older brass ballcocks: replacement is usually better than forcing corroded parts.
- Dual-flush toilets: adjust the float only after confirming both flush buttons release normally.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: avoid cracking the tank lid or stressing brittle plastic. Keep water below the overflow tube and call a plumber if the shutoff, tank, or supply line leaks.
