## Goal

The table stands steady under normal use by correcting a short leg, loose glide, or loose joint.

## Preconditions

- Table is cleared of fragile items and can be moved or flipped safely.
- You have felt pads or shims, screwdriver or hex key, wood glue if needed, clamps, and a level.
- The floor area is the floor where the table will actually live.

## Steps

1. **Find the wobble direction.** Press each corner gently and note which leg lifts or taps. → *Expect:* the short corner or loose area is identified.
2. **Check the floor first.** Move the table a few inches and test again. [BRANCH: wobble follows table | wobble stays with floor] table means repair it, floor means shim for that location. → *Expect:* you know whether the problem is table or floor.
3. **Inspect feet and glides.** Look for missing pads, backed-out glides, or damaged caps. → *Expect:* simple foot faults are visible.
4. **Adjust or shim the short leg.** Turn adjustable glides or add thin felt under the short leg. → *Expect:* the tapping corner touches the floor.
5. **Tighten joints.** Tighten bolts, screws, brackets, or apron fasteners a little at a time. → *Expect:* legs no longer twist by hand.
6. **Re-glue loose wood joints.** Open the joint slightly, add wood glue, clamp square, and wipe squeeze-out. → *Expect:* the joint is clamped in alignment.
7. **Retest under light load.** Set the table upright, place hands near opposite corners, and press normally. → *Expect:* the table stays steady.

## Decision points

- Wobble appears only on one floor spot → use a removable shim or furniture pad, not permanent leg shortening.
- Joint is cracked, not just loose → repair the split before tightening hardware.
- Table has folding locks → confirm the locks are fully engaged before adjusting legs.
- Glue repair is wet → let it cure for the label time before loading the table.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shim falls out:** detect renewed wobble after moving the table, recover with adhesive felt or an adjustable glide.
- **F2 Screw spins freely:** detect no tightening resistance, recover with a larger screw or wood plug repair.
- **F3 Joint opens again:** detect a visible gap after curing, recover by cleaning old glue and clamping with fresh wood glue.
- **F4 Table rocks after leveling:** detect movement under normal pressure, recover by checking diagonal twist and all foot contact.

## Verification

On its normal floor, the table does not rock when each corner is pressed and all joints remain tight under light load.

## Variations

- `metal-frame`: use threadlocker on clean bolts if they repeatedly loosen.
- `antique-table`: avoid permanent cuts or aggressive sanding that reduces value.
- `outdoor-table`: use weather-resistant pads, glides, and glue.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch pinch points when flipping the table, and do not sit or stand on a table that needed joint repair until the repair has cured.
