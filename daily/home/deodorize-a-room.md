## Goal

A room smells neutral because the odor source is removed first, then air, surfaces, and absorbent materials are refreshed.

## Preconditions

- Trash bags, laundry access, and basic cleaner.
- Ventilation from a window, fan, or HVAC.
- Baking soda, activated charcoal, or another unscented odor absorber.

## Steps

1. **Find the source.** Check trash, laundry, drains, pet areas, food, shoes, damp fabrics, and hidden spills. → *Expect:* the strongest smell has an identifiable location or category.
2. **Remove the source.** Take out trash, wash fabric, clean spills, or move damp items to dry. → *Expect:* the room smells better within a few minutes.
3. **Ventilate.** Open windows if outdoor air is acceptable, or run exhaust and a fan. → *Expect:* stale air moves out and fresh air enters.
4. **Clean contact surfaces.** Wipe hard surfaces near the source with appropriate cleaner and dry them. → *Expect:* sticky or dusty residue is gone.
5. **Absorb remaining odor.** Place bowls of baking soda or activated charcoal near the odor zone for several hours. → *Expect:* smell fades without perfume covering it.
6. **Avoid masking only.** Use sprays only after cleaning, and choose light amounts. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* heavy fragrance can soak into fabric and create a second odor problem, so do not saturate upholstery or carpets. → *Expect:* room smells clean rather than perfumed.
7. **Recheck next day.** Close the room for 1 hour, then re-enter and smell near likely sources. → *Expect:* odor does not rebound strongly.

## Decision points

- Musty smell returns → inspect for moisture, leaks, or mold.
- Rotten smell with no visible source → check under furniture, vents, and appliances.
- Chemical or gas smell → leave the area and contact the utility or emergency service.
- Pet odor remains → wash bedding and use an enzymatic cleaner on accidents.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Hidden source:** detect smell returning after ventilation; recover by searching low areas, closets, bins, and soft goods.
- **F2 Damp odor:** detect mustiness and humidity; recover by drying the room and fixing the water source.
- **F3 Perfume overload:** detect strong fragrance but original odor remains; recover by ventilating and cleaning rather than adding more spray.
- **F4 Drain odor:** detect smell strongest near sink or floor drain; recover by cleaning the drain and restoring water in the trap.

## Verification

After the room has been closed for 1 hour, re-entering produces no strong odor source, and the air smells neutral without relying on heavy fragrance.

## Variations

- Bedroom: strip bedding and check under the bed before using absorbers.
- Kitchen: clean trash can rims, sink drain, and refrigerator spill zones.
- Smoke odor: wash fabrics and hard surfaces, then use charcoal and ventilation over several days.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from cleaners and poor air quality. Do not mix cleaning chemicals, and treat gas, sewage, or mold odors as more than deodorizing tasks.
