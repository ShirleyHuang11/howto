## Goal

A grill is burned off, brushed, degreased, emptied, inspected, and lightly seasoned so it is clean enough for the next cook and safer from flare-ups.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: grill brush or scraper, heat-resistant gloves, mild dish soap, bucket, cloths, putty knife or scraper, cooking oil, paper towel.
- Grill is outdoors in a ventilated area and stable.
- For gas grills, the propane tank or gas line can be turned off after burn-off.

## Steps

1. **Heat the grill for burn-off.** Run burners high or let charcoal heat the grates for 10-15 minutes with lid closed. → *Expect:* stuck food turns brittle and ashy.
2. **Brush hot grates safely.** Wearing gloves, brush grates front to back while hot. → *Expect:* carbon flakes off and grate bars look exposed.
3. **Shut down and cool partly.** Turn off gas or close vents, then wait until parts are warm but not dangerously hot. → *Expect:* cleaning can continue without burn risk.
4. **Remove grates and flavorizer parts.** Lift out grates, plates, or charcoal grate according to the grill type. → *Expect:* grease tray and firebox are visible.
5. **Scrape the firebox.** Push loose carbon and grease toward the tray or ash catcher. → *Expect:* heavy buildup is removed from the bottom.
6. **Degrease removable parts.** [BRANCH: greasy grates | lightly soiled grates] wash greasy parts with hot soapy water; wipe light soil with damp cloths. → *Expect:* sticky grease is gone.
7. **Empty the tray or ash catcher.** Remove cool grease liners or fully cold ash and dispose safely. → *Expect:* flare-up fuel is removed.
8. **Dry and season the grates.** Dry parts fully, reinstall, then wipe grates with a thin film of cooking oil. → *Expect:* grates are clean, dry, and lightly protected.

## Decision points

- Wire brush has loose bristles → replace it or use a bristle-free scraper.
- Grease tray is overflowing → clean before the next cook to reduce fire risk.
- Cast iron grates → dry immediately and oil lightly to prevent rust.
- Gas flame is uneven after cleaning → check burner ports for blockage when cool.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Grease fire starts:** turn off gas if safe, close lid, and use a fire extinguisher if it grows → never use water on grease fire.
- **F2 Brush bristle sticks to grate:** remove it before cooking → inspect grates and consider bristle-free tools.
- **F3 Rust appears after washing:** dry with heat and oil lightly → store covered.
- **F4 Grill smells soapy:** rinse removable parts again and heat briefly before cooking → food should not contact residue.

## Verification

The grates are brushed and lightly oiled, grease tray or ash catcher is empty, loose carbon is removed, and no soap, bristles, or heavy grease remain.

## Variations

- Charcoal grill: ash removal matters as much as grate cleaning.
- Gas grill: inspect burner tubes and heat tents while parts are out.
- Public grill: line cooking surfaces with foil or clean extra thoroughly before use.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from heat, grease fires, and sharp grill tools. Clean outdoors, handle ash only when cold, and keep children away from hot metal.
