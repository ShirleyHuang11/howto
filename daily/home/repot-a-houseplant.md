## Goal

A houseplant is moved into a slightly larger draining pot with fresh mix, loosened roots, and recovery conditions that reduce transplant shock.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: new pot with drainage, fresh potting mix suited to the plant, gloves, trowel, scissors or pruners, tray or newspaper.
- The new pot is only one size larger, about 2-5 cm wider than the old pot.
- The plant has been watered recently enough that the root ball is slightly moist, not bone dry.

## Steps

1. **Prepare the new pot.** Add fresh mix to the bottom so the plant will sit at its old soil height. → *Expect:* pot has drainage and a shallow base layer of mix.
2. **Slide the plant out.** Tip the old pot, support the stems at soil level, and squeeze or tap the pot rather than pulling leaves. → *Expect:* root ball comes out mostly intact.
3. **Inspect the roots.** [BRANCH: healthy roots | rotten roots] keep firm pale or tan roots; trim black, mushy, smelly roots with clean scissors. → *Expect:* only sound roots remain.
4. **Loosen the root edge.** Tease circling roots outward and roughen the bottom lightly. → *Expect:* roots point into new soil instead of wrapping the old shape.
5. **Set the plant in the new pot.** Center it and keep the crown at the same height it grew before. → *Expect:* stem base is not buried and root ball is not exposed.
6. **Backfill with fresh mix.** Add mix around the sides, firming lightly with fingers, not packing hard. → *Expect:* plant stands upright with small air pockets closed.
7. **Water to settle.** Water slowly until drainage appears, then empty the saucer after 15-20 minutes. → *Expect:* mix settles around roots and no standing water remains.
8. **Give recovery conditions.** Put the plant in bright indirect light and skip fertilizer for 3-4 weeks. → *Expect:* plant has stable moisture and low stress while roots adjust.

## Decision points

- Roots circle densely around the pot → repot now and loosen more assertively.
- Soil smells sour or roots are mushy → treat as root rot and trim before repotting.
- Plant is flowering or stressed → wait unless the pot is failing or roots are rotting.
- No drainage hole → use it as a cover pot only, with a draining inner pot.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Plant wilts after repotting:** check soil moisture and light → keep evenly moist and out of direct sun while it recovers.
- **F2 Water runs around the root ball:** old soil is hydrophobic → water in two slow passes or bottom-water briefly.
- **F3 Plant sinks too low:** lift it and add mix underneath → buried crowns rot.
- **F4 New pot stays wet for days:** pot is too large or mix too dense → move to a smaller draining pot or airier mix.

## Verification

The plant sits upright at its original soil height, water drains freely, no saucer water remains, and the plant is placed in bright indirect light for recovery.

## Variations

- Succulents: use gritty cactus mix and wait a few days before watering if roots were cut.
- Orchids: use orchid bark and keep the crown exposed.
- Large floor plants: repot on a tarp and have a second person steady the stem.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Wear gloves for irritating sap, clean pruners after diseased roots, and keep loose soil away from pets and drains.
