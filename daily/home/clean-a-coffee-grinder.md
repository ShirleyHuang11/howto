## Goal

Clean coffee oils and grounds from a blade or burr grinder without wetting the motor or leaving cleaner in the grind path.

## Preconditions

- The grinder is unplugged and cool.
- You have a dry brush, microfiber cloth, grinder-cleaning tablets or dry uncooked rice if the manual allows it, and a small vacuum if available.
- The hopper, grounds bin, or removable burr parts can be handled according to the manual.
- No water will be poured into the motor base.

## Steps

1. **Unplug the grinder.** Remove the plug before touching blades, burrs, or the hopper. → *Expect:* the grinder cannot start accidentally.
2. **Empty loose beans and grounds.** Pour out beans and remove the grounds bin or catcher. → *Expect:* the grind chamber is mostly clear.
3. **Remove safe parts.** [BRANCH: burr grinder | blade grinder] take out removable burrs or hopper parts only if the manual allows it; leave fixed parts in place. → *Expect:* cleanable areas are exposed without forcing parts.
4. **Brush burrs or blades.** Use a dry brush to loosen packed grounds from teeth, blades, corners, and chute openings. → *Expect:* dry grounds fall free.
5. **Vacuum loose debris.** Use a small vacuum nozzle near the chamber without touching delicate parts. → *Expect:* loosened grounds are removed.
6. **Use tablets or rice if suitable.** Run grinder-cleaning tablets, or a small amount of dry uncooked rice only if the manufacturer allows it. → *Expect:* oils and fine residue are absorbed into the powder.
7. **Discard the cleaning powder.** Empty the bin and brush away remaining tablet or rice dust. → *Expect:* no visible cleaning residue remains in the chamber.
8. **Wipe removable plastic parts.** Use a barely damp cloth on removable bins or lids, then dry fully before reassembly. → *Expect:* parts are clean and completely dry.
9. **Keep the motor dry.** Never rinse, soak, or spray the motor base. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* water inside the motor can ruin the grinder or create shock risk, so clean it only with a dry or barely damp cloth. → *Expect:* the base exterior is clean and dry.
10. **Reassemble and purge.** Put parts back, plug in, and grind a small spoonful of beans to clear residue. → *Expect:* the grinder runs normally and first purge grounds can be discarded.

## Decision points

- The manual forbids rice → use grinder tablets or dry brushing only.
- Burrs are stuck in place → do not pry; brush around them and consult the manual.
- The grinder smells rancid after cleaning → repeat tablet cleaning and purge with beans.
- Water touched the motor base → leave unplugged and fully dry before considering use.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Grinder will not run after reassembly:** detect by no motor response → recover by checking that hopper, lid, and safety locks are seated.
- **F2 Cleaning dust in coffee:** detect by pale powder in the first grounds → recover by brushing and purging with more beans.
- **F3 Wet motor base:** detect by visible liquid near seams or switch → recover by keeping it unplugged and allowing full drying before use.
- **F4 Burr alignment changed:** detect by unusual rubbing or grind size shift → recover by reseating burrs according to the manual.

## Verification

The grinder is dry, reassembled, free of visible grounds or cleaning residue, and runs normally during a short purge grind.

## Variations

- Blade grinder: brush around the blade carefully and wipe the cup with a barely damp cloth.
- Espresso burr grinder: follow the manual's burr removal and calibration steps if deep cleaning.
- Spice use: dedicate a separate grinder if strong odors persist after cleaning.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk if unplugged. Keep fingers away from blades and burr teeth, never wet the motor base, let washed removable parts dry fully, and discard cleaning powder before brewing.
