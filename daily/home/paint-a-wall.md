## Goal

One interior wall carries two even coats of the new color with crisp edges at the ceiling, corners, and trim, and no drips, lap marks, or paint on the floor.

## Preconditions

- Enough paint for two coats (roughly 1 L per 10 m² per coat; check the can's coverage figure).
- Roller with the right nap (10 mm for lightly textured walls, 6 mm for smooth), roller tray, 50-64 mm angled sash brush, painter's tape, drop cloths, filler and sandpaper for wall defects.
- Room ventilated: window openable, furniture movable at least 1 m off the wall.
- 4 or more hours of daylight remaining; you will want raking light to check coverage.

## Steps

1. **Clear and protect.** Move furniture off the wall, remove outlet and switch plates, lay drop cloths tight against the baseboard. → *Expect:* bare wall, plates in a labeled bag with their screws, floor covered with no gaps at the base.
2. **Wash and repair the wall.** Wipe down with sugar soap or diluted degreaser (kitchen-adjacent walls especially), fill holes and dents, sand repairs flush, dust off. → *Expect:* wall feels smooth and slightly matte; your hand comes away clean.
3. **Tape the borders.** Tape along ceiling line, adjacent walls, and trim; press the tape edge down firmly with a fingernail or card. A loosely pressed edge is the single biggest cause of bleed. → *Expect:* continuous tape lines with the leading edge burnished shiny.
4. **Cut in the edges with the brush.** Load the bottom third of the brush, paint a 5-8 cm band along all taped edges and corners, working one wall section at a time. → *Expect:* an even wet band framing the wall, no drips running from the band.
5. **Roll the field in a W pattern.** Load the roller evenly in the tray, roll a large W in a roughly 1 m square, then fill it in with vertical strokes without reloading; work top to bottom, each square overlapping the last while its edge is still wet. → *Expect:* uniform wet sheen across each square, no thick lines at the roller edges.
6. **Keep the wet edge moving.** Do not stop mid-wall for a break; a dried edge painted over later shows as a lap mark. Finish the full wall in one pass. → *Expect:* whole wall wet-coated in one continuous session.
7. **Let the first coat dry fully.** Follow the can's recoat time (typically 2-4 h for water-based paint), not touch-dry time. Wrap the brush and roller tightly in plastic instead of washing between coats. → *Expect:* coat is dry to firm pressure; first-coat coverage looks streaky and thin, which is normal.
8. **Roll the second coat the same way.** Cut in again, then W-pattern the field. → *Expect:* color reads solid and even; the streaks from coat one are gone.
9. **Pull the tape while the paint is barely wet.** Pull at a 45 degree angle, slowly, back over itself. If the paint has fully dried, score the tape line lightly with a blade first. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* ripping dried paint off with the tape tears the new coat and sometimes the old one; when in doubt, score. → *Expect:* crisp paint lines, tape comes away with no flakes attached.
10. **Clean up.** Water-based: wash brush and roller until water runs clear, spin dry. Reinstall plates after the paint at their edges is dry. → *Expect:* tools clean and reshaped, plates back on, drop cloths folded paint-side-in.

## Decision points

- Wall color is dark or strongly saturated and the new color is light → add a coat of stain-blocking primer before step 4, or budget a third coat.
- Wall is fresh plaster or bare drywall → prime first; topcoat straight onto bare substrate flashes and peels.
- Glossy existing finish → scuff-sand the whole wall (fine grit) in step 2 or the new coat will not bond.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tape bleed:** wobbling or feathered paint line after tape pull → let dry, retape on the good side, touch up with the sash brush freehand.
- **F2 Lap marks:** visible darker stripes where a dried edge was overpainted → let the coat dry fully, then apply one more full coat keeping the wet edge; spot-fixing makes it worse.
- **F3 Drips and runs:** found while wet → roll them out immediately; found dry → sand the run flat, spot-prime, recoat that area edge to edge.
- **F4 Roller stipple or orange peel:** paint too thick or roller overloaded → thin per the can's instructions, load less, and finish each square with light unloaded strokes.
- **F5 Missed patches visible next morning:** check with raking light (torch held flat against the wall) before declaring done → recoat thin spots wall-edge to wall-edge, not as islands.

## Verification

Under raking light from both sides, the wall shows uniform color and sheen with no thin patches, drips, or lap marks; ceiling and trim lines are straight; plates are reinstalled; no paint is on the floor, trim, or ceiling.

## Variations

- Oil-based or alkyd paint: solvent cleanup (white spirit), longer recoat times, ventilation is mandatory rather than nice.
- Textured or previously patched walls: use a thicker nap roller and expect a third coat over patches unless they were spot-primed.
- Ceiling-color mismatch at the top edge: a beveled "cutting-in" line 1-2 mm below the ceiling looks straighter than chasing an uneven ceiling exactly.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk overall. Ventilate during and after painting, keep a stable step stool rather than stretching for the ceiling line, and never leave solvent-soaked rags bunched up (oil-based rags can self-ignite; dry them flat outdoors). Wet paint on skin is a soap problem, not an emergency.
