## Goal

Ants are removed by finding the trail, cleaning the scent path, using bait for the colony, and sealing entry points.

## Preconditions

- Paper towels, dish soap, and water or household cleaner.
- Ant bait stations or gel bait labeled for indoor ants.
- Caulk, weatherstripping, or tape for temporary sealing.

## Steps

1. **Follow the trail.** Watch where ants travel for several minutes before cleaning. → *Expect:* you identify a food source, wall gap, window edge, or pipe opening.
2. **Remove the food source.** Seal sugar, crumbs, pet food, and trash. → *Expect:* no exposed food remains near the trail.
3. **Clean the scent path.** Wipe the trail with soapy water, then dry the surface. → *Expect:* fewer ants follow the exact old line.
4. **Place bait near activity.** Set bait beside the trail but away from children, pets, and food-prep surfaces. → *Expect:* ants find bait without crossing clean counters.
5. **Avoid spraying the trail after baiting.** Let worker ants carry bait back for several days. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* insecticide spray can scatter the colony and contaminate food surfaces, so do not spray where bait needs active foragers. → *Expect:* activity may rise briefly, then fall.
6. **Seal entry points.** Caulk cracks, tighten door sweeps, or tape gaps temporarily after bait has started working. → *Expect:* fewer ants enter from the same point.
7. **Monitor for a week.** Refresh bait as the label allows and keep surfaces clean. → *Expect:* traffic drops steadily or disappears.

## Decision points

- Ants are large and appear near damp wood → suspect carpenter ants and call a pest professional.
- Bait is ignored → try a different bait type, sweet or protein-based.
- Ants are outdoors only → keep bait outside and avoid drawing them indoors.
- Infestation persists after 1 to 2 weeks → call a licensed pest control service.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Trail returns:** detect ants using the same path; recover by cleaning again and sealing the exact gap.
- **F2 Bait avoidance:** detect ants walking around bait; recover by moving bait closer to the trail or changing bait formula.
- **F3 Pet access:** detect bait reachable by pets; recover by moving it into a bait station or blocked crevice.
- **F4 Nest in wall:** detect recurring ants from outlets or trim; recover by calling a professional rather than opening walls.

## Verification

For 48 hours, no active ant trail appears at the original food source or entry point, and bait remains placed or removed according to its label.

## Variations

- Kitchen: clean under appliances and behind countertop items where crumbs collect.
- Apartment: report recurring entry points to building management if sealing requires exterior work.
- Outdoor sugar ants: trim branches touching the building and clean sticky patio spills.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk if baits are placed carefully. Follow pesticide labels, avoid spraying food areas, and keep bait away from children and animals.
