## Goal

A faulty toilet fill valve is removed and replaced so the tank refills quietly to the correct level without leaks.

## Preconditions

- You have a replacement fill valve compatible with the toilet, adjustable wrench, sponge, bucket, towel, and scissors if the refill tube must be trimmed.
- You can reach a working toilet shutoff valve.
- Turn the water off first and flush before loosening the supply connection.
- Call a plumber if the shutoff will not close, the supply tube is rigid and corroded, or the tank is cracked.

## Steps

1. **Shut off water.** Turn the toilet shutoff valve clockwise until it stops. → *Expect:* no new water enters the tank.
2. **Drain the tank.** Flush, hold the handle down, then sponge remaining water into a bucket. → *Expect:* the tank bottom is nearly dry.
3. **Disconnect the supply line.** Place a towel under the tank and loosen the nut below the fill valve. → *Expect:* the supply line hangs free with only a small spill.
4. **Remove the old valve.** Unscrew the mounting nut under the tank and lift the fill valve out from above. → *Expect:* the tank hole is open and the old gasket is removed.
5. **Set the new height.** Adjust the new valve so its critical-level mark sits at least 1 inch above the overflow tube. → *Expect:* the valve height matches the toilet and maker instructions.
6. **Install the new gasket.** Insert the valve through the tank hole with the rubber gasket inside the tank. → *Expect:* the valve stands straight and the gasket lies flat.
7. **Tighten the mounting nut.** Hand-tighten, then snug slightly with a wrench without stressing the tank. → *Expect:* the valve does not wobble.
8. **Connect the refill tube.** Clip the tube to the overflow tube above the water line, not pushed down inside it. → *Expect:* refill water can flow into the overflow opening without siphoning.
9. **Reconnect the supply.** Attach the supply line to the valve shank and tighten until snug. → *Expect:* the connector seats squarely.
10. **Restore and test.** Open the shutoff slowly, let the tank fill, and adjust the float to the fill line. → *Expect:* the tank fills and stops without dripping below.

## Decision points

- Supply line washer is cracked or flattened → replace the supply line before turning water back on.
- Valve shank leaks below the tank → shut water off, drain, reseat the gasket, and retighten gently.
- Water enters the overflow tube after refill → lower the float.
- Tank porcelain cracks or flexes while tightening → stop and call a plumber.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Shutoff fails:** water continues running, close the main water valve or call a plumber.
- **F2 Cross-threaded supply:** nut will not turn smoothly, back it off and restart by hand.
- **F3 Slow leak below tank:** towel becomes wet at the mounting nut, shut off water and reseat the valve gasket.
- **F4 Refill tube siphons:** bowl trickles after refill, clip the tube above the overflow rim.

## Verification

With the tank full, the fill valve stops at the marked level and the tank bottom, supply nut, and shutoff stay dry for ten minutes.

## Variations

- Dual-flush toilets: use the manufacturer-specified valve if clearance is tight.
- One-piece toilets: access under the tank may be narrow and require a basin wrench.
- Old rigid supply tubes: replace with a braided toilet connector if local code allows.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: a loose tank connection can leak into floors. Turn water off before removal, open water slowly for testing, and call a licensed plumber for seized valves, cracked porcelain, or leaks you cannot stop.
