## Goal

Old failing grout is removed and replaced with fresh grout that is tooled, cleaned, cured, and sealed where appropriate.

## Preconditions

- You have a grout saw or oscillating grout blade, vacuum, sponge, bucket, grout float, matching grout, microfiber cloth, sealer, gloves, and eye protection.
- Tiles are firmly bonded and the substrate is dry.
- Turn off nearby water if removing grout around fixtures could expose plumbing or wet wall damage.
- Call a tile pro if tiles are loose, walls feel soft, black mold is present, or waterproofing may be compromised.

## Steps

1. **Inspect the field.** Tap tiles and press grout lines to find loose tile or soft areas. → *Expect:* only stable areas are selected for regrouting.
2. **Remove old grout.** Rake out damaged grout to at least half the tile thickness, keeping the tool centered in the joint. → *Expect:* joints are open and tile edges are intact.
3. **Clean the joints.** Vacuum dust and wipe with a damp sponge, then let the joints dry. → *Expect:* no loose powder remains.
4. **Mix grout.** Prepare grout to the maker's consistency and slake if instructed. → *Expect:* grout is smooth and spreadable.
5. **Pack the joints.** Hold the float diagonally and force grout into the lines. → *Expect:* joints are completely filled.
6. **Tool the lines.** Draw the float or sponge lightly over the joints to shape them evenly. → *Expect:* grout sits slightly below the tile face with no voids.
7. **Wipe haze.** [BRANCH: first wipe | final buff] use a barely damp sponge first, then buff dry haze with microfiber after it forms. → *Expect:* tile faces are clean without pulling grout from joints.
8. **Cure undisturbed.** Keep the area dry and avoid scrubbing for the grout's cure time. → *Expect:* grout hardens without cracking.
9. **Seal if needed.** Apply penetrating sealer to cement grout after cure, wiping excess from tile. → *Expect:* grout darkens briefly and then dries protected.

## Decision points

- Tiles move or sound hollow → stop and repair tile bond before regrouting.
- Grout joint is under 1/8 inch → use unsanded grout unless product instructions say otherwise.
- Shower corners or wall-to-floor changes are cracked → use flexible caulk there, not grout.
- Mold or wet drywall is found → call a pro before closing the surface.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tile edge chips:** tool nicks the glaze, slow down and use hand tools near fragile edges.
- **F2 Grout powdery:** mix was too wet or overwashed, remove weak grout and redo after drying.
- **F3 Haze hardens:** film remains on tile, use grout haze remover compatible with the tile.
- **F4 Cracks return:** joints split after cure, check for tile movement or wrong material at corners.

## Verification

After curing, grout lines are full, firm, evenly tooled, haze-free, and sealed where the grout type requires sealing.

## Variations

- Epoxy grout: follow exact working times and cleanup steps because it cures hard.
- Natural stone: preseal stone and use stone-safe grout and cleaner.
- Shower walls: wait the product's full cure time before using the shower.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: dust and hidden water damage are the main hazards. Wear eye protection and a dust mask, ventilate, avoid damaging waterproofing, and call a pro for loose tile or mold.
