## Goal

A loose cabinet hinge is tightened or re-anchored so the door swings cleanly and lines up with neighboring doors.

## Preconditions

- Correct screwdriver or driver bit for the hinge screws.
- Toothpicks or wooden matchsticks, wood glue, and a utility knife for stripped wood holes.
- Helper or support for heavy doors.

## Steps

1. **Support the door.** Hold the cabinet door level or place a folded towel under it. → *Expect:* hinge is not carrying extra twisting weight while you work.
2. **Tighten obvious screws.** Use the correct driver and turn hinge screws clockwise until snug. → *Expect:* screws stop wobbling and hinge plate sits flat.
3. **Test movement.** Open and close the door slowly. → *Expect:* door moves without clunking or pulling away.
4. **Repair stripped holes.** If a screw spins, remove it, dip toothpicks in wood glue, pack the hole, snap flush, and reinstall after the glue begins to grab. → *Expect:* screw bites into wood again.
5. **Realign the door.** Adjust hinge position or European hinge screws in small turns until gaps are even. → *Expect:* top and side gaps look parallel.
6. **Snug, do not crush.** Tighten final screws by hand for the last turns. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overtightening can strip particleboard or split the stile, so stop when the hinge is firm. → *Expect:* hinge does not shift under gentle pressure.
7. **Clean up and recheck.** Wipe glue squeeze-out and open the door several times. → *Expect:* door stays aligned after repeated movement.

## Decision points

- Screw head is stripped → use a rubber band under the driver or replace the screw.
- Hinge cup is loose in the door → tighten cup screws or repair those holes too.
- Door sags because cabinet frame is damaged → use longer screws into solid material or call a repair person.
- Two hinges fight each other → loosen both slightly, align the door, then retighten.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Screw spins:** detect no tightening resistance; recover with toothpicks and glue or a wider screw.
- **F2 Door binds:** detect rubbing at the frame; recover by adjusting hinge screws in small increments.
- **F3 Split wood:** detect cracking near the hole; recover by stopping and clamping with glue before reinstalling hardware.
- **F4 Wrong driver:** detect cam-out or chewed screw head; recover by switching to the exact bit size.

## Verification

The cabinet door opens and closes five times without sagging, rubbing, or hinge movement, and the visible gaps remain even.

## Variations

- European concealed hinge: use the depth, side, and height adjustment screws rather than bending the hinge.
- Particleboard cabinet: toothpicks may be temporary; threaded inserts or repair plates hold better.
- Heavy door: remove the door or have a helper support it while repairing holes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from sharp tools, pinched fingers, and falling doors. Support heavy doors and keep glue off finished surfaces.
