## Goal

Mice are addressed humanely by removing food access, sealing entry points, using live traps responsibly, and escalating when needed.

## Preconditions

- Gloves, flashlight, steel wool, caulk, and sealant or hardware cloth.
- Airtight food containers and trash control.
- Live traps allowed by local rules, plus a release plan.

## Steps

1. **Confirm signs.** Look for droppings, gnaw marks, scratching sounds, or greasy rub marks along walls. → *Expect:* you can identify where mice travel.
2. **Remove food access.** Store grains, pet food, and snacks in hard sealed containers and clean crumbs nightly. → *Expect:* counters, floors, and pantry shelves have no exposed food.
3. **Find entry points.** Check gaps around pipes, doors, vents, baseboards, and foundations. → *Expect:* holes the size of a pencil or larger are marked for sealing.
4. **Seal durable gaps.** Pack small holes with steel wool and seal over it; cover larger gaps with hardware cloth. → *Expect:* material cannot be pushed aside by a finger.
5. **Set live traps on travel paths.** Place traps against walls with bait such as peanut butter or oats. → *Expect:* trap entrances face the route mice already use.
6. **Check traps often.** Inspect at least twice daily and follow local release rules for distance and habitat. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* releasing animals in the wrong place may be illegal or fatal to them, so confirm local rules before trapping. → *Expect:* trapped mice are not left without water or exposed to heat or cold.
7. **Escalate if activity continues.** Call a humane pest professional for repeated captures, nests, or building-wide problems. → *Expect:* a plan covers exclusion, sanitation, and monitoring.

## Decision points

- You see many droppings daily → treat as an infestation and call a professional.
- You cannot seal exterior gaps because you rent → document them and contact the landlord.
- Live release is restricted locally → ask animal control or a pest professional about legal humane options.
- You smell decay → a mouse may be trapped in a wall; call for help if the source is inaccessible.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Trap avoidance:** detect bait gone nearby but trap empty; recover by moving traps tight to walls and wearing gloves.
- **F2 Re-entry:** detect new droppings after release; recover by sealing missed gaps before setting more traps.
- **F3 Food relapse:** detect chewed packaging; recover by moving all pantry goods into hard containers.
- **F4 Unsafe cleanup:** detect dry droppings; recover by ventilating, wetting with disinfectant, and avoiding sweeping dust.

## Verification

For 7 days, no new droppings, gnaw marks, nighttime scratching, or trap captures appear after food is sealed and entry points are closed.

## Variations

- Garage: focus on birdseed, grass seed, and gaps under the garage door.
- Apartment: coordinate with neighboring units and management because mice travel through shared walls.
- Cold climates: inspect more often in fall when mice seek warmth.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from bites, droppings, disease exposure, and legal release rules. Wear gloves, avoid poison if humane removal is the goal, and involve professionals for infestations.
