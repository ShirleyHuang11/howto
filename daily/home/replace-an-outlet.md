## Goal

A worn standard electrical outlet is replaced with a matching rated receptacle after power is shut off and verified dead.

## Preconditions

- You have a matching outlet, cover plate, screwdriver, wire stripper, needle-nose pliers, non-contact voltage tester, and plug-in receptacle tester.
- You know which breaker controls the outlet and can keep it off while working.
- ⚠️ *Irreversible:* do not proceed unless the breaker is off and every wire tests dead.
- Call a licensed electrician for aluminum wiring, burnt insulation, loose boxes, two-prong conversions, GFCI uncertainty, multiwire circuits, or any voltage you cannot make dead.

## Steps

1. **Switch off the breaker.** Turn off the breaker feeding the outlet and tell others not to restore it. → *Expect:* plugged-in lamps or devices at that outlet turn off.
2. **Test the outlet dead.** Use a known-working tester on the outlet slots, then test the tester on a live outlet again. → *Expect:* the target outlet shows no voltage and the tester is proven functional.
3. **Remove the cover.** Unscrew the wall plate and set screws aside. → *Expect:* the receptacle mounting screws are visible.
4. **Pull the receptacle out.** Remove mounting screws and pull the device straight out by the yoke. → *Expect:* wires are visible without stretching.
5. **Test every wire dead.** Check hot, neutral, ground, and each cable entering the box. → *Expect:* no conductor indicates voltage.
6. **Note the wiring.** Photograph the outlet and label hot wires on brass screws, neutral wires on silver screws, and ground on green. → *Expect:* each wire's original terminal is documented.
7. **Transfer one wire at a time.** [BRANCH: side screw | backwire clamp] move each wire to the matching terminal on the new outlet, avoiding push-in backstab holes. → *Expect:* hot, neutral, and ground are on correct terminals.
8. **Mount the outlet.** Fold wires gently, secure the receptacle plumb, and reinstall the cover plate. → *Expect:* the outlet is firm and the cover is not cracked.
9. **Restore and test.** Turn the breaker on and use a receptacle tester. → *Expect:* the tester reports correct wiring and a plug fits snugly.

## Decision points

- Tester shows voltage after breaker off → stop and identify the correct circuit.
- Two hot wires on a split receptacle with a broken tab → call an electrician unless you understand the circuit.
- No ground wire is present → replace only with code-allowed options such as GFCI labeling or call a pro.
- Wire insulation is brittle, burnt, or nicked → stop and call a licensed electrician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Breaker trips:** power trips immediately, turn it off and recheck for crossed wires or damaged insulation.
- **F2 Tester shows open ground:** receptacle works but lacks ground, turn power off and inspect ground connection or call a pro.
- **F3 Outlet is loose:** plug wiggles or device moves, tighten mounting screws or repair the box.
- **F4 Wire will not hold:** conductor slips from terminal, restrip to the proper length and tighten under the correct clamp.

## Verification

With the cover installed and breaker on, a plug-in tester reports correct wiring and the outlet holds a plug firmly without heat, buzz, or movement.

## Variations

- GFCI outlet: connect line and load exactly as labeled, then test reset and trip buttons.
- Kitchen, bath, garage, and exterior outlets: GFCI and weather-resistant rules often apply.
- Smart outlet: confirm neutral requirements and box space before purchase.

## Safety & privacy

High risk: household voltage can kill or start a fire. Breaker off and test dead before touching wires, keep others away from the panel, and call a licensed electrician for any uncertainty.
