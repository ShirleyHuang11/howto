## Goal

Separate two nested drinking glasses without cracking, chipping, or shattering them.

## Preconditions

- The glasses are empty and not already cracked.
- A sink, cold water, warm tap water, dish towel, and optional dish soap are available.
- Hands are dry enough to keep a secure grip.

## Steps

1. **Move to the sink.** Set the stuck glasses over a towel in the sink or on a towel-lined counter. → *Expect:* any slip would land on a padded surface.
2. **Check for cracks.** Rotate the glasses slowly and inspect the rims and sides. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* forcing cracked glass can shatter it; discard cracked glass instead of separating it. → *Expect:* only intact glass is handled.
3. **Add cold water inside.** Pour cold water or ice water into the inner glass only. → *Expect:* the inner glass cools and contracts slightly.
4. **Warm the outside.** Run warm, not boiling, water over the outside glass while keeping cold water inside the inner glass. → *Expect:* the outer glass warms and expands slightly.
5. **Grip with towels.** Wrap each glass with a dry towel to improve grip and protect your hands. → *Expect:* both glasses can be held without slipping.
6. **Wiggle gently.** Twist the glasses in opposite directions with light pressure, then lift the inner glass straight up. → *Expect:* you feel small movement instead of a hard lock.
7. **Add soap at the rim.** If still stuck, drip a little dish soap where the rims meet and repeat the temperature step. → *Expect:* soap works into the seam between the glasses.
8. **Stop before force.** If the glasses do not move after several gentle tries, set them aside to rest at room temperature and retry later. → *Expect:* no bending, prying, or hard pulling is used.
9. **Dry and store loosely.** After separating, dry both glasses and avoid stacking identical tight glasses while damp. → *Expect:* glasses are separate, dry, and not nested tightly.

## Decision points

- Glass is thin, antique, or valuable → use extra padding and stop earlier.
- Glasses are hot from a dishwasher → let them cool before using cold water.
- The rim has a chip → stop and discard the damaged glass safely.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 No movement:** detect a locked feel after gentle twisting → repeat cold-inside and warm-outside once, then rest.
- **F2 Slippery grip:** detect towels getting wet or hands sliding → dry the outside and use fresh towels.
- **F3 Shatter risk:** detect creaking, cracking, or flexing → stop, put the glasses down, and handle as broken glass if needed.
- **F4 Soap residue:** detect slick film after separation → wash and rinse both glasses before use.

## Verification

The glasses separate cleanly with no chips, cracks, or sharp fragments, and both are washed before reuse.

## Variations

- Metal tumbler outside glass: warm the metal outside gently while cooling the inner glass.
- Plastic cup inside glass: use mild temperature changes only to avoid warping.
- No ice: use the coldest tap water available inside the inner glass.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from shattering glass. Never force, pry with tools, or use boiling water on cold glass.
