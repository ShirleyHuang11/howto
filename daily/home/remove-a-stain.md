## Goal

A fresh stain is treated by type without spreading it, setting it with heat, or damaging the material.

## Preconditions

- Clean white cloths or paper towels, cold water, mild detergent, and stain remover if available.
- Access to the care label for fabric items.
- A hidden test area for upholstery, carpet, or delicate fabric.

## Steps

1. **Identify the material.** Check whether the item is washable, dry-clean only, carpet, upholstery, or hard surface. → *Expect:* you know whether water is safe to use.
2. **Blot, do not rub.** Press a clean cloth onto the stain and lift straight up. → *Expect:* color transfers to the cloth without the stain spreading wider.
3. **Remove solids.** Lift food, mud, or wax with a dull edge before adding liquid. → *Expect:* loose material is gone and only the stain remains.
4. **Use cold water first.** Flush from the back of washable fabric when possible. → *Expect:* stain moves out of the fibers instead of deeper into them.
5. **Triage the stain type.** [BRANCH: protein like blood or egg | oil or grease | tannin like coffee or wine | dye or ink] choose the matching treatment. → *Expect:* the next product fits the stain instead of guessing.
6. **Test treatment first.** Apply a tiny amount in a hidden spot and wait a few minutes. → *Expect:* no color loss, ring, or texture change appears.
7. **Apply targeted cleaner.** Use detergent for protein and oils, alcohol for some inks, or oxygen bleach for many tannins on safe fabrics. → *Expect:* the stain lightens from the outside inward.
8. **Rinse and inspect.** Rinse or blot with clean water, then inspect while still wet. → *Expect:* cleaner residue is reduced and remaining stain is visible.
9. **Repeat before drying.** Treat again if color remains. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* dryer heat can permanently set many stains, so do not machine dry until the mark is gone. → *Expect:* the item is held out of heat until clean.
10. **Launder or finish.** Wash according to the care label or air-dry the treated area. → *Expect:* the material dries without a ring or cleaner crust.

## Decision points

- Blood, dairy, egg, or sweat → cold water only at first because heat cooks protein into fibers.
- Oil or grease → detergent needs contact time before water rinsing.
- Dry-clean only → blot and take it to a cleaner with the stain source identified.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stain spreads:** detect a larger wet ring; switch to smaller blotting motions from the outside edge inward.
- **F2 Color lifts:** detect dye on the cloth from the fabric itself; stop the cleaner and rinse with cold water.
- **F3 Heat-set mark:** detect a stain that survived drying; use oxygen bleach if safe or accept that removal may be partial.
- **F4 Cleaner ring:** detect a pale outline after drying; feather with clean water and blot wider than the ring.

## Verification

The treated area is dry or ready to launder, with the stain removed or clearly reduced and no new color loss or fabric damage.

## Variations

- Carpet: work from edge to center and avoid soaking the pad underneath.
- Red wine: blot, dilute with cold water, then use oxygen-based cleaner if the material allows.
- Ink: test alcohol carefully because it can dissolve dyes and finishes.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Ventilate solvent use, keep cleaners away from children and pets, and never mix bleach with ammonia or acids.
