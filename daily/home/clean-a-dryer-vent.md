## Goal

The dryer vent path is cleared from the machine outlet to the outside flap so air can move freely and lint cannot build into a fire hazard.

## Preconditions

- Dryer is cool, empty, and reachable from behind without straining the cord or gas line.
- You have a vacuum, dryer vent brush, screwdriver or nut driver, gloves, and a flashlight.
- For gas dryers, you know where the gas shutoff is and will stop if the flexible gas line must be moved.

## Steps

1. **Power down the dryer.** Unplug the dryer, or switch off the dedicated breaker if the plug is inaccessible. → *Expect:* the dryer display is dark and the drum will not start.
2. **Pull the dryer forward carefully.** Move it just far enough to reach the duct, keeping hoses, cords, and any gas line unstressed. → *Expect:* the vent connection is visible and nothing is kinked or taut.
3. **Disconnect the duct.** Loosen the clamp or tape at the dryer outlet and at the wall if needed. [BRANCH: flexible duct | rigid duct] flex duct may compress, rigid duct may need a gentler straight pull. → *Expect:* the duct end is open and lint is visible.
4. **Vacuum the dryer outlet.** Vacuum loose lint from the dryer port without pushing debris deeper into the machine. → *Expect:* the outlet lip and first few inches are clear.
5. **Brush the duct.** Run the vent brush through the duct in short passes, then vacuum the loosened lint. → *Expect:* the brush comes back with less lint on each pass.
6. **Check the outside flap.** Go outside, clear lint from the hood, and make sure the flap opens and closes freely. → *Expect:* the flap moves without sticking and the hood is not blocked by nests, leaves, or screens packed with lint.
7. **Reconnect with a smooth path.** Refit the duct, tighten clamps, replace damaged foil tape, and keep the run as short and uncrushed as possible. → *Expect:* both ends are secure and the duct has no sharp folds.
8. **Run an air test.** Restore power and run air-only for two minutes while watching the outside flap. → *Expect:* strong airflow opens the flap and no lint smell, rattle, or leak appears indoors.

## Decision points

- Gas dryer line is pulled tight → stop and call a qualified technician before moving the dryer farther.
- Duct is plastic, brittle, crushed, or torn → replace it with listed metal duct before using the dryer again.
- Outside hood is high, roof-mounted, or inaccessible → clean the reachable end only and hire vent service for the full run.
- Drying still takes too long after cleaning → inspect the lint trap path and consider professional duct measurement.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dryer will not power back on:** detect a dead panel after reconnection, recover by checking the plug, breaker, and door switch before assuming appliance failure.
- **F2 Clamp leaks lint:** detect dust blowing at the joint during the air test, recover by reseating the duct and tightening the clamp.
- **F3 Vent brush gets stuck:** detect resistance that will not reverse, recover by turning gently backward and pulling slowly rather than forcing it.
- **F4 Outside flap stays closed:** detect no movement during the air test, recover by clearing the hood or replacing a seized flap.

## Verification

With the dryer running on air-only, the outside flap opens strongly and no duct joint leaks lint or warm air indoors.

## Variations

- `stacked-laundry`: use a second person or service call if the dryer cannot move without tipping risk.
- `condensing-dryer`: clean the condenser path and lint filters instead of an outside vent.
- `long-duct-run`: schedule professional rotary cleaning when the duct has multiple bends or exceeds the brush length.

## Safety & privacy

⚠️ Fire risk: lint buildup and weak airflow can ignite under dryer heat.

High risk because dryer lint is combustible and blocked vents are a real fire cause. Never run the dryer with a disconnected, crushed, or plastic vent, and do not strain a gas line.
