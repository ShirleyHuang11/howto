## Goal

A jammed garbage disposal is safely freed, reset, and tested without putting hands into the grinding chamber.

## Preconditions

- You have the disposal hex key or a 1/4 inch Allen wrench, flashlight, tongs or pliers, and access under the sink.
- You can identify the disposal switch or unplug the disposal under the sink.
- Turn power off first; never put a hand inside the disposal, and call a plumber or electrician for leaks, damaged wiring, or repeated breaker trips.

## Steps

1. **Turn power off.** Switch off the wall switch and unplug the disposal if it has a cord. → *Expect:* the disposal cannot start accidentally.
2. **Look into the chamber.** Use a flashlight from above without placing fingers inside. → *Expect:* visible objects can be located safely.
3. **Remove loose objects.** Use tongs or pliers to lift out utensils, glass, bones, or other debris. → *Expect:* foreign objects are removed without hand contact.
4. **Insert the hex key.** Put the hex key into the center socket on the bottom of the disposal. → *Expect:* the key seats firmly.
5. **Rock the motor free.** Turn the key back and forth until it rotates more freely. → *Expect:* resistance decreases and the flywheel moves.
6. **Clear remaining debris.** Recheck from above with the flashlight and remove loosened pieces with tongs. → *Expect:* the chamber looks clear.
7. **Press reset.** Push the red reset button on the bottom or side of the disposal. → *Expect:* the button clicks in and stays in.
8. **Restore power.** Plug the disposal back in or prepare to use the wall switch. → *Expect:* power is available only when you choose to test.
9. **Run water first.** Turn cold water on full before starting the disposal. → *Expect:* water flows freely into the drain.
10. **Pulse the disposal.** [BRANCH: normal hum and spin | hum only] if it spins, run briefly; if it only hums, turn it off immediately and repeat freeing once. → *Expect:* the disposal spins without loud grinding.
11. **Flush.** Let cold water run for 20 to 30 seconds after turning the disposal off. → *Expect:* loosened waste clears the drain.

## Decision points

- Disposal hums but does not spin → turn it off within a few seconds and free the jam again.
- Reset button will not stay in → wait ten minutes for cooling, then try once.
- Water leaks from the disposal body → stop and replace or call a plumber.
- Breaker trips repeatedly → stop and call an electrician or appliance technician.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Jam remains:** hex key will not turn after gentle rocking, stop before forcing and call for service.
- **F2 Object cannot be reached:** debris is visible but trapped, use long pliers or service help, not fingers.
- **F3 Disposal smells after reset:** odor remains, grind ice and citrus peels if allowed by the maker and flush well.
- **F4 Drain backs up:** sink fills while running water, stop and clear the drain before using the disposal.

## Verification

With cold water running, the disposal starts, spins normally, drains without backing up, and remains off when the switch is off.

## Variations

- No bottom hex socket: use a wooden spoon handle from above only with power off to rotate the plate if the maker allows.
- Batch-feed disposal: follow the stopper interlock instructions before testing.
- Septic systems: follow local and manufacturer guidance on disposal use.

## Safety & privacy

High risk: disposal blades and unexpected starts can injure hands, and water near wiring is hazardous. Power off before clearing, never put your hand in the chamber, and call a pro for leaks, electrical faults, or repeated jams.
