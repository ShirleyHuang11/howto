## Goal

Damaged or uneven furniture feet are removed, matched by fitting type, replaced, and leveled so the furniture sits stable without wobble.

## Preconditions

- You have replacement feet or glides, screwdriver, wrench, tape measure, felt pads if needed, wood filler for minor stripped holes, and a helper for heavy furniture.
- The furniture can be emptied and safely tipped or lifted.
- Protect the floor before turning furniture over.
- Call a furniture repair pro if a leg socket, frame corner, or load-bearing rail is cracked.

## Steps

1. **Empty and protect.** Remove loose items, drawers if practical, and place a blanket on the floor. → *Expect:* the furniture can be moved without scratching floors or spilling contents.
2. **Tip safely.** With help for heavy pieces, tilt or invert the furniture onto padding. → *Expect:* all feet are reachable and the piece is stable.
3. **Identify the fitting.** [BRANCH: screw-in foot | plate-mounted foot | glide] inspect whether the foot threads into an insert, screws to a plate, or presses into a socket. → *Expect:* the attachment type is clear.
4. **Remove one foot.** Unscrew, unbolt, or pry out one damaged foot while supporting the frame. → *Expect:* the mounting point is exposed.
5. **Match size and height.** Compare thread diameter, plate hole spacing, stem length, and foot height. → *Expect:* the replacement matches the old fitting.
6. **Repair minor looseness.** Tighten loose plates or fill small stripped wood screw holes before installing. → *Expect:* the mount holds screws firmly.
7. **Install replacements.** Fit the new foot straight and tighten until snug, repeating for matching feet as needed. → *Expect:* feet sit square to the furniture base.
8. **Set the piece upright.** Lower the furniture gently onto all feet. → *Expect:* weight is carried evenly.
9. **Level and test wobble.** Adjust threaded feet or add suitable glides, then press each corner. → *Expect:* the furniture stays steady without rocking.

## Decision points

- Only one foot is broken but height differs → replace the matching pair or full set.
- Threaded insert pulls out → repair the insert or call a furniture repair pro.
- Floor is uneven → adjust leveling feet instead of cutting furniture legs.
- Heavy furniture cannot be tipped safely → get help or hire a mover or repair pro.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong thread:** new foot will not start by hand, stop and match thread size before forcing.
- **F2 Plate holes stripped:** screws spin, use larger approved screws or repair with wood filler and pilot holes.
- **F3 Furniture still wobbles:** one foot is short, adjust levelers or install matching-height feet.
- **F4 Frame cracks:** wood splits near the mount, unload the piece and call a repair pro.

## Verification

The furniture rests on all feet, does not wobble when each corner is pressed, and moves without scraping or tipping.

## Variations

- Sofas and chairs: check center support feet as well as visible corner legs.
- Metal furniture: match threaded stems exactly and avoid cross-threading inserts.
- Floor-protection glides: choose felt, nylon, or rubber based on floor type.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: heavy furniture can crush fingers or tip. Empty the piece, use a helper when needed, protect floors, and do not use cracked load-bearing mounts.
