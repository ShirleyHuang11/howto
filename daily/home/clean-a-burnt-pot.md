## Goal

Burnt food is loosened from a pot without gouging the surface or damaging nonstick coating.

## Preconditions

- Burnt pot is cool enough to handle.
- Baking soda, water, dish soap, and a wooden or plastic scraper.
- White vinegar if the pot is stainless steel, not aluminum or nonstick unless the maker allows it.

## Steps

1. **Let the pot cool.** Move it off heat and wait until it is warm, not hot. → *Expect:* water added to the pot will not flash into steam.
2. **Soak with soap.** Add warm water and a few drops of dish soap, then soak 15 to 30 minutes. → *Expect:* loose food softens and the water darkens.
3. **Scrape gently.** Use a wooden spoon, nylon scraper, or plastic spatula to lift softened bits. → *Expect:* some residue releases without scratching.
4. **Boil baking soda water.** Add enough water to cover the burnt area and 2 tablespoons baking soda, then simmer 5 to 10 minutes. → *Expect:* residue bubbles and loosens from the base.
5. **Cool and scrub.** Turn off heat, let the pot cool, then scrub with a non-scratch pad. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* metal tools can permanently scratch nonstick and enamel, so do not use steel wool or a knife on coated surfaces. → *Expect:* burnt film thins or lifts.
6. **Use vinegar for stainless.** For stainless steel only, add a splash of vinegar after baking soda treatment, then rinse well. → *Expect:* mineral haze and brown stains lighten.
7. **Wash normally.** Finish with dish soap, rinse, and dry. → *Expect:* pot feels smooth and has no cleaner residue.

## Decision points

- Pot is nonstick → avoid abrasive pads, metal scrapers, and long acidic soaks.
- Burnt sugar is stuck → use repeated hot-water soaks rather than force.
- Stainless steel has rainbow stains → vinegar helps after food is removed.
- Enamel is chipped → stop scraping and avoid using the damaged pot for acidic foods.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scratches appearing:** detect dull lines or coating flakes; recover by stopping abrasive cleaning immediately.
- **F2 Burn remains hard:** detect black crust after soaking; recover with another baking soda simmer and longer rest.
- **F3 Cleaner residue:** detect gritty baking soda; recover by rinsing several times with warm water.
- **F4 Warped pot risk:** detect a very hot empty pot; recover by cooling naturally before adding water.

## Verification

The cooking surface is smooth to the touch after washing, with no loose burnt food, no gritty cleaner, and no new scratches from metal tools.

## Variations

- Stainless steel: barkeeper-style oxalic cleaners can help if used by label directions.
- Cast iron: avoid long soaking and dry thoroughly, then oil lightly.
- Enamel: use baking soda paste and patience rather than abrasive pads.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot water and cleaning abrasion. Ventilate if burnt food smoked, and never shock a hot pot with cold water.
