## Goal

A showerhead with weak or uneven spray is cleared of mineral buildup and reinstalled without leaks.

## Preconditions

- You have white vinegar, a plastic bag, rubber band or twist tie, toothbrush, towel, and plumber's tape if the head is removed.
- The shower valve is off and the shower area is safe to stand in.
- Know whether the showerhead is fixed, handheld, or filtered before taking parts apart.
- Call a plumber if the shower arm moves in the wall, leaks behind the escutcheon, or the valve will not shut off.

## Steps

1. **Turn the shower off.** Confirm both hot and cold flow are fully closed. → *Expect:* no water drips from the head except residual drops.
2. **Bag-soak the head.** Fill a bag with enough vinegar to cover the spray face, secure it around the showerhead, and wait 30 to 60 minutes. → *Expect:* mineral deposits soften.
3. **Remove the bag carefully.** Hold the bag upright and pour the vinegar down the drain. → *Expect:* vinegar does not splash onto eyes or stone surfaces.
4. **Brush the jets.** Scrub rubber nozzles and holes with a toothbrush, flicking soft nozzles if present. → *Expect:* loosened scale falls away.
5. **Flush the spray.** Run warm water for 30 seconds. → *Expect:* debris rinses out and spray pattern improves.
6. **Remove the head if still clogged.** [BRANCH: hand-loose | wrench-needed] unscrew by hand or use a cloth-wrapped wrench on the flats. → *Expect:* the head separates from the shower arm.
7. **Backflush and soak again.** Rinse water backward through the inlet, remove loose screen debris, and soak the head again if needed. → *Expect:* water passes through more freely.
8. **Refit the head.** Replace the washer if damaged, wrap shower-arm threads with plumber's tape only if required, and screw the head on straight. → *Expect:* the connection is snug and aligned.
9. **Spray test.** Turn the shower on gradually and inspect the joint and spray face. → *Expect:* spray is even and no water leaks at the threads.

## Decision points

- Showerhead improves after bag soak → skip removal and finish with a spray test.
- Shower arm turns with the head → stop before loosening wall piping and call a plumber.
- Screen is packed with grit → clean it, then run water briefly before reinstalling.
- Filtered showerhead has a cartridge → replace the cartridge if soaking does not restore flow.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Vinegar damages finish:** finish dulls or flakes, rinse immediately and switch to manufacturer-approved cleaner.
- **F2 Thread leak:** water sprays at the joint, shut off, reseat washer, and reinstall straight.
- **F3 Weak flow remains:** showerhead is clean but flow is low, check house pressure or valve issues.
- **F4 Cracked plastic nut:** fitting splits during tightening, replace the showerhead.

## Verification

With the shower running, most jets spray evenly, the pattern is centered, and the showerhead connection stays dry for one minute.

## Variations

- Handheld heads: also clean the hose washer screens at both ends.
- Rain heads: support the head while unscrewing so the arm is not bent.
- Natural stone showers: keep vinegar off stone and rinse surfaces immediately.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: vinegar can irritate eyes and damage some finishes or stone. Ventilate the bathroom, avoid standing on slippery surfaces, and stop if wall piping moves.
