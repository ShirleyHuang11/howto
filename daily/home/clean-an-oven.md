## Goal

An oven with baked-on grime is cleaned by paste-and-patience chemistry (or its own self-clean cycle, used correctly), racks included, and finishes ready to bake without smoking.

## Preconditions

- A cool, switched-off oven. Cleaning starts at room temperature, always.
- Baking soda, water, a spray bottle with vinegar, cloths, a plastic scraper or old spatula, and rubber gloves. This is the default chemistry; commercial oven cleaners are stronger, harsher, and demand their label's ventilation and glove rules exactly.
- Newspaper or towels for the floor in front of the oven, and a sink or tub that fits the racks.

## Steps

1. **Empty the oven and start the racks soaking.** Racks, trays, and thermometer out; racks into a tub or sink of hot water with a good squirt of dish soap (the bathtub lined with a towel works for full-size racks). → *Expect:* bare oven; racks beginning their long soak.
2. **Scrape the loose debris first.** Plastic scraper on carbonized lumps and flakes; sweep or vacuum the crumbs out of the box. Dry removal first makes the paste's job possible. → *Expect:* only bonded grime remains, no loose char.
3. **Paste the interior.** Baking soda mixed with just enough water to make a spreadable paste; coat the walls, floor, and door interior, avoiding heating elements and the door gasket. → *Expect:* a white paste layer over every grimy surface, elements bare.
4. **Wait hours; overnight is the real recipe.** The paste browns as it lifts grease. Do something else; the chemistry does not need supervision. → *Expect:* hours later, a browned, dried paste layer.
5. **Wipe out, spray, wipe again.** Damp cloth removes the paste and the grime it captured; stubborn patches get a vinegar spray onto the soda residue (it foams, loosening the last film) and another wipe. The scraper returns for any softened islands. → *Expect:* bare enamel emerging; cloths rinsed repeatedly; the vinegar pass leaving no white haze.
6. **Scrub and rinse the racks.** After their hours of soaking, a scouring pad takes the softened grime; rinse and dry. → *Expect:* racks clean enough to touch without gray fingers.
7. **Do the door glass and reassemble.** Paste or a glass-safe scrub on the door window (a razor scraper held flat works on glass only, carefully); wipe the gasket gently with plain water. Racks back in, and run the empty oven at medium heat for ten minutes with the fan on or a window open. → *Expect:* clear-ish glass, everything reassembled, and the burn-off run producing faint steam but no billowing smoke.

## Decision points

- Self-clean (pyrolytic) ovens: remove the racks (self-clean warps most racks), wipe out loose debris, run the cycle per the manual with the kitchen ventilated and pets out of the room (the fumes are hard on birds especially), then wipe the ash out when cool. The paste recipe still handles the door glass and racks.
- Commercial cleaner cases: years of neglect or a rental handover deadline justify the strong stuff; the label is the recipe then, gloves and ventilation non-negotiable, and it never touches elements, gaskets, or aluminum.
- Frequency: a paste clean two or three times a year, plus wiping spills once the oven is merely warm (`daily/food/clean-as-you-cook` step 4 applied to ovens), keeps this from ever being an archaeology project.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Smoking on the next bake:** residue left in a corner or under the floor's lip; find the smoke's source when cool and re-wipe; one missed drip smokes for weeks if ignored.
- **F2 White haze after cleaning:** soda residue underdone by the rinse; a vinegar-damp cloth pass clears it.
- **F3 Element scrubbed by accident:** inspect for damage when dry; a damaged element shows as sparking or dead zones on next use and is a replace-part, not a hazard to ignore.
- **F4 Door glass still cloudy from the inside of the pane:** grime between the double glazing needs the door's service disassembly (many doors open their casing with screws per the manual); a judgment call between the manual, a technician, and acceptance.

## Verification

The interior wipes clean with no paste haze, racks slide in clean, the door shows its food through the glass again, the gasket is intact and untouched by harsh chemistry, and the ten-minute burn-off runs without smoke.

## Variations

- Stovetop and extractor-hood filters respond to the same soak-and-soda chemistry and are the natural same-day companions.
- Steam-clean cycles (lighter than pyrolytic) soften rather than incinerate: run them, then do steps 5 to 7; the paste stays in reserve for the corners.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk in three lines: everything starts cold, elements and gaskets are exempt from chemistry and scrubbing, and strong commercial cleaners get their label obeyed like a protocol. The reward is an oven that heats food instead of its own history.
