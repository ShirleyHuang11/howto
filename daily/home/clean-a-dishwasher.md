## Goal

A dishwasher is cleaned so the filter, spray arms, seals, and interior can move water effectively without odor or grit redepositing on dishes.

## Preconditions

- Empty dishwasher and access to the bottom rack.
- Sink, soft brush, toothpick or small wire, cloth, and white vinegar or dishwasher cleaner.
- The appliance manual if the filter or spray arms are unfamiliar.
- No active leak, burning smell, or electrical fault.

## Steps

1. **Empty and inspect.** Remove dishes and pull out the lower rack → *Expect:* the tub bottom, filter area, and spray arm are visible.
2. **Remove the filter.** Unlock and lift out the filter assembly according to the manual [BRANCH: removable filter | self-cleaning filter] → *Expect:* the filter comes free without force or cracking.
3. **Wash the filter.** Rinse under warm water and brush away grease, labels, seeds, and grit → *Expect:* water passes through the mesh easily.
4. **Clear the sump area.** Wipe food pieces from the filter well without pushing debris deeper → *Expect:* no loose scraps remain around the drain opening.
5. **Check spray-arm jets.** Spin each arm and clear blocked holes with a toothpick or small wire → *Expect:* arms rotate freely and jets are open.
6. **Wipe seals and edges.** Clean the door gasket, detergent cup area, and lower door lip with a damp cloth → *Expect:* black residue, crumbs, and slime are removed.
7. **Run a cleaning cycle.** Place a cup of vinegar upright on the top rack or use dishwasher cleaner, then run a hot empty cycle → *Expect:* steam and hot water circulate through an otherwise empty machine.
8. **Reassemble and air out.** Reinstall the filter, slide racks back, and leave the door cracked after the cycle → *Expect:* parts sit flush and odor is reduced.

## Decision points

- Manufacturer warns against vinegar → use an approved dishwasher cleaner.
- Filter will not unlock → consult the manual rather than prying.
- Standing water remains after cleaning → check the drain path and garbage disposal knockout if recently installed.
- Dishes are still gritty → verify spray arms are not blocked by tall dishes during normal loading.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Filter installed wrong:** detect a loose or tilted filter, recover by reseating until it locks flat.
- **F2 Spray arm blocked:** detect arms that hit dishes or do not spin, recover by clearing jets and loading with more clearance.
- **F3 Odor persists:** detect smell after the hot cycle, recover by cleaning the gasket, drain area, and filter again.
- **F4 White film remains:** detect mineral haze, recover with dishwasher cleaner and check rinse aid.
- **F5 Leak appears:** detect water at the door or floor, recover by stopping use and checking seals, level, and service needs.

## Verification

After cleaning, the filter is locked in place, spray arms spin freely by hand, and a hot empty cycle completes without odor, leaks, or standing debris.

## Variations

- Stainless interior: wipe with the grain if polishing visible surfaces.
- Hard water: use rinse aid and periodic dishwasher descaler.
- Garbage disposal connection: run the disposal before starting the dishwasher.
- Heavy label debris: remove labels from jars before washing them.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from hot water and sharp debris. Let parts cool before reaching into the sump, and do not mix vinegar with bleach-containing cleaners.
