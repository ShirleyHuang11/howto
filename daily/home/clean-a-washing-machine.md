## Goal

The washing machine is free of detergent scum, mold, and drain debris: the drum smells of nothing, the drawer and gasket are visibly clean, and future laundry stops coming out with a musty edge.

## Preconditions

- Machine is empty of laundry.
- Supplies: white vinegar or a commercial machine cleaner (or soda crystals), an old toothbrush, rags, and a shallow tray plus towel if the machine has a front drain-pump filter.
- About 15 minutes of hands-on work; the rest is the machine running.

## Steps

1. **Run an empty hot maintenance cycle.** No laundry, hottest cycle available (60-90 °C), with either a commercial machine cleaner (follow its packet) or ~250 ml white vinegar in the drum. Modern cool-wash habits are exactly why machines grow smelly biofilm; the hot empty cycle is the cure. → *Expect:* cycle completes; water may come out visibly grey the first time.
2. **Remove the detergent drawer completely and scrub it.** Most drawers release via a tab or by pressing the softener siphon while pulling. Soak in hot water, scrub every channel with the toothbrush; the fabric-softener compartment hides the worst gunk under its siphon cap, so pop the cap off. → *Expect:* drawer white/clear again, siphon cap clean inside.
3. **Scrub the drawer housing in the machine.** The roof of the drawer slot grows black mold where the water jets enter. Toothbrush and rag as far as fingers reach. → *Expect:* no black or slimy residue on a rag wiped through the slot.
4. **Wipe the door gasket, inside the fold.** [BRANCH: front-loader → pull back each fold of the rubber gasket and wipe the hidden channel all the way around, where water, lint, coins, and black mold collect | top-loader → wipe under the top rim and around the agitator instead] Stubborn black spots: rag soaked in diluted bleach laid on the spot for an hour, then rinse. Note that established black mold stains the rubber permanently even once the growth is dead. → *Expect:* the gasket channel wipes clean; trapped debris (hair pins, coins, socks) recovered.
5. **Clean the drain-pump filter (front-loaders).** Behind the small hatch at the bottom front: lay down the towel, hold the shallow tray under the hatch, open the little drain hose or unscrew the filter slowly; up to a litre of standing water comes out, so drain in stages. Remove lint, coins, and debris from the filter, rinse, screw back firmly. → *Expect:* filter clear; cap screwed tight (a loose cap leaks on the next wash).
6. **Wipe the drum, door glass, and exterior.** Vinegar-damp rag over the drum interior and the inside of the door glass, where scum films build. → *Expect:* glass squeak-clean, drum smells neutral or faintly of vinegar.
7. **Run a short empty rinse cycle.** Flushes loosened residue and any bleach or vinegar traces before the next real load. → *Expect:* rinse water runs clean.
8. **Leave the door and drawer ajar.** From now on, after every wash: door open a hand's width, drawer cracked open, gasket wiped dry weekly. A machine that dries out between loads cannot grow mold; this habit matters more than any deep clean. → *Expect:* interior dry to the touch within a day.

## Decision points

- Machine smells musty again within weeks → the biofilm is established in the outer drum; run the hot empty cycle monthly for 2-3 months, and audit habits (too much detergent, all-cold washes, door kept shut).
- Filter cap will not unscrew or sprays water when cracked → stop, confirm the machine is off and the drum has drained; if seized, do not force with tools that could crack the plastic; a plumber call beats a snapped cap.
- Persistent odor after full cleaning → check the standpipe/drain hose for a partial blockage; smells can come from the house drain, not the machine.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Floor flood at the pump filter:** filter opened fully in one go → close partially, bail with the tray in stages; keep the towel line in place before opening at all.
- **F2 Drawer will not come out:** release tab not pressed → look for a colored tab or push down on the softener siphon while pulling; never yank, the rails snap.
- **F3 Black gasket spots survive scrubbing:** growth is dead but stain is set in the rubber → cosmetic only; a gasket replacement is the fix if it bothers you.
- **F4 Next wash smells of vinegar or bleach:** rinse cycle skipped → run one empty rinse; never let bleach and vinegar meet in the same cycle (chlorine gas).

## Verification

With the cycle history done: the drum and door glass are film-free, the drawer and its housing show no residue, the gasket fold wipes clean and dry, the pump filter is empty and tight, and a sniff of the closed drum after ten minutes yields no musty smell.

## Variations

- Top-loaders: no gasket fold or front pump filter; instead clean under the top rim, around the agitator, and run the same hot empty cycle with vinegar.
- Hard-water areas: add a descaling run (citric acid, ~100 g in the empty hot cycle) twice a year; scale insulates the heater and kills machines early.
- High-efficiency (HE) machines: use only HE detergent at minimal dose; over-dosing detergent is the single biggest cause of the smell that prompts this recipe.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Two rules: never mix bleach with vinegar or other cleaners (toxic gas), and expect real water at the pump filter, so protect the floor and any electrical sockets at floor level. Unplug the machine before reaching deep into the drawer housing.
