## Goal

A still wine bottle is opened cleanly, with the cork removed intact and no foil, cork crumbs, or sudden splashes in the glass.

## Preconditions

- A bottle of still wine, not sparkling wine.
- A corkscrew with a foil cutter or small knife.
- A stable counter and a towel for grip or drips.

## Steps

1. **Stand the bottle upright.** Set it on a stable surface and wipe dust from the top. [BRANCH: cork closure | screw cap] use this recipe for cork, twist off screw caps by hand. → *Expect:* the bottle is steady and the capsule is easy to see.
2. **Cut the foil below the lip.** Use the foil cutter around the lower edge of the raised glass lip, then lift the top of the capsule away. → *Expect:* the cork top is fully exposed with no foil where wine will pour.
3. **Center the worm.** Put the corkscrew tip in the exact center of the cork and press lightly. → *Expect:* the screw stands upright, not leaning into the bottle neck.
4. **Twist straight down.** Turn the worm until about one curl remains visible. → *Expect:* the screw is deep enough to grip without punching through the cork bottom.
5. **Use the first lever.** Hook the short notch on the bottle lip and lift the handle smoothly. → *Expect:* the cork rises partway with a soft movement, not a snap.
6. **Use the second lever.** Switch to the longer notch if your corkscrew has one, then lift again. → *Expect:* most of the cork is out and still aligned with the neck.
7. **Ease the last bit by hand.** Grip the cork and wiggle upward while keeping the bottle steady. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a hard sideways pull can break old cork, so slow down once the cork is nearly free. → *Expect:* the cork releases with a small sigh, not a pop.
8. **Wipe and pour.** Wipe the mouth, sniff for obvious cork taint if you know it, and pour a small taste first. → *Expect:* wine flows cleanly with no foil flakes or cork crumbs.

## Decision points

- Cork starts crumbling → stop pulling hard, screw deeper, and extract slowly.
- Worm goes off-center → back it out and restart before leverage tears the side.
- Bottle is sparkling → use a sparkling-wine opening method, not a corkscrew.
- Cork smells like wet cardboard → taste before serving, since the wine may be corked.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Broken cork:** detect by a cork top snapping off → screw into the remaining center and pull gently.
- **F2 Cork pushed in:** detect by the cork dropping into the wine → pour through a clean strainer.
- **F3 Foil in pour path:** detect by torn capsule touching the rim → cut it away before pouring.
- **F4 Slippery bottle:** detect by rotation under leverage → wrap the base in a towel and reset.

## Verification

The cork is out, the bottle mouth is clean, and a test pour lands in the glass without cork pieces.

## Variations

- Wing corkscrew: twist until both wings rise, then press the wings down evenly.
- Waiter's key: use the two-stage hinge for better control.
- Synthetic cork: expect more friction and use slower, steadier leverage.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk for still wine. Keep knives and foil cutters pointed away from hands, stabilize glass bottles, and serve alcohol only where legal and appropriate.
