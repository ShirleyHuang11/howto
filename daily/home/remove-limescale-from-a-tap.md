## Goal

White limescale is softened and removed from a tap or faucet using a controlled vinegar soak without harming delicate finishes.

## Preconditions

- White vinegar, water, cloth, small bag, rubber band, and soft brush.
- Knowledge of the tap finish, such as chrome, stainless, brass, nickel, stone nearby, or specialty coating.
- Sink access for rinsing.

## Steps

1. **Check the finish.** Identify whether the tap is chrome or stainless, or a finish that may dislike acid. → *Expect:* you know whether vinegar is safe to test.
2. **Test a hidden spot.** Dab diluted vinegar on an unseen area for 5 minutes, then rinse and dry. → *Expect:* no discoloration, dulling, or peeling appears.
3. **Prepare the soak.** Use equal parts vinegar and water for routine scale, or plain vinegar for sturdy chrome with heavy scale. → *Expect:* cloth or bag is wet but not dripping across stone or grout.
4. **Wrap the tap.** Press the soaked cloth on the scale, or secure a small vinegar bag around the spout. → *Expect:* scale stays in contact with the wet material.
5. **Let it dwell.** Wait 15 to 30 minutes, checking delicate finishes sooner. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* acid can etch stone and damage some brass, nickel, and coated finishes, so keep vinegar off those surfaces and rinse spills immediately. → *Expect:* white crust softens.
6. **Brush gently.** Remove the cloth and scrub with a soft toothbrush or microfiber cloth. → *Expect:* flakes and chalky residue lift away.
7. **Rinse and dry.** Flush with clean water and buff dry. → *Expect:* tap is shiny and no vinegar smell remains.

## Decision points

- Finish changes during test → stop and use manufacturer-approved cleaner only.
- Scale is inside the aerator → unscrew it, soak the screen separately, and reinstall.
- Scale remains after 30 minutes → repeat a shorter soak rather than scraping hard.
- Nearby surface is marble or limestone → cover it before using vinegar.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dull finish:** detect cloudy or changed metal; recover by rinsing immediately and stopping acid treatment.
- **F2 Stubborn ring:** detect scale around the base; recover with a soaked cloth pressed into the joint.
- **F3 Scratched metal:** detect lines from an abrasive pad; recover by switching to microfiber and avoiding powders.
- **F4 Leaky aerator:** detect spray after reinstalling; recover by reseating the washer and tightening by hand.

## Verification

The tap surface and aerator are free of chalky limescale, water flows normally, and the finish is rinsed, dry, and unchanged in color.

## Variations

- Shower head: use the same bag method, but support the bag so weight does not stress the pipe.
- Heavy hard water: repeat monthly rather than using stronger acid casually.
- Specialty finish: use only the cleaner recommended by the manufacturer.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk from mild acid and slippery spills. Do not mix vinegar with bleach, and protect stone, grout, and plated finishes from prolonged acid contact.
