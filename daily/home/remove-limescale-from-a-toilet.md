## Goal

Toilet limescale is softened with an acid soak, scrubbed under the rim and waterline, and removed without mixing chemicals or scratching the bowl unnecessarily.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: gloves, toilet brush, citric acid powder or vinegar, cup or small bucket, pumice stone made for toilets, cloth.
- No bleach, toilet cleaner, or disinfectant has been used in the bowl during this session.
- Bathroom ventilation is available.
- ⚠️ Never mix bleach with vinegar, citric acid, descaler, or toilet cleaner.

## Steps

1. **Ventilate and flush.** Turn on the fan, put on gloves, and flush to wet the bowl. → *Expect:* fresh water is in the trap and air is moving.
2. **Lower the waterline if needed.** Push water down the trap with the toilet brush, or turn off supply and flush once. → *Expect:* more scale at the waterline is exposed.
3. **Apply the acid soak.** Pour vinegar or dissolved citric acid onto scale and under the rim, soaking paper towel against vertical deposits if needed. → *Expect:* scale stays wet with acid.
4. **Let it dwell.** Wait 30-60 minutes with the lid open and room ventilated. → *Expect:* crust looks chalky, softened, or easier to scrape.
5. **Scrub under the rim and waterline.** Use the toilet brush firmly, focusing where water exits and where the ring formed. → *Expect:* scale flakes or lightens.
6. **Use pumice only on stubborn ceramic scale.** Wet the pumice and bowl, then rub gently on hard deposits. [BRANCH: porcelain ceramic | colored, plastic, or coated surface] use pumice only on porcelain ceramic. → *Expect:* deposit abrades while the wet surface stays smooth.
7. **Flush and repeat once if needed.** Rinse the bowl, inspect, and repeat the acid soak only if scale remains. → *Expect:* water runs clear and the ring is mostly gone.
8. **Restore and prevent.** Turn water back on if closed, flush twice, and clean more often or treat hard water. → *Expect:* toilet functions normally and new scale has less time to build.

## Decision points

- Recent bleach cleaner was used → wait, flush several times, and clean another day with acid.
- Thick brown mineral crust persists → repeat acid dwell before more pumice.
- Bowl is not porcelain ceramic → skip pumice and use chemical dwell only.
- Recurring fast buildup → consider water softening or weekly mild acid maintenance.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Chemical gas or sharp smell:** leave immediately, ventilate, and do not add anything else → rinse only when safe.
- **F2 Pumice scratches:** stop using it → keep the stone and bowl wetter, or switch back to acid soak.
- **F3 Scale remains under rim:** soak paper towel under the rim longer → scrub water jets with a small brush.
- **F4 Toilet will not refill:** supply valve is still off → reopen it and check the tank.

## Verification

The bowl and under-rim area are rinsed clean, visible limescale is gone or reduced to non-scratching residue, and no bleach or other cleaner was mixed with acid.

## Variations

- Hard-water homes: weekly vinegar or citric acid maintenance prevents heavy rings.
- Septic systems: use moderate acid amounts and avoid harsh antibacterial cleaners.
- Commercial descaler: follow the label dwell time and still obey the no-bleach rule.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from chemical incompatibility and splashes. ⚠️ Use one product family only, ventilate, wear gloves, and never mix acid descalers with bleach.
