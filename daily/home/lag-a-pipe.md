## Goal

Exposed pipework is insulated with sleeves or wrap so it loses less heat and is less likely to freeze in cold spaces.

## Preconditions

- The pipe is accessible, dry, and cool enough to handle.
- You have a tape measure, foam sleeves or pipe wrap, utility knife or scissors, tape, and cable ties if needed.
- You know whether the pipe carries hot water, cold water, or heating water.

## Steps

1. **Trace the pipe run.** Identify straight sections, elbows, valves, and gaps where cold air reaches the pipe. → *Expect:* you know every section that needs insulation.
2. **Measure the diameter and length.** Measure outside pipe diameter and total length, adding extra for elbows and mistakes. → *Expect:* the insulation size matches the pipe.
3. **Choose the covering.** [BRANCH: foam sleeve | wrap] use sleeves for straight accessible pipe and wrap for awkward elbows or tight spaces. → *Expect:* the material fits without crushing the pipe.
4. **Cut straight sleeves to length.** Cut square ends and open the slit along the pipe. → *Expect:* each sleeve section covers a straight run cleanly.
5. **Cover elbows and bends.** Miter foam pieces or spiral wrap around bends so no bare pipe shows. → *Expect:* elbows have continuous insulation.
6. **Seal the joints.** Tape sleeve seams, butt joints, and wrap ends, leaving service valves usable. → *Expect:* insulation stays closed when touched.
7. **Check clearances.** Keep insulation away from flues, hot appliance cases, and moving parts. → *Expect:* the pipe is covered but hazards and controls remain clear.

## Decision points

- Pipe is already wet → fix condensation or leaks before insulating.
- Pipe is near a boiler flue or heat source → use only materials rated for that location.
- Outdoor pipe is exposed to weather → use weatherproof insulation or a protective cover.
- Valve or drain cock needs access → leave the handle free and insulate around, not over, the control.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sleeve will not close:** detect a gaping seam, recover by using the next larger size or wrap for that section.
- **F2 Insulation slides:** detect movement along vertical pipe, recover with tape or cable ties that do not crush the foam.
- **F3 Condensation appears:** detect water trapped under insulation, recover by removing it, drying the pipe, and addressing humidity or cold bridging.
- **F4 Elbow remains bare:** detect visible copper or plastic at bends, recover with wrap or mitered offcuts.

## Verification

All reachable vulnerable pipe sections, including elbows, are covered with secure insulation while valves and safety clearances remain accessible.

## Variations

- `cold-loft`: prioritize pipes near eaves, vents, and unheated voids.
- `hot-water`: thicker insulation saves more heat on long runs.
- `plastic-pipe`: use insulation sized for the actual outside diameter, not the nominal pipe label.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Cut away from your body, keep insulation clear of combustion air and flues, and do not hide leaks under new lagging.
