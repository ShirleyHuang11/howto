## Goal

A dead bulb is replaced with a working one of the correct type, and the fixture lights normally.

## Preconditions

- A replacement bulb matching the fixture: same base (screw E26/E27, small screw E12/E14, bayonet, or pin), wattage within the fixture's marked maximum, and the desired color temperature.
- The switch controlling the fixture is known.
- For ceiling fixtures: a stable step stool or ladder — not a swivel chair.

## Steps

1. **Turn the light switch off and, ideally, leave the bulb to cool for a few minutes.** Incandescent/halogen bulbs burn skin for minutes after going out. → *Expect:* switch down/off; bulb cool to a near-touch hover.
2. **Set up stable access.** Place the step stool directly under the fixture, all feet flat. → *Expect:* you can reach the bulb with your elbow bent, not at full stretch.
3. **Remove any cover/shade.** Most twist counterclockwise or unclip; support it with one hand as you release it. → *Expect:* cover off and set somewhere it can't be stepped on.
4. **Remove the old bulb.** Screw base: turn counterclockwise until free. Bayonet: push in gently, quarter-turn counterclockwise, release. Pin (halogen capsule): pull straight out — with a cloth, not bare fingers. → *Expect:* bulb comes free without forcing; socket looks clean and intact.
5. **Check the new bulb against the old one.** Same base and size; wattage ≤ the fixture's marked max (LED equivalents are far under old limits). → *Expect:* bases match; markings within limits.
6. **Install the new bulb.** Screw: clockwise until snug — *snug, not cranked*; over-tightening breaks bulbs and seizes bases. Bayonet: push, quarter-turn clockwise. Halogen pins: push straight in via cloth (skin oil shortens halogen life). → *Expect:* bulb seats firmly with light resistance at the end.
7. **Switch on and observe.** → *Expect:* the bulb lights steadily. If not, F1.
8. **Replace the cover, climb down, and dispose of the old bulb properly.** Incandescent: household trash (wrapped). CFL: hazardous/recycling point — contains mercury. LED: e-waste recycling where available. → *Expect:* fixture reassembled; old bulb routed to the right waste stream.

## Decision points

- Bulb flickers or the *fixture* is suspect (multiple bulbs died fast, scorch marks, buzzing) → stop; that's an electrician's problem, not a bulb problem.
- Recessed or specialty fittings (GU10 twist-lock, fluorescent tubes) → match the removal motion to the fitting: GU10 quarter-turn; tubes rotate 90° and drop — check the fitting type before pulling.
- Hard-to-reach fixture above stairs → don't improvise ladder placement on steps; use a proper stair platform or get help.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 New bulb doesn't light:** confirm the switch and any dimmer are on; test the new bulb in a known-good lamp; if the bulb works elsewhere, the socket/fixture is the fault — stop and get it checked.
- **F2 Old bulb stuck or seized:** grip with a rubber glove for torque; if it still won't turn, stop — forcing shatters glass into your palm. (Broken-base extraction with the *breaker off at the panel* is its own careful procedure.)
- **F3 Bulb breaks during removal:** step away, switch off at the breaker if the base is exposed, wear gloves, and extract the base with pliers turning counterclockwise; sweep — never bare-hand — the glass.
- **F4 Dimmer + LED flicker:** the LED isn't dimmable or the dimmer isn't LED-compatible — fit a "dimmable" marked LED or swap the dimmer.

## Verification

The fixture lights steadily from its own switch, the cover is back on, the ladder is put away, and the old bulb is in the correct waste stream — trash, or hazardous collection for CFLs.

## Variations

- Smart bulbs: after physical installation, the bulb needs pairing via its app; "doesn't light" may mean "not paired", not "broken".
- Halogen capsules run extremely hot — the cool-down in step 1 is mandatory, not cautious.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from three directions: falls (stable stool, no chairs), heat (cool-down), and electricity (switch off; breaker off for anything beyond a simple swap). CFL breakage adds mercury — ventilate and follow local cleanup guidance rather than vacuuming.
