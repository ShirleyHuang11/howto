## Goal

A loose doorknob or lever is tightened, aligned with the latch, and tested so it turns and latches without wobble.

## Preconditions

- You have a screwdriver set, small hex key set, flashlight, and threadlocker if the manufacturer allows it.
- The door is open and cannot accidentally lock you out.
- Do not overtighten screws into stripped wood or soft metal.
- Call a locksmith if the lock cylinder binds, keys stick, or security hardware is damaged.

## Steps

1. **Open the door.** Work with the door open and latch visible. → *Expect:* you can test the knob without being locked out.
2. **Find visible screws.** Look on the rose, base plate, or latch-side face for exposed screws. → *Expect:* the main tightening points are identified.
3. **Find a hidden set screw.** Check the underside or neck of the knob or lever for a small slot or hex screw. → *Expect:* the handle retaining screw is located if present.
4. **Expose the base plate.** [BRANCH: snap cover | threaded rose] gently pry or unscrew the cover plate if mounting screws are hidden. → *Expect:* the through-bolts are visible.
5. **Tighten through-bolts.** Hold both sides of the knob aligned and tighten screws evenly. → *Expect:* the knobs pull snug against the door faces.
6. **Tighten the set screw.** Seat the knob fully on the spindle and tighten the set screw. → *Expect:* the handle no longer slides on the spindle.
7. **Align the latch.** Turn the knob and confirm the latch retracts fully and enters the strike plate centered. → *Expect:* the latch moves without scraping.
8. **Adjust the strike if needed.** Tighten strike screws or shift the plate slightly within existing play. → *Expect:* the latch catches without pushing or lifting the door.
9. **Test operation.** Open and close the door several times from both sides. → *Expect:* the knob feels firm and the latch releases cleanly.

## Decision points

- Knob has no visible screws → look for a release slot and concealed mounting plate.
- Screws tighten but loosen again → add removable threadlocker if allowed by the hardware maker.
- Latch does not retract fully → inspect the internal latch mechanism or replace the set.
- Lock binds or key will not turn → call a locksmith.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Stripped screw:** screw spins in the door, use a longer screw only where it will not hit the lock body.
- **F2 Cover plate bends:** pry marks appear, stop and find the release notch or threaded direction.
- **F3 Latch sticks:** knob is tight but latch drags, loosen slightly and realign the two halves.
- **F4 Door will not latch:** strike is misaligned, tighten hinge screws or adjust the strike plate.

## Verification

The knob or lever has no side play, retracts the latch fully, and opens and closes from both sides five times without sticking.

## Variations

- Privacy knobs: check the emergency release hole after tightening.
- Exterior locksets: do not disturb keyed cylinders unless rekeying is intended.
- Lever handles: set screws are often on the underside near the neck.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: avoid locking yourself out or weakening security hardware. Keep the door open while testing and call a locksmith for lock-cylinder or exterior-security problems.
