## Goal

Mineral stains from hard water are dissolved and scrubbed off glass, fixtures, tile, or porcelain without damaging stone or plated finishes.

## Preconditions

- White vinegar or citric acid, water, cloths, non-scratch pad, and gloves.
- Dish soap for final cleaning.
- Knowledge of the surface material, especially whether it is natural stone.
- Ventilation if working in a small bathroom.

## Steps

1. **Identify the surface.** Confirm whether the stained area is glass, ceramic, chrome, stainless, plastic, or natural stone → *Expect:* acid-safe and acid-sensitive areas are separated.
2. **Protect stone and delicate finishes.** Keep vinegar and citric acid off marble, limestone, travertine, unsealed grout, and damaged plating → *Expect:* only acid-safe areas remain in the work zone.
3. **Pre-clean soap film.** Wash the area with dish soap and water, then rinse → *Expect:* greasy film is gone and mineral spots are easier to see.
4. **Apply acid dwell.** Lay vinegar-soaked cloths on the stains or spray a citric acid solution [BRANCH: light stains | heavy crust] → *Expect:* the stained area stays wet for 5 to 15 minutes.
5. **Scrub gently.** Use a non-scratch pad or soft brush while the deposit is still wet → *Expect:* white scale starts to smear, powder, or lift.
6. **Repeat for crust.** Reapply acid and dwell again rather than grinding hard → *Expect:* thick deposits soften layer by layer.
7. **Rinse thoroughly.** Flush the area with clean water and wipe away acidic residue → *Expect:* the surface no longer feels slick or smells strongly of vinegar.
8. **Dry and prevent.** Buff dry, then plan to squeegee or wipe after future use → *Expect:* fewer spots remain once water evaporates.

## Decision points

- Surface is natural stone → skip acid and use a stone-safe cleaner.
- Fixture finish is peeling or pitted → avoid abrasive pads and use only mild cleaner.
- Deposit is around a faucet base → soak a cloth and wrap it, but do not flood seams.
- Stains return quickly → consider wiping dry after use or addressing hard water with a filter or softener.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Etched stone:** detect dull marks after acid contact, recover by stopping and using stone polishing or a professional repair.
- **F2 Scratched glass or chrome:** detect visible scratch lines, recover by switching to a softer pad and avoiding powders.
- **F3 Stain unchanged:** detect no softening after dwell, recover by increasing dwell time or using citric acid instead of weak vinegar.
- **F4 Vinegar smell remains:** detect sharp odor after cleaning, recover by rinsing twice and ventilating.
- **F5 Rust mixed with scale:** detect orange-brown stains, recover with a rust-specific cleaner safe for the surface.

## Verification

After the surface is rinsed and dry, mineral spots are gone or visibly reduced without new dull patches, scratches, or finish damage.

## Variations

- Shower glass: keep cloths pressed against vertical spots so the acid does not run off.
- Faucet aerator: remove and soak separately if flow is weak.
- Toilet bowl ring: use citric acid or vinegar below the waterline only with good ventilation.
- Stone shower: use a stone-safe descaler rather than vinegar.

## Safety & privacy

Medium chemical and surface-damage risk. Never mix acidic cleaners with bleach, and test an inconspicuous spot before treating a large visible area.
