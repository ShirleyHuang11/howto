## Goal

A house spider relocated outdoors alive via the glass-and-card method, with the household's arachnophobe calmed and the whole operation over in two minutes.

## Preconditions

- A spider on a reachable, reasonably flat surface (wall, floor, ceiling-adjacent corner), and the two tools: a clear glass or jar, and a stiff piece of card or paper (postcard, cereal-box flap).
- Regional honesty check: in most temperate regions house spiders are harmless and this recipe applies freely; in regions with medically significant species (`au`, parts of `us`), identify before handling and default to more caution or a longer-handled tool.

## Steps

1. **Track, don't chase.** Keep the spider in sight while you fetch the tools; a spider lost behind the bookcase is tonight's suspense. Announce your plan to any distressed observers — narration genuinely calms rooms. → *Expect:* spider stationary or slow, tools in hand, audience upgraded from screaming to supervising.
2. **Place the glass over the spider in one smooth motion.** Approach slowly from behind its heading, then decisive placement, rim flush with the surface. Speed matters more at the end than the start; spiders bolt at looming shadows. → *Expect:* spider enclosed under the glass, unharmed, probably annoyed.
3. **Slide the card under the rim.** Ease one edge of the card under the glass, sliding slowly along the surface; the spider will step onto the card or up the glass wall as it advances. → *Expect:* the card fully underlapping the rim, forming a sealed floor; the spider contained between glass and card.
4. **Invert and carry with the card held firmly against the rim.** One hand pressing card to glass, walk to the door or window. Ceiling captures: same moves overhead with the glass pushed up and the card slid across — brace your forearm. → *Expect:* a portable spider observatory; children en route may inspect the passenger through the glass, which converts fear to biology in one generation.
5. **Release properly: outside, at ground level, near shelter.** A bush, woodpile, or wall base a few meters from the door; tip the glass and let it walk off — don't fling. → *Expect:* spider off into cover; glass and card retrieved.
6. **Debrief and wash the glass.** A rinse for the glass (or dishwasher), and the traditional closing statement to the household that the spider "was more scared of you." → *Expect:* equipment restored to drinking duty, household peace restored to baseline.

## Decision points

- Very fast or large specimens: a bigger vessel (large jar, upturned container) widens your margin, and a broom-guided walk into a dustpan-with-a-lid works for floor sprinters.
- Webs with resident spiders in low-traffic corners: the zero-effort option is diplomatic coexistence — the resident is eating your actual pest problem; relocation is for high-traffic and bedroom disputes.
- Winter releases: house spiders adapted to indoor life may not survive outside in freezing weather; a garage, shed, or cellar is the humane winter destination.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Spider bolted at the glass's shadow:** normal; track it to its next stop and approach lower and slower — glass angled ahead of its escape line works better than descending from above.
- **F2 A leg caught under the rim:** lift the glass a millimeter and re-place; the whole method's gentleness lives in unhurried rim placement.
- **F3 Spider vanished mid-operation:** leave the room's door closed and check the classic refuges (skirting shadows, under furniture edges) with a torch; failing that, concede the round publicly — it helps the household's morale to treat it as sport.
- **F4 Observer panic spiking mid-carry:** route around them, keep narrating ("contained, fully contained"), and complete the release before the retrospective; exposure at a safe distance is, quietly, how the fear shrinks.

## Verification

The spider left alive under its own power near outdoor shelter, no legs were harmed in the containment, the glass is washed, and the household's next spider encounter starts from a calmer baseline — the recipe's compounding return.

## Variations

- Commercial spider-catchers (long-handled bristle tools) do steps 2 to 4 at a meter's distance for ceiling work and the deeply reluctant.
- The same glass-and-card method relocates wasps and bees from windows: slower movements, and release *promptly* — a wasp's patience is not a spider's.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk in most regions; the identify-first rule covers the exceptions. The larger point the recipe smuggles in: the humane option is usually also the fastest one, and the household's spider policy is a small, teachable piece of its character.
