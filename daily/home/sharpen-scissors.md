## Goal

Household scissors cut paper or fabric cleanly again by improving the edge and checking the hinge tension.

## Preconditions

- Dull scissors that are not cracked, badly rusted, or serrated specialty shears.
- Fine sandpaper, aluminum foil, or a sharpening stone.
- A screwdriver or coin if the scissors have an adjustable screw.

## Steps

1. **Clean the blades.** Wipe off tape glue, lint, and old grime before judging sharpness. [BRANCH: sticky blades | truly dull blades] clean sticky blades first, sharpen only if cutting is still poor. → *Expect:* blades close without dragging on residue.
2. **Test on scrap.** Cut plain paper from heel to tip and note where it folds or tears. → *Expect:* the dull section is identified.
3. **Make several sandpaper cuts.** Cut fine sandpaper with the abrasive side facing out, using full strokes through the blade length. → *Expect:* the scissors feel slightly cleaner through paper.
4. **Use foil for light honing.** Fold aluminum foil several times and cut thin strips from heel to tip. → *Expect:* small burrs are knocked down and the cut improves modestly.
5. **Hone on a stone for better results.** Open the scissors, match the existing bevel angle, and stroke the beveled side along the stone from pivot to tip. → *Expect:* a fresh bright line appears along the cutting edge.
6. **Remove the burr.** Close the scissors once or twice, then wipe the blades. → *Expect:* no gritty metal dust remains.
7. **Check screw tension.** Tighten the pivot until blades meet along their length but still open smoothly. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* grinding the flat inner faces can ruin the scissor geometry, so sharpen only the beveled outside edge. → *Expect:* the blades cross with light pressure and no wobble.
8. **Retest on scrap.** Cut paper, thread, or fabric suitable for those scissors. → *Expect:* the cut is clean from heel to tip.

## Decision points

- Scissors are hair shears or expensive fabric shears → use a professional sharpener.
- Blades have nicks → sandpaper will not remove them; stone work or replacement is needed.
- Pivot is riveted and loose → sharpening cannot fully fix the wobble.
- Scissors cut only at the tip → tension or blade alignment is the likely problem.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Paper still folds:** detect by buckling between blades → tighten the pivot slightly and clean again.
- **F2 Edge feels rough:** detect by snagging fibers → make a few gentle foil cuts and wipe.
- **F3 Bevel rounded over:** detect by a shiny wide edge after stone work → stop and use professional sharpening.
- **F4 Screw too tight:** detect by handles sticking open → back off the screw a tiny amount.

## Verification

The scissors cut a clean continuous line through scrap material from the base of the blades to the tip.

## Variations

- Kitchen shears: separate the blades if designed to come apart, then clean before sharpening.
- Craft scissors: reserve one pair for paper and one for fabric after sharpening.
- Emergency fix: sandpaper cuts are fast, but they are maintenance, not a full sharpening.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from sharp blades. Cut away from fingers, keep the pivot stable, and do not test sharpness on skin.
