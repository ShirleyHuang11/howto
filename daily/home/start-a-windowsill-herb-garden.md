## Goal

A small windowsill herb garden is planted in draining pots, placed in enough light, watered correctly, and harvested in a way that keeps herbs growing.

## Preconditions

- Supplies: herb seedlings or seeds, pots with drainage, saucers, potting mix, watering can, scissors.
- A bright window is available, ideally 4-6 hours of sun for leafy herbs and more for rosemary or thyme.
- The sill can tolerate saucers or has a waterproof tray.

## Steps

1. **Choose herbs for the light you have.** [BRANCH: bright sun | moderate light] use basil, thyme, rosemary for sun; mint, parsley, chives for moderate light. → *Expect:* plant choices match the window.
2. **Prepare draining pots.** Fill pots with fresh mix and set them on saucers or a tray. → *Expect:* excess water has somewhere to drain without damaging the sill.
3. **Plant at the right depth.** Set seedlings at the same soil level, or sow seeds at packet depth. → *Expect:* crowns are not buried and seeds are covered lightly.
4. **Water to settle.** Water until a little drains out, then empty saucers after 15 minutes. → *Expect:* mix is evenly moist, not sitting in water.
5. **Place in strong light.** Put pots close to the glass without leaves touching cold or hot panes. → *Expect:* herbs get direct or bright indirect light for several hours.
6. **Check moisture by touch.** Water when the top 1-2 cm dries for most leafy herbs. → *Expect:* soil cycles from moist to slightly dry, not soaked.
7. **Harvest little and often.** Snip above a leaf pair and never remove more than one-third of a plant at once. → *Expect:* herbs branch instead of being stripped bare.
8. **Fix legginess early.** Rotate pots, pinch tips, and add a grow light if stems stretch toward the window. → *Expect:* growth becomes shorter and leafier.

## Decision points

- Window has less than 4 hours bright light → use a small grow light or choose mint, parsley, and chives.
- Basil flowers → pinch flowers immediately to keep leaves tender.
- Mint is included → give it its own pot because it spreads aggressively.
- Leaves yellow at the bottom → check for soggy soil and blocked drainage.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Seedlings flop:** light is too weak or watering is heavy → move brighter and let the surface dry slightly.
- **F2 Herbs taste weak:** growth is too lush or light too low → increase light and harvest younger tips.
- **F3 Soil gnats appear:** soil stays wet → let the top dry and bottom-water less often.
- **F4 Sill stains:** saucers overflow → empty after watering and use a waterproof tray.

## Verification

Each herb is in a draining pot on a protected sill, receives suitable light, has moist but not waterlogged soil, and can be harvested without removing more than one-third.

## Variations

- Seeds: start with basil, cilantro, parsley, and chives, but expect slower results than seedlings.
- Kitchen with no sun: use a timer grow light 10-14 hours daily.
- Hot south window: move delicate herbs slightly back from the glass at midday.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Confirm herbs are edible varieties, keep plant food away from children and pets, and do not let water run into outlets or painted woodwork.
