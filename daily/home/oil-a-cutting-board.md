## Goal

A wooden cutting board is cleaned, dried, saturated with food-safe mineral oil, and buffed so it resists drying and cracking.

## Preconditions

- You have food-safe mineral oil or board cream, clean cloths or paper towels, mild dish soap, and a dry work surface.
- The board is wood or bamboo, not plastic, glass, or sealed decorative wood.
- The board is not wet from washing or warped from soaking.
- Cooking oils such as olive, vegetable, or coconut oil are not used because they can turn rancid.

## Steps

1. **Clean the board first.** Wash with mild soap and warm water, then rinse quickly. → *Expect:* food residue and odors are gone without soaking the board.
2. **Dry it fully.** Stand the board on edge with air on both faces until it is dry to the touch. → *Expect:* no cool damp patches remain.
3. **Place it on a protected surface.** Lay down a towel or parchment and keep the bottle cap away from the oiled area. → *Expect:* drips will not stain the counter.
4. **Apply mineral oil generously.** Pour a small puddle and spread with the grain over the top, sides, handle cutouts, and end grain. → *Expect:* the wood darkens evenly as it drinks in oil.
5. **Wait for absorption.** Leave the board flat for 20 to 60 minutes. [BRANCH: dry board drinks oil fast | maintained board stays glossy] → *Expect:* dry areas appear where oil has soaked in.
6. **Add a second pass if needed.** Re-oil any pale or dry patches, especially end grain and knife-scarred areas. → *Expect:* the whole surface keeps a slight sheen for several minutes.
7. **Buff off excess.** Wipe firmly with a clean dry cloth until the board no longer feels greasy. → *Expect:* the board feels smooth, not slick, and no oil transfers heavily to a finger.
8. **Set a cadence.** Repeat monthly for regular use, or whenever water no longer beads on the board. → *Expect:* a simple maintenance trigger is chosen.

## Decision points

- If the board smells sour, clean and deodorize before oiling so odor is not trapped.
- If the board has deep cracks or black mold, retire it or sand and sanitize before oiling.
- If using board cream, apply it after mineral oil for a waxier water barrier.
- If the board is brand new and pale, expect two or three oiling rounds in the first week.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Used cooking oil:** detect sticky feel or stale smell later, recover by washing, drying, and switching to mineral oil.
- **F2 Board feels greasy:** detect oil transfer after buffing, recover by wiping again and letting it stand overnight on edge.
- **F3 Uneven color:** detect pale patches, recover by adding a small amount only to dry spots.
- **F4 Raised grain:** detect rough fibers after washing, recover by drying fully and sanding lightly before the next oiling.
- **F5 Counter stained:** detect oily marks under the board, recover by using dish soap promptly and protecting the surface next time.

## Verification

The dry board has an even slightly richer color, feels smooth but not greasy, and a few drops of water bead briefly instead of soaking in immediately.

## Variations

- Butcher blocks: use the same oil, but work in zones and let end grain absorb extra.
- Boards used daily: oil weekly until water beads reliably, then monthly.
- Very dry climates: shorten the cadence when edges look pale or fuzzy.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Use only food-safe products, keep oily cloths away from flames, and do not hide cracks, mold, or rancid smells with more oil.
