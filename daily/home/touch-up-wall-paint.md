## Goal

Small wall scuffs, chips, or patches blend into the surrounding paint by matching color, sheen, and texture.

## Preconditions

- You have matching paint or a paint chip for matching, a small brush or mini roller, clean cloth, mild cleaner, painter's tape, and drop cloth.
- The wall is dry and the damaged area is small enough for touch-up rather than full wall repainting.
- For lead paint risk in older homes, do not sand unknown paint without testing or professional guidance.

## Steps

1. **Identify the existing finish.** Check whether the wall is flat, eggshell, satin, semi-gloss, or gloss. → *Expect:* the touch-up paint sheen matches the wall.
2. **Confirm the color.** Stir stored paint well or get a store match from a clean chip. → *Expect:* paint color is as close as available.
3. **Clean the area.** Wipe grime with mild cleaner, rinse lightly, and let dry. → *Expect:* paint will stick to a clean dry surface.
4. **Repair tiny defects.** Fill small nail holes if needed, let filler dry, and sand only the patch smooth. → *Expect:* the surface feels even.
5. **Test in a hidden spot.** Apply a small dab where lighting is similar but less visible. → *Expect:* dry color and sheen are acceptable.
6. **Apply a thin coat.** [BRANCH: brush | mini roller] use a brush for chips and a mini roller for textured roller walls. → *Expect:* paint covers the defect without a raised blob.
7. **Feather the edge.** Lightly spread paint outward with less pressure at the perimeter. → *Expect:* there is no hard paint ridge.
8. **Let it dry fully.** Wait the label's recoat time and avoid judging wet paint. → *Expect:* color lightens or darkens to its dry appearance.
9. **Add a second thin coat if needed.** Cover only where the defect still shows and feather again. → *Expect:* coverage improves without building a patch edge.
10. **Check under room lighting.** Look from normal viewing angles in daylight and evening light. → *Expect:* the touch-up is not obvious during normal use.

## Decision points

- Paint sheen does not match → repaint the whole wall section or buy the correct sheen.
- Stored paint has lumps, rust, or sour smell → do not use it; get a fresh match.
- Patch is larger than a hand → rolling corner to corner may blend better than spot touch-up.
- Wall has smoke, grease, or water stains → prime first or address the source before painting.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Flashing:** touched area shines differently, use the correct sheen or repaint the whole wall plane.
- **F2 Color mismatch:** patch is visible after drying, get a better color match from a chip or repaint the section.
- **F3 Raised edge:** paint ridge catches light, let cure, sand lightly, and recoat thinly.
- **F4 Stain bleeds through:** discoloration returns, seal with stain-blocking primer before repainting.

## Verification

After drying under normal daylight and evening lighting, the repaired spot blends with the wall from typical viewing distance without visible ridges or sheen mismatch.

## Variations

- Textured walls: use the same roller nap or texture method as the original wall.
- Flat paint: touch-ups blend more easily but mark more easily later.
- Glossy paint: expect to repaint a larger area because sheen differences are obvious.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk: ventilate the room, keep paint off floors, and treat pre-1978 or otherwise unknown old paint as possible lead paint before sanding or scraping.
