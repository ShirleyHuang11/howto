## Goal

Hang string lights along a planned path so they are supported safely, powered correctly, and working before final use.

## Preconditions

- You have string lights, outdoor-rated extension cords if outside, hooks or clips, a ladder if needed, and the product label.
- The outlet is accessible and protected by GFCI when used outdoors or near damp areas.
- The mounting path avoids doors, walkways, fans, heaters, and sharp edges.
- The lights are not plugged in while being installed.

## Steps

1. **Test the lights first.** Plug them in at ground level, confirm every section works, then unplug them. → *Expect:* bad bulbs or dead strands are found before hanging.
2. **Plan the path.** Lay out the route and mark support points every few feet with enough slack for gentle curves. → *Expect:* the full span reaches the outlet without tight pulls.
3. **Check the rating.** [BRANCH: indoor | outdoor] use outdoor-rated lights and cords outside, and keep indoor-only lights indoors. → *Expect:* the label matches the location.
4. **Calculate the load.** Add the wattage of connected strands and compare it with the product limit and outlet circuit. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* overheated wiring can cause fire, so do not exceed listed limits or daisy-chain beyond the label. → *Expect:* the planned connection is within the safe limit.
5. **Install hooks or clips.** Fasten hooks into solid trim, posts, or anchors, not into the electrical cord. → *Expect:* each support point is firm when tugged lightly.
6. **Hang without staples.** Place the cord on hooks or clips and never staple, nail, pinch, or pierce it. → *Expect:* insulation is intact and the cord hangs freely.
7. **Keep plugs protected.** Position plug connections off the ground and away from puddles. → *Expect:* connections are dry and not strained.
8. **Secure loose spans.** Add supports where the strand sags into walking paths or rubs on a sharp edge. → *Expect:* lights stay clear of heads, doors, and abrasion points.
9. **Plug in and inspect.** Turn the lights on and walk the full route. → *Expect:* bulbs light evenly and no plug, cord, or socket feels warm after several minutes.
10. **Set normal use.** Add an outdoor-rated timer if desired and turn lights off when unattended for long periods. → *Expect:* the lights operate from a controlled, accessible switch or timer.

## Decision points

- The lights are for outdoor use → confirm weatherproof labeling on lights, extension cord, and timer.
- The outlet is not GFCI protected outside → use a GFCI outlet or portable GFCI adapter.
- The cord must cross a walkway → reroute overhead with enough clearance or choose another outlet.
- A socket, plug, or cord gets warm → unplug and reduce load before using again.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Dead strand after hanging:** detect by an unlit section → recover by unplugging, reseating bulbs or fuses, and replacing the strand if needed.
- **F2 Overloaded socket:** detect by warmth, flicker, tripped breaker, or label math over the limit → recover by unplugging and splitting the load.
- **F3 Damaged insulation:** detect by cuts, pinches, staples, or exposed wire → recover by discarding the damaged strand.
- **F4 Outdoor moisture risk:** detect by indoor-only labels or plugs lying in wet areas → recover with outdoor-rated gear and raised connections.

## Verification

The lights are supported by hooks or clips, rated for their location, within electrical load limits, and run for 10 minutes without flicker, heat, or tripping.

## Variations

- Brick or concrete: use masonry anchors or adhesive clips rated for the surface.
- Rental home: use removable outdoor clips where allowed by the product label.
- Solar string lights: place the panel where it receives direct sun and keep the same support rules.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because electricity, ladders, and outdoor moisture can cause injury or fire. Use outdoor-rated equipment outside, keep cords intact, avoid overloaded sockets, and have another person steady the ladder when practical.
