## Goal

A slow or blocked sink/shower drain flows freely again using mechanical methods first, without chemical injuries or a flooded cabinet.

## Preconditions

- A blocked or slow drain (sink, basin, or shower — toilets are a different procedure and tool).
- Basic tools as available: rubber gloves, a cup plunger, a drain snake/zip-it strip, bucket, old towel; optionally a wrench for the P-trap.
- **No chemical drain cleaner already poured** — if one was, do not plunge or open the trap; see Safety.

## Steps

1. **Diagnose the blockage's neighborhood.** Only this fixture slow → local clog (proceed). Multiple fixtures backing up, or gurgling elsewhere → main-line problem: stop, that's a plumber call. → *Expect:* confirmed single-fixture clog.
2. **Clear the strainer/stopper first.** Pop or unscrew the stopper; pull off surface hair and gunk (the zip-it strip excels here — insert, twist, pull). Shower clogs end at this step surprisingly often. → *Expect:* a satisfyingly disgusting wad removed; test the flow.
3. **Try hot (not boiling into porcelain) water + dish soap for grease clogs.** Kitchen sinks: a kettle of very hot water with a squirt of dish soap, poured in stages. → *Expect:* improvement between pours if it's grease; none → step 4.
4. **Plunge.** Block the overflow hole with a wet cloth (the plunge needs pressure), enough water to cover the cup, seal the plunger over the drain, 10–15 sharp push-pulls, then lift. → *Expect:* water suddenly swirling away = cleared; repeat once or twice before moving on.
5. **Open the P-trap (sink cabinets).** Bucket and towel *under the trap first*; unscrew the two slip nuts by hand or gently with the wrench; drop the trap and empty its contents into the bucket; probe the stub pipes with a finger/wire. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* jewelry lost down this sink lives in this trap — check the sludge before discarding it. → *Expect:* trap off, gunk out, visible clear passage through the trap body.
6. **Snake past the trap if still blocked.** Feed the snake into the wall stub until resistance, crank to bite the clog, pull back and clean the head; repeat until it runs free. → *Expect:* the snake returns with clog material, then meets no resistance.
7. **Reassemble and flush-test.** Trap back on (washers seated, nuts hand-tight plus a nudge), then run *hot* water full-bore for a minute while watching the trap joints. → *Expect:* fast drainage, dry joints under a finger-swipe.

## Decision points

- Chemical cleaner temptation → skip it: caustic products that *don't* clear the clog leave a chemical lake you must then work in, and they damage older pipes. Mechanical first, always.
- Trap nuts corroded solid → stop before rounding them off; penetrating oil and patience, or hand it to a plumber with the trap intact.
- Recurring clogs in the same drain → adopt a strainer basket (kitchen: no grease down the drain *ever* — jar it), and a monthly hot-water-flush habit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Plunged and dirty water erupted from the overflow/second basin:** you didn't seal the other opening — block it (wet cloth) and re-plunge; wipe down with disinfectant after.
- **F2 Trap reassembled but drips:** re-seat the cone washer (tapered side toward the joint) and square the nut threads; still dripping → the washer is cracked, a hardware-store part.
- **F3 Snake stuck:** rotate the *other* direction while pulling gently — never yank; if truly seized, unscrew the trap arm to free it.
- **F4 Cleared, but slow again within days:** you punched a hole through the clog rather than removing it — repeat with the snake, aiming to retrieve material, not just to make water pass.

## Verification

The fixture drains at full speed with no gurgle, the trap joints stay dry through a one-minute hot flush, tools are cleaned and gloves off with washed hands, and the recovered-valuables check (step 5 ⚠️) is done.

## Variations

- Bathroom basin pop-up stoppers: many unclip from the pivot rod under the basin — removing that pulls the whole hair mat at step 2.
- Flat/apartment: shared stacks mean your "local" clog may be building-level — report to the building manager rather than snaking past your own pipework.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: trap water is biologically foul (gloves; wash after); the real danger is chemical drain cleaner — never plunge or open a trap after pouring it (splash-back causes chemical burns), and never mix products. Boiling water belongs in metal pipes only — very hot (not boiling) for porcelain and PVC.
