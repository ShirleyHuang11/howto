## Goal

A screw with a damaged drive recess is removed without badly damaging the surrounding material.

## Preconditions

- Correct screwdriver bits, a drill or driver, rubber band, pliers, and optional screw extractor.
- Eye protection and good lighting.
- Replacement screw is available before reassembly.

## Steps

1. **Clean the head.** Pick out paint, dirt, or metal crumbs from the recess. → *Expect:* the bit can sit as deep as the damaged recess allows.
2. **Press in the right bit.** Try the correct size screwdriver by hand with firm downward pressure. → *Expect:* the bit catches or clearly slips.
3. **Use the rubber band trick.** Place a wide rubber band over the head and press the bit through it. → *Expect:* the rubber fills gaps and improves grip.
4. **Try a bigger bit.** Move up one bit size or switch from Phillips to a square or Torx bit if it bites. → *Expect:* the bit grabs without camming out.
5. **Back out slowly.** Turn counterclockwise with steady pressure and low speed if using a driver. → *Expect:* the screw rises slightly with each turn.
6. **Grip proud heads.** [BRANCH: head sticks above surface | head is flush] use locking pliers on proud heads, otherwise continue to extraction. → *Expect:* pliers can clamp flat sides if exposed.
7. **Use an extractor.** Drill the pilot side if required, then run the extractor counterclockwise. → *Expect:* the extractor bites and starts turning the screw.
8. **Replace the screw.** Remove debris and install a new screw of the same length and type. → *Expect:* the new screw seats firmly without slipping.

## Decision points

- Screw is in a finished visible surface → protect the area with tape before using pliers or extractors.
- Screw head is rusted → apply penetrating oil and wait 10 minutes.
- Screw will not move and material is valuable → stop before drilling deeper.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bit keeps slipping:** detect a spinning bit, recover by stopping before more damage and trying rubber band or larger bit.
- **F2 Head breaks off:** detect the head separating, recover by removing the part and gripping the remaining shank.
- **F3 Extractor snaps:** detect hardened metal stuck in the screw, recover by stopping and using pliers or professional help.
- **F4 Surface gouge:** detect tool marks around the screw, recover by taping the area and switching to hand tools.

## Verification

The stripped screw is fully out, the surrounding material still holds a replacement screw, and no broken tool tip remains in the hole.

## Variations

- Painted screw: score paint around the head before turning.
- Tiny electronics screw: use a fresh precision bit and skip power tools.
- Deck screw: impact drivers help only if the bit still has solid engagement.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk from sharp metal, drill slips, and flying fragments. Wear eye protection, clamp loose work, and do not force brittle extractor bits.
