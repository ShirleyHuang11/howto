## Goal

A single cracked ceramic or porcelain tile is removed and replaced with a matching tile, fresh adhesive, grout, and sealer.

## Preconditions

- You have a replacement tile, grout saw, cold chisel, hammer, scraper, thinset or tile adhesive, notched trowel, spacers, grout, sponge, sealer, gloves, and eye protection.
- The crack is limited to one tile and the surface below is stable and dry.
- Protect nearby fixtures and turn off water to nearby plumbing if impacts could contact pipes.
- Call a tile pro if many tiles are loose, the wall is wet or moldy, radiant heat is below the tile, or asbestos flooring may be present.

## Steps

1. **Protect the area.** Cover nearby surfaces and tape the edges of adjacent tiles. → *Expect:* surrounding tile and fixtures are shielded.
2. **Clear the grout.** Rake grout from all sides of the cracked tile without cutting into neighboring tile edges. → *Expect:* the tile is isolated by open joints.
3. **Break the damaged tile.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* confirm the replacement fits, then crack the bad tile inward from the center with light hammer taps. → *Expect:* fragments loosen away from adjacent tiles.
4. **Chip out pieces.** Use the chisel at a shallow angle and remove tile fragments from the center outward. → *Expect:* the old tile is gone and neighboring tiles remain intact.
5. **Scrape the substrate.** Remove old adhesive until the surface is flat and vacuum dust. → *Expect:* the new tile can sit level.
6. **Dry-fit the tile.** Place the new tile with spacers and check height and joint width. → *Expect:* the face is flush with nearby tiles.
7. **Set the tile.** Spread thinset or adhesive, press the tile in, wiggle slightly, and insert spacers. → *Expect:* the tile is aligned and supported evenly.
8. **Grout the joints.** After adhesive cures as directed, pack grout into the joints and tool them smooth. → *Expect:* joints are full and even.
9. **Clean and seal.** Wipe haze after grout firms, then seal when the grout cure time allows. → *Expect:* the repair is clean and protected.

## Decision points

- Tile lifts with damp backing or mold → stop and fix the water source before replacement.
- Replacement tile is thicker or thinner → adjust adhesive bed only within product limits.
- Adjacent tile loosens during chipping → expand the repair or call a pro.
- Floor may contain asbestos or old cutback adhesive → do not disturb it and get professional testing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Neighbor tile chips:** edge damage appears, stop hammering and use lighter chisel angles.
- **F2 New tile sits proud:** face is higher than surrounding tile, lift it before cure and scrape adhesive flatter.
- **F3 Hollow sound:** tile sounds unsupported after setting, remove before cure and reset with full coverage.
- **F4 Grout cracks:** joint opens after cure, remove loose grout and regrout after checking tile movement.

## Verification

After cure, the replacement tile is flush, firmly bonded, evenly grouted, sealed if required, and does not rock or sound hollow.

## Variations

- Shower tile: use materials rated for wet areas and repair waterproofing if damaged.
- Natural stone: use stone-safe adhesive, grout, and sealer.
- Large-format tile: back-butter the tile to improve coverage.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: broken tile is sharp and hidden water damage can spread. Wear eye protection and gloves, control dust, and call a pro for wet walls, asbestos risk, or unstable substrates.
