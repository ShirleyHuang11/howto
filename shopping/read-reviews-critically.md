## Goal

Use reviews to judge whether an item is likely to work for your specific need, instead of trusting the star rating alone.

## Preconditions

- You have the product page open on a store, marketplace, or review site.
- You know the feature or failure you care about most.
- You can sort, filter, or search within reviews.

## Steps

1. **Start with the distribution.** Note the number of reviews, the average rating, and whether the ratings cluster at five stars or spread across the range. → *Expect:* you can say whether the score comes from many buyers or only a thin sample.
2. **Sort by most recent.** Read the newest five to ten reviews before reading the top-rated ones. → *Expect:* recent comments mention current packaging, shipping, sizing, software version, or seller behavior.
3. **Filter for critical reviews.** [BRANCH: one-star and two-star | three-star] read complaints first, then compare them with neutral reviews. → *Expect:* you know the most common reasons buyers regret the purchase.
4. **Search for your use case.** Search terms like "wide feet", "apartment", "Android", "leaks", "battery", or the model name of your device. → *Expect:* at least one review addresses a situation close to yours, or none do.
5. **Check verified purchases.** Give more weight to reviews marked verified, especially when a product attracts fans, rivals, or free-sample reviewers. → *Expect:* the strongest evidence comes from people who likely bought the item.
6. **Spot fake-review patterns.** Look for repeated phrases, many reviews on the same date, vague praise, photos that look reused, or accounts reviewing unrelated items in the same wording. → *Expect:* suspicious clusters are separated from detailed buyer reports.
7. **Compare volume with quality.** Treat 4.7 stars from 30 vague reviews as weaker than 4.3 stars from 2,000 detailed reviews with consistent pros and cons. → *Expect:* your confidence reflects both sample size and detail.
8. **Read seller responses and updates.** Notice whether the seller solves defects, blames customers, or ignores repeated problems. → *Expect:* you know how the seller behaves after the sale.
9. **Make a buy, wait, or skip decision.** Write the top reason to buy and the top reason not to buy in one sentence each. → *Expect:* the decision is based on evidence you can point to.

## Decision points

- Recent reviews got worse after a redesign → prefer a different model or wait for more current feedback.
- Critical reviews all mention the same defect → assume the defect is real unless the seller documents a fix.
- Five-star reviews are vague but three-star reviews are detailed → trust the detailed middle ratings more.
- Verified reviews are rare on a marketplace listing → check another retailer or the manufacturer's site.
- Your use case is absent from reviews → treat the purchase as uncertain and favor easy returns.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Rating trap:** detect a high average with few details, recover by reading recent critical reviews before deciding.
- **F2 Fake-review cluster:** detect repeated wording or same-day praise, recover by ignoring that cluster and checking another source.
- **F3 Wrong-version evidence:** detect reviews for an older model, recover by filtering for the exact model number or current date range.
- **F4 Complaint overreaction:** detect one dramatic story with no repeats, recover by looking for pattern frequency.
- **F5 Review-site bias:** detect only affiliate-style praise, recover by checking owner forums, retailer returns, or independent tests.

## Verification

You can name the three most repeated strengths, the three most repeated complaints, and whether those complaints matter for your use case.

## Variations

- Marketplace listings: compare reviews for the seller and the product because both can affect the outcome.
- Local services: sort by recent and look for owner responses, missed appointments, and refund behavior.
- Apps and software: read reviews from the current major version, not years-old complaints.
- Big-ticket items: add independent lab tests or long-term owner forums before buying.

## Safety & privacy

Reviews can expose health details, addresses in photos, or usernames. Do not upload personal screenshots to ask strangers for advice unless account names, order numbers, and locations are hidden.
