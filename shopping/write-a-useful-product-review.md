## Goal

You publish a review that actually helps the next buyer decide: specific about your use, honest about pros and cons, grounded in how long you have used the item, and supported by a photo where it matters.

## Preconditions

- A product you have genuinely used (not just unboxed).
- The account or order that lets you post a verified review.
- Optionally, a photo or two of the item in real use.

## Steps

1. **Wait until you have real use behind you.** A review after one hour and a review after a month say different things; note which you are writing. → *Expect:* you can state roughly how long and how often you have used it.
2. **Lead with your use case and context.** Who you are as a user and what you needed it for (e.g. "daily commuter backpack for a 15-inch laptop"). Context lets readers judge relevance. → *Expect:* the first lines say what you used it for and for how long.
3. **Give a balanced pros and cons.** List concrete strengths and concrete weaknesses; a review with only praise or only complaints is less trusted and less useful. → *Expect:* at least one specific pro and one specific con.
4. **Be specific, not vague.** Replace "great quality" with measurable detail: battery lasted two days, strap frayed after three weeks, runs true to size. → *Expect:* claims tied to observable facts, numbers, or timeframes.
5. **Report failures and durability honestly.** If it broke, say when and how, and whether support helped. This is often the single most valuable part for the next buyer. → *Expect:* any defect or wear is described with its timing.
6. **Add a photo or short video where it clarifies.** Show scale, color accuracy, a flaw, or the item in real use. → *Expect:* an attached image that shows something words cannot.
7. **Rate consistently with your text.** Do not write a glowing paragraph and give two stars (or the reverse); the star count should match the words. → *Expect:* star rating and written sentiment agree.
8. **Disclose anything that biases you.** Free/discounted unit, replacement, or gift. Skip fake-sounding superlatives and keep it in your own words. → *Expect:* any incentive is stated; tone reads as a real person.
9. **Proofread and submit.** → *Expect:* the review posts and appears under the correct product.

## Decision points

- Problem is really shipping or seller service, not the product → put it in a seller review, not the product's star rating, so future buyers judge the item fairly.
- Received the item free in exchange for a review → disclose it; many platforms require this and readers weight it accordingly.
- Still early in ownership → say so and consider updating later rather than overstating durability now.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Review rejected by moderation:** detect a policy notice → remove prohibited content (external links, contact info, profanity, other brand names) and resubmit.
- **F2 Wrong-variant confusion:** detect your review would mislead about size/color → state exactly which variant you bought so the rating maps to the right option.
- **F3 Reads as fake:** detect generic superlatives with no specifics → rewrite with concrete details and your actual timeline; specificity is what signals authenticity.
- **F4 Opinion changed after longer use:** detect the item degraded or improved → edit or add a dated update rather than leaving a stale first impression.

## Verification

The published review states your use case and duration, contains at least one concrete pro and one concrete con with specific detail, carries a star rating consistent with the text, and discloses any incentive.

## Variations

- Verified-purchase badges: reviews tied to a real order carry more weight; post from the account that bought it.
- Update culture: for durability-sensitive items (electronics, shoes), a dated long-term update adds outsized value.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not include personal information, your address, or order numbers in a public review, and do not post photos that reveal your home or documents in the background. Keep it honest; fabricated or paid-for reviews can violate platform rules and consumer-protection law.
