## Goal

Get a retailer to match a lower eligible price before or shortly after buying an item.

## Preconditions

- You know the exact item, including brand, model, size, color, and bundle contents.
- You have the competitor price visible on a public page, ad, or receipt.
- You can access the store's price-match policy.

## Steps

1. **Find the store policy.** Search the retailer name plus "price match policy" and open the current official policy page. → *Expect:* you know which competitors, item types, and time windows are eligible.
2. **Match the exact item.** Compare model number, color, size, quantity, warranty, and included accessories. → *Expect:* the lower-priced item is identical, not just similar.
3. **Confirm stock and seller.** Check that the competitor has the item in stock and sold by an eligible seller, not a third-party marketplace seller if excluded. → *Expect:* the competitor page shows available inventory and an acceptable seller.
4. **Capture proof.** Save a screenshot or keep the live page, ad, or printed flyer with date, price, and item details visible. → *Expect:* you can show the lower price without searching again at the counter or chat window.
5. **Choose the right channel.** [BRANCH: before purchase | after purchase] ask at checkout before paying, or contact support within the policy's adjustment window after purchase. → *Expect:* you are using the channel the policy allows.
6. **Ask plainly.** Say, "Can you price match this exact item to this competitor price?" and show the proof. → *Expect:* the associate or agent checks the policy instead of treating it like a coupon.
7. **Review exclusions.** Watch for exclusions such as clearance, bundles, membership prices, coupon prices, flash sales, refurbished items, or financing offers. → *Expect:* you know why the match is approved or denied.
8. **Confirm the final amount.** Check tax, shipping, and any price adjustment line before agreeing. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* if you pay, confirm the adjusted total first because refunds may require a separate return or support case. → *Expect:* the receipt or cart shows the matched price.
9. **Keep the receipt.** Save the receipt and proof until the return window closes. → *Expect:* you can follow up if the adjustment disappears or posts incorrectly.

## Decision points

- The lower price is online-only → use chat or online checkout if the store excludes in-store matching.
- The store only matches before purchase → do not buy first unless you accept the listed price.
- The item is sold by a third party → find a direct retailer listing or skip the match.
- The proof is a coupon or member-only price → check whether coupon stacking or membership pricing is excluded.
- The price dropped at the same store after purchase → ask for a price adjustment, not a competitor match.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Model mismatch:** detect different SKU or bundle contents, recover by finding the exact SKU or abandoning the claim.
- **F2 Expired proof:** detect an old ad or ended sale, recover by using a live page or current dated flyer.
- **F3 Channel mismatch:** detect an in-store refusal for an online-only policy, recover by contacting the correct support channel.
- **F4 Tax surprise:** detect the matched price but higher total, recover by comparing subtotal separately from tax and shipping.
- **F5 Agent uncertainty:** detect vague refusal without policy citation, recover by asking which policy line applies.

## Verification

The final receipt, cart, or refund confirmation shows the lower matched item price for the exact item.

## Variations

- In-store: bring the product to the service desk and keep the competitor page ready on your phone.
- Online chat: paste the URL and attach the screenshot only if the agent asks.
- After purchase: note the purchase date and policy deadline before opening a support case.
- Local ads: the printed flyer may work when a web listing does not show local stock.

## Safety & privacy

Do not send full payment card numbers or account passwords to prove a price. Only share order number, item link, and the minimum receipt detail needed for the adjustment.
