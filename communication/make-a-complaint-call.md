## Goal

You reach a company or service by phone, state a complaint clearly and calmly, and obtain a concrete resolution or a documented path to one.

## Preconditions

- The account/order/reference number and any relevant dates and amounts.
- The correct phone number (from an official statement or the company's site, not a search-ad number).
- A quiet place, something to take notes with, and time to hold.

## Steps

1. **Assemble evidence before dialing.** Order number, dates, amounts, prior email/chat references, and a one-sentence statement of what went wrong and what you want. → *Expect:* a short written list you can read from under pressure.
2. **Define your target outcome and your fallback.** E.g. "full refund" with a fallback of "credit plus fee waiver". → *Expect:* two acceptable outcomes written down before you call.
3. **Call and navigate the menu to a human.** Note the time you called. Pressing 0, saying "agent", or choosing "cancel service" often routes to a person fastest. → *Expect:* connected to or queued for a live representative.
4. **Get the agent's name and location at the start.** "Who am I speaking with, and can I get a reference number for this call?" → *Expect:* a name and, ideally, a case/ticket number written in your notes.
5. **State the complaint in three parts: what happened, the impact, what you want.** Keep it factual and brief; then stop talking and let them respond. → *Expect:* the agent restates the issue and begins working it.
6. **Stay calm and firm; separate the problem from the person.** The agent did not cause it and can be your ally. If you feel anger rising, slow your speech rather than raise your voice. → *Expect:* a working, cooperative tone; the agent proposes a resolution or next step.
7. **If the answer is inadequate, climb the escalation ladder.** Ask: "I understand your limit here. May I speak with a supervisor?" Note each name and time. → *Expect:* transfer to someone with more authority, or a firm commitment with a reference number.
8. **Confirm and log the resolution.** Repeat back the agreed action, amount, and timeframe, and ask for written confirmation by email. → *Expect:* a clear commitment (who, what, by when) plus a case number and a promised email.

## Decision points

- Front-line agent has no authority → request a supervisor politely but explicitly; do not re-argue the same point louder.
- No resolution after supervisor → ask for the complaints/retention department, or the regulator/ombudsman route, and say you will follow up in writing.
- Call drops mid-resolution → your case/reference number is why you took it; call back and quote it rather than starting over.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Endless hold:** use a callback option if offered; otherwise call at opening time when queues are shortest, and put the call on speaker while you do something else.
- **F2 You lose your temper:** an abusive caller gets minimal help; if you slip, apologize once, restate the facts, and continue, or ask to call back after a break.
- **F3 Agent promises but nothing happens:** the reference number, name, and time you logged let you escalate the broken promise on the next call.
- **F4 Runaround between departments:** ask each transfer to note the case number so you are not re-explaining; if it loops, request a supervisor to own it end to end.

## Verification

You have a written record of the call (agent name, time, case number) and either a completed resolution or a specific commitment with a date, ideally confirmed by a follow-up email.

## Variations

- `regulated-utility-or-bank`: mention you are aware of the formal complaints procedure and the ombudsman; regulated firms track these and often escalate faster.
- `written-follow-up`: if the phone stalls, switch channels and send a dated written complaint referencing the call, which starts a formal clock in many jurisdictions.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Verify you dialed the official number (scam call-back numbers impersonate support lines). Never read out a full card number or password; legitimate agents ask only for limited verification digits.
