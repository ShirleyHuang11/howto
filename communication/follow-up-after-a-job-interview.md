## Goal

Send a professional follow-up after a job interview that thanks the interviewer, reinforces fit, and asks about timing when appropriate.

## Preconditions

- Interviewer name, email or messaging channel, and the role title.
- Notes from the interview or one specific topic you discussed.
- The date of the interview and any hiring timeline they mentioned.

## Steps

1. **Send within 24 hours.** Draft the note the same day if possible, or the next morning. → *Expect:* the follow-up arrives while the conversation is still fresh.
2. **Use a clear subject.** Reply to the existing thread or use a subject like `Thank you - [Role] interview`. → *Expect:* the recipient can identify the interview quickly.
3. **Open with thanks.** Thank them for their time and name the role. → *Expect:* first line is direct and professional.
4. **Reference something specific.** Mention a project, challenge, team goal, or detail from the conversation. → *Expect:* the note does not read like a template.
5. **Reinforce one fit point.** Connect your experience to one need they described. → *Expect:* the message adds useful context without repeating your whole resume.
6. **Ask about timeline only if needed.** If no timeline was given, ask when they expect next steps. If they gave one, acknowledge it. → *Expect:* the timing question is polite and not pushy.
7. **Keep it short.** Limit the message to one or two brief paragraphs. → *Expect:* the note can be read in under a minute.
8. **Proofread and send.** Check name spelling, company name, and role title before sending. → *Expect:* no copied or incorrect details remain.
9. **Wait before a second follow-up.** If the stated timeline passes, wait 1 to 2 business days before checking in again. → *Expect:* you have a date for the next reasonable follow-up.

## Decision points

- Multiple interviewers → send separate notes when you can personalize each one.
- Recruiter coordinated the process → copy or separately thank the recruiter if they were directly involved.
- You forgot a detail → keep the note simple rather than inventing specificity.
- You need to correct an answer → add one concise clarification without apologizing at length.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Too generic:** detect no reference to the conversation, recover by adding one true detail from your notes.
- **F2 Too eager:** detect repeated messages before the timeline, recover by waiting until the agreed date has passed.
- **F3 Wrong recipient detail:** detect misspelled name or wrong role, recover by correcting before send, or sending a brief correction if already sent.
- **F4 Overlong note:** detect several dense paragraphs, recover by cutting to thanks, specific reference, fit, and timeline.

## Verification

The follow-up was sent within 24 hours, includes a specific interview reference, and either acknowledges or politely asks about the hiring timeline.

## Variations

- Panel interview: send one group note only if individual emails are unavailable.
- Final interview: add a brief sentence about continued interest in the role.
- No email available: send through the recruiter or application portal messaging system.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not disclose confidential work examples, personal pressure, or competing-offer details unless strategically necessary and true.
