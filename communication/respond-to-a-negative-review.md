## Goal

A public response to a negative review that acknowledges the customer, protects the business's credibility, and moves detailed repair offline.

## Preconditions

- You have authority to respond for the business or organization.
- You have read the review, checked available records, and know any privacy limits.
- You can offer a next contact point or remedy process if appropriate.

## Steps

1. **Pause before replying.** Read the review twice and wait until you can answer without arguing. → *Expect:* the response is professional, not reactive.
2. **Verify what you can.** Check order, appointment, staff notes, photos, or policies without exposing private details. → *Expect:* you know whether apology, clarification, or investigation is needed.
3. **Thank the reviewer.** Open with a brief thank-you for feedback, even if the review feels unfair. → *Expect:* readers see respect before defense.
4. **Acknowledge the concern.** Name the issue in neutral language: wait time, damaged item, billing confusion, rude interaction, or missed expectation. → *Expect:* the reviewer can tell you read the actual complaint.
5. **Apologize where due.** Say sorry for the experience or for the specific failure you can confirm. → *Expect:* responsibility is taken without inventing facts.
6. **Offer to fix offline.** [BRANCH: known customer | unknown reviewer] If you can identify them, invite them to contact a named channel; if not, ask them to reach out with details. → *Expect:* private information leaves the public thread.
7. **Keep it short and final.** Do not debate, reveal records, mention bans, or accuse the reviewer publicly. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* public replies can be screenshotted and amplified, so write for future customers as much as the reviewer. → *Expect:* the response improves trust even if the rating remains.
8. **Log the issue internally.** Record the review, response, owner, and follow-up task. → *Expect:* the complaint can lead to operational repair.

## Decision points

- If the review alleges safety, discrimination, fraud, or harassment, escalate internally before posting details.
- If the review is fake or abusive, use the platform's report process and still consider a neutral public note.
- If the business was clearly wrong, apologize plainly and avoid "if you felt" language.
- If staff are named, protect employee privacy while investigating.
- If the reviewer updates their review, update the internal log and avoid pressuring them.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Defensive reply:** detect it when the response says "actually" or blames the customer → rewrite around thanks, acknowledgment, and repair.
- **F2 Privacy breach:** detect it when order, health, payment, or identity details appear → remove immediately if possible and seek platform or legal guidance.
- **F3 Endless debate:** detect it when replies repeat the same dispute → give one final offline contact and stop.
- **F4 Empty apology:** detect it when no next step exists → add a contact channel or internal corrective action.

## Verification

The public response thanks the reviewer, acknowledges the issue, apologizes where appropriate, offers offline follow-up, and avoids private details.

## Variations

- Restaurant: mention service standards and invite direct contact with a manager.
- Medical or legal practice: never confirm someone is a client or patient.
- Marketplace seller: include order support channel and replacement or refund process.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because public replies affect reputation and may expose protected information. Never disclose customer records, employee discipline, medical details, or payment information.
