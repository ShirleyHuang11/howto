## Goal

Write a short, professional resignation letter that states the resignation, final working date, notice period, thanks, and transition intent.

## Preconditions

- You know your manager's name, employer name, and required notice period.
- You have checked your contract, handbook, or local rules for resignation requirements.
- You have chosen the final working date you intend to give.

## Steps

1. **Confirm notice requirements.** Check contract, handbook, union agreement, or local law before drafting. → *Expect:* the minimum notice period is known.
2. **Open with the decision.** State clearly that you are resigning from your position. → *Expect:* the first paragraph cannot be mistaken for a complaint or negotiation.
3. **State the final date.** Include the calendar date of your last working day. → *Expect:* HR can calculate the notice period without guessing.
4. **Keep reasons brief or omit them.** If you include a reason, make it neutral and factual. → *Expect:* the letter does not burn bridges.
5. **Add thanks.** Mention appreciation for the opportunity, team, or experience in one sentence. → *Expect:* the tone remains professional even if circumstances are mixed.
6. **Offer transition help.** Say you will help document work or hand over responsibilities during the notice period. → *Expect:* the employer knows you intend an orderly exit.
7. **Review for risky language.** Remove blame, threats, jokes, or confidential details. → *Expect:* the letter would still look appropriate if forwarded to HR.
8. **Send through the right channel.** [BRANCH: email | printed letter | HR portal] deliver it according to company practice after telling your manager if appropriate. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* submitting resignation may trigger offboarding, so confirm final date and decision before sending. → *Expect:* the manager or HR receives the resignation.
9. **Save proof.** Keep a copy and any acknowledgement. → *Expect:* you can prove date, wording, and delivery.

## Decision points

- Contract requires written notice to HR → send to HR and copy the manager if policy allows.
- Relationship is tense → use shorter wording and avoid explanations.
- You want a counteroffer → do not use a resignation letter as a negotiation tactic unless you accept that it may be accepted.
- Final date falls on a holiday or weekend → state the last working day explicitly.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ambiguous resignation:** detect wording like "thinking of leaving," recover by stating the decision directly.
- **F2 Wrong notice date:** detect final date shorter than required, recover by revising before sending or asking HR to confirm.
- **F3 Bridge-burning tone:** detect blame or emotional detail, recover by cutting to facts and thanks.
- **F4 Missing record:** detect no sent copy or acknowledgement, recover by saving email headers or requesting confirmation.
- **F5 Confidential disclosure:** detect client, salary, or internal issue details, recover by removing them.

## Verification

The letter states resignation, position, final working date, notice period compliance, thanks, and transition intent in a professional record you saved.

## Variations

- Immediate resignation: state the effective date and keep reasons minimal, but understand contract and pay consequences.
- Internal transfer: follow internal process and coordinate timing before formal resignation from the old role.
- Retirement: include retirement date and benefits contact details if HR requests them.
- Contractor: reference the contract notice clause instead of employee handbook language.

## Safety & privacy

Medium career and income risk. Do not include grievances, protected medical details, trade secrets, or future employer details unless required.
