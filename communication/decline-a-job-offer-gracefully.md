## Goal

Decline a job offer promptly and professionally while preserving goodwill with the recruiter, hiring manager, and future version of the opportunity.

## Preconditions

- A written or verbal job offer you have decided not to accept.
- Any deadline, compensation details, and negotiation history reviewed.
- Confidence that declining is final, or a clear counteroffer request if it is not final.

## Steps

1. **Make the decision cleanly.** Confirm you are declining, not negotiating, stalling, or waiting for another answer you still need. → *Expect:* you know whether the next message is a decline or a request for more time.
2. **Respond promptly.** Decline as soon as the decision is firm, ideally before the deadline. → *Expect:* the employer can move to another candidate without unnecessary delay.
3. **Use the right channel.** If the relationship was mostly email, email is enough; if a recruiter invested heavily, a brief call plus written confirmation may be kinder. [BRANCH: email only | call then email] choose call for close relationships or senior roles; choose email for standard processes. → *Expect:* the decline is clear and documented.
4. **Lead with gratitude.** Thank them for the offer, time, conversations, and specific thing you appreciated about the team or role. → *Expect:* the tone recognizes their effort.
5. **Give a brief reason.** Keep it simple: accepted another role, timing, compensation, location, scope, or fit. Do not litigate every concern. → *Expect:* they understand the category without receiving a teardown.
6. **State the decision plainly.** Use "I have decided to decline the offer" rather than soft language that invites confusion. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once you decline, the role may be offered to someone else immediately, so confirm before sending. → *Expect:* there is no ambiguity about acceptance.
7. **Keep the door open if true.** Say you hope to stay in touch or would welcome future paths crossing, but only if you mean it. → *Expect:* the relationship closes with respect.
8. **Send written confirmation.** After any call, send a short email restating the decision and thanks. → *Expect:* both sides have a record and no one is left guessing.

## Decision points

- If money is the only blocker → negotiate before declining, not after.
- If you need more time → ask for a specific extension before the deadline.
- If the process was poor → stay factual and brief; save detailed feedback for a requested survey if you choose.
- If you signed paperwork already → review obligations before withdrawing.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Message sounds apologetic and unclear:** detect phrases like "I think maybe" → replace with a direct decision sentence.
- **F2 You overexplain:** detect a long list of criticisms → cut to one neutral reason.
- **F3 Recruiter pushes back:** detect attempts to reopen the decision → thank them and repeat that your decision is final.
- **F4 You delayed too long:** detect the deadline has passed → respond immediately, apologize for the delay, and make the decision clear.

## Verification

The employer receives a prompt written decline that includes gratitude, a clear decision, a brief reason, and no ambiguous acceptance language.

## Variations

- Declining after negotiation: acknowledge the effort they made to improve the offer.
- Declining an internal role: thank the manager and preserve the working relationship with extra care.
- Declining for another offer: no need to name the company unless useful and comfortable.

## Safety & privacy

Medium career risk because tone can affect references and future hiring. Do not share confidential offer terms casually, disparage interviewers, or imply legal commitments you have not reviewed.
