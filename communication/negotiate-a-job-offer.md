## Goal

Negotiate a job offer respectfully by getting terms in writing, evaluating the whole package, countering clearly, managing deadlines, and closing gracefully.

## Preconditions

- You have a verbal or written offer and know the role, level, location, and start date.
- You know your minimum acceptable compensation and top priorities.
- You can compare market data and benefits with your current situation.

## Steps

1. **Get the offer in writing.** Ask for salary, bonus, equity, benefits, start date, title, manager, location, and deadline. → *Expect:* the negotiable terms are visible in one document or email.
2. **Evaluate the whole package.** Include base pay, bonus probability, equity terms, insurance, retirement, time off, remote policy, commute, and growth. → *Expect:* you know the real value and tradeoffs.
3. **Pick negotiation priorities.** Choose 1 to 3 items that matter most, such as base salary, signing bonus, start date, or remote days. → *Expect:* the counter is focused.
4. **Ground the counter.** Use market data, competing constraints, experience, or role scope to justify the request. → *Expect:* the employer hears a business reason, not a demand.
5. **Write the counter respectfully.** State enthusiasm, ask for specific changes, and invite discussion. → *Expect:* the tone preserves the relationship.
6. **Manage the deadline.** If you need time, ask for an extension before the deadline and give a reason. → *Expect:* the employer confirms the active decision window.
7. **Review the revised offer.** [BRANCH: improved | unchanged | worse] compare every term against the original and your minimum. → *Expect:* you know whether to accept, counter again, or decline.
8. **Accept only in writing.** Confirm the final terms and start date before resigning from a current job. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* resignation and relocation decisions can be costly, so confirm final written terms first. → *Expect:* the accepted offer matches what you agreed.
9. **Decline gracefully if needed.** Thank them, state you are declining, and avoid criticism or unnecessary detail. → *Expect:* the relationship remains professional.

## Decision points

- Offer is below minimum → counter once clearly or decline instead of accepting resentment.
- Employer says salary is fixed → ask about signing bonus, review timing, level, vacation, or start date.
- Deadline is too short → request a specific extension and continue communicating.
- Competing offer exists → mention it truthfully without revealing confidential documents.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Verbal-only terms:** detect missing written offer, recover by asking for written details before negotiating.
- **F2 Counter too broad:** detect a list of many demands, recover by narrowing to the highest-value items.
- **F3 Deadline missed:** detect expired response window, recover by contacting the recruiter immediately and asking if the offer remains open.
- **F4 Hidden tradeoff:** detect higher salary but worse benefits or commute, recover by recalculating total value.
- **F5 Burned bridge:** detect harsh decline language, recover by rewriting with thanks and neutral reasoning.

## Verification

You have a final written offer or written decline record that reflects the negotiated terms, deadline handling, and professional close.

## Variations

- Early career: negotiate politely, but prioritize role quality and learning if pay bands are rigid.
- Senior role: include scope, title, equity, severance, and decision authority.
- Relocation: negotiate moving costs, temporary housing, and start date together.
- Public sector: pay may be fixed, but classification or step placement may still matter.

## Safety & privacy

Medium financial and career risk. Do not fabricate competing offers, share confidential offer letters broadly, or resign until the final accepted terms are written.
