## Goal

A parcel is packed to survive transit, correctly addressed and labeled, handed to a carrier with tracking, and it arrives.

## Preconditions

- The item, a box or padded mailer sized to it, and cushioning (paper, bubble wrap) for anything that can rattle.
- Recipient's full address and a phone number (couriers increasingly require one).
- A carrier decision pending (post office vs. private courier) and payment.

## Steps

1. **Pack to the shake test.** Item centered with ~3 cm cushioning on all sides; fill voids until *nothing shifts when shaken*; heavy items in strong boxes, fragiles double-boxed if they matter. → *Expect:* a shaken box that sounds like a solid object, not maracas.
2. **Seal with real packing tape.** H-pattern: along the center seam and across both edge seams, top and bottom. (Not household sticky tape, not string — string jams machinery.) → *Expect:* seams fully covered; flaps immobile.
3. **Address it.** Recipient block (name, street, unit, city, postal code, country, phone) large and flat on the *top* face; your return address smaller, top-left or back. Remove/black out any old labels and barcodes on a reused box. → *Expect:* one unambiguous address block; zero stale barcodes.
4. **Choose the service.** [BRANCH: post office (cheap, fine for non-urgent) | courier (faster, door pickup, better tracking) | drop-off point/locker networks (cheapest hybrid)] Compare on the two axes that matter: deadline and value. Insure above the carrier's default liability if the contents warrant. → *Expect:* service chosen with a known price, ETA, and insurance level.
5. **Buy the label and declare honestly.** At the counter, kiosk, or online (online is usually cheaper — print or QR at drop-off). International: the customs form wants contents, value, and category truthfully — vague or false declarations park parcels in customs. → *Expect:* label affixed flat on the top face; customs form attached in its pouch if international.
6. **Hand over and keep the proof.** Counter, pickup, or locker; take the receipt with the tracking number. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* once in the stream, contents are committed — the last look inside happens before step 2's tape. → *Expect:* receipt + tracking number saved; first scan appears within hours.
7. **Send the tracking number to the recipient and watch delivery.** They can catch failed-delivery notices on their end. → *Expect:* tracking progresses to "delivered"; recipient confirms.

## Decision points

- Prohibited/restricted contents (batteries, liquids, aerosols, food across borders) → check the carrier's list *before* packing; lithium batteries especially have specific rules, not a general ban.
- Value high enough to hurt → insurance + signature-on-delivery; and photograph the packed contents before sealing (claim evidence).
- Deadline critical (documents, gifts) → pay for the dated service and send days earlier than the promise anyway; promises are medians, not guarantees.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Tracking stalls:** carriers' own inquiry process starts the trace — sender (you) must usually open it, not the recipient; have the receipt's numbers ready.
- **F2 Arrived damaged:** recipient photographs box + contents *before* discarding packaging; you file the claim with those photos plus your pre-seal photos and the insurance receipt.
- **F3 Returned to sender (address issue, refused, customs):** the sticker states why; fix the cause (address correction, missing customs info, recipient contact) and re-send — postage is consumed, lesson is cheap.
- **F4 Size/weight surcharge shock at the counter:** carriers price by volumetric weight too — a big light box costs like a heavy one; repack smaller or accept it; next time measure before choosing the box.

## Verification

The receipt's tracking number is saved and shared, tracking reached "delivered", and the recipient confirms contents arrived intact — or a documented claim/trace is in motion.

## Variations

- Marketplace sales (resale platforms): the platform's prepaid label defines the carrier and insurance — your job reduces to the pack, the shake test, and the drop deadline.
- Locker-to-locker networks (`eu`, `cn` widespread): cheapest and queue-free; size classes are strict — measure first.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to property, not people. Honest customs declarations are also a legal matter, not just a delivery one; and the address block reveals your home — a work or pickup-point return address serves for sensitive sends.
