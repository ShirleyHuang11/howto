## Goal

Write a concise cover letter that connects your experience to a specific role and is ready to submit with a job application.

## Preconditions

- The job posting, your resume, and the employer name.
- A place to draft and export text, such as a document editor or application form.
- One or two examples of relevant work, projects, or outcomes.

## Steps

1. **Read the role for signals.** Highlight the job title, required skills, team mission, and repeated words. → *Expect:* a short list of 3 to 5 role priorities.
2. **Choose the main match.** Pick the strongest reason you fit this role, not every reason you are qualified. → *Expect:* one sentence can explain why this application makes sense.
3. **Open with the role and fit.** Name the position and state the relevant strength you bring. → *Expect:* first paragraph identifies the job and avoids generic filler.
4. **Show, do not tell.** Use one concrete example with action, context, and result instead of claiming you are passionate or hardworking. → *Expect:* the reader can picture what you did.
5. **Tie the example back to the employer.** Explain how that experience helps with the role's stated needs. → *Expect:* the letter feels tailored to this posting.
6. **Keep the length controlled.** Aim for 250 to 400 words, usually 3 or 4 short paragraphs. → *Expect:* the letter fits on one page with readable spacing.
7. **Close with next action.** Thank the reader and say you would welcome a conversation. → *Expect:* ending is polite and does not pressure the employer.
8. **Proofread in layers.** Check names, job title, spelling, dates, and copied text from old letters. → *Expect:* no wrong company name, wrong role, or obvious typo remains.
9. **Save in the requested format.** Export PDF unless the application asks for plain text or a document file. → *Expect:* filename and format are ready for upload.

## Decision points

- Posting asks for a specific prompt → answer that prompt first and keep the cover-letter structure secondary.
- You lack a listed requirement → address adjacent experience briefly and focus on what transfers.
- Applying by email → use a shorter letter in the email body and attach the resume.
- Strong referral → mention the person only if they agreed to be named.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Generic letter:** detect sentences that could fit any employer, recover by adding role-specific language from the posting.
- **F2 Resume repetition:** detect copied bullet points, recover by turning one bullet into a short story with outcome.
- **F3 Too long:** detect more than one page, recover by cutting background and keeping one strongest example.
- **F4 Wrong details:** detect old company, role, or hiring manager names, recover by proofreading after every reuse.

## Verification

The final letter names the correct role and employer, includes one specific evidence-based example, stays under one page, and has been proofread against the posting.

## Variations

- Career change: lead with transferable work and explain the pivot in one clear sentence.
- Internal application: connect prior company knowledge to the new team's needs.
- Required text box: paste plain text and recheck line breaks before submitting.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Do not include private salary history, protected health details, immigration details, or references' contact information unless the application explicitly asks.
