## Goal

Ask someone to serve as a professional or academic reference in a way that is clear, respectful, and easy for them to accept.

## Preconditions

- A short list of possible references who know your work well.
- The role, program, or opportunity you are applying for.
- Your current resume and any materials that would help the reference speak accurately.

## Steps

1. **Choose the right person.** Pick someone who can speak to relevant work, reliability, judgment, or growth. → *Expect:* the person has direct experience with you.
2. **Ask early.** Contact them before listing their name, ideally at least 1 to 2 weeks before a deadline. → *Expect:* they have time to decide and prepare.
3. **Make the request direct.** Ask whether they would be comfortable giving a positive reference for this opportunity. → *Expect:* they can say yes, no, or not this time.
4. **Give the context.** Include the role, organization, deadline, and what the reference may be asked to do. → *Expect:* they understand the commitment.
5. **Provide supporting material.** Send your resume, job description, key projects, and 2 or 3 points they might mention. → *Expect:* they do not need to reconstruct your history from memory.
6. **Confirm contact details.** Ask which email, phone number, title, and organization they prefer you to list. → *Expect:* the application information is accurate.
7. **Thank them either way.** Reply promptly with appreciation, whether they accept or decline. → *Expect:* the relationship stays respectful.
8. **Notify before each use.** Tell them when you submit their name and what timeline to expect. → *Expect:* they are not surprised by a call or email.
9. **Update after the result.** Share the outcome and thank them again. → *Expect:* they know their help mattered and are not left wondering.

## Decision points

- The person hesitates → choose someone else rather than pushing for a weak reference.
- Application wants references immediately → ask before submitting if time allows; otherwise do not list someone you cannot reach.
- Need a letter instead of a call → provide the deadline, submission link, and any formatting rules.
- Reference is from years ago → include a brief reminder of your shared work and current direction.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Lukewarm reference:** detect vague or hesitant response, recover by thanking them and asking another person.
- **F2 Missing deadline:** detect no confirmation near the due date, recover with one polite reminder and a backup reference.
- **F3 Wrong contact info:** detect bounced email or outdated title, recover by confirming preferred details before submission.
- **F4 Reference surprised:** detect they did not know they were listed, recover by apologizing, sending context, and avoiding repeat use without notice.

## Verification

The reference has explicitly agreed, has the opportunity details and materials, and you have confirmed their preferred contact information.

## Variations

- Academic reference: include course, term, papers, grades if relevant, and program deadline.
- Internal transfer: ask whether they can speak confidentially within the organization.
- LinkedIn recommendation: ask for a short public recommendation only if it fits the opportunity.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Share only application-relevant materials and do not expose a reference's private contact details beyond the application process.
