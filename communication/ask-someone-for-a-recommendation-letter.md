## Goal

A recommender is asked early, respectfully, and given enough material to write a specific letter by the deadline.

## Preconditions

- You know the opportunity, deadline, submission method, and what qualities the letter should address.
- You have a resume, transcript or work summary if relevant, draft statement, and reminder of your work with the recommender.
- You have chosen someone who can speak well and specifically about you.

## Steps

1. **Ask early.** Aim for at least three to four weeks before the deadline, more for busy seasons. → *Expect:* the person has real time to say yes and write.
2. **Ask in person or live if appropriate.** [BRANCH: in person | email] Ask in person or on a call when the relationship is active; use email when distance, schedule, or formality makes that better. → *Expect:* the ask feels personal and easy to answer.
3. **Make yes or no safe.** Say "Would you feel comfortable writing a strong recommendation for me?" rather than assuming. → *Expect:* they can decline without damaging the relationship.
4. **Give the core details.** Include opportunity name, deadline, submission link, recipient, and whether the letter is confidential. → *Expect:* logistics are not hidden in follow-up messages.
5. **Provide useful material.** Send your resume, draft statement, relevant work, achievements, and a short note reminding them what you did together. → *Expect:* they can write with specific examples.
6. **Suggest focus areas lightly.** Mention two or three qualities the opportunity values, without scripting praise. → *Expect:* the recommender knows what would help.
7. **Send reminders respectfully.** Check in one week before the deadline, then two days before if there is still no confirmation. → *Expect:* the deadline stays visible without pestering.
8. **Do not submit their name casually.** ⚠️ *Irreversible:* some systems email recommenders immediately, so get consent before entering their contact information. → *Expect:* no one receives a surprise automated request.
9. **Thank them and update them.** Send a thank-you after submission and later share the outcome. → *Expect:* the relationship is maintained beyond the transaction.

## Decision points

- If they hesitate, ask whether another recommender would be better and accept the answer.
- If the deadline is under a week away, acknowledge the short notice and offer an easy decline.
- If they ask you to draft the letter, follow the rules of the institution or employer.
- If you need multiple letters, track each recommender separately.
- If the portal fails, send alternate instructions before the deadline.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Weak recommender:** detect it when they cannot recall your work → choose someone closer to the relevant evidence.
- **F2 Missing materials:** detect it when they ask basic questions → send one organized packet immediately.
- **F3 No submission confirmation:** detect it near the deadline → send a concise reminder with the link and due date.
- **F4 Surprise conflict:** detect it when they disclose a policy or time issue → thank them and ask another recommender promptly.

## Verification

The recommender agreed, has the deadline and submission instructions, received supporting material, and was thanked after submitting.

## Variations

- Professor: include course, semester, grade if relevant, papers, and office-hour context.
- Manager: include role, dates, projects, outcomes, and job description.
- Scholarship: include mission fit and the traits the committee requests.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Share only materials the recommender needs, and do not pressure someone to write a confidential letter they are not comfortable writing.
