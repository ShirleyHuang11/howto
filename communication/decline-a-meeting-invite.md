## Goal

Decline a meeting invite while preserving the work relationship and, when useful, offering a better way to handle the topic.

## Preconditions

- The meeting invite, agenda or topic, organizer name, and your calendar constraints.
- A sense of whether your attendance is required for a decision.
- Access to the calendar or messaging tool where you will respond.

## Steps

1. **Check the purpose.** Read the title, notes, attendees, and any attached agenda. → *Expect:* you know whether the meeting needs you specifically.
2. **Decide the response path.** [BRANCH: decline | tentative | propose new time] Choose decline when you cannot or should not attend. → *Expect:* your calendar action matches your actual availability.
3. **Respond quickly.** Decline as soon as you know, especially for small meetings. → *Expect:* the organizer has time to adjust.
4. **Keep the reason optional.** Give a brief reason only if it helps, such as conflict, focus block, or not the right owner. → *Expect:* message stays concise and does not overexplain.
5. **Offer an alternative when needed.** Suggest async notes, a delegate, a decision doc, or one alternate time. → *Expect:* the organizer has a next step besides losing your attendance.
6. **Protect focus respectfully.** If declining for priority work, say you are preserving time for a committed deadline. → *Expect:* the message frames the decline around work impact.
7. **Use the calendar response.** Click decline and add a note if the tool allows it. → *Expect:* the event is removed or marked declined on your calendar.
8. **Follow up separately if important.** Send a short chat or email if the calendar note may be missed. → *Expect:* the organizer sees the context before the meeting.

## Decision points

- You are optional → decline without a long explanation and ask for notes if needed.
- You own a decision item → offer a delegate, written input, or a shorter replacement discussion.
- Meeting has no agenda → ask for the desired outcome before accepting a reschedule.
- Recurring meeting no longer needs you → ask to be removed from the series.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Organizer thinks you ignored it:** detect no response or only deleting the event, recover by sending a calendar decline with a note.
- **F2 Too much explanation:** detect personal details in the note, recover by replacing with a brief work-relevant reason.
- **F3 Work blocked by absence:** detect decision waits on you, recover by sending input or naming a delegate before the meeting.
- **F4 Declined wrong instance:** detect whole series declined by mistake, recover by reaccepting the series and declining only the intended occurrence.

## Verification

The calendar shows the invite as declined or tentative as intended, and the organizer has either a short reason or a practical alternative.

## Variations

- External meeting: be slightly more formal and include one useful alternative.
- Manager meeting: include enough context to show priority tradeoffs.
- Large informational meeting: decline and ask for recording or notes if they exist.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid sharing private medical, family, or sensitive calendar details unless you intentionally choose to disclose them.
