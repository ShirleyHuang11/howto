## Goal

You leave a short, clear voicemail that tells the listener who called, why, and how and when to reach you back, in under 30 seconds.

## Preconditions

- The correct number and the name of the person or office you are calling.
- Your callback number and the times you are reachable.
- A quiet spot; a one-line note of your reason if the call matters.

## Steps

1. **Plan the message before it beeps.** Jot four things: your name, your number, the reason, the callback window. → *Expect:* a four-point note in front of you.
2. **Wait for the beep, then greet and name yourself immediately.** "Hi, this is [full name] calling for [person/office]." → *Expect:* your name is the first content, not buried after rambling.
3. **State your phone number slowly, early.** Say it digit by digit at dictation speed. → *Expect:* a listener could write it down on first hearing.
4. **Give the reason in one or two sentences.** Concrete and specific: what you need and any reference number. → *Expect:* the listener knows why you called without guessing.
5. **State when you are reachable (the callback window).** "I'm available today until 5 p.m., or tomorrow morning." → *Expect:* the listener knows a good time, reducing phone tag.
6. **Repeat your name and number a second time at the end.** This is the one repetition that matters; the listener may have missed it the first time. → *Expect:* name and number given exactly twice, bookending the message.
7. **Close and hang up cleanly.** "Thanks, again this is [name] at [number]. Talk soon." Press end; do not trail off. → *Expect:* message ends crisply, total length under ~30 seconds.

## Decision points

- Voicemail is for a business/office → include your account or reference number and keep it strictly factual.
- Sensitive or complex matter → leave only enough to prompt a callback ("calling about my account, please call me back"), not the full details.
- No callback needed (pure FYI) → say so explicitly: "no need to call back, just letting you know."

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 You rambled and ran long:** many systems let you press # or a key to review and re-record; listen for the "to re-record press..." prompt.
- **F2 Number said too fast:** re-record if you can; otherwise the end-of-message repeat is your safety net, so slow down there.
- **F3 You froze at the beep:** hang up before speaking and call back with your four-point note ready; a blank or stammered message is worse than a second call.
- **F4 Mailbox full or not set up:** you cannot leave one; send a text or email instead and note that the mailbox was unavailable.

## Verification

The message is under ~30 seconds and contains, in order, your name, number (spoken slowly), a clear reason, a callback window, and a closing repeat of name and number.

## Variations

- `personal-casual`: warmth over structure, but still lead with your name and end with your number if a callback is wanted.
- `professional-cold-call`: add the company name and a one-line value reason; keep it even tighter, and never leave more than one voicemail without a gap of a day or two.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Assume a voicemail may be overheard or forwarded: do not leave passwords, full card numbers, or private medical details. For sensitive topics, leave only a callback request.
