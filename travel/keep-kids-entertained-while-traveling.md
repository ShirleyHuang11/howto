## Goal

Prepare and use snacks, offline media, small activities, surprise items, and breaks so children stay occupied during a trip.

## Preconditions

- You know the travel duration, wait times, and baggage limits.
- You know each child's age, food restrictions, motion-sickness risk, and screen limits.
- You have a small bag that can stay within reach during travel.

## Steps

1. **Break the trip into time blocks.** Divide travel into boarding, first hour, meal, quiet time, arrival wait, and delays. → *Expect:* you know when entertainment is most needed.
2. **Pack tidy snacks.** Choose filling, low-mess foods and a spill-resistant drink if allowed. → *Expect:* snacks are portioned and easy to reach.
3. **Download offline media.** Save shows, audiobooks, music, or games before leaving reliable Wi-Fi. → *Expect:* content opens in airplane mode.
4. **Add small activities.** Pack stickers, drawing pad, magnetic games, cards, fidgets, or a short book. → *Expect:* activities fit on a tray table or lap.
5. **Create a surprise bag.** Hold back a few cheap new items and reveal them one at a time. → *Expect:* novelty is available during the hardest stretch.
6. **Plan movement breaks.** [BRANCH: airport/station | car stop | train aisle] identify safe times to walk, stretch, or use restrooms. → *Expect:* children get breaks before restlessness peaks.
7. **Rotate, do not dump.** Offer one item at a time and switch only when interest fades. → *Expect:* the bag lasts across the trip instead of ten minutes.
8. **Use screens deliberately.** Save screens for long waits, delays, or quiet periods when other options are spent. → *Expect:* screen time solves the hardest period, not the easiest one.
9. **Reset before arrival.** Collect wrappers, small pieces, headphones, and comfort items before disembarking. → *Expect:* the seat area and activity bag are under control.

## Decision points

- Child gets motion sick → avoid reading or close-up screens during moving car or bus segments.
- Trip overlaps nap time → prioritize comfort object, quiet audio, and a window or low-traffic seat.
- Security or customs limits food → pack sealed snacks and know what must be discarded before arrival.
- Multiple children share items poorly → make separate small kits to avoid conflict.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Downloads missing:** detect media will not play offline, recover by switching to audio, drawing, or preloaded games.
- **F2 Snack spill:** detect wet clothes or sticky seat, recover with wipes, spare bag, and a dry backup snack.
- **F3 Boredom spike:** detect escalating restlessness, recover by using a surprise item or planned movement break.
- **F4 Lost small pieces:** detect toy parts falling under seats, recover by choosing contained activities for the rest of the trip.
- **F5 Battery drained:** detect low device charge, recover by using power bank or switching to non-screen activities.

## Verification

The child has accessible snacks, offline entertainment, small activities, a reserved surprise item, and a planned break for each long segment of the trip.

## Variations

- Road trip: schedule stops before everyone is desperate, not after.
- Flight: use headphones that fit the child and comply with cabin announcements.
- Train: walking breaks are easier, but small pieces can roll away.
- Toddler: prioritize repetition, snacks, and safe sensory objects over complex games.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Avoid choking hazards for young children, respect nearby travelers with volume control, and do not let children use unlocked devices with payment apps or private messages open.
