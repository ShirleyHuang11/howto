## Goal

Keep a baby as comfortable and calm as possible during a flight while protecting feeding, sleep, changing, and caregiver bandwidth.

## Preconditions

- Diapers, wipes, spare clothes, feeding supplies, pacifier if used, and any medicine are in the personal item.
- You know airline rules for lap infants, car seats, bassinets, milk, formula, and stroller gate-checking.
- The baby is healthy enough to fly or has clinician clearance when needed.

## Steps

1. **Pack the change plan on top.** Put one diaper kit, wipes, changing pad, spare outfit, bag for soiled clothes, and caregiver shirt where one hand can reach it. → *Expect:* a blowout can be handled without unpacking the whole bag.
2. **Board with realistic timing.** [BRANCH: preboard | board later] preboard if you need to install a car seat or organize supplies, or board later if less cabin time helps the baby. → *Expect:* the boarding choice matches your setup burden.
3. **Feed or offer sucking on ascent.** Nurse, bottle-feed, or offer a pacifier during taxi and climb when ears may pressurize. → *Expect:* swallowing helps equalize ear pressure.
4. **Keep sleep cues familiar.** Use the normal sleep sack, song, pacifier, bottle rhythm, or white-noise setting at a respectful volume. → *Expect:* the baby recognizes a portable version of bedtime.
5. **Control stimulation.** Dim the screen, reduce toy rotation, and avoid passing the baby among excited relatives during tired windows. → *Expect:* fussing does not escalate from overload.
6. **Use the walk when safe.** When the seat belt sign is off, walk the aisle briefly, bounce gently near your row, or stand at the galley edge with crew permission. → *Expect:* movement resets fussing without blocking service.
7. **Change before crisis.** Use a lavatory changing table early when the diaper is clearly wet or dirty, not after the baby is fully upset. → *Expect:* the baby returns to the seat cleaner and calmer.
8. **Feed or offer sucking on descent.** Start before the final steep descent if possible, not after crying begins. → *Expect:* ear discomfort is reduced during landing pressure changes.
9. **Use patience as a tool.** Keep your voice low, accept that crying happens, and solve one need at a time: ears, hunger, diaper, temperature, sleep. → *Expect:* caregiver calm keeps the situation manageable.

## Decision points

- Baby is asleep before descent → do not wake solely for ear pressure unless they usually struggle; have pacifier or bottle ready.
- Seat belt sign stays on → soothe in place with feeding, pacifier, rocking motion in arms, and low voice.
- Formula or milk needs warming → ask crew what is possible, but carry a backup the baby will accept at room temperature.
- You are traveling solo → keep the aisle seat if possible and ask crew for timing help before opening the changing kit.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Ear pain crying:** crying spikes during climb or descent → feed, offer pacifier, keep baby upright, and stay calm.
- **F2 Diaper blowout:** clothes or seat area are soiled → use the top-packed kit, bag soiled items, and ask crew for trash guidance.
- **F3 Missed nap:** baby becomes overtired → reduce stimulation, use familiar cues, and stop trying new toys every minute.
- **F4 Supplies out of reach:** bag is overhead during turbulence → ask crew when it is safe to retrieve it, then keep essentials under the seat.
- **F5 Caregiver overload:** you are too stressed to problem-solve → pause, breathe, and ask your travel partner or crew for a simple assist.

## Verification

The baby completes the flight with feeding, diapering, and sleep needs handled, and any crying is addressed without unsafe movement or missing critical supplies.

## Variations

- Car seat onboard: install according to airline and seat instructions, usually window seat to avoid blocking exit.
- Bassinet flights: request early and bring a backup sleep plan because bassinets are limited.
- Short flights: skip elaborate toys and focus on ears, diaper, and one feed.
- Long-haul flights: rotate quiet activities slowly and protect caregiver rest when another adult is available.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Follow crew instructions during turbulence, keep hot liquids away from the baby, sanitize changing surfaces, and do not post boarding passes or infant identity documents in travel photos.
