## Goal

Reduce motion sickness before and during travel, and recover quickly if nausea starts.

## Preconditions

- You know the travel mode: car, bus, train, boat, or plane.
- You have water and a light snack available.
- Optional: ginger, acupressure bands, or an over-the-counter motion sickness medicine you have safely used before.

## Steps

1. **Choose the most stable seat.** Pick the front passenger seat in a car, over the wing in a plane, midship on a boat, forward-facing near the front on a bus, or a forward-facing train seat. → *Expect:* your body feels fewer turns, pitches, and visual conflicts.
2. **Face the direction of travel.** Avoid backward-facing seats unless the vehicle is very smooth. → *Expect:* your eyes and inner ear receive more consistent motion cues.
3. **Look at the horizon or far road.** Keep your gaze outside and far away, not on the floor, phone, tray table, or passing objects close to the window. → *Expect:* nausea pressure stays lower because your visual field matches the motion.
4. **Time ginger or medicine before symptoms peak.** [BRANCH: ginger | medicine] take ginger 30-60 minutes before travel, or take motion sickness medicine exactly as labeled, often 30-60 minutes before departure. → *Expect:* prevention is active before the first rough segment.
5. **Create steady airflow.** Open a vent toward your face, crack a window when safe, or step onto a deck only where permitted. → *Expect:* cool moving air reduces heat, stale smells, and nausea buildup.
6. **Skip reading and screen work.** Put the book, laptop, and scrolling phone away during movement; use audio instead. → *Expect:* symptoms do not spike from near-focus visual conflict.
7. **Eat light and plain.** Choose crackers, toast, banana, rice, or small sips of water; avoid heavy, greasy, spicy, or alcoholic intake before and during travel. → *Expect:* your stomach feels settled rather than full or irritated.
8. **Reset early if symptoms start.** Stop talking, loosen tight clothing, cool your face, breathe slowly, and return your gaze to the horizon. → *Expect:* nausea plateaus instead of racing toward vomiting.
9. **Recover after arrival.** Sit still in fresh air, sip water, and eat bland food only after the spinning feeling fades. → *Expect:* balance and appetite return gradually without a second wave.

## Decision points

- You must drive → do not take sedating medicine; use airflow, horizon gaze, light food, and stops instead.
- Symptoms happen on boats → choose outside air near the center of the vessel and avoid enclosed cabins if safe.
- You need to read for work → wait for straight smooth segments and stop at the first hint of nausea.
- You are pregnant, treating a child, older, or on other medication → ask a clinician or pharmacist before using medicine.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Took medicine too late:** nausea starts before it works → stop screens, cool down, hydrate in sips, and let the medicine catch up.
- **F2 Seat is assigned badly:** you are near the back or facing backward → ask crew or another passenger politely for a swap before departure.
- **F3 Strong smell triggers nausea:** fuel, perfume, food, or restroom odor is nearby → move seats, aim airflow at your face, or use a clean mask as a filter.
- **F4 Vomiting starts:** you cannot suppress it → use a bag, rinse your mouth, sip water slowly, and rest before eating.
- **F5 Drowsy from medication:** you feel impaired → do not drive, operate equipment, or make tight connections without help.

## Verification

You complete the trip with nausea controlled or recovering within 30 minutes of arrival without unsafe medication use.

## Variations

- Cars: the front passenger seat and a smooth driver matter more than any accessory.
- Planes: the wing area moves less, and cool overhead air helps during taxi and descent.
- Cruises and ferries: midship, lower decks, horizon view, and fresh air are the usual winning combination.
- Children: use snacks, airflow, window view, and frequent breaks before medicine unless a clinician has advised it.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Motion sickness medicine can cause drowsiness and interact with alcohol or other drugs. Confirm labels before use, keep sick bags accessible, and do not let embarrassment delay asking for fresh air, a seat change, or a stop.
