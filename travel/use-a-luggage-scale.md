## Goal

Measure luggage accurately before travel and adjust bags before an airline counter or train platform makes it expensive.

## Preconditions

- You have a handheld luggage scale or a bathroom scale.
- You know the baggage allowance for each bag on your ticket.
- Bags are mostly packed, including liquids, shoes, chargers, gifts, and return-trip items.

## Steps

1. **Set the scale units.** Choose pounds or kilograms to match the airline allowance. → *Expect:* the display uses the same unit as your ticket rules.
2. **Zero the scale.** Turn it on with nothing pulling the hook or strap. → *Expect:* the display reads zero before the bag is attached.
3. **Hook the main handle.** Thread the strap or hook through the strongest top handle, then close the clasp fully. → *Expect:* the bag hangs from a secure point without slipping.
4. **Lift with legs and a straight wrist.** Raise the bag just high enough to clear the floor and hold still for two seconds. → *Expect:* the number locks or stabilizes.
5. **Read the weight at eye level.** If the display swings, lower the bag and repeat once. → *Expect:* two readings are within a small margin.
6. **Compare against the strictest allowance.** Leave a buffer of at least 0.5-1 kg or 1-2 lb for scale differences and last-minute items. → *Expect:* the bag is clearly under, close, or over.
7. **Redistribute before paying.** Move dense items such as books, shoes, chargers, and toiletries into a lighter checked bag, personal item, or jacket pockets if allowed. → *Expect:* the overweight bag drops below the limit without losing essentials.
8. **Use the carry-on trick carefully.** Weigh carry-on and personal item too, especially on airlines that enforce cabin weight. → *Expect:* the bag you plan to carry is not rejected at the gate for weight.
9. **Reweigh after every change.** Do not trust the first reading once items move. → *Expect:* final weights are known for each bag.

## Decision points

- Bag is only slightly over → redistribute dense items before buying more allowance.
- Bag is far over → pay for a higher allowance online if cheaper than airport fees, or remove items.
- Airline has both size and weight limits → a light but oversized bag can still fail, so check dimensions.
- Return trip includes purchases → pack outbound below the limit or bring a packable bag.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Scale reading jumps:** the bag swings or the battery is weak → repeat on a firm stance, change batteries, or use a bathroom scale method.
- **F2 Hook slips:** handle shape does not hold → use the strap around the handle instead of balancing on a hook.
- **F3 Overweight at airport:** the airline scale is stricter → move dense allowed items to personal item or pay before the bag is accepted.
- **F4 Carry-on rejected:** gate staff weighs or sizes it → remove valuables and medicine before gate-checking.
- **F5 Back strain:** the bag is too heavy to lift safely → use two people or a bathroom scale method.

## Verification

Every bag has a recent measured weight under its applicable allowance with a small buffer before leaving for the airport or station.

## Variations

- Bathroom scale method: weigh yourself, weigh yourself holding the bag, then subtract.
- No scale: use airline self-service scales at the airport before joining the bag-drop line.
- Multi-airline trips: use the lowest allowance across all carriers on the itinerary.
- Group travel: pool checked-bag allowance only when the airline explicitly permits it.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Lift only what you can control, keep toes clear if the bag drops, and do not move medications, passports, batteries, or valuables into checked luggage just to fix weight.
