## Goal

Submit a travel visa application with the right documents early enough to reduce the risk of denial, delay, or wasted travel costs.

## Preconditions

- You know your destination, citizenship, trip purpose, approximate dates, and passport expiration date.
- You can access the official embassy, consulate, or immigration website for the destination country.
- You have time before travel for forms, appointments, mailing, and processing.
- You can pay application fees through an official channel.

## Steps

1. **Check requirements early.** Use the official destination government site, not ads or third-party summaries, to confirm whether you need a visa. → *Expect:* visa type, eligibility, fee, and processing estimate are known.
2. **Confirm passport validity.** Check blank page rules and how many months of validity are required after entry or departure. → *Expect:* your passport meets the stated rule or you know it must be renewed first.
3. **Build the document pack.** Gather passport, photo, application form, itinerary, accommodation, funds proof, employment or school proof, invitation letter, and travel insurance if required. → *Expect:* every required item is present in the requested format.
4. **Avoid non-refundable bookings when possible.** Use refundable reservations or hold off until the visa is issued if the rules allow. → *Expect:* a denial or delay would not waste major prepaid costs.
5. **Complete the application carefully.** Match names, passport number, dates, addresses, and trip purpose exactly to documents. → *Expect:* the form has no mismatched identity or travel details.
6. **Book the appointment or mailing slot.** [BRANCH: consulate appointment | visa center | mail-in] choose the required submission route and calendar it with travel time. → *Expect:* you have a confirmation number and deadline.
7. **Attend or submit with copies.** Bring originals, copies, photos, payment proof, and appointment confirmation; keep tracking for mailed passports. → *Expect:* the application is accepted for processing or you receive a clear missing-item notice.
8. **Monitor processing.** Track status only through official tools and avoid buying final tickets until the visa and passport are returned. → *Expect:* you know whether the application is pending, approved, refused, or needs more documents.
9. **Verify the issued visa.** Check name, passport number, entries, validity dates, duration of stay, and work or study restrictions. → *Expect:* errors are caught before departure.

## Decision points

- Requirements differ by citizenship or residence → use the rule for the passport you will travel on.
- Processing time is close to departure → consider expedited service only if offered officially.
- The itinerary includes multiple countries → check transit and re-entry visa rules.
- A prior denial, overstay, or criminal issue exists → seek qualified immigration advice before applying.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong visa type:** detect by trip purpose not matching the application, recover by stopping and applying for the correct category.
- **F2 Missing document:** detect by checklist gap or intake rejection, recover by obtaining the document before resubmission.
- **F3 Passport trapped too long:** detect by processing exceeding travel needs, recover by contacting the official office and avoiding new travel commitments.
- **F4 Visa data error:** detect by misspelled name or wrong dates on issuance, recover by requesting correction before travel.

## Verification

The visa application is accepted or issued, the passport and visa details match your trip, and no non-refundable major booking depends on an unissued visa.

## Variations

- Electronic visa: save the approval PDF and check whether it must be printed.
- Visa on arrival: confirm airline boarding rules because airlines may require proof before departure.
- Business visit: invitation and employer letters often need exact purpose and dates.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because applications expose passports, finances, biometrics, and travel plans. Use official sites, beware visa scams, and do not mail a passport without tracking.
