## Goal

Board a plane smoothly, keep the aisle moving, and reach your seat with essentials accessible.

## Preconditions

- You have a boarding pass, ID or passport if required, and an assigned or valid seat.
- Carry-on and personal item comply with the airline's size and item limits.
- Medicines, documents, valuables, and in-flight essentials are separated from any bag that might be gate-checked.

## Steps

1. **Check the boarding group system.** Read the boarding pass and gate screen for group, zone, row, or priority rules. → *Expect:* you know when to approach without blocking earlier groups.
2. **Prepare before your group is called.** Put ID away if no longer needed, hold boarding pass ready, and move headphones, water, medicine, charger, and layers into the personal item. → *Expect:* you will not unpack in the aisle.
3. **Decide on gate-checking early.** [BRANCH: keep carry-on | gate-check carry-on] if overhead space looks tight and the airline offers free gate-check, remove valuables and batteries first. → *Expect:* any checked bag contains nothing critical.
4. **Join only when called.** Stand near the lane only for your group and keep the walkway clear. → *Expect:* the scanner line moves without crowding the gate.
5. **Scan and clear the doorway.** Board pass-first, then walk onto the jet bridge without stopping to repack. → *Expect:* passengers behind you keep moving.
6. **Move to your row with the bag in front.** Carry the roller low and controlled, backpack forward or in hand, and avoid hitting seated passengers. → *Expect:* you reach the row without snagging armrests or people.
7. **Stow fast.** Place the roller wheels-first or as crew directs, use nearby space only if your row is full, and put the personal item under the seat. → *Expect:* bags are placed in one motion and the aisle opens.
8. **Step into the row before organizing.** Let others pass, then settle seat belt, phone, book, and headphones. → *Expect:* the aisle is clear while you arrange yourself.
9. **Sit down and stay aware.** Keep feet and straps out of the aisle and listen for crew instructions about bags or seats. → *Expect:* boarding continues around you with minimal friction.

## Decision points

- You boarded late and bins are full → ask crew where to place the bag instead of hunting backward through the cabin.
- You need overhead medical equipment → tell crew early so it is not separated from you.
- Family or assistance boarding is offered → use it only if you need the extra time.
- Seat is occupied → check row and seat letter calmly before involving crew.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Bag must be gate-checked:** overhead bins are full → remove keys, medicine, batteries, documents, and valuables before surrendering it.
- **F2 Aisle backup:** you need something from a stowed bag → wait until passengers pass, then retrieve it during a pause.
- **F3 Wrong seat:** row or side is mistaken → move promptly and apologize without debating in the aisle.
- **F4 Personal item too large:** it will not fit under the seat → ask crew before forcing it or blocking legroom.
- **F5 Boarding pass issue:** scanner rejects the pass → step aside with staff so the lane keeps moving.

## Verification

You are seated in the correct seat with required items accessible, carry-on safely stowed, and no critical item left in a gate-checked bag.

## Variations

- Southwest-style open seating: boarding position matters, so have essentials ready before the group lines up.
- Small regional jets: assume larger rollers may be valet-tagged and pack critical items separately.
- International flights: passport checks can occur at the gate, so keep documents available until seated.
- Tight connections: walk with purpose but do not push through boarding groups unless staff directs it.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep bags from striking people, do not block emergency equipment, and never place power banks, spare lithium batteries, passports, or medicine into checked baggage.
