## Goal

You check into your reserved room at the front desk, understand the deposit hold placed on your card, verify the room matches what you booked, and know the wifi and breakfast details before you settle in.

## Preconditions

- A confirmed reservation (confirmation number or booking-app entry) and arrival within the property's check-in window, usually mid-afternoon onward.
- A government photo ID and the payment card, ideally the same card used to book.
- Knowledge of your rate's inclusions (breakfast, resort fees, cancellation terms) so you can spot a discrepancy at the desk.

## Steps

1. **Go to the front desk and give your name and reservation.** Have the confirmation number and ID ready. → *Expect:* the clerk finds your booking and confirms the room type, rate, and number of nights.
2. **Present ID and a payment card.** Hotels take a card even on prepaid bookings, to cover a security or incidentals deposit. → *Expect:* the clerk takes an imprint or taps the card and returns your ID.
3. **Understand the deposit hold before you agree.** Ask the amount and whether it is a hold (released at checkout) or a charge. A hold reduces your available balance for days after you leave. ⚠️ *Irreversible in the moment:* on a debit card the held funds are genuinely locked until the bank releases them, sometimes a week later, which can strand you on a tight balance. → *Expect:* a stated hold amount you have confirmed your card can absorb, ideally on a credit card rather than debit.
4. **Confirm the practical details while you are still at the desk.** Ask the wifi network and password, breakfast hours and whether it is included in your rate, checkout time, and where to park. → *Expect:* wifi credentials, breakfast time and inclusion status, and checkout time noted.
5. **Take the key and note the room number privately.** Clerks write the number rather than say it aloud for security; keep the key sleeve, which usually has it. → *Expect:* a working key card and your room number recorded discreetly.
6. **Do a room-check ritual on entering.** Test that the key works, confirm the room type and bed configuration match the booking, check the lock and deadbolt, run the shower and toilet, test the AC or heat, look at the bed and bathroom for cleanliness and any pre-existing damage, and confirm wifi connects. → *Expect:* everything works and matches; note any pre-existing damage now so you are not blamed later.
7. **Report any mismatch or fault immediately, from the room.** Call the desk while the evidence is fresh. → *Expect:* a fix, a room change, or a logged note; a same-day report is far stronger than one at checkout.

## Decision points

- Arriving before check-in time: ask to leave bags with the desk or bell staff and request early check-in; it is often granted free if a room is ready.
- Debit card and a large hold: ask whether you can put the incidentals hold on a credit card instead, or reduce it, to avoid locking your travel funds.
- Room does not match the booking (wrong bed type, smoking, noisy location): raise it at once and ask for the booked type; the desk has the most flexibility right after check-in.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Reservation not found:** show the confirmation email or app booking with its number and the card charged. Third-party bookings sometimes sit under the agency's name; the clerk can search by that.
- **F2 Hold declined or card rejected:** try another card, or ask the desk to lower the incidentals hold; many properties will if you decline in-room charges.
- **F3 Room dirty, damaged, or faulty on the room check:** call the desk immediately with specifics and photos; request a fix or a different room while occupancy is being assigned.
- **F4 Deposit not released after checkout:** keep the checkout receipt; holds auto-expire in a few business days, and the receipt plus a call to the hotel and your bank resolves a stuck one.

## Verification

You hold a working key to a room that matches your booking, you know the deposit hold amount and how it clears, and you have the wifi and breakfast details, with any pre-existing room faults already reported.

## Variations

- `us` properties commonly add a per-night incidentals hold (50 to 100) and sometimes a resort fee not shown in the prepaid rate; `eu` and some Asian hotels may collect a city or tourist tax in cash at the desk.
- Fully digital properties send a mobile key and skip the desk; the room-check ritual and hold questions still apply, handled in the app.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to your money via the deposit hold and to your identity via the ID and card you present. Prefer a credit card for holds, keep the room number off overheard conversation, use the deadbolt, and know your floor's fire exit. The hotel retains your ID and stay details; that is normal, but decline to let anyone photograph your passport beyond a required legal scan where you are uncomfortable.
