## Goal

A beach bag contains sun, hydration, phone, food, and sand-management essentials without carrying unnecessary valuables.

## Preconditions

- You know the weather, expected beach rules, and how long you plan to stay.
- You have a bag that can handle sand, damp towels, and sunscreen.
- You have drinking water and sun protection.
- You can leave valuables at home or secure them elsewhere.

## Steps

1. **Choose the bag layout.** Use a tote, backpack, or mesh bag with a smaller dry bag or zip pouch inside. → *Expect:* wet, sandy, and delicate items can be separated.
2. **Pack sun protection first.** Add sunscreen, hat, sunglasses, cover-up, and lip balm with SPF. → *Expect:* sun items are easy to reach before you sit down.
3. **Add water.** Pack enough water for each person, plus extra for heat or a long walk from parking. → *Expect:* bottles stand upright or seal tightly.
4. **Protect the phone.** Put phone, keys, cards, and any cash in a dry bag or sealed pouch. → *Expect:* electronics are isolated from sunscreen, sand, and wet towels.
5. **Pack snacks.** Choose sturdy snacks that tolerate heat, such as fruit, nuts, crackers, or sandwiches in a cooler if needed. → *Expect:* food will not melt loose into the bag.
6. **Limit valuables.** [BRANCH: solo trip | group trip] bring only essentials, or assign one person to watch items during swims. → *Expect:* the bag contains little that would be painful to lose.
7. **Plan for sand.** Add a towel, small sheet, brush, spare plastic bag, or mesh pouch for sandy shoes and wet clothing. → *Expect:* sand has a place to go besides every pocket.
8. **Do a final weight and leak check.** Close bottles, cap sunscreen, shake the bag lightly, and remove extras. → *Expect:* the bag is carryable and nothing leaks.

## Decision points

- Beach has no shade → add umbrella, sun shirt, or plan a shorter visit.
- Swimming is planned → add dry clothes, a wet bag, and a second towel if space allows.
- Food needs cooling → use an insulated pouch and ice pack, or choose shelf-stable snacks.
- You must bring ID or payment card → keep one card and ID in the dry bag, not a full wallet.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Sunscreen leaks:** detect greasy bottle or loose cap → wipe it, seal it in a pouch, and keep it away from phone and food.
- **F2 Water runs short:** detect one small bottle for a long hot visit → buy or fill more before reaching the beach.
- **F3 Phone gets sandy:** detect grit on case or pouch → keep it sealed and rinse only waterproof gear according to the manufacturer.
- **F4 Bag is too heavy:** detect shoulder strain before leaving → remove duplicate clothes, full-size products, and extra valuables.

## Verification

Before leaving, the bag contains sun protection, water, phone protection, snacks if needed, and a sand or wet-item plan, with valuables minimized.

## Variations

- `family`: add diapers, kid sunscreen, extra water, shade, and labeled snacks.
- `transit`: favor backpack carry, sealed bottles, and a small towel.
- `rocky-beach`: add water shoes and a more durable sitting pad.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk, mostly sunburn, dehydration, lost valuables, and spoiled food. Keep medications, phone, ID, and keys protected from heat and water, and do not leave valuables unattended on a crowded beach.
