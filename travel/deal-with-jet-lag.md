## Goal

Your body clock is realigned to the destination time zone within a few days of arrival, so you sleep at local night and function during local day.

## Preconditions

- You know the time difference and its direction (eastward travel, losing hours, is harder than westward).
- You can control light exposure to some degree (sunglasses, a window, a dark room).
- No medical reason to avoid melatonin or caffeine; if unsure, check with a clinician first.

## Steps

1. **Compute the shift and its direction.** Eastward (e.g. US to Europe) advances your clock and is the hard direction; westward delays it and is easier. → *Expect:* a number of hours and a label, "advance" or "delay".
2. **Pre-shift 2 to 3 days before flying.** Eastward: go to bed and wake 1 hour earlier each day. Westward: 1 hour later each day. → *Expect:* by departure, your sleep window has moved 2 to 3 hours toward destination time.
3. **Set your watch and phone to destination time as you board.** Then act on that time: decide "is it day or night there right now?" → *Expect:* every food/sleep decision now references local destination time, not origin.
4. **Sleep on the plane only if it is night at the destination.** If it is destination-daytime, stay awake (screen, walk the aisle). Use an eye mask and earplugs for the sleep window. → *Expect:* your onboard rest aligns with destination night, not origin night.
5. **On landing, get outdoor daylight if it is local morning or midday.** [BRANCH: arrive local morning/day → seek bright light and stay up until local evening | arrive local night → keep lights dim, go to bed even if not fully tired] → *Expect:* light exposure reinforces the correct new schedule.
6. **Apply the first-day rule: no nap longer than 20 to 30 minutes.** Set an alarm. A long afternoon crash resets progress. → *Expect:* you reach local bedtime genuinely sleepy.
7. **Use caffeine as a tool, not a habit.** Have it in the local morning to stay awake; stop 8+ hours before target bedtime so it does not block sleep. → *Expect:* alert during local day, able to fall asleep at local night.
8. **Optional melatonin, low dose (0.5 to 1 mg), 30 to 60 min before target bedtime for eastward trips.** Small doses work better than large ones for timing. → *Expect:* easier sleep onset at the new bedtime; repeat 2 to 4 nights.

## Decision points

- Trip is under 2 to 3 days → do not adapt at all; stay on home time and just power through, adaptation costs more than it saves.
- Crossing 8+ zones eastward → expect 4 to 6 days; front-load light management and be strict about naps.
- Arrival at 3 a.m. local with no daylight cue → treat it as night, sleep, and let the next morning's light do the work.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 The killer afternoon nap:** you "rest your eyes" and wake 3 hours later at dusk → next night will be wrecked; if it happens, still force yourself to local bedtime and lean harder on morning light the next day.
- **F2 Caffeine too late:** an evening coffee leaves you wired at 1 a.m. → cut off caffeine by early afternoon going forward.
- **F3 Wrong-direction light:** bright light at the wrong time can push your clock the wrong way → eastward travelers should avoid bright light in the early morning of the first day or two and seek it late morning instead.
- **F4 Dehydration mistaken for jet lag:** headache and fog from a dry cabin → drink water on and after the flight before blaming the time zone.

## Verification

By day 3 to 4 at the destination you fall asleep within ~30 minutes at local bedtime, sleep through most of the local night, and stay awake without a long nap through the local day.

## Variations

- `westward`: easier; staying up later is more natural, so use evening light and a later caffeine window.
- `business-short-trip`: for 1 to 2 night trips, keep meetings scheduled during your home-time peak hours and skip adaptation entirely.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Melatonin and caffeine interact with some conditions and medications; confirm they are appropriate for you. Do not drive while fighting sleep in a new time zone.
