## Goal

Your passport, ID, cards, and travel documents are protected against loss and theft, and a loss can be recovered from quickly using copies and split storage.

## Preconditions

- The physical documents in hand: passport, ID, tickets, insurance, credit/debit cards.
- A phone with cloud storage or secure notes, plus an email account you can reach abroad.
- Access to a scanner or phone camera before you leave.

## Steps

1. **Inventory what you are carrying.** List passport number, card numbers, insurance policy number, and the emergency phone lines for each card issuer and your embassy. → *Expect:* a single list naming every critical document and its ID number.
2. **Make copies in three forms.** Scan/photograph each document. Keep (a) a digital copy in encrypted cloud storage, (b) a copy emailed to yourself, and (c) one paper photocopy. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* if the cloud copy is not password-protected, a phone theft exposes your identity data, so lock it before uploading. → *Expect:* each document retrievable from at least two independent places.
3. **Split physical storage.** Never keep all documents together. Carry your passport and one card on your person; leave a backup card and the paper copies in a separate bag or the hotel safe. → *Expect:* no single bag whose loss takes everything.
4. **Carry the daily minimum only.** Once at the destination, leave the passport in the hotel safe and carry a photocopy plus a secondary ID for day trips, unless local law requires the original. → *Expect:* the original passport is not in your pocket in a crowd.
5. **Use the hotel safe correctly.** Confirm it locks and unlocks with your chosen code before loading it; photograph the closed safe contents. → *Expect:* safe accepts and re-opens with your code on a test cycle.
6. **Keep a decoy and a real wallet.** An old wallet with a few small bills for a mugging scenario; the real cards elsewhere on your body (money belt or interior pocket). → *Expect:* a handover option that does not cost you your real cards.
7. **Record the loss protocol before you need it.** Save embassy address/hours and card-cancellation numbers offline on your phone. → *Expect:* you can act on a loss without internet access.

## Decision points

- Country requires carrying original ID at all times → carry it but in a money belt, not a back pocket, and still keep copies in the safe.
- No in-room safe → use the front-desk safe with a signed receipt, or a portable travel lock on your bag.
- Traveling as a group → distribute documents across people so one lost bag never holds a whole family's passports.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Passport lost or stolen:** report to local police for a report number, then contact your embassy for an emergency travel document; the copies you made speed this enormously.
- **F2 Cards stolen:** call the issuer's emergency line immediately to freeze them (many apps let you freeze instantly), then use your backup card.
- **F3 Phone with all copies stolen:** the emailed copies and paper copy are your fallback; this is exactly why storage is split across media.
- **F4 Safe won't reopen:** do not force it; call the front desk, who can override with a master key and a witnessed log.

## Verification

Every critical document exists in at least two independent locations (one physical, one digital-offline), no single bag holds all originals, and you can name your embassy contact and card-cancellation numbers without internet.

## Variations

- `long-trip`: register with your home country's traveler enrollment program so the embassy can reach you in a crisis.
- `family`: give each adult a copy of the others' passport photo pages in case one person is separated.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk: these documents are your identity and money. Encrypt digital copies, never post boarding passes or passport photos to social media (the barcodes and numbers are exploitable), and shred paper copies after the trip.
