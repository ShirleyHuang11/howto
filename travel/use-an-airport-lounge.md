## Goal

Enter and use an airport lounge without wasting time, violating guest rules, or missing the flight.

## Preconditions

- You have a same-day boarding pass.
- You know your terminal, departure gate, and boarding time.
- You have a possible access method: eligible card, lounge membership, day pass, premium cabin, airline status, or paid entry.

## Steps

1. **Identify your access route.** [BRANCH: card/pass | cabin/status | paid entry] check the card app, airline app, lounge network app, or booking page for eligibility, guest allowance, and entry fee. → *Expect:* you know exactly which credential will be scanned.
2. **Find a reachable lounge.** Search by terminal and concourse after security, then compare it with your gate location and any train or passport-control barriers. → *Expect:* the lounge is on your side of the airport or clearly worth the transfer.
3. **Check hours and capacity notes.** Confirm open hours, maximum stay, and whether walk-ins or guest access are restricted at peak times. → *Expect:* you avoid walking to a lounge that is closed or denying entry.
4. **Time the visit backward from boarding.** Plan to leave the lounge 10-20 minutes before boarding starts, more if the gate is far or international checks are possible. → *Expect:* lounge time fits the flight, not the other way around.
5. **Present credentials at reception.** Show boarding pass plus card, membership QR, invitation, or premium cabin document; mention guests before they scan. → *Expect:* staff confirms entry, fee, and guest count.
6. **Locate essentials first.** Find restrooms, water, coffee, power outlets, quiet areas, and the departure screen before settling in. → *Expect:* you can use the lounge efficiently without wandering later.
7. **Use included services reasonably.** Take food, drinks, Wi-Fi, showers, or work areas that are actually included; ask before assuming spa, nap rooms, premium alcohol, or takeaway boxes are free. → *Expect:* no surprise charge or awkward correction.
8. **Follow lounge etiquette.** Keep calls quiet, wear shoes, supervise children, bus obvious trash, avoid speaker audio, and do not reserve multiple seats with bags. → *Expect:* you get the benefit without becoming the problem in a crowded room.
9. **Leave on a gate-based alarm.** Set an alarm for your walk time and check the departure board before exiting. → *Expect:* you arrive at the gate before your boarding group is active.

## Decision points

- Lounge is before security → use it only if you have a long buffer and security lines are predictable.
- Multiple lounges are available → choose the one in your departure terminal over the one with better reviews unless layover time is generous.
- Day pass costs more than a meal → buy entry only if showers, quiet work space, or delay protection make the price rational.
- You have guests → verify guest fees before scanning because some charges post immediately.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Denied for capacity:** reception refuses eligible access → ask when to retry, then use a restaurant, quiet gate, or another eligible lounge.
- **F2 Wrong terminal:** the lounge is unreachable after security → stop walking, recheck the airport map, and stay near your gate.
- **F3 Surprise fee:** a guest or card visit is chargeable → decline before entry if the value is not worth it.
- **F4 Boarding changes:** the gate moves or boarding starts early → leave immediately and skip food or showers.
- **F5 Belongings spread out:** items are easy to forget → pack everything before getting food or visiting the restroom.

## Verification

You entered with a valid access method, used only included services or approved paid extras, and reached the gate before boarding closed.

## Variations

- International departures: lounges may sit after passport control, so confirm the border-control side.
- Arrival lounges: access rules often differ from departure lounges and may require an arriving long-haul ticket.
- Credit card programs: app listings can change, so rely on the current app entry and reception staff.
- Airline clubs: partner flights may qualify differently from flights operated by the airline itself.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Watch bags even in premium spaces, keep boarding documents private at check-in, and be cautious with alcohol before travel. Paid entry and guest fees can be nonrefundable once processed.
