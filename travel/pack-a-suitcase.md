## Goal

A suitcase packed for a trip: everything needed, nothing punitive to carry, liquids compliant, documents on the body — and it closes without sitting on it.

## Preconditions

- Trip parameters: days, destination weather (`daily/self-care/dress-for-the-weather`'s four numbers, for *there*), formality needs (one nice outfit?), and the luggage rules of your ticket (`travel/book-a-flight`'s bag decisions: carry-on dimensions and weight, checked allowance).
- The bag itself, plus pouches/packing cubes if you have them; a luggage scale for tight allowances.

## Steps

1. **Build the list from days × activities, not from the wardrobe.** Outfits per day with re-wear honesty (bottoms 2–3 wears, tops 1–2), one weather-contingency layer, sleepwear, underwear/socks = days + 1. Write it — packing from a list is the anti-forget technology. → *Expect:* a countable list matched to the trip, not a maybe-pile.
2. **Stage everything on the bed before anything enters the bag.** The full list laid out, then the edit pass: each "just in case" item defends itself (rule of thumb: if you can buy it there cheaply, it doesn't ride). → *Expect:* a final staged set, visibly smaller than the first pile.
3. **Pack heavy-low, wrinkle-smart.** Shoes (soles-out, stuffed with socks) and heavy items at the wheeled end; rolled casualwear fills the middle (rolling beats folding for space and creases); the nice outfit flat on top or in a garment fold; cubes group by category. → *Expect:* a stable, layered bag with dense mass at the bottom-when-upright.
4. **Do liquids and restricted items by the rules.** Carry-on: containers ≤100 ml in the one clear bag, ready to present; power banks *must* ride carry-on (never checked); anything sharp/tools → checked only. Checked bag: liquids bagged anyway (pressure leaks) inside a plastic layer. → *Expect:* security-line compliance pre-solved; no leak vectors loose among clothes.
5. **Keep the critical layer on your body/personal item, never in the case.** Documents (passport `government/renew-a-passport`, boarding pass `travel/check-in-for-a-flight`), medications (`healthcare/refill-a-prescription` supply + a buffer, in original labeled packaging), valuables, one change of underwear if the bag is checked (lost-bag insurance), chargers. → *Expect:* losing the suitcase entirely would be annoying, not trip-ending.
6. **Close, weigh, and tag.** Zip test without force (expansion zip is the honest relief valve); weigh against the allowance *now*, not at the counter; name/contact tag outside, and a copy *inside* the bag. → *Expect:* under-allowance confirmed at home; the bag identifiable inside and out.

## Decision points

- Carry-on-only vs checked → trips under ~a week almost always fit carry-on with the roll-and-cube discipline, and carry-on structurally cannot be lost by the airline; checked buys liquids freedom and knees that don't hoist.
- Laundry-capable destinations (`housing`-style stays, holiday rentals) → halve the clothing list and pack a travel detergent sheet; `daily/home/do-the-laundry` works on the road.
- Gifts/souvenir space → pack at 80% capacity out, or bring a packable duffel — the return trip is always bigger.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overweight at the counter:** the on-body layer absorbs dense items (books, chargers) into pockets/personal item; wearing the heavy jacket is the classic legal move; this is why step 6 weighs at home.
- **F2 Forgot something anyway:** triage by buyability — toothbrushes exist everywhere (`daily/errands/buy-groceries` abroad is itself a pleasure); only documents and prescription meds justify real recovery efforts (embassy/pharmacy respectively).
- **F3 Liquid leaked in transit:** the plastic layer contained it (or teaches why it exists); rinse-wearables first (`daily/home/do-the-laundry` F-series logic), and the shampoo travels in a sealed bag forever after.
- **F4 Bag didn't arrive (checked):** report *before leaving the airport* at the carrier's desk — reference number + the inside tag's contact; step 5's on-body layer is what makes the following 24 h livable; keep receipts for the interim essentials (airlines reimburse within rules).

## Verification

The list is fully crossed off, the bag closes unforced and weighs under allowance (verified at home), liquids and batteries sit where the rules put them, and the on-body layer passes the thought experiment: "suitcase vanishes — trip survives."

## Variations

- Business trips: the garment layer dominates (suit bag or careful top-fold), and the everything-charges-one-cable kit is the real packing skill.
- Backpack travel: same list discipline, compression instead of cubes, and weight moves *up and close to the spine* — the heavy-low rule inverts.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. The two rules with legal/safety teeth: power banks in the cabin, sharps in the hold. The tag's phone-not-home-address convention keeps your empty house's location off the carousel.
