## Goal

Get through an overnight flight with the best practical chance of sleep and a functional arrival day.

## Preconditions

- A booked overnight flight and arrival time known.
- Any medication, sleep aid, or health need cleared for safe use.
- A small personal item allowed at the seat.

## Steps

1. **Choose the seat deliberately.** Pick window if you sleep leaning against the wall, aisle if you need frequent bathroom access. → *Expect:* the seat supports your most likely overnight need.
2. **Set the arrival plan.** Decide how you will reach lodging, whether early check-in exists, and what must happen before you can rest. → *Expect:* landing does not start with basic logistics confusion.
3. **Pack a sleep kit.** Include eye mask, earplugs or headphones, neck support, warm layer, toothbrush, and any medication. → *Expect:* sleep items are reachable under the seat.
4. **Hydrate before boarding.** Drink water and bring a filled bottle if airport rules allow. → *Expect:* you start the flight hydrated without relying only on service carts.
5. **Time caffeine.** Avoid caffeine late in the departure day unless you are intentionally staying awake until arrival. → *Expect:* caffeine is not fighting your sleep window.
6. **Set your clock to destination time.** Mentally follow the destination night as soon as you board. → *Expect:* meal, screen, and sleep choices line up with arrival.
7. **Create darkness.** Dim screens, use the eye mask, and avoid bright overhead light once ready to sleep. → *Expect:* your body gets a clear night signal.
8. **Reduce interruptions.** Buckle the seat belt over the blanket and keep essentials in one pouch. → *Expect:* crew can see the belt and you do not rummage in the dark.
9. **Wake before landing.** Use the last 30 to 45 minutes for bathroom, water, face wipe, and documents. → *Expect:* you are awake enough to leave the aircraft calmly.
10. **Protect the arrival day.** Get daylight, eat a normal meal, and nap only briefly if needed. → *Expect:* you can function without losing the whole day to sleep.

## Decision points

- You cannot sleep on planes → plan quiet rest instead of chasing perfect sleep.
- Arrival is morning → seek daylight and delay long sleep until local bedtime.
- Arrival is late night → keep the transfer simple and go straight to bed.
- You use sleep medication → avoid alcohol and confirm timing with a clinician or label.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Seat mismatch:** detect discomfort from aisle bumps or window confinement, recover with pillow placement and realistic expectations.
- **F2 Dry cabin headache:** detect thirst, dry mouth, or headache, recover with water and reduced alcohol.
- **F3 Screen keeps you awake:** detect scrolling past intended sleep time, recover by putting the device away and using audio only.
- **F4 Arrival crash:** detect a long daytime nap urge, recover with a short alarmed nap and daylight afterward.

## Verification

You arrive with essential items accessible, onward transport known, and a plan that supports the destination day's schedule.

## Variations

- Short red-eye: skip elaborate sleep setup and focus on quiet rest and fast arrival logistics.
- Long-haul red-eye: add compression socks if appropriate and walk periodically.
- Traveling with children: pack separate sleep cues, snacks, and a landing cleanup kit.

## Safety & privacy

Low risk. Keep passports and medication on your person, do not mix alcohol with sedatives, and avoid driving immediately if severely sleep-deprived.
