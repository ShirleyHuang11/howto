## Goal

A delayed or canceled flight converted from helpless waiting into managed rebooking: your connection protected or replaced, your rights and receipts collected, and your evening salvaged by moving faster than the queue.

## Preconditions

- A delay or cancellation announced (board, app notification, or the quiet gate-change shuffle that precedes announcements).
- The airline's app installed and logged in (`travel/check-in-for-a-flight` set this up) — in a disruption, the app and the phone line are faster than the desk queue for almost everything.

## Steps

1. **Establish the real situation before reacting.** The app's flight status, the inbound aircraft's status (flight trackers show whether your plane has even left its previous city — the honest predictor), and the stated reason (weather vs technical vs crew matters for both prognosis and compensation). → *Expect:* a working estimate of "short slip," "long delay," or "this flight is quietly dead."
2. **Do the connection math immediately.** Delay end-to-end vs your connection's minimum time; international connections need more. The moment the math fails, you are in rebooking territory *now*, while seats on alternatives still exist. → *Expect:* a verdict: ride it out, or rebook.
3. **Rebooking: race on three channels at once.** Join the desk queue *and* call the airline *and* open the app's rebooking flow; the first channel to answer wins, and the phone agent can do everything the desk can. Know your ask before anyone answers: the specific alternative flights you already looked up. → *Expect:* a confirmed new routing spoken back to you, with the record locator updated in the app.
4. **Trigger your care rights as thresholds pass.** Meal vouchers at the 2-to-3-hour marks, hotel + transfers on overnight strandings, rebooking-or-refund on cancellations: regimes differ (EU261-class rules are the strongest; US and others vary) but the desk provides more when *asked with the threshold named*. → *Expect:* vouchers/hotel in hand where applicable — and every receipt kept when you self-pay under duress (reimbursement claims live on receipts).
5. **Protect the far end.** Message whoever meets you, re-time the hotel's late arrival (`travel/book-a-hotel` step 7's warning habit), shift the ground plans; checked-bag question asked at rebooking ("does my bag follow automatically?"). → *Expect:* the destination side re-synced; no one circling arrivals for a ghost.
6. **File the compensation claim afterward, from your sofa.** Delay length, reason, and route determine cash compensation in several regimes (distinct from the care above; airline-caused long delays often owe fixed sums under EU261-class rules). The airline's claim form + your receipts + the delay documentation (screenshot the boards/app now, during the chaos). → *Expect:* a filed claim with evidence attached; patience for the response cycle.

## Decision points

- Tight-but-alive connections: tell the crew on landing (they sometimes radio ahead), know your arrival gate-to-gate route, and walk with intent — but never sprint past the rebooking desk if the app already shows the connection lost.
- Weather vs airline-fault: weather typically voids cash compensation but not care duties; the reason on record matters, so note what agents and boards actually say.
- Self-paying to escape (a train, another carrier): sometimes rational on short routes — but confirm refund rights on the abandoned ticket first, and know self-booked escapes are reimbursed only in the stronger regimes with a fight.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Everyone rebooked before you moved:** the three-channel race exists because the desk queue is where options go to die; salvage via the app's standby lists and the next morning's first flights, hotel triggered per step 4.
- **F2 Bag went where you didn't:** report before leaving the airport (`travel/pack-a-suitcase` F4's protocol); rebookings are the top bag-separation cause, and the report ties it to your new routing.
- **F3 Voucher refused / rights denied at the desk:** stay calm, get the refusal *in the record* (agent name, time), self-pay minimally with receipts, and let the written claim (step 6) re-litigate — desks under siege refuse things regulators later pay.
- **F4 Rage at the gate agent rising:** the agent didn't delay the plane and controls your outcome; the travelers who get quietly upgraded out of chaos are unfailingly the pleasant ones. This is strategy, not just manners.

## Verification

You know why and how long, the connection math was run early, any rebooking is confirmed in-app, care thresholds were claimed as crossed, receipts and screenshots exist, the far end is re-synced, and the compensation claim (where owed) is filed with evidence.

## Variations

- Last-flight-of-the-day cancellations: the hotel decision leads everything — trigger it before the queue exhausts the nearby rooms.
- Group/family strandings: one adult works the channels while one holds the camp; splitting the rebooking across the party's record sometimes frees seat combinations solo travelers can't see.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to money and plans, none to person. The recipe's core inversion: the delay is unmanageable, but the *response market* (seats, rooms, vouchers) is first-come — speed and civility are the two currencies, and screenshots are the receipts of both.
