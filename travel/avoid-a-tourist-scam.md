## Goal

Recognize and avoid common tourist scams while keeping money, documents, and attention under your control.

## Preconditions

- You know the rough local price range for taxis, tours, common souvenirs, and entry tickets.
- Cash, cards, phone, and documents are separated instead of stored in one easy target.
- You have offline access to your hotel address and a map.

## Steps

1. **Set a refusal script.** Use a calm "No, thank you" and keep walking without debate. → *Expect:* you have a default response before pressure starts.
2. **Treat urgency as a warning.** Reject offers that require deciding now, following someone, or handing over money before seeing terms. → *Expect:* the too-good deal slows down or disappears.
3. **Use official taxi and ride channels.** Choose marked taxi stands, app rides, hotel-called cars, or transit; confirm meter use or fixed fare before moving. → *Expect:* the fare method is agreed before the ride begins.
4. **Keep documents out of casual hands.** Show ID only to legitimate officials, hotels, rental agencies, or ticket checks; do not let a stranger "inspect" a passport or wallet. → *Expect:* your critical documents remain physically controlled.
5. **Watch the distraction setup.** If someone spills, bumps, asks for a photo, drops jewelry, ties a bracelet, or creates a crowd, protect pockets and bags first. → *Expect:* attention returns to valuables before a second person can act.
6. **Verify tickets and tours at the source.** Buy through the venue, official app, licensed booth, or a reputable platform with reviews and cancellation terms. → *Expect:* ticket validity can be checked before payment.
7. **Control payment exposure.** Count cash privately, cover PIN entry, keep the card in sight, and decline currency conversion at card terminals when your card offers a fair rate. → *Expect:* payment happens once, in the expected currency.
8. **Walk away from forced gifts.** Bracelets, roses, CDs, petitions, tea invitations, and "free" photos can become demands for payment. → *Expect:* no object or obligation is accepted by accident.
9. **Debrief after any strange contact.** Step into a shop, hotel, or busy safe area and check wallet, phone, passport, and bag zippers. → *Expect:* missing items are noticed quickly enough to act.

## Decision points

- A uniformed person asks for ID → look for official setting, marked badge, and nearby authority; comply respectfully when legitimacy is clear.
- A taxi refuses the meter → agree a fair fixed fare before departure or leave and take another ride.
- A deal seems excellent → verify independently online or at the venue before paying.
- You feel crowded or followed → enter a staffed business and arrange transport from there.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Overcharged taxi:** fare is far above expected → stay calm, ask for receipt, pay only what is safe to dispute, and report through the app, hotel, or taxi authority.
- **F2 Fake ticket:** entry is refused → document the seller details, dispute the payment, and buy from the official source.
- **F3 Pickpocket attempt:** zipper open or item missing → move to a safe place, lock cards, track phone, and report passport loss immediately.
- **F4 Fake official pressure:** someone demands a fine on the street → ask to go to a police station or official office before paying.
- **F5 Group separated:** one person is pulled into a shop or side street → reunite in a public place and do not negotiate alone.

## Verification

You complete the outing with valuables, documents, and payment cards accounted for, and any paid service has a verifiable official source or agreed price.

## Variations

- Markets: bargaining can be normal, but forced handling or blocked exits are not.
- Nightlife districts: drink spiking, inflated bills, and fake cover charges deserve extra caution.
- Religious or cultural sites: unsolicited guides may be unofficial even when they sound authoritative.
- Border areas: money-changing and transport scams cluster where travelers are tired and carrying cash.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk. Avoid escalating arguments over small losses when physical safety is uncertain. Keep passport photos and emergency numbers stored securely, but do not display full identity documents unless there is a legitimate need.
