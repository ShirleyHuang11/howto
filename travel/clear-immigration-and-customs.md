## Goal

You pass through passport control (immigration) and then customs at an international arrival, entering the country legally with your belongings, and exit into the arrivals hall without a secondary inspection or penalty.

## Preconditions

- A valid passport with any required visa or electronic authorization already approved before travel.
- An accurate sense of what you are carrying: cash over the declaration threshold, food, plants, alcohol and tobacco over allowance, commercial goods, or anything restricted.
- Any arrival or customs declaration form or app entry completed (some countries push this to a kiosk or a mobile app filed before landing).

## Steps

1. **Follow the arrivals signs and pick the right immigration queue.** Lanes split by passport type: citizens or residents, this-country nationals, all-passports or foreign nationals, and often e-gates for eligible passports. Joining the wrong lane costs you the whole wait. → *Expect:* you are queued in a lane your passport is eligible for.
2. **Have documents ready before you reach the desk.** Passport open to the photo page, visa or authorization accessible, landing card and onward-ticket or address details in hand. Remove hat and sunglasses; silence the phone. → *Expect:* everything the officer will ask for is in your hand, not buried in a bag.
3. **Approach the desk or e-gate only when signaled.** Wait behind the line until called or the light turns green. → *Expect:* the officer waves you forward or the e-gate opens.
4. **Answer immigration questions plainly and honestly.** Purpose of visit, length of stay, where you are staying. Answer only what is asked, briefly and truthfully. Look up for the camera and fingerprint scan if requested. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* a false statement to an immigration officer can mean refused entry and a lasting record; never guess or embellish. → *Expect:* the passport is stamped or the entry recorded electronically, and you are released toward baggage.
5. **Collect checked baggage.** Read the screen for your flight's carousel; wait for and grab your bags. → *Expect:* all your checked bags in hand before you approach customs.
6. **Choose the correct customs channel.** Green "nothing to declare" only if you genuinely carry nothing over the limits; red "goods to declare" if you carry anything declarable, or the single channel where officers direct everyone. → *Expect:* you are in the channel that matches what you actually carry, ready to declare if needed.
7. **Declare honestly if you have anything to declare.** Hand over your completed form, state the goods, pay any duty. Declaring is routine and cheap; hiding is the offense. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* smuggling or a false customs declaration risks seizure, fines, and prosecution far exceeding any duty owed. → *Expect:* the officer processes the declaration, you pay any duty, and you are cleared.
8. **Walk through and keep any receipt or stamped form.** Some exits scan your form or ticket. → *Expect:* you exit into the public arrivals hall, clear of both checkpoints.

## Decision points

- Unsure whether an item is declarable (a food gift, a large watch, borderline cash): declare it. The green channel is only for genuine certainty; an honest declaration is never the thing that gets you in trouble.
- E-gate eligible: use it for a faster, question-free immigration pass, but you still must choose a customs channel afterward.
- Traveling with a family or group: most countries let a household clear customs together on one declaration; check the form.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Wrong immigration lane:** if you notice before the desk, move to the correct lane; if the officer redirects you, comply politely and rejoin the right queue.
- **F2 Missing or unclear document at the desk:** stay calm and provide what you have (booking confirmations, address, onward ticket on your phone). Officers can look up much of it; honesty and patience resolve most of it.
- **F3 Selected for secondary customs inspection:** cooperate fully, let them open bags, answer truthfully. A clean, honest traveler clears secondary routinely; resistance escalates it.
- **F4 Realize at the green channel you should have declared something:** turn back to the red channel or flag an officer before you exit. Voluntary correction is treated far more leniently than being caught.

## Verification

Your passport shows an entry stamp or recorded electronic entry, you cleared customs through the correct channel with any duty paid, and you are standing in the public arrivals hall with all your baggage and no pending inspection.

## Variations

- `us` uses CBP with a mobile pre-filing app for eligible travelers and Global Entry kiosks; `eu` Schengen splits EU and all-passports lanes and skips internal-border customs; many countries run a single combined channel where officers profile and pull travelers aside.
- Cash declaration thresholds differ widely (commonly around 10,000 in the local major currency); over the limit you must declare even though carrying it is legal.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk to your legal standing and record, not your body. Immigration collects biometrics and travel history. The single rule that protects you at both checkpoints is honesty: answer truthfully, declare when in doubt, and never attempt to conceal goods or misstate your purpose.
