## Goal

Wallet, phone, passport, and cards are carried so they are harder to steal in crowded travel areas and easier to recover from if something goes missing.

## Preconditions

- You know which items are essential for the outing.
- You have pockets with zips, a crossbody bag, money belt, or inner pouch.
- You can split cash, cards, and documents instead of carrying everything in one wallet.
- Emergency card numbers or digital backups are stored securely.

## Steps

1. **Reduce what you carry.** Leave spare cards, excess cash, and nonessential documents locked at lodging if safe. → *Expect:* losing the day bag would not remove every resource.
2. **Move valuables to controlled locations.** Put phone and wallet in zipped front pockets, inner jacket pockets, or a crossbody bag worn in front. → *Expect:* valuables are visible or touch-detectable by you.
3. **Use a decoy wallet if useful.** Carry a small wallet with a little cash and expired or low-risk cards in an accessible pocket. → *Expect:* essential cards and passport remain elsewhere.
4. **Secure the main bag.** Keep zippers closed, pulls forward, and the bag on the front of your body in crowds. → *Expect:* no pocket or zipper is open behind you.
5. **Recognize crowded-risk zones.** Increase attention at train doors, escalators, markets, tourist viewpoints, street performances, and ticket machines. → *Expect:* your hand naturally checks the bag when bodies press close.
6. **Watch for distraction plays.** [BRANCH: spill or bump | petition, photo, or commotion] step aside with your bag held, or keep walking and decline while checking valuables. → *Expect:* attention stays on possessions, not just the distraction.
7. **Handle payments deliberately.** Take out only the card or cash needed, then close the pocket or bag before moving away. → *Expect:* money is not exposed in a crowd.
8. **Respond quickly if something is missing.** Move to a safe spot, lock phone or cards, contact bank or lodging, and file a report if documents are gone. ⚠️ *Irreversible:* chasing a suspected thief can escalate danger, so prioritize account locks, location safety, and official help. → *Expect:* loss response starts without physical confrontation.

## Decision points

- You need a passport that day → carry it in an inner pouch, not the outer bag.
- A stranger tries to clean a spill, tie a bracelet, or block your path → step back and secure your bag first.
- Transit doors are opening or closing → stop phone scrolling and keep one hand on valuables.
- You feel crowded from multiple sides → rotate the bag to your chest and move to an edge.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Back pocket habit:** detect wallet or phone in a rear pocket → move it to a zipped front or inner pocket immediately.
- **F2 Open zipper:** detect bag gap after movement or sitting → close it and reposition pulls toward your hand.
- **F3 Tabletop phone grab:** detect phone left on cafe table edge → move it to a pocket or under your hand.
- **F4 Theft discovered late:** detect missing item after leaving area → lock cards or phone, notify providers, and report passport loss.

## Verification

Before entering a crowd, all essential valuables are zipped, front-carried, split across locations, and no phone or wallet is in a rear pocket or open bag.

## Variations

- `solo-travel`: keep one backup card and emergency cash separate from the day wallet.
- `night-market`: use minimal cash, front bag carry, and no loose phone in hand.
- `airport-or-station`: be most alert during boarding, ticket checks, and security repacking.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because theft can expose identity, money, travel documents, and personal data. Use device locks, bank alerts, and digital document copies stored securely. Avoid confrontation and protect your body before property.
