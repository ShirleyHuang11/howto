## Goal

Track shared trip expenses clearly so friends can settle once, fairly, and without awkward memory debates.

## Preconditions

- The group agrees which costs are shared and which are personal.
- Everyone has a payment method for settlement or agrees to cash.
- You have a shared tracker app, spreadsheet, notes document, or expense-splitting app.

## Steps

1. **Agree the split rules before spending.** Decide equal split, per-person nights, couples as one share or two, kids discounted, and which upgrades are personal. → *Expect:* the group can say what belongs in the shared pot.
2. **Create one shared tracker.** Use an app or spreadsheet with columns for date, payer, amount, currency, category, participants, and note. → *Expect:* everyone can see the running record.
3. **Enter expenses immediately.** Add who paid what while the receipt is still in hand. → *Expect:* the tracker reflects real spending instead of memory.
4. **Attach proof for large items.** Save receipt photos for lodging, rental cars, tours, fuel, groceries, and big meals. → *Expect:* big numbers are explainable later.
5. **Record participants per expense.** Mark if someone skipped dinner, joined late, had a solo drink, or used a separate room. → *Expect:* the split matches who benefited.
6. **Handle currencies consistently.** [BRANCH: app conversion | manual conversion] let the app convert on transaction date, or record local amount plus the card charge in home currency. → *Expect:* exchange-rate arguments are minimized.
7. **Do a midtrip check.** Spend five minutes every few days correcting missing entries and checking that one person is not floating too much cash. → *Expect:* errors are small and fixable.
8. **Settle at the end once.** Let the tracker calculate net balances, then make the fewest transfers possible. → *Expect:* everyone pays or receives a clear final amount.
9. **Close the loop politely.** Send final balances, payment handles, and a deadline before people scatter. → *Expect:* settlement feels administrative, not personal.

## Decision points

- One person paid a huge deposit → collect partial contributions early instead of making them finance the whole group.
- Someone has a tighter budget → separate optional upgrades before booking them.
- Cash economy destination → designate a cash log and photograph large receipts.
- Different trip lengths → split lodging and transport by actual nights or legs used.

## Failure modes & recovery

- **F1 Missing expense:** someone remembers a cost late → add it with best evidence and notify the group.
- **F2 Duplicate entry:** two people enter the same meal → compare receipt, delete one, and keep the payer accurate.
- **F3 Currency mismatch:** local amount and card charge differ → use the actual card charge when known.
- **F4 Awkward payer imbalance:** one friend keeps paying everything → rotate payers or send a midtrip reimbursement.
- **F5 Disputed shared item:** someone did not participate → remove them from that expense or mark the upgrade personal.

## Verification

The tracker shows every shared expense with payer and participants, and final net settlements are paid or explicitly acknowledged by all friends.

## Variations

- Couples: decide whether each couple pays as two people or by room before booking lodging.
- Road trips: fuel, tolls, parking, and rental insurance should be entered the day they happen.
- Group groceries: split staples evenly, but mark personal alcohol, supplements, or specialty items separately.
- International trips: choose one exchange-rate method and apply it consistently.

## Safety & privacy

Medium risk because money, payment handles, and travel dates are shared. Limit tracker access to the group, avoid posting receipts with full card numbers, and treat repayment expectations as explicit commitments.
